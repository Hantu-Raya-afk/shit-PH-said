Dzulkefly Promises — Again — to Crack Down on Workplace Bullying in Government Hospitals

Said by: Dzulkefly Ahmad, Health Minister

Category: Health

Date: 2026-03-10

📌 What Happened

Health Minister Dzulkefly Ahmad announced on March 10, 2026 that the Health Ministry will “bolster enforcement” against workplace bullying in government health facilities. He said public hospitals should be “professional and ethical workplace environments” and pointed to a Public Services Department (PSD) circular that bans coarse language and verbal abuse in the workplace. He pledged to “enhance implementation” of the circular to ensure “no ambiguity” in handling misconduct — language that strongly implies the existing rules are not being enforced.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a new announcement. Dzulkefly has been making near-identical promises since at least August 2025, when the Health Ministry recorded 430 bullying complaints with only four under investigation. He launched “zero tolerance” pledges, announced new guidelines classifying verbal threats and mockery as bullying (due in October 2025), and even threatened to fire senior officials who conceal cases. Months later, he is back at the podium saying the same things — which tells you everything about whether the previous rounds of promises produced results. Housemen and junior doctors continue to report systemic harassment, and the ministry’s response remains performative announcements rather than structural accountability.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

When a minister announces the same policy three times in under a year, the announcement is the policy — not the enforcement. Dzulkefly’s playbook is textbook PH governance: identify a real problem, hold a press conference, issue a circular, then do nothing until the next round of bad press forces another press conference. The circular he cited already exists. The guidelines were already launched. The zero-tolerance stance was already declared. If any of those measures had worked, he would not be standing up in March 2026 promising to “enhance implementation.” The problem is not ambiguity in the rules — it is impunity for senior medical staff who bully juniors, and a ministry that treats announcements as action.

📜 Previous Cases

In August 2025, the same minister made headlines for declaring zero tolerance on bullying after the case of “Dr S,” a houseman in Sabah allegedly bullied so severely she was confined to her room for years while the perpetrator moved abroad to pursue specialist training. In July 2025, the ministry announced new guidelines classifying insults, threats, and mockery as workplace bullying — guidelines supposedly launching in October 2025. Pressure group Hartal Doktor Kontrak has repeatedly called for systemic reform, not just guidelines, pointing to research showing high prevalence of bullying among Malaysian junior doctors across multiple government hospitals.

🎯 PH Callout

Anwar’s Madani government promised institutional reform and a government that works for the people. Dzulkefly is a senior PH minister in a critical portfolio, and the best he can offer after three years in power is re-announcing the same circular and calling it progress. Junior doctors — the backbone of Malaysia’s public health system — deserve more than recycled press releases. Either enforce the rules you already have, fire the people responsible, and publish the outcomes — or stop pretending the announcements mean anything.

Sources: Free Malaysia Today | The Malaysian Reserve (Aug 2025) | The Sun

Contract Doctors: 33,000 STILL Waiting for Permanent Posts — Promise Made in 2020, Still Broken in 2026

Said by: Malaysian Medical Association / Contract Doctors

Category: Health

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As of January 2026, approximately 33,000 contract doctors in Malaysia’s public healthcare system continue to work on temporary contracts without job security, pension benefits, or career advancement pathways available to permanent medical officers. The contract system was introduced as a “temporary” measure to absorb a surge of medical graduates. Six years later, it is still “temporary.” Contract doctors do the same work as permanent doctors — they see the same patients, work the same 84-hour weeks, handle the same emergencies — but without permanent employment status. CNA reported on the “doctor shortage, low pay, 84-hour working week” crisis. P4H Network described Malaysia’s healthcare system as a “ticking bomb.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A two-tier medical workforce where thousands of doctors live with constant job insecurity is unsustainable. Many leave public service entirely — heading to Singapore, Australia, or the private sector — contributing to brain drain and worsening the doctor shortage. Malaysia is already short 11,000 specialists. Junior doctors work 84-hour weeks. Public hospitals are overwhelmed as even T20 families switch from private healthcare due to unaffordable costs. The contract system is not just unfair to doctors — it is destroying the healthcare system from within. Every contract doctor who emigrates takes decades of medical training and public investment with them.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Both BN and PH promised to resolve the contract doctor issue. In 2021, contract doctors staged the rare “Black Monday” protest — doctors protesting should alarm any government, because doctors don’t protest unless things are desperate. PH politicised the issue, attacking the PN government for mistreating healthcare workers. Then PH took power and continued mistreating healthcare workers. Three years into PH 2.0, the majority are still on contracts. The government finds RM2.6 billion for JAKIM’s religious affairs budget and RM85.5 million for J-Kom propaganda — but can’t find permanent positions for 33,000 doctors keeping the public health system alive. The priorities are explicit.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The contract doctor crisis has been escalating since 2016 when the system was introduced. Under Muhyiddin’s PN government, the “Black Monday” protest drew national attention. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) did not resolve it. PH 2.0 promised permanent absorption — then absorbed a fraction. The pattern across administrations is identical: promise, delay, absorb a small batch for headlines, leave the rest on contracts. Meanwhile, Malaysia’s healthcare spending remains at 2.83% of GDP — far below the WHO recommendation and below regional peers like Thailand (3.8%) and Vietnam (3.0%).

🎯 PH CALLOUT: 33,000 doctors are keeping your hospitals running on temporary contracts while you fill GLC boards with political appointees on permanent salaries. You find RM2.6B for JAKIM and hundreds of millions for highway toll freezes — but can’t find positions for the doctors who save Malaysian lives every day. Every contract doctor who leaves for Singapore takes decades of training and public money with them. You’re not just breaking a promise — you’re dismantling the healthcare system one departure at a time. Absorb all 33,000 or explain to the rakyat why religious bureaucracy matters more than the people keeping them alive.

Malaysia's Healthcare System: A 'Ticking Bomb' — 2.83% of GDP While Countries Spend Double

Said by: P4H Network / University of Malaya Working Paper / The Star

Category: Health

Date: 2025-09-08

📌 What Happened

Multiple independent analyses have declared Malaysia’s healthcare system a ’ticking bomb.’ Public health expenditure sits at just 2.83% of GDP in 2023, far below WHO-recommended levels and regional peers. The doctor-to-patient ratio of 1:417 masks the true crisis in the public sector, which handles 80% of hospital beds with 54% of doctors. The country is 11,000 specialists short. Junior doctors work 84-hour weeks.

🔥 Why It Matters

Even T20 (top income) families are now switching to public hospitals because private healthcare has become unaffordable. When the rich start using government hospitals, the entire healthcare financing model has broken down. Public hospitals face overcrowding, equipment shortages, and staff burnout simultaneously.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The government’s response? ‘Targeted subsidies’ and a healthcare ‘reset plan.’ But experts warn this risks ’leaving the poor behind.’ The University of Malaya working paper called for a ‘paradigm shift’ from treating health funding as consumption to treating it as ‘strategic national investment.’ Instead, the government increases JAKIM’s budget and civil service pensions while hospitals crumble.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s healthcare was once the pride of the nation. The rot began with decades of underfunding across BN and now PH administrations. Contract doctors were promised permanent posts — still waiting. Medical graduates face uncertain futures while the government fast-tracks political appointees to cushy GLC positions.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised healthcare reform. Instead, you’ve presided over a system where Malaysians are literally dying on waiting lists for specialist care — 14 cardiothoracic surgeons for 33 million people. Your response is another committee, another ‘plan,’ another press conference. Fix your hospitals first.

Said by: DPM Ahmad Zahid / AADK

Category: Health

Date: 2025-08-10

📌 What Happened

A devastating new drug crisis is sweeping through Malaysian schools and communities: K-pods and synthetic drug-laced vapes are becoming the drug delivery mechanism of choice for young Malaysians. Deputy PM Ahmad Zahid acknowledged the situation was at a ‘critical level,’ with drug abuse cases surging 32.5% from 2023 to 2024 and amphetamine-type stimulants involved in over 70% of cases.

🔥 Why It Matters

The K-pod phenomenon — vape cartridges laced with synthetic drugs — is particularly insidious because it disguises drug use as ordinary vaping, making detection by parents and teachers nearly impossible. The government’s response has been the Anti-Drug Communication Plan 2025-2027 — essentially a PR campaign when what’s needed is enforcement and interdiction. Malaysia needs to ‘change its strategy’ according to Zahid, but the strategic change hasn’t materialised beyond press conferences. Meanwhile, a generation of young Malaysians is being chemically rewired.

🎯 PH Callout

Your DPM calls it ‘critical level’ and your response is a communication plan. Kids are vaping synthetic drugs in school bathrooms and you’re writing brochures. Shut down the Telegram channels. Enforce at the border. Fund school-level intervention. Or admit that ‘critical level’ is just another phrase you use for press conferences before moving on.

Fact: The Malaysian Medical Association called for the complete abolition of the contract system, which locks doctors into 3+2+2+4 year contracts with no job security.

🎯 PH Callout

Fourteen heart surgeons for 33 million people. And when someone suggests training more doctors regardless of race, your response is political outrage. You’re not protecting Bumiputera rights — you’re protecting a policy that kills Bumiputera patients by ensuring there aren’t enough specialists to treat them.
