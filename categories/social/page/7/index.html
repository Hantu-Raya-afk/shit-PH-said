[BM] Bloomberg: Malaysia 'Hadapi Tentangan Atas Rancangan Meluaskan Kawalan Internet'

Said by: Bloomberg and critics

Category: Social

Date: 2024-12-04

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada Disember 2024, Bloomberg melaporkan bahawa Malaysia menghadapi tentangan atas rancangan mengetatkan cengkaman ke atas internet. Kerajaan mencari kuasa dan penalti yang lebih luas untuk penyalahgunaan internet melalui peraturan baharu. Rest of World melaporkan bahawa pencipta kandungan di Malaysia menapis sendiri untuk mengelak tindakan keras — “Peraturan baharu menyukarkan untuk mencari rezeki,” kata mereka. ARTICLE 19 memanggilnya keputusan Mahkamah Persekutuan AKM yang mengembalikan peruntukan “sangat menyinggung” sebagai “pukulan kepada kebebasan bersuara.” Laman web berita kritikal disekat. SKMM diberi kuasa mengenakan denda ke atas platform sehingga RM10 juta.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Internet Malaysia pernah menjadi antara yang paling bebas di rantau ini — dilindungi oleh Jaminan MSC Malaysia yang berjanji tiada penapisan internet. PH secara sistematik membongkar jaminan itu. Apabila pencipta kandungan menapis sendiri, kesan menakutkan sudah berjaya — tiada pendakwaan diperlukan. Gabungan penyekatan laman web, pelesenan platform, penalti media sosial dan peruntukan AKM “sangat menyinggung” yang dikembalikan mewujudkan infrastruktur penapisan komprehensif yang tidak wujud ketika PH berkuasa.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: PH menunggang internet bebas untuk berkuasa. Facebook, YouTube dan WhatsApp adalah platform yang memecahkan monopoli media BN. Malaysiakini dan blog bebas adalah talian hayat PH apabila media arus perdana dikawal. Kini PH menyekat laman web, melesenkan platform, menghukum kandungan dan mewujudkan penapisan sendiri melalui ancaman pengawalseliaan. Jaminan MSC — janji pengasas ekonomi digital Malaysia — sudah mati. PH bukan sahaja memungkiri janji; ia menguburkannya di bawah pelbagai lapisan peraturan baharu yang memberi SKMM lebih kuasa daripada yang pernah ada di bawah BN.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Di bawah BN, Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar adalah bukti utama PH bagi kawalan internet autoritarian. PH memansuhkannya pada 2018. Di bawah PH 2.0, rejim pengganti lebih komprehensif: penyekatan laman web melalui SKMM, pelesenan platform, Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian, pindaan AKM dengan penalti RM10 juta, dan penapisan sendiri yang didorong oleh ketidakpastian pengawalseliaan. Setiap langkah individu dibingkaikan sebagai melindungi kanak-kanak, membanteras penipuan, atau melawan maklumat palsu. Secara kolektif, ia membentuk peralatan penapisan yang melebihi apa yang BN bina.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Bloomberg, Rest of World, ARTICLE 19, dan Freedom House semua bersetuju: anda sedang membina rejim penapisan internet. Pencipta menapis sendiri. Laman web disekat. Platform menghadapi penalti RM10 juta. Jaminan MSC menjanjikan tiada penapisan internet. Anda telah memungkiri janji itu lebih menyeluruh daripada mana-mana kerajaan sebelum ini. Tarik balik penalti berlebihan. Nyahsekat laman berita kritikal. Lindungi internet bebas yang memberi anda kuasa. Atau terima bahawa anda telah membina infrastruktur penapisan yang setiap kerajaan autoritarian masa depan akan berterima kasih kepada anda.

Bloomberg: Malaysia 'Faces Backlash Over Plan to Broaden Internet Controls'

Said by: Bloomberg and critics

Category: Social

Date: 2024-12-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In December 2024, Bloomberg reported that Malaysia faced backlash over plans to tighten its grip on the internet. The government sought broader powers and penalties for internet misuse through new regulations. Rest of World reported that creators in Malaysia were self-censoring to avoid crackdowns — “The new rules make it harder to make a living,” they said. ARTICLE 19 called the Federal Court’s CMA decision reinstating “grossly offensive” provisions “a blow to freedom of expression.” Critical news websites were blocked. MCMC was given powers to penalise platforms up to RM10 million.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia’s internet was once one of the freest in the region — protected by the MSC Malaysia Bill of Guarantees that promised no internet censorship. PH is systematically dismantling that guarantee. When content creators self-censor, the chilling effect has already succeeded — no prosecution needed. The combination of website blocking, platform licensing, social media penalties, and the reinstated “grossly offensive” CMA provision creates a comprehensive censorship infrastructure that didn’t exist when PH took power.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH rode the free internet to power. Facebook, YouTube, and WhatsApp were the platforms that broke BN’s media monopoly. Malaysiakini and independent blogs were PH’s lifeline when mainstream media was controlled. Now PH is blocking websites, licensing platforms, penalising content, and creating self-censorship through regulatory threat. The MSC Bill of Guarantees — a founding promise of Malaysia’s digital economy — is dead. PH didn’t just break the promise; it buried it under multiple layers of new regulation that give MCMC more power than it ever had under BN.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, the Anti-Fake News Act was PH’s primary evidence of authoritarian internet control. PH repealed it in 2018. Under PH 2.0, the replacement regime is more comprehensive: website blocking through MCMC, platform licensing, the Online Safety Act, CMA amendments with RM10 million penalties, and self-censorship driven by regulatory uncertainty. Each individual measure is framed as protecting children, combating scams, or fighting misinformation. Collectively, they constitute a censorship apparatus that exceeds anything BN built.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Bloomberg, Rest of World, ARTICLE 19, and Freedom House all agree: you’re building an internet censorship regime. Creators are self-censoring. Websites are blocked. Platforms face RM10 million penalties. The MSC Bill of Guarantees promised no internet censorship. You’ve broken that promise more comprehensively than any previous government. Withdraw the excessive penalties. Unblock critical news sites. Protect the free internet that gave you power. Or accept that you’ve built the censorship infrastructure every future authoritarian government will thank you for.

[BM] Pelanggaran Hak Tanah Orang Asli Berterusan — Komuniti Asal Dipindahkan Untuk Pembalakan Dan Perladangan

Said by: Amnesty International / Indigenous Rights Groups

Category: Social

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Sepanjang 2024, Orang Asli dan komuniti asal di Semenanjung Malaysia terus menghadapi pelanggaran sistematik hak tanah adat. Hutan datuk nenek moyang ditebang untuk konsesi pembalakan dan perladangan kelapa sawit, sering kali tanpa persetujuan bebas, termaklum dan terdahulu. Amnesty International membenderakan hak orang asal sebagai satu daripada tujuh bidang di mana Malaysia gagal memenuhi obligasi hak asasi manusia. Orang Asli membentuk hanya 0.2% perkhidmatan awam Malaysia walaupun merangkumi kira-kira 0.7% populasi — malah perwakilan berkadar pun dinafikan.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Orang Asli adalah komuniti paling terpinggir di Malaysia. Hak tanah adat mereka — diiktiraf oleh mahkamah tetapi secara sistematik dilanggar oleh kerajaan negeri — adalah asas identiti, mata pencarian dan kelangsungan budaya mereka. Apabila hutan datuk nenek moyang ditebang untuk perladangan, Orang Asli bukan sahaja kehilangan tanah — mereka kehilangan sumber makanan, kawasan tadahan air, tumbuhan ubatan dan tanah perkuburan. Kerajaan PH, yang mendakwa memperjuangkan hak asasi manusia dan keadilan sosial, tidak melakukan apa-apa untuk mengukuhkan perlindungan undang-undang bagi hak tanah orang asal atau menguatkuasakan keputusan mahkamah sedia ada.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: PH termasuk ahli politik yang memperjuangkan hak orang asal semasa pembangkang. Baru Bian — peguam hak tanah terkemuka dan AP PH — telah melawan kes tanah orang asal selama berdekad. Namun dalam kerajaan, PH tidak memperkenalkan undang-undang untuk mengkodifikasikan hak tanah adat, tidak menguatkuasakan keputusan mahkamah sedia ada yang melindungi wilayah orang asal, dan tidak meningkatkan perwakilan Orang Asli dalam perkhidmatan awam atau badan pembuat keputusan. Angka perwakilan perkhidmatan awam 0.2% bermakna Orang Asli hampir tidak kelihatan dalam kerajaan yang mendakwa bercakap bagi pihak semua rakyat Malaysia.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Perampasan tanah orang asal telah berterusan sejak kemerdekaan. Di bawah BN, konsesi pembalakan dikeluarkan kepada syarikat yang berhubungan politik tanpa perundingan. Di bawah PH 1.0, moratorium ringkas ke atas konsesi tertentu diumumkan sebelum Langkah Sheraton. Di bawah PH 2.0, corak ini disambung semula. Keputusan mahkamah yang mengiktiraf hak tanah adat — termasuk keputusan penting Mahkamah Persekutuan — secara rutin diabaikan oleh kerajaan negeri. Rangka kerja undang-undang wujud untuk melindungi hak orang asal. Kemauan politik untuk menguatkuasakannya tiada.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Tanah Orang Asli masih diambil. Keputusan mahkamah masih diabaikan. Perkhidmatan awam anda mempunyai 0.2% perwakilan Orang Asli. Amnesty membenderakannya. Kumpulan hak orang asal mendokumentasikannya. Dan kerajaan anda berdiam diri. Kodifikasikan hak tanah adat dalam perundangan persekutuan. Kuatkuasakan keputusan mahkamah sedia ada. Tingkatkan perwakilan orang asal dalam kerajaan. Atau terus bercakap tentang ’tamadun Madani’ sementara penduduk asal Malaysia menyaksikan hutan datuk nenek moyang mereka dijadikan perladangan kelapa sawit — di bawah pengawasan anda.

Orang Asli Land Rights Violations Continue — Indigenous Communities Displaced for Logging and Plantations

Said by: Amnesty International / Indigenous Rights Groups

Category: Social

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Throughout 2024, Orang Asli and indigenous communities in Peninsular Malaysia continued to face systematic violations of customary land rights. Ancestral forests were cleared for logging concessions and palm oil plantations, often without free, prior, and informed consent. Amnesty International flagged indigenous rights as one of seven areas where Malaysia fell short of its human rights obligations. Orang Asli make up just 0.2% of Malaysia’s civil service despite comprising approximately 0.7% of the population — even proportional representation is denied.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Orang Asli are Malaysia’s most marginalised community. Their customary land rights — recognised by courts but systematically violated by state governments — are the foundation of their identity, livelihood, and cultural survival. When ancestral forests are cleared for plantations, Orang Asli don’t just lose land — they lose food sources, water catchments, medicinal plants, and burial grounds. The PH government, which claims to champion human rights and social justice, has done nothing to strengthen legal protections for indigenous land rights or enforce existing court rulings.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH includes politicians who have advocated for indigenous rights in opposition. Baru Bian — a prominent land rights lawyer and PH MP — has fought indigenous land cases for decades. Yet in government, PH has not introduced legislation to codify customary land rights, has not enforced existing court rulings protecting indigenous territories, and has not increased Orang Asli representation in the civil service or decision-making bodies. The 0.2% civil service representation figure means Orang Asli are virtually invisible in the government that claims to speak for all Malaysians.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Indigenous land dispossession has been ongoing since independence. Under BN, logging concessions were issued to politically connected companies with no consultation. Under PH 1.0, a brief moratorium on certain concessions was announced before the Sheraton Move. Under PH 2.0, the pattern has resumed. Court rulings recognising customary land rights — including landmark Federal Court decisions — are routinely ignored by state governments. The legal framework exists to protect indigenous rights. The political will to enforce it does not.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Orang Asli land is still being taken. Court rulings are still being ignored. Your civil service has 0.2% Orang Asli representation. Amnesty flagged it. Indigenous rights groups are documenting it. And your government is silent. Codify customary land rights in federal legislation. Enforce existing court rulings. Increase indigenous representation in government. Or keep talking about “Madani civilisation” while Malaysia’s original inhabitants watch their ancestral forests being turned into palm oil plantations — under your watch.

Jika semua agensi ini terlepas apa yang berlaku di rumah GISBH, itu bukan penyembunyian — itu kegagalan institusi. Dan kegagalan institusi di Selangor adalah tanggungjawab Amirudin.

Wartawan dan aktivis telah membangkitkan kebimbangan mengenai organisasi itu selama bertahun-tahun. Corak pihak berkuasa mengabaikan penderaan yang didokumentasikan dengan baik sehingga ia meletup menjadi skandal awam adalah tradisi tadbir urus Malaysia yang PH sepatutnya menamatkan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Kanak-kanak didera di rumah kebajikan di negeri anda, di bawah tadbir urus anda, oleh organisasi yang telah berada dalam radar selama tiga puluh tahun. Alasan anda ialah mereka ‘menyembunyikan’ diri dengan menjadi perniagaan. Itu bukan penjelasan — itu pengakuan bahawa sistem pemeriksaan kebajikan, pelesenan dan pemantauan anda rosak. Betulkan, atau GISBH seterusnya sudah pun beroperasi di Selangor sementara anda berucap tentang pembangunan.

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Hundreds of children were rescued from GISBH-linked premises across Malaysia, with many found in Selangor.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: GISBH operated openly in Selangor for years. Its premises were known. Its business operations were visible. Its welfare homes housed hundreds of children. Amirudin’s explanation that GISBH ‘concealed’ itself by becoming a business is an admission that his state government was unable to monitor organisations operating openly within its borders.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: You don’t get to govern Selangor for six years, have a massive child abuse ring operating in your state, and then say ’they hid it well.’ State welfare departments are supposed to inspect children’s homes. Local authorities issue business licences. JAIS oversees Islamic organisations. If all these agencies missed what was happening in GISBH homes, that’s not concealment — that’s institutional failure. And institutional failure in Selangor is Amirudin’s responsibility.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Al-Arqam was banned in 1994 — thirty years ago. Its transformation into GISBH was well-documented. Journalists and activists had raised concerns about the organisation for years. The pattern of authorities ignoring well-documented abuses until they explode into public scandal is a Malaysian governance tradition that PH was supposed to end.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Children were abused in welfare homes in your state, under your governance, by an organisation that has been on the radar for thirty years. Your excuse is that they ‘concealed’ themselves by becoming a business. That is not an explanation — it is a confession that your welfare inspection, licensing, and monitoring systems are broken. Fix them, or the next GISBH is already operating in Selangor while you make speeches about development.

Category: Social

Date: 2024-07-28

Menteri Komunikasi Fahmi Fadzil berkata langkah itu diperlukan untuk membanteras jenayah siber termasuk penipuan dalam talian, eksploitasi kanak-kanak, dan deepfake. Apabila kerajaan boleh mengugut untuk membatalkan lesen operasi platform, ia mempunyai kuasa besar ke atas kandungan yang kekal dan yang diturunkan. Di negara yang mempunyai sejarah menggunakan Akta Komunikasi dan Multimedia untuk mendakwa pengkritik, menyerahkan lebih kuasa kawal selia kepada SKMM ke atas media sosial adalah senjata yang berisi.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: PH menunggang media sosial untuk berkuasa. Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, dan WhatsApp adalah platform yang membolehkan PH memintas kawalan BN terhadap media arus perdana. Tanpa media sosial, skandal 1MDB mungkin tidak pernah sampai kepada orang ramai Malaysia pada skala sedemikian. Kini berkuasa, PH melesenkan platform yang sama, mengugut tindakan undang-undang terhadap yang tidak mematuhi, dan menyekat ciri AI. Penyampaian “keselamatan kanak-kanak” itu bijak tetapi telus — rangka kerja pelesenan terpakai kepada semua kandungan, bukan hanya keselamatan kanak-kanak. PH memansuhkan Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar dan memanggil diri mereka pembela kebebasan internet. Kemudian mereka memperkenalkan rejim pelesenan yang memberi SKMM lebih kuasa daripada Akta yang mereka mansuhkan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Di bawah BN, Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar 2018 adalah bukti A PH untuk kawalan internet autoritarian. PH memansuhkannya pada 2018. Kini PH menggantikannya dengan sesuatu yang boleh dikatakan lebih berkuasa: rejim pelesenan yang memberi SKMM daya tarikan kawal selia berterusan ke atas platform, dan bukannya hanya pendakwaan selepas fakta. PH juga menentang penggunaan Seksyen 233 CMA oleh SKMM untuk mendakwa pengkritik di bawah BN. Di bawah PH, seksyen yang sama terus digunakan, dan platform sendiri kini boleh dipertanggungjawabkan melalui rangka kerja pelesenan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda memansuhkan Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar dan memanggil diri anda pembela kebebasan internet. Kemudian anda memperkenalkan rejim pelesenan yang memberi SKMM lebih kuasa ke atas media sosial daripada BN pernah ada. Anda melesenkan internet sambil mendakwa melindunginya. Hujah keselamatan kanak-kanak itu sah — tetapi rangka kerja pelesenan jauh melampaui keselamatan kanak-kanak. Jujurlah tentang apa yang anda bina: alat kawal selia yang boleh menekan platform agar mematuhi permintaan kandungan kerajaan. Itu bukan reformasi. Itu kawalan internet BN dengan label PH.

Fahmi Fadzil Forces Social Media Licensing on Platforms — Critics Call It PH's Version of Internet Censorship

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2024-07-28

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 28 July 2024, the PH government announced that social media platforms with more than 8 million users in Malaysia would be required to apply for a regulatory licence from MCMC, effective 1 August. Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil said the measure was needed to combat cyber offences including online scams, child exploitation, and deepfakes. By late 2025, Fahmi was threatening legal action against non-compliant platforms. In January 2026, MCMC temporarily blocked X’s Grok AI feature over deepfake concerns. ARTICLE 19 called on Malaysia to “halt hasty imposition of mandatory electronic verification,” warning about implications for privacy and free speech.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Social media licensing gives the government a lever to pressure platforms into compliance with content removal requests. While the stated goals — combating scams, protecting children — are legitimate, the mechanism creates obvious censorship risks. When a government can threaten to revoke a platform’s operating licence, it has enormous power over what content stays up and what comes down. In a country with a history of using the Communications and Multimedia Act to prosecute critics, handing MCMC more regulatory power over social media is a loaded gun.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH rode social media to power. Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, and WhatsApp were the platforms that allowed PH to circumvent BN’s control of mainstream media. Without social media, the 1MDB scandal might never have reached the Malaysian public at the scale it did. Now in power, PH is licensing the same platforms, threatening legal action against those that don’t comply, and blocking AI features. The “child safety” framing is clever but transparent — the licensing framework applies to all content, not just child safety. PH repealed the Anti-Fake News Act and called themselves defenders of internet freedom. Then they introduced a licensing regime that gives MCMC even more power than the Act they repealed.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, the Anti-Fake News Act 2018 was PH’s exhibit A for authoritarian internet control. PH repealed it in 2018. Now PH has replaced it with something arguably more powerful: a licensing regime that gives MCMC ongoing regulatory leverage over platforms, rather than just post-hoc prosecution. PH also opposed MCMC’s use of Section 233 of the CMA to prosecute critics under BN. Under PH, the same section continues to be used, and platforms themselves can now be held liable through the licensing framework.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You repealed the Anti-Fake News Act and called yourselves defenders of internet freedom. Then you introduced a licensing regime that gives MCMC even more power over social media than BN ever had. You’re licensing the internet while claiming to protect it. The child safety argument is legitimate — but the licensing framework goes far beyond child safety. Be honest about what you’re building: a regulatory tool that can pressure platforms into compliance with government content demands. That’s not reform. That’s BN’s internet controls with a PH label.
