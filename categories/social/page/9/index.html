Chegubard Charged with SEDITION for Reporting Forest City Casino Claims — Reformasi's Free Speech Promise Dies

Said by: Johor Sessions Court / Badrul Hisham (Chegubard)

Category: Social

Date: 2024-04-30

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 30 April 2024, activist Badrul Hisham Shaharin (Chegubard) was charged under Malaysia’s colonial-era Sedition Act for a social media post about casino plans at Forest City in Johor. The Sedition Act is the same law PH promised to repeal during its 2018 and 2022 election campaigns. As of March 2026, Chegubard’s appeal is still pending at the Johor Bahru Sessions Court. Instead of repealing the Sedition Act, the Anwar government is now weaponising it against critics — the exact same playbook used by BN against Anwar himself in the 1990s.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Every repressive law PH promised to abolish — the Sedition Act, the Printing Presses and Publications Act, SOSMA, Section 233 of the Communications and Multimedia Act — is still on the books and being actively used by PH. Press freedom organisations condemned the prosecution as a chilling signal to journalists and activists: in Anwar’s Malaysia, questioning powerful interests — especially those connected to the Forest City special economic zone — can land you in court. The message is clear: free speech ends where powerful business interests begin.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar Ibrahim was himself charged under the Sedition Act. He knows what it feels like to face prosecution for speech. He built an entire political movement — Reformasi — on the injustice of being silenced by the state. Now his government is using the same law against a man who reported on casino plans at a development the government is actively promoting. The Sedition Act wasn’t just left unrepealed — it was expanded, with over 90 sedition-related arrests under PH. The man who was once a victim of these laws is now wielding them.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, the Sedition Act was used against opposition politicians, academics, lawyers, and journalists. Anwar, Karpal Singh, Azmi Sharom, and dozens of others were charged or investigated. PH condemned every single prosecution as authoritarian overreach. Under PH 2.0, the same law has been used against Chegubard, social media commentators, and critics of government policy. The only difference is the name on the charge sheet — the law, the process, and the chilling effect are identical.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You were jailed under these laws. You know what they feel like. You promised to repeal them. And now you’re using them against the same kind of activists who marched for your freedom. Repeal the Sedition Act — as you promised in 2018 and 2022. Drop the charges against Chegubard. Or admit that Reformasi was never about free speech — it was about freeing one man, not the system.

[BM] Polis Ugut Peserta Perhimpunan Bersih — Kerajaan PH Guna Taktik Sama Yang Pernah Dikecam

Category: Social

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Perhimpunan Bersih adalah oksigen kebangkitan PH kepada kuasa. Perhimpunan Bersih 2.0 hingga 5.0 membina gerakan massa yang akhirnya menjatuhkan BN. Pemimpin PH bukan sahaja menyokong perhimpunan ini — mereka berpawai di dalamnya, ditembak gas pemedih mata, dan menggunakannya untuk membina kelayakan demokratik mereka. Lawatan rumah, panggilan telefon, dan pemantauan media sosial digunakan untuk menghalang kehadiran di perhimpunan Bersih. PH mengecam taktik ini sebagai autoritarian. Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International, dan setiap pertubuhan kebebasan sivil Malaysia mendokumentasikan penindasan BN terhadap hak berhimpun. Kini pertubuhan yang sama mendokumentasikan taktik yang sama di bawah PH — dan kumpulan masyarakat sivil yang sama yang pernah berpawai dengan PH menjadi sasaran PH.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda berpawai dengan Bersih. Anda ditembak gas pemedih mata dengan Bersih. Anda membina gerakan anda atas hak berhimpun secara aman. Kini anda menghantar polis ke rumah orang untuk mengamaran mereka daripada menghadiri perhimpunan Bersih. Lawyers for Liberty memanggilnya pengkhianatan. CSIS kata masyarakat sivil terbelah. Rakyat yang berpawai untuk anda kini diamaran daripada berpawai untuk akauntabiliti. Jaminkan hak berhimpun secara aman tanpa ugutan polis. Atau akui bahawa kebebasan berhimpun hanya berguna apabila anda yang berhimpun.

Date: 2024-02-26

CSIS noted that civil society groups are “torn between their historical alliance with PH and the government’s failure to implement its reform agenda.” Bersih — the electoral reform coalition that was PH’s closest ally during the BN era — was now being treated as a threat.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: LFL’s condemnation specifically cited a 2014 Court of Appeal ruling — from PKR’s own Nik Nazmi’s case — that struck down restrictive provisions of the Peaceful Assembly Act. PH’s own legal victory against protest suppression is now being ignored by PH’s own government. Nik Nazmi, who was literally charged under the PAA for organising a protest in 2013, was a PH minister when police threatened Bersih participants. The hypocrisy is not subtle — it is the defining feature of PH’s approach to civil liberties: demand them in opposition, suppress them in government.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, police intimidation of rally participants was standard practice. Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International, and every Malaysian civil liberties organisation documented BN’s suppression of assembly rights. Now the same organisations are documenting the same tactics under PH — and the same civil society groups that once marched with PH are being targeted by PH.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You marched with Bersih. You were tear-gassed with Bersih. You built your movement on the right to peaceful assembly. Now you’re sending police to people’s homes to warn them against attending a Bersih rally. Lawyers for Liberty calls it a betrayal. CSIS says civil society is torn. The rakyat who marched for you are now being warned against marching for accountability. Guarantee the right to peaceful assembly without police intimidation. Or admit that freedom of assembly was only useful when you were the ones assembling.

Date: 2024-01-11

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada Januari 2024, Human Rights Watch mengeluarkan Laporan Dunia 2024, memberikan keputusan yang dahsyat terhadap tahun pertama Anwar Ibrahim berkuasa: “Agenda Reformasi Terbantut.” HRW menyatakan bahawa Anwar telah “sebahagian besarnya gagal menunaikan janjinya untuk menangani penindasan dan rasuah” dan menyeru kerajaan “mengembalikan komitmen kepada agenda yang menghormati hak dengan menukarkan janji kempen yang diketepikan menjadi tindakan.” Laporan itu mendokumentasikan kegagalan merentas kebebasan bersuara, perhimpunan, kebebasan media, dan reformasi institusi. Tahun 3: kawalan internet yang diperluaskan, 90+ tangkapan hasutan, dan tangkapan wartawan. Akta Hasutan bukan sekadar dibiarkan dalam buku — ia diperluaskan. Kebebasan media bukan sekadar diabaikan — wartawan ditangkap. 📜 KES TERDAHULU: HRW telah mengkritik setiap kerajaan Malaysia. Di bawah Najib, penilaian tertumpu pada 1MDB, undang-undang menindas, dan tindakan keras terhadap masyarakat sivil. Di bawah Muhyiddin dan Ismail Sabri, tumpuan adalah sekatan era pandemik dan kemunduran demokrasi. Tetapi PH sepatutnya berbeza — ia adalah kerajaan yang cadangan HRW sejajar dengannya. Apabila pertubuhan hak asasi manusia yang sepatutnya menjadi sekutu semula jadi anda mengatakan anda telah terbantut dan mundur, masalahnya bukan pengkritik. Ia kerajaan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Human Rights Watch memberikan anda setahun. Kemudian setahun lagi. Keputusan menjadi lebih teruk, bukan lebih baik. “Terbantut” menjadi “kemunduran.” Jika trajektori berterusan, Tahun 4 akan menjadi “kemerosotan.” Laksanakan reformasi yang dikenal pasti HRW: mansuhkan Akta Hasutan, kukuhkan perlindungan kebebasan media, jamin hak berhimpun, dan pulihkan kebebasan SPRM. Atau terus menolak kritikan antarabangsa sebagai “tidak memahami konteks” — sementara konteksnya ialah anda melakukan tepat apa yang BN lakukan, dengan bahasa Inggeris yang lebih baik.

HRW 2024: 'Reform Agenda Stalled' — Government Failed to Uphold Pledges in First Year

Said by: Human Rights Watch

Category: Social

Date: 2024-01-11

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2024, Human Rights Watch released its 2024 World Report, delivering a damning verdict on Anwar Ibrahim’s first year in office: “Reform Agenda Stalled.” HRW stated that Anwar had “largely failed to uphold his pledges to address repression and corruption” and called on the government to “recommit to a rights-respecting agenda by turning its sidelined campaign promises into action.” The report documented failures across freedom of expression, assembly, media freedom, and institutional reform. A year later, HRW’s 2025 assessment was even harsher: “Rights Backslide.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Two consecutive years of HRW condemnation is not a bump in the road — it is a trajectory. Year 1: stalled. Year 2: backslide. Year 3: expanded internet controls, 90+ sedition arrests, and journalist arrests. When the world’s most prominent human rights organisation says your reform agenda has stalled — and then says it’s actively going backwards — the problem is not perception. It is policy. PH was elected on a reform mandate. The international human rights community is documenting, in real time, the abandonment of that mandate.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s response to HRW’s assessment was predictable: “They don’t understand our context.” “Coalition constraints.” “We need time.” But HRW gave PH a year before issuing the “stalled” verdict. Then another year before escalating to “backslide.” The organisation is not being impatient — it is documenting a government that promised reform and delivered regression. The Sedition Act was not just left on the books — it was expanded. Media freedom was not just neglected — journalists were arrested. Assembly rights were not just unprotected — Bersih rallies were suppressed. This is not stalling. This is reversal.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: HRW has criticised every Malaysian government. Under Najib, the assessments focused on 1MDB, repressive laws, and crackdowns on civil society. Under Muhyiddin and Ismail Sabri, the focus was pandemic-era restrictions and democratic backsliding. But PH was supposed to be different — it was the government that HRW’s recommendations aligned with. When the human rights organisation that should be your natural ally says you’ve stalled and backslid, the problem is not the critic. It’s the government.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Human Rights Watch gave you a year. Then another year. The verdict got worse, not better. “Stalled” became “backslide.” If the trajectory continues, Year 4 will be “regression.” Implement the reforms HRW identified: repeal the Sedition Act, strengthen media freedom protections, guarantee assembly rights, and restore MACC independence. Or keep dismissing international criticism as “not understanding context” — while the context is that you’re doing exactly what BN did, with better English.

[BM] Pangkalan Data PADU Dilancar Sebagai Mimpi Ngeri Privasi — Sesiapa Dengan Nombor IC Anda Boleh Akses Data Anda

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2024-01-02

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 2 Januari 2024, Anwar melancarkan Hab Pangkalan Data Pusat (PADU) — sistem yang bertujuan merevolusikan penyampaian subsidi dengan menyasarkan bantuan kepada mereka yang memerlukan. Lebih 233,000 pengguna mendaftar pada hari pelancaran. Dalam 48 jam, penyelidik keselamatan mendapati pengesahan itu sangat lemah: sesiapa yang mempunyai nombor IC dan poskod rumah orang lain boleh mendaftar dan mengakses data mereka. CybersecurityAsia memanggilnya “mimpi ngeri privasi.” Sarawak menghentikan penyertaannya sepenuhnya pada Mac 2024, memetik kebimbangan mengenai autonomi negeri dan data peribadi rakyat. Menjelang pertengahan 2025, PADU senyap-senyap diketepikan — reformasi subsidi RON95 langsung tidak menggunakannya, menggunakan data pendaftaran kenderaan JPJ sebaliknya.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Kerajaan melancarkan pangkalan data kebangsaan dengan pengesahan yang sangat lemah sehingga ia pada dasarnya akses terbuka. Nombor IC bukan rahsia — ia muncul pada setiap borang kerajaan dan kerap bocor dalam pelanggaran data. Menggunakannya sebagai faktor pengesahan tunggal untuk pangkalan data yang mengandungi data pendapatan, isi rumah, dan pekerjaan bukan kecacatan reka bentuk — ia adalah kegagalan sistemik tadbir urus digital. ISEAS menyatakan PADU “mencetuskan ribut perdebatan mengenai isu privasi, federalisme, dan sifat kontrak sosial antara rakyat dan negara.” Apabila Sarawak enggan menyertai dan membina sistemnya sendiri, mesej tersirat itu dahsyat: kami tidak mempercayai anda dengan data rakyat kami.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: PADU sepatutnya menjadi tulang belakang keseluruhan agenda reformasi subsidi Malaysia. Kerajaan menghabiskan bertahun berhujah bahawa subsidi sejagat perlu digantikan dengan yang bersasar, dan PADU adalah mekanismenya. Apabila ujian terbesar tiba — reformasi RON95 — PADU langsung tidak digunakan. Berbilion dibelanjakan membina sistem yang ditinggalkan tanpa pengakuan atau akauntabiliti awam. Tiada bedah siasat diterbitkan. Tiada akauntabiliti diberikan. Tiada perakaunan kos disediakan. Inilah yang berlaku apabila dasar didorong oleh pengumuman bukannya pelaksanaan — PADU menghasilkan tajuk utama yang hebat pada hari pelancaran, dan hakikat bahawa ia tidak selamat, tidak lengkap, dan akhirnya tidak boleh digunakan dilayan sebagai masalah yang semua orang lupakan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Rekod IT kerajaan Malaysia adalah kubur kegagalan yang mahal. Sistem MyKad telah dibelenggu isu data selama bertahun. Aplikasi COVID MySejahtera dibelitkan kontroversi pemilikan dan privasi data. Laman web kerajaan kerap digodam atau dirosakkan. Setiap projek mengikut corak yang sama: lancar dengan gembar-gembur, temui kecacatan, tampung secara reaktif, dan akhirnya tinggalkan. PADU adalah iterasi paling mahal kitaran ini — pangkalan data kebangsaan yang dibina tanpa keselamatan yang mencukupi, dilancarkan tanpa ujian yang mencukupi, dan diketepikan tanpa penjelasan yang mencukupi.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda membelanjakan ratusan juta membina pangkalan data kebangsaan. Ia dilancarkan dengan keselamatan yang sangat lemah sehingga sesiapa boleh mengakses data sesiapa. Sarawak enggan menyertai. Dan apabila reformasi subsidi terbesar anda tiba, anda langsung tidak menggunakannya. Terbitkan jumlah kos PADU. Jelaskan mengapa ia diketepikan. Jalankan bedah siasat awam mengenai apa yang salah. Atau terus melancarkan inisiatif digital yang menjadi tajuk utama yang berkos berbilion dan tidak menyampaikan apa-apa — sementara data peribadi rakyat duduk dalam pangkalan data yang tidak mampu mengesahkan siapa yang mengaksesnya.

PADU Database Launches as Privacy Nightmare — Anyone With Your IC Number Can Access Your Data

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2024-01-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 2 January 2024, Anwar launched the Central Database Hub (PADU) — a system meant to revolutionise subsidy delivery by targeting aid to those who need it. Over 233,000 users registered on launch day. Within 48 hours, security researchers discovered the authentication was laughably weak: anyone armed with another person’s IC number and home postcode could register and access their data. CybersecurityAsia called it a “privacy nightmare.” Sarawak halted its participation entirely in March 2024, citing concerns over state autonomy and citizens’ personal data. By mid-2025, PADU was quietly sidelined — the RON95 subsidy reform bypassed it entirely, using JPJ vehicle registration data instead.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The government launched a national database with authentication so weak it was effectively open access. IC numbers are not secrets — they appear on every government form and are routinely leaked in data breaches. Using them as the sole authentication factor for a database containing income, household, and employment data is not a design flaw — it is a systemic failure of digital governance. ISEAS noted that PADU “ignited a firestorm of debate over issues of privacy, federalism, and the very nature of the social contract between citizens and the state.” When Sarawak refused to participate and built its own system, the implied message was devastating: we don’t trust you with our citizens’ data.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PADU was meant to be the backbone of Malaysia’s entire subsidy reform agenda. The government spent years arguing that blanket subsidies needed to be replaced with targeted ones, and PADU was the mechanism. When the biggest test came — RON95 reform — PADU was bypassed entirely. Billions were spent building a system that was abandoned without public acknowledgment or accountability. No postmortem was published. No accountability was assigned. No cost accounting was provided. This is what happens when policy is announcement-driven rather than implementation-driven — PADU made a great headline on launch day, and the fact that it was insecure, incomplete, and ultimately unusable was treated as everyone’s forgotten problem.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s government IT track record is a graveyard of expensive failures. The MyKad system has been plagued by data issues for years. The MySejahtera COVID app was mired in ownership and data privacy controversies. Government websites are routinely hacked or defaced. Each project follows the same pattern: launch with fanfare, discover flaws, patch reactively, and eventually abandon. PADU is the most expensive iteration of this cycle — a national database built without adequate security, launched without adequate testing, and shelved without adequate explanation.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You spent hundreds of millions building a national database. It launched with security so weak anyone could access anyone’s data. Sarawak refused to participate. And when your biggest subsidy reform came, you didn’t even use it. Publish the total cost of PADU. Explain why it was sidelined. Conduct a public postmortem on what went wrong. Or keep launching headline-grabbing digital initiatives that cost billions and deliver nothing — while the rakyat’s personal data sits in a database that couldn’t even verify who was accessing it.

[BM] Hukuman Mati Masih Kekal Untuk 27 Kesalahan — 'Pemansuhan' Hanya Separuh Jalan

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration

Category: Social

Date: 2023-07-04

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 4 Julai 2023, PH menggembar-gemburkan “pemansuhan” hukuman mati mandatori — menggantikannya dengan hukuman budi bicara untuk kesalahan berat. Walau bagaimanapun, hukuman mati itu sendiri masih kekal untuk 27 kesalahan, termasuk pengedaran dadah, pembunuhan, dan kesalahan senjata api. Hakim kini mempunyai budi bicara untuk menjatuhkan hukuman mati atau hukuman alternatif, tetapi tiang gantung masih tersedia. Amnesty International menyeru pemansuhan penuh. Pada akhir 2025, Malaysia mengumumkan “kajian” mengenai pemansuhan penuh bermula Januari 2026 — tiga tahun selepas reformasi awal.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: PH membingkaikan reformasi hukuman mati mandatori sebagai pencapaian hak asasi manusia yang bersejarah. Pada hakikatnya, ia adalah langkah separuh. Menghapuskan perkataan “mandatori” tidak memansuhkan hukuman mati — ia memindahkan keputusan daripada statut kepada budi bicara kehakiman. Hakim dalam sistem mahkamah Malaysia, yang PH sendiri telah mengkritik kerana campur tangan politik, kini menentukan siapa yang hidup dan siapa yang mati. Bagi kerajaan yang mendakwa memperjuangkan hak asasi manusia, mengekalkan hukuman mati untuk 27 kesalahan sambil mengumumkan “kajian” mengenai pemansuhan — tiga tahun selepas reformasi — bukan kemajuan. Ia penangguhan yang dijenamakan sebagai prinsip.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: “Kajian” yang diumumkan untuk Januari 2026 adalah taktik penangguhan lalai kerajaan Malaysia: umumkan kajian, bentuk jawatankuasa, tunggu kitaran berita bertukar, dan tidak pernah melaksanakan penemuan. Jika PH percaya pada pemansuhan, ia akan memansuhkan hukuman mati sepenuhnya pada 2023 ketika ia mempunyai bilangan parlimen dan momentum awam. Sebaliknya, ia memilih pilihan yang selamat secara politik — buang “mandatori,” kekalkan hukuman, dan tuntut kredit untuk reformasi. Amnesty International nampak terus. Rakyat juga sepatutnya.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia telah menghukum mati banduan di bawah setiap pentadbiran. Di bawah BN, hukuman mati digunakan terutamanya untuk pengedaran dadah — secara tidak seimbang menjejaskan defendan miskin, terpinggir, dan warga asing. PH 1.0 memulakan perbincangan mengenai pemansuhan tetapi runtuh sebelum bertindak. PH 2.0 mempunyai tiga tahun untuk menyelesaikan kerja. Sebaliknya, ia bersetuju dengan hukuman budi bicara dan “kajian” masa depan. Corak ini konsisten: kerajaan Malaysia mengakui hukuman mati bermasalah, membuat perubahan bertahap, dan tidak pernah menyelesaikan tugas.

Death Penalty Still on Books for 27 Offences — 'Abolition' Was Only Half-Done

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration

Category: Social

Date: 2023-07-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 4 July 2023, PH trumpeted the “abolition” of the mandatory death penalty — replacing it with discretionary sentencing for capital offences. However, the death penalty itself remains on the books for 27 offences, including drug trafficking, murder, and firearms offences. Judges now have discretion to impose death or alternative sentences, but the gallows remain available. Amnesty International called for full abolition. In late 2025, Malaysia announced a “study” on full abolition to begin in January 2026 — three years after the initial reform.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: PH framed the mandatory death penalty reform as a landmark human rights achievement. In reality, it was a half-measure. Removing the word “mandatory” does not abolish the death penalty — it transfers the decision from statute to judicial discretion. Judges in Malaysia’s court system, which PH itself has criticised for political interference, now decide who lives and who dies. For a government that claims to champion human rights, maintaining the death penalty for 27 offences while announcing a “study” on abolition — three years after reform — is not progress. It’s procrastination branded as principle.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The “study” announced for January 2026 is the Malaysian government’s default delaying tactic: announce a study, form a committee, wait for the news cycle to move on, and never implement the findings. If PH believed in abolition, it would have abolished the death penalty entirely in 2023 when it had the parliamentary numbers and the public momentum. Instead, it chose the politically safe option — remove “mandatory,” keep the penalty, and claim credit for reform. Amnesty International saw through it immediately. The rakyat should too.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has executed prisoners under every administration. Under BN, the death penalty was used primarily for drug trafficking — disproportionately affecting poor, marginalised, and foreign defendants. PH 1.0 initiated discussions on abolition but collapsed before acting. PH 2.0 had three years to complete the work. Instead, it settled for discretionary sentencing and a future “study.” The pattern is consistent: Malaysian governments acknowledge the death penalty is problematic, make incremental changes, and never finish the job.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You called it abolition. It wasn’t. The death penalty remains for 27 offences. You announced a “study” on full abolition — three years after your initial reform. If you need three years to study whether the state should kill its citizens, the answer is already no. Abolish the death penalty for all offences. Commute existing death sentences. Stop studying and start acting. Or admit that “abolition” was a press release, not a policy.
