[BM] Malaysia Enggan Ratifikasi ICERD — Anwar Tunduk Kepada Politik Perkauman Soal Perjanjian Hak Asasi Antarabangsa

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Malay-Muslim Groups

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Malaysia kekal antara sedikit negara di dunia yang belum meratifikasi Konvensyen Antarabangsa Mengenai Penghapusan Segala Bentuk Diskriminasi Kaum (ICERD). Pada 2018, percubaan PH meratifikasi ICERD ditinggalkan selepas bantahan besar-besaran yang dipimpin kumpulan Melayu-Muslim yang berhujah ia akan menjejaskan keistimewaan Bumiputera. Anwar, pernah pemimpin progresif yang komited kepada kesamarataan, tidak menyentuh semula isu ini sejak menjadi PM.

🔥 KENAPA IA PENTING: Ratifikasi ICERD akan mengikat Malaysia untuk menghapuskan diskriminasi kaum dalam undang-undang dan amalan. Keengganan Malaysia meratifikasi — bersama negara seperti Korea Utara dan Myanmar — meletakkannya dalam kumpulan yang memalukan. Keengganan itu mengisyaratkan kepada masyarakat antarabangsa bahawa Malaysia tidak sanggup mendedahkan dasar berasaskan kaum kepada penelitian antarabangsa.

💩 KENAPA IA KARUT: Anwar menghabiskan berdekad memperjuangkan “Malaysian Malaysia” — kesamarataan tanpa mengira kaum. Beliau menubuhkan gabungan pelbagai kaum PH atas prinsip semua rakyat Malaysia layak dilayan sama rata. Ratifikasi ICERD adalah ungkapan semula jadi prinsip itu. Sebaliknya, Anwar menerima kekalahan 2018 dan teruskan. Reformis telah menyerah kepada kuasa yang dibinanya untuk dilawan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia menandatangani ICERD pada 1966 tetapi tidak pernah meratifikasinya. Setiap kerajaan memutuskan bahawa kos politik mencabar diskriminasi kaum lebih tinggi daripada kos mengekalkannya.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Kamu membina gabungan atas kesamarataan pelbagai kaum. Manifesto kamu menjanjikan ‘Malaysia Baru’ di mana kaum tidak menentukan hak. Meratifikasi ICERD adalah ungkapan paling ringkas dan langsung janji itu. Sebaliknya, kamu menerima kekalahan 2018 dan teruskan — sama seperti setiap kerajaan sebelum kamu. Sama ada ICERD konsisten dengan Malaysia yang kamu janjikan, atau Malaysia yang kamu janjikan adalah pembohongan. Yang mana satu?

Said by: Lawyers for Liberty / Bar Council / Amnesty International

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Akta Kesalahan Keselamatan (Langkah-Langkah Khas) 2012 (SOSMA), yang membenarkan penahanan tanpa bicara sehingga 28 hari dan menyekat pengawasan kehakiman kes keselamatan, kekal beroperasi sepenuhnya di bawah kerajaan Anwar. PH secara jelas berjanji untuk memansuhkan SOSMA dalam kedua-dua manifesto 2018 dan 2022, memanggilnya ‘drakonian’ dan tidak serasi dengan tadbir urus demokratik.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Penahanan tanpa bicara secara asasnya tidak serasi dengan kedaulatan undang-undang. SOSMA memberikan kerajaan kuasa untuk menahan individu selama hampir sebulan tanpa tuduhan dan tanpa penelitian kehakiman penuh. Di tangan yang salah, ia alat untuk penganiayaan politik. Setiap hari ia kekal dalam undang-undang ialah hari mana-mana rakyat Malaysia boleh ditahan tanpa perlindungan yang dijanjikan demokrasi.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

PH memanggil SOSMA ‘drakonian’ apabila BN menggunakannya. Ahli politik PH memberi ucapan mengecamnya. Lawyers for Liberty mendokumentasikan penyalahgunaannya. Majlis Peguam menyeru pemansuhannya. Amnesty International membenderakannya setiap tahun. Kini PH berkuasa dengan majoriti besar, dan SOSMA kekal. Tidak dipinda. Tidak direformasi. Tidak dimansuhkan. Tidak berubah dan tersedia untuk digunakan.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Di bawah BN, SOSMA digunakan terhadap aktivis politik. Di bawah PH 1.0, tiada pemansuhan dalam 22 bulan. Di bawah PH 2.0, lebih tiga tahun, tiada pemansuhan. Akta Hasutan: cerita sama. PPPA: cerita sama. SETIAP undang-undang represif yang PH janjikan untuk dimansuhkan: cerita sama. Corak ini bukan kebetulan.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu mempunyai jumlah. Kamu mempunyai mandat. Kamu menghabiskan BERTAHUN-TAHUN memanggil SOSMA drakonian. Mansuhkannya. Kamu tidak akan — kerana mempunyai kuasa untuk menahan tanpa bicara adalah mudah, walaupun untuk ‘reformis.’ Keenggananmu membuktikan penentanganmu sentiasa tentang siapa yang memegang kuasa, bukan tentang prinsip.

Gambling Addiction Crisis — 4D, Toto, and Online Betting Destroy Families While Government Collects Revenue

Said by: Health Ministry / NGOs

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia faces a growing gambling addiction crisis driven by legal 4D and Toto outlets, sports betting, and an explosion of illegal online gambling platforms. The government profits from gambling through licensing fees and gaming taxes — collecting billions annually from licensed operators. Meanwhile, gambling addiction destroys lower-income families, with treatment services remaining minimal compared to the scale of the problem. Online gambling has exploded beyond MCMC’s enforcement capacity, with estimates suggesting billions of ringgit flow through illegal platforms annually.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Gambling addiction disproportionately affects B40 families who can least afford losses. The social costs — family breakdown, debt, crime, mental health deterioration — are borne by communities while the government collects revenue. Licensed gambling operators contribute to political campaigns and enjoy regulatory protection. The government has a financial incentive to maintain gambling revenue and a social responsibility to address addiction. It consistently chooses revenue over responsibility.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government simultaneously profits from gambling and claims to combat it. Licensing fees and gaming taxes generate billions for the Treasury. Online gambling enforcement is performative — MCMC blocks a few sites while thousands more operate freely through VPNs and messaging apps. Treatment services for gambling addiction are chronically underfunded. The conflict of interest is structural: the government is both regulator and beneficiary of the industry it’s supposed to control. That’s not governance — it’s a revenue addiction masquerading as regulation.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Gambling regulation in Malaysia has always been politically convenient. Under BN, licensed operators flourished with political connections. Under PH 1.0, no changes were made. Under PH 2.0, the same operators continue with the same political connections, and online gambling has grown unchecked. Each government collects the revenue, underfunds treatment, and claims to be fighting the problem. The Forest City SFZ’s potential for gaming activities has raised further concerns about the government’s relationship with gambling revenue.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You collect billions from gambling while addiction destroys families. Your enforcement against online gambling is performative. Your treatment services are underfunded. The conflict of interest is obvious: you profit from the industry you’re supposed to regulate. Either significantly increase gambling addiction treatment funding — proportional to the revenue you collect — or admit that gambling regulation is a revenue operation, not a public health programme.

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 What Happened

Despite Anwar’s government having prominent women in cabinet and occasional rhetoric about gender equality, Malaysia’s gender pay gap remains stubbornly persistent at an estimated 6-9% across sectors, with significantly larger gaps in certain industries. Women bear a disproportionate burden of unpaid care work, face career penalties for motherhood, and are underrepresented in senior management and board positions.

🎯 PH Callout

Half of Malaysia’s population deserves more than tokenistic representation and empty rhetoric. Put women in cabinet for the photos, then ignore the structural inequality that holds women back economically. Pay transparency costs nothing to legislate. Gender budgeting is best practice globally. If you won’t do the easy things, you certainly won’t do the hard ones.

Malaysia Refuses to Ratify ICERD — Anwar Caves to Racial Politics on International Human Rights Treaty

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Malay-Muslim Groups

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia remains one of the few countries in the world that has not ratified the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (ICERD). In 2018, PH’s attempt to ratify ICERD was abandoned after mass protests led by Malay-Muslim groups who argued it would undermine Bumiputera privileges. Anwar, once a progressive leader committed to equality, has not revisited the issue since becoming PM. The “reformist” Prime Minister has accepted the status quo on racial discrimination — ICERD ratification is not on the agenda.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: ICERD ratification would commit Malaysia to eliminating racial discrimination in law and practice. Malaysia’s refusal to ratify — alongside countries like North Korea and Myanmar — places it in embarrassing company. The refusal signals to the international community that Malaysia is unwilling to subject its race-based policies to international scrutiny. For non-Bumiputera Malaysians, the refusal to ratify confirms that their rights are subordinate to racial politics.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar spent decades advocating for a “Malaysian Malaysia” — equality regardless of race. He founded the multi-racial PH coalition on the principle that all Malaysians deserve equal treatment. ICERD ratification would be the natural expression of that principle. Instead, Anwar has accepted the 2018 defeat and moved on. The man who was imprisoned partly for challenging racial politics has, as PM, decided that racial politics are too powerful to challenge. The reformist has capitulated to the very forces he built his career opposing.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia signed ICERD in 1966 but never ratified it. Under Mahathir 1.0, ratification was never considered. Under Najib, it was ignored. Under PH 1.0, ratification was announced and then abandoned after protests. Under PH 2.0, it hasn’t even been raised. Each government decides that the political cost of challenging racial discrimination is higher than the cost of maintaining it. ICERD joins the Sedition Act, the PPPA, and SOSMA on the list of reforms PH promised and abandoned.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You built a coalition on multiracial equality. Your manifesto promised a “New Malaysia” where race wouldn’t determine rights. Ratifying ICERD would be the simplest, most direct expression of that promise. Instead, you’ve accepted the 2018 defeat and moved on — just like every government before you. Either ICERD is consistent with the Malaysia you promised, or the Malaysia you promised was a lie. Which is it?

Road Deaths at Catastrophic Levels — Malaysia Among Deadliest Roads in ASEAN, Government's Response: 'Speed Cameras'

Said by: MIROS / WHO Road Safety Data

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia records over 6,000 road deaths annually — approximately 16 people every day. Motorcyclists make up the largest share of fatalities. WHO data ranks Malaysia among the deadliest roads in ASEAN with a road traffic death rate significantly above the global average. The government’s primary response has been speed cameras, periodic enforcement campaigns, and awareness programmes. No structural reform of road safety infrastructure, licensing standards, or vehicle safety requirements has been implemented under PH.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: 6,000 deaths per year is a public health crisis that receives a fraction of the attention given to lesser issues. For context, Malaysia loses more people to road accidents annually than to most diseases. The economic cost — medical expenses, lost productivity, insurance claims — runs into billions. Motorcyclists, who are disproportionately B40 and young, bear the highest risk. The death toll has remained at catastrophic levels for over a decade with no meaningful decline, suggesting that current interventions are not working.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Speed cameras and enforcement campaigns are band-aid solutions. The structural causes of Malaysia’s road death crisis are well-documented: poor road design (especially rural roads without barriers), lax licensing standards (motorcycle licences are too easy to obtain), inadequate vehicle safety requirements (no mandatory ABS on motorcycles, no crash testing regime), and a culture of enforcement-then-amnesty that undermines deterrence. The government knows what works — segregated motorcycle lanes, mandatory safety equipment, stricter licensing — but implementing these requires investment and political will. Speed cameras are cheap. Structural reform is not.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Road safety has been a declared government priority under every administration since the 1990s. Under Mahathir 1.0, the Road Safety Department was established. Under Najib, the Road Safety Plan was launched. Under PH, the same plans continue with the same results: 6,000+ deaths per year. The UN Decade of Action for Road Safety (2011-2020) set targets Malaysia missed. The new decade (2021-2030) is heading the same direction. Each government announces road safety campaigns. None reduces the death toll.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Sixteen Malaysians die on the roads every day. Your response is speed cameras. The structural interventions that work — segregated motorcycle lanes, mandatory ABS, stricter licensing, road design reform — require investment and political courage you haven’t shown. Allocate a dedicated road safety infrastructure budget. Mandate motorcycle ABS. Reform the licensing system. Or keep installing speed cameras while 6,000 families lose someone every year — and pretend the problem is speed, not infrastructure.

Sedition Act STILL Not Repealed — PH Promised to Abolish It in 2018 and 2022, Then Used It Instead

Said by: Lawyers for Liberty / Article 19 / Bar Council

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 What Happened

The Sedition Act 1948 — a colonial-era law that PH explicitly promised to repeal in both its 2018 and 2022 election manifestos — remains fully operational under the Anwar government. Not only has it not been repealed, it is being actively used against government critics. Activist Chegubard was charged under the Act for his Forest City casino social media post.

🔥 Why It Matters

The Act criminalises speech that is ‘seditious,’ a vaguely defined term that gives authorities enormous discretion to prosecute political expression. Lawyers for Liberty has repeatedly condemned the government’s continued reliance on repressive laws. The Malaysian Bar Council has called for its repeal. International press freedom organisations have flagged its use as incompatible with democratic norms.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Alongside the Sedition Act, other repressive laws PH promised to repeal remain on the books: the Printing Presses and Publications Act 1984, the Security Offences (Special Measures) Act 2012 (SOSMA), and Section 233 of the Communications and Multimedia Act (used to prosecute online speech). Every single repressive law PH campaigned against is still available for PH to use — and PH is using them. The betrayal is complete.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised to repeal these laws. Twice. In writing. In your manifesto. You didn’t just break a promise — you weaponised the very tools you swore to destroy. When you use the Sedition Act against critics, you’re not governing — you’re doing exactly what BN did, with the added insult of having promised not to.

Single Mothers in Poverty — 300,000+ Families Led by Women Living Below the Poverty Line

Said by: Women’s Ministry / DOSM Data

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 What Happened

An estimated 300,000+ single-mother households in Malaysia live at or below the poverty line, facing inadequate government support, employment discrimination, and insufficient childcare infrastructure. Single mothers are disproportionately represented among B40 households and face barriers including limited access to affordable housing, healthcare, and skills training.

🔥 Why It Matters

Single mothers and their children represent one of the most vulnerable demographics in Malaysia. Children in these households face higher risks of educational dropout, malnutrition, and intergenerational poverty. The lack of affordable childcare forces many single mothers to choose between working (and leaving children unsupervised) or staying home (and being unable to afford basic needs).

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Government programmes for single mothers exist on paper but are fragmented, underfunded, and bureaucratically complex to access. The Bantuan Ibu Tunggal and various state-level programmes reach only a fraction of eligible recipients. Meanwhile, the government finds billions for GLC bailouts, political appointments, and civil service perks. The contrast between how Malaysia treats political elites and how it treats single mothers tells you everything about government priorities.

📜 Previous Cases

Under BN, single mother welfare was also inadequate. PH promised to do better. Under PH 1.0, minimal progress was made before the government collapsed. Under PH 2.0, single mothers remain an afterthought — occasionally mentioned in budget speeches, rarely served by meaningful policy. The gender pay gap (6-9%), lack of affordable childcare, and employment discrimination that compound single mothers’ poverty remain unaddressed.

🎯 PH Callout

You talk about ‘Madani’ values — compassion, civilisation, humanity. There are 300,000+ families led by single mothers living in poverty. These are real women and real children, not statistics. Your RM2.6 billion JAKIM budget could transform single mother welfare overnight. Your RM85.5 million J-Kom propaganda budget could fund childcare centres. But you’d rather fund religious bureaucracy and government PR than feed hungry children. That’s not Madani — that’s malicious.

SOSMA Still Unrepealed — PH Using the Security Law They Called 'Draconian' to Detain Without Trial

Said by: Lawyers for Liberty / Bar Council / Amnesty International

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 What Happened

The Security Offences (Special Measures) Act 2012 (SOSMA), which allows detention without trial for up to 28 days and restricts judicial oversight of security cases, remains fully operational under the Anwar government. PH explicitly promised to repeal SOSMA in both its 2018 and 2022 manifestos, calling it ‘draconian’ and incompatible with democratic governance.

🔥 Why It Matters

Detention without trial is fundamentally incompatible with the rule of law. SOSMA gives the government the power to detain individuals for nearly a month without charge and without full judicial scrutiny. In the wrong hands, it’s a tool for political persecution. Every day it remains on the books is a day any Malaysian can be detained without the protections that democracy promises.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH called SOSMA ‘draconian’ when BN used it. PH politicians gave speeches condemning it. Lawyers for Liberty documented its abuse. The Bar Council called for its repeal. Amnesty International flagged it annually. Now PH is in power with a supermajority, and SOSMA remains. Not amended. Not reformed. Not repealed. Unchanged and available for use.

📜 Previous Cases

Under BN, SOSMA was used against political activists. Under PH 1.0, no repeal in 22 months. Under PH 2.0, over three years, no repeal. The Sedition Act: same story. PPPA: same story. EVERY repressive law PH promised to abolish: same story. The pattern is not accidental.

🎯 PH Callout

You have the numbers. You have the mandate. You spent YEARS calling SOSMA draconian. Repeal it. You won’t — because having the power to detain without trial is convenient, even for ‘reformists.’ Your refusal proves your opposition was always about who held the power, never about the principle.

[BM] Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian Diluluskan — SKMM Dapat Kuasa Penapisan Menyeluruh, Tiada Pengawasan

Said by: Parliament / Fahmi Fadzil

Category: Social

Date: 2024-12-20

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada Disember 2024, Parlimen meluluskan Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian dan pindaan AKM yang memberi SKMM kuasa penapisan menyeluruh dengan penalti sehingga RM10 juta bagi platform yang tidak patuh. Perundangan ini memberi kuasa kepada SKMM untuk mengarahkan penyingkiran kandungan, menyekat laman web dan menghukum syarikat media sosial — semuanya tanpa pengawasan kehakiman bebas. Menteri Komunikasi Fahmi Fadzil menggambarkannya sebagai perlu untuk melindungi kanak-kanak dan membanteras ancaman siber. Freedom House membenderakannya sebagai kemunduran besar dalam kebebasan internet. Tiada mekanisme pengawasan bebas dimasukkan.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian memberi agensi kerajaan — SKMM, yang melaporkan kepada Menteri Komunikasi — kuasa untuk menentukan apa yang rakyat Malaysia boleh lihat dalam talian. Tiada tribunal bebas, tiada keperluan semakan kehakiman sebelum penyingkiran kandungan, dan tiada mekanisme ketelusan untuk kandungan yang disekat. Apabila agensi kerajaan boleh menyingkir kandungan dan menghukum platform sehingga RM10 juta tanpa pengawasan bebas, setiap platform mempunyai insentif untuk mematuhi secara berlebihan — menyingkir kandungan sempadan secara proaktif berbanding mengambil risiko penalti. Hasilnya ialah penapisan melalui insentif, bukan sahaja penapisan melalui perintah.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: PH semasa pembangkang melawan tepat jenis undang-undang ini. Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar adalah bukti utama PH bagi autoritarianisme BN — dan Akta itu lebih ringan daripada apa yang PH kini luluskan. Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar mempunyai denda sehingga RM500,000. Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian mempunyai penalti sehingga RM10 juta. Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar menyasarkan penceramah individu. Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian menyasarkan platform — bermakna SKMM boleh mengancam menutup keseluruhan perkhidmatan melainkan mereka mematuhi. PH telah menggantikan undang-undang buruk dengan yang lebih buruk dan memanggilnya reformasi.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Di bawah BN, Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar (2018), Akta Hasutan, dan Seksyen 233 AKM membentuk peralatan penapisan internet. PH memansuhkan Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar pada 2018 dan berjanji mereformasi AKM. Di bawah PH 2.0, AKM diperkuatkan (bukan direformasi), Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian diluluskan (menambah kuasa baharu), dan SKMM diberi alat penguatkuasaan yang melebihi apa yang BN pernah miliki. Infrastruktur penapisan yang PH bina dalam tiga tahun lebih komprehensif daripada apa yang BN bina dalam 60 tahun.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda memansuhkan Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar kerana ia memberi kerajaan terlalu banyak kuasa ke atas ucapan dalam talian. Kemudian anda meluluskan Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian, yang memberi kerajaan lebih banyak kuasa lagi dengan penalti lebih besar dan tiada pengawasan bebas. Anda tidak mereformasi peraturan internet — anda menguatkannya secara turbo. Masukkan pengawasan kehakiman bebas untuk semua perintah penyingkiran kandungan. Hadkan penalti pada tahap yang tidak mewujudkan insentif untuk pematuhan berlebihan. Terbitkan laporan ketelusan tentang semua permintaan penyingkiran kandungan SKMM. Atau terima bahawa anda telah membina rangka kerja penapisan internet paling komprehensif dalam sejarah Malaysia — dan akui ia.
