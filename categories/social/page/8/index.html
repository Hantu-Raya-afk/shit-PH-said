Fahmi Fadzil Forces Social Media Licensing on Platforms — Critics Call It PH's Version of Internet Censorship

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2024-07-28

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 28 July 2024, the PH government announced that social media platforms with more than 8 million users in Malaysia would be required to apply for a regulatory licence from MCMC, effective 1 August. Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil said the measure was needed to combat cyber offences including online scams, child exploitation, and deepfakes. By late 2025, Fahmi was threatening legal action against non-compliant platforms. In January 2026, MCMC temporarily blocked X’s Grok AI feature over deepfake concerns. ARTICLE 19 called on Malaysia to “halt hasty imposition of mandatory electronic verification,” warning about implications for privacy and free speech.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Social media licensing gives the government a lever to pressure platforms into compliance with content removal requests. While the stated goals — combating scams, protecting children — are legitimate, the mechanism creates obvious censorship risks. When a government can threaten to revoke a platform’s operating licence, it has enormous power over what content stays up and what comes down. In a country with a history of using the Communications and Multimedia Act to prosecute critics, handing MCMC more regulatory power over social media is a loaded gun.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH rode social media to power. Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, and WhatsApp were the platforms that allowed PH to circumvent BN’s control of mainstream media. Without social media, the 1MDB scandal might never have reached the Malaysian public at the scale it did. Now in power, PH is licensing the same platforms, threatening legal action against those that don’t comply, and blocking AI features. The “child safety” framing is clever but transparent — the licensing framework applies to all content, not just child safety. PH repealed the Anti-Fake News Act and called themselves defenders of internet freedom. Then they introduced a licensing regime that gives MCMC even more power than the Act they repealed.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, the Anti-Fake News Act 2018 was PH’s exhibit A for authoritarian internet control. PH repealed it in 2018. Now PH has replaced it with something arguably more powerful: a licensing regime that gives MCMC ongoing regulatory leverage over platforms, rather than just post-hoc prosecution. PH also opposed MCMC’s use of Section 233 of the CMA to prosecute critics under BN. Under PH, the same section continues to be used, and platforms themselves can now be held liable through the licensing framework.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You repealed the Anti-Fake News Act and called yourselves defenders of internet freedom. Then you introduced a licensing regime that gives MCMC even more power over social media than BN ever had. You’re licensing the internet while claiming to protect it. The child safety argument is legitimate — but the licensing framework goes far beyond child safety. Be honest about what you’re building: a regulatory tool that can pressure platforms into compliance with government content demands. That’s not reform. That’s BN’s internet controls with a PH label.

[BM] Kad Akhbar Sebagai Senjata — Kod Etika Baharu Biarkan Kerajaan Tentukan Siapa Wartawan 'Sebenar'

Said by: Freedom House / Journalists’ Union

Category: Social

Date: 2024-06-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada pertengahan 2024, kerajaan mengeluarkan Kod Etika Wartawan yang dikemas kini yang secara efektif mewujudkan sistem pelesenan de facto untuk kewartawanan di Malaysia. Akses media ke acara kerajaan, sidang akhbar, dan sumber rasmi semakin bergantung pada pematuhan kod — bermakna media yang menerbitkan laporan kritikal berisiko kehilangan akses. Freedom House mendokumentasikan ini sebagai satu bentuk penapisan lembut. Penapisan kendiri menjadi mekanisme: cerita kritikal berisiko kehilangan akses, boikot pengiklanan daripada syarikat berkaitan kerajaan, atau siasatan SKMM.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Apabila kerajaan memutuskan siapa yang layak sebagai wartawan “sebenar,” ia mengawal persekitaran maklumat tanpa perlu menapis secara rasmi. Kod Etika tidak mengharamkan penerbitan — ia mengawal akses. Wartawan yang mematuhi mendapat kad akhbar, akses kepada menteri, dan jemputan ke acara rasmi. Wartawan yang tidak mematuhi kehilangan semua ini. Hasilnya adalah penapisan lembut: editor belajar cerita mana yang merugikan akses mereka, dan mereka menyesuaikan diri. Tiada undang-undang dilanggar. Tiada media diharamkan secara rasmi. Akhbar hanya diuruskan.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: PH berkempen secara eksplisit atas kebebasan akhbar. Anwar, banduan politik yang kasusnya diperjuangkan sebahagiannya oleh organisasi kebebasan akhbar di seluruh dunia, sepatutnya memahami lebih daripada sesiapa kepentingan akhbar bebas. Sebaliknya, kerajaannya telah mewujudkan kod etika yang berfungsi sebagai mekanisme penapis — memutuskan siapa yang boleh menjadi wartawan dan siapa yang tidak, berdasarkan pematuhan terhadap piawaian yang ditetapkan kerajaan. Setiap wartawan yang menapis sendiri untuk mengekalkan akses mereka adalah wartawan yang PH diamkan tanpa keburukan optik larangan rasmi. Penapisan halus tetap penapisan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Di bawah BN, Akta Mesin Cetak dan Penerbitan memerlukan lesen tahunan untuk media cetak — penapisan langsung yang PH berjanji untuk mansuhkan. Mereka tidak memansuhkannya. Di bawah PH, pendekatan lebih halus tetapi sama berkesan: berbanding memerlukan lesen untuk penerbitan, anda memerlukan pematuhan untuk akses. Akta Hasutan, PPPA, dan kini Kod Etika membentuk sistem kawalan media berlapis yang lebih sofistikated daripada BN tetapi mencapai hasil yang sama — akhbar yang tahu sempadan dan kekal di dalamnya.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Akhbar bebas melindungi demokrasi. Anda tahu ini — anda adalah banduan politik yang sebahagiannya diselamatkan oleh kebebasan akhbar. Kini anda telah mewujudkan sistem di mana wartawan memerlukan kelulusan kerajaan untuk menjadi wartawan “sebenar.” Tarik balik Kod Etika sebagai mekanisme penapis. Jamin akses kepada semua wartawan bertauliah tanpa mengira baris editorial. Mansuhkan PPPA seperti dijanjikan. Atau terus menguruskan akhbar melalui kawalan akses — dan terima bahawa rekod kebebasan akhbar anda tidak dapat dibezakan daripada BN.

[BM] Kekerasan Berlebihan Polis Di Protes — Pemimpin Reformasi Kini Guna Polis Rusuhan Terhadap Penyokong Sendiri

Said by: Lawyers for Liberty / Suaram / Bersih

Category: Social

Date: 2024-06-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Sepanjang 2024, pelbagai insiden polis menggunakan kekerasan berlebihan dan tangkapan awal terhadap penunjuk perasaan aman didokumentasikan di bawah kerajaan Anwar. Lawyers for Liberty, Suaram, dan Bersih semuanya mengeluarkan kecaman. Polis melakukan lawatan rumah untuk memberi amaran kepada peserta perhimpunan, menangkap penganjur sebelum acara, dan mengerahkan kekuatan yang tidak setimpal pada demonstrasi. Akta Perhimpunan Aman — yang PH telah berjanji untuk mereformasi — digunakan untuk mewajarkan penindasan terhadap kebebasan sivil yang sama yang pernah diperjuangkan PH.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Anwar Ibrahim membina seluruh kerjaya politiknya atas protes jalanan. Gerakan Reformasi bermula sebagai protes besar-besaran. Perhimpunan Bersih adalah oksigen politik PH. Hak untuk berhimpun secara aman adalah kebebasan sivil asas yang membolehkan kebangkitan PH ke kuasa. Kini PH menggunakan jentera PDRM yang sama — polis rusuhan yang sama, peruntukan PAA yang sama, taktik ugutan yang sama — untuk menindas penunjuk perasaan yang menuntut reformasi yang PH janjikan. Transformasi dari gerakan protes ke penindas protes mengambil masa kurang daripada dua tahun.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: PH berjanji mereformasi Akta Perhimpunan Aman untuk menghapuskan keperluan pemberitahuan awal dan kuasa veto polis. Sebaliknya, polis menggunakan Akta itu lebih agresif daripada di bawah BN untuk acara setaraf. Lawyers for Liberty mendokumentasikan kes di mana polis merujuk kes undang-undang Nik Nazmi sendiri pada 2013 — undang-undang yang PKR perjuangkan dan menang — untuk mewajarkan penindasan hak perhimpunan di bawah kerajaan PH. Parti yang mencipta preseden undang-undang untuk hak protes kini mengabaikan preseden itu. Ini bukan hanyutan dasar — ini penindasan yang disengajakan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Di bawah BN, tindakan keras polis terhadap perhimpunan Bersih menjadi berita utama antarabangsa. Gas pemedih mata di Bersih 2.0 (2011), meriam air di Bersih 3.0 (2012), dan tangkapan beramai-ramai pada perhimpunan berikutnya dirujuk oleh HRW, Amnesty, dan RSF sebagai bukti pemerintahan autoritarian Malaysia. PH menggunakan setiap tindakan keras untuk membina simpati, merekrut penyokong, dan mengutip dana. Komuniti antarabangsa mengiktiraf PH sebagai gerakan demokrasi yang memperjuangkan kebebasan asas. Kini komuniti antarabangsa yang sama mendokumentasikan PH melakukan perkara yang sama seperti BN.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda adalah gerakan protes. Pemimpin anda dipenjarakan kerananya. AP anda didakwa kerananya. Penyokong anda digas pemedih mata kerananya. Dan kini anda melakukannya kepada orang lain. Reformasi Akta Perhimpunan Aman seperti dijanjikan. Keluarkan arahan melarang tangkapan awal dan lawatan rumah untuk peserta perhimpunan. Pertanggungjawabkan PDRM atas kekerasan berlebihan. Atau terima bahawa Reformasi berakhir pada hari anda mula menggunakan polis rusuhan terhadap orang yang sama yang berarak untuk kebebasan anda.

[BM] SKMM Sekat Laman Berita Kritikal — MalaysiaNow, Asia Sentinel, UtusanTV Ditapis

Said by: Freedom House Report 2024

Category: Social

Date: 2024-06-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada pertengahan 2024, SKMM menyekat laman berita yang kritikal terhadap kerajaan termasuk MalaysiaNow, UtusanTV, TV Pertiwi, dan Asia Sentinel. Kod Etika baharu secara efektif memberikan atau menghalang akses media berdasarkan pematuhan — mewujudkan sistem pelesenan de facto untuk kewartawanan. Seorang lelaki dijatuhi hukuman 6 bulan penjara kerana hantaran Facebook yang menghina Sultan. Laporan Freedom House 2024 mendokumentasikan kebebasan internet Malaysia yang merosot di bawah PH, menandakan sekatan laman berita dan perluasan kuasa penyederhanaan kandungan SKMM sebagai kebimbangan utama.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Menyekat seluruh laman berita — bukan artikel individu, tetapi seluruh penerbitan — adalah tingkah laku autoritarian tanpa mengira parti mana yang melakukannya. MalaysiaNow, Asia Sentinel, dan UtusanTV disekat kerana mereka menerbitkan kandungan yang kerajaan dapati menyusahkan, bukan kerana mereka menerbitkan bahan haram. Apabila kerajaan mengawal laman berita mana yang rakyat Malaysia boleh akses, ia mengawal persekitaran maklumat. Freedom House secara eksplisit menandakan ini sebagai kemerosotan kebebasan internet — di bawah kerajaan yang berjanji melindunginya.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: Anwar pernah bergantung kepada media bebas untuk bertahan secara politik. Tanpa Malaysiakini, Sarawak Report, dan media antarabangsa yang melaporkan 1MDB, kebangkitan politiknya tidak mungkin berlaku. Kini kerajaannya menyekat laman berita yang menerbitkan liputan kritikal. Kod Etika mewujudkan sistem ganjaran-dan-hukuman di mana media yang patuh mendapat akses dan media kritikal disekat. Ini bukan regulasi — ini pengurusan akhbar oleh negara. Ironi bekas banduan politik yang menapis akhbar yang membantu membebaskannya melampaui parodi.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Di bawah BN, Sarawak Report disekat kerana liputan 1MDB. The Malaysian Insider ditutup. Akta Mesin Cetak dan Penerbitan memerlukan lesen tahunan untuk media cetak. PH mengecam semua ini sebagai autoritarian. Di bawah PH, pendekatan lebih halus tetapi sama berkesan: berbanding mengharamkan penerbitan melalui pelesenan, anda menyekatnya melalui SKMM dan mengawal akses melalui Kod Etika. Hasil akhir adalah sama — kerajaan memutuskan media mana yang orang ramai boleh gunakan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda bertahan kerana media bebas menceritakan kisah anda apabila kerajaan mahu mendiamkan anda. Kini anda adalah kerajaan, dan anda mendiamkan media bebas. Buka sekatan MalaysiaNow, Asia Sentinel, dan UtusanTV. Tarik balik Kod Etika yang berfungsi sebagai mekanisme penapis. Biarkan wartawan membuat kerja mereka tanpa kelulusan kerajaan. Atau terus menyekat akhbar sambil mendakwa melindungi kebebasan akhbar — dan berharap rakyat Malaysia tidak perasan percanggahannya.

MCMC Blocks Critical News Sites — MalaysiaNow, Asia Sentinel, UtusanTV Censored

Said by: Freedom House Report 2024

Category: Social

Date: 2024-06-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In mid-2024, MCMC blocked news outlets critical of the government including MalaysiaNow, UtusanTV, TV Pertiwi, and Asia Sentinel. A new Code of Ethics effectively grants or bars media access based on compliance — creating a de facto licensing system for journalism. A man was sentenced to 6 months for a Facebook post insulting a Sultan. Freedom House’s 2024 report documented Malaysia’s internet freedom as declining under PH, flagging the blocking of news sites and the expansion of MCMC’s content moderation powers as key concerns.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Blocking entire news outlets — not individual articles, but entire publications — is authoritarian behaviour regardless of which party does it. MalaysiaNow, Asia Sentinel, and UtusanTV were blocked because they published content the government found inconvenient, not because they were publishing illegal material. When the government controls which news outlets Malaysians can access, it controls the information environment. Freedom House explicitly flagged this as a decline in internet freedom — under a government that promised to protect it.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar once relied on independent media to survive politically. Without Malaysiakini, Sarawak Report, and international outlets covering 1MDB, his political comeback would not have been possible. Now his government is blocking news sites that publish critical coverage. The Code of Ethics creates a carrot-and-stick system where compliant media gets access and critical media gets blocked. This is not regulation — it is state management of the press. The irony of a former political prisoner censoring the press that helped free him is beyond parody.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, Sarawak Report was blocked for its 1MDB coverage. The Malaysian Insider was shut down. The Printing Presses and Publications Act required annual licences for print media. PH condemned all of this as authoritarian. Under PH, the approach is subtler but equally effective: instead of banning publications through licensing, you block them through MCMC and control access through the Code of Ethics. The end result is identical — the government decides which media the public can consume.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You survived because independent media told your story when the government wanted to silence you. Now you’re the government, and you’re silencing independent media. Unblock MalaysiaNow, Asia Sentinel, and UtusanTV. Withdraw the Code of Ethics that functions as a gatekeeping mechanism. Let journalists do their jobs without government approval. Or keep blocking the press while claiming to protect press freedom — and hope Malaysians don’t notice the contradiction.

Police Excessive Force at Protests — Reformasi Leader Now Uses Riot Police Against His Own Supporters

Said by: Lawyers for Liberty / Suaram / Bersih

Category: Social

Date: 2024-06-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Throughout 2024, multiple incidents of police using excessive force and pre-emptive arrests against peaceful protesters were documented under the Anwar government. Lawyers for Liberty, Suaram, and Bersih all issued condemnations. Police conducted home visits to warn potential rally participants, arrested organisers before events, and deployed disproportionate force at demonstrations. The Peaceful Assembly Act — which PH had promised to reform — was used to justify suppression of the same civil liberties PH once championed.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Anwar Ibrahim built his entire political career on street protests. The Reformasi movement began as a mass protest. Bersih rallies were PH’s political oxygen. The right to peaceful assembly was the foundational civil liberty that made PH’s rise to power possible. Now PH uses the same PDRM apparatus — the same riot police, the same PAA provisions, the same intimidation tactics — to suppress protesters demanding the reforms PH promised. The transformation from protest movement to protest suppressor took less than two years.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH promised to reform the Peaceful Assembly Act to remove prior notification requirements and police veto powers. Instead, police are using the Act more aggressively than under BN for equivalent events. Lawyers for Liberty documented cases where police cited Nik Nazmi’s own 2013 case law — law that PKR fought and won — to justify suppressing assembly rights under PH’s government. The party that created the legal precedent for protest rights is now ignoring that precedent. This isn’t policy drift — it’s deliberate repression.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, police crackdowns on Bersih rallies were front-page international news. Tear gas at Bersih 2.0 (2011), water cannons at Bersih 3.0 (2012), and mass arrests at subsequent rallies were cited by HRW, Amnesty, and RSF as evidence of Malaysia’s authoritarian governance. PH used every single crackdown to build sympathy, recruit supporters, and fundraise. The international community recognised PH as a democratic movement fighting for fundamental freedoms. Now the same international community is documenting PH doing exactly what BN did.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You were the protest movement. Your leader was jailed for it. Your MPs were charged for it. Your supporters were tear-gassed for it. And now you’re doing it to others. Reform the Peaceful Assembly Act as promised. Issue a directive prohibiting pre-emptive arrests and home visits for rally participants. Hold PDRM accountable for excessive force. Or accept that Reformasi ended the day you started using riot police against the same people who marched for your freedom.

Press Cards as Weapons — New Code of Ethics Lets Government Decide Who Is a 'Real' Journalist

Said by: Freedom House / Journalists’ Union

Category: Social

Date: 2024-06-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In mid-2024, the government released an updated Code of Ethics for Journalists that effectively creates a de facto licensing system for journalism in Malaysia. Media access to government events, press conferences, and official sources is increasingly dependent on compliance with the code — meaning outlets that publish critical reporting risk losing access. Freedom House documented this as a form of soft censorship. Self-censorship becomes the mechanism: critical stories risk loss of access, advertising boycotts from government-linked companies, or MCMC investigations.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When the government decides who qualifies as a “real” journalist, it controls the information environment without needing to formally censor anyone. The Code of Ethics doesn’t ban publications — it gates access. Journalists who comply get press cards, access to ministers, and invitations to official events. Journalists who don’t comply lose all of this. The result is soft censorship: editors learn which stories cost them access, and they adjust accordingly. No law is broken. No outlet is formally banned. The press is simply managed.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH campaigned explicitly on press freedom. Anwar, a political prisoner whose case was partly championed by press freedom organisations worldwide, should understand better than anyone the importance of a free press. Instead, his government has created a code of ethics that functions as a gatekeeping mechanism — deciding who gets to be a journalist and who doesn’t, based on compliance with government-set standards. Every journalist who self-censors to keep their access is a journalist PH has silenced without the bad optics of a formal ban. Subtle censorship is still censorship.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, the Printing Presses and Publications Act required annual licences for print media — direct censorship that PH promised to repeal. They didn’t repeal it. Under PH, the approach is subtler but equally effective: instead of requiring licences for publications, you require compliance for access. The Sedition Act, the PPPA, and now the Code of Ethics form a layered system of media control that is more sophisticated than BN’s but achieves the same outcome — a press that knows the boundaries and stays within them.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: A free press protects democracy. You know this — you were a political prisoner partly rescued by press freedom. Now you’ve created a system where journalists need government approval to be “real” journalists. Withdraw the Code of Ethics as a gatekeeping mechanism. Guarantee access to all accredited journalists regardless of editorial line. Repeal the PPPA as promised. Or keep managing the press through access control — and accept that your press freedom record is indistinguishable from BN’s.

Manifesto PH 2022 termasuk komitmen untuk memansuhkan Akta Hasutan, mereformasi Akta Mesin Cetak dan Penerbitan, dan melindungi kebebasan akhbar. Trajektori jelas: PH memberikan kemajuan kebebasan akhbar dalam pembangkang dan awal pemerintahan, kemudian kembali kepada penindasan sebaik penelitian media menjadi menyusahkan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda jatuh 34 tangga dalam kebebasan akhbar dalam satu tahun sahaja. RSF panggil Malaysia “situasi sukar.” Manifesto anda menjanjikan kebebasan media. Kerajaan anda memberikan penindasan media. Buka sekatan MalaysiaNow dan Asia Sentinel. Gugurkan pertuduhan terhadap wartawan. Mansuhkan Akta Hasutan seperti yang dijanjikan. Tarik balik rejim pelesenan media sosial. Atau terus jatuh dalam kedudukan — dan berhenti berpura-pura Malaysia adalah demokrasi apabila pemerhati kebebasan akhbar dunia mengklasifikasikannya sebagai sukar.

Press Freedom Plunges 34 Spots to 107th — 'Difficult Situation' Under PH

Said by: Reporters Without Borders (RSF)

Category: Social

Date: 2024-05-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In May 2024, Reporters Without Borders (RSF) released the 2024 World Press Freedom Index, in which Malaysia plunged 34 places — from 73rd to 107th — under the PH government. RSF classified Malaysia as a “difficult situation” for press freedom. The collapse was driven by sedition charges against journalists and activists, MCMC content blocking, social media licensing requirements, and the arrest of FMT journalist Rex Tan. Even after a partial recovery to 88th in 2025, Malaysia still trails its 2023 ranking under PH.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A 34-place drop in press freedom under a government that promised media reform is not a statistical fluctuation — it is an indictment. PH’s 2022 manifesto included commitments to repeal the Sedition Act, reform the Printing Presses and Publications Act, and protect press freedom. Instead, under PH, journalists have been arrested, news sites blocked, and a social media licensing regime introduced. RSF’s “difficult situation” classification places Malaysia alongside countries PH claims to be morally superior to. The ranking reflects not just specific incidents but a systemic deterioration in the media environment.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s response to the ranking drop was to blame “inherited problems” and promise improvement. But the specific incidents RSF cited — Chegubard’s sedition charge, Rex Tan’s arrest, MCMC blocking MalaysiaNow and Asia Sentinel — all occurred under PH’s watch. These are not inherited problems; they are PH-created problems. The government that promised to protect journalists is now prosecuting them. The government that promised to unblock websites is now blocking more. Every press freedom commitment has been reversed.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under Najib, Malaysia’s press freedom ranking hovered around 140th — driven by 1MDB-related media suppression, the blocking of Sarawak Report, and sedition charges against editors. PH 1.0 achieved a dramatic improvement to 101st in 2019. PH 2.0 initially maintained the improvement before the 2024 collapse. The trajectory is clear: PH delivers press freedom gains in opposition and early government, then reverts to suppression once media scrutiny becomes inconvenient. The 34-place drop happened in a single year — suggesting a deliberate policy shift, not gradual decline.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You dropped 34 places in press freedom in a single year. RSF calls Malaysia a “difficult situation.” Your manifesto promised media freedom. Your government delivered media suppression. Unblock MalaysiaNow and Asia Sentinel. Drop charges against journalists. Repeal the Sedition Act as promised. Withdraw the social media licensing regime. Or keep falling in the rankings — and stop pretending Malaysia is a democracy when the world’s press freedom watchdog classifies it as difficult.

[BM] Chegubard Didakwa HASUTAN Kerana Melaporkan Dakwaan Kasino Forest City — Janji Kebebasan Bersuara Reformasi Mati

Said by: Johor Sessions Court / Badrul Hisham (Chegubard)

Category: Social

Date: 2024-04-30

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 30 April 2024, aktivis Badrul Hisham Shaharin (Chegubard) didakwa di bawah Akta Hasutan era kolonial Malaysia atas hantaran media sosial mengenai rancangan kasino di Forest City, Johor. Akta Hasutan adalah undang-undang yang sama yang PH berjanji untuk memansuhkan semasa kempen pilihan raya 2018 dan 2022. Sehingga Mac 2026, rayuan Chegubard masih belum selesai di Mahkamah Sesyen Johor Bahru. Bukannya memansuhkan Akta Hasutan, kerajaan Anwar kini menjadikannya senjata terhadap pengkritik — buku panduan yang sama tepat-tepat digunakan oleh BN terhadap Anwar sendiri pada tahun 1990-an.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Setiap undang-undang menindas yang PH berjanji untuk memansuhkan — Akta Hasutan, Akta Mesin Cetak dan Penerbitan, SOSMA, Seksyen 233 Akta Komunikasi dan Multimedia — masih kekal dan digunakan secara aktif oleh PH. Pertubuhan kebebasan akhbar mengecam pendakwaan itu sebagai isyarat yang menakutkan kepada wartawan dan aktivis: di Malaysia Anwar, mempersoalkan kepentingan berkuasa — terutamanya yang berkaitan dengan zon ekonomi khas Forest City — boleh membawa anda ke mahkamah. Mesejnya jelas: kebebasan bersuara berakhir di mana kepentingan perniagaan berkuasa bermula.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Anwar Ibrahim sendiri didakwa di bawah Akta Hasutan. Beliau tahu apa rasanya menghadapi pendakwaan atas ucapan. Beliau membina keseluruhan gerakan politik — Reformasi — atas ketidakadilan dibungkamkan oleh negara. Kini kerajaannya menggunakan undang-undang yang sama terhadap seorang lelaki yang melaporkan rancangan kasino di sebuah pembangunan yang kerajaan secara aktif mempromosikan. Akta Hasutan bukan sekadar tidak dimansuhkan — ia diperluaskan, dengan lebih 90 tangkapan berkaitan hasutan di bawah PH. Lelaki yang pernah menjadi mangsa undang-undang ini kini menggunakannya.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Di bawah BN, Akta Hasutan digunakan terhadap ahli politik pembangkang, ahli akademik, peguam, dan wartawan. Anwar, Karpal Singh, Azmi Sharom, dan berpuluh yang lain didakwa atau disiasat. PH mengecam setiap pendakwaan sebagai keterlaluan autoritarian. Di bawah PH 2.0, undang-undang yang sama telah digunakan terhadap Chegubard, pengulas media sosial, dan pengkritik dasar kerajaan. Satu-satunya perbezaan ialah nama pada kertas pertuduhan — undang-undang, proses, dan kesan yang menakutkan adalah serupa.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda dipenjarakan di bawah undang-undang ini. Anda tahu apa rasanya. Anda berjanji untuk memansuhkannya. Dan kini anda menggunakannya terhadap jenis aktivis yang sama yang berpawai untuk kebebasan anda. Mansuhkan Akta Hasutan — seperti yang anda janjikan pada 2018 dan 2022. Jatuhkan pertuduhan terhadap Chegubard. Atau akui bahawa Reformasi tidak pernah mengenai kebebasan bersuara — ia mengenai membebaskan seorang lelaki, bukan sistem.
