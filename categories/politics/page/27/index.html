MACC Seizes Daim's Ilham Tower in Anti-Money Laundering Probe — Selective Prosecution or Genuine Accountability?

Said by: Azam Baki, MACC Chief Commissioner / Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-12-21

📌 What Happened

On December 21, 2023, MACC seized the 60-storey Ilham Tower in Kuala Lumpur — worth an estimated RM1 to RM1.5 billion — under Section 51 of the Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA). The seizure was part of a widening probe into former finance minister Tun Daim Zainuddin over a 1997 corporate transaction worth RM2.3 billion involving Renong Bhd and United Engineers Malaysia (UEM), both linked to UMNO. MACC Chief Azam Baki confirmed the seizure. Daim denied any wrongdoing and maintained that the investigations were politically motivated. MACC also charged Daim and his wife Naimah Abdul Khalid for failing to declare assets, and issued notices to locate four of Daim’s children.

🔥 Why It Matters

On paper, investigating a former finance minister’s hidden wealth is exactly what an anti-corruption government should do. But the context matters: Daim was a key ally of Mahathir, who is now politically opposed to Anwar. Mahathir’s allies — Daim, various opposition figures — faced MACC scrutiny under Anwar’s watch, while Anwar’s own coalition partner Zahid had 47 charges dropped months earlier. The pattern is unmistakable: MACC goes hard on political opponents and soft on political allies. This isn’t anti-corruption — it’s the weaponization of prosecution.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The same MACC chief, Azam Baki, who aggressively pursued Daim is the same man who faced his own shareholding scandal and was protected by Anwar’s government. The same AG’s office that signed off on the Daim probe also signed off on dropping Zahid’s 47 charges. If MACC were genuinely independent, the public might celebrate the Daim investigation as a win. But when the anti-corruption agency’s chief is himself mired in controversy, and the government picks and chooses which corruption to prosecute based on political alignment, the whole exercise looks like state power dressed up as accountability.

📜 Previous Cases

Selective prosecution has a long pedigree in Malaysia. Under Najib, MACC was accused of targeting opposition figures while ignoring BN-linked corruption. Under Mahathir 2.0, UMNO leaders faced charges that conveniently served political goals. Under Anwar, the target list shifted again: Mahathir’s allies get raided, Anwar’s allies get DNAA. The ICIJ’s Pandora Papers investigation linked Daim to $52 million in offshore trusts — so there may be genuine substance to the probe. But the selective application of justice ensures that even legitimate investigations are tainted by political suspicion.

🎯 PH Callout

You want credit for going after Daim? Fine. Then explain why you dropped Zahid’s 47 charges three months earlier. Explain why Azam Baki — a man with his own asset scandals — is leading this probe. You cannot prosecute your opponents and protect your allies and call it Reformasi. The rakyat sees the pattern. Either everyone is subject to the law, or nobody is. Right now, it looks like the law only applies to people who are politically inconvenient to Anwar Ibrahim.

[BM] AG Gugurkan Semua 47 Pertuduhan Terhadap Zahid Hamidi Tengah Perbicaraan — Anwar Kata 'Tidak Campur Tangan,' Tiada Siapa Percaya

Said by: Ahmad Zahid Hamidi, Deputy Prime Minister / Attorney General’s Chambers

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-09-04

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 4 September 2023, Jabatan Peguam Negara memohon untuk menghentikan semua 47 pertuduhan rasuah terhadap Timbalan Perdana Menteri Ahmad Zahid Hamidi — 12 pertuduhan pecah amanah jenayah, 27 pengubahan wang haram, dan 8 rasuah melibatkan puluhan juta ringgit daripada Yayasan Akalbudi. Mahkamah Tinggi memberikan Pelepasan Tanpa Pembebasan (DNAA). Ini berlaku selepas Hakim Collin Lawrence Sequerah sudah memutuskan bahawa pihak pendakwaan berjaya membuktikan kes prima facie terhadap semua pertuduhan, dan perbicaraan sudah di peringkat pembelaan. Pendakwaan memetik 11 alasan termasuk keperluan ‘siasatan lebih menyeluruh.’ Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim menafikan sebarang campur tangan, berkata ‘AG mempunyai kuasa untuk mengendalikan kes tersebut.’

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Ini adalah pukulan paling merosakkan terhadap kredibiliti antirasuah PH sejak berkuasa. Zahid adalah presiden UMNO dan pengerusi BN — rakan gabungan paling penting Anwar. Tanpa kerusi parlimen UMNO, kerajaan perpaduan runtuh. Masa — tengah perbicaraan, selepas keputusan prima facie — tidak pernah berlaku dan luar biasa dari segi undang-undang. Majlis Peguam Malaysia mengambil langkah luar biasa memfailkan semakan kehakiman terhadap keputusan AG. Seluruh jenama politik Anwar dibina atas perjuangan menentang rasuah. Membiarkan rakan gabungannya terlepas dari 47 pertuduhan memusnahkan jenama itu dalam sehari.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Pertahanan Anwar ‘saya tidak campur tangan’ adalah helah paling lama dalam buku panduan naungan: anda tidak perlu angkat telefon apabila seluruh sistem faham bahawa TPM terlepas adalah syarat kelangsungan kerajaan anda. AG melapor kepada PM. PM perlukan Zahid. Pertuduhan digugurkan. Matematinya bercakap sendiri. PH menghabiskan sedekad menjerit tentang campur tangan AG di bawah Najib dan Muhyiddin — kini mereka mahu kita percaya pejabat yang sama bertindak secara bebas untuk membebaskan sekutu terpenting PM pada masa paling sesuai dari segi politik. Majlis Peguam tidak percaya. Rakyat tidak percaya. Satu-satunya yang percaya adalah mereka yang kelangsungan politik bergantung pada berpura-pura.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Ini mengikut corak tepat tradisi ‘ahli politik tidak boleh disentuh’ Malaysia. Pertuduhan 1MDB Najib difailkan selepas BN hilang kuasa; pertuduhan Zahid digugurkan selepas UMNO sertai PH. Di bawah Mahathir 2.0, tiada pemimpin UMNO didakwa dengan serius sehingga angin politik berubah. Peranan dwi AG — penasihat undang-undang kerajaan DAN pendakwa raya — telah menjadi mekanisme campur tangan politik dalam pendakwaan sejak merdeka. PH janji untuk memisahkan peranan ini. Pada 2023, mereka belum buat. Menjelang 2026, masih belum.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Korang kata korang berbeza. Korang kata Reformasi bermakna tiada lagi urus niaga politik, tiada lagi pemimpin kebal, tiada lagi pendakwaan terpilih. Lepas tu kerajaan korang bebaskan seorang lelaki yang hadapi 47 pertuduhan rasuah sebab korang perlukan kerusi partinya. Setiap ceramah, setiap sepanduk, setiap slogan tentang tadbir urus bersih — inilah hari ia mati. Korang tak boleh berkempen atas platform antirasuah dan kemudian berdagang pendakwaan untuk aritmetik parlimen. Rakyat ingat.

AG Drops All 47 Charges Against Zahid Hamidi Mid-Trial — Anwar Says He 'Did Not Interfere,' Nobody Believes Him

Said by: Ahmad Zahid Hamidi, Deputy Prime Minister / Attorney General’s Chambers

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-09-04

📌 What Happened

On September 4, 2023, the Attorney General’s Chambers applied to discontinue all 47 corruption charges against Deputy Prime Minister Ahmad Zahid Hamidi — 12 counts of criminal breach of trust, 27 of money laundering, and 8 of bribery involving tens of millions from his Yayasan Akalbudi charitable foundation. The High Court granted a Discharge Not Amounting to Acquittal (DNAA). This was after presiding judge Justice Collin Lawrence Sequerah had already ruled that the prosecution had established a prima facie case on all charges, and the trial was already at the defence stage. The prosecution cited 11 reasons including the need for ‘more comprehensive investigations.’ Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim denied any involvement, saying ’the AG had the powers to handle the case.’

🔥 Why It Matters

This was the single most damaging blow to PH’s anti-corruption credibility since taking power. Zahid is the president of UMNO and chairman of Barisan Nasional — Anwar’s essential coalition partner. Without UMNO’s parliamentary seats, the unity government collapses. The timing — mid-trial, after a prima facie ruling — was unprecedented and legally extraordinary. The Malaysian Bar took the extraordinary step of filing a judicial review against the AG’s decision, calling it unjustifiable. Anwar’s entire political brand was built on fighting corruption. Letting his coalition partner walk free from 47 charges shattered that brand in a single day.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar’s ‘I did not interfere’ defence is the oldest trick in the patronage playbook: you don’t need to pick up the phone when the entire system understands that the deputy PM walking free is a condition of your government surviving. The AG reports to the PM. The PM needs Zahid. The charges were dropped. The math does the talking. PH spent a decade screaming about AG interference under Najib and Muhyiddin — now they want us to believe the same office acted independently to free the PM’s most important ally at the most politically convenient moment. The Malaysian Bar didn’t buy it. The public didn’t buy it. The only people who bought it are the ones whose political survival depends on pretending.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows the exact pattern of Malaysia’s ‘untouchable politician’ tradition. Najib’s 1MDB charges were filed after BN lost power; Zahid’s charges were dropped after UMNO joined PH. Under Mahathir 2.0, no UMNO leader faced serious prosecution until the political winds changed. The AG’s dual role — government legal adviser AND public prosecutor — has been the mechanism for political interference in prosecution since independence. PH promised to separate these roles. In 2023, they hadn’t done it. By 2026, they still haven’t.

🎯 PH Callout

You told us you were different. You told us Reformasi meant no more political deals, no more untouchable leaders, no more selective prosecution. Then your government freed a man facing 47 corruption charges because you needed his party’s seats. Every ceramah, every banner, every slogan about clean governance — this is the day it died. You don’t get to campaign on anti-corruption and then trade prosecutions for parliamentary arithmetic. The rakyat remembers.

[BM] SKMM Sekat Laman Media Pembangkang Sebelum Pilihan Raya Negeri — Fahmi Kata 'Saya Tak Beri Arahan,' RSF Panggil Ia Penapisan

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister / MCMC

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-08-07

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada Ogos 2023, menjelang enam pilihan raya negeri, SKMM Malaysia menyekat akses kepada sekurang-kurangnya empat laman berita pro-pembangkang — MalaysiaNow, UtusanTV, Malaysia Today, dan TV Pertiwi — serta blog Wee Choo Keong. Pengguna dialihkan ke alamat IP SKMM. Tiada satu pun media tersebut menerima amaran atau penjelasan tentang artikel mana yang mencetuskan sekatan. Menteri Komunikasi Fahmi Fadzil menafikan memberi sebarang arahan kepada SKMM, berkata ‘Saya tidak memberi arahan kepada SKMM untuk menyekat sesiapa.’ Wartawan Tanpa Sempadan (RSF) mengeluarkan kenyataan mengecam tindakan itu dan menggesa Fahmi menghentikan penapisan. Sekatan terhadap MalaysiaNow dan UtusanTV ditarik dalam beberapa hari selepas pilihan raya, tetapi Malaysia Today dan laman lain kekal disekat.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

PH berkuasa pada PRU14 2018 sebahagiannya atas janji mengakhiri penindasan media. The Economist meletakkan Malaysia sebagai negara paling demokratik di Asia Tenggara tahun itu. Lima tahun kemudian, PH menyekat media pembangkang tepat sebelum pilihan raya — buku panduan tepat rejim BN yang mereka gantikan. Masanya tidak halus: sekat suara kritikal sebelum hari mengundi, buka semula sesetengah selepas keputusan keluar. Ini menandakan komitmen PH terhadap kebebasan akhbar adalah bersyarat — bebas apabila sesuai, disekat apabila tinjauan ketat.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Pertahanan Fahmi ‘saya tak beri arahan’ adalah buku panduan penafian munasabah. SKMM berada di bawah kementeriannya. Sekatan berlaku semasa tempoh paling sensitif politik 2023. Jika Menteri Komunikasi tidak tahu siapa mengarahkan agensinya sendiri menyekat laman berita beberapa hari sebelum pilihan raya, dia sama ada tidak cekap atau berbohong — dan kedua-dua jawapan tidak boleh diterima. PH dalam pembangkang menjerit bila BN sekat media. PH dalam kerajaan kata ‘bukan salah saya, SKMM bertindak sendiri.’ Kemunafikan ini mengejutkan.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Penindasan media adalah tradisi dwipartisan Malaysia. BN sekat The Malaysian Insider dan Sarawak Report. Kerajaan Muhyiddin menekan Al Jazeera dan menahan wartawan. PH janji mansuhkan Akta Mesin Cetak, Akta Hasutan, dan Seksyen 233 CMA. Tiada satu pun dimansuhkan. Sebaliknya, PH menggunakan undang-undang yang sama — kadang-kadang lebih agresif — daripada pendahulu mereka.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Korang janji kebebasan media. Korang janji hentikan penapisan SKMM. Korang janji kepada rakyat bahawa kerajaan PH tidak akan sekat laman berita untuk kepentingan politik. Lepas tu korang sekat empat laman tepat sebelum pilihan raya dan bagitahu kita bukan korang. Kalau kerajaan ‘reformasi’ korang tak boleh jamin wartawan boleh terbit tanpa disekat negara, korang bukan reformis — korang naluri autoritarian yang sama pakai jersi berbeza.

MCMC Blocks Opposition Media Sites Before State Elections — Fahmi Says 'I Gave No Instruction,' RSF Calls It Censorship

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister / MCMC

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-08-07

📌 What Happened

In August 2023, in the lead-up to six state elections, Malaysia’s MCMC blocked access to at least four pro-opposition news sites — MalaysiaNow, UtusanTV, Malaysia Today, and TV Pertiwi — as well as blogger Wee Choo Keong’s site. Users were redirected to an MCMC IP address. None of the outlets received any warning or explanation of which articles triggered the blocks. Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil denied giving any instruction to MCMC, saying ‘I have given no instruction to MCMC to block anyone.’ Reporters Without Borders (RSF) issued a statement slamming the crackdowns and urging Fahmi to end such censorship. While blocks on MalaysiaNow and UtusanTV were lifted within days after the elections, Malaysia Today and other sites remained blocked long after.

🔥 Why It Matters

PH came to power in GE14 in 2018 partly on a promise to end media repression. The Economist ranked Malaysia the most democratic country in Southeast Asia that year. Five years later, PH is blocking opposition media right before elections — the exact playbook of the BN regimes they replaced. The timing was not subtle: block critical voices before voting day, unblock some after results are in. This signalled that PH’s commitment to press freedom was conditional — free when it suits them, restricted when the polls are tight.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Fahmi’s ‘I gave no instruction’ defence is the plausible deniability playbook. MCMC falls under his ministry. The blocks happened during the most politically sensitive period of 2023. If the Communications Minister doesn’t know who ordered his own agency to block news sites days before an election, he is either incompetent or lying — and neither answer is acceptable. PH in opposition screamed when BN blocked media. PH in government says ’not my fault, MCMC acted independently.’ The hypocrisy is breathtaking. RSF saw right through it. The international community saw right through it. Only Fahmi pretends there’s nothing to see.

📜 Previous Cases

Media suppression is a bipartisan Malaysian tradition. BN blocked The Malaysian Insider and Sarawak Report. Muhyiddin’s government pressured Al Jazeera and detained journalists. PH promised to repeal the Printing Presses and Publications Act, the Sedition Act, and Section 233 of the CMA. None have been repealed. Instead, PH has been using the same laws — sometimes more aggressively — than their predecessors, as documented by the US State Department’s 2023 human rights report on Malaysia.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised media freedom. You promised to end MCMC censorship. You promised the rakyat that a PH government would never block news sites for political convenience. Then you blocked four outlets right before an election and told us it wasn’t you. If your ‘reform’ government cannot even guarantee that journalists can publish without being blocked by the state, you are not reformers — you are the same authoritarian impulse wearing a different jersey.

[BM] Anwar Lantik Anak Sendiri Nurul Izzah Sebagai Penasihat Kanan Ekonomi — Kemudian Kata Bukan Nepotisme

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-01-31

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 31 Januari 2023 — dua bulan selepas menjadi perdana menteri — Anwar Ibrahim melantik anaknya Nurul Izzah Anwar sebagai Penasihat Kanan Ekonomi dan Kewangan di Jabatan Perdana Menteri. Ini berlaku selepas Nurul Izzah kalah di kerusi Parlimen Permatang Pauh dalam PRU15 kepada Perikatan Nasional dengan hampir 6,000 undi — kerusi yang merupakan kubu tradisional PKR. Pelantikan itu tanpa gaji, yang digunakan Anwar sebagai pembelaan utamanya. Beliau memberitahu wartawan: “Nepotisme adalah apabila ahli keluarga diberi jawatan untuk menyalahguna kuasa, memperkayakan diri, mendapatkan kontrak dan dibayar sejumlah besar. Ini bukan kesnya.”

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Nepotisme tidak ditakrifkan oleh gaji — ia ditakrifkan oleh akses. Penasihat tanpa gaji kepada Perdana Menteri mempunyai lebih banyak pengaruh institusi daripada kebanyakan penjawat awam bergaji. Peranan itu memberikan kedekatan kepada dasar, kontrak, dan pembuatan keputusan di peringkat tertinggi. Melantik calon yang baru ditolak oleh pengundi ke peranan penasihat yang tidak dipilih adalah tamparan kepada hasil demokrasi. Jika PM boleh melantik ahli keluarga ke peranan penasihat yang tidak dipilih dan memanggilnya boleh diterima, maka setiap PM masa depan boleh melakukan perkara yang sama. Preseden sudah ditetapkan.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Anwar menghabiskan 25 tahun menjenamakan dirinya sebagai penawar kepada politik naungan yang mengakar di Malaysia. Dua bulan selepas berkuasa, beliau melantik anak sendiri. Pembelaan — “ia tanpa gaji, oleh itu bukan nepotisme” — adalah pengelakan buku teks. Adakah terdapat panggilan terbuka? Senarai pendek? Jawatankuasa tapisan? Tidak. Beliau dilantik kerana beliau anak PM, dan setiap rakyat Malaysia tahu ini. Nurul Izzah memegang ijazah dari Johns Hopkins, tetapi kelayakan bukan isu — proses yang menjadi isu. Mahathir melantik ahli keluarga ke jawatan perniagaan. Kroni Najib memperkaya saudara-mara melalui urusan berkaitan 1MDB. Anwar berjanji untuk berbeza. Beliau tidak berbeza.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Dinasti politik adalah endemik dalam politik Malaysia. Anak-anak Mahathir memegang empayar perniagaan yang dibina atas hubungan politik. Anak tiri Najib, Riza Aziz, didakwa dalam skandal 1MDB. Rangkaian naungan UMNO memperkaya keluarga selama berdekad. PH mengkritik semua ini tanpa henti. Pelantikan Nurul Izzah oleh Anwar — dalam minggu-minggu memegang jawatan — mengisyaratkan bahawa buku panduan reformasi mempunyai asterisk: tidak terpakai kepada keluarga PM. Nurul Izzah sejak itu dilantik sebagai timbalan presiden PKR, mengukuhkan dinasti Anwar dalam parti.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda menghabiskan 25 tahun berkhutbah menentang naungan. Anda melantik anak anda dalam 60 hari. Beliau kalah kerusinya dan anda berikan peranan penasihat. Beliau kini timbalan presiden PKR. Ini bukan meritokrasi — ini pembinaan dinasti dengan PR yang lebih baik. Semua pelantikan penasihat PM harus melalui proses tapisan telus. Ahli keluarga PM yang sedang berkhidmat harus secara automatik hilang kelayakan daripada peranan yang tidak dipilih dalam JPM. Akui optiknya daripada mentakrifkan semula nepotisme untuk memenuhi keadaan anda.

Anwar Appoints Own Daughter Nurul Izzah as Senior Economic Adviser — Then Calls It Not Nepotism

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-01-31

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 31 January 2023 — two months into his premiership — Anwar Ibrahim appointed his daughter Nurul Izzah Anwar as Senior Adviser on Economics and Finance in the Prime Minister’s Department. This came after Nurul Izzah lost her Permatang Pauh parliamentary seat in GE15 to Perikatan Nasional by nearly 6,000 votes — a seat that was a traditional PKR stronghold. The appointment was unpaid, which Anwar used as his primary defence. He told reporters: “Nepotism is where a family member is given a position to abuse power, enrich themselves, obtain contracts and get paid a huge sum. This is not the case.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Nepotism is not defined by salary — it is defined by access. An unpaid adviser to the Prime Minister has more institutional influence than most paid civil servants. The role grants proximity to policy, contracts, and decision-making at the highest level. Appointing a candidate who was just rejected by voters to an unelected advisory role is a slap in the face of democratic outcomes. If the PM can appoint family members to unelected advisory roles and call it acceptable, then every future PM can do the same. The precedent is set.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar spent 25 years branding himself as the antidote to Malaysia’s entrenched patronage politics. Two months into office, he appointed his own daughter. The defence — “it’s unpaid, therefore not nepotism” — is a textbook deflection. Was there an open call? A shortlist? A vetting committee? No. She was appointed because she is the PM’s daughter, and every Malaysian knows it. Nurul Izzah holds degrees from Johns Hopkins, but qualification is not the issue — process is. Mahathir appointed family members to business positions. Najib’s circle enriched relatives through 1MDB-adjacent deals. Anwar promised to be different. He wasn’t.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Political dynasties are endemic in Malaysian politics. Mahathir’s sons held business empires built on political connections. Najib’s stepson Riza Aziz was charged in the 1MDB scandal. UMNO’s patronage networks enriched families for decades. PH criticised all of this relentlessly. Anwar’s appointment of Nurul Izzah — within weeks of taking office — signalled that the reformasi playbook had an asterisk: does not apply to the PM’s family. Nurul Izzah has since been installed as PKR deputy president, consolidating the Anwar dynasty within the party.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You spent 25 years preaching against patronage. You appointed your daughter within 60 days. She lost her seat and you gave her an advisory role. She’s now PKR deputy president. This is not meritocracy — it’s dynasty-building with better PR. All PM advisory appointments should go through a transparent vetting process. Family members of the sitting PM should be automatically disqualified from unelected roles in the PMO. Acknowledge the optics instead of redefining nepotism to suit your situation.

[BM] Manifesto PRU15 PH: 60 Janji Dibuat, Majoriti Dimungkiri Atau Ditinggalkan — Penipuan Terbesar Dalam Politik Malaysia

Said by: Malaysiakini Manifesto Tracker / Straits Times Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2022-11-19

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Manifesto PRU15 PH — dikeluarkan pada 19 November 2022 — mengandungi kira-kira 60 janji yang boleh diukur. Seperti yang dijejak oleh Penjejak Manifesto Malaysiakini dan dianalisis oleh Straits Times, majoriti telah dimungkiri, ditangguhkan, atau senyap-senyap ditinggalkan. Kegagalan utama termasuk: pemansuhan undang-undang menindas (Akta Hasutan diperluaskan sebaliknya, 90+ tangkapan), pemansuhan tol (kerajaan bayar RM591J untuk bekukan kenaikan), reformasi perkhidmatan awam (bilangan pekerja bertambah), had tempoh PM (rang undang-undang gagal di Parlimen), ratifikasi ICCPR (tidak dilakukan), pemansuhan AUKU (tidak dilakukan), pemisahan PG-PP (dihantar ke jawatankuasa), dan pengukuhan undang-undang antilompat parti (dilemahkan oleh pengecualian).

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Manifesto pilihan raya adalah kontrak antara kerajaan dan pengundi. Apabila majoriti janji dimungkiri, kontrak sosial runtuh. PH tidak kehilangan janji-janji ini kerana keadaan luaran — mereka memilih untuk meninggalkannya. Akta Hasutan tidak dimansuhkan kerana PH mendapatinya berguna. Tol tidak dimansuhkan kerana pemegang konsesi mempunyai hubungan politik. Rang undang-undang had tempoh PM gagal kerana AP PH sendiri tidak hadir. Ini bukan janji yang dimungkiri — ini adalah pembalikan dasar yang disamarkan sebagai pragmatisme.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Pembelaan PH sentiasa “kekangan gabungan” dan “kami perlukan sokongan UMNO.” Tetapi PH menulis manifesto dengan mengetahui ia memerlukan rakan gabungan. Jika anda menjanjikan reformasi yang tidak boleh anda sampaikan kerana aritmetik gabungan anda sendiri, anda berbohong pada ketika janji dibuat, bukan pada ketika pengkhianatan. Manifesto itu bukan aspirasi — ia transaksional. PH menukar janji dengan undi, mengumpul undi, dan mungkiri janji. Dalam konteks lain, ini adalah penipuan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Setiap kerajaan Malaysia telah memungkiri janji manifesto. Manifesto BN adalah senarai impian yang tiada siapa jangka akan ditunaikan. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) mencapai reformasi separa dalam 22 bulan sebelum Langkah Sheraton. Tetapi PH 2.0 unik: ia mempunyai majoriti lebih besar, lebih banyak masa, dan kurang alasan — namun rekod reformasinya lebih teruk daripada PH 1.0. Straits Times menyatakan bahawa kadar pematuhan manifesto PH telah menurun dengan setiap tahun berkuasa, bukan meningkat. Janji-janji bukan ditangguhkan — ia ditinggalkan.

PH's GE15 Manifesto: 60 Promises Made, Majority Broken or Abandoned — The Biggest Con Job in Malaysian Politics

Said by: Malaysiakini Manifesto Tracker / Straits Times Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2022-11-19

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: PH’s GE15 manifesto — released on 19 November 2022 — contained approximately 60 measurable promises. As tracked by Malaysiakini’s Manifesto Tracker and analysed by the Straits Times, the majority have been broken, delayed, or quietly abandoned. Key failures include: repeal of repressive laws (Sedition Act expanded instead, 90+ arrests), abolition of tolls (government pays RM591M to freeze hikes instead), civil service reform (headcount grew), PM term limits (bill failed in Parliament), ICCPR ratification (not done), UUCA abolition (not done), AG-PP separation (sent to committee), and anti-hopping law strengthening (weakened by exceptions).

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Election manifestos are the contract between a government and voters. When a majority of promises are broken, the social contract collapses. PH did not lose these promises to external circumstances — they chose to abandon them. The Sedition Act wasn’t repealed because PH found it useful. Tolls weren’t abolished because concessionaires have political connections. The PM term limit bill failed because PH’s own MPs didn’t show up. These aren’t broken promises — they’re policy reversals dressed up as pragmatism.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s defence is always “coalition constraints” and “we need UMNO’s support.” But PH wrote the manifesto knowing it would need coalition partners. If you promise reforms you can’t deliver because of your own coalition arithmetic, you were lying at the point of the promise, not at the point of the betrayal. The manifesto wasn’t aspirational — it was transactional. PH traded promises for votes, collected the votes, and defaulted on the promises. In any other context, this is fraud.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian government has broken manifesto promises. BN’s manifestos were wish lists that no one expected to be honoured. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) achieved partial reforms in 22 months before the Sheraton Move. But PH 2.0 is unique: it has a larger majority, more time, and fewer excuses — yet its reform record is worse than PH 1.0’s. The Straits Times noted that PH’s manifesto compliance rate has declined with each year in power, not improved. Promises aren’t being delayed — they’re being abandoned.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You wrote 60 promises. You broke most of them. You had three years, a supermajority, and the most favourable parliamentary arithmetic in Malaysian history. If you couldn’t deliver reform under these conditions, you never will. Publish a clause-by-clause manifesto compliance report. Explain which promises were abandoned and why. Set binding timelines for remaining commitments. Or admit what every Malaysian already knows: the manifesto was a campaign prop, not a governing plan.
