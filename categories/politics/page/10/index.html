MACC Seeks to Seize RM3 Billion in Daim Family Assets — Critics Ask If It's Justice or Vendetta

Said by: MACC (Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission)

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-07-11

Seizing RM3 Billion From a Dead Man’s Family

In July 2025, MACC announced it was seeking to seize and forfeit more than RM3 billion in assets across 11 countries linked to the late Daim Zainuddin, his family, and associates. Daim had died eight months earlier, maintaining his innocence.

The Facts

Fact: Daim served twice as Finance Minister and was credited with helping Malaysia recover from the 1997 Asian Financial Crisis.

Fact: He was charged in January 2024 for failing to declare assets, following Pandora Papers revelations. The charge was dropped after his death in November 2024.

Fact: Despite his death, MACC continued investigations and issued fresh forfeiture notices in December 2025.

Fact: Daim had denied all wrongdoing, saying the investigations were “politically motivated.”

Fact: Bloomberg asked directly: “Could Malaysia seize $700 million from a dead tycoon?”

Why It Matters

  • If Daim’s wealth was illegally obtained, forfeiture is justified. But pursuing a dead man’s family while Zahid’s 47 charges were dropped raises uncomfortable questions about selective justice.
  • MACC’s credibility depends on consistency. Going after Daim’s billions while defending Azam Baki’s share scandal looks like political targeting. *Sources: Bloomberg · Bloomberg · *Malay Mail

Lim Guan Eng Corruption Trial — MACC Officer Testifies About Money Given to Former Penang CM

Said by: Lim Guan Eng, former Penang CM / MACC

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-07-10

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 10 July 2025, a MACC investigating officer told the Sessions Court that during a fraud investigation in December 2019, Datuk Zarul Ahmad Mohd Zulkifli — then director of Consortium Zenith Construction (CZCSB) — revealed he had given money to both G. Gnanaraja and Lim Guan Eng in connection with the Penang undersea tunnel project. MACC Senior Superintendent Ng Heng Jun confirmed he had informed his superiors about Zarul’s statement. The tunnel project, worth RM6.3 billion, has been described by MACC witnesses as ‘akin to a land scam.’

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Lim Guan Eng was DAP’s most prominent leader and Penang’s Chief Minister for 10 years. He’s the face of DAP’s governance model. If the tunnel corruption allegations are proven, it doesn’t just implicate one politician — it implicates DAP’s entire Penang legacy. A RM6.3 billion project that MACC witnesses describe as a ’land scam’ would be the biggest corruption case linked to a DAP leader in the party’s history.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: DAP has positioned itself as the anti-corruption party for decades. Its entire brand is clean governance and meritocracy. The Penang tunnel trial threatens to demolish that brand. And DAP’s response? Silence from the national leadership. No public demand for full accountability. No independent review of Penang’s procurement processes under Guan Eng. The party that demands accountability from everyone else cannot bring itself to demand it from its own.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The tunnel project was approved during Guan Eng’s tenure, using a ‘request for proposal’ mechanism rather than open tender — a procurement method that critics say favours connected companies. The same procurement opacity was criticised in the Penang Transport Master Plan (PTMP). Under DAP, Penang’s mega-projects consistently avoided open tender processes.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your former Penang CM is on trial for corruption related to a RM6.3 billion project that MACC calls a ’land scam.’ Your current Penang CM says the tunnel project is ‘still active.’ Your party has said nothing meaningful about accountability. Either demand full transparency from your own leaders or stop pretending DAP is the anti-corruption party. You can’t have it both ways.

Civil Service Reform: Three Years of Talk, Zero Structural Change — 1.6 Million Bureaucrats Untouched

Said by: IDEAS / Public Policy Analysts

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-07-04

📌 What Happened

Anwar Ibrahim entered office promising sweeping civil service reform — modernisation, meritocracy, right-sizing a bloated bureaucracy that consumes a massive share of the national budget. Three years later, the civil service remains essentially unchanged: 1.3-1.6 million employees (excluding police and military), 90% Bumiputera, consuming approximately 40% of total government operating expenditure in salaries and pensions.

🔥 Why It Matters

IDEAS think tank found that the Anwar government actually increased political appointments to GLCs and statutory bodies, averaging 47.5 per year — exceeding even Mahathir 2.0’s rate of 43 per year. The bloated bureaucracy means Malaysia spends more on civil servant wages than on development spending. Pension obligations continue to balloon with no structural reform.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Proposals for performance-based assessments, right-sizing, and merit-based promotions have gone nowhere because the civil service vote is politically crucial — no government wants to antagonise 1.6 million voters and their families. Reform was always a lie; the civil service is PH’s vote bank just as it was BN’s.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised meritocracy. You delivered more political appointments than Mahathir. You promised right-sizing. The bureaucracy grew. You promised modernisation. The same inefficiencies persist. At some point, ‘reform’ stops being a promise and becomes a punchline. You passed that point two years ago.

GLCs as Political Dumping Ground — 47.5 Appointees Per Year, Even MORE Than Mahathir 2.0

Said by: IDEAS Think Tank Research

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-07-04

📌 What Happened

Research by IDEAS think tank found that the Anwar government appointed an average of 47.5 political appointees per year to Government-Linked Companies (GLCs) and statutory bodies — exceeding even Mahathir 2.0’s rate of 43 per year and far surpassing the stated goal of merit-based appointments. A total of 238 political appointments were documented across Anwar’s first years.

🔥 Why It Matters

GLCs control a massive share of the Malaysian economy — Petronas, Khazanah, TNB, Telekom, MAHB, and dozens more. When board positions are filled based on political loyalty rather than competence, the consequences are enormous: poor strategic decisions, corruption, and mismanagement. Every political appointee who lacks the skills for their role costs the economy in efficiency, innovation, and shareholder value.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH ran on an EXPLICIT platform of ending political patronage in GLCs. Anwar himself criticised BN’s practice. He promised merit-based appointments and performance accountability. Instead, he’s appointing political allies at a FASTER rate than his predecessors. The reformist who promised to end patronage has industrialised it.

📜 Previous Cases

Under Najib, GLC appointments were a cornerstone of UMNO patronage. The 1MDB scandal itself was partly enabled by politically-appointed boards that failed in oversight. Under Mahathir 2.0, 43/year. Under Anwar, 47.5/year. Each government promises reform; each government entrenches patronage deeper.

🎯 PH Callout

47.5 political appointees per year. MORE than Mahathir’s government. You didn’t reform GLC governance — you accelerated the patronage machine. Every unqualified political appointee on a GLC board represents a qualified professional passed over. This is corruption with extra steps.

Syed Saddiq Acquitted After '5 Years of Hell' — Was It Political Prosecution?

Said by: Court of Appeal / Prosecution

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-06-25

📌 What Happened

Court of Appeal unanimously acquitted Syed Saddiq of all 4 charges, admonishing the High Court for not throwing out the case earlier. He had been sentenced to 7 years jail and caning by the lower court. The prosecution then appealed to the Federal Court, which gave a rare ‘cooling-off period.’ SCMP called it ‘5 years of hell.’

🔥 Why It Matters

A young opposition politician was put through half a decade of legal proceedings that the Court of Appeal said should have been dismissed far earlier. Whether or not it was politically motivated, the system failed him. The original conviction was so weak that the appellate court was visibly frustrated.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

If the judiciary can do this to a sitting MP, it can do it to anyone. The case raises questions about prosecutorial discretion under successive AGs — and why the government appealed even after the acquittal. Selective prosecution remains Malaysia’s most potent political weapon.

🎯 PH Callout

You claim to champion judicial independence and due process. But when an opposition MP is acquitted after five years of hell — and your prosecution service still appeals — you’re not defending justice, you’re prolonging persecution. Drop the appeal or explain why the Court of Appeal’s unanimous verdict isn’t good enough.

CSIS Declares 'The Death of Reformasi' — Anwar's Own Movement Pronounced Dead by Washington Think Tank

Said by: Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS)

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-06-01

📌 What Happened

The Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS), one of Washington’s most influential policy think tanks, published a devastating analysis titled ‘The Death of Reformasi: Anwar Ibrahim, UMNO, and the Betrayal of a Movement.’ The piece argues that Anwar’s political identity has been ‘increasingly defined by the compromises he has made to secure his position within the government.’

🔥 Why It Matters

Reformasi wasn’t just a political movement — it was a generational promise. Millions of Malaysians, from university students to working-class families, invested decades of hope, protest, and sacrifice in the belief that Anwar Ibrahim represented a fundamentally different kind of politics. CSIS declaring it dead isn’t opposition rhetoric — it’s the verdict of the international policy establishment.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent 11 years in prison, endured two sodomy trials, survived political exile, and built an entire movement around one word: reform. He asked Malaysians to trust him. They did. And what did they get? A coalition with UMNO, the very party that imprisoned him. Zahid Hamidi, who was UMNO’s attack dog, is now his deputy. The man who fought the system IS the system.

📜 Previous Cases

In 2018, PH won on a reform mandate but collapsed within 22 months due to internal betrayal (Sheraton Move). Anwar was supposed to be the corrective — the leader who would finally deliver. Instead, he formed an even more compromised coalition than PH 1.0.

🎯 PH Callout

You built a movement on the promise of change. You asked people to march, to risk arrest, to believe. And then you partnered with the exact people they marched against. That’s not pragmatism — that’s the ultimate betrayal. Reformasi isn’t dead because it failed. It’s dead because you killed it.

Nik Nazmi Resigns as Environment Minister After Losing PKR Party Election — Exposes PH's Feudal Politics

Said by: Nik Nazmi Nik Ahmad, Former Natural Resources and Environmental Sustainability Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-05-28

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On May 28, 2025, Natural Resources and Environmental Sustainability Minister Nik Nazmi Nik Ahmad tendered his resignation from Cabinet, effective July 4. His resignation came hours after Economy Minister Rafizi Ramli also resigned — both following their defeats in PKR’s internal party elections five days earlier.

Nik Nazmi lost his PKR vice-president position and said he could no longer serve as minister without a full party mandate. “Being a minister can trap you in a ‘bubble’ — with privileges, security escorts, and protocols at every event,” he said. “I believe that becoming too attached to such a lifestyle can be dangerous.”

Anwar publicly asked Nik Nazmi to reconsider. Nik Nazmi refused, saying his decision was firm and he would focus on serving his Setiawangsa constituents.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Two Cabinet ministers resigning within hours of each other over internal party politics is not normal governance. It exposes the fact that PH ministerial appointments are fundamentally driven by party faction dynamics, not competence or national interest.

The PKR party election that triggered these resignations was itself marred by allegations of vote-rigging and nepotism — Anwar’s daughter Nurul Izzah defeated Rafizi for deputy president. When the PM’s daughter wins an internal contest and the losers are forced out of Cabinet, that’s not democratic renewal. That’s feudal politics dressed up in reform rhetoric.

The simultaneous departure of the Economy Minister and Environment Minister created months of policy uncertainty in two critical portfolios, with vacancies unfilled until December 2025.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Nik Nazmi deserves some credit for his principled stance — he’s one of the few Malaysian politicians who voluntarily gave up power. But the system that produced this outcome is rotten.

PKR’s party elections are supposed to be internal democratic exercises. Instead, they became proxy wars for the Anwar family’s consolidation of power. Nurul Izzah’s victory over Rafizi wasn’t just a party contest — it was the PM’s daughter defeating his most capable economic technocrat, leading to months of policy vacuum in the Economy Ministry.

And Anwar’s response? He begged Nik Nazmi to stay — not because of policy continuity, but because two ministerial resignations looked bad. The PM couldn’t even control the fallout from his own party’s elections. This is the “reformist” party that’s supposed to be different from UMNO’s feudal politics.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

UMNO’s history is littered with internal power struggles that spilled into governance — factional fights between Mahathir and Ku Li, between Mahathir and Anwar, between Najib and Muhyiddin. PKR was supposed to be different. It was founded on reformasi principles. Instead, it’s replicated the exact same patronage-driven internal politics, complete with a dynastic succession battle.

The PKR 2025 election also echoed the 2018 party polls, which were marred by similar allegations of irregularities. The party has never managed a clean internal election in its entire history.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You built PKR on the promise of democratic reform. Then your party election was marred by vote-rigging allegations, your daughter defeated your most prominent economic reformer, and two ministers resigned in protest. The result? Months of policy vacuum in the Economy and Environment ministries. This isn’t reform — it’s UMNO with better branding. If PKR can’t even run a clean internal election, why should Malaysians trust you to reform the country?

Rafizi Ramli Resigns as Economy Minister After PKR Humiliation — The Reformist Who Got Purged

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, former Economy Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-05-28

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 28 May 2025, Economy Minister Rafizi Ramli submitted his resignation from Cabinet, effective 17 June. His ally, Natural Resources Minister Nik Nazmi Nik Ahmad, also announced his exit, effective 4 July.

Rafizi said: “I entered politics to cultivate a new political culture grounded in accountability and the people’s mandate. My recent defeat in the PKR elections signifies that I no longer possess the party’s mandate.”

He noted that in countries with democratic principles, “leaders who lose in party elections should make way for the victors.” His final act as minister was completing the 13th Malaysia Plan.

Anwar reportedly urged Rafizi to reconsider. He refused.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Rafizi was the most credible reformist in Anwar’s Cabinet. He designed PADU, pushed subsidy rationalisation, championed data-driven governance, and was the intellectual engine behind PH’s economic reform agenda. His departure left a vacuum at exactly the moment Malaysia needed steady economic stewardship.

Bloomberg called it “a blow to Anwar’s government.” The Straits Times noted his exit “will leave a gap at a time when Malaysia’s economic reforms are stalling.” CNA reported that the party election was “marred by allegations of vote-rigging and nepotism.”

Two ministers quitting over a party election isn’t normal. It’s a signal that something is deeply wrong inside PKR.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Anwar lost his best minister because he allowed — or enabled — his daughter to challenge and destroy the incumbent. Rafizi didn’t lose because he was incompetent. He lost because the party machinery was mobilised for the boss’s daughter.

The fact that Anwar then asked Rafizi to stay is the most cynical part. You orchestrate a humiliation, then ask the humiliated to keep working for you. That’s not leadership — that’s gaslighting.

Since Rafizi’s departure, he has become an increasingly vocal critic. He warned Anwar not to “underestimate public sentiment.” He called for MACC chief Azam Baki’s suspension. He’s now under MACC investigation himself — a timing that critics call retaliatory.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Malaysian politics has a long tradition of purging internal rivals. Mahathir sidelined Anwar in 1998. Now Anwar has effectively sidelined Rafizi in 2025. The method is different — party elections instead of sodomy charges — but the outcome is the same: the leader’s most capable rival is removed.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You lost your best economic mind because family politics mattered more than national interest. Rafizi built your policy credibility. He was the reason investors took PH seriously. And you let him walk — or pushed him out. The 13th Malaysia Plan was his last gift to you. What have you done with it since?

Nurul Izzah Wins PKR Deputy President — Anwar Says It's Democracy, Not Nepotism

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister & PKR President

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-05-24

From Adviser to Deputy President: The Anwar Dynasty Takes Shape

In May 2025, Nurul Izzah Anwar was elected PKR Deputy President — the number two position in the party led by her father, the sitting Prime Minister. Anwar Ibrahim dismissed all criticism with a wave of the hand: she was chosen by members. Case closed. Except it isn’t.

What Happened

At the PKR National Congress in Johor Bahru on 24 May 2025, Nurul Izzah won the contest for Deputy President. The presidency — held by Anwar — was not up for contest. This means the top two positions in PKR are now held by father and daughter.

Anwar’s response was direct:

People will say that my wife and daughter are in the party. That may be true, but when we were fighting in 1998, there wasn’t even a congress.

The Problem With “Chosen by Members”

Yes, party members voted. But context matters:

  • Anwar is the party president. His endorsement — even if unstated — carries enormous weight in internal elections. PKR members know that voting against the president’s daughter carries political risk.
  • Patronage flows downward. In a system where the PM controls government appointments, contracts, and GLC positions, party members who align with the leadership family are better positioned. This is structural pressure, not free choice.
  • Former aide Ezam Nor went viral explaining that this was less about democracy and more about Anwar consolidating power ahead of anticipated political and legal challenges.

The Dynasty Tracker

  • Dr Wan Azizah Wan Ismail (Anwar’s wife): former PKR President, former Deputy PM
  • Nurul Izzah (daughter): former MP, Senior Economic Adviser to PM, now PKR Deputy President
  • The Anwar family has held or currently holds the top two party positions and a senior government advisory role Fact: No other political family in Malaysia’s reformist movement holds this concentration of power within a single party.

The Steelman (and Why It Fails)

Nurul Izzah has been in politics since her twenties, survived prison visits, campaigned during her father’s incarceration, and has policy credentials. She is not unqualified. But the question is not about qualifications — it is about whether a reformist party should have a father-daughter leadership structure while the father is also Prime Minister. In any mature democracy, this would be considered a governance red flag.

What Should Happen

  1. PKR should adopt rules preventing immediate family members of the president from holding the deputy presidency simultaneously.
  2. Anwar should have publicly stayed neutral in the contest.
  3. Malaysian civil society should stop accepting “the members chose” as a complete answer when the chooser’s father controls the party and the government.

Verdict: The reformasi movement was born out of opposition to Mahathir’s concentration of power. Three decades later, a new concentration is forming — this time with a family crest on it.
