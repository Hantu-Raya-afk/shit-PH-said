Jika ini bukan pelantikan politik, apa namanya? Laksanakan tempoh penyejukan. Wujudkan jawatankuasa pelantikan bebas. Atau akui sahaja sistem naungan kamu berfungsi tepat seperti BN, dengan PR yang sedikit lebih baik.

Anwar's Cabinet Reshuffle Sparks Race Controversy — Hannah Yeoh as FT Minister, Adam Adli as Religious Affairs Deputy

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-16

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On December 16, 2025, Anwar Ibrahim announced his second cabinet reshuffle, involving 28 ministerial and deputy ministerial changes. Among the most controversial appointments:

Hannah Yeoh was named Minister in the Prime Minister’s Department (Federal Territories) — the first non-Malay to hold the portfolio. PAS and right-wing Malay groups immediately attacked the appointment, claiming it would strengthen “DAP dominance” in KL and threaten Malay interests. Anwar called the criticism “atrocious” and said race should not determine ministerial capability.

Adam Adli — a former student activist — was appointed Deputy Minister in the Prime Minister’s Department (Religious Affairs), sparking questions about whether he had the credentials or background to oversee Islamic governance matters.

The reshuffle came after months of vacancies following Rafizi and Nik Nazmi’s resignations, and was widely seen as positioning for a potential snap election.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

The Hannah Yeoh appointment exposed the fundamental contradiction of Malaysian politics: a government that claims to be multiracial but operates in a system where race determines who can hold which portfolio. The FT Minister portfolio had always been held by a senior UMNO Malay. Anwar’s decision to give it to a Chinese DAP leader was either a bold reform move or a political miscalculation that handed ammunition to the opposition.

The Adam Adli appointment raised legitimate questions about competence. Religious affairs governance requires specific knowledge of Islamic jurisprudence, state-federal religious dynamics, and the politics of religious authorities. Appointing a former student activist with no demonstrated expertise in these areas looked like a political reward, not a merit-based decision.

The Straits Times noted that “controversies cloud Malaysian PM Anwar’s new Cabinet line-up” — not the kind of headline you want when you’re positioning for a snap election.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

There’s bullshit on multiple sides here. The race-based attacks on Hannah Yeoh are deplorable — she has a strong track record across multiple portfolios. Her race should be irrelevant to her competence.

But Anwar walked into this controversy with his eyes open. He knew appointing a non-Malay FT minister would trigger a racial backlash. If he wanted to make that stand on principle, fine — but then commit to it. Don’t make the appointment and then have UMNO’s Zahid Hamidi rush out to reassure Malays that it won’t undermine the “Malay agenda.” That sends the message that the appointment needs defending on racial grounds, which undermines the very principle Anwar claimed to be championing.

And the Adam Adli appointment? That’s just patronage. Reward a loyal activist with a portfolio he’s not qualified for. It’s exactly what PH criticised BN for doing.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Every Malaysian cabinet reshuffle generates racial controversy — it’s baked into the system. But PH was supposed to transcend this. In 2018, the appointment of Lim Guan Eng as Finance Minister was attacked on racial grounds. PH defended it then on merit. Seven years later, the same party is still fighting the same battles — suggesting that PH’s “multiracial” vision has not actually shifted the political landscape.

The pattern of rewarding loyalty over competence is also not new for PH. The Nurul Izzah adviser appointment, various political secretary scandals, and now Adam Adli all follow the same logic: political loyalty first, governance competence second.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You said you’d appoint ministers based on merit, not race. Then you made an appointment that triggered a racial firestorm, and instead of standing firm, your coalition partner UMNO rushed to reassure Malays. Pick a lane. Either you believe in multiracial meritocracy — and defend it without qualifiers — or admit that race still drives your Cabinet math. And while you’re at it, explain why a former student activist is overseeing religious affairs governance. Because “loyalty” is not a qualification.

Zafrul Appointed MIDA Chairman After Leaving MITI — The Revolving Door Never Stops Spinning

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: When the December 2025 cabinet reshuffle removed Tengku Zafrul from the MITI portfolio, he wasn’t sent to the backbench. The man who oversaw MIDA as minister now chairs it as a political appointee.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The revolving door between ministerial oversight and agency chairmanship is a textbook governance concern. Zafrul approved MIDA’s policies, budgets, and strategies as MITI Minister. Now he chairs the same agency he regulated. Investment decisions he influenced as minister — including the US trade deal’s implementation — will now be executed under his chairmanship. There is no cooling-off period, no separation, no firewall.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH campaigned on ending the revolving door between politics and GLCs. IDEAS documented 238 political appointments to GLCs and statutory bodies under Anwar — exceeding Mahathir 2.0’s rate. Zafrul’s MIDA appointment is the most brazen example: not even a pretence of distance between the ministerial role and the agency appointment. The same person, the same portfolio, different title. This is not governance reform; it’s musical chairs with name cards.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, ministers who lost portfolios were given GLC sinecures as compensation. PH criticised this practice relentlessly. Under Anwar, the practice continues with better PR — Zafrul’s appointment is framed as ‘continuity and expertise’ rather than what it is: a political reward and soft landing for a reshuffled ally.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You documented 238 political appointments. You campaigned against the GLC revolving door. Then you appointed your former MITI minister as chairman of MIDA — the agency MITI oversees. If this isn’t a political appointment, what is? Implement cooling-off periods. Create independent appointment committees. Or just admit that your patronage system works exactly like BN’s, with slightly better PR.

Date: 2025-12-15

Melayu memegang 77.5% daripada semua jawatan dan lebih 80% jawatan pengurusan tertinggi. Malaysia Cina hanya memegang 5.7% dan Malaysia India 3.8% — di sebuah negara di mana Cina membentuk 23% dan India 7% daripada populasi. Straits Times melaporkan “perdebatan dasar Bumiputera-dahulu” telah mengambil “campuran kuat kesihatan, pendidikan dan politik.”

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Perkhidmatan awam yang tidak mencerminkan demografi negara tidak boleh melayani semua rakyat secara saksama. Pengambilan berasaskan kaum dalam perkhidmatan awam mengekalkan pengecualian etnik dan menjejaskan meritokrasi — prinsip yang Anwar berkempen atasnya. Apabila 90% kakitangan kerajaan datang daripada satu kumpulan etnik, seluruh jentera negara — daripada reka bentuk dasar hingga pelaksanaan hingga penyampaian perkhidmatan — ditapis melalui satu kanta budaya.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: Jenama “Malaysia Madani” Anwar menjanjikan Malaysia yang bertamadun, meritokratik, dan pelbagai kaum. Namun lengan negara Malaysia yang paling ketara — perkhidmatan awam — kekal 90% satu kaum. Di bawah PH, ini tidak boleh disentuh. Bukan kerana pembaharuan mustahil, tetapi kerana 1.6 juta penjawat awam juga 1.6 juta undi — undi Melayu yang Anwar tidak mampu kehilangan menjelang PRU16. Kerajaan “reformis” tidak melakukan apa-apa — bukan program perintis, bukan sasaran, malah pengakuan awam pun tiada — untuk menangani ketidakseimbangan struktur ini.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Kuota perkhidmatan awam DEB diperkenalkan pada tahun 1970-an sebagai tindakan afirmatif. Lima puluh tahun kemudian, langkah “sementara” itu kekal, kuota berakar umbi, dan komposisi kaum hanya menjadi lebih homogen dari semasa ke semasa. Setiap PM — Mahathir, Abdullah, Najib, Muhyiddin, Anwar — meninggalkannya tanpa sentuhan.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Perkhidmatan awam kamu 90% satu kaum di negara pelbagai kaum. Jenama “Madani” kamu menjanjikan meritokrasi. Dua fakta ini tidak boleh wujud bersama. Tetapkan sasaran kepelbagaian. Buka pengambilan. Wujudkan laluan untuk rakyat Malaysia bukan Bumiputera berkhidmat untuk kerajaan mereka sendiri. Atau akui bahawa “Malaysia Madani” adalah slogan untuk eksport — jenama yang kamu jual kepada pelabur asing sambil menjalankan negara berasaskan kaum di tanah air.

Civil Service is 90% Bumiputera — Malays Hold 80%+ of Top Posts

Said by: Eurasia Review Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: According to Eurasia Review analysis published in December 2025, Malaysia’s civil service of 1.3 to 1.6 million employees is approximately 90% Bumiputera. Malays hold 77.5% of all posts and over 80% of top management positions. Chinese Malaysians hold just 5.7% and Indian Malaysians 3.8% — in a country where Chinese make up 23% and Indians 7% of the population. The Straits Times reported the “Bumiputera-first policy debate” has taken on a “heady mix of health, education and politics.” When a UiTM professor suggested opening Bumiputera-only programmes to other races to address specialist shortages, the backlash was immediate and the idea was shut down.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A civil service that doesn’t reflect the country’s demographics cannot serve all citizens equally. Race-based hiring in public service perpetuates ethnic exclusion and undermines meritocracy — the very principle Anwar campaigned on. The civil service is simultaneously Malaysia’s largest employer and its most racially homogeneous institution. When 90% of government servants come from one ethnic group, the entire apparatus of state — from policy design to implementation to service delivery — is filtered through one cultural lens. Non-Bumiputera citizens interact with a government that barely represents them.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s “Malaysia Madani” brand promises a civilised, meritocratic, multiracial Malaysia. Yet the most visible arm of the Malaysian state — the civil service — remains 90% one race. Under PH, this is untouchable. Not because reform is impossible, but because 1.6 million civil servants are also 1.6 million votes — overwhelmingly Malay votes that Anwar cannot afford to lose ahead of GE16. The “reformist” government has done nothing — not a pilot programme, not a target, not even a public acknowledgment — to address the structural imbalance. Meritocracy applies to everyone except the government’s own workforce.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The NEP’s civil service quotas were introduced in the 1970s as affirmative action. Fifty years later, the “temporary” measures are permanent, the quotas are entrenched, and the racial composition has only become more homogeneous over time. Every PM — Mahathir, Abdullah, Najib, Muhyiddin, Anwar — has left this untouched. The UiTM incident is instructive: even suggesting partial reform triggered enough backlash to kill the idea instantly. The third rail of Malaysian politics isn’t just electrified — it’s surrounded by a moat.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your civil service is 90% one race in a multiracial country. Your “Madani” brand promises meritocracy. These two facts cannot coexist. Set diversity targets. Open recruitment. Create pathways for non-Bumiputera Malaysians to serve their own government. Or admit that “Malaysia Madani” is a slogan for export — a brand you sell to foreign investors while running a race-based state at home. 1.6 million civil servants, 90% Bumiputera. That’s not meritocracy. That’s a monoculture wearing a multiracial mask.

PM's Own Aide Shamsul Iskandar Charged with Bribery — Anti-Corruption Crusade Eats Itself

Said by: Reuters / Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 4 December 2025, Anwar Ibrahim’s senior political secretary Shamsul Iskandar Mohd Akin was charged with receiving bribes totalling RM176,829 from a local businessman. The charges allege Shamsul used his privileged position in the Prime Minister’s office to assist the businessman in obtaining mineral mining licences in Sabah, among other favours. Shamsul resigned just days before being charged, pleaded not guilty, and was granted bail. He called the charges an “attack” against him. Reuters reported the charges “significantly increased scrutiny on the premier’s pledge to crack down on corruption.” CNA reported Anwar now faces a “graft crisis” alongside “PKR party fractures” and “PH coalition jitters.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This is not a minor official in a distant agency. This is the PM’s senior political secretary — someone with direct, daily access to the most powerful office in the country. The charges allege he was running a pay-for-play operation for mining licences from inside the PM’s office. If the allegations are true, the anti-corruption crusade’s own headquarters was a corruption venue. Reuters framing it as increasing “scrutiny on the premier’s pledge to crack down on corruption” is diplomatic understatement — this demolishes the pledge entirely.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: This is the same Anwar who built his entire political career on anti-corruption rhetoric. He spent decades as Malaysia’s moral compass on corruption. He went to prison (on charges he said were politically motivated). He built Reformasi as a movement against the abuse of power. And his closest aide was allegedly selling mining licences from the PM’s office. The irony is suffocating: the man who vowed to clean up Malaysia now has his own office as a crime scene. Shamsul calling the charges an “attack” echoes every corrupt Malaysian politician’s defence — and PH used to mock that defence when BN politicians used it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s history of aide and secretary corruption is extensive. Under Najib, aides and associates were implicated in 1MDB-related schemes. Under Muhyiddin, political secretaries were linked to procurement irregularities. The pattern: powerful aides use proximity to the PM to monetise access, knowing that their position provides both opportunity and protection. Anwar’s office is not different — it is the latest iteration. The Shamsul case is particularly damaging because it involves Sabah mining licences, connecting to the broader Sabah resource corruption ecosystem that PH promised to dismantle.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your closest aide was charged with bribery for selling access to mining licences from YOUR office. Not a distant state official. Not a minor functionary. Your political secretary. The person who sat outside your door. What did you know? When did you know it? Did Shamsul’s activities never come to your attention despite years of proximity? Either you knew and tolerated it, or you didn’t know — in which case your office management is dangerously negligent. Answer the question. The anti-corruption crusade doesn’t survive its own headquarters being a crime scene.

🎯 PH Callout

We didn’t elect you to be equally corrupt as the opposition. We elected you to be BETTER. ‘They do it too’ is not a reform agenda — it’s a confession that you’ve given up on reform. Sebaliknya, apa yang Malaysia ada ialah kerajaan yang melaksanakan pengurusan ekonomi yang cekap sambil secara sistematik meninggalkan janji demokratik yang membawanya berkuasa. Pertukaran itu kini jelas: kamu dapat pertumbuhan KDNK, tetapi kamu kehilangan kebebasan sivil, kemerdekaan institusi, dan akauntabiliti.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: Pembelaan PH sentiasa “kami mewarisi keadaan huru-hara” dan “beri kami masa.” Tetapi mereka sudah ada masa — lebih masa daripada yang PH 1.0 pernah dapat. Akta Hasutan bukan sahaja dibiarkan dalam buku undang-undang — ia dipersenjatakan lebih agresif daripada di bawah Najib. AUKU bukan sahaja dikekalkan — pelajar yang membantah diabaikan. Pemisahan PG-PP bukan sahaja ditangguhkan — ia dibentangkan dan kemudian segera dihantar ke jawatankuasa untuk mati senyap. Setiap janji reformasi utama telah sama ada ditinggalkan, ditangguhkan, atau diterbalikkan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: PH 1.0 (2018-2020) mempunyai 22 bulan dan mencapai reformasi separa sebelum runtuh melalui Langkah Sheraton. PH 2.0 mempunyai 36 bulan dengan majoriti lebih besar dan mencapai lebih sedikit reformasi.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Kamu dipilih atas reformasi. Kamu menyampaikan ekonomi. Itu bukan perkara yang sama. BN juga menyampaikan ekonomi — selama berdekad — sambil melumpuhkan institusi. Kamu kini melakukan perkara yang sama dengan PR yang lebih baik. Mansuhkan Akta Hasutan. Mansuhkan AUKU. Ratifikasi ICCPR. Pisahkan PG dan PP. Pulihkan kemerdekaan SPRM. Atau berhenti berpura-pura kamu berbeza daripada orang yang kamu gantikan.
