[BM] Tiada Kerangka Akauntabiliti PRU16 — PH Tiada Sistem untuk Pengundi Jejak Kemajuan Manifesto

Said by: Governance Reform Advocates

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Tidak seperti beberapa negara demokrasi di mana kemajuan manifesto kerajaan dijejak secara terbuka, PH tidak mewujudkan sistem rasmi untuk pengundi memantau penyampaian terhadap kira-kira 60 janji yang dibuat dalam PRU15. Tiada papan pemuka awam, tiada laporan kemajuan manifesto tahunan, dan tiada mekanisme berstruktur untuk masyarakat sivil mempertanggungjawabkan kerajaan terhadap komitmen khususnya. Malaysiakini mewujudkan penjejak manifesto bebas kerana kerajaan tidak mahu. The Straits Times menjejak kemajuan PH 1.0 dan mendapati kebanyakan janji “masih jauh dari tercapai” selepas hanya satu tahun.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Demokrasi berfungsi atas akauntabiliti. Apabila pengundi tidak dapat menjejak penyampaian janji, ahli politik tidak menghadapi akibat untuk memungkiri komitmen. Manifesto menjadi dokumen pemasaran dan bukannya kontrak pentadbiran. Tanpa penjejakan berstruktur, janji yang dimungkiri lenyap ke dalam kitaran berita, dan menjelang PRU16, PH boleh membuat janji YANG SAMA sekali lagi — kerana tiada siapa mempunyai rekod jelas tentang apa yang disampaikan dan apa yang tidak. Ketiadaan sistem penjejakan bukan kesilapan — ia adalah strategi.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: Malaysiakini mewujudkan penjejak manifesto bebas kerana kerajaan tidak mahu. Pertubuhan masyarakat sivil menjejak janji kerana PH tidak mahu menjejak sendiri. Kerajaan yang yakin dengan penyampaiannya akan MENGALU-ALUKAN ketelusan — menerbitkan laporan kemajuan berkala, menjemput penelitian, dan mempertanggungjawabkan diri sendiri. Kesunyian PH mengenai penjejakan manifesto memberitahu anda segala-galanya: mereka tahu kad laporan itu memalukan, jadi mereka memastikan tiada kad laporan rasmi wujud. Apabila penjejak bebas menunjukkan majoriti janji tidak ditunaikan selepas tiga tahun, respons kerajaan bukan mempercepatkan penyampaian tetapi mengabaikan penjejak.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Pada 2019, The Straits Times menjejak kemajuan manifesto PH 1.0 dan mendapati kebanyakan janji “masih jauh dari tercapai” selepas hanya satu tahun. Penjejakan bebas PH 2.0 menunjukkan cerita serupa — pemisahan PG-PP tertangguh, AUKU masih utuh, Akta Hasutan diperluaskan, ICCPR tidak ditandatangani, tol masih dikutip, pilihan raya tempatan masih digantung. Coraknya: janji besar, tiada penjejakan, tiada akauntabiliti, kemudian janji baru pada pilihan raya seterusnya. Demokrasi Malaysia telah menjadi skim pengubahan manifesto — komitmen yang sama dikitar semula setiap lima tahun tanpa mekanisme mempertanggungjawabkan sesiapa untuk pusingan kegagalan sebelumnya.

🎯 TEGURAN PH: Terbitkan laporan kemajuan manifesto. Semak setiap satu daripada 60 janji anda dan beritahu rakyat Malaysia di mana setiap satu berdiri — disampaikan, sedang berjalan, ditangguhkan, atau ditinggalkan. Anda tidak akan — kerana laporan itu akan menjadi katalog komitmen yang dimungkiri. Kerajaan yang tidak mahu menjejak janjinya sendiri tidak berniat untuk menunaikannya. Pengundi layak mendapat lebih baik daripada kerja penipuan empat tahunan di mana janji yang sama dibuat, dimungkiri, dan dibuat semula dengan tiada siapa menyimpan markah.

Anwar's Signature Move: Whataboutism — 'But BN Did It Too' Is Not a Reform Agenda

Said by: Parliamentary Hansard / Media Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: A clear rhetorical pattern has emerged in Anwar Ibrahim’s parliamentary and public responses to criticism: deflect to what previous governments or the opposition did. When challenged on support letters for government tenders, he pointed to Kelantan. When challenged on lobbying, he brought up PKR’s past. When challenged on reform failures, he invokes the “mess we inherited.” This is systematic whataboutism deployed as governance strategy. The Star reported Anwar “calls out opposition’s hypocrisy over support letters” — turning a question about his own government’s conduct into an attack on the opposition. CSIS, in its “Death of Reformasi” analysis, noted how Anwar consistently deflects accountability by reframing criticism as opposition bad faith.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Whataboutism is the oldest trick in the political playbook — and it’s a dead giveaway that a leader has no substantive defence. When your answer to “why haven’t you reformed X?” is “well, BN also didn’t reform X,” you’re admitting you’re no better than the government you replaced. The entire purpose of changing governments is to get DIFFERENT outcomes. Voters didn’t elect PH to be equal to BN. They elected PH to be better. Every time Anwar deflects to BN or PN, he is implicitly admitting that his government hasn’t met its own standards.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: “They did it too” isn’t accountability — it’s collective guilt used as a shield. A PM who spends parliamentary time attacking the opposition’s record instead of defending his own is a PM without a record worth defending. When asked about support letters, the correct answer is: “Here is our procurement reform policy.” When asked about the Sedition Act, the correct answer is: “Here is our timeline for repeal.” Instead, Anwar’s answer is always: “But look at what they did.” This is not governance. It is debate-team deflection from a career politician who has mastered the art of answering a different question from the one asked.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Najib used the same tactic — when confronted about 1MDB, he pointed to Mahathir-era losses. Mahathir deflected to BN’s corruption when PH 1.0 failed to deliver. It’s a Malaysian political tradition: blame your predecessors forever, and never answer for your own record. Anwar was supposed to break this cycle. Instead, he has perfected it — adding rhetorical sophistication to the same underlying dodge. The whataboutism isn’t accidental; it is the centrepiece of PH’s parliamentary communication strategy.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Stop telling us what BN did. We KNOW what BN did — that’s why we voted them out. Start telling us what YOU’VE done. And when the answer is “the same things BN did” — support letters, political appointments, selective prosecution, stalled reforms — stop pretending you’re a reform government. You’re not. You’re BN with better PR and a more eloquent PM who uses five-dollar words to dodge ten-cent questions.

No GE16 Accountability Framework — PH Has No System for Voters to Track Manifesto Progress

Said by: Governance Reform Advocates

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Unlike some democracies where government manifesto progress is publicly tracked, PH has established no formal system for voters to monitor delivery against the approximately 60 promises made in GE15. There is no public dashboard, no annual manifesto progress report, and no structured mechanism for civil society to hold the government accountable to its specific commitments. Malaysiakini created an independent manifesto tracker because the government wouldn’t. The Straits Times tracked PH 1.0’s progress and found most promises “still had some way to go” after just one year.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Democracy functions on accountability. When voters can’t track promise delivery, politicians face no consequences for breaking commitments. The manifesto becomes a marketing document rather than a governance contract. Without structured tracking, broken promises disappear into the news cycle, and by GE16, PH can make the SAME promises again — because nobody has a clear record of what was delivered and what wasn’t. The absence of a tracking system is not an oversight — it is a strategy.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Malaysiakini created an independent manifesto tracker because the government wouldn’t. Civil society organisations track promises because PH won’t track itself. A government confident in its delivery would WELCOME transparency — publishing regular progress reports, inviting scrutiny, and holding itself accountable. PH’s silence on manifesto tracking tells you everything: they know the scorecard is damning, so they ensure no official scorecard exists. When independent trackers show the majority of promises unfulfilled after three years, the government’s response is not to accelerate delivery but to ignore the trackers.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: In 2019, the Straits Times tracked PH 1.0’s manifesto progress and found most promises “still had some way to go” after just one year. Independent tracking of PH 2.0 tells a similar story — AG-PP separation pending, UUCA intact, Sedition Act expanded, ICCPR unsigned, tolls still collected, local elections still suspended. The pattern: big promises, no tracking, no accountability, then new promises at the next election. Malaysian democracy has become a manifesto laundering scheme — the same commitments recycled every five years with no mechanism to hold anyone accountable for the previous round of failures.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Publish a manifesto progress report. Go through every one of your 60 promises and tell Malaysians where each one stands — delivered, in progress, delayed, or abandoned. You won’t — because the report would be a catalogue of broken commitments. A government that won’t track its own promises has no intention of keeping them. Voters deserve better than a quadrennial con job where the same promises are made, broken, and made again with nobody keeping score.

UMNO's Stranglehold on PH — Zahid Walks Free While Dictating Coalition Policy

Said by: Political Analysts / Opposition

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The single most corrosive dynamic in Malaysian politics today is UMNO’s outsized leverage over PH within the unity government coalition. DPM Ahmad Zahid Hamidi — who had 47 corruption charges dropped in an NFA decision that shocked the legal community — now effectively holds veto power over government policy. UMNO’s support is essential for PH’s parliamentary majority, and UMNO knows it. The result: Zahid walks free, Najib gets sentence reductions and house arrest bids, the AG-PP separation is stalled, DAP is sidelined in the cabinet reshuffle, and the anti-corruption agenda is functionally dead. UCA News described the unity coalition as having a “fractured mandate.” CNA reported Anwar faces a “graft crisis” with “PKR party fractures” and “PH coalition jitters.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This creates a toxic dynamic where Anwar cannot pursue reforms that threaten UMNO’s patronage networks, cannot hold UMNO politicians accountable for past misconduct, and cannot implement the institutional changes PH promised voters. Every major governance failure of the past three years — the Zahid NFA, Najib’s halved sentence, the stalled AG reform, the 238 political appointments, the cabinet reshuffle rewarding UMNO — traces back to one structural reality: UMNO has veto power, and UMNO uses it to protect its own.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH voters who marched for Reformasi got a government that is hostage to the very party they fought against for decades. Anwar’s defenders argue coalition politics requires compromise. But compromising on corruption and accountability isn’t pragmatism — it’s capitulation. The unity government isn’t a grand coalition; it’s UMNO’s rehabilitation programme funded by PH’s credibility. Anwar gets to be PM. UMNO gets its leaders cleared, its patronage restored, and its cabinet positions upgraded. The rakyat gets broken promises. The transaction is transparent to everyone except the people making it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has seen coalition dynamics corrupt governance before. Under BN, component parties traded silence on corruption for patronage allocation. Under Muhyiddin’s Perikatan Nasional, the Sheraton Move coalition was held together by ministerial appointments and GLC posts. Anwar’s unity government follows the same logic — the coalition survives not because it governs well, but because every partner gets something: UMNO gets legal protection, GPS gets Sabah/Sarawak autonomy rhetoric, DAP gets a seat at the table (increasingly a smaller one). The formula is identical across administrations. Only the brand changes.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You told voters UMNO was the enemy. Then you made UMNO your deputy. You told voters corruption must end. Then you dropped Zahid’s charges. You told voters Najib must pay. Then you halved his sentence. At what point does “coalition compromise” become “everything we fought against”? You passed that point when Zahid’s 47 charges were NFA’d. Every day you govern with UMNO holding veto power over reform is another day Reformasi dies — not with a bang, but with a coalition meeting.

[BM] Tawaran Tahanan Rumah Najib Ditolak — Mahkamah Putuskan Adendum Diraja Tidak Sah

Said by: High Court Judge Alice Loke / Najib Razak

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-22

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 22 Disember 2025, Mahkamah Tinggi KL memutuskan bahawa adendum diraja yang membenarkan bekas PM Najib Razak yang disabitkan kesalahan menjalani baki hukuman 1MDB di bawah tahanan rumah adalah tidak sah. Hakim Alice Loke berkata budi bicara Yang di-Pertuan Agong mesti dilaksanakan “demi kepentingan awam” dan mendapati adendum itu tidak mempunyai asas undang-undang yang kukuh. Al Jazeera dan Reuters kedua-duanya melaporkan keputusan tersebut. Sultan Selangor sebelum ini memberi amaran bahawa pengampunan tidak boleh “sewenang-wenangnya untuk pihak tertentu.” Kerajaan Anwar sudah pun memotong separuh hukuman Najib melalui Lembaga Pengampunan pada Februari 2024 — tawaran tahanan rumah adalah langkah seterusnya dalam corak kelonggaran yang semakin meningkat bagi seorang kleptokrat yang disabitkan kesalahan yang kini partinya menjadi sandaran Anwar.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Adendum tahanan rumah secara meluas dilihat sebagai perjanjian belakang tabir untuk mengekalkan UMNO dalam gabungan. Hukuman Najib mula-mula dipotong separuh (dari 12 ke 6 tahun), kemudian adendum tahanan rumah muncul sebagai cara untuk mengeluarkannya daripada penjara sepenuhnya sambil secara teknikal masih “menjalani” hukumannya. Penolakan mahkamah mendedahkan jentera politik di sebaliknya — ini bukan pengampunan berdasarkan merit, tetapi kelonggaran yang direka untuk pengurusan gabungan. Badan kehakiman mempertahankan garis yang eksekutif tidak mahu pertahankan. Keputusan Hakim Alice Loke adalah saat jarang kemerdekaan institusi dalam sistem di mana setiap semakan lain telah gagal.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: Anwar membina kerjaya politiknya menentang Najib. Beliau menghabiskan bertahun-tahun menyebut 1MDB sebagai skandal kewangan terbesar dalam sejarah Malaysia. Beliau menuntut akauntabiliti. Kemudian beliau mengambil kuasa, memerlukan undi UMNO, dan mula secara sistematik mengurangkan akibat bagi orang yang mencuri berbilion. Pertama, Lembaga Pengampunan memotong separuh hukuman. Kemudian, adendum tahanan rumah muncul. Jika mahkamah tidak campur tangan, Najib — yang disabitkan mencuri daripada rakyat Malaysia — akan tinggal di sebuah rumah agam sementara negara masih berhutang RM50 bilion daripada skandalnya. Orang yang menyebabkan hutang dapat rumah agam. Rakyat yang membayar hutang dapat kenaikan tol dan kenaikan harga diesel. Inilah Malaysia Madani Anwar.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia mempunyai sejarah bermasalah dengan pengampunan dan pengurangan hukuman untuk banduan yang mempunyai hubungan politik. Anwar sendiri mendapat manfaat daripada pengampunan diraja selepas PRU14 — yang penyokongnya berhujah adalah wajar memandangkan sifat bermotif politik dakwaan terhadapnya. Perbezaan dengan Najib ialah sabitan kesalahannya adalah untuk jenayah kewangan sebenar dengan kerugian berbilion yang didokumenkan. Memotong separuh hukumannya dan mencuba tahanan rumah menetapkan preseden: jika anda curi cukup banyak dan parti anda cukup berguna, akibatnya boleh dirunding. Amaran Sultan Selangor — bahawa pengampunan tidak boleh “sewenang-wenangnya untuk pihak tertentu” — adalah teguran diraja yang jarang berlaku yang tidak diendahkan oleh kerajaan.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Badan kehakiman menyelamatkan kamu daripada diri sendiri. Jika mahkamah tidak memutuskan adendum itu tidak sah, kamu akan menempatkan seorang kleptokrat yang disabitkan kesalahan di rumah agam sementara rakyat Malaysia membayar RM50 bilion untuk jenayahnya. Kamu sudah potong separuh hukumannya. Kamu sudah gugurkan 47 pertuduhan TPMnya. Berapa banyak lagi kelonggaran yang UMNO perlukan sebelum kamu akui Reformasi sudah mati dan kelangsungan gabungan sahaja yang penting? Mahkamah mempertahankan garis. Adakah kamu?

Najib House Arrest Bid Rejected — Court Rules Royal Addendum Invalid

Said by: High Court Judge Alice Loke / Najib Razak

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-22

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 22 December 2025, the KL High Court ruled that the royal addendum allowing convicted former PM Najib Razak to serve his remaining 1MDB sentence under house arrest was invalid. Judge Alice Loke said the King’s discretion must be exercised “for the public good” and found the addendum lacked proper legal basis. Al Jazeera and Reuters both reported the ruling. The Selangor Sultan had earlier warned that pardons should not be “arbitrary for certain parties.” Anwar’s government had already halved Najib’s sentence via the Pardons Board in February 2024 — the house arrest bid was the next step in an escalating pattern of leniency for a convicted kleptocrat whose party Anwar now depends on.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The house arrest addendum was widely seen as a backroom deal to keep UMNO in the coalition. Najib’s sentence was first halved (from 12 to 6 years), then the house arrest addendum emerged as a way to get him out of prison entirely while technically still “serving” his sentence. The court’s rejection exposed the political machinery behind it — this was not clemency based on merit, but leniency engineered for coalition management. The judiciary held the line that the executive would not. Judge Alice Loke’s ruling was a rare moment of institutional independence in a system where every other check has failed.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar built his political career opposing Najib. He spent years calling 1MDB the greatest financial scandal in Malaysian history. He demanded accountability. Then he took power, needed UMNO’s votes, and began systematically reducing the consequences for the man who stole billions. First, the Pardons Board halved the sentence. Then, the house arrest addendum appeared. If the court hadn’t intervened, Najib — convicted of stealing from the Malaysian people — would be living in a mansion while the country still owes RM50 billion from his scandal. The man who caused the debt gets a mansion. The rakyat who pays the debt gets toll hikes and diesel price increases. This is Anwar’s Malaysia Madani.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a troubled history with pardons and sentence reductions for politically connected convicts. Anwar himself benefited from a royal pardon after GE14 — which his supporters argued was justified given the politically motivated nature of his conviction. The difference with Najib is that his conviction was for an actual financial crime with billions in documented losses. Halving his sentence and attempting house arrest sets a precedent: if you steal enough and your party is useful enough, the consequences are negotiable. The Selangor Sultan’s warning — that pardons should not be “arbitrary for certain parties” — was a rare royal rebuke that went unheeded by the government.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: The judiciary saved you from yourself. If the court hadn’t ruled the addendum invalid, you would have put a convicted kleptocrat in a mansion while Malaysians pay RM50 billion for his crimes. You already halved his sentence. You already dropped his DPM’s 47 charges. How much more leniency does UMNO need before you admit that Reformasi is dead and coalition survival is all that matters? The court held the line. Will you?

Anwar melakukan tepat apa yang dikritiknya tentang pendahulunya.

Anwar Says 'Long Way to Go' on Elections in December 2025 — Now Preparing Snap GE16 for 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-17

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On December 17, 2025, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told editors and senior journalists at a briefing in Seri Perdana that he had ruled out any possibility of snap general elections in 2026. “The election still has a long way to go. I have not thought about it,” he said. “I am focusing on how we continue to implement reforms and alleviate the problems people face regarding the higher cost of living.”

By February 2026, less than two months later, speculation was mounting that GE16 could be called as early as July 2026. CNA reported that party insiders and civil servants confirmed preparations were underway. Reuters reported in March 2026 that coalition partners were already positioning themselves for snap polls.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This is a PM lying to the press about his election timeline. In December, he said elections were “a long way to go” and he hadn’t even thought about it. By February, his own party machinery was gearing up for a snap election. The current mandate doesn’t expire until February 2028 — calling elections nearly two years early is a major strategic decision that doesn’t happen overnight.

Anwar either lied to the press in December, or he made a radical strategic shift in under two months. Neither explanation inspires confidence. If he lied, it’s calculated deception of the public. If he genuinely hadn’t considered it, then the snap election push was driven by panic over the Azam Baki scandal, the PKR internal fractures, and coalition instability — not strategic planning.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

“I have not thought about it.” That’s what the PM said in December. The same PM who is the chairman of his party, the head of his coalition, and a career politician who has been thinking about elections every day for 30 years.

Of course he was thinking about it. The Sabah election in November 2025 — where PH was routed — had already forced a strategic reckoning. The PKR party election mess, Rafizi’s departure, and the cabinet reshuffle were all moves that only make sense if you’re positioning for an early general election.

Anwar told journalists what they wanted to hear — “focus on reforms, focus on cost of living” — while his political operation was already calculating seat projections and coalition arithmetic. This is not a man who hasn’t thought about elections. This is a man who doesn’t want you to know he’s thinking about elections.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Every Malaysian PM who has called snap elections has denied planning them right up until the announcement. Najib denied planning GE14 until he dissolved Parliament. Muhyiddin denied planning the Melaka and Johor state elections until they were called. It’s a Malaysian political tradition: lie about election timing, then call elections when it’s strategically advantageous.

Anwar is doing exactly what he criticised his predecessors for doing. The reformist is following the BN playbook.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You told the press in December that elections were “a long way to go.” Two months later, your coalition is gearing up for snap polls. Either you lied in December or you’re making major democratic decisions on the fly. Neither is acceptable for a PM who claims to be different from his predecessors. If you’re going to call an early election, at least be honest about why — don’t pretend it’s about reform when it’s about survival.

Hannah Yeoh Appointment Sparks Race-Based Backlash — PAS and UMNO Both Object

Said by: Takiyuddin Hassan, PAS Secretary-General

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-17

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In December 2025, Anwar appointed Hannah Yeoh as Federal Territories Minister in his cabinet reshuffle. PAS Secretary-General Takiyuddin Hassan immediately objected — not to Yeoh’s qualifications, but to her race. He argued the appointment would “strengthen DAP’s influence” in urban areas and disadvantage Malays. Rightist Malay groups warned that DAP was now “controlling Malay landholding” through the Federal Territories portfolio, which oversees KL, Putrajaya, and Labuan. Even UMNO leaders quietly raised concerns. Anwar hit back: “To reject a person because of her colour and race, this is atrocious in this day and age.” Social media reactions were polarised.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The backlash exposes how normalised ethno-religious gatekeeping remains in Malaysian politics — even under a supposedly reformist government. A minister was judged not by competence, track record, or policy vision, but by ethnicity. This is the structural racism PH was elected to dismantle. Instead, the party that appointed Yeoh simultaneously downgraded other DAP portfolios in the same reshuffle to signal “Malay strength” to voters. Yeoh’s appointment was brave — but it was also the exception that proved the rule. The rest of the reshuffle was racial calculus.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The contradiction is structural: Anwar wants to appear multiracial while managing a coalition that requires Malay-first optics. He appoints one Chinese-Malaysian woman to a prominent portfolio, then compensates by downgrading DAP elsewhere and elevating UMNO ministers. The multiracial gesture is real but it is hedged — designed to be just bold enough for DAP voters, but not so bold that Malay voters feel threatened. Analyst Azmi Hassan said the reshuffle “can only be read as sending a signal to Malays that Anwar is pushing for stronger Malay presence in government.” Yeoh’s appointment is the progressive leaf on an ethno-nationalist tree.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every DAP appointment to a senior portfolio triggers the same race-based backlash. When Lim Guan Eng became Finance Minister in PH 1.0, Malay groups protested. When DAP won state seats, opponents framed it as Chinese encroachment. The cycle is depressingly predictable: appoint a non-Malay, face racial backlash, either stand firm (briefly) or retreat. Anwar stood firm on Yeoh — which is commendable — but his overall cabinet strategy still operates within the racial framework rather than dismantling it. A government that makes one multiracial appointment while conducting the rest of its governance on racial lines is not reforming race relations — it is managing optics.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Defending Hannah Yeoh’s appointment was the right thing to do. Now defend the principle behind it — that competence matters more than ethnicity — across your ENTIRE government. Stop making one multiracial appointment while conducting racial calculus everywhere else. If you believe race shouldn’t determine cabinet positions, prove it with every appointment — not just the one that gets you international praise. And if PAS objects because a minister is Chinese, call it what it is: racism. Not “cultural concern,” not “community sensitivity” — racism. You said it was “atrocious.” Now act like you mean it.
