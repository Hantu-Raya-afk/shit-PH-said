Anwar Invokes 'Zionists' and 'Bankers' in Parliament to Smear Daim's Widow — Lawyers Call It an 'Outright Lie'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 What Happened

On March 3, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told the Dewan Rakyat that the alleged plot to destabilise his government — attributed to Na’imah Abdul Khalid, the widow of former Finance Minister Daim Zainuddin — involved “prominent Zionist groups” and international bankers. He claimed a UK-based PR firm had been engaged to launch a coordinated attack on national institutions. This came one day after MalaysiaNow revealed that PKR leaders, including figures close to Anwar, had themselves engaged a US consultant in 2016 to build White House pressure on then-PM Najib Razak. Na’imah’s lawyers, Rajesh Nagarajan and Sachpreetraj Singh, issued a statement the same day calling Anwar’s claims “false, preposterous, outrageous, and a blatant attempt to mislead Parliament.”

🔥 Why It Matters

Anwar used the most incendiary term in Malaysian political vocabulary — “Zionist” — under parliamentary immunity, where he cannot be sued. This is not casual rhetoric. In a Muslim-majority country where anti-Zionism carries enormous emotional weight, invoking Zionists to discredit a political opponent is a deliberate weaponisation of public sentiment. It is the same playbook PH once accused UMNO and PAS of running: using fear and identity to distract from institutional accountability. The timing is critical — Anwar is under pressure over the MACC abuse allegations, Bloomberg exposés, and growing calls from within his own coalition for real reform. The “Zionist plot” narrative reframes domestic accountability demands as foreign sabotage.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Na’imah’s lawyers stated unequivocally: “There is not one word about Zionism or Israel or anything related to it in the entire communications plan.” The PR document, they said, was about reassuring bankers of the Daim family’s financial viability — standard crisis communications for a family facing asset seizure. Anwar fabricated the Zionist angle in Parliament, where he enjoys legal immunity, knowing full well he would not repeat it outside. Meanwhile, his own party engaged foreign consultants to lobby against a sitting Malaysian prime minister in 2016. The hypocrisy is not subtle — it is structural. PKR engaging foreign firms to pressure Putrajaya was political strategy. Na’imah engaging a PR firm to protect her family’s banking relationships is a Zionist plot. The difference? One serves Anwar’s interests. The other threatens them.

📜 Previous Cases

Anwar has a long history of invoking conspiracy when cornered. During the MACC crisis in February-March 2026, he accused critics of being part of a plot by “wealthy elites” manipulating media. He dismissed Bersih’s call for MACC accountability as “unfair.” He told the public not to act as “prosecutor or judge” during the KK Mart firebombing crisis. The “foreign plot” narrative is a classic authoritarian deflection — and it is the exact tactic Najib used during the 1MDB scandal, when he blamed foreign media, George Soros, and opposition sabotage for the exposés. Anwar spent a decade mocking that playbook. Now he is running it.

🎯 PH Callout

You campaigned on transparency, accountability, and institutional reform. You told Malaysians that the days of smearing critics as foreign agents were over. You mocked Najib for blaming Soros. You mocked Mahathir for blaming “foreign speculators.” And now, when a widow’s lawyers publish a PR document that contains zero references to Zionism, you stand in Parliament — protected by immunity — and invoke “Zionists” and “bankers” to discredit her. You are not defending Malaysia from foreign interference. You are using the most loaded term in the country’s political lexicon to shut down legitimate scrutiny of your government’s conduct. That is not reform. That is not even competent spin. That is Najib-era politics with a reformasi sticker on it.

Anwar Condemns US-Israel Strikes on Iran in Parliament — 'We Can No Longer Swallow' Western Human Rights Lectures, PH MP Calls for US Embassy Boycott

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Syahredzan Johan (PH-Bangi)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 2 March 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim tabled an emergency motion in the Dewan Rakyat condemning the US and Israel’s strikes on Iran, which began on 28 February and killed at least 201 people including Iran’s Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei.

Anwar called the strikes a “flagrant violation of international law” and described Western lectures on human rights and democracy as hypocrisy:

“In my engagements with the American and European leadership, I am uneasy about their lectures on human rights and democracy. We can no longer swallow such arguments from them.”

The motion passed unanimously with bipartisan support. Opposition Leader Hamzah Zainudin praised Anwar for a “stern and firm statement.”

But the most politically significant moment came from PH backbencher Syahredzan Johan (Bangi), who called on all MPs to boycott events hosted by the US embassy in Kuala Lumpur:

“Let’s not go. Even though we get invited to their Independence Day celebrations, let’s not go. We need to show that we can no longer accept what they do.”

Anwar also signaled a diplomatic repositioning toward Iran, calling for closer education and trade ties, claiming Malaysia was “the only country to show a firm, brave stance.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

The condemnation itself is unremarkable — Malaysia has consistently supported Palestinian and Iranian sovereignty. What matters is the escalation in rhetoric and the gap between words and actions.

First, Anwar calling Western human rights lectures “hypocrisy” is rich coming from a prime minister whose own government is under fire for:

  • Protecting MACC chief Azam Baki despite multiple corruption allegations
  • Using police investigations to target critics under the guise of a “Zionist plot”
  • Halving Najib’s sentence while the 1MDB debt still runs into billions
  • Failing to pass even basic institutional reforms like the PM tenure bill Anwar is lecturing the West about hypocrisy while running a government that has abandoned virtually every reform promise it made.

Second, Syahredzan’s call to boycott the US embassy is performative populism with real diplomatic consequences. Malaysia’s economy depends on US trade and investment. The US is Malaysia’s third-largest trading partner. Calling for a diplomatic boycott from the floor of Parliament is not a principled stand — it’s cheap political theatre that someone else will have to clean up.

Third, the pivot toward Iran is diplomatically reckless. Iran’s Shia denomination is officially considered “deviant” in Sunni-majority Malaysia. Anwar is signaling solidarity with a theocratic state whose religious identity his own government criminalises. The contradiction is absurd.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Let’s count the contradictions:

On human rights hypocrisy:

  • Anwar says he can “no longer swallow” Western lectures on human rights. This is the same man who spent years lobbying Western governments, NGOs, and media outlets for his own human rights — during his imprisonment under Mahathir and Najib. His entire political comeback was built on international sympathy and foreign advocacy.

  • Now that he’s in power, foreign advocacy is a “Zionist plot” and Western human rights standards are “hypocrisy.” The only thing that changed is which side of the PM’s desk he sits on. On the US embassy boycott:

  • Syahredzan Johan is a PH backbencher calling for a diplomatic boycott while his own government maintains full diplomatic, trade, and military relations with the US. Malaysia just signed a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership with Washington in October 2025.

  • If PH is serious about opposing US actions, why stop at embassy cocktail parties? Cancel the trade deal. Recall the ambassador. Oh wait — that would cost money. On Iran solidarity:

  • Malaysia criminalises Shia practice. Anwar’s own religious affairs apparatus treats Shia Muslims as deviants. Declaring solidarity with Iran while persecuting its co-religionists at home is not principled — it’s opportunistic.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

  • 2018-2022: Anwar’s own foreign lobbying. Anwar and PKR allies engaged Washington consultancies, international media, and foreign governments to pressure the Najib and Muhyiddin administrations. He accepted this support gratefully. Now foreign engagement is subversion.
  • October 2025: Malaysia-US Comprehensive Strategic Partnership. Just five months before calling US human rights lectures “hypocrisy,” Anwar’s government signed an upgraded bilateral deal with Washington covering trade, critical minerals, and market access.
  • Malaysia’s track record on Iran. Malaysia has historically been cautious with Iran due to the Sunni-Shia divide. Anwar’s pivot is a departure from decades of careful diplomatic hedging, driven by the emotional politics of the moment rather than strategic calculation.
  • 2024-2025: Gaza solidarity without substance. Malaysia has been vocal on Palestine but has taken no meaningful economic or diplomatic action against Israel beyond rhetoric. The pattern is clear: loud words, zero follow-through.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

Anwar, you told Parliament: “We can no longer swallow such arguments from them.”

Fair enough. But the rakyat can no longer swallow yours either.

You lecture the West on hypocrisy while your MACC chief sits on RM800,000 in undisclosed shares. You call foreign advocacy a “Zionist plot” after spending decades doing exactly the same thing. You signal solidarity with Iran while your religious authorities criminalise Shia practice at home.

And your backbencher calls for boycotting US embassy parties — the absolute lowest-cost, lowest-risk form of protest imaginable — while your government maintains a full strategic partnership with Washington.

This isn’t principled foreign policy. It’s Ramadan-season populism. You’re performing outrage for domestic consumption while doing nothing that would actually cost you anything.

If you want to be taken seriously on human rights and sovereignty, start at home. Fix your own institutions. Reform your own enforcement agencies. Stop using conspiracy theories to silence critics. Then — and only then — will anyone take your lectures to the West seriously.

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

The emails included an 84-page dossier making serious allegations against Najib, with the goal of getting Washington to publicly rebuke the Malaysian government.

Former PKR vice-president Sivarasa Rasiah confirmed the communications, saying party leaders including himself were involved. He framed it as legitimate advocacy to free Anwar Ibrahim, who was then a political prisoner.

The revelation came as Anwar’s government was simultaneously accusing former finance minister Daim Zainuddin’s family of using foreign agents to destabilise the current administration — calling it a national security threat.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This exposes a massive double standard at the heart of PH’s current political strategy. Anwar is framing Daim’s family’s alleged foreign lobbying as treasonous — a plot to topple the government using foreign influence. But his own party did the exact same thing when they were in opposition.

Opposition MP Shahidan Kassim pressed Anwar in Parliament to explain the difference. The answer matters because the government is using the Daim case to justify expanded national security rhetoric, deflect from the Azam Baki scandal, and paint critics as foreign agents. If PKR’s own lobbying was legitimate, then the same standard must apply to others.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Sivarasa’s defence — “we were trying to free a political prisoner” — is convenient but doesn’t hold up under scrutiny. The 84-page dossier didn’t just ask for Anwar’s release. It made sweeping allegations against Najib and explicitly sought US intervention in Malaysian domestic politics. That’s foreign lobbying. Period.

When PKR does it, it’s “advocacy.” When Daim’s family allegedly does it, it’s a “Zionist conspiracy” and a national security threat. The double standard is so blatant that even a casual observer can see through it.

Anwar’s government is essentially saying: foreign lobbying is patriotic when we do it, and treasonous when our opponents do it. That’s not a principle — it’s a convenience.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Malaysian political parties have a long history of international lobbying. UMNO maintained Washington lobbyists for decades. Anwar himself cultivated deep relationships with US policymakers, think tanks, and media during his years in opposition. None of this was considered problematic when PH was the beneficiary.

The selective outrage follows the same pattern as PH’s treatment of press freedom (Bloomberg good when exposing Najib, Bloomberg bad when exposing Azam Baki) and institutional accountability (MACC is weaponised under BN, MACC is independent under PH).

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You hired a US firm to lobby Washington against a sitting Malaysian PM, produced an 84-page dossier of allegations, and sought American intervention in Malaysian politics. Your own former vice-president confirmed it. Now you’re telling Malaysians that when others do the same thing, it’s a Zionist-backed conspiracy to topple the government? Pick a lane. Either foreign lobbying is a legitimate part of political advocacy — in which case stop criminalising your opponents for it — or it’s a national security threat, in which case PKR’s own leaders should be investigated too. You can’t have it both ways.

Anwar was responding to an opposition MP who asked if ministers would be forced to resign for poor performance. His reply: “Accountability is not only about punishment but also correction, continuous improvement, and the moral responsibility ministers held for the people.” No KPIs have ever been publicly disclosed for any minister in Anwar’s cabinet. 🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Claiming 100% KPI compliance without disclosing the KPIs is the corporate equivalent of giving yourself a perfect performance review and refusing to show the form. Given that 77% of Malaysians report struggling with cost of living, university rankings are dropping, the CPI is stuck at 1MDB-era levels, and reform promises remain unmet, the claim is laughable on its face. Transparency means publishing the KPIs, the methodology, and the results — and letting the public judge. “Trust me, everyone is great” is not reform governance.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: A PM who holds both the PM and Finance Minister portfolios is essentially grading himself on fiscal management. There is no independent check on the most powerful office in the country. The Najib government also claimed ministers were performing. Singapore publishes ministerial performance metrics and links them to public service delivery targets. South Korea conducts annual government performance evaluations with published results. Malaysia’s approach under the “reformist” PM: trust his word. The Merdeka Center’s February 2026 poll showed public satisfaction with economic management at 38% — suggesting that whatever KPIs exist, the public disagrees they are being met.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian PM has claimed their cabinet is performing well. Najib claimed his ministers delivered results — while 1MDB bled billions. Muhyiddin claimed pandemic management was effective — while thousands died and billions in stimulus were unaccounted for. The pattern is universal: self-assessment without external verification always produces perfect scores. Anwar promised to be different. He isn’t. The KPI charade is a bipartisan tradition that PH has enthusiastically adopted.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Publish the KPIs. Go through every minister, every metric, every target, and every result. Put it online. Let the rakyat see whether their Education Minister’s KPIs include declining PISA scores, whether the Health Minister’s KPIs include 33,000 contract doctors still waiting for permanent posts, whether the Economy Minister’s KPIs include 77% of Malaysians struggling with costs. You won’t — because the scorecard would be devastating. A government that grades itself in secret has no credibility claiming accountability.

Date: 2026-02-21

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 21 February 2026, Penang CM Chow Kon Yeow announced that DAP would hold a special congress on 12 July 2026 to decide the party’s role in Anwar’s government. Chow insisted DAP was ’not withdrawing support from the Madani government’ but was discussing ‘whether to continue holding ministerial posts and certain positions.’ DAP’s 40 MPs would continue supporting the government. All members, including leaders, must abide by the congress decision.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: DAP is threatening to leave government without actually leaving government. The special congress is political theatre — a way to pressure Anwar for better terms while maintaining the safety of coalition membership. If DAP genuinely had policy disagreements, they’d articulate them publicly. Instead, they’re holding an internal vote on ‘whether to hold ministerial posts’ — which is a negotiation tactic dressed up as democratic deliberation.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: This is the political equivalent of threatening to quit your job while continuing to cash the paycheck. DAP wants credit for independence (‘we’re questioning our role!’) while enjoying the benefits of power (‘we’re not actually leaving!’). The special congress gives DAP negotiating leverage for the next cabinet reshuffle without any real risk. If the congress votes to stay — which it will — DAP gets to say ’the members decided.’ If they vote to leave cabinet — which they won’t — it would collapse the government. Both outcomes benefit DAP; neither benefits the rakyat.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: DAP has a history of internal drama over its role in coalition governments. In the Mahathir 2.0 era, DAP faced similar tensions over compromises with PPBM. The pattern is consistent: DAP enters coalitions, compromises on principles, faces internal backlash, holds dramatic internal events, then stays. The cycle repeats because the alternative — opposition — is worse for the party apparatus.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: If you have policy disagreements with the government, state them publicly. If you think the coalition isn’t delivering for your voters, say why. But don’t hold a special congress to ‘decide your role’ while simultaneously promising to keep supporting the government. That’s not democratic deliberation — it’s a negotiating ploy. The rakyat can see through it.
