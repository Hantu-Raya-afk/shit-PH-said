Jho Low STILL Free After 8 Years — Malaysia's Most Wanted Fugitive Living Comfortably Somewhere

Said by: Interpol / MACC / DOJ

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As of 2025, Low Taek Jho (Jho Low) — the central figure in the 1MDB scandal — remains free despite Interpol red notices, international arrest warrants, and charges in multiple jurisdictions. Three different Malaysian governments have failed to secure his extradition. Anwar made 1MDB accountability his signature promise, yet has made no visible progress in locating or extraditing Jho Low. Reports suggest he has been residing in China or Macau, but the Malaysian government has not disclosed any diplomatic efforts to secure his return.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Jho Low orchestrated the theft of billions from a Malaysian sovereign wealth fund. He is the most wanted fugitive in Malaysian history. His freedom — eight years after charges were filed — represents the single largest accountability failure in Malaysian governance. Every day Jho Low remains free is a day the rakyat services debt he helped create while he lives comfortably beyond the reach of Malaysian law. If Malaysia cannot bring its most-wanted kleptocrat to justice, its entire anti-corruption framework is performative.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar spent years demanding 1MDB accountability. “Where is Jho Low?” was a PH rallying cry. Three years into government, the answer remains the same: somewhere comfortable, apparently beyond Anwar’s reach. The US DOJ recovered over $1 billion in 1MDB-linked assets. Singapore jailed bankers. Switzerland sanctioned institutions. Malaysia — the country that was robbed — has not managed to bring the principal orchestrator to trial. If the political will existed, diplomatic pressure on China (where Jho Low is believed to reside) would be a priority. It clearly isn’t.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under Najib, the 1MDB investigation was actively suppressed — the AG was fired, MACC officers were transferred, and Jho Low was protected. Under Muhyiddin, investigations continued but Jho Low remained free. Under Anwar, investigations concluded with settlements and Najib’s reduced sentence, but Jho Low’s extradition has not progressed. The pattern suggests that every Malaysian government has found it politically convenient to talk about catching Jho Low while doing nothing that would actually bring him back.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: “Where is Jho Low?” — you asked that question for five years. Now you’re the PM. You control the diplomatic apparatus. You have Interpol red notices. You have international cooperation frameworks. And Jho Low is still free. Disclose what diplomatic steps you’ve taken. Publish the extradition requests. Pressure China publicly if necessary. Or admit that catching Jho Low was always a slogan, never a priority — and that the biggest kleptocrat in Malaysian history will die free because no government cared enough to bring him home.

Sabah and Sarawak Autonomy Demands Still Unmet — East Malaysia Remains the Forgotten Stepchild

Said by: Sabah & Sarawak Leaders / GPS

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Despite 2021 constitutional amendments restoring Sabah and Sarawak’s status as equal partners in the Federation, East Malaysia remains structurally marginalised. Sabah is Malaysia’s poorest state. MA63 (Malaysia Agreement 1963) recommendations are gathering dust. Oil and gas royalty disputes remain unresolved. Equal partnership exists on paper but not in budget allocation, infrastructure investment, or political representation. Sarawak’s GPS maintains leverage through coalition politics, but Sabah — where PH won only 1 of 20 seats in the 2025 state election — has almost no bargaining power.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Sabah and Sarawak contributed the resources — oil, gas, timber, land — that built Malaysia’s modern economy. In return, they received the worst infrastructure, healthcare, education, and connectivity in the country. The MA63 framework guarantees equal treatment that has never been delivered. When Sabah remains the poorest state despite sitting on some of Malaysia’s richest natural resources, the federal system has failed East Malaysia. Every year the disparities persist is another year the federation’s promise is broken.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar promised to honour MA63 and treat Sabah and Sarawak as equal partners. Three years later, the specific demands — increased oil royalties, control over immigration, education autonomy, infrastructure investment — remain unresolved. The constitutional amendments were symbolic victories that changed no budget line items. Sabah still has the highest poverty rate. Sarawak still has the worst road infrastructure. Both still have the weakest healthcare coverage. Symbolic equal partnership without fiscal equal partnership is not partnership — it is a rebrand.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, East Malaysian states were coalition partners who received patronage in exchange for parliamentary seats — but never structural reform. Under PH 1.0, a committee was formed to study MA63. Under PH 2.0, the committee’s recommendations remain largely unimplemented. Under every government, the pattern is the same: promise East Malaysian autonomy, study the issue, delay implementation, and rely on coalition arithmetic to avoid accountability. Sixty years after Malaysia Agreement, the agreement’s terms remain unfulfilled.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You amended the Constitution to restore equal partner status. Then you failed to follow through with equal budgets, equal infrastructure, and equal services. Sabah is still the poorest state. Sarawak still has the worst roads. Both still have the worst healthcare access. Constitutional amendments without fiscal commitments are empty gestures. Increase federal allocation to East Malaysia proportional to their resource contribution. Resolve oil royalty disputes. Deliver infrastructure on par with Peninsular Malaysia. Or keep calling them “equal partners” while treating them like subsidiaries.

Rosmah Acquitted of All 17 Money Laundering Charges — PH Politicians Who Spent a Decade Attacking Her Go Silent

Said by: PH Politicians (Tony Pua, Rafizi Ramli, et al.) / High Court

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-12-19

📌 What Happened

On December 19, 2024, High Court judge K. Muniandy acquitted and discharged Datin Seri Rosmah Mansor — wife of jailed former PM Najib Razak — of all 17 charges against her in a money laundering and tax evasion case involving RM7.09 million. The judge ruled the prosecution failed to present key elements of the offence. Rosmah had been charged in October 2018 with 12 counts of money laundering and 5 counts of failing to declare income. MalaysiaNow contacted multiple PH leaders for comment — almost all refused. Tony Pua, who had sarcastically mocked Najib just two months earlier, did not answer calls. Rafizi Ramli, PH’s most vocal anti-corruption crusader, also stayed silent.

🔥 Why It Matters

Rosmah was PH’s ultimate political piñata — the living symbol of BN excess, corruption, and impunity. For over a decade, PH leaders from Anwar to Pua to Rafizi used her name, her handbags, and her alleged crimes to rally voters and justify their existence as a reform movement. When the court acquitted her, the silence was deafening. Not a single senior PH leader issued a statement demanding accountability, questioning the prosecution’s failure, or committing to appeal. This silence tells you everything about what PH’s anti-corruption crusade was always about: opposition theatre, not institutional reform.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH spent years telling Malaysians that Rosmah was the face of kleptocracy. They won elections on that narrative. Now that they’re in government — with control over the AGC, the police, and prosecution strategy — they can’t even muster a press statement when the case collapses? If the prosecution was weak, whose fault is that? PH has been in power since November 2022. They had two years to ensure cases were airtight. Instead, key witnesses reportedly died or went missing, and the AGC later dropped the appeal entirely. This isn’t just silence — it’s complicity in the collapse of accountability.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows the exact pattern of Najib’s pardon in February 2024, where the Pardons Board halved his sentence while Anwar served as PM. It follows the dropping of 47 corruption charges against DPM Zahid Hamidi in September 2023. Every major BN-era corruption case under Anwar’s watch has either been weakened, settled, or collapsed — and every time, PH’s former attack dogs go quiet. The anti-corruption movement that defined Malaysian politics for a decade is being dismantled by the very people who built it.

🎯 PH Callout

You built Pakatan Harapan on the promise that Malaysia deserved better than Najib and Rosmah. You told voters that justice would be served. Now Rosmah walks free, your own prosecutors fumbled the case, and not one of you has the spine to explain what went wrong. If this is what ‘Reformasi’ looks like in practice — silent acceptance when your political enemies go free on your watch — then you have become exactly what you promised to replace.

Garis peminggiran di Malaysia didokumentasikan dengan baik: kuota etnik dalam pendidikan dan pekerjaan sektor awam, ketidaksamaan jantina dalam hak undang-undang, penumpuan kekayaan di kalangan elit yang berhubungan politik, dan peminggiran Malaysia Timur. PH berjanji menangani semua ini. Penilaian Asia Maior mengesyorkan tiada satu pun yang dihadapi secara substantif.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Anwar membina kerjaya politiknya atas mencabar struktur yang tepat yang dikenal pasti Asia Maior sebagai tidak disentuh. Reformasi sepatutnya bermakna mereformasi sistem naungan etnik, membuka wacana awam, dan mewujudkan Malaysia berdasarkan merit bukan identiti. Tiga tahun dalam kerajaan, wacana tidak dibuka — ia diuruskan. Sistem naungan tidak dibongkar — ia dijenamakan semula. Pendekatan PH terhadap peminggiran adalah sama dengan BN: akui dalam ucapan, elak dalam dasar, dan harap ekonomi tumbuh cukup pantas supaya tiada siapa perasan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Setiap penilaian akademik Malaysia mengikut trajektori yang serupa. Sarjana antarabangsa mencatat jurang antara retorik dan realiti PH dengan ketepatan yang semakin meningkat. Yayasan Rosa Luxemburg mendokumentasikan “rasuah, jurang kaum dan rasa tidak puas hati ekonomi.” East Asia Forum mendokumentasikan aspirasi reformasi bercanggah dengan realiti politik. Asia Maior mendokumentasikan pengelakan wacana terbuka. Setiap penilaian datang dari sudut yang berbeza tetapi mencapai kesimpulan yang sama: PH bercakap reformasi tetapi mengamalkan tadbir urus status quo.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Jurnal akademik mendokumentasikan apa yang rakyat Malaysia sudah tahu: retorik reformasi anda tidak sepadan dengan realiti tadbir urus anda. “Pelbagai garis peminggiran” kekal tidak ditangani kerana menanganinya akan merugikan anda secara politik. Buka wacana yang Anwar janjikan. Arahkan semakan bebas kuota etnik, pengambilan sektor awam dan pengagihan kekayaan. Terbitkan data. Biar rakyat Malaysia berdebat penemuan. Atau terus mengelak perbualan sementara sarjana mendokumentasikan pengelakan anda — dan saksikan mandat reformasi menguap.
