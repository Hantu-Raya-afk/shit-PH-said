Asia Maior: Anwar Sidelines 'Open Discussion on Multiple Lines of Disenfranchisement'

Said by: Asia Maior Academic Journal

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late 2024, the academic journal Asia Maior published an assessment of Anwar Ibrahim’s governance, concluding that he faced challenges “without substantially interrogating a public discourse that has historically sidelined open discussion on multiple socio-economic lines of disenfranchisement.” The analysis documented how Anwar’s reformist rhetoric coexisted with a status quo reality — maintaining the same patronage structures, ethnic frameworks, and institutional arrangements that he had spent decades promising to dismantle.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When an academic journal assesses that your government avoids “open discussion on multiple lines of disenfranchisement,” it is saying that you have chosen political safety over substantive reform. The lines of disenfranchisement in Malaysia are well-documented: ethnic quotas in education and public sector employment, gender inequality in legal rights, wealth concentration among politically connected elites, and East Malaysian marginalisation. PH promised to address all of these. Asia Maior’s assessment suggests none have been substantively confronted.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar built his political career on challenging the very structures Asia Maior identifies as untouched. Reformasi was supposed to mean reforming the ethnic patronage system, opening public discourse, and creating a Malaysia based on merit rather than identity. Three years into government, the discourse hasn’t opened — it has been managed. The patronage system hasn’t been dismantled — it has been rebranded. PH’s approach to disenfranchisement is identical to BN’s: acknowledge it in speeches, avoid it in policy, and hope the economy grows fast enough that nobody notices.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian academic assessment follows a similar trajectory. International scholars note the gap between PH’s rhetoric and reality with increasing precision. The Rosa Luxemburg Foundation documented “corruption, racial divides, and economic discontent.” East Asia Forum documented reform aspirations clashing with political realities. Asia Maior documented the avoidance of open discourse. Each assessment comes from a different angle but reaches the same conclusion: PH talks reform but practices status quo governance.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Academic journals are documenting what Malaysians already know: your reform rhetoric doesn’t match your governance reality. “Multiple lines of disenfranchisement” remain unaddressed because addressing them would cost you politically. Open the discourse Anwar promised. Commission an independent review of ethnic quotas, public sector hiring, and wealth distribution. Publish the data. Let Malaysians debate the findings. Or keep avoiding the conversation while scholars document your avoidance — and watch the reform mandate evaporate.

Rosa Luxemburg Foundation: 'Corruption, Racial Divides, Economic Discontent' Under Anwar

Said by: Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late 2024, the Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung (RLS), a German political foundation, published a detailed assessment of Malaysia under Anwar Ibrahim. The report concluded: “High-level corruption persists, rising racial tensions with politicians exploiting sensitive issues, government response focused on managing incidents through archaic laws rather than promoting open dialogue.” RLS documented how the Anwar government maintained the same patronage structures and ethnic frameworks it had promised to dismantle, while using repressive laws to manage dissent rather than addressing root causes.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When an international political foundation with no partisan stake in Malaysian politics describes your government as maintaining “high-level corruption” and managing racial tensions through “archaic laws,” it is a clinical diagnosis of governance failure. RLS is not an opposition mouthpiece — it is an academic institution that analyses political economies globally. Its assessment aligns with HRW, Amnesty International, Asia Maior, and East Asia Forum — a consensus among international observers that PH’s reform agenda has been abandoned.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s defence against every international assessment is the same: “they don’t understand Malaysian context.” But when five different international institutions — from different continents, with different methodologies — all reach the same conclusion, the problem is not their understanding. It is your governance. RLS specifically noted that the government’s response is to manage incidents rather than promote open dialogue. That’s not reform — it’s crowd control. Managing racial tensions through the Sedition Act is not addressing racism. It is suppressing the conversation while the underlying problems fester.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: International assessments of Malaysian governance have followed a consistent arc under PH 2.0. HRW: “Reform Agenda Stalled” (2024), “Rights Backslide” (2025). Amnesty: “Betrayed reform pledges” (2024). RSF: 34-place drop in press freedom (2024). Asia Maior: avoids “open discussion on multiple lines of disenfranchisement.” East Asia Forum: “reform aspirations clashed with political realities.” RLS: “corruption, racial divides, economic discontent.” Each assessment arrives independently at the same destination: PH talks reform but governs like BN.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: International observers have reached a consensus: your reform agenda has been abandoned. Corruption persists. Racial tensions are managed through archaic laws, not addressed through open dialogue. Economic discontent is growing. You can dismiss one critical report. You cannot dismiss five from five different institutions on five different continents. Either recommit to the reform agenda that got you elected — or accept that the international community has classified your government as what it is: a continuation of the status quo with better English.

Anwar's Overseas Trips: 75% Funded by Private Companies — Then Government Says Actually No

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister (and Fahmi Fadzil)

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-11-22

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In November 2024, Anwar told Parliament that private companies like Petronas and Proton covered approximately 75% of his overseas travel costs under a new cost-sharing strategy. The next day, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil “clarified” that corporate executives “paid their own way” while the government covered Anwar’s delegation. The government had spent RM1.6 million to charter a plane for a four-country trip. When questioned further, Anwar called it a “crazy era” that transparency was being punished. CNA analysts warned the practice “could expose vulnerabilities in Malaysia’s political financing framework.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The PM said one thing in Parliament — private companies covered 75% of travel costs. His Communications Minister said something different the next day — companies paid for their own executives, not Anwar’s delegation. Which is it? If private companies are co-funding PM travel, there are legitimate questions about access and influence. Companies that fly with the PM get face time, relationship-building, and perceived government endorsement. That access has value — and when it’s purchased through travel funding, it raises conflict-of-interest concerns.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Calling critics ungrateful for questioning government spending is not transparency — it is deflection. Anwar styled the cost-sharing as fiscal responsibility, but the messaging collapse within 24 hours suggests the arrangement was either poorly structured or poorly understood by his own cabinet. A government that can’t keep its story straight on travel expenses for one news cycle is not demonstrating transparency — it is demonstrating dysfunction. And RM1.6 million to charter a plane for four countries is not exactly belt-tightening, regardless of who pays.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: PM travel expenses have been contentious under every administration. Najib’s use of a government jet for personal trips and the purchase of a luxury jet were signature scandals. PH criticised every ringgit of Najib’s travel spending. Under PH 2.0, Anwar has taken over 100 overseas trips in three years — more than any previous Malaysian PM in the same period. The cost-sharing arrangement was supposed to answer criticism about travel frequency. Instead, it created a new controversy about corporate influence.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your PM told Parliament one thing. Your Communications Minister said something different the next day. Then your PM complained that transparency is being punished. That’s not transparency — that’s contradictory messaging followed by self-pity. Publish a clear, detailed accounting of every overseas trip: government cost, corporate participation, and what business outcomes resulted. If the cost-sharing is legitimate, prove it with data. If it’s not, stop doing it. But don’t tell Parliament one figure and have your minister contradict it within 24 hours — and expect Malaysians to call that governance.

MACC Seizes RM6M and Freezes Assets Linked to Selangor Menteri Besar Inc in Sand Mining Corruption Probe

Said by: Amirudin Shari / MACC

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-10-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In October 2024, the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC) seized RM6 million and froze assets linked to Selangor Menteri Besar Incorporated (MBI) in connection with a sand mining corruption probe. MACC Chief Commissioner Azam Baki confirmed that five firms linked to a Selangor politician were under investigation for alleged corruption involving a sand mining concession. The probe uncovered a network of contracts and payments that raised questions about the integrity of Selangor’s sand mining approvals.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Selangor MBI is the state’s investment arm — directly under the Menteri Besar’s purview. When MACC seizes millions linked to the MB’s own corporate vehicle, it implicates the governance of the state’s highest office. Sand mining is one of Selangor’s most corruption-prone sectors, involving environmental destruction, river pollution, and billions in revenue. The fact that MACC had to intervene means internal checks failed.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Amirudin has been MB since 2018. Sand mining corruption in Selangor has been documented for years. Environmental groups have warned about illegal mining destroying riverbanks and poisoning water sources. Under Amirudin’s watch, the problem grew until MACC had to step in. That’s not governance — that’s negligence. The MB either didn’t know (incompetence) or didn’t act (complicity). Neither is acceptable.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: In September 2025, MACC confirmed new developments in the MBI corruption probe. A former Selangor GLC officer was charged over RM1.7 million in graft. Earlier, activist Chegubard lodged a MACC report alleging RM10 billion in irregularities linked to the Selangor Maritime Gateway project. The pattern is consistent: Selangor under PH is a corruption hotspot that the government refuses to acknowledge.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: MACC is investigating your state investment arm for corruption. Your own GLC officers have been charged. Your sand mining sector is a mess. And your response is silence. If this happened under a BN menteri besar, PKR would be holding nightly ceramah demanding resignation. Under Amirudin? Crickets.

Forest City Casino Saga — Anwar Threatens JAIL for Those Who Report on It

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-05-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In May 2024, Anwar Ibrahim called allegations about a casino at Forest City in Johor “malicious” and suggested those spreading the rumours could face jail. SCMP reported that Anwar and tycoons connected to the Forest City Special Financial Zone were “urged to walk back threats” after the aggressive response drew backlash. Chegubard — the activist who reported on the casino plan — was actually charged with sedition for his social media post. The government’s response to journalism about Forest City was not transparency or denial with evidence — it was threats of imprisonment.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When a Prime Minister threatens jail for people who report on a development project, it sends a devastating signal about press freedom and public accountability. Forest City is a politically sensitive project — a Chinese-backed development in Johor that the government is promoting as a Special Financial Zone with 0% tax incentives. Questions about what activities will be permitted there are legitimate public interest journalism, not sedition. The threat of jail for reporting is not a response to misinformation — it is a suppression of scrutiny.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: If the casino allegations were false, the government had every tool available to debunk them: issue a formal denial, publish the SFZ regulations, show that gaming is excluded. Instead, Anwar chose threats. That’s not the response of a government with nothing to hide — it’s the response of a government that wants questions to stop. SCMP’s reporting — that Anwar and tycoons were “urged to walk back threats” — suggests even allies recognised the response was disproportionate. When your reaction to journalism is more aggressive than your reaction to corruption, your priorities are transparent.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysian PMs have a long history of threatening critics instead of addressing allegations. Mahathir threatened journalists who questioned his policies. Najib’s government used the Official Secrets Act, Sedition Act, and MACC against critics of 1MDB. Muhyiddin used emergency ordinances to suppress dissent during COVID. PH promised to break this pattern. Instead, Anwar’s response to Forest City reporting was indistinguishable from his predecessors: threaten jail, charge the critic, and hope the story dies.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: The correct response to false allegations is evidence, not threats. If there’s no casino planned at Forest City, publish the SFZ regulations and prove it. If there is, own it and defend the policy. But threatening jail for reporters asking questions about a publicly funded special zone is authoritarian — and you know it, because you spent 25 years on the receiving end of exactly this tactic. Stop threatening journalists. Start answering questions.

Police Crack Down on Bersih Rally Under PH Government — The Reformists Become the Repressors

Said by: PH Government; Police (PDRM)

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-02-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 26-27 February 2024, Bersih held a rally calling on the PH government to implement the political reforms it had promised during the 2022 election campaign — including institutional independence, anti-corruption measures, and parliamentary oversight. Police responded with a crackdown that drew immediate condemnation from civil society. Lawyers for Liberty issued a statement condemning the government’s response, criticising the police’s lack of understanding of the Peaceful Assembly Act and the Federal Constitution. Bersih also handed a memorandum to the government urging implementation of long-delayed reforms.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Bersih’s rallies were the oxygen of PH’s rise to power. The Bersih 2.0, 3.0, 4.0, and 5.0 rallies between 2011 and 2016 built the mass movement that eventually toppled BN. PH leaders didn’t just support these rallies — they marched in them. Anwar himself was a beneficiary of Bersih’s advocacy. Now, the same government that rode Bersih’s wave to power is using police to suppress Bersih’s demands for the reforms PH promised. Freedom of assembly is only acceptable when it serves PH’s interests.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH leaders spent a decade demanding the right to peaceful assembly. Nik Nazmi — who was literally charged under the PAA for organising a protest in 2013 — was a PH minister when police cracked down on Bersih. LFL’s condemnation specifically cited a 2014 Court of Appeal ruling from Nik Nazmi’s own case that struck down restrictive provisions of the PAA. PH’s own legal victory against protest suppression is being ignored by PH’s own government. This isn’t about public order — it’s about a government that promised reform and is now using the same repressive tools as its predecessors to avoid being held accountable for broken promises.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, police crackdowns on Bersih rallies were front-page scandals. Tear gas, water cannons, and mass arrests at Bersih 2.0 and 3.0 were cited by international human rights organisations as evidence of Malaysia’s democratic backsliding. PH used these crackdowns to build their narrative as defenders of democracy. Human Rights Watch’s 2024 World Report stated that Malaysia’s new government “largely failed to uphold its pledges to address repression” in its first year — specifically noting the gap between PH’s reform promises and its actual governance record.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You marched with Bersih. You were tear-gassed with Bersih. You used Bersih’s rallies to build the movement that brought you to power. Now you’re sending police after Bersih because they’re demanding you keep the promises you made. The reformists have become the repressors. If you can’t handle peaceful criticism from the very people who put you in power, you don’t deserve that power.

Najib's Jail Sentence Halved — Pardons Board Under Anwar's Watch

Said by: Pardons Board / King of Malaysia

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-02-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 2 February 2024, the Pardons Board — which operates under the constitutional authority of the King, with the PM as a member — halved Najib Razak’s 12-year jail sentence to 6 years for his role in the 1MDB scandal. Najib’s RM210 million fine was also reduced to RM50 million. The decision came amid a “royal addendum” controversy, with Najib’s camp claiming an additional document existed granting house arrest. Reuters reported the pardon came “amid accusations of backsliding on reforms.” Critics called it a backroom deal to keep UMNO in the coalition — UMNO had made Najib’s release a precondition for continued support of Anwar’s government.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Najib was convicted of the largest financial scandal in Malaysian history — billions stolen from a sovereign wealth fund, laundered through global banks, spent on yachts, jewellery, and Hollywood films. The conviction was PH’s signature achievement under PH 1.0. Halving the sentence under PH 2.0 sent a devastating message: in Malaysia, corruption penalties are negotiable if you have enough political leverage. The pardon was not based on new evidence or legal merit — it was based on coalition arithmetic. UMNO’s support for PH came with an implicit price, and the rakyat is watching that price get paid.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar spent his entire political career demanding accountability for Najib. “Malu Apa Bossku” was the slogan PH mocked relentlessly. Now Najib’s sentence is halved, his fine reduced by 76%, and his supporters are pushing for house arrest — all under Anwar’s watch. The Pardons Board includes the PM. The decision cannot be separated from the political context: UMNO wanted Najib’s sentence reduced, UMNO is essential to PH’s majority, and the sentence was reduced. Calling this anything other than a political deal requires a level of naivety that no Malaysian possesses.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a history of politically convenient pardons. Anwar himself was pardoned by the King after PH 1.0’s victory in 2018 — a pardon that was celebrated as justice. The difference: Anwar’s conviction was widely seen as politically motivated. Najib’s conviction was upheld by every level of the judiciary based on overwhelming evidence. Pardoning a political prisoner and reducing the sentence of a convicted kleptocrat are fundamentally different acts — but PH’s willingness to facilitate both suggests that the pardons process is a political tool, not a justice mechanism.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You built your movement on holding Najib accountable. Then you halved his sentence to keep UMNO happy. The rakyat marched in Bersih rallies, voted in GE14 and GE15, and waited years for justice — and you traded it for coalition stability. If Najib’s crime was worth 12 years when PH was in opposition, it doesn’t become worth 6 years just because UMNO is in your coalition. Every ringgit Najib stole is still missing. Every year removed from his sentence is a discount on accountability. The rakyat will remember.

Amnesty International 2024: Anwar 'Betrayed the Reform Pledges He Made to Malaysian Voters'

Said by: Amnesty International / Shayna Bauchner

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-01-11

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2024, Amnesty International’s annual report stated that Anwar Ibrahim had “betrayed the reform pledges he made to Malaysian voters so he could hold his coalition together.” The report, authored by researcher Shayna Bauchner, identified seven areas where Malaysia fell short of its human rights obligations, including freedom of expression, freedom of assembly, media freedom, migrant rights, LGBTQ+ rights, and the death penalty. Amnesty noted that the government had not only failed to advance reforms but had actively used repressive laws it had promised to repeal.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When Amnesty International — the world’s largest human rights organisation — uses the word “betrayed,” it is not making a casual criticism. It is documenting a government that explicitly promised specific reforms, received votes on that basis, and then reversed course. The word choice matters: “stalled” implies delay; “betrayed” implies intent. Amnesty is saying that PH’s abandonment of reform was not due to constraints or complexity — it was a deliberate choice to prioritise coalition stability over human rights commitments.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s defence — “coalition constraints require compromise” — is exactly the argument BN used for decades. UMNO said it couldn’t reform because of coalition partners. PAS said it couldn’t moderate because of its base. Now PH says it can’t deliver rights because UMNO is in the coalition. The excuse is identical; only the party name has changed. If you knew UMNO would block reforms when you formed the coalition, then your manifesto promises were knowingly false. If you didn’t anticipate it, you’re incompetent. Either way, Amnesty’s use of “betrayed” is accurate.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Amnesty has documented Malaysian human rights failures across every administration. Under Mahathir 1.0, the focus was ISA detentions and crackdowns on dissent. Under Najib, it was 1MDB-related repression and the Sedition Act. Under PH 1.0, Amnesty noted partial progress before the Sheraton Move. Under PH 2.0, the verdict is the harshest yet — because the gap between promise and delivery has never been wider. PH made the most ambitious human rights commitments in Malaysian history, and broke them more comprehensively than any predecessor.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Amnesty International says you “betrayed” your voters. Not “disappointed.” Not “fell short.” Betrayed. That word will follow this government into every international forum, every diplomatic meeting, and every future election. You can still change the trajectory: repeal the Sedition Act, protect assembly rights, guarantee press freedom, and stop using repressive laws against critics. Or let “betrayed” become the one-word summary of the Anwar era — because right now, Amnesty and HRW agree that it is.

Anwar Appoints Own Daughter Nurul Izzah as Senior Economic Adviser — Then Calls It Not Nepotism

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-01-31

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 31 January 2023 — two months into his premiership — Anwar Ibrahim appointed his daughter Nurul Izzah Anwar as Senior Adviser on Economics and Finance in the Prime Minister’s Department. This came after Nurul Izzah lost her Permatang Pauh parliamentary seat in GE15 to Perikatan Nasional by nearly 6,000 votes — a seat that was a traditional PKR stronghold. The appointment was unpaid, which Anwar used as his primary defence. He told reporters: “Nepotism is where a family member is given a position to abuse power, enrich themselves, obtain contracts and get paid a huge sum. This is not the case.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Nepotism is not defined by salary — it is defined by access. An unpaid adviser to the Prime Minister has more institutional influence than most paid civil servants. The role grants proximity to policy, contracts, and decision-making at the highest level. Appointing a candidate who was just rejected by voters to an unelected advisory role is a slap in the face of democratic outcomes. If the PM can appoint family members to unelected advisory roles and call it acceptable, then every future PM can do the same. The precedent is set.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar spent 25 years branding himself as the antidote to Malaysia’s entrenched patronage politics. Two months into office, he appointed his own daughter. The defence — “it’s unpaid, therefore not nepotism” — is a textbook deflection. Was there an open call? A shortlist? A vetting committee? No. She was appointed because she is the PM’s daughter, and every Malaysian knows it. Nurul Izzah holds degrees from Johns Hopkins, but qualification is not the issue — process is. Mahathir appointed family members to business positions. Najib’s circle enriched relatives through 1MDB-adjacent deals. Anwar promised to be different. He wasn’t.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Political dynasties are endemic in Malaysian politics. Mahathir’s sons held business empires built on political connections. Najib’s stepson Riza Aziz was charged in the 1MDB scandal. UMNO’s patronage networks enriched families for decades. PH criticised all of this relentlessly. Anwar’s appointment of Nurul Izzah — within weeks of taking office — signalled that the reformasi playbook had an asterisk: does not apply to the PM’s family. Nurul Izzah has since been installed as PKR deputy president, consolidating the Anwar dynasty within the party.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You spent 25 years preaching against patronage. You appointed your daughter within 60 days. She lost her seat and you gave her an advisory role. She’s now PKR deputy president. This is not meritocracy — it’s dynasty-building with better PR. All PM advisory appointments should go through a transparent vetting process. Family members of the sitting PM should be automatically disqualified from unelected roles in the PMO. Acknowledge the optics instead of redefining nepotism to suit your situation.

PH's GE15 Manifesto: 60 Promises Made, Majority Broken or Abandoned — The Biggest Con Job in Malaysian Politics

Said by: Malaysiakini Manifesto Tracker / Straits Times Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2022-11-19

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: PH’s GE15 manifesto — released on 19 November 2022 — contained approximately 60 measurable promises. As tracked by Malaysiakini’s Manifesto Tracker and analysed by the Straits Times, the majority have been broken, delayed, or quietly abandoned. Key failures include: repeal of repressive laws (Sedition Act expanded instead, 90+ arrests), abolition of tolls (government pays RM591M to freeze hikes instead), civil service reform (headcount grew), PM term limits (bill failed in Parliament), ICCPR ratification (not done), UUCA abolition (not done), AG-PP separation (sent to committee), and anti-hopping law strengthening (weakened by exceptions).

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Election manifestos are the contract between a government and voters. When a majority of promises are broken, the social contract collapses. PH did not lose these promises to external circumstances — they chose to abandon them. The Sedition Act wasn’t repealed because PH found it useful. Tolls weren’t abolished because concessionaires have political connections. The PM term limit bill failed because PH’s own MPs didn’t show up. These aren’t broken promises — they’re policy reversals dressed up as pragmatism.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s defence is always “coalition constraints” and “we need UMNO’s support.” But PH wrote the manifesto knowing it would need coalition partners. If you promise reforms you can’t deliver because of your own coalition arithmetic, you were lying at the point of the promise, not at the point of the betrayal. The manifesto wasn’t aspirational — it was transactional. PH traded promises for votes, collected the votes, and defaulted on the promises. In any other context, this is fraud.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian government has broken manifesto promises. BN’s manifestos were wish lists that no one expected to be honoured. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) achieved partial reforms in 22 months before the Sheraton Move. But PH 2.0 is unique: it has a larger majority, more time, and fewer excuses — yet its reform record is worse than PH 1.0’s. The Straits Times noted that PH’s manifesto compliance rate has declined with each year in power, not improved. Promises aren’t being delayed — they’re being abandoned.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You wrote 60 promises. You broke most of them. You had three years, a supermajority, and the most favourable parliamentary arithmetic in Malaysian history. If you couldn’t deliver reform under these conditions, you never will. Publish a clause-by-clause manifesto compliance report. Explain which promises were abandoned and why. Set binding timelines for remaining commitments. Or admit what every Malaysian already knows: the manifesto was a campaign prop, not a governing plan.

Sources: Malaysiakini Manifesto Tracker | Straits Times
