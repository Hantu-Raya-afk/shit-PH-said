Anwar Launches Capital Market Masterplan, Says 'Reform Non-Negotiable' — While Every Actual Reform Gets Delayed or Killed

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim delivered the keynote address at the launch of the Securities Commission’s Capital Market Masterplan 2026–2030 (CMP4) on March 9, 2026. He declared that “the government has made reform non-negotiable” and that Malaysia’s capital market “must drive shared prosperity, not just profit.”

The CMP4 targets growing Malaysia’s capital market to RM6.3 trillion by 2030 and includes initiatives like the “MY Value Up” program to boost PLC valuations.

Also present were Second Finance Minister Datuk Seri Amir Hamzah Azizan and SC Chairman Datuk Mohammad Faiz Azmi.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

The phrase “reform non-negotiable” would be powerful if it came from a government that was actually delivering reform. Instead, it comes from the same Prime Minister whose government, in the past two weeks alone:

  • Failed to pass the PM tenure limit bill because 8 of its own MPs didn’t show up (March 3)
  • Postponed the AG-Public Prosecutor separation bill for a 3-month committee review — a core reform promise delayed yet again (March 4)
  • Refused to suspend MACC chief Azam Baki despite mounting allegations, opting instead to let his contract quietly expire in May (reported March 9)
  • Delayed RON95 reform repeatedly — Anwar himself admitted he could only hold the price “one or two more months” (March 7) “Reform non-negotiable” is a slogan delivered at a securities industry event while actual institutional reform — the kind that strengthens governance and accountability — keeps getting pushed back, watered down, or abandoned.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Anwar uses “reform” the way a luxury brand uses “exclusive” — it is a marketing word detached from the product.

The Capital Market Masterplan is a Securities Commission document. It is a technocratic roadmap for market growth, IPO pipelines, and PLC valuations. That is fine. But Anwar does not get to stand at a podium, declare “reform non-negotiable,” and receive credit for reform when:

  • The Attorney General still doubles as the Public Prosecutor — a conflict of interest PH has promised to fix since 2018.
  • The MACC chief remains in office despite allegations that would have triggered immediate suspension in any serious anti-corruption jurisdiction.
  • Institutional reforms that require political courage — not just SC planning documents — are systematically delayed. Capital market growth is not reform. Raising PLC valuations is not reform. “Shared prosperity” is not reform. Reform is when you change the rules that protect the powerful. Anwar has not done that.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

  • 2018–2020 (PH 1.0): The first PH government also promised AG-PP separation, IPCMC, and institutional reform. None were completed before the Sheraton Move collapsed the government.
  • March 4, 2026: Fahmi Fadzil announced the AG-PP separation bill would be delayed for a 3-month parliamentary committee review — the latest in a long line of postponements.
  • March 3, 2026: The PM tenure limit constitutional amendment failed because PH couldn’t muster its own full attendance — 8 government and independent MPs were absent.
  • December 2025: Anwar halfed Najib’s sentence while the 1MDB debt — RM50 billion — remained on the rakyat’s books.
  • The pattern is consistent: PH announces bold reform frameworks at formal events, then delays or abandons the actual legislative and institutional changes required to implement them.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You say reform is “non-negotiable.” Then why does every reform end up being negotiated away?

The AG-PP separation — delayed. The PM tenure limit — failed on your own attendance. Azam Baki — still in office. RON95 — still subsidised with no timeline. IPCMC — abandoned.

You have the parliamentary majority. You have the institutional mandate. You have the public goodwill — or what remains of it.

What you don’t have is the political will to spend your coalition capital on anything that doesn’t benefit the coalition itself.

Stop telling the Securities Commission audience that reform is non-negotiable. Start telling your own Cabinet.

Anwar's Government Fights 531,976 Retirees in Court — Wins Appeal to Block RM1.7 Billion Pension Payout

Said by: Federal Government / Senior Federal Counsel Sallehuddin Ali

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 What Happened

On 9 March 2026, the Court of Appeal unanimously overturned a High Court order that would have required Anwar Ibrahim’s government to pay RM1.7 billion in pension arrears to 531,976 former civil servants. The three-judge panel — led by Justice Azhahari Kamal Ramli, with Justices Lim Chong Fong and Meor Hashimi Abdul Hamid — ruled that the pensioners’ application was res judicata (already adjudicated) and constituted an “abuse of court process.” The government, represented by Senior Federal Counsel Sallehuddin Ali, successfully argued that the 2016 Public Service Department Circular (PP 1/2016) was an “enhancement” to the civil service scheme, not a mandatory salary revision triggering pension adjustments. The original case was brought by Aminah Ahmad, a retired Ministry of Foreign Affairs officer, on behalf of 56 other retirees in 2017.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Madani government actively going to court to avoid paying half a million retirees money they were legally awarded. The average payout per retiree would have been roughly RM3,195 — not a fortune, but real money for people on fixed incomes. The government chose to fight this rather than honour a High Court order. This is a government that spends billions on Hari Raya cash handouts and election-year STR payments, but when pensioners win a court ruling for pension adjustments based on the government’s own 2016 circular, Putrajaya deploys federal counsel to crush the claim on a technicality. The signal is clear: if you’re politically useful, you get cash. If you’re a retiree with a legal claim, you get lawyers.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim built his career on defending the little guy. His government now argues that a PSD circular meant to adjust pensions in line with civil service salary changes was merely an “enhancement” — bureaucratic doublespeak for “we wrote it, but we didn’t mean it.” The Court of Appeal’s ruling that this was “abuse of process” doesn’t mean the pensioners’ grievance is illegitimate — it means the government found a procedural exit ramp and took it. Meanwhile, Putrajaya approved RM15 billion in STR cash handouts just days earlier. The message: Anwar’s government has money for voters, not for retirees who already served the country.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysian governments have a long history of treating pension adjustments as political favours rather than legal obligations. The 2013 amendment to the Pension Adjustment Act — which provided retirees with a fixed annual 2% increase — was itself invalidated by the Court of Appeal in 2022, stripping retirees of a guaranteed mechanism. Every administration since has treated pension reform as a hot potato: promise it during campaigns, dodge it in office. The Madani government is no different. Anwar’s own 2022 election manifesto spoke of “dignifying” civil servants and retirees, but the legal strategy his government is pursuing tells a different story.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised kerajaan Madani — a civilised government that upholds justice and human dignity. Half a million people who spent their careers serving this country were told by a High Court that they are owed pension adjustments. Your government’s response was not to pay, not to negotiate, but to appeal and win on a technicality. You found RM15 billion for pre-Raya handouts but fought tooth and nail against RM1.7 billion for pensioners. That is not reform. That is not Madani. That is the same old Putrajaya arithmetic: spend where the votes are, litigate where they aren’t.

Deputy Economy Minister Says Growth Steady at 4-4.5% — Amid a Regional War and Global Volatility

Said by: Mohd Shahar Abdullah, Deputy Economy Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 9 March 2026, Deputy Economy Minister Mohd Shahar Abdullah projected 4–4.5% GDP growth for Malaysia at the Asean Banking and Finance Summit, citing domestic consumption, infrastructure investment, the digital economy, and Visit Malaysia 2026. He made this statement at the very summit whose chairman, Tan Sri Majid Khan, warned that geopolitical tensions are “repricing risk across our entire region.” The US-Iran conflict was escalating, oil prices were spiking due to Strait of Hormuz disruptions, and GPS jamming was affecting shipping across the Gulf.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A deputy minister projecting steady growth at a summit convened to discuss regional instability suggests a dangerous disconnect between political messaging and economic reality. The growth drivers cited — tourism, digital economy, domestic consumption — are all vulnerable to the very geopolitical risks flagged at the same event. Oil price spikes may temporarily boost petroleum revenue, but they raise input costs across the economy and threaten the government’s already fragile fuel subsidy position. When your own summit chairman is sounding the alarm, doubling down on optimism reads less like confidence and more like denial.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Malaysia’s economy is heavily trade-dependent and exposed to every global shock the deputy minister chose to ignore. Projecting 4-4.5% growth while a regional war threatens shipping lanes, oil markets, and investor confidence is not leadership — it’s PR. The government has repeatedly been caught off-guard by external shocks (COVID, supply chain disruptions, commodity swings) because it prioritises messaging over contingency planning. Telling bankers “everything is fine” while the building shakes is not reassuring — it’s alarming.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysian governments have a long history of projecting optimism that doesn’t survive contact with reality. In 2020, the government insisted the economy was “resilient” weeks before COVID lockdowns crashed GDP. In 2023, growth projections were revised downward multiple times. The pattern: announce best-case projections, hope the world cooperates, scramble when it doesn’t. PH inherited this habit from BN and has done nothing to change it.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: If you’re going to host a summit about regional risk, don’t send your deputy minister to say everything is fine. Either acknowledge the risks and present contingency plans, or stop pretending your growth projections are anything more than political targets. Malaysians deserve honest economic forecasting, not election-season optimism disguised as policy analysis.

It's March 2026 — Where Is Anwar's Promise to Eradicate Hardcore Poverty by Q1?

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 7 December 2025, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim publicly declared that Malaysia would eradicate hardcore poverty by the first quarter of 2026. Speaking at the closing of the Rancakkan Madani programme in Putrajaya, Anwar cited a hardcore poverty rate of 0.09% — roughly 7,000 households — and said the remaining cases could be resolved within months. “We are one of the countries in the world… we have managed to reduce the national rate of hardcore poverty to just 0.09 per cent,” Anwar said. “We can end it by the first quarter of 2026.” The promise was reported by Bernama, Malay Mail, The Star, New Straits Times, The Edge, and The Sun. It was a flagship claim — repeated during Budget 2026 and the Rancakkan Madani roadshow. It is now 9 March 2026. Q1 ends in three weeks. There has been no official announcement that hardcore poverty has been eradicated. No press conference. No Bernama flash. No triumphant PMO statement.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This was not a casual remark. Anwar made the eradication of hardcore poverty a centrepiece of the Madani economic narrative. It was embedded into Budget 2026 messaging, cited in international forums, and used to distinguish Malaysia from Western economies plagued by homelessness. The World Bank even weighed in, commending Malaysia’s progress while noting that “further improvements in education and health outcomes are crucial.” The December 2025 promise was designed to be a legacy moment — the PM who ended extreme poverty. If the target has been met, the silence is bizarre. If it hasn’t been met, the silence is damning. Either way, the rakyat deserves an update. Poverty eradication claims also carry methodological risk. Economists have previously questioned whether Malaysia’s hardcore poverty line (RM1,169/month per household) is set too low, making “eradication” a statistical exercise rather than a lived reality. A government that declares victory without addressing these concerns is doing PR, not policy.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: This is a textbook case of promise inflation followed by quiet deflation. Anwar made the pledge when the numbers looked good and the cameras were rolling. He set a specific, time-bound target — Q1 2026 — knowing full well it would either be met or missed in public view. Now that the deadline is here, the government has gone quiet. No update. No revised timeline. No acknowledgement that the target even exists. This is the same administration that loudly claims credit for every ringgit of GDP growth and every decimal point drop in inflation. But when it comes to a concrete promise with a concrete deadline? Silence. If hardcore poverty has been eradicated, say so. Publish the data. Show the methodology. Let independent economists verify it. If it hasn’t been eradicated, say that too. Explain what went wrong. Revise the timeline. Be honest. What you don’t get to do is make a grand promise, ride the PR wave, and then pretend the deadline doesn’t exist.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: This is not PH’s first vanishing deadline:

  • RON95 fuel subsidy reform has been delayed repeatedly. Anwar himself admitted in early March 2026 that the government could only hold the price for “one or two more months” — yet no concrete reform plan has been announced.
  • The AG-Public Prosecutor separation bill was postponed in March 2026 for a 3-month committee review, after being a signature reform promise.
  • The PM term limit constitutional amendment failed in Parliament in March 2026 — despite PH’s commanding majority — because 8 government MPs didn’t even show up to vote. The pattern is clear: big promises, loud announcements, quiet retreats. The Madani government treats reform pledges as PR events, not binding commitments. Malaysia’s own history is littered with poverty eradication claims that didn’t survive contact with reality. The Najib administration claimed similar progress; independent audits later showed significant undercounting.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You told 33 million Malaysians that hardcore poverty would be gone by Q1 2026. You said it on camera, in Putrajaya, at your own showcase event. Bernama carried it. Every major outlet reported it. It’s March 9. Q1 is almost over. Where’s the announcement? Where’s the data? Where’s the press conference where you pop the champagne and show us the zero? Or did you hope we’d forget? Here’s the thing about specific promises with specific deadlines: people remember. You don’t get to coast on vibes when you gave us a date. Either deliver the results or deliver an explanation. The rakyat who are still living on less than RM1,169 a month deserve more than silence.

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 6 Mac, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim bercakap dengan pemberita selepas solat Jumaat di IOI City Mall Putrajaya dan menasihati rakyat Malaysia supaya “berhemat, belajar berjimat cermat dan menyimpan” sebagai “langkah berjaga-jaga” menghadapi kesan ekonomi konflik AS-Iran. Perdana Menteri minta rakyat biasa ikat perut pada masa kerajaan sendiri longgarkan pili fiskal. Dalam kitaran berita yang sama, kerajaan komit RM15 bilion bantuan Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah (STR), umumkan bantuan khas RM500 kepada 1.7 juta penjawat awam dan kakitangan masjid (dibayar 13 Mac), dan telah ikrarkan RM200 juta secara keseluruhan untuk Palestin. Kalau krisis ini cukup serius untuk minta rakyat berjimat menjelang Hari Raya, ia juga cukup serius untuk potong perbelanjaan naungan politik, bekukan bonus GLC, dan tangguhkan projek tidak perlu. Sebaliknya, kerajaan berbelanja dan suruh rakyat berjimat.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Corak ini bukan baru untuk Anwar. Pada 2023, beliau suruh rakyat “ubah gaya hidup” untuk menangani rasionalisasi subsidi — sambil mengekalkan Kabinet bengkak lebih 60 menteri dan timbalan menteri. Pada 2024, kerajaan teruskan potongan subsidi diesel bersasar sambil meluluskan projek mega baru seperti SEZ Johor-Singapura. Buku panduan sentiasa sama: kerajaan komit kepada projek politik mahal, kemudian suruh rakyat biasa sesuaikan diri. Mahathir buat begitu. Najib buat begitu. Anwar buat dengan PR yang lebih cantik.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Kamu janji “Belanjawan Rakyat.” Kamu janji disiplin fiskal. Kamu janji kerajaan Madani akan berbeza — tiada lagi naungan, tiada lagi perbelanjaan membazir, tiada lagi suruh rakyat sengsara sementara elit berpesta. Jadi inilah ujiannya: kalau krisis Timur Tengah cukup serius untuk suruh rakyat tak pergi shopping Raya, ia juga cukup serius untuk batalkan bonus RM500, bekukan perjalanan luar negara menteri, hentikan semua projek mega GLC baru, dan terbitkan akaun terperinci ke mana setiap ringgit perbelanjaan krisis pergi. Apa-apa kurang dari itu hanyalah seorang ahli politik yang suruh rakyat makan kurang supaya kerajaan boleh makan lebih.

Anwar Tells Rakyat to 'Spend Wisely' and 'Be Thrifty' — After Handing Out RM15 Billion in Cash, RM500 Bonuses, and RM200 Million for Palestine

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 What Happened

On March 6, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim addressed reporters after Friday prayers at IOI City Mall Putrajaya and told Malaysians to “be prudent, learn to be thrifty and save” as a “precautionary measure” against the economic fallout of the US-Iran conflict. He cited soaring oil prices, the blockade of over 200 tankers in the Strait of Hormuz, and spiking food and fertiliser costs from Ukraine, Russia and Europe. By March 8, the advice had become a national headline: “Spend wisely, PM urges.”

🔥 Why It Matters

The Prime Minister is asking ordinary Malaysians to tighten their belts at the exact moment his own government is loosening the fiscal tap. In the same news cycle, the government committed RM15 billion in Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah (STR) handouts, announced RM500 special aid for 1.7 million civil servants and mosque personnel (payable March 13), and has pledged RM200 million in total for Palestine. The message to the rakyat is: we can spend freely, but you must save. This is the fundamental contradiction at the heart of the Madani government’s economic messaging — fiscal populism for the headlines, austerity preaching for the people.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

A Prime Minister who just signed off on billions in election-cycle handouts does not get to lecture the rakyat on thrift. The RM500 bonus for civil servants alone costs hundreds of millions. The RM15B STR programme is the largest cash transfer in Malaysian history. The RM200M Palestine allocation — regardless of its merits — is discretionary spending announced at a rally. Anwar’s government is not practising what it preaches. If the crisis is serious enough to ask Malaysians to scrimp ahead of Hari Raya, it is serious enough to cut political patronage spending, freeze GLC bonuses, and defer non-essential projects. Instead, the government spends and then tells the rakyat to save.

📜 Previous Cases

This pattern is not new for Anwar. In 2023, he told Malaysians to “change their lifestyle” to cope with subsidy rationalisation — while maintaining a bloated Cabinet of over 60 ministers and deputy ministers. In 2024, the government pushed through targeted diesel subsidy cuts while approving new megaprojects like the Johor-Singapore SEZ. The playbook is always the same: the government commits to expensive political projects, then tells ordinary people to adjust. Mahathir did it. Najib did it. Anwar does it with better PR.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised a “Rakyat’s Budget.” You promised fiscal discipline. You promised that the Madani government would be different — no more patronage, no more wasteful spending, no more telling the people to suffer while the elites feast. So here’s the test: if the Middle East crisis is serious enough to tell Malaysians to skip their Raya shopping, it is serious enough to cancel the RM500 bonuses, freeze ministerial overseas travel, halt all new GLC megaprojects, and publish a line-by-line accounting of where every ringgit of crisis spending goes. Anything less is a politician telling the rakyat to eat less so the government can eat more.

[BM] Anwar Akui Hanya Mampu Kekal Subsidi RON95 'Satu Atau Dua Bulan' — Sementara Harga Minyak Melonjak Merata

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 6 Mac 2026, Perdana Menteri dan Menteri Kewangan Anwar Ibrahim memberitahu pemberita bahawa Malaysia boleh mengekalkan harga petrol RON95 bersubsidi pada RM1.99 seliter di bawah skim Budi95 — tetapi hanya untuk ‘satu atau dua bulan.’ Kata-kata tepat beliau: ‘Kita akan cuba mengawal kesan konflik Iran, termasuk untuk RON95, iaitu RM1.99 seliter. Kita akan memantau keadaan ini kerana setakat ini, kita masih boleh menangguhkan selama satu atau dua bulan.’ Ini pada minggu sama RON97 naik 10 sen ke RM3.25 seliter, RON95 tak bersubsidi naik 8 sen ke RM2.67, dan diesel di Semenanjung naik 8 sen ke RM3.12.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Ini kali pertama Anwar secara terbuka meletakkan had masa terhadap keupayaan kerajaan mengekalkan subsidi bahan api. Bahasa berselindung — ‘kami akan cuba,’ ’tangguhkan,’ ‘pantau’ — adalah perubahan ketara daripada jaminan sebelumnya. Jika gangguan Selat Hormuz berterusan, dan 20% bekalan minyak dunia kekal berisiko, harga RM1.99 menjadi tidak mampu dikekalkan secara fiskal. Tetapi Anwar tidak mampu secara politik menaikkan harga bahan api — terutamanya dengan Hari Raya beberapa minggu lagi, khabar angin pilihan raya kilat untuk Julai, dan bantuan tunai STR RM15 bilion sudah dikerahkan.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Anwar menghabiskan tiga tahun memberitahu rakyat Malaysia bahawa sistem subsidi bersasar Budi95 adalah kepakaran reformasi fiskal — lebih bijak, lebih adil, dan lebih mampan daripada subsidi menyeluruh. Kini, saat harga minyak global benar-benar menguji sistem, beliau sudah berselindung dan berkata hanya mampu bertahan beberapa bulan. Apa jadi dengan penjimatan RM800 juta yang kerajaan dakwa daripada Budi95? Kebenaran adalah subsidi bersasar sentiasa direka untuk laut tenang. Saat kejutan sebenar melanda, seluruh sistem mula bergegar. Dan bukannya pelan, kita dapat ‘kami akan cuba.’

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini corak berulang dengan Anwar mengenai dasar bahan api: janji kestabilan, sampaikan ketidakpastian. Harga bahan api telah merangkak naik secara berterusan sejak Januari 2026, dengan diesel sahaja naik dari RM2.84 pada pertengahan Januari ke RM3.12 menjelang awal Mac. Setiap minggu membawa pelarasan harga baharu ke atas, sementara kerajaan berkeras keadaan ’terkawal.’ Ia mencerminkan buku panduan era BN: meyakinkan rakyat sementara angka senyap-senyap merosot, kemudian berlakon terkejut apabila empangan pecah.

🎯 Seruan Kepada PH

Anda memberitahu rakyat Malaysia bahawa Budi95 adalah reformasi. Kini anda mengakui, secara terbuka, bahawa sistem itu hanya mampu bertahan ‘satu atau dua bulan’ tekanan global sebenar. Jika ini reformasi fiskal, ia dibina atas kadbod. Mana pelan luar jangka? Mana perbualan jujur dengan rakyat tentang apa yang berlaku jika minyak cecah USD100 setong dan kekal di situ? Berhenti menguruskan tajuk berita dan mula menguruskan ekonomi.
