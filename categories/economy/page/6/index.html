[BM] Anwar Suruh Rakyat 'Berbelanja Bijak' Dan 'Berjimat Cermat' — Selepas Hulur RM15 Bilion Tunai, Bonus RM500, Dan RM200 Juta Untuk Palestin

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 6 Mac, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim bercakap dengan pemberita selepas solat Jumaat di IOI City Mall Putrajaya dan menasihati rakyat Malaysia supaya “berhemat, belajar berjimat cermat dan menyimpan” sebagai “langkah berjaga-jaga” menghadapi kesan ekonomi konflik AS-Iran. Beliau merujuk kepada harga minyak yang melambung tinggi, lebih 200 kapal tangki disekat di Selat Hormuz, dan kos makanan serta baja dari Ukraine, Rusia dan Eropah yang melonjak naik. Menjelang 8 Mac, nasihat ini menjadi tajuk utama nasional: “Berbelanja bijak, kata PM.”

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Perdana Menteri minta rakyat biasa ikat perut pada masa kerajaan sendiri longgarkan pili fiskal. Dalam kitaran berita yang sama, kerajaan komit RM15 bilion bantuan Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah (STR), umumkan bantuan khas RM500 kepada 1.7 juta penjawat awam dan kakitangan masjid (dibayar 13 Mac), dan telah ikrarkan RM200 juta secara keseluruhan untuk Palestin. Mesej kepada rakyat: kami boleh berbelanja sesuka hati, tapi kamu kena berjimat. Inilah percanggahan asas dalam mesej ekonomi kerajaan Madani — populisme fiskal untuk tajuk berita, khutbah berjimat cermat untuk rakyat.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Seorang Perdana Menteri yang baru sahaja meluluskan berbilion ringgit bantuan tunai musim pilihan raya tidak layak berceramah kepada rakyat tentang berjimat cermat. Bonus RM500 untuk penjawat awam sahaja menelan ratusan juta. Program STR RM15B adalah pemindahan tunai terbesar dalam sejarah Malaysia. Peruntukan RM200J untuk Palestin — walau apa pun meritnya — adalah perbelanjaan budi bicara yang diumumkan di perhimpunan. Kerajaan Anwar tidak mengamalkan apa yang dikhutbahkan. Kalau krisis ini cukup serius untuk minta rakyat berjimat menjelang Hari Raya, ia juga cukup serius untuk potong perbelanjaan naungan politik, bekukan bonus GLC, dan tangguhkan projek tidak perlu. Sebaliknya, kerajaan berbelanja dan suruh rakyat berjimat.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Corak ini bukan baru untuk Anwar. Pada 2023, beliau suruh rakyat “ubah gaya hidup” untuk menangani rasionalisasi subsidi — sambil mengekalkan Kabinet bengkak lebih 60 menteri dan timbalan menteri. Pada 2024, kerajaan teruskan potongan subsidi diesel bersasar sambil meluluskan projek mega baru seperti SEZ Johor-Singapura. Buku panduan sentiasa sama: kerajaan komit kepada projek politik mahal, kemudian suruh rakyat biasa sesuaikan diri. Mahathir buat begitu. Najib buat begitu. Anwar buat dengan PR yang lebih cantik.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Kamu janji “Belanjawan Rakyat.” Kamu janji disiplin fiskal. Kamu janji kerajaan Madani akan berbeza — tiada lagi naungan, tiada lagi perbelanjaan membazir, tiada lagi suruh rakyat sengsara sementara elit berpesta. Jadi inilah ujiannya: kalau krisis Timur Tengah cukup serius untuk suruh rakyat tak pergi shopping Raya, ia juga cukup serius untuk batalkan bonus RM500, bekukan perjalanan luar negara menteri, hentikan semua projek mega GLC baru, dan terbitkan akaun terperinci ke mana setiap ringgit perbelanjaan krisis pergi. Apa-apa kurang dari itu hanyalah seorang ahli politik yang suruh rakyat makan kurang supaya kerajaan boleh makan lebih.

Anwar Tells Rakyat to 'Spend Wisely' and 'Be Thrifty' — After Handing Out RM15 Billion in Cash, RM500 Bonuses, and RM200 Million for Palestine

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 What Happened

On March 6, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim addressed reporters after Friday prayers at IOI City Mall Putrajaya and told Malaysians to “be prudent, learn to be thrifty and save” as a “precautionary measure” against the economic fallout of the US-Iran conflict. He cited soaring oil prices, the blockade of over 200 tankers in the Strait of Hormuz, and spiking food and fertiliser costs from Ukraine, Russia and Europe. By March 8, the advice had become a national headline: “Spend wisely, PM urges.”

🔥 Why It Matters

The Prime Minister is asking ordinary Malaysians to tighten their belts at the exact moment his own government is loosening the fiscal tap. In the same news cycle, the government committed RM15 billion in Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah (STR) handouts, announced RM500 special aid for 1.7 million civil servants and mosque personnel (payable March 13), and has pledged RM200 million in total for Palestine. The message to the rakyat is: we can spend freely, but you must save. This is the fundamental contradiction at the heart of the Madani government’s economic messaging — fiscal populism for the headlines, austerity preaching for the people.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

A Prime Minister who just signed off on billions in election-cycle handouts does not get to lecture the rakyat on thrift. The RM500 bonus for civil servants alone costs hundreds of millions. The RM15B STR programme is the largest cash transfer in Malaysian history. The RM200M Palestine allocation — regardless of its merits — is discretionary spending announced at a rally. Anwar’s government is not practising what it preaches. If the crisis is serious enough to ask Malaysians to scrimp ahead of Hari Raya, it is serious enough to cut political patronage spending, freeze GLC bonuses, and defer non-essential projects. Instead, the government spends and then tells the rakyat to save.

📜 Previous Cases

This pattern is not new for Anwar. In 2023, he told Malaysians to “change their lifestyle” to cope with subsidy rationalisation — while maintaining a bloated Cabinet of over 60 ministers and deputy ministers. In 2024, the government pushed through targeted diesel subsidy cuts while approving new megaprojects like the Johor-Singapore SEZ. The playbook is always the same: the government commits to expensive political projects, then tells ordinary people to adjust. Mahathir did it. Najib did it. Anwar does it with better PR.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised a “Rakyat’s Budget.” You promised fiscal discipline. You promised that the Madani government would be different — no more patronage, no more wasteful spending, no more telling the people to suffer while the elites feast. So here’s the test: if the Middle East crisis is serious enough to tell Malaysians to skip their Raya shopping, it is serious enough to cancel the RM500 bonuses, freeze ministerial overseas travel, halt all new GLC megaprojects, and publish a line-by-line accounting of where every ringgit of crisis spending goes. Anything less is a politician telling the rakyat to eat less so the government can eat more.

[BM] Anwar Akui Hanya Mampu Tahan Subsidi RON95 'Satu Atau Dua Bulan' — Sementara Harga Minyak Meletup Merata

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 6 Mac 2026, Perdana Menteri dan Menteri Kewangan Anwar Ibrahim memberitahu pemberita bahawa Malaysia boleh mengekalkan harga petrol RON95 bersubsidi pada RM1.99 seliter di bawah skim Budi95 — tetapi hanya untuk ‘satu atau dua bulan.’ Kata-kata tepat beliau: ‘Kami akan cuba mengawal kesan konflik Iran, termasuk untuk RON95, iaitu RM1.99 seliter. Kami akan memantau keadaan ini kerana setakat ini, kami masih boleh menahan untuk satu atau dua bulan.’ Ini datang pada minggu yang sama RON97 naik 10 sen ke RM3.25 seliter, RON95 tidak bersubsidi naik 8 sen ke RM2.67, dan diesel di Semenanjung naik 8 sen ke RM3.12.

🔥 Kenapa Ia Penting

Ini kali pertama Anwar secara terbuka meletakkan had masa terhadap keupayaan kerajaan menahan subsidi minyak. Bahasa berhati-hati — ‘kami akan cuba,’ ‘menahan,’ ‘memantau’ — adalah perubahan ketara daripada jaminan sebelumnya. Jika gangguan Selat Hormuz berterusan, dan 20% bekalan minyak dunia kekal berisiko, harga RM1.99 menjadi tidak mapan secara fiskal. Tetapi Anwar tidak mampu secara politik untuk menaikkan harga minyak — terutamanya dengan Hari Raya beberapa minggu lagi dan khabar angin pilihan raya kilat untuk Julai.

💩 Kenapa Ia Karut

Anwar menghabiskan tiga tahun memberitahu rakyat Malaysia bahawa sistem subsidi bersasar Budi95 adalah kepakaran reformasi fiskal — lebih bijak, lebih adil, dan lebih mapan daripada subsidi menyeluruh. Kini, saat harga minyak global benar-benar menguji sistem, beliau sudah berhati-hati dan berkata hanya mampu menahannya beberapa bulan. Apa jadi dengan penjimatan RM800 juta yang kerajaan dakwa daripada Budi95? Kebenaran ialah subsidi bersasar sentiasa direka untuk laut tenang. Saat kejutan sebenar melanda, seluruh sistem mula goyah. Dan bukannya pelan, kita dapat ‘kami akan cuba.’

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini corak berulang dengan Anwar mengenai dasar minyak: janji kestabilan, sampaikan ketidakpastian. Harga minyak terus merangkak naik sejak Januari 2026, dengan diesel sahaja naik dari RM2.84 pada pertengahan Januari ke RM3.12 menjelang awal Mac. Setiap minggu membawa pelarasan harga naik, sementara kerajaan bertegas keadaan ’terkawal.’

🎯 Panggilan PH

Anda beritahu rakyat Malaysia Budi95 adalah reformasi. Anda beritahu mereka subsidi bersasar akan melindungi rakyat sambil menjimatkan wang kerajaan. Kini anda mengakui, secara terbuka, bahawa sistem hanya mampu bertahan ‘satu atau dua bulan’ tekanan global sebenar. Mana pelan luar jangka? Mana perbualan jujur dengan orang ramai tentang apa yang berlaku jika minyak mencecah USD100 setong dan kekal? Berhenti menguruskan tajuk dan mula menguruskan ekonomi.

[BM] Anwar Bangga Pelaburan Diluluskan 'Rekod' RM426.7B — Tapi 'Diluluskan' Bukan Bermakna 'Tiba'

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

Menurut MIDA, 8,390 projek diluluskan, dengan potensi mewujudkan 244,902 pekerjaan. Anwar membingkaikan ini sebagai pembenaran agenda reformasinya dan bukti keyakinan global terhadap hala tuju ekonomi Malaysia.

🔥 Kenapa Ia Penting

Kerana pelaburan diluluskan tidak sama dengan pelaburan direalisasikan, dan orang awam Malaysia telah disuapkan tajuk tanpa perbezaan ini selama berdekad. MIDA menjejak kelulusan — pada dasarnya, surat niat dan sijil pelaburan. Wang sebenar yang mengalir ke dalam ekonomi — pelaburan langsung asing (FDI) direalisasikan — secara sejarahnya 50-60% daripada angka yang diluluskan di Malaysia. Ini bermakna sehingga separuh daripada angka tajuk ini mungkin tidak pernah diterjemahkan kepada kilang, pekerjaan, atau aktiviti ekonomi sebenar.

💩 Kenapa Ia Karut

Mari kita tepat tentang apa yang berlaku: Perdana Menteri berdiri di podium dan meraikan angka yang beliau tahu tidak mencerminkan pelaburan sebenar. Setiap laporan tahunan MIDA membezakan antara pelaburan diluluskan dan direalisasikan. Setiap ahli ekonomi serius di Malaysia tahu jurang realisasi. Anwar — seorang yang telah berada dalam dan sekitar penggubalan dasar ekonomi Malaysia selama tiga dekad — tahu ini juga. Namun: tiada kadar realisasi didedahkan. Tiada pecahan berapa banyak pelaburan 2023 atau 2024 yang diluluskan sebenarnya terwujud. Tiada metrik akauntabiliti. Hanya angka terbesar, tajuk terbaik, dan dakwaan “keyakinan global.”

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini bukan baru. Setiap kerajaan Malaysia bermain permainan diluluskan-lawan-direalisasikan. Di bawah Najib, pelaburan diluluskan kerap melebihi RM200B+ setahun, tetapi FDI direalisasikan ketinggalan jauh di belakang. Coraknya sentiasa sama: umumkan angka terbesar yang mungkin di sidang akhbar, biar media siarkan tajuk, dan tidak pernah membuat susulan dengan apa yang sebenarnya berlaku.

🎯 Panggilan PH

Anda menjanjikan ketelusan dan akauntabiliti sebagai jenama anda. Jadi mana laporan realisasi? Mana pecahan berapa banyak pelaburan 2023 dan 2024 yang diluluskan sebenarnya muncul sebagai wang sebenar, kilang sebenar, dan pekerjaan sebenar? Jika anda mahu penghargaan untuk RM426.7 bilion, tunjukkan resit — bukan jemputan. Sehingga itu, ini teater ekonomi yang sama yang rakyat Malaysia tonton sejak 1990-an, hanya dengan cat reformis.

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 What Happened

On March 6, 2026, Prime Minister and Finance Minister Anwar Ibrahim told reporters that Malaysia can maintain the subsidised RON95 petrol price at RM1.99 per litre under the Budi95 scheme — but only for ‘one or two months.’ His exact words: ‘We will try to control the effects of the Iranian conflict, including for RON95, which is RM1.99 per litre. We will monitor this situation because so far, we can still hold off for one or two months.’ This came the same week that RON97 petrol rose 10 sen to RM3.25 per litre, unsubsidised RON95 climbed 8 sen to RM2.67, and diesel in Peninsular Malaysia jumped 8 sen to RM3.12 — all driven by the escalating Middle East conflict and 200 vessels stuck in the Strait of Hormuz.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the first time Anwar has publicly put a time limit on the government’s ability to hold fuel subsidies. The hedging language — ‘we will try,’ ‘hold off,’ ‘monitor’ — is a significant shift from previous assurances. If the Strait of Hormuz disruption continues, and 20% of the world’s oil supply remains at risk, the RM1.99 price becomes fiscally unsustainable. But Anwar cannot politically afford a fuel price hike — especially not with Hari Raya weeks away, snap election rumours swirling for July, and RM15 billion in STR cash handouts already deployed. He’s caught between fiscal reality and electoral survival, and the ‘one or two months’ language tells you which one he’s prioritising.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent three years telling Malaysians that Budi95’s targeted subsidy system was a masterstroke of fiscal reform — smarter, fairer, and more sustainable than blanket subsidies. Now, the moment global oil prices actually test the system, he’s already hedging and saying he can only hold it for a couple of months. What happened to the RM800 million in savings the government claimed from Budi95? What happened to Petronas’s RM32 billion dividend to the government? The truth is that the targeted subsidy was always designed for calm seas. The moment a real shock hits — which anyone tracking Middle East geopolitics could have predicted — the whole system starts to wobble. And instead of a plan, we get ‘we will try.’

📜 Previous Cases

This is a recurring pattern with Anwar on fuel policy: promise stability, deliver uncertainty. In early March, he told Dewan Negara the government would ’try to keep’ RON95 at RM1.99 — vague language that gave no timeline. Fuel prices have been creeping up steadily since January 2026, with diesel alone rising from RM2.84 in mid-January to RM3.12 by early March. Each week brings a new price adjustment upward, while the government insists the situation is ‘under control.’ It mirrors the BN-era playbook: reassure the public while the numbers quietly deteriorate, then act surprised when the dam breaks.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians that Budi95 was reform. You told them targeted subsidies would protect the rakyat while saving the government money. Now you’re admitting, publicly, that the system can only survive ‘one or two months’ of real global pressure. If this is fiscal reform, it’s built on cardboard. Where is the contingency plan? Where is the honest conversation with the public about what happens if oil hits USD100 a barrel and stays there? Stop managing headlines and start managing the economy. The rakyat deserves more than ‘we will try’ from the man who is simultaneously Prime Minister and Finance Minister.

Anwar Boasts RM426.7B 'Record' Approved Investments — But 'Approved' Doesn't Mean 'Arrived'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On March 6, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced that Malaysia recorded its highest-ever approved investments of RM426.7 billion in 2025 — an 11% increase from RM384.4 billion in 2024. According to MIDA, 8,390 projects were approved, with the potential to create 244,902 jobs. Anwar framed this as a vindication of his reform agenda and evidence of global confidence in Malaysia’s economic direction.

The announcement was made at the launch of the Capital Market Masterplan 2026-2030, with Anwar also serving as Finance Minister — a dual role he has held since taking power in 2022.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Because approved investments are not the same as realized investments, and the Malaysian public has been fed this distinction-free headline for decades.

MIDA tracks approvals — essentially, letters of intent and investment certificates. These are commitments on paper. The actual money flowing into the economy — foreign direct investment (FDI) realized — has historically been 50-60% of approved figures in Malaysia. That means up to half of these headline numbers may never translate into actual factories, jobs, or economic activity.

Anwar is using approved investment figures the same way every Malaysian PM before him has: as a political headline, not an economic reality check. The 244,902 jobs are potential jobs — not confirmed, not created, not guaranteed.

This matters because Malaysians are living through cost-of-living pressures, stagnant real wages, and subsidy rationalization. A headline number that may be half-real does not pay anyone’s bills.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Let’s be precise about what happened here: the Prime Minister stood at a podium and celebrated a number that he knows does not reflect actual investment.

Every MIDA annual report distinguishes between approved and realized investments. Every serious economist in Malaysia knows the realization gap. Anwar — a man who has been in and around Malaysian economic policymaking for three decades — knows this too.

And yet: no realization rate was disclosed. No breakdown of how much of 2023 or 2024’s approved investments actually materialized. No accountability metric. Just the biggest number, the best headline, and a claim of “global confidence.”

This is the economic equivalent of counting RSVPs as attendance. You don’t get to claim 244,902 jobs when those jobs exist only in approval letters. You don’t get to claim RM426.7 billion when a significant chunk of it may never arrive.

The UNCTAD Global Investment Trends Monitor (January 2026) itself noted that much of global FDI growth was concentrated in financial centres rather than productive assets. MIDA’s own press release acknowledged this — then quietly moved on to the headline number.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

This is not new. Every Malaysian government has played the approved-vs-realized game:

  • Under Najib, approved investments regularly topped RM200B+ annually, but realized FDI lagged significantly behind.
  • Under Mahathir 2.0, the same pattern continued — big approvals, quiet underperformance.
  • Anwar’s own government has not published a comprehensive, transparent realization audit since taking office. The pattern is always the same: announce the biggest possible number at a press conference, let the media run the headline, and never follow up with what actually happened. No PM has ever been held accountable for the gap between approved and realized investments.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You promised transparency and accountability as your brand. You said you were different from BN. You said you wouldn’t spin numbers.

So where is the realization report? Where is the breakdown of how much of 2023’s and 2024’s approved investments actually showed up as real money, real factories, and real jobs?

If you want credit for RM426.7 billion, show the receipts — not the invitations. Until then, this is the same economic theatre Malaysians have been watching since the 1990s, just with a reformist coat of paint.

[BM] Hampir RM1 Bilion Dibayar Untuk Ganti Rugi Mahkamah Akibat Kecuaian Kerajaan — Penyelesaian Anwar? Surat Teguran

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Perdana Menteri dan Menteri Kewangan Anwar Ibrahim mendedahkan dalam jawapan bertulis parlimen — menjawab soalan William Leong (Selayang) dari PH sendiri — bahawa kerajaan Malaysia membayar RM933,360,633 dalam ganti rugi yang diarahkan mahkamah antara 2020 dan 2025. Bayaran ini adalah akibat kecuaian, salah laku dan kesalahan oleh pegawai dan agensi kerajaan. Anwar menggariskan langkah-langkah termasuk mengarahkan kementerian menjalankan siasatan dan “mencadangkan tindakan disiplin” — dan secara mengagumkan, mengeluarkan surat teguran.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Hampir RM1 bilion wang rakyat diserahkan kerana pegawai kerajaan cuai, tidak bertanggungjawab atau rasuah dalam menjalankan tugas — dan mekanisme akauntabiliti yang dicadangkan kerajaan adalah surat yang ditulis dengan nada tegas. Angka ini merangkumi pentadbiran Muhyiddin, Ismail Sabri, dan kini Anwar sendiri. Hakikat pendedahan ini dibuat secara terpendam dalam jawapan bertulis parlimen dan bukannya diumumkan secara proaktif memberitahu kamu segala-galanya tentang betapa seriusnya kerajaan ini mengambil akauntabiliti fiskal.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Surat teguran. Untuk hampir satu bilion ringgit kerugian akibat kecuaian kerajaan. Biar ia meresap. Dalam sektor swasta, satu kes kecuaian profesional yang mengakibatkan kerugian kewangan besar akan mencetuskan pemecatan, saman dan tindakan kawal selia. Dalam Malaysia Anwar, ia mencetuskan memo yang tegas. Ini bukan akauntabiliti — ini sandiwara. Jika PH serius tentang reformasi, ia akan menamakan kementerian yang terlibat, mendedahkan kes-kes individu, mengenakan surcaj kepada pegawai yang bertanggungjawab dan merujuk kecuaian jenayah kepada pihak berkuasa. Sebaliknya, Perdana Menteri memfailkan jawapan bertulis yang senyap dan meneruskan langkah. Rakyat membayar bil; tiada siapa membayar harganya.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Malaysia mempunyai sejarah panjang kecuaian kerajaan yang merugikan pembayar cukai berbilion-bilion — dari skandal PKFZ (RM12.5 bilion dalam lebihan kos) hingga saga projek kerajaan terbengkalai yang tidak berkesudahan. PH sendiri berkempen untuk menamatkan kitaran ini pada 2018 dan 2022. Semasa PH 1.0 di bawah Mahathir, kerajaan menjalankan audit perbelanjaan era 1MDB tetapi secara senyap menangguhkan tindakan susulan penguatkuasaan. Kini di bawah PH 2.0, kerajaan Anwar mendedahkan kerugian tetapi mencadangkan pemulihan yang begitu lemah sehingga menjadi jemputan untuk terus melakukannya.

🎯 Seruan Kepada PH

Kamu berjanji kepada rakyat kamu akan berbeza. Kamu kata akan ada akibat. Kamu kata tadbir urus bersih bukan slogan — ia komitmen. Jadi di mana akibat untuk hampir RM1 bilion ganti rugi yang diarahkan mahkamah yang disebabkan oleh jentera kerajaan kamu sendiri? Surat teguran adalah apa yang kamu beri kepada seseorang yang letak kereta di tempat salah, bukan pegawai yang kecuaiannya merugikan negara ratusan juta. Namakan kementerian. Terbitkan kes-kes. Kenakan surcaj kepada pegawai. Tunjukkan kepada negara bahawa “Madani” bermakna sesuatu selain logo di kepala surat kerajaan.

[BM] SPRM Siasat Rafizi Atas Perjanjian ARM Holdings RM1.1 Bilion — Pembantunya Lari Ke UK

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, Former Economy Minister; Azam Baki, MACC Chief

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU

Pada Februari-Mac 2026, SPRM melancarkan siasatan rasmi terhadap perjanjian kerajaan bernilai RM1.1 bilion dengan gergasi cip UK ARM Holdings, yang ditandatangani pada Mac 2025 semasa tempoh Rafizi Ramli sebagai Menteri Ekonomi. Perjanjian itu melibatkan Lembaga Pembangunan Pelaburan Malaysia (MIDA) memperoleh lesen harta intelek dan subsistem pengkomputeran bernilai AS$250 juta, dibayar selama 10 tahun.

Ketua SPRM Azam Baki mengesahkan 12 saksi daripada pelbagai agensi kerajaan telah memberi keterangan. Lebih dramatik, SPRM mengeluarkan notis awam mencari bekas pembantu Rafizi, Chai Jin Shern (James Chai), yang telah meninggalkan Malaysia dan bekerja di UK. 🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING

Perjanjian RM1.1 bilion adalah wang yang serius. Jika terdapat unsur penyalahgunaan kuasa, penipuan atau kegagalan tadbir urus dalam cara perjanjian ini distrukturkan, rakyat Malaysia berhak mendapat jawapan. Hakikat bekas pembantu Rafizi telah meninggalkan negara menimbulkan persoalan jelas.

Tetapi masalah yang lebih mendalam: pemasaannya mencurigakan. Siasatan ini datang selepas Rafizi kalah dalam pertandingan timbalan presiden PKR kepada Nurul Izzah dan meletak jawatan Kabinet. 💩 KENAPA INI KARUT

Karut di sini berlapis. Jika perjanjian itu benar-benar bermasalah, kenapa SPRM tidak menyiasatnya semasa ia ditandatangani pada Mac 2025? Kenapa tunggu sehingga Rafizi keluar Kabinet dan lemah secara politik? Pemasaan mencadangkan ini sama ada balasan atau oportunistik.

Tetapi Rafizi juga tidak terlepas. Pembelaannya — “ia melalui Kabinet” — adalah tepat apa yang setiap menteri katakan apabila ditangkap. Kelulusan Kabinet tidak mengimunkan sesuatu perjanjian daripada penelitian. Dan hakikat pembantunya melarikan diri dari negara bukan rupa yang bagus untuk seseorang yang membina kerjayanya atas ketelusan.

Seluruh sirkus ini adalah kajian kes kegagalan institusi Malaysia: agensi antirasuah yang terjejas menyiasat reformis yang cedera secara politik, sementara PM memanggil Bloomberg komplot Zionis kerana mendedahkan ketua SPRM.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH

Kamu menjanjikan SPRM yang bebas. Kamu kekalkan Azam Baki — seorang dengan dua skandal pegangan saham — sebagai ketua. Kini ketua SPRM yang terjejas sama sedang menyiasat bekas menteri kamu sendiri dalam apa yang kelihatan seperti siasatan bermasa politik. Kamu tidak boleh menyiasat rasuah secara sahih apabila ketua agensi antirasuah kamu sendiri sedang disiasat.

MACC Investigates Rafizi Over RM1.1 Billion ARM Holdings Deal — His Aide Flees to UK

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, Former Economy Minister; Azam Baki, MACC Chief

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

In February-March 2026, MACC launched a formal investigation into a RM1.1 billion government deal with UK chip giant ARM Holdings, signed in March 2025 during Rafizi Ramli’s tenure as Economy Minister. The deal involved Malaysia Investment Development Authority (MIDA) obtaining intellectual property licences and compute subsystems valued at US$250 million, payable over 10 years.

MACC chief Azam Baki confirmed that 12 witnesses from various government agencies had given statements. More dramatically, MACC issued a public notice seeking Rafizi’s former aide, Chai Jin Shern (James Chai), who had left Malaysia and was working in the UK. MACC ordered Chai to return and cooperate with the investigation.

Rafizi called the investigation “politically motivated” and said it was “odd” that MACC hadn’t summoned him directly despite the probe running for over a month. He said both the Silicon Vision Programme and the National Energy Transition Roadmap went through multiple layers of government approval, including Cabinet endorsement.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

A RM1.1 billion deal is serious money. If there were elements of abuse of power, fraud, or governance failures in how this deal was structured, Malaysians deserve answers. The fact that Rafizi’s former aide has left the country and is unresponsive to MACC’s attempts to contact him raises obvious questions about what he might know.

But here’s the deeper problem: the timing stinks. This investigation comes after Rafizi lost his PKR deputy presidency race to Nurul Izzah and resigned from Cabinet. It comes while Azam Baki himself is under investigation for his own shareholding scandal. The appearance of MACC being weaponised against a political figure who just lost an internal power struggle is impossible to ignore.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

The bullshit here is layered. If the deal was genuinely problematic, why didn’t MACC investigate it when it was signed in March 2025? Why wait until Rafizi is out of Cabinet and politically weakened? The timing suggests this is either retaliatory or opportunistic — neither of which inspires confidence in MACC’s independence.

But Rafizi doesn’t get a pass either. His defence — “it went through Cabinet” — is exactly what every minister says when caught out. Cabinet approval doesn’t immunise a deal from scrutiny. And the fact that his aide fled the country is not a great look for someone who built his career on transparency and accountability.

The whole circus is a case study in Malaysian institutional failure: a compromised anti-corruption agency investigating a politically wounded reformist, while the PM calls Bloomberg a Zionist plot for exposing the MACC chief. Nobody comes out clean.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Malaysia has a long history of politically timed MACC investigations. Under Najib, MACC was accused of going after opposition figures while ignoring 1MDB. Under PH 1.0, MACC went after BN figures. Now, under PH 2.0, MACC appears to be going after a fallen PH insider. The institution remains a political tool regardless of who holds power.

Rafizi himself was once MACC’s loudest champion when it investigated BN. Now he’s calling the same agency politically motivated. The irony writes itself.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You promised an independent MACC. You kept Azam Baki — a man with two shareholding scandals — as chief. Now that same compromised MACC chief is investigating your own former minister in what looks like a politically timed probe. If you wanted Malaysians to trust MACC investigations, you should have cleaned house first. You can’t credibly investigate corruption when the head of your anti-corruption agency is himself under investigation. Fix the institution, then go after the wrongdoers. In that order.

Nearly RM1 Billion Paid in Court Damages for Government Negligence — Anwar's Fix? Letters of Reprimand

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister and Finance Minister Anwar Ibrahim disclosed in a written parliamentary reply — responding to PH’s own William Leong (Selayang) — that the Malaysian government paid out RM933,360,633 in court-awarded damages between 2020 and 2025. The payouts were the result of negligence, misconduct, and other wrongdoing by government officials and agencies. Anwar outlined measures including instructing ministries to conduct investigations into the cases and “recommend disciplinary action” against those found negligent — and, spectacularly, issuing letters of reprimand.

🔥 Why It Matters

Nearly RM1 billion of public money was handed over because government officials were negligent, irresponsible, or outright corrupt in carrying out their duties — and the government’s proposed accountability mechanism is a sternly worded letter. This figure spans the administrations of Muhyiddin, Ismail Sabri, and now Anwar himself. The fact that this disclosure came buried in a written parliamentary reply rather than proactively announced tells you everything about how seriously this government takes fiscal accountability. PH promised to end the culture of impunity. Instead, it’s inherited it and furnished it with a fresh coat of bureaucratic language.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Letters of reprimand. For nearly a billion ringgit in losses caused by government negligence. Let that sink in. In the private sector, a single case of professional negligence resulting in significant financial loss would trigger terminations, lawsuits, and regulatory action. In Anwar’s Malaysia, it triggers a stern memo. This isn’t accountability — it’s theatre. If PH were serious about reform, it would name the ministries involved, disclose the individual cases, surcharge the officers responsible, and refer criminal negligence to the relevant authorities. Instead, the Prime Minister filed a quiet written reply and moved on. The rakyat pays the bill; nobody pays the price.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long, inglorious history of government negligence costing taxpayers billions — from the PKFZ scandal (RM12.5 billion in cost overruns and soft loans) to the endless saga of abandoned government projects and botched procurement exercises under successive administrations. PH itself campaigned on ending this cycle in both 2018 and 2022. During PH 1.0 under Mahathir, the government conducted an audit of 1MDB-era spending but quietly shelved follow-up enforcement. Now under PH 2.0, Anwar’s government discloses the losses but proposes remedies so weak they amount to an invitation to keep doing it.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised the rakyat you would be different. You said there would be consequences. You said clean governance wasn’t a slogan — it was a commitment. So where are the consequences for nearly RM1 billion in court-ordered damages caused by your own government machinery? Letters of reprimand are what you give someone who parks in the wrong spot, not officials whose negligence cost the nation hundreds of millions. Name the ministries. Publish the cases. Surcharge the officers. Show the country that “Madani” means something beyond a logo on a government letterhead.
