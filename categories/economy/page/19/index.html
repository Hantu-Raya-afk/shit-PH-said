[BM] Kekurangan Beras Memperdalam Kebimbangan Keselamatan Makanan — Anwar Mahu 'Lebih Pemantauan'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-11-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada akhir 2024, kekurangan beras secara sporadik melanda Malaysia bersama banjir yang dahsyat. Rak pasar raya kehabisan jenis beras tempatan di pelbagai negeri. Tindak balas Anwar adalah menyeru “pemantauan dan penguatkuasaan yang kerap” — respons lalai kerajaan apabila tiada rancangan. Malaysia mengimport lebih 30% bekalan berasnya, dan industri padi domestik telah diabaikan selama berdekad. Harga beras global mencecah paras tertinggi berbilang tahun disebabkan sekatan eksport India, kesan El Niño terhadap tuaian Asia Tenggara, dan gangguan rantaian bekalan.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Beras adalah makanan ruji bagi 33 juta rakyat Malaysia. Apabila rak beras kosong, keselamatan makanan bukan teori — ia segera. Kebergantungan import Malaysia melebihi 30% bermakna sebarang gangguan bekalan global secara langsung mengancam keselamatan makanan domestik. Industri padi telah kekurangan dana, berpecah, dan diuruskan secara politik selama berdekad. Petani kecil menerima sokongan yang tidak mencukupi. Infrastruktur pengairan reput. Hasil setiap hektar ketinggalan di belakang Thailand, Vietnam, dan Indonesia. Kekurangan beras itu boleh dijangka — dan kerajaan tidak bersedia.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: “Pemantauan dan penguatkuasaan” adalah apa yang anda lakukan apabila tiada rancangan. Ia setara birokrasi dengan memerhati masalah bertambah buruk sambil kelihatan sibuk. PH menjanjikan reformasi keselamatan makanan. Tiga tahun kemudian, kebergantungan import beras Malaysia tidak berkurang, pengeluaran domestik tidak meningkat secara bermakna, dan tindak balas kerajaan terhadap kekurangan adalah penguatkuasaan — menghukum peniaga kerana menyorok berbanding menangani defisit bekalan struktur. Anda tidak boleh menguatkuasakan beras ke dalam kewujudan. Anda perlu menanamnya, dan itu memerlukan pelaburan yang kerajaan tidak buat.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia telah menghadapi kekurangan beras berkala di bawah setiap kerajaan. Di bawah BN, tindak balas sentiasa sama: lepaskan rizab strategik, salahkan penyorok, janjikan pelaburan dalam padi. Di bawah PH 1.0, keselamatan makanan sebentar dinaikkan sebagai keutamaan dasar sebelum Langkah Sheraton. Di bawah PH 2.0, kerajaan melancarkan inisiatif “keselamatan makanan” yang menghasilkan sidang akhbar tetapi tiada penambahbaikan yang boleh diukur dalam nisbah sara diri. Masalah struktur — penurunan keluasan padi, petani yang semakin tua, pengairan tidak mencukupi, dan kebergantungan import — kekal sama seperti 20 tahun lalu.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Rak beras kosong. Tindak balas anda adalah “pemantauan.” Malaysia mengimport 30%+ berasnya dan anda tidak melakukan apa-apa untuk mengurangkan kebergantungan itu dalam tiga tahun. Laburkan dalam infrastruktur padi — pengairan, mekanisasi, penambahbaikan hasil. Sokong petani kecil dengan harga minimum terjamin. Bina rizab strategik yang boleh menyerap kejutan bekalan global. Atau terus “memantau” sementara rakyat Malaysia tidak dapat mencari beras di pasar raya — dan harap mereka tidak perasan bahawa “pemantauan” bukan keselamatan makanan.

Rice Shortages Deepen Food Security Fears — Anwar Wants 'More Monitoring'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-11-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late 2024, sporadic rice shortages hit Malaysia alongside devastating floods. Supermarket shelves emptied of local rice varieties in multiple states. Anwar’s response was to call for “frequent monitoring and enforcement” — the government’s default when it has no plan. Malaysia imports over 30% of its rice supply, and the domestic padi industry has been neglected for decades. Global rice prices hit multi-year highs due to India’s export restrictions, El Niño impacts on Southeast Asian harvests, and supply chain disruptions.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rice is the staple food for 33 million Malaysians. When rice shelves are empty, food security is not theoretical — it is immediate. Malaysia’s 30%+ import dependency means any global supply disruption directly threatens domestic food security. The padi industry has been underfunded, fragmented, and politically managed for decades. Smallholder farmers receive insufficient support. Irrigation infrastructure is crumbling. Yields per hectare lag behind Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The rice shortage was predictable — and the government was not prepared.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: “Monitoring and enforcement” is what you do when you have no plan. It is the bureaucratic equivalent of watching the problem get worse while looking busy. PH promised food security reform. Three years later, Malaysia’s rice import dependency hasn’t decreased, domestic production hasn’t meaningfully increased, and the government’s response to shortages is enforcement — punishing traders for hoarding rather than addressing the structural supply deficit. You can’t enforce rice into existence. You need to grow it, and that requires investment the government hasn’t made.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has faced periodic rice shortages under every government. Under BN, the response was always the same: release strategic reserves, blame hoarders, promise investment in padi. Under PH 1.0, food security was briefly elevated as a policy priority before the Sheraton Move. Under PH 2.0, the government launched a “food security” initiative that generated press conferences but no measurable improvement in self-sufficiency ratios. The structural problem — declining padi acreage, aging farmers, insufficient irrigation, and reliance on imports — remains identical to what it was 20 years ago.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Rice shelves are empty. Your response is “monitoring.” Malaysia imports 30%+ of its rice and you’ve done nothing to reduce that dependency in three years. Invest in padi infrastructure — irrigation, mechanisation, yield improvement. Support smallholder farmers with guaranteed minimum prices. Build strategic reserves that can absorb global supply shocks. Or keep “monitoring” while Malaysians can’t find rice at the supermarket — and hope they don’t notice that “monitoring” is not food security.

[BM] Sistem Levi Pekerja Asing Pelbagai Peringkat — Diumumkan 2023, Masih Belum Dilaksanakan Sepenuhnya

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-10-18

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada September 2023, Anwar mengumumkan sistem levi pelbagai peringkat untuk pekerja asing yang direka bagi mengurangkan pergantungan Malaysia terhadap tenaga kerja import. Sistem ini diulang dalam Belanjawan 2025 dengan butiran lanjut. Persekutuan Majikan-Majikan Malaysia (MEF) mengadu sistem itu masih tidak adil dan pelaksanaannya tidak mencukupi. Janji Anwar untuk memotong pergantungan pekerja asing kepada 15% tenaga kerja menjelang 2025 tidak dipenuhi. Sehingga akhir 2025, Malaysia masih menggaji kira-kira 2.2 juta pekerja asing berdokumen, dengan anggaran 1-2 juta tidak berdokumen — pergantungan yang tidak berubah secara bermakna di bawah PH.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Pergantungan pekerja asing menekan gaji tempatan, terutamanya bagi rakyat Malaysia B40 yang bersaing secara langsung dengan tenaga kerja import dalam pembinaan, pertanian, pembuatan dan perkhidmatan. Levi pelbagai peringkat sepatutnya menjadikannya semakin mahal bagi majikan untuk mengambil pekerja asing, memberi insentif kepada automasi, peningkatan produktiviti dan pengambilan tempatan. Dua tahun selepas pengumuman, sistem itu masih belum dilaksanakan sepenuhnya. Sementara itu, industri terus mengimport pekerja pada tahap yang melemahkan lantai gaji untuk warganegara Malaysia.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Mengumumkan dasar dan melaksanakannya adalah dua perkara berbeza — dan PH mahir dalam yang pertama sambil gagal dalam yang kedua. Levi pelbagai peringkat diumumkan pada 2023, diulang pada 2024, dan dimasukkan dalam Belanjawan 2025. Pada setiap peringkat, ia dibentangkan sebagai inisiatif baru. Sasaran 15% secara senyap digugurkan apabila jelas ia tidak akan dicapai. Aduan MEF mengesyorkan bahawa bahagian yang dilaksanakan pun direka dengan buruk. Lobi pekerja asing — mewakili syarikat perladangan, firma pembinaan dan pengilang dengan hubungan politik — telah berjaya menangguhkan reformasi bermakna sementara pengumuman diteruskan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Setiap kerajaan Malaysia telah berjanji mengurangkan pergantungan pekerja asing. Di bawah Mahathir 1.0, janji itu dibuat pada 1990-an. Di bawah Najib, pelbagai kenaikan levi diumumkan. Di bawah PH 1.0, pembekuan permit pekerja asing baru dilaksanakan secara ringkas sebelum ditarik balik. Di bawah PH 2.0, levi pelbagai peringkat diumumkan dengan penuh gembar-gembur tetapi pelaksanaan sangat perlahan. Coraknya konsisten: umumkan reformasi, hadapi tentangan industri, tangguhkan pelaksanaan, umumkan semula dalam belanjawan seterusnya. Sementara itu, 3-4 juta pekerja asing kekal di negara ini.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda mengumumkan levi pelbagai peringkat pada 2023. Sekarang 2026 dan ia masih belum dilaksanakan sepenuhnya. Sasaran 15% anda tidak tercapai. MEF kata sistem itu tidak adil. Industri terus mengimport pekerja pada kadar yang sama. Sama ada laksanakan levi dengan ketegasan — penguatkuasaan sebenar, kenaikan kos bermakna untuk majikan yang terlalu bergantung kepada tenaga kerja asing — atau akui bahawa pengumuman itu untuk tajuk berita, bukan untuk dasar. Pekerja Malaysia layak mendapat perlindungan gaji tempatan. Mereka tidak mendapatnya kerana kerajaan anda lebih takut mengganggu lobi perladangan daripada melindungi rakyat.

Foreign Worker Levy Multi-Tier System — Announced 2023, Still Not Fully Implemented

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-10-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In September 2023, Anwar announced a multi-tier levy system for foreign workers designed to reduce Malaysia’s dependency on imported labour. The system was repeated in Budget 2025 with further details. The Malaysian Employers Federation (MEF) complained the system was still unfair and insufficiently implemented. Anwar’s promise to cut foreign worker dependency to 15% of the workforce by 2025 was not met. As of late 2025, Malaysia still employed approximately 2.2 million documented foreign workers, with an estimated 1-2 million undocumented — a dependency that has not meaningfully changed under PH.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Foreign worker dependency suppresses local wages, particularly for B40 Malaysians who compete directly with imported labour in construction, agriculture, manufacturing, and services. The multi-tier levy was supposed to make it progressively more expensive for employers to hire foreign workers, incentivising automation, productivity improvements, and local hiring. Two years after announcement, the system is still not fully implemented. Meanwhile, industries continue importing workers at levels that undercut the wage floor for Malaysian citizens.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Announcing a policy and implementing it are two different things — and PH excels at the former while failing at the latter. The multi-tier levy was announced in 2023, repeated in 2024, and included in Budget 2025. At each stage, it was presented as a new initiative. The 15% target was quietly dropped when it became clear it wouldn’t be met. MEF’s complaints suggest that even the implemented portions are poorly designed. The foreign worker lobby — representing plantation companies, construction firms, and manufacturers with political connections — has successfully delayed meaningful reform while the announcements continue.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian government has promised to reduce foreign worker dependency. Under Mahathir 1.0, the promise was made in the 1990s. Under Najib, various levy increases were announced. Under PH 1.0, a freeze on new foreign worker permits was briefly implemented before being reversed. Under PH 2.0, the multi-tier levy was announced with fanfare but implementation has been glacial. The pattern is consistent: announce reform, face industry pushback, delay implementation, announce it again in the next budget. Meanwhile, 3-4 million foreign workers remain in the country.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You announced the multi-tier levy in 2023. It’s 2026 and it’s still not fully implemented. Your 15% target was missed. MEF says the system is unfair. Industries keep importing workers at the same rate. Either implement the levy with teeth — real enforcement, meaningful cost increases for employers who over-rely on foreign labour — or admit that the announcement was for headlines, not for policy. Malaysian workers deserve local wage protection. They’re not getting it because your government is more afraid of upsetting the plantation lobby than protecting the rakyat.

[BM] Zon Kewangan Khas Forest City — Cukai 0% Untuk Golongan Kaya Raya Sementara Rakyat Bayar SST 8%

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Securities Commission

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-10-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada Oktober 2024, kerajaan PH melancarkan Zon Kewangan Khas (ZKK) Forest City di Johor, menawarkan cukai korporat 0% sehingga 15 tahun untuk perniagaan yang layak, cukai 0% untuk pejabat keluarga bernilai tinggi, dan insentif untuk syarikat fintech, pengurusan dana dan aset digital. ZKK direka untuk menyelamatkan Forest City — projek hartanah bernilai RM400 bilion yang disokong China yang kebanyakannya kosong — dengan mengubahnya menjadi hab kewangan. Sementara itu, rakyat Malaysia biasa membayar SST 8% ke atas perkhidmatan harian termasuk penjagaan kesihatan, pendidikan dan sewa.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: ZKK mewujudkan sistem cukai dua peringkat: 0% untuk golongan kaya raya yang meletak kekayaan mereka di Forest City, dan SST 8% untuk rakyat Malaysia biasa yang membeli barangan runcit, berjumpa doktor, atau menghantar anak-anak ke sekolah. Optiknya merosakkan — kerajaan pada masa yang sama mengembangkan SST untuk meliputi keperluan asas sambil menawarkan status bebas cukai kepada pejabat keluarga bilionair. Ini bukan tarikan pelaburan — ini adalah tempat perlindungan kekayaan yang dijenamakan sebagai pembangunan ekonomi.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Forest City adalah projek hartanah yang gagal. Berbilion dolar pelaburan China menghasilkan bandar hantu yang Mahathir sendiri ejek. Daripada membiarkan pasaran menilai kegagalan itu, kerajaan Anwar menggunakan dasar awam — pengecualian cukai, insentif pengawalseliaan, perbelanjaan infrastruktur — untuk menyelamatkan pembangunan swasta. Penerima manfaat adalah pemaju hartanah dan pelabur kaya raya. Kos ditanggung oleh pembayar cukai Malaysia yang kehilangan hasil yang perniagaan dikecualikan cukai akan sumbangkan. Menyosialisasikan kerugian dan menswastakan keuntungan adalah definisi kapitalisme kroni — dan PH berjanji untuk menamatkannya.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia mempunyai sejarah panjang zon ekonomi khas yang menjanjikan transformasi dan menyampaikan pemberian cukai. Wilayah Pembangunan Iskandar menawarkan insentif cukai selama lebih sedekad — ketidaksamaan Johor bertambah buruk. Pusat kewangan luar pesisir Labuan menarik syarikat cangkerang lebih daripada pelaburan tulen. Cyberjaya sepatutnya menjadi Silicon Valley Malaysia — ia menjadi taman pejabat. Setiap zon mengikut corak yang sama: insentif cukai yang murah hati, mekanisme pemulangan minimum, dan manfaat yang terakru kepada modal bukan buruh. ZKK Forest City menambah bab lain kepada corak ini.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda menawarkan cukai 0% kepada pejabat keluarga bilionair sambil mengenakan SST 8% ke atas penjagaan kesihatan dan pendidikan rakyat. Anda menyelamatkan projek hartanah China yang gagal dengan insentif dasar awam. Anda menyosialisasikan kerugian dan menswastakan keuntungan — tepat seperti yang anda tuduh BN lakukan. Terbitkan analisis kos-manfaat ZKK. Tunjukkan berapa banyak pekerjaan Malaysia yang akan diwujudkan berbanding berapa banyak hasil cukai Malaysia hilang. Jelaskan mengapa pejabat keluarga kaya raya layak mendapat cukai 0% sedangkan peniaga kecil membayar SST. Atau akui bahawa ZKK adalah penyelamatan untuk pemaju, bukan strategi ekonomi untuk Malaysia.

Forest City Special Financial Zone — 0% Tax for the Ultra-Rich While Rakyat Pays 8% SST

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Securities Commission

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-10-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In October 2024, the PH government launched the Forest City Special Financial Zone (SFZ) in Johor, offering 0% corporate tax for up to 15 years for qualifying businesses, 0% tax for ultra-high-net-worth family offices, and incentives for fintech, fund management, and digital asset companies. The SFZ was designed to rescue Forest City — a RM400 billion Chinese-backed property development that sat largely empty — by transforming it into a financial hub. Meanwhile, ordinary Malaysians pay 8% SST on everyday services including healthcare, education, and rent.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The SFZ creates a two-tier tax system: 0% for the ultra-rich who park their wealth in Forest City, and 8% SST for ordinary Malaysians buying groceries, seeing a doctor, or sending their children to school. The optics are devastating — the government is simultaneously expanding SST to cover basic necessities while offering tax-free status to billionaires’ family offices. This is not investment attraction — it is a wealth shelter branded as economic development.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Forest City was a failed property project. Billions of dollars in Chinese investment produced a ghost city that Mahathir himself mocked. Instead of letting the market price in the failure, the Anwar government is using public policy — tax exemptions, regulatory incentives, infrastructure spending — to rescue a private development. The beneficiaries are property developers and ultra-wealthy investors. The cost is borne by Malaysian taxpayers who lose the revenue that tax-exempt businesses would otherwise contribute. Socialising losses and privatising gains is the definition of crony capitalism — and PH promised to end it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a long history of special economic zones that promised transformation and delivered tax giveaways. The Iskandar Development Region offered tax incentives for over a decade — Johor’s inequality worsened. Labuan’s offshore financial centre attracted shell companies more than genuine investment. Cyberjaya was supposed to be Malaysia’s Silicon Valley — it became an office park. Each zone follows the same pattern: generous tax incentives, minimal clawback mechanisms, and benefits that accrue to capital rather than labour. Forest City SFZ adds another chapter to this pattern.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You’re offering 0% tax to billionaires’ family offices while charging 8% SST on the rakyat’s healthcare and education. You’re rescuing a failed Chinese property project with public policy incentives. You’re socialising the losses and privatising the gains — exactly what you accused BN of doing. Publish a cost-benefit analysis of the SFZ. Show how many Malaysian jobs it will create versus how much tax revenue Malaysia loses. Explain why ultra-wealthy family offices deserve 0% tax when hawkers pay SST. Or admit that the SFZ is a bailout for developers, not an economic strategy for Malaysia.

[BM] Harga Diesel Melompat 56% Semalaman — Anwar Kata Pengkritik Akan Serang Beliau Walau Apa Pun

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-06-10

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 10 Jun 2024, harga diesel di Semenanjung Malaysia melompat daripada RM2.15 kepada RM3.35 seliter — kenaikan 56% semalaman. Menteri Kewangan II Amir Hamzah Azizan mengumumkan pengapungan harga pada 9 Jun, berkuat kuasa keesokan harinya — satu hari notis untuk kejutan yang merambat melalui logistik, harga makanan, dan perniagaan kecil. Sabah, Sarawak, dan Labuan kekal pada RM2.15. Anwar memberitahu wartawan: “Kita juga perlu tahu bahawa apa sahaja yang kita buat, kita akan dikritik hebat dengan segala jenis fitnah dan pembohongan.” Tindak balasnya terhadap rakyat yang menghadapi kenaikan kos 56% adalah mengadu tentang kualiti kritikan yang diterimanya.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Bil subsidi diesel telah membengkak kepada RM14.3 bilion setahun, dan kebanyakan ahli ekonomi bersetuju subsidi menyeluruh itu regresif. Dasar ini mungkin perlu dari segi ekonomi, tetapi pelaksanaannya cuai dari segi politik. Kenaikan harga 56% dengan satu hari notis adalah kejutan kepada isi rumah, perniagaan kecil, nelayan, dan petani yang tidak boleh menyerap kenaikan kos semalaman. Pelaksanaan berperingkat selama 6-12 bulan akan mencapai matlamat fiskal yang sama dengan sebahagian kecil kesakitan.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: Penolakan Anwar terhadap semua kritikan sebagai “fitnah dan pembohongan” bukan sahaja pekak nada — ia menghina. Ramai mereka yang bergelut dengan harga diesel yang lebih tinggi adalah pengundi PH. Mereka tidak memfitnah PM — mereka memberitahunya mereka tidak mampu mengisi lori mereka. Sistem harga dua peringkat mewujudkan masalah arbitraj segera — penyeludupan diesel dari Sabah dan Sarawak meningkat, menambah kos penguatkuasaan. Kerajaan menjanjikan penjimatan RM14.3 bilion akan membiayai pengangkutan awam dan bantuan bersasar. Tetapi PADU diketepi. Perluasan pengangkutan awam di luar KL kekal tidak ketara.

Diesel Price Jumps 56% Overnight — Anwar Says Critics Will Attack Him Regardless

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-06-10

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 10 June 2024, diesel prices in Peninsular Malaysia jumped from RM2.15 to RM3.35 per litre — a 56% increase overnight. Finance Minister II Amir Hamzah Azizan announced the price float on 9 June, effective the next morning — one day’s notice for a shock that rippled through logistics, food prices, and small businesses. Sabah, Sarawak, and Labuan remained at RM2.15. Anwar told reporters: “We must also know that whatever we do, we will be severely criticised with all sorts of slander and lies.” His response to citizens facing a 56% cost increase was to complain about the quality of criticism he was receiving.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The diesel subsidy bill had ballooned to RM14.3 billion annually, and most economists agreed the blanket subsidy was regressive. The policy may be economically necessary, but the execution was politically negligent. A 56% price hike with one day’s notice is a shock to households, small businesses, fishermen, and farmers who cannot absorb overnight cost increases. Phased implementation over 6-12 months would have achieved the same fiscal goal with a fraction of the pain. The Diplomat noted that diesel increases have cascading effects — logistics, food transport, construction, agriculture, and fishing all depend on diesel.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s dismissal of all criticism as “slander and lies” is not just tone-deaf — it is contemptuous. Many of the people struggling with higher diesel costs are PH voters. They are not slandering the PM — they are telling him they cannot afford to fill their trucks. The two-tier pricing system created an immediate arbitrage problem — diesel smuggling from Sabah and Sarawak increased, adding enforcement costs. The government promised RM14.3 billion in savings would fund public transport and targeted aid. But PADU was sidelined. Public transport expansion outside KL remains negligible. The money was saved, but the promised reinvestment is largely invisible to the people who sacrificed.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: When Najib attempted fuel subsidy reform in 2014, PH was among the loudest critics. When petrol prices fluctuated under the managed float system, PH accused BN of burdening the people. Now in government, PH implemented an even more aggressive subsidy cut — 56% overnight versus Najib’s gradual float — and called critics slanderers. The hypocrisy of implementing tougher measures than those you attacked your predecessors for is the defining feature of PH’s economic governance.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You raised diesel by 56% overnight with one day’s notice. Your response to criticism was to call it slander. Your promised reinvestment — public transport, targeted aid — hasn’t materialised. PADU was sidelined. Buses didn’t appear. If the rakyat must tighten their belts, show them you’re tightening yours first. Publish where the RM14.3 billion in savings went. Deliver the public transport improvements you promised. Phase future subsidy reforms gradually. Or keep imposing overnight shocks and dismissing the pain as slander.
