[BM] Mat Sabu Suruh Kakitangan Kementerian Tanam Sayur Sendiri — Pelan Keterjaminan Makanan Kerajaan Kini Kebun Belakang Rumah

Said by: Mohamad Sabu, Agriculture and Food Security Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Menteri Pertanian dan Keterjaminan Makanan Mohamad Sabu, berucap selepas mesyuarat Kabinet khas pada 13 Mac, mengarahkan kakitangan kementerian yang tinggal di rumah bertanah supaya mula menanam sayur sendiri. Beliau juga menggalakkan isi rumah yang mempunyai tanah kosong menanam tanaman cepat tuai seperti cili, terung dan kucai untuk menambah bekalan makanan. Kabinet juga bersetuju meningkatkan stok beras negara untuk menampung sembilan bulan. Kementerian kemudiannya terpaksa menjelaskan arahan menanam sayur itu “bukan arahan” selepas mendapat tentangan awam.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Ini adalah sumbangan Menteri Pertanian kepada tindak balas krisis Malaysia ketika minyak melepasi $100 setong, KLCI jatuh bawah 1700, dan inflasi makanan mempercepatkan. Daripada mengumumkan kawalan harga makanan kecemasan, subsidi yang diperluaskan untuk petani, atau campur tangan rantaian bekalan yang konkrit, pengumuman utama Mat Sabu selepas Kabinet ialah rakyat Malaysia patut menanam sayur di halaman rumah. Ia menandakan sebuah kerajaan yang kehabisan alat institusi — atau tidak pernah memilikinya — dan kini mengalihkan tanggungjawab keterjaminan makanan kepada isi rumah individu sambil mengekalkan bil subsidi bahan api RM3.2 bilion.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

PH berkuasa dengan janji reformasi institusi, tadbir urus moden, dan dasar keterjaminan makanan yang serius. Tiga tahun kemudian, pelan krisis Menteri Pertanian mereka ialah kebun kampung. Ini seorang menteri yang portfolionya merangkumi bajet RM5 bilion, FAMA, MARDI, dan keseluruhan rantaian bekalan makanan negara — dan tindak balasnya terhadap krisis tenaga global yang mengganggu rantaian bekalan ialah “tanam sayur sendiri.” Hakikat bahawa kementeriannya sendiri terpaksa menjelaskan ia “bukan arahan” memberitahu anda segalanya: birokrat sendiri tahu ini memalukan. Thailand dan Vietnam tidak menyuruh rakyat menanam terung — mereka mengarahkan kerja dari rumah, mengehadkan harga, dan mengaktifkan rizab strategik.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Ini menyusul jaminan Mat Sabu pada 12 Mac selepas Kabinet bahawa bekalan makanan “mencukupi sehingga Mei atau Jun” sambil menyuruh rakyat Malaysia jangan membeli secara panik — menteri yang sama yang telah membuat jaminan serupa sejak krisis bermula pada 28 Februari. Pada September 2025, Anwar sendiri menggesa rakyat Malaysia “bertani di rumah” dan “jangan biarkan tanah terbiar” di majlis Pasar Tani Kebangsaan. Retorik tanam-makanan-sendiri kini menjadi pengelakan berulang PH: apabila kerajaan tidak mampu menyampaikan keterjaminan makanan melalui dasar, ia menyuruh rakyat menjadi petani sara diri.

🎯 Teguran PH

Anda berjanji Malaysia Madani — sebuah kerajaan yang moden, responsif, dan cekap. Pelan krisis Menteri Pertanian anda ialah menyuruh penjawat awam menanam cili di kebun. Ini bukan dasar keterjaminan makanan. Ini kerajaan yang menggantikan sesi bergambar mesra Instagram dengan kapasiti institusi. Jika nasihat terbaik anda kepada rakyat Malaysia yang menghadapi minyak $100-setong dan inflasi makanan yang mempercepatkan ialah “tanam terung,” maka anda telah gagal dalam fungsi paling asas kementerian. Rakyat tidak mengundi untuk petua berkebun — mereka mengundi untuk tadbir urus.

[BM] Mesyuarat Khas Kabinet Untuk Krisis Minyak — Keputusan WFH Anwar? Satu Lagi Kajian, Balik Selasa

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada Jumaat, 13 Mac, kerajaan Malaysia mengadakan Mesyuarat Khas Kabinet yang dinanti-nantikan untuk menangani kesan ekonomi daripada krisis minyak Asia Barat, dengan minyak mentah Brent melepasi $100/tong dan Selat Hormuz terganggu. Menteri Komunikasi Fahmi Fadzil mengumumkan selepas itu bahawa Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim telah mengarahkan Ketua Setiausaha Negara (KSN) dan Ketua Pengarah Perkhidmatan Awam (KPPA) untuk “mengkaji cadangan WFH bagi memastikan beberapa aspek diselaraskan dengan baik.” Keputusan sama ada melaksanakan kerja dari rumah bagi penjawat awam akan dibuat pada Selasa, 17 Mac — empat hari kemudian, dan seminggu penuh selepas Thailand dan Vietnam mengarahkan WFH untuk sektor awam mereka.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Ini sepatutnya menjadi Kabinet krisis — mesyuarat yang dipanggil khusus untuk mengambil tindakan tegas terhadap kejutan minyak. Kabinet Thailand mengarahkan WFH wajib untuk pegawai kerajaan pada 10 Mac, hari yang sama Vietnam mengeluarkan arahan serupa. Kedua-dua negara bertindak dalam masa 48 jam selepas gangguan Selat Hormuz. Malaysia mengadakan mesyuarat khas tiga hari kemudian dan menghasilkan… satu lagi kajian. Corak ini tidak dapat disangkal: apabila berhadapan dengan keputusan ekonomi mendesak yang melibatkan 1.7 juta penjawat awam, kerajaan ini memilih untuk mengkaji, menyemak dan menangguhkan — walaupun semasa apa yang dipanggilnya krisis negara.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Mesyuarat khas Kabinet sepatutnya menghasilkan keputusan khas. Bukan satu lagi pusingan “mari kita selaraskan.” KSN dan KPPA telah mempunyai masa sejak sekurang-kurangnya 10 Mac — apabila Menteri Sumber Manusia Ramanan pertama kali menyebut mengkaji WFH — untuk menyediakan pilihan. Itu tiga hari bekerja penuh. Ketua Pengarah Perkhidmatan Awam sendiri memberitahu Bernama pada 12 Mac bahawa Malaysia sudah membuktikan semasa COVID-19 bahawa sektor awam boleh menyesuaikan diri dengan WFH “melalui penggunaan teknologi dan kaedah kerja baharu.” Jadi duluan ada, infrastruktur ada, permintaan ada — yang tiada ialah kemahuan politik. Kerajaan Anwar lebih sanggup membelanjakan RM2 bilion untuk subsidi bahan bakar daripada menjimatkan sebahagian kecil daripada itu dengan membenarkan penjawat awam bekerja dari rumah.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini adalah tadbir urus Madani vintaj: umumkan, kaji, tangguh, ulang. Rang undang-undang had tempoh PM dibentang, gagal, dan akan “dibentang semula pada Jun.” Reformasi subsidi bahan bakar telah “akan datang” sejak 2023. Laporan Azam Baki dibincangkan oleh Kabinet dan diserah kepada Ketua Setiausaha. Dalam setiap kes, mekanismenya sama — wujudkan penampilan tindakan sambil menangguhkan keputusan sebenar ke tarikh akan datang, di mana kitaran berita telah bergerak.

🎯 Teguran PH

Anda panggil mesyuarat khas Kabinet untuk krisis. Anda beritahu rakyat ini mendesak. Thailand memutuskan dalam satu mesyuarat. Vietnam memutuskan dalam satu mesyuarat. Mesyuarat khas anda menghasilkan kerja rumah untuk KSN yang perlu siap Selasa depan. Jika kerajaan anda tidak mampu membuat keputusan operasi yang mudah — biarkan penjawat awam bekerja dari rumah semasa krisis tenaga — semasa mesyuarat yang diadakan khusus untuk krisis itu, maka Kabinet itu untuk apa sebenarnya?

Amir Hamzah Says Economy 'Expected to Remain Strong' in 2026 — While Oil Tops $100, KLCI Crashes, and Cabinet Announces Austerity

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Finance Minister II Amir Hamzah Azizan told Bernama on March 13, 2026, that Malaysia’s economic performance in 2025 is “expected to continue driving the country’s economic momentum” in 2026. He said this despite acknowledging that the West Asia war had driven oil past US$100 per barrel, and on the same day the Cabinet announced cost-cutting measures including banning Raya open houses and restricting ministerial overseas travel. The KLCI had crashed below 1700 that same day, and the monthly fuel subsidy bill had ballooned to RM3.2 billion.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the third senior minister in two days to project economic optimism while the country’s finances are visibly deteriorating. Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah said essentially the same thing on March 12. The pattern is clear: the government’s crisis communication strategy is to keep repeating growth targets as if macro numbers insulate the rakyat from fuel price shocks, food inflation, and a tanking stock market. When Finance Minister II says the economy will be “strong” on the same day the Cabinet imposes austerity, the disconnect between governance and messaging becomes a credibility problem.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

If the economy is so strong, why did the Cabinet just ban Hari Raya open houses and restrict overseas travel for fiscal discipline? Why is the government tripling the RON95 subsidy to RM2 billion per month? Why is the KLCI below 1700? Amir Hamzah is not describing reality — he is performing confidence. The job of a Finance Minister is not to be a cheerleader; it is to level with the public about risks and prepare them for trade-offs. Instead, we get the same playbook as always: cite last year’s GDP, project this year’s growth range, and pretend the crisis doesn’t exist.

📜 Previous Cases

This is a recurring pattern. On March 12, Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah maintained the 4.0–4.5% growth target and celebrated 2025’s 5.2% GDP on the same day fuel prices exploded. On March 9, Deputy Economy Minister Mohd Shahar projected “steady” 4–4.5% growth amid a regional war. On March 8, Anwar himself told Malaysians to “be thrifty” — days after committing RM15 billion in cash handouts. The government’s economic messaging exists in a parallel universe from its own policy decisions.

🎯 PH Callout

You campaigned on transparency and competent governance. Your own Cabinet just imposed emergency austerity measures because the fiscal situation is deteriorating. And on the same afternoon, your Finance Minister II tells the press the economy is “expected to remain strong”? Pick one. Either the crisis is serious enough to ban Raya open houses, or the economy is strong. It cannot be both. The rakyat can read headlines — they know when they’re being managed instead of governed.

Anwar Says RON95 Subsidy Could Hit RM24 Billion — His Solution? 'Pray for Peace'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim revealed on March 13 that the government’s subsidy bill to maintain RON95 at RM1.99 per litre under the BUDI95 scheme could reach RM24 billion by year-end if the Middle East conflict persists. Speaking after the special Cabinet meeting, Anwar said the calculation was based on a monthly RON95 subsidy cost of RM2 billion. His proposed solution: “We must pray for a swift end to this conflict so that we won’t be burdened any further.” He added that the government would look for “other ways” to fund the subsidy through cost-cutting.

🔥 Why It Matters

The RM24 billion figure is a fiscal bombshell. The entire 2026 fuel subsidy allocation was originally a fraction of this. The number lays bare the total failure of subsidy reform — a policy Anwar promised since taking office in November 2022. Instead of a targeted subsidy mechanism, Malaysia now has a blanket subsidy that costs three times what it did a month ago, with no exit plan beyond hoping that a war ends. This is not fiscal management. This is fiscal surrender dressed up as compassion.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar had three years to implement targeted fuel subsidies. He launched BUDI95 in November 2025, but it was designed for stable oil prices, not a crisis. When the crisis hit, instead of activating the targeting mechanism he built, he froze RON95 at RM1.99 for everyone — including wealthy Malaysians driving luxury cars — and his solution is prayer. “Pray for peace” is not a fiscal strategy. It is the response of a government that spent its reform capital on election-cycle handouts and is now trapped by its own populism.

📜 Previous Cases

This echoes the BN playbook Anwar once campaigned against. Under Najib Razak, blanket fuel subsidies ballooned and were used as a political tool to buy loyalty before elections. Anwar explicitly promised to end this cycle. In 2023, he said targeted subsidies would be implemented “within months.” In 2024, he said “soon.” In 2025, he launched BUDI95 — and immediately undermined it by freezing the price floor when prices spiked. The RM24 billion figure is the cost of three years of broken promises.

🎯 PH Callout

You said you were different from BN. You said blanket subsidies were wasteful. You said targeting was the answer. Now you’re spending more on blanket subsidies than Najib ever did, and your backup plan is prayer. If RM24 billion doesn’t buy reform, what does it buy? It buys votes. And the rakyat — the ones you claim to protect — will pay for it in inflation, debt, and austerity down the road. This is not Madani. This is Najib with a better vocabulary.

Government Triples RON95 Subsidy to RM2 Billion — Three Years of 'Reform' Ends With a Bigger Blanket

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

On Friday, March 13, Finance Minister II Amir Hamzah Azizan announced that the government would increase its spending on RON95 petrol subsidies from RM700 million to RM2 billion — nearly tripling the allocation — to maintain the fixed retail price of RM1.99 per litre amid surging global oil prices past $100/barrel. The announcement came after the Special Cabinet Meeting convened to address the West Asia conflict’s economic impact. The subsidy covers RON95, the most widely used transport fuel in Malaysia, consumed by the vast majority of motorists.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the final burial of Anwar’s subsidy reform agenda. Since taking office in late 2022, the Madani government has repeatedly promised to move from blanket to targeted fuel subsidies — a reform the World Bank, IMF, and Malaysia’s own fiscal advisors have urged for years. The diesel subsidy was partially rationalised in mid-2024. RON95 was supposed to follow. Instead, with a global energy crisis exposing every weakness in the current system, the government’s response is to pour three times more money into the same untargeted mechanism. The RM2 billion figure reveals the true fiscal cost of inaction: had targeted subsidies been implemented on schedule, the government would be spending a fraction of this amount while still protecting B40 households.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Three years. That’s how long this government has had to implement targeted fuel subsidies. Every budget speech since 2023 has included some version of “subsidy rationalisation will proceed in phases.” The T20 income verification system was supposedly ready. The Budi mechanism for diesel was supposed to be the template. And yet here we are in March 2026, and the only tool in the government’s kit is to triple the blanket subsidy — meaning T20 earners driving luxury SUVs get the same RM1.99 petrol as B40 workers on motorcycles. This is not crisis management. This is the consequence of three years of delayed reform catching up with a government that was too afraid of electoral backlash to act when oil was cheap.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has been here before. Najib’s government removed fuel subsidies in 2014, then brought them back when oil prices dropped, missing the reform window entirely. Anwar criticised that flip-flop from the opposition bench. Now he’s doing the same thing with extra steps. The diesel subsidy reform of June 2024 was supposed to be Phase 1 of a broader rationalisation. Phase 2 (RON95) was initially targeted for late 2024, then pushed to 2025, then to “after economic conditions stabilise.” The conditions never stabilise because this government keeps waiting for the perfect moment that never comes.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reform. You promised targeted subsidies. You had years of relatively stable oil prices to implement them. You didn’t. Now you’re tripling the blanket subsidy and calling it crisis management. Every ringgit of that RM2 billion that flows to T20 drivers is money that could have gone to B40 cash transfers, public transport, or food price interventions. You didn’t build the targeted system when you could. Now the rakyat pays for your procrastination — twice: once through inflation, and again through a fiscal bill that crowds out spending on everything else.

🔥 Why It Matters

Not another round of “let’s coordinate.” The KSN and KPPA have had since at least March 10 — when Human Resources Minister Ramanan first mentioned studying WFH — to prepare options. The Public Service DG himself told Bernama on March 12 that Malaysia already proved during COVID-19 that the public sector can adapt to WFH “through the use of technology and new working methods.” So the precedent exists, the infrastructure exists, the demand exists — what doesn’t exist is political will. Anwar’s government would rather spend RM2 billion on fuel subsidies than save a fraction of that by letting civil servants work from home.

The Azam Baki report was discussed by Cabinet and punted to the Chief Secretary. In each case, the mechanism is identical — create the appearance of action while deferring the actual decision to a future date, by which time the news cycle has moved on.

🎯 PH Callout

You called a special Cabinet meeting for a crisis. You told the rakyat this was urgent. Thailand decided in one meeting. Vietnam decided in one meeting. Your special meeting produced a homework assignment for the KSN due next Tuesday. If your government cannot make a straightforward operational decision — let civil servants work from home during an energy crisis — during a meeting specifically convened for that crisis, then what exactly is the Cabinet for?

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Menteri Ekonomi Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir mengekalkan ramalan pertumbuhan Malaysia 2026 pada 4.0% hingga 4.5% pada 12 Mac 2026, sambil melancarkan sistem pemantauan pelaksanaan Rancangan Malaysia Ke-13 (MyRMK) di Putrajaya. Beliau meraikan pertumbuhan KDNK 2025 sebanyak 5.2%, pengangguran pada paras terendah sedekad iaitu 2.9%, dan inflasi menurun kepada 1.4% — mengisytiharkan bahawa ‘usaha berterusan kerajaan dalam mengukuhkan ekonomi dan meningkatkan kesejahteraan rakyat mula menunjukkan keputusan positif.’ Beliau mengakui konflik Asia Barat ‘boleh mengganggu kestabilan bekalan tenaga global dan perdagangan antarabangsa’ tetapi menegaskan sasaran pertumbuhan kekal.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Kenyataan ini dibuat pada hari yang sama Kementerian Kewangan mengumumkan kenaikan harga bahan api paling tajam dalam tempoh seminggu sejak bertahun-tahun: RON97 naik 60 sen kepada RM3.85 (18%), RON95 tak bersubsidi naik 60 sen kepada RM3.27, dan diesel naik 80 sen kepada RM3.92 (26%). Selat Hormuz terganggu, minyak melepasi $100 setong, dan jawatankuasa khas kerajaan sendiri bergegas menilai kesan ekonomi — namun respons Menteri Ekonomi ialah majlis pelancaran papan pemuka pemantauan dan perayaan angka tahun lepas. Apabila rumah terbakar, kamu tidak menunjukkan pelan bangunan kepada tetamu.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Mengekalkan sasaran pertumbuhan 4.0-4.5% sementara harga bahan api meletup, rantaian bekalan retak, dan inflasi bakal melonjak bukanlah keyakinan — ia penafian berselindung di sebalik kestabilan. Bloomberg melaporkan pada 6 Mac bahawa Malaysia sudah ‘menangguhkan peningkatan sasaran pertumbuhan’ kerana ketidaktentuan Timur Tengah. Kini krisis telah meningkat secara dramatik, dan terbaik yang Menteri Ekonomi boleh tawarkan ialah angka KDNK yang melihat ke belakang dan sistem pemantauan baharu. Jurang antara keyakinan Putrajaya dan realiti rakyat di pam minyak tidak pernah selebar ini.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Ini adalah satu corak. Timbalan Menteri Ekonomi Mohd Shahar Abdullah membuat dakwaan 4.0-4.5% yang sama di Sidang Kemuncak Perbankan dan Kewangan ASEAN pada 9 Mac — walaupun pengerusi sidang kemuncak itu sendiri memberi amaran bahawa ketegangan geopolitik sedang ‘menilai semula risiko di seluruh rantau.’ Sebelum krisis Timur Tengah, Anwar sendiri bercakap tentang meningkatkan sasaran pertumbuhan. Kini pendirian kerajaan telah berubah daripada ‘kami akan tingkatkan’ kepada ‘kami akan kekalkan’ kepada ‘kami akan pantau’ — setiap langkah satu undur yang disamarkan sebagai berhemah.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu menjanjikan tadbir urus berasaskan data dan dasar berasaskan bukti. Data menunjukkan bahan api naik 18-26% dalam seminggu, minyak melepasi $100, dan penimbal bekalan kamu habis pada Mei. Jawatankuasa khas kamu sendiri bermesyuarat pada Jumaat kerana keadaan genting. Namun Menteri Ekonomi di atas pentas meraikan angka 2025 dan melancarkan papan pemuka. Rakyat tidak memerlukan sistem pemantauan — mereka memerlukan kerajaan yang berterus terang tentang apa yang bakal datang. Katakan dengan jelas: sasaran pertumbuhan berisiko, kos sara hidup akan bertambah buruk, dan inilah yang akan kami lakukan. Apa-apa yang kurang daripada itu adalah putaran.
