Amir Hamzah Tells Malaysians 'Don't Speculate' on Fuel After Special Cabinet — While KLCI Crashes Below 1700 and Subsidy Bill Balloons to RM3.2 Billion

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Finance Minister II Datuk Seri Amir Hamzah Azizan held a press conference after the special Cabinet meeting on March 13 — the meeting that was supposed to deliver the government’s comprehensive crisis response to the West Asia conflict. His headline message: the Malaysian economy “remains resilient,” the government is sourcing additional oil, and Malaysians should stop spreading or acting on speculation about fuel availability because it “could create unnecessary negative consequences.” No new economic relief measures, no targeted aid packages, no toll freezes, no food price interventions — just reassurance.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was the special Cabinet meeting that was supposed to matter — the one Fahmi previewed on March 11, the one Fadillah built up on March 12, the one the whole country was watching. And the outcome? A Finance Minister telling citizens not to worry and not to talk about it. Meanwhile, the FBM KLCI crashed below 1,700 for the first time in years, the monthly fuel subsidy bill has ballooned to RM3.2 billion, and economists are warning of a potential global recession triggered by the oil shock. The gap between what the government says and what the market shows has never been wider.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

When your special Cabinet meeting produces nothing except “don’t speculate” and “economy is resilient,” you don’t have a crisis response — you have a PR operation. Amir Hamzah is literally telling Malaysians to stop talking about the problem instead of solving it. This is the same government that promised fiscal reform, subsidy rationalization, and institutional capacity. Three years in, their crisis playbook is: hold a special meeting, issue reassuring soundbites, tell people to stay calm, and defer every hard decision. Thailand mandated WFH immediately. Malaysia formed a committee, deferred to Tuesday, held a special meeting, and told citizens to shut up.

📜 Previous Cases

This is not new. When the ringgit fell past 4.80 to the dollar in early 2024, Anwar’s response was to blame speculators and tell Malaysians to have confidence. When inflation concerns mounted post-SST reintroduction, the messaging was “everything is under control.” When Bloomberg reported MACC misconduct, Fahmi’s first instinct was to question the source, not the substance. PH’s pattern is consistent: when the facts are bad, attack the people pointing out the facts. Amir Hamzah’s “don’t speculate” is just the latest variation.

🎯 PH Callout

You called a special Cabinet meeting — the whole country waited. And your answer is “don’t speculate”? The rakyat are paying RM3.85 for RON97, RM3.92 for diesel, watching their portfolios bleed, and wondering how long the RM1.99 RON95 lifeline will hold. They don’t need to be told to calm down. They need a government that shows up with a plan — not a press conference full of reassurance and empty of action. You promised competence, reform, and fiscal discipline. Deliver, or admit you can’t.

Anwar Says RON95 Subsidy Could Hit RM24 Billion — His Solution? 'Pray for Peace'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim revealed on March 13 that the government’s subsidy bill to maintain RON95 at RM1.99 per litre under the BUDI95 scheme could reach RM24 billion by year-end if the Middle East conflict persists. Speaking after the special Cabinet meeting, Anwar said the calculation was based on a monthly RON95 subsidy cost of RM2 billion. His proposed solution: “We must pray for a swift end to this conflict so that we won’t be burdened any further.” He added that the government would look for “other ways” to fund the subsidy through cost-cutting.

🔥 Why It Matters

The RM24 billion figure is a fiscal bombshell. The entire 2026 fuel subsidy allocation was originally a fraction of this. The number lays bare the total failure of subsidy reform — a policy Anwar promised since taking office in November 2022. Instead of a targeted subsidy mechanism, Malaysia now has a blanket subsidy that costs three times what it did a month ago, with no exit plan beyond hoping that a war ends. This is not fiscal management. This is fiscal surrender dressed up as compassion.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar had three years to implement targeted fuel subsidies. He launched BUDI95 in November 2025, but it was designed for stable oil prices, not a crisis. When the crisis hit, instead of activating the targeting mechanism he built, he froze RON95 at RM1.99 for everyone — including wealthy Malaysians driving luxury cars — and his solution is prayer. “Pray for peace” is not a fiscal strategy. It is the response of a government that spent its reform capital on election-cycle handouts and is now trapped by its own populism.

📜 Previous Cases

This echoes the BN playbook Anwar once campaigned against. Under Najib Razak, blanket fuel subsidies ballooned and were used as a political tool to buy loyalty before elections. Anwar explicitly promised to end this cycle. In 2023, he said targeted subsidies would be implemented “within months.” In 2024, he said “soon.” In 2025, he launched BUDI95 — and immediately undermined it by freezing the price floor when prices spiked. The RM24 billion figure is the cost of three years of broken promises.

🎯 PH Callout

You said you were different from BN. You said blanket subsidies were wasteful. You said targeting was the answer. Now you’re spending more on blanket subsidies than Najib ever did, and your backup plan is prayer. If RM24 billion doesn’t buy reform, what does it buy? It buys votes. And the rakyat — the ones you claim to protect — will pay for it in inflation, debt, and austerity down the road. This is not Madani. This is Najib with a better vocabulary.

Government Triples RON95 Subsidy to RM2 Billion — Three Years of 'Reform' Ends With a Bigger Blanket

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

On Friday, March 13, Finance Minister II Amir Hamzah Azizan announced that the government would increase its spending on RON95 petrol subsidies from RM700 million to RM2 billion — nearly tripling the allocation — to maintain the fixed retail price of RM1.99 per litre amid surging global oil prices past $100/barrel. The announcement came after the Special Cabinet Meeting convened to address the West Asia conflict’s economic impact. The subsidy covers RON95, the most widely used transport fuel in Malaysia, consumed by the vast majority of motorists.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the final burial of Anwar’s subsidy reform agenda. Since taking office in late 2022, the Madani government has repeatedly promised to move from blanket to targeted fuel subsidies — a reform the World Bank, IMF, and Malaysia’s own fiscal advisors have urged for years. The diesel subsidy was partially rationalised in mid-2024. RON95 was supposed to follow. Instead, with a global energy crisis exposing every weakness in the current system, the government’s response is to pour three times more money into the same untargeted mechanism. The RM2 billion figure reveals the true fiscal cost of inaction: had targeted subsidies been implemented on schedule, the government would be spending a fraction of this amount while still protecting B40 households.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Three years. That’s how long this government has had to implement targeted fuel subsidies. Every budget speech since 2023 has included some version of “subsidy rationalisation will proceed in phases.” The T20 income verification system was supposedly ready. The Budi mechanism for diesel was supposed to be the template. And yet here we are in March 2026, and the only tool in the government’s kit is to triple the blanket subsidy — meaning T20 earners driving luxury SUVs get the same RM1.99 petrol as B40 workers on motorcycles. This is not crisis management. This is the consequence of three years of delayed reform catching up with a government that was too afraid of electoral backlash to act when oil was cheap.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has been here before. Najib’s government removed fuel subsidies in 2014, then brought them back when oil prices dropped, missing the reform window entirely. Anwar criticised that flip-flop from the opposition bench. Now he’s doing the same thing with extra steps. The diesel subsidy reform of June 2024 was supposed to be Phase 1 of a broader rationalisation. Phase 2 (RON95) was initially targeted for late 2024, then pushed to 2025, then to “after economic conditions stabilise.” The conditions never stabilise because this government keeps waiting for the perfect moment that never comes.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reform. You promised targeted subsidies. You had years of relatively stable oil prices to implement them. You didn’t. Now you’re tripling the blanket subsidy and calling it crisis management. Every ringgit of that RM2 billion that flows to T20 drivers is money that could have gone to B40 cash transfers, public transport, or food price interventions. You didn’t build the targeted system when you could. Now the rakyat pays for your procrastination — twice: once through inflation, and again through a fiscal bill that crowds out spending on everything else.

Mat Sabu Says Food Supply 'Sufficient Until June' and Tells Rakyat Not to Panic — While Food Prices Spike and Oil Tops $100

Said by: Mohamad Sabu, Agriculture and Food Security Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Agriculture and Food Security Minister Mohamad Sabu told a post-Cabinet press conference on March 13, 2026 that Malaysia’s food supply — rice, chicken, eggs, fish, beef, buffalo meat, fresh milk, vegetables and fruits — is ‘sufficient at least until May or June.’ He urged Malaysians not to panic-buy ahead of Hari Raya and to avoid food wastage. This came after the special Cabinet meeting convened to assess the fallout from the West Asia crisis, with oil past $100/barrel and the subsidy bill ballooning to RM3.2 billion monthly.

🔥 Why It Matters

Mat Sabu’s reassurance follows the same crisis-management playbook the entire Cabinet has adopted this week: tell Malaysians not to worry, don’t speculate, don’t panic. But food inflation is not a future risk — it is already happening. Transport costs feed directly into food prices. The government has announced no food price caps, no targeted subsidies for essential goods, and no concrete intervention beyond monitoring. ‘Sufficient supply’ and ‘affordable prices’ are not the same thing, and Mat Sabu conflated the two.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Telling Malaysians not to panic while offering zero new measures to cushion food price inflation is not governance — it is vibes. Thailand has mandated WFH and capped select food prices. Vietnam acted immediately on energy-saving directives. Malaysia’s Agriculture Minister is telling people the fridge is full while ignoring that the price tags on everything inside it have gone up. ‘Sufficient until June’ is also a conveniently short timeline — what happens after June when Hari Raya is over and the crisis hasn’t resolved? The government is managing news cycles, not food security.

📜 Previous Cases

Mat Sabu has been making the same ‘food supply is stable’ reassurances since the crisis escalated in early March 2026, telling reporters on March 5 and again on March 6 that imports are diversified and supply chains are intact. He said the same things during the 2022 chicken shortage crisis and the 2023 egg price spikes. The pattern is clear: announce stability, avoid price controls, and hope the market sorts itself out before the political cost hits.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Ekonomi MADANI would protect the rakyat from exactly this kind of squeeze. Oil is at $100, your subsidy bill has tripled, food prices are climbing, and your Agriculture Minister’s best offer is ‘don’t panic, there’s enough rice until June.’ Where are the emergency food price interventions? Where is the targeted relief for B40 households whose grocery bills have already spiked? Telling people not to worry is not a policy. It is a press conference.

Mat Sabu Tells Ministry Staff to Grow Their Own Vegetables — Government's Food Security Plan Is Now a Backyard Garden

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Agriculture and Food Security Minister Mohamad Sabu, speaking after the special Cabinet meeting on March 13, directed ministry staff living in landed properties to start growing their own vegetables. He also encouraged households with available land to cultivate fast-harvesting crops such as chilli, eggplant and chives to supplement food supply. The Cabinet also agreed to increase the national rice stockpile to cover nine months. The ministry later had to clarify the vegetable-growing instruction was “not a directive” after public backlash.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Agriculture Minister’s contribution to Malaysia’s crisis response as oil tops $100 a barrel, the KLCI crashes below 1700, and food inflation accelerates. Instead of announcing emergency food price controls, expanded subsidies for farmers, or concrete supply chain interventions, Mat Sabu’s marquee post-Cabinet announcement was that Malaysians should plant vegetables in their backyards. It signals a government that has run out of institutional tools — or never had them — and is now outsourcing food security to individual households while maintaining a RM3.2 billion fuel subsidy bill.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH came to power promising institutional reform, modern governance, and serious food security policy. Three years later, their Agriculture Minister’s crisis plan is a kampung garden. This is a minister whose portfolio covers a RM5 billion budget, FAMA, MARDI, and the entire national food supply chain — and his response to a global energy crisis disrupting supply chains is “tanam sayur sendiri.” The fact that his own ministry had to clarify it was “not a directive” tells you everything: even the bureaucrats know this is embarrassing. You don’t see Thailand or Vietnam telling citizens to plant eggplant — they mandate WFH, cap prices, and activate strategic reserves.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows Mat Sabu’s March 12 post-Cabinet reassurance that food supply is “sufficient until May or June” while telling Malaysians not to panic-buy — the same minister who has been making identical assurances since the crisis began on February 28. In September 2025, Anwar himself urged Malaysians to “farm at home” and “not leave land idle” at a National Farmers’ Market event. The grow-your-own-food rhetoric is now a recurring PH deflection: when the state can’t deliver food security through policy, it tells citizens to become subsistence farmers.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia Madani — a modern, responsive, competent government. Your Agriculture Minister’s crisis plan is telling civil servants to plant chilli in their garden. This is not food security policy. This is a government that has substituted Instagram-friendly photo ops for institutional capacity. If your best advice to Malaysians facing $100-barrel oil and accelerating food inflation is “grow eggplant,” then you have failed at the most basic function of the ministry. The rakyat didn’t vote for gardening tips — they voted for governance.

Special Cabinet Meets for Oil Crisis — Anwar's WFH Decision? Another Review, Come Back Tuesday

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

On Friday, March 13, the Malaysian government held its much-anticipated Special Cabinet Meeting to address the economic fallout from the West Asia oil crisis, with Brent crude past $100/barrel and the Strait of Hormuz disrupted. Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil announced afterwards that Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim had instructed the Chief Secretary to the Government (KSN) and the Public Service Department director-general (KPPA) to “review the WFH proposal to ensure several aspects are properly coordinated.” The decision on whether to implement work-from-home for civil servants would be made on Tuesday, March 17 — four days later, and a full week after Thailand and Vietnam mandated WFH for their public sectors.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was supposed to be the crisis Cabinet — the meeting called specifically to take decisive action on the oil shock. Thailand’s Cabinet ordered mandatory WFH for government officials on March 10, the same day Vietnam issued similar directives. Both countries acted within 48 hours of the Strait of Hormuz disruption. Malaysia convened a special meeting three days later and produced… another review. The pattern is unmistakable: when confronted with urgent economic decisions that affect 1.7 million civil servants, this government defaults to study, review, and defer — even during what it calls a national crisis.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

A special Cabinet meeting is supposed to produce special decisions. Not another round of “let’s coordinate.” The KSN and KPPA have had since at least March 10 — when Human Resources Minister Ramanan first mentioned studying WFH — to prepare options. That’s three full working days. The Public Service DG himself told Bernama on March 12 that Malaysia already proved during COVID-19 that the public sector can adapt to WFH “through the use of technology and new working methods.” So the precedent exists, the infrastructure exists, the demand exists — what doesn’t exist is political will. Anwar’s government would rather spend RM2 billion on fuel subsidies than save a fraction of that by letting civil servants work from home.

📜 Previous Cases

This is vintage Madani governance: announce, study, defer, repeat. The PM term limit bill was tabled, failed, and will be “re-tabled in June.” Fuel subsidy reform has been “coming soon” since 2023. The Azam Baki report was discussed by Cabinet and punted to the Chief Secretary. In each case, the mechanism is identical — create the appearance of action while deferring the actual decision to a future date, by which time the news cycle has moved on.

🎯 PH Callout

You called a special Cabinet meeting for a crisis. You told the rakyat this was urgent. Thailand decided in one meeting. Vietnam decided in one meeting. Your special meeting produced a homework assignment for the KSN due next Tuesday. If your government cannot make a straightforward operational decision — let civil servants work from home during an energy crisis — during a meeting specifically convened for that crisis, then what exactly is the Cabinet for?

[BM] Menteri Ekonomi Raikan Angka 2025 Dan Kekal Sasaran Pertumbuhan 4.5% — Pada Hari Sama Harga Minyak Meletup

Said by: Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir, Economy Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Menteri Ekonomi Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir mengekalkan ramalan pertumbuhan Malaysia 2026 pada 4.0% hingga 4.5% pada 12 Mac 2026, sambil melancarkan sistem pemantauan pelaksanaan Rancangan Malaysia Ke-13 (MyRMK) di Putrajaya. Beliau meraikan pertumbuhan KDNK 2025 sebanyak 5.2%, pengangguran pada paras terendah sedekad iaitu 2.9%, dan inflasi menurun kepada 1.4% — mengisytiharkan bahawa ‘usaha berterusan kerajaan dalam mengukuhkan ekonomi dan meningkatkan kesejahteraan rakyat mula menunjukkan keputusan positif.’ Beliau mengakui konflik Asia Barat ‘boleh mengganggu kestabilan bekalan tenaga global dan perdagangan antarabangsa’ tetapi menegaskan sasaran pertumbuhan kekal.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Kenyataan ini dibuat pada hari yang sama Kementerian Kewangan mengumumkan kenaikan harga bahan api paling tajam dalam tempoh seminggu sejak bertahun-tahun: RON97 naik 60 sen kepada RM3.85 (18%), RON95 tak bersubsidi naik 60 sen kepada RM3.27, dan diesel naik 80 sen kepada RM3.92 (26%). Selat Hormuz terganggu, minyak melepasi $100 setong, dan jawatankuasa khas kerajaan sendiri bergegas menilai kesan ekonomi — namun respons Menteri Ekonomi ialah majlis pelancaran papan pemuka pemantauan dan perayaan angka tahun lepas. Apabila rumah terbakar, kamu tidak menunjukkan pelan bangunan kepada tetamu.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Mengekalkan sasaran pertumbuhan 4.0-4.5% sementara harga bahan api meletup, rantaian bekalan retak, dan inflasi bakal melonjak bukanlah keyakinan — ia penafian berselindung di sebalik kestabilan. Bloomberg melaporkan pada 6 Mac bahawa Malaysia sudah ‘menangguhkan peningkatan sasaran pertumbuhan’ kerana ketidaktentuan Timur Tengah. Kini krisis telah meningkat secara dramatik, dan terbaik yang Menteri Ekonomi boleh tawarkan ialah angka KDNK yang melihat ke belakang dan sistem pemantauan baharu. Jurang antara keyakinan Putrajaya dan realiti rakyat di pam minyak tidak pernah selebar ini.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Ini adalah satu corak. Timbalan Menteri Ekonomi Mohd Shahar Abdullah membuat dakwaan 4.0-4.5% yang sama di Sidang Kemuncak Perbankan dan Kewangan ASEAN pada 9 Mac — walaupun pengerusi sidang kemuncak itu sendiri memberi amaran bahawa ketegangan geopolitik sedang ‘menilai semula risiko di seluruh rantau.’ Sebelum krisis Timur Tengah, Anwar sendiri bercakap tentang meningkatkan sasaran pertumbuhan. Kini pendirian kerajaan telah berubah daripada ‘kami akan tingkatkan’ kepada ‘kami akan kekalkan’ kepada ‘kami akan pantau’ — setiap langkah satu undur yang disamarkan sebagai berhemah.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu menjanjikan tadbir urus berasaskan data dan dasar berasaskan bukti. Data menunjukkan bahan api naik 18-26% dalam seminggu, minyak melepasi $100, dan penimbal bekalan kamu habis pada Mei. Jawatankuasa khas kamu sendiri bermesyuarat pada Jumaat kerana keadaan genting. Namun Menteri Ekonomi di atas pentas meraikan angka 2025 dan melancarkan papan pemuka. Rakyat tidak memerlukan sistem pemantauan — mereka memerlukan kerajaan yang berterus terang tentang apa yang bakal datang. Katakan dengan jelas: sasaran pertumbuhan berisiko, kos sara hidup akan bertambah buruk, dan inilah yang akan kami lakukan. Apa-apa yang kurang daripada itu adalah putaran.

[BM] Pelan Krisis Minyak Loke: Biar Syarikat Penerbangan Cas Anda Lebih, Amaran Penerbangan Mungkin Dibatal — Selamat Hari Raya

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 12 Mac 2026, Menteri Pengangkutan Anthony Loke mempengerusikan mesyuarat bersama pengurusan syarikat penerbangan, pengendali lapangan terbang, pembekal bahan api jet dan pihak berkuasa penerbangan untuk membincangkan impak konflik Asia Barat terhadap kos bahan api yang melambung. Loke mengumumkan bahawa Kementerian Pengangkutan telah mencapai “persetujuan prinsip” untuk membenarkan syarikat penerbangan mengenakan bayaran tambahan — surcaj bahan api — kepada pelanggan bagi menampung kos yang meningkat. Beliau juga memberi amaran bahawa “jika harga bahan api terus meningkat, syarikat penerbangan mungkin perlu menggantung operasi penerbangan tertentu.” Kementerian komited untuk mengadakan mesyuarat mingguan dengan pihak berkepentingan penerbangan selagi konflik berterusan. Langkah jangka pendek turut dibincangkan untuk memastikan perkhidmatan kargo udara bagi bekalan makanan terus beroperasi menjelang Aidilfitri.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Ini buku panduan krisis penerbangan kerajaan dalam satu mesyuarat: tiada intervensi, tiada subsidi, tiada penyerapan kos — hanya kebenaran untuk syarikat penerbangan mengenakan bayaran lebih kepada rakyat. Malaysia sedang memasuki musim puncak perjalanan Hari Raya dengan harga minyak melepasi $100 setong dan Selat Hormuz di bawah ancaman tentera. Berjuta-juta rakyat Malaysia bergantung kepada penerbangan domestik untuk balik kampung, terutama dari Sabah dan Sarawak. Jawapan Loke ialah biarkan syarikat penerbangan kenakan surcaj dan bayangkan kemungkinan pembatalan penerbangan. Inilah kerajaan yang mendakwa sedang “melindungi rakyat” daripada krisis.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Anwar menghabiskan minggu ini memberitahu rakyat Malaysia bahawa kerajaan akan “melindungi” mereka daripada kesan ekonomi konflik Timur Tengah. Kabinet kekalkan RON95 pada RM1.99 dan umumkan RM15 bilion bantuan STR. Tetapi bila melibatkan penerbangan — talian hayat keluarga Malaysia Timur dan kos utama perjalanan Hari Raya — jawapan kerajaan ialah serahkan bil kepada penumpang. Tiada subsidi bahan api jet. Tiada had sementara tambang. Tiada peruntukan kecemasan. Hanya “mesyuarat mingguan” dan persetujuan untuk biar syarikat penerbangan cas lebih. Loke pada dasarnya memberitahu rakyat Malaysia: penerbangan Raya anda akan lebih mahal, dan sesetengahnya mungkin tak terbang langsung. Itu bukan pengurusan krisis. Itu penyerahan krisis.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Corak ini sudah biasa. Semasa pemulihan penerbangan pasca-pandemik 2022, syarikat penerbangan menaikkan tambang dan kerajaan tidak campur tangan walaupun menjanjikan perlindungan pengguna. Loke sendiri memperjuangkan pindaan Kod Perlindungan Pengguna Penerbangan Malaysia pada 2024, mewajibkan bayaran balik bagi kelewatan — tetapi perlindungan struktur terhadap penindasan harga semasa krisis masih tiada. Sementara itu, negara seperti Thailand dan Indonesia bergerak lebih pantas dengan intervensi bahan api dan pakej sokongan penerbangan. Buku panduan Malaysia tetap sama: tubuh jawatankuasa, adakan mesyuarat, keluarkan kenyataan akhbar, dan biar pasaran buat sesuka hati.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Korang janji kerajaan yang utamakan rakyat. Korang habiskan minggu ini mengumumkan bantuan tunai dan mendakwa disiplin fiskal. Tapi bila berjuta-juta rakyat Malaysia perlu terbang pulang untuk Hari Raya dan syarikat penerbangan nak hentak mereka dengan surcaj — atau batalkan penerbangan terus — penyelesaian Menteri Pengangkutan korang ialah bagi kebenaran kepada syarikat penerbangan untuk cas lebih. Mana dana kecemasan penerbangan? Mana subsidi sementara bahan api jet? Mana had tambang untuk laluan domestik semasa musim perayaan? Korang ada masa nak haramkan rumah terbuka Raya atas nama “disiplin fiskal” tapi takda masa nak lindungi rakyat daripada dikeruk di lapangan terbang. Reformasi, rupanya, tak sampai ke harga tiket penerbangan.

[BM] Ramanan Kata Kerajaan 'Mengkaji' Kerja Dari Rumah Untuk Sektor Awam — Thailand Bertindak Segera, Malaysia Masih Buat Kajian

Said by: R. Ramanan, Human Resources Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 12 Mac 2026, Menteri Sumber Manusia Ramanan Ramakrishnan memberitahu wartawan di Putrajaya bahawa langkah-langkah penjimatan kos yang mungkin — termasuk kerja dari rumah bagi kakitangan sektor awam dan swasta — sedang ‘dikaji’ dan akan dibentangkan di mesyuarat Kabinet khas pada Jumaat, 14 Mac. ‘Kami telah melihat apa yang Thailand lakukan, dan kami sedang menjalankan kajian mengenai perkara ini,’ kata Ramanan. Beliau menambah bahawa beliau akan menerima taklimat daripada timbalan-timbalan ketua setiausaha dan kemudian membentangkan penemuan kepada Kabinet. Beliau mengakui konflik Timur Tengah yang berpanjangan dan penutupan Selat Hormuz sebagai punca krisis.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Kabinet Thailand meluluskan arahan kerja dari rumah wajib bagi pegawai kerajaan serta-merta apabila krisis tenaga meningkat. Kerajaan Malaysia — menghadapi krisis yang sama, dengan RON97 naik 18%, diesel naik 26%, dan minyak melepasi $100 setong — masih ‘menjalankan kajian.’ Perbezaannya amat memalukan. Setiap hari pekerja Malaysia berulang-alik ke pejabat yang boleh mereka bekerja dari jauh adalah satu hari kerajaan memaksa rakyat menanggung kos bahan api yang boleh dielakkan dengan satu arahan sahaja. Kata-kata Ramanan sendiri mendedahkan hakikat: ‘Jika kita sendiri tidak mengamalkan penjimatan tetapi meminta rakyat berbuat demikian, itu tidak adil.’ Betul. Jadi mengapa kamu masih mengkaji dan bukannya bertindak?

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Pandemik COVID-19 membuktikan — tiga tahun lalu — bahawa kakitangan awam dan pekerja sektor swasta Malaysia boleh bekerja dari rumah dengan berkesan. Infrastrukturnya ada. Duluan sudah ada. Kerangka undang-undang untuk pengaturan kerja fleksibel sudah wujud di bawah Akta Kerja yang dipinda. Tiada apa lagi yang perlu ‘dikaji.’ Apa yang Ramanan sebenarnya katakan ialah kerajaan tidak mahu mengeluarkan arahan tanpa perlindungan politik daripada mesyuarat Kabinet yang belum berlaku. Ini bukan tadbir urus. Ini birokrasi menunggu kebenaran untuk melakukan perkara yang jelas. Thailand bertindak dalam beberapa jam. Malaysia mengambil berhari-hari — dan masih mengira.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Pada 11 Mac, pangkalan data ini mencatat bahawa respons krisis Anwar terhadap minyak melebihi $100 termasuk ciapan solidariti dan ‘pemantauan’ — tetapi tiada arahan kerja dari rumah, tiada pengecualian tol, tiada kawalan harga makanan. Kini, sehari kemudian, kerajaan telah naik tahap daripada ‘memantau’ kepada ‘mengkaji.’ Semasa Sidang Kemuncak ASEAN pada Mei 2025, Fahmi Fadzil menghabiskan berminggu-minggu mengatakan Kabinet sedang ‘membincangkan’ kerja dari rumah sebelum akhirnya melaksanakannya. Coraknya konsisten: kerajaan Malaysia memperlakukan setiap keputusan seolah-olah memerlukan proses maksimum dan kecekapan minimum, walaupun kos kelewatan ditanggung sepenuhnya oleh pekerja biasa.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu memberitahu rakyat Malaysia kamu akan memimpin melalui teladan. Ramanan sendiri berkata: adalah ’tidak adil’ meminta rakyat berjimat sementara kerajaan tidak berbuat demikian. Jadi bertindaklah. Keluarkan arahan hari ini. Thailand tidak memerlukan mesyuarat Kabinet pada hari Jumaat untuk memutuskan pekerja kerajaan patut berhenti membakar bahan api mahal untuk duduk di pejabat yang mempunyai sambungan internet yang berfungsi sempurna. Setiap hari kamu ‘mengkaji’ adalah satu hari kamu memilih proses berbanding rakyat. Rakyat tidak mampu menanggung kelajuan pertimbangan kamu.

[BM] RON97 Naik 18%, Diesel Naik 26% — Kerajaan Biar Harga Minyak Tak Bersubsidi Meletup Sambil Dakwa 'Melindungi Rakyat'

Said by: Ministry of Finance / Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Kementerian Kewangan mengumumkan pada 11 Mac bahawa berkuat kuasa 12-18 Mac, petrol RON97 akan melonjak 60 sen seliter kepada RM3.85 — kenaikan 18% daripada RM3.25. RON95 tak bersubsidi naik 60 sen kepada RM3.27 — lonjakan 22%. Diesel di Semenanjung Malaysia melonjak 80 sen kepada RM3.92 — kenaikan 26% yang mengejutkan. Kerajaan mengekalkan kadar bersubsidi Budi95 pada RM1.99 bagi pemandu yang layak dan menawarkan tambahan RM100 bagi pemilik kenderaan diesel di bawah skim sedia ada, menaikkan bantuan tunai interim daripada RM200 kepada RM300 bagi bulan Mac.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Ini ialah pelarasan harga bahan api mingguan paling tajam sejak krisis Timur Tengah meningkat. Diesel pada RM3.92 secara langsung memukul logistik, pengangkutan, dan rantaian bekalan makanan — kos yang akan dipindahkan kepada pengguna dalam beberapa hari. Sistem dua peringkat bahan api kini teregang sehingga hampir pecah: RON95 bersubsidi pada RM1.99 manakala RON95 tak bersubsidi pada RM3.27, jurang 64% yang menggalakkan penyeludupan. Perniagaan yang bergantung kepada diesel — nelayan, petani, pemandu lori, pengeluar makanan — menanggung lonjakan kos 26% tanpa kawalan harga hiliran.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Pembingkaian kerajaan ialah teater semata-mata. ‘Kerajaan akan terus menanggung sebahagian subsidi untuk melindungi kesan’ — mereka mensubsidi satu gred bahan api dan membiarkan yang lain meletup. Tambahan diesel RM100 adalah penghinaan: jika kamu pengendali lori yang membakar ratusan liter seminggu, RM300 sebulan menampung kira-kira satu isian pada harga baharu. Sementara itu, baris KKM tentang harga ’lebih rendah daripada negara jiran’ ialah pesongan tertua dalam buku subsidi — ia tidak memberitahu kamu sama ada gaji Malaysia mampu menanggung kos ini. Anwar menjanjikan rasionalisasi subsidi bahan api yang ‘adil dan beransur-ansur.’ Lonjakan diesel 26% dalam seminggu bukan adil mahupun beransur-ansur.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Kerajaan ini telah menendang tin subsidi bahan api sejak memegang jawatan. Subsidi bersasar untuk RON95 (Budi95) sepatutnya menggantikan subsidi menyeluruh, tetapi rasionalisasi diesel telah berulang-ulang berhenti-mula sejak Jun 2024. Setiap kali harga global melonjak, kerajaan bergegas dengan bantuan tunai ad hoc dan bukannya reformasi struktur. Minggu lepas sahaja, Anwar mengakui beliau hanya mampu mengekalkan subsidi RON95 untuk ‘satu atau dua bulan’ — dan kini harga di sekelilingnya meletup.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu memberitahu rakyat Malaysia kamu akan merasionalisasi subsidi secara adil. Kamu memberitahu mereka ekonomi kukuh dan diurus dengan baik. Kamu memberitahu mereka kamu ada rancangan. Lonjakan 18% RON97 dan 26% diesel dalam seminggu bukan rancangan — ia penyerahan kepada kuasa pasaran dengan kenyataan akhbar dilampirkan. Rakyat yang menggunakan RON97 atau menjalankan perniagaan bergantung diesel bukan ‘dilindungi’ — mereka terdedah.
