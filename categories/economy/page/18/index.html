[BM] Sindiket Rasuah Imigresen Subur — Eksploitasi Pekerja Asing Industri Berbilion Ringgit

Said by: Enforcement Agencies / Media Investigations

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-03-01

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Sistem imigresen Malaysia kekal dibelenggu sindiket rasuah yang mengeksploitasi berjuta pekerja asing, mengekstrak berbilion ringgit melalui levi haram, penipuan dokumen, dan pemerdagangan buruh. Walaupun janji kerajaan berulang kali untuk membersihkan sistem, siasatan terus mendedahkan rangkaian yang berakar umbi melibatkan pegawai imigresen, ejen buruh, dan majikan.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Pekerja asing — dianggarkan 2-3 juta berdokumen dan berjuta lagi tanpa dokumen — membayar yuran pengambilan yang melampau, sering menjadi hamba terikat yang terperangkap dalam kitaran ikatan hutang. Sistem levi pekerja asing pelbagai peringkat telah berulang kali ditangguhkan, mewujudkan ketidaktentuan yang menguntungkan perantara rasuah. Laporan Pemerdagangan Orang Jabatan Negara AS telah secara konsisten menandakan isu buruh paksa Malaysia, terutamanya dalam pertanian perladangan, pembinaan, dan pembuatan.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Kitaran respons kerajaan boleh diramalkan: umumkan tindakan keras, lakukan beberapa serbuan berprofil tinggi, keluarkan kenyataan tegas, kemudian biarkan sistem kembali seperti biasa. Rasuah imigresen adalah masalah struktur yang memerlukan reformasi sistematik proses pengambilan, kutipan levi, dan perlindungan pekerja — bukan teater penguatkuasaan berkala.

🎯 Seruan Kepada PH

Anda mewarisi sistem yang rosak dan berjanji untuk membetulkannya. Sebaliknya, sindiket masih beroperasi, pekerja masih menderita, dan berbilion masih mengalir kepada perantara rasuah. Jika anda tidak boleh membersihkan jabatan imigresen sendiri, berhenti berpura-pura anda kerajaan reformasi.

Immigration Corruption Syndicates Thrive — Foreign Worker Exploitation a Multi-Billion Ringgit Industry

Said by: Enforcement Agencies / Media Investigations

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-03-01

📌 What Happened

Malaysia’s immigration system remains plagued by corruption syndicates that exploit millions of foreign workers, extracting billions of ringgit through illegal levies, document fraud, and labour trafficking. Despite repeated government promises to clean up the system, investigations continue to reveal deeply entrenched networks involving immigration officials, labour agents, and employers.

🔥 Why It Matters

Foreign workers — numbering an estimated 2-3 million documented and millions more undocumented — pay exorbitant recruitment fees, often becoming indentured servants trapped in cycles of debt bondage. The multi-tier foreign worker levy system has been delayed repeatedly, creating uncertainty that benefits corrupt intermediaries. US State Department Trafficking in Persons reports have consistently flagged Malaysia’s forced labour issues, particularly in plantation agriculture, construction, and manufacturing.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The government’s response cycle is predictable: announce a crackdown, conduct a few high-profile raids, issue stern statements, then allow the system to return to business as usual. Immigration corruption is a structural problem requiring systemic reform of recruitment processes, levy collection, and worker protection — not periodic enforcement theatre.

🎯 PH Callout

You inherited a broken system and promised to fix it. Instead, the syndicates still operate, the workers still suffer, and the billions still flow to corrupt intermediaries. If you can’t clean up your own immigration department, stop pretending you’re a reform government.
