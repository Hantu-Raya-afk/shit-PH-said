Goldman Sachs 1MDB Settlement Review — Anwar Threatens Lawsuits, Nothing Happens

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2023-08-21

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In August 2023, Anwar Ibrahim called the $3.9 billion Goldman Sachs 1MDB settlement “done hastily” under the previous government and threatened to take Goldman to court for a larger recovery. He vowed Malaysia would “recover every single sen.” Goldman reportedly made “fresh overtures” in response. Years later, the review has produced nothing concrete — no lawsuit, no renegotiation, no additional recovery. CNA uncovered details suggesting the confidential settlement agreement may have shortchanged Malaysia, with Goldman paying a fraction of the losses it facilitated.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Goldman Sachs helped arrange $6.5 billion in bonds for 1MDB, earning hundreds of millions in fees while billions were stolen. The $3.9 billion settlement — negotiated under Muhyiddin — was criticised as inadequate given the scale of Goldman’s role. Anwar’s promise to review the settlement and recover more raised expectations. But promises without follow-through are just performance. If the settlement was “done hastily” and shortchanged Malaysia, why hasn’t Anwar filed suit in three years? The answer is either that the legal case is weaker than claimed, or that Goldman’s political leverage is stronger than Anwar’s resolve.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Threatening Goldman Sachs with lawsuits makes for great headlines. Actually suing Goldman Sachs requires resources, political will, and a willingness to antagonise a powerful Wall Street institution with extensive business interests in Malaysia. Anwar chose the headline over the lawsuit. Three years of “review” with no outcome is not due diligence — it’s indefinite deferral. The rakyat is still paying the 1MDB debt — RM50 billion on the books — while the bank that facilitated the theft walks away with a settlement that Anwar himself called inadequate.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The Goldman settlement was negotiated under Muhyiddin’s government in 2020 — a $3.9 billion package including $2.5 billion in cash and a $1.4 billion guarantee on 1MDB asset recovery. PH 1.0 under Mahathir had initiated legal proceedings against Goldman but was removed before completion. The Sheraton Move government settled. Anwar promised to reopen the case. Three administrations later, Goldman’s liability has been capped while Malaysia’s debt continues to compound.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You called the Goldman settlement “hasty” and promised to recover “every single sen.” That was three years ago. Where’s the lawsuit? Where’s the renegotiation? Where’s the additional recovery? Goldman facilitated the theft of billions from Malaysia. The rakyat is still paying the debt. Either file suit or explain why you can’t. But stop threatening action you have no intention of taking — because every day you delay, Goldman’s lawyers grow more confident that your threats were empty.

[BM] Skandal Gerbang Maritim Selangor RM10 Bilion — Laporan SPRM Difailkan Soal Urus Niaga Tanah Dan Saham

Said by: Amirudin Shari / Chegubard / MACC

Category: Economy

Date: 2023-08-07

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Aktivis Badrul Hisham Shaharin (Chegubard) memfailkan laporan SPRM pada Ogos 2023 mengenai dakwaan penyelewengan RM10 bilion dalam projek Gerbang Maritim Selangor — pembangunan sepanjang 56km di sepanjang Sungai Selangor. Projek itu dilaporkan melibatkan penjualan saham majoriti dan ratusan ekar tanah milik kerajaan kepada syarikat swasta yang dikaitkan dengan ahli perniagaan Vincent Tan. Amirudin menghantar surat tuntutan kepada MB Kedah Sanusi Md Nor kerana mengulas skandal itu secara terbuka.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: RM10 bilion dalam dakwaan penyelewengan melibatkan pemindahan tanah negeri dan penjualan saham bukan isu tadbir urus kecil — ia adalah jenis skandal yang menamatkan kerjaya politik dalam demokrasi yang berfungsi. Gerbang Maritim Selangor adalah projek infrastruktur terbesar negeri, dan dakwaan bahawa tanah kerajaan dijual kepada entiti swasta di bawah nilai pasaran sementara saham dipindahkan dalam keadaan yang dipersoalkan menyentuh nadi kepercayaan awam.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Respons Amirudin terhadap dakwaan RM10 bilion bukan membuka siasatan atau menuntut audit awam — ia menghantar surat guaman kepada ahli politik pembangkang yang bercakap mengenainya. Itu memberitahu anda segala-galanya mengenai pendekatan PH terhadap akauntabiliti: diamkan pengkritik, abaikan substansi. Jika seseorang menuduh anda menyalah urus RM10 bilion aset negeri, respons yang betul adalah audit bebas, bukan surat peguam.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Ini adalah sebahagian daripada corak kebimbangan pengurusan aset Selangor yang lebih luas di bawah Amirudin — dari rasuah perlombongan pasir kepada siasatan MBI kepada siasatan dana warga emas SMUE. Setiap kes melibatkan sumber negeri, setiap satu melibatkan SPRM, dan setiap satu disambut dengan respons yang sama: nafi, alih perhatian, ugut tindakan undang-undang.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: RM10 bilion aset negeri. Tanah kerajaan dijual kepada kepentingan swasta. Saham dipindahkan. Dan respons anda ialah menyaman orang yang bercakap mengenainya. Jika urusan itu bersih, terbitkan kontrak, penilaian, dan dokumen kelulusan. Jika ia tidak bersih, siasat. Tetapi mengugut saman fitnah terhadap pengkritik sementara SPRM menyiasat negeri anda bukan reformasi — ia penyembunyian dengan langkah tambahan.

RM10 Billion Selangor Maritime Gateway Scandal — MACC Report Filed Over Land and Shares Deal

Said by: Amirudin Shari / Chegubard / MACC

Category: Economy

Date: 2023-08-07

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Activist Badrul Hisham Shaharin (Chegubard) lodged a MACC report in August 2023 over alleged RM10 billion irregularities in the Selangor Maritime Gateway project — a 56km development along the Selangor River. The project reportedly involved the sale of majority shares and hundreds of acres of government-owned land to a private company linked to businessman Vincent Tan. Amirudin sent a letter of demand to Kedah MB Sanusi Md Nor for commenting on the scandal publicly.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: RM10 billion in alleged irregularities involving state land transfers and share sales is not a minor governance issue — it’s the kind of scandal that ends political careers in functional democracies. The Selangor Maritime Gateway is the state’s largest infrastructure project, and allegations that government land was sold to a private entity below market value while shares were transferred under questionable circumstances strike at the heart of public trust.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Amirudin’s response to the RM10 billion allegation was not to open an investigation or demand a public audit — it was to send a legal letter to the opposition politician who talked about it. That tells you everything about PH’s approach to accountability: silence the critics, ignore the substance. If someone accuses you of mishandling RM10 billion in state assets, the correct response is an independent audit, not a lawyer’s letter.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: This is part of a broader pattern of Selangor asset management concerns under Amirudin — from sand mining corruption to MBI investigations to the SMUE elderly fund probe. Each case involves state resources, each involves MACC, and each is met with the same response: deny, deflect, threaten legal action.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: RM10 billion in state assets. Government land sold to private interests. Shares transferred. And your response was to sue the person who talked about it. If the deal is clean, publish the contracts, the valuations, and the approval documents. If it’s not clean, investigate. But threatening defamation suits against critics while MACC investigates your state is not reform — it’s cover-up with extra steps.

[BM] Kos ECRL Membengkak Kepada RM75 Bilion — Anwar Teruskan Juga, Panggil Ia 'Dikurangkan'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2022-12-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Anwar Ibrahim mendedahkan bahawa kos asal Laluan Rel Pantai Timur (ECRL) ialah RM86 bilion dan mendakwa kerajaannya “mengurangkan” ia kepada RM75 bilion. Pembinaan sahaja berjumlah RM50.27 bilion, dengan tambahan RM24.69 bilion dalam kos faedah dan pemerolehan tanah. Projek ini — projek mega yang disokong China menghubungkan pantai timur dan barat Semenanjung Malaysia — dirundingkan semula di bawah kerajaan PH 1.0 Mahathir dan diteruskan di bawah Muhyiddin dan Ismail Sabri. Kerajaan Anwar meneruskan pembinaan, membingkaikan “pengurangan” RM11 bilion sebagai pencapaian fiskal.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: RM75 bilion adalah komitmen infrastruktur tunggal terbesar dalam sejarah Malaysia. Komponen faedah sahaja — RM24.69 bilion — melebihi keseluruhan bajet tahunan kebanyakan kementerian kerajaan. Pengkritik, termasuk ahli ekonomi dan AP pembangkang, mempersoalkan sama ada ECRL akan menjana pulangan ekonomi yang mencukupi untuk mewajarkan kos, menyatakan bahawa laluan itu terutamanya melayani kawasan berkepadatan rendah dengan aktiviti komersial terhad. Projek ini dibiayai melalui pinjaman dari Exim Bank China, menimbulkan kebimbangan kebergantungan hutang pada masa nisbah hutang-kepada-KDNK Malaysia sudah melebihi 64%.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Memanggil projek RM75 bilion “dikurangkan” kerana ia asalnya RM86 bilion adalah seperti meraikan diskaun 13% untuk sesuatu yang anda tidak mampu. Analisis kos-manfaat ECRL tidak pernah diterbitkan sepenuhnya kepada umum. Beban faedah sahaja akan memakan hasil kerajaan selama berdekad. Penciptaan pekerjaan tempatan telah dikritik sebagai minimum — kebanyakan tenaga kerja pembinaan dan kepakaran kejuruteraan datang dari China. Anwar meneruskan projek yang dimulakan oleh Najib, diteruskan oleh setiap PM selepas itu, dan dipersoalkan oleh ahli ekonomi merentas spektrum politik — kerana pembatalan akan merosakkan hubungan China dan mencetuskan klausa penalti. Ini bukan keputusan dasar; ini adalah situasi tebusan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: ECRL pada asalnya ditandatangani oleh Najib pada 2016 sebagai sebahagian daripada Inisiatif Jalur dan Laluan China, pada kos yang melambung yang dilihat secara meluas dikaitkan dengan rundingan penyelamatan 1MDB. Mahathir menggantungnya pada 2018, kemudian merundingkan semula pada kos yang lebih rendah. Muhyiddin menyambung semula pembinaan. Setiap kerajaan mewarisi dan meneruskan projek ini kerana kos tenggelam dan akibat diplomatik pembatalan bertambah dengan setiap tahun yang berlalu. ECRL adalah perangkap fiskal Malaysia — setiap PM menyentuhnya dan tidak boleh melepaskan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda mewarisi projek mega kontroversi yang disokong China daripada Najib, melalui Mahathir, melalui Muhyiddin, melalui Ismail Sabri. Bukannya menjalankan semakan kos-manfaat bebas, anda meneruskan dan memanggil kos “dikurangkan.” Terbitkan analisis kos-manfaat penuh. Dedahkan kadar faedah dan jadual pembayaran balik. Tunjukkan berapa banyak pekerjaan tempatan berbanding pekerja import yang dihasilkan projek ini. Jelaskan bagaimana laluan rel RM75 bilion melalui kawasan berkepadatan rendah akan menjana pulangan yang mewajarkan hutang. Atau akui bahawa anda meneruskan projek ini kerana menghentikannya lebih sukar secara politik daripada membayarnya.

ECRL Cost Balloons to RM75 Billion — Anwar Proceeds Anyway, Calls It 'Reduced'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2022-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Anwar Ibrahim revealed that the original East Coast Rail Link (ECRL) cost was RM86 billion and claimed his government “reduced” it to RM75 billion. Construction alone accounts for RM50.27 billion, with an additional RM24.69 billion in interest and land acquisition costs. The project — a China-backed megaproject connecting the east and west coasts of Peninsular Malaysia — was renegotiated under Mahathir’s PH 1.0 government and continued under Muhyiddin and Ismail Sabri. Anwar’s government proceeded with construction, framing the RM11 billion “reduction” as a fiscal achievement.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: RM75 billion is the single largest infrastructure commitment in Malaysian history. The interest component alone — RM24.69 billion — exceeds the entire annual budget of most government ministries. Critics, including economists and opposition MPs, have questioned whether the ECRL will generate sufficient economic returns to justify the cost, noting that the route primarily serves low-density areas with limited commercial activity. The project is funded through a loan from China’s Exim Bank, raising debt dependency concerns at a time when Malaysia’s debt-to-GDP ratio already exceeds 64%.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Calling a RM75 billion project “reduced” because it was originally RM86 billion is like celebrating a 13% discount on something you can’t afford. The ECRL’s cost-benefit analysis has never been publicly released in full. The interest burden alone will consume government revenue for decades. Local job creation has been criticised as minimal — much of the construction workforce and engineering expertise comes from China. Anwar is proceeding with a project initiated by Najib, continued by every subsequent PM, and questioned by economists across the political spectrum — because cancellation would damage the China relationship and trigger penalty clauses. This isn’t a policy decision; it’s a hostage situation.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The ECRL was originally signed by Najib in 2016 as part of China’s Belt and Road Initiative, at an inflated cost that was widely seen as linked to 1MDB bailout negotiations. Mahathir suspended it in 2018, then renegotiated at a lower cost. Muhyiddin resumed construction. Each government has inherited and continued the project because the sunk costs and diplomatic consequences of cancellation grow with each passing year. The ECRL is Malaysia’s fiscal tar baby — every PM touches it and can’t let go.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You inherited a controversial China-backed megaproject from Najib, via Mahathir, via Muhyiddin, via Ismail Sabri. Instead of conducting an independent cost-benefit review, you proceeded and called the cost “reduced.” Publish the full cost-benefit analysis. Disclose the interest rate and repayment schedule. Show how many local jobs versus imported workers the project creates. Explain how a RM75 billion rail line through low-density areas will generate returns that justify the debt. Or admit that you’re continuing the project because stopping it is politically harder than paying for it.
