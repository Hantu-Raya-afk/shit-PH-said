LRT3 Delayed AGAIN — Software Glitches, Failed Test Runs, RM475M Penalties Looming

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The LRT3 line from Bandar Utama to Johan Setia — a multi-billion ringgit project intended to transform connectivity in the Klang Valley — has been postponed repeatedly due to signalling software failures and the inability to complete fault-free test runs. Transport Minister Anthony Loke warned contractors may face penalties exceeding RM475 million for the delays. The opening date remains unconfirmed. The Edge and Lowyat both reported the latest round of testing setbacks as the system failed to achieve the required reliability benchmarks.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: LRT3 was supposed to be operational years ago. Repeated delays mean commuters continue relying on cars, worsening congestion and pollution in the Klang Valley. Public money is tied up in a system nobody can use — billions invested with zero return to commuters. The signalling software failures suggest deeper issues with project management, contractor capability, and government oversight. Meanwhile, commuters who were told LRT3 would change their lives continue sitting in traffic.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Loke’s penalty threats ring hollow. The government has a track record of renegotiating rather than enforcing consequences on mega-project contractors. Penalties get waived, deadlines get extended, cost overruns get absorbed by the public, and nobody is held accountable. Infrastructure delays under PH are no different from infrastructure delays under BN — the “reform” government delivers the same results with the same contractors and the same excuses. If the RM475M in penalties are actually enforced, it would be the first time in Malaysian infrastructure history. Don’t hold your breath.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s mega-infrastructure project history is a catalogue of delays, cost overruns, and renegotiated contracts. MRT2 was delayed. The ECRL was renegotiated (costs ballooned from RM55B to reportedly higher). The Penang Transport Master Plan is stalled. LRT3 now joins the list. The pattern across administrations: announce ambitious projects, award contracts to politically connected firms, miss deadlines, threaten penalties, renegotiate quietly, and repeat. The contractors get paid regardless. The public gets delays regardless. Only the minister cutting the ribbon changes.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: If RM475M in penalties are owed, collect them. If the contractor can’t deliver, replace them. If the signalling software doesn’t work after years of testing, find out why and publish the findings. Stop threatening consequences you never enforce. Infrastructure accountability starts with one simple principle: contracts that aren’t met should have real consequences. Under BN, they didn’t. Under PH, they still don’t. Prove us wrong. Enforce the RM475M. We dare you.

[BM] Anwar Janji Hapus Kemiskinan Tegar Menjelang Q1 2026 — Sekarang Mac 2026 Dan Tiada Siapa Bercakap Tentangnya

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-07

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 7 Disember 2025, di majlis penutup program Rancakkan Madani di Putrajaya, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim mengisytiharkan bahawa kadar kemiskinan tegar Malaysia telah jatuh kepada 0.09 peratus — melibatkan kira-kira 7,000 isi rumah — dan memberitahu penjawat awam bahawa memandangkan angka itu “terlalu kecil,” kerajaan boleh “menghapuskannya menjelang suku pertama 2026.” Bernama, Malay Mail, The Star, Business Today — semuanya menjadikannya berita utama. Kini pertengahan Mac 2026, jauh masuk ke dalam Q1. Tiada pengisytiharan kejayaan, tiada kemas kini tentang 7,000 isi rumah itu, dan tiada sesiapa dalam kerajaan yang kelihatan membincangkannya.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Ini bukan kenyataan sambil lewa. Anwar menjadikan penghapusan kemiskinan tegar tunjang utama Bajet 2026, memperuntukkan RM15 bilion bantuan langsung, dan menggunakan angka 0.09% dalam pelbagai ucapan sebagai bukti kejayaan MADANI. Ahli ekonomi sudah pun mempersoalkan sama ada garis kemiskinan kerajaan — RM1,169 sebulan untuk kemiskinan tegar — benar-benar menggambarkan kesusahan hidup di negara yang sewa rumah sahaja boleh melebihi jumlah itu. Tarikh akhir Q1 2026 sepatutnya menjadi pencapaian kemuncak. Sebaliknya, ia senyap-senyap berlalu sementara kerajaan sibuk dengan krisis harga minyak, skandal Azam Baki, dan undang-undang sekatan kebebasan bersuara. Kesunyian itu memberitahu segalanya tentang betapa seriusnya janji itu dibuat.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Ini buku panduan PH yang sama: buat janji besar bergegar dengan tarikh akhir yang jelas, harap tiada siapa akan follow up, dan biarkan kitaran berita menguburkannya. Anwar bukan sekadar berkata “kita harap boleh kurangkan kemiskinan” — dia bagi suku tahun, peratusan, dan arahan khusus kepada penjawat awam. Kalau sasaran dah tercapai, mesti ada sidang akhbar, berita Bernama, dan hashtag. Ketiadaan sambutan itu sendiri adalah bukti. Sementara itu, rakyat yang sama yang Anwar janji nak angkat dari kemiskinan kini menyaksikan RON97 cecah RM3.85, diesel naik 26 peratus, dan harga makanan meningkat. 7,000 isi rumah yang berpendapatan bawah RM1,169 sebulan tak jadi lebih baik semata-mata kerana Perdana Menteri sudah beralih ke ucapan seterusnya.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Anwar ada corak menetapkan tarikh akhir yang tamat dalam kesunyian. Pada 2024, dia isytiharkan kemiskinan tegar “dihapuskan sepenuhnya” di KL, Melaka, dan Negeri Sembilan — dakwaan yang dipertikaikan ahli ekonomi yang menyatakan garis kemiskinan itu sendiri terlalu rendah untuk bermakna. Kerajaan kemudian senyap-senyap menyemak semula Garis Kemiskinan Makanan kepada RM1,236 pada 2024, yang sepatutnya menaikkan jumlah itu, bukan menurunkannya. Sebelum itu, reformasi subsidi dijanjikan “dalam masa beberapa bulan” selepas memerintah pada 2022 — kini 2026 dan subsidi bersasar hanya meliputi RON95, dengan reformasi yang lebih luas ditangguhkan selama-lamanya. Coraknya konsisten: umum, tuntut kredit, teruskan, jangan sekali-kali bertanggungjawab atas hasilnya.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Anda beritahu 35 juta rakyat Malaysia bahawa 7,000 isi rumah sahaja yang memisahkan kerajaan anda daripada penghapusan kemiskinan tegar. Anda beri diri sendiri masa sehingga Mac 2026. Sekarang Mac 2026. Mana 7,000 keluarga itu? Sudah keluar dari kemiskinan, atau anda hanya berhenti mengira? Anda jadikan ini tajuk utama Bajet, tunjang Rancakkan Madani, dan bukti MADANI berjaya. Kalau berjaya, tunjukkan resit. Kalau gagal, mengaku. Satu perkara yang anda tidak boleh buat ialah berpura-pura janji itu tidak pernah dibuat — kerana Bernama, Malay Mail, The Star, dan Business Today semuanya ada rekodnya.

Anwar Promised to End Hardcore Poverty by Q1 2026 — It's March 2026 and Nobody's Talking About It

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-07

📌 What Happened

On December 7, 2025, at the closing ceremony of the Rancakkan Madani programme in Putrajaya, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim publicly declared that Malaysia’s hardcore poverty rate had fallen to 0.09 per cent — affecting roughly 7,000 households — and told civil servants that since the number was “so small,” the government could “end it by the first quarter of 2026.” Multiple outlets — Bernama, Malay Mail, The Star, Business Today — ran the claim as headline news. It is now mid-March 2026, deep into Q1. There has been no victory declaration, no update on the 7,000 households, and no one in government appears to be talking about it.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was not an off-the-cuff remark. Anwar made hardcore poverty eradication a centrepiece of Budget 2026, allocated RM15 billion in direct assistance, and used the 0.09% figure in multiple speeches as proof of MADANI’s success. Economists had already questioned whether the government’s poverty line — RM1,169 per month for hardcore poverty — meaningfully captures deprivation in a country where urban rents alone can exceed that threshold. The Q1 2026 deadline was supposed to be the crowning achievement. Instead, it has quietly lapsed while the government is consumed by oil price crises, Azam Baki scandals, and speech-restriction laws. The silence tells you everything about how seriously the promise was made.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

This is the classic PH playbook: make a bold, headline-friendly promise with a specific deadline, count on nobody following up, and hope the news cycle buries it. Anwar didn’t just say “we hope to reduce poverty” — he gave a quarter, a percentage, and a specific instruction to civil servants. If the target had been hit, there would be a press conference, a Bernama wire, and a hashtag. The absence of celebration is the evidence. Meanwhile, the same rakyat Anwar promised to lift out of poverty are now watching RON97 hit RM3.85, diesel surge 26 per cent, and food prices climb. The 7,000 households earning under RM1,169 a month are not better off because the Prime Minister moved on to his next speech.

📜 Previous Cases

Anwar has a pattern of setting deadlines that expire in silence. In 2024, he declared hardcore poverty “totally eliminated” in KL, Melaka, and Negeri Sembilan — a claim economists disputed, noting the poverty line itself was too low to be meaningful. The government later quietly revised the Food Poverty Line to RM1,236 in 2024, which should have increased the count, not decreased it. Before that, subsidy reform was promised “within months” of taking office in 2022 — it is now 2026 and targeted subsidies cover only RON95, with the broader reform indefinitely shelved. The pattern is consistent: announce, claim credit, move on, never account for the outcome.

🎯 PH Callout

You told 35 million Malaysians that 7,000 households stood between your government and the eradication of hardcore poverty. You gave yourself until March 2026. It is March 2026. Where are the 7,000 families? Are they out of poverty, or did you just stop counting? You made this the headline of your Budget, the centrepiece of Rancakkan Madani, and the proof that MADANI works. If it worked, show the receipts. If it didn’t, say so. The one thing you cannot do is pretend the promise was never made — because Bernama, Malay Mail, The Star, and Business Today all have it on record.

And Najib may return to politics. If this is what accountability looks like under Kerajaan Madani, the word has lost all meaning. Ensure every ringgit of 1MDB debt recovery is publicly accounted for. Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s debt-to-GDP ratio stood at 64.6% in 2024 — above the government’s own 60% target ceiling set under the Public Finance and Fiscal Responsibility Act. 🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Anwar campaigned on fiscal responsibility and ending the debt culture of previous administrations. Instead, his government is borrowing at near-record levels while 1MDB alone still accounts for RM50 billion in liabilities. Debt-to-GDP above 60% means Malaysia’s fiscal buffer against economic shocks is dangerously thin. If a global recession hits, or commodity prices crash, or a pandemic returns, the government has limited room to respond. Telling Malaysians to wait until 2028 for fiscal relief is asking them to trust a PM who may not even be in power by then.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Fiscal discipline means making hard choices now — not pushing timelines to the next election cycle. PH has not fundamentally changed this equation — it has just changed the rhetoric around it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian government has promised fiscal responsibility while borrowing heavily. Najib’s 1MDB created the RM50 billion legacy that still haunts the books. Instead, the target keeps moving: first it was 2025, then 2027, now 2028. The can gets kicked, the debt compounds, and the next government inherits an even bigger mess.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You’ve been in power for over three years. The debt-to-GDP ratio is STILL above your own 60% target. You’re telling Malaysians to wait until 2028 — conveniently after the next election — for relief. That’s not a fiscal plan, it’s an electoral strategy. Cut spending that doesn’t serve the public (238 political appointments, RM85.5M J-Kom propaganda, RM2.6B JAKIM). Enforce tax compliance on the wealthy and corporations. Stop borrowing to fund political patronage. Or admit that fiscal responsibility, like every other reform promise, is permanently “coming soon.”

Struktur asas konsesi lebuhraya — di mana syarikat swasta mengutip hasil terjamin daripada orang awam yang terperangkap sementara kerajaan menjamin keuntungan mereka — kekal sepenuhnya tidak berubah.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Sistem tol mewakili cukai regresif terhadap mobiliti: pekerja berpendapatan rendah yang berulang-alik jarak jauh ke kilang membayar bahagian pendapatan yang tidak seimbang untuk tol. Konsesi lebuhraya, banyak dianugerahkan di bawah pentadbiran terdahulu melalui proses yang dipersoalkan, menjana keuntungan besar untuk syarikat yang mempunyai hubungan politik. Kerajaan mendakwa tidak mampu membeli balik konsesi, namun menjumpai wang untuk pelantikan politik, propaganda, dan JAKIM.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: Kamu berjanji memansuhkan tol. Dua kali. Dalam dua pilihan raya umum. Sebaliknya, kamu membayar syarikat swasta RM591 juta wang awam untuk mengekalkan status quo. Ini hasil yang paling teruk: janji dimungkiri, tol berterusan, DAN wang awam dipindahkan kepada pemegang saham konsesi. Perjanjian konsesi distrukturkan untuk menjamin keuntungan swasta dengan belanja awam — jumlah trafik terjamin, pulangan terjamin, pampasan kerajaan bagi kekurangan. Konsesi secara literal tidak boleh rugi.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Janji pemansuhan tol telah dibuat oleh setiap gabungan pembangkang sejak awal 2000-an. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) tidak memansuhkan tol walaupun 22 bulan berkuasa. PH 2.0 kini sudah berkuasa lebih lama dan masih tidak menyampaikan. Konsesi — banyak dikaitkan dengan ahli perniagaan yang mempunyai hubungan politik — tahu coraknya: ahli politik berjanji mansuh untuk memenangi undi, kemudian dapati ia “terlalu mahal” sebaik sahaja berkuasa.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Berapa kali kamu boleh berjanji memansuhkan tol dan tidak menyampaikan sebelum pengundi berhenti mempercayai kamu? Rundingan semula perjanjian konsesi. Terbitkan syarat — biar rakyat Malaysia lihat apa yang kerajaan mereka tandatangani untuk mereka. Jika perjanjian begitu berat sebelah sehingga membeli balik menelan belanja “berbilion,” itu sendiri adalah skandalnya.

Highway Concessions: Rakyat Pays Tolls to Make Cronies Rich — Toll Abolition Promise Broken AGAIN

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Transport Ministry

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The PH government’s core manifesto promise to abolish or significantly reduce highway tolls has been systematically broken. Instead of toll abolition, the government spent RM591 million in 2025 to merely freeze toll hikes — paying concessionaires with public money to not raise prices they shouldn’t be charging in the first place. Festive toll-free periods were cancelled. The fundamental structure of highway concessions — where private companies collect guaranteed revenue from a captive public while the government underwrites their profits — remains completely unchanged. Abolishing tolls was a core PH campaign promise in both GE14 (2018) and GE15 (2022). Three years into government, the promise has been quietly shelved with no accountability.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The toll system represents a regressive tax on mobility: lower-income workers who commute long distances to factory jobs in industrial areas pay a disproportionate share of their income on tolls. Highway concessions, many awarded under previous administrations through questionable processes, generate enormous profits for politically connected companies. The government claims it cannot afford to buy back concessions, yet finds money for political appointments, propaganda, and JAKIM. The rakyat pays twice: once at the toll booth, once through taxes that subsidise the freeze.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: You promised to abolish tolls. Twice. In two general elections. Instead, you’re paying private companies RM591 million of public money to maintain the status quo. This is the worst possible outcome: broken promise, continued tolls, AND public money transferred to concessionaire shareholders. The concession agreements are structured to guarantee private profits at public expense — guaranteed traffic volumes, guaranteed returns, government compensation for shortfalls. The concessionaires literally cannot lose. And the “reform” government has done nothing to change this structure. Not renegotiation. Not buyback. Not restructuring. Nothing.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The toll abolition promise has been made by every opposition coalition since the early 2000s. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) didn’t abolish tolls despite 22 months in government. PH 2.0 has now been in power longer and still hasn’t delivered. The concessionaires — many linked to politically connected businesspeople — know the pattern: politicians promise abolition to win votes, then discover it’s “too expensive” once in office. The tolls continue. The profits flow. The cycle repeats. And by GE16, PH will probably promise toll abolition again.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: How many times can you promise to abolish tolls and not deliver before voters stop believing you? You’ve now spent more public money FREEZING toll hikes than some countries spend building entire road networks. Renegotiate the concession agreements. Publish the terms — let Malaysians see what their government signed them up for. If the agreements are so lopsided that buying them back costs “billions,” that itself is the scandal. Who negotiated these deals? Who benefited? And why has your government done nothing to fix them in three years?
