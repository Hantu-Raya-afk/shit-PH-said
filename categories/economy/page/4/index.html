1MDB Debt Still RM50 Billion — Malaysia's Largest Fiscal Burden Under Anwar's Watch

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As of December 2025, Malaysia’s 1MDB debt still stands at approximately RM50 billion — the largest single fiscal burden in the country’s history. RM42 billion has already been paid in principal and interest. RM29 billion has been recovered through enforcement actions. Approximately RM13 billion remains to be settled. Under Anwar’s watch, Najib Razak — the PM who presided over 1MDB — had his jail sentence halved by the Pardons Board and was subsequently granted house arrest. The rakyat continues to pay the debt while the man responsible serves a comfortable sentence.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Every ringgit spent servicing 1MDB debt is a ringgit not spent on schools, hospitals, infrastructure, or social protection. The RM50 billion total — principal plus interest — represents the largest single act of kleptocracy in Malaysian history. Anwar made 1MDB accountability his signature promise. Instead, Najib got a halved sentence, house arrest, and whispers of a political return. The debt remains. The accountability was diluted. The rakyat pays.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar built his political career on holding Najib accountable for 1MDB. “Kerajaan Madani” was supposed to mean no impunity for corruption. Instead, under Anwar’s government: the Pardons Board halved Najib’s sentence, the courts granted house arrest, and political allies openly discuss Najib’s “contribution” to UMNO. The man who stole billions lives comfortably while 33 million Malaysians service the debt he created. This is not accountability — it is accommodation.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: 1MDB has been investigated on four continents. The US DOJ recovered over $1 billion in assets. Singapore jailed bankers. Switzerland sanctioned institutions. Goldman Sachs paid $3.9 billion in settlements. Malaysia — the country that was robbed — has done the least of any jurisdiction involved. Under Najib, the investigation was suppressed. Under Muhyiddin, it continued slowly. Under Anwar, the investigation supposedly concluded but the principal figure serves a reduced, comfortable sentence.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You promised 1MDB accountability. You delivered a halved sentence and house arrest for the man who presided over the theft. RM50 billion in total cost. RM13 billion still owed. And Najib may return to politics. If this is what accountability looks like under Kerajaan Madani, the word has lost all meaning. Ensure every ringgit of 1MDB debt recovery is publicly accounted for. Block any attempt at a full pardon. Or admit that 1MDB accountability was a campaign slogan, not a governance commitment.

Debt-to-GDP Still at 64.6% — Anwar Says Wait Until 2028 for Relief

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s debt-to-GDP ratio stood at 64.6% in 2024 — above the government’s own 60% target ceiling set under the Public Finance and Fiscal Responsibility Act. In December 2025, Anwar told Bloomberg that debt pressure would “ease from end-2028” — six years into his tenure before fiscal health even begins to improve. New borrowings were reduced to RM75 billion from RM100 billion but remain massive. The 1MDB legacy alone still accounts for RM50 billion in liabilities. CNA reported on Malaysia’s “increased spending” alongside the “low debt ratio” aspiration that remains unfulfilled.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Anwar campaigned on fiscal responsibility and ending the debt culture of previous administrations. Instead, his government is borrowing at near-record levels while 1MDB alone still accounts for RM50 billion in liabilities. Debt-to-GDP above 60% means Malaysia’s fiscal buffer against economic shocks is dangerously thin. If a global recession hits, or commodity prices crash, or a pandemic returns, the government has limited room to respond. Telling Malaysians to wait until 2028 for fiscal relief is asking them to trust a PM who may not even be in power by then.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Fiscal discipline means making hard choices now — not pushing timelines to the next election cycle. The 2028 target conveniently falls after GE16, meaning Anwar wants voters to re-elect him on the promise that fiscal improvement is “just around the corner” — the same promise he made in 2022. Meanwhile, spending continues to rise: civil service wages (1.6 million employees), subsidies (even after “rationalisation”), development projects (many driven by political rather than economic logic), and debt servicing (16% of revenue). The structural deficit means every year the government spends more than it earns, and borrows the difference. PH has not fundamentally changed this equation — it has just changed the rhetoric around it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian government has promised fiscal responsibility while borrowing heavily. Najib’s 1MDB created the RM50 billion legacy that still haunts the books. Muhyiddin’s pandemic spending was necessary but poorly tracked. Anwar inherited a mess — that’s true. But three years is enough time to demonstrate a credible path to fiscal consolidation. Instead, the target keeps moving: first it was 2025, then 2027, now 2028. The can gets kicked, the debt compounds, and the next government inherits an even bigger mess.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You’ve been in power for over three years. The debt-to-GDP ratio is STILL above your own 60% target. You’re telling Malaysians to wait until 2028 — conveniently after the next election — for relief. That’s not a fiscal plan, it’s an electoral strategy. Cut spending that doesn’t serve the public (238 political appointments, RM85.5M J-Kom propaganda, RM2.6B JAKIM). Enforce tax compliance on the wealthy and corporations. Stop borrowing to fund political patronage. Or admit that fiscal responsibility, like every other reform promise, is permanently “coming soon.”

Highway Concessions: Rakyat Pays Tolls to Make Cronies Rich — Toll Abolition Promise Broken AGAIN

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Transport Ministry

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The PH government’s core manifesto promise to abolish or significantly reduce highway tolls has been systematically broken. Instead of toll abolition, the government spent RM591 million in 2025 to merely freeze toll hikes — paying concessionaires with public money to not raise prices they shouldn’t be charging in the first place. Festive toll-free periods were cancelled. The fundamental structure of highway concessions — where private companies collect guaranteed revenue from a captive public while the government underwrites their profits — remains completely unchanged. Abolishing tolls was a core PH campaign promise in both GE14 (2018) and GE15 (2022). Three years into government, the promise has been quietly shelved with no accountability.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The toll system represents a regressive tax on mobility: lower-income workers who commute long distances to factory jobs in industrial areas pay a disproportionate share of their income on tolls. Highway concessions, many awarded under previous administrations through questionable processes, generate enormous profits for politically connected companies. The government claims it cannot afford to buy back concessions, yet finds money for political appointments, propaganda, and JAKIM. The rakyat pays twice: once at the toll booth, once through taxes that subsidise the freeze.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: You promised to abolish tolls. Twice. In two general elections. Instead, you’re paying private companies RM591 million of public money to maintain the status quo. This is the worst possible outcome: broken promise, continued tolls, AND public money transferred to concessionaire shareholders. The concession agreements are structured to guarantee private profits at public expense — guaranteed traffic volumes, guaranteed returns, government compensation for shortfalls. The concessionaires literally cannot lose. And the “reform” government has done nothing to change this structure. Not renegotiation. Not buyback. Not restructuring. Nothing.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The toll abolition promise has been made by every opposition coalition since the early 2000s. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) didn’t abolish tolls despite 22 months in government. PH 2.0 has now been in power longer and still hasn’t delivered. The concessionaires — many linked to politically connected businesspeople — know the pattern: politicians promise abolition to win votes, then discover it’s “too expensive” once in office. The tolls continue. The profits flow. The cycle repeats. And by GE16, PH will probably promise toll abolition again.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: How many times can you promise to abolish tolls and not deliver before voters stop believing you? You’ve now spent more public money FREEZING toll hikes than some countries spend building entire road networks. Renegotiate the concession agreements. Publish the terms — let Malaysians see what their government signed them up for. If the agreements are so lopsided that buying them back costs “billions,” that itself is the scandal. Who negotiated these deals? Who benefited? And why has your government done nothing to fix them in three years?

National Debt Servicing Eats 16% of Revenue — Every Sixth Ringgit Goes to Paying Interest

Said by: Ministry of Finance / Bloomberg

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s debt servicing costs consume approximately 16% of federal government revenue — meaning roughly every sixth ringgit collected in taxes goes straight to paying interest on accumulated debt rather than funding schools, hospitals, or infrastructure. With the debt-to-GDP ratio at 64.6% — above the government’s own 60% target — and 1MDB legacy debt still totalling RM50 billion on the books, fiscal space remains severely constrained. Anwar told Bloomberg that debt pressure would only “ease from end-2028.” The Star reported that 1MDB debt remains “Malaysia’s largest fiscal burden.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Debt servicing at 16% of revenue is a silent tax on the future. Every ringgit spent on interest is a ringgit NOT spent on healthcare (where 33,000 doctors are on temporary contracts), education (where PISA scores are declining), infrastructure (where LRT3 is delayed and roads crumble), or social safety nets (where B40 families struggle with costs). The compound effect is devastating: high debt requires high servicing, which reduces fiscal space, which forces more borrowing, which increases future servicing costs. It’s a fiscal doom loop — and PH has done nothing to break it.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government continues to find new spending commitments — subsidies, civil service wages, development projects, 238 political appointments to GLCs — that make fiscal consolidation an ever-receding target. The structural deficit means future generations of Malaysians will inherit a mountain of debt for spending decisions made today, with compound interest ensuring the problem only grows. When Anwar says debt pressure will “ease from end-2028,” he’s asking Malaysians to accept six years of fiscal pain for a promise of eventual improvement. That’s not a plan — it’s a hope, and hope compounds about as well as debt does.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s debt burden is not new, but it has been consistently worsened by political spending. 1MDB added RM50 billion. Pandemic stimulus under Muhyiddin added tens of billions more. Subsidy commitments under every government add annual billions. The Public Finance and Fiscal Responsibility Act was supposed to impose discipline — but the 60% debt-to-GDP target has been breached without consequence. The act has no enforcement mechanism, making it aspirational rather than binding. Every government passes fiscal responsibility laws and then ignores them.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Every sixth ringgit of tax revenue goes to paying interest — not building hospitals, not hiring teachers, not fixing roads. 16% of revenue servicing debt is a governance failure that spans administrations, but you’ve been in power long enough to own it now. Cut wasteful spending: 238 political GLC appointments, RM85.5M propaganda budgets, and discretionary allocations that serve politics rather than people. Enforce corporate tax compliance. Publish a credible fiscal consolidation roadmap with annual targets. Or keep kicking the can to 2028 — and let the next generation pay the bill.

Felda-Terengganu Land War — 15,000 Hectares Exploited for 40 Years Without Paying the State a Sen

Said by: Terengganu State Government / Felda

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-30

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: A decades-long land dispute between the Federal Land Development Authority (Felda) and the Terengganu state government exploded in late November 2025 when the state issued trespass notices covering 10 plantations totalling approximately 15,000 hectares. Terengganu accused Felda of developing state land for over 40 years without paying premiums, taxes, or profit shares — essentially extracting billions in palm oil revenue from state land for free. FGV Holdings was also served notices. DPM Zahid eventually stepped in for negotiations.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This is not a minor bureaucratic dispute — it is a case of a federal agency extracting billions from a state for four decades without compensation. The Terengganu state Exco accused Felda of using settlers as “human shields,” placing ordinary farmers in the middle of what is fundamentally a corporate land grab. States like Pahang and Sabah had received their payments — Terengganu was simply being ignored. The selective treatment raises the question: does the federal government treat PN-governed states differently from PH-governed ones?

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Terengganu demanded 30% of Felda’s gross profits in 2024 but Felda refused to even propose a counter-figure. The federal government — under PH oversight — allowed a GLC to freeload on state land for four decades while settlers bore the reputational cost. Only when Terengganu threatened eviction did DPM Zahid step in. That’s not governance — that’s damage control triggered by embarrassment. If this were a PH-governed state, the federal response would have been immediate. The delay tells you everything about how this government treats opposition states.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Federal-state land disputes are a recurring theme in Malaysian politics, and they almost always break along political lines. Kelantan’s oil royalty dispute with Petronas dragged on for decades under BN. Sabah and Sarawak have fought for resource rights since Malaysia’s formation. The pattern is consistent: federal agencies extract, states complain, and resolution only comes when political pressure becomes unbearable. Felda itself has a long history of mismanagement — RM4 billion in questionable investments under FGV, settler land schemes that enriched intermediaries rather than farmers.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You preach federalism and fair treatment of all states. But when it’s a PN-governed state being exploited by a federal agency, you looked the other way for years. Felda’s behaviour under your watch is indistinguishable from colonial extraction — take the resources, ignore the locals, and hide behind the settlers when caught. Pay Terengganu what it is owed. Audit every Felda operation on state land nationwide. Publish the revenue extracted versus premiums paid for every state. Or admit that your federalism only applies to states that voted for you.

Penang PTMP Procurement: No Open Tenders, Just 'Request for Proposals' for Billions in Contracts

Said by: Chow Kon Yeow / DAP Penang / Civil Society Critics

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-21

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Multiple reports, including from FMT and the academic community, documented that DAP’s Penang Transport Master Plan (PTMP) relied on ‘request-for-proposal’ (RFP) procurement mechanisms rather than open competitive tenders for billions of ringgit in infrastructure contracts. Civil society groups criticised this as opaque and developer-friendly. The PTMP includes the undersea tunnel (now under corruption trial), the Mutiara LRT, and various road projects — all procured without the standard open tender process that PH demands from the federal government.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Open competitive tendering is the gold standard for public procurement — it ensures the best price, prevents corruption, and allows public scrutiny. DAP demands open tenders from the federal government for every contract above RM50 million. But in Penang, where DAP has total control, billions in infrastructure contracts were awarded through RFP mechanisms that allow the state to pre-select bidders. The hypocrisy is structural, not incidental.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: DAP’s position on procurement is: open tenders for everyone else, RFPs for us. The party that lectured BN for decades about procurement transparency adopted the same opaque mechanisms when it controlled the purse strings. The undersea tunnel — the PTMP’s flagship project — is now a corruption case. MACC witnesses described it as a ’land scam.’ These are the outcomes of opaque procurement. DAP created the conditions for corruption by avoiding the safeguards it demands from others.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every major corruption scandal in Malaysian infrastructure — from 1MDB to PKFZ to the Pandora Papers — involved opaque procurement. DAP’s response to each was to demand transparency and open tenders. Yet in Penang, the same party used RFPs for its most expensive projects. The evidence from the Guan Eng trial suggests the procurement process was exploited for personal benefit.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You demand open tenders from Putrajaya. You use RFPs in Penang. Your former CM is on trial over a project procured through your preferred opaque method. The lesson is obvious: adopt open competitive tendering for all state projects above RM10 million. Publish all procurement documents. And stop using a procurement method that your own corruption case demonstrates can be exploited.

Data Centre Boom Drains Water and Power — Johor Told to Wait Until 2027 for Water Supply

Said by: Johor state government and Anwar Ibrahim

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s data centre gold rush hit a wall in late 2025 when Johor — ground zero for the country’s silicon valley ambitions — told investors to postpone water-cooled projects for at least 18 months because the state couldn’t supply enough water. By February 2026, Anwar froze all non-AI data centre approvals entirely. Between 2023 and 2025, the government had approved billions in data centre projects from Microsoft, Google, AWS, Oracle, ByteDance, and GDS Holdings — without ensuring the infrastructure could support them. Malaysia’s national non-revenue water rate is 37.1%, meaning over a third of treated water is lost to leaking pipes before reaching consumers.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The government celebrated billions in data centre investment commitments as proof that the Madani economy was attracting global capital. Now it’s telling those same investors to wait because there’s no water and not enough electricity. You cannot approve massive industrial projects that each consume as much water as a small city without ensuring the infrastructure can support them. This is basic governance, and the government failed at it because investment announcements make better headlines than infrastructure audits. The freeze creates investor uncertainty at exactly the wrong moment — companies that committed billions based on government approvals are now being told to wait.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government simultaneously approved a Johor special economic zone designed to attract even more industrial investment, creating a direct conflict between economic ambition and infrastructure reality. Malaysia loses 37.1% of treated water to leaking pipes. States like Perlis and Kelantan waste 50-60%. Building water-intensive industries on top of a crumbling water system is negligence. The irony is suffocating: the government that can’t maintain water pipes for its own citizens approved water-guzzling data centres for foreign corporations, then had to tell both groups to wait. Governance by headline, failure by infrastructure.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a pattern of approving mega-projects before building the infrastructure to support them. The Iskandar Malaysia development zone promised a “world-class city” but still struggles with basic services. Forest City was approved as a RM400 billion development but sits largely empty. Each time, the government prioritises investment announcements over infrastructure planning, then scrambles when reality catches up. The data centre freeze is the latest iteration of the same pattern: approve first, plan never.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You approved billions in data centre investment without ensuring water and electricity supply. Now you’re telling investors to wait 18 months. Fix the pipes first — 37.1% non-revenue water is a national embarrassment. Publish a national infrastructure capacity assessment before approving any new industrial projects. Stop announcing investment wins that you can’t deliver on. Or keep building a silicon valley on a foundation of leaking pipes and overloaded grids — and watch investors move to Thailand and Indonesia instead.

Penang Undersea Tunnel Project 'Still Active' — 14 Years, Zero Construction, RM6.3B Price Tag

Said by: Chow Kon Yeow, Penang Chief Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-14

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 14 November 2025, Chow Kon Yeow told media that the Penang Undersea Tunnel Project is ’not an abandoned project but is still active and in the technical review and design completion phase.’ The RM6.3 billion project was proposed in 2011 under the Penang Transport Master Plan (PTMP). Fourteen years later, not a single metre of tunnel has been constructed. Meanwhile, the corruption trial of former CM Lim Guan Eng over the project continues, with MACC witnesses describing it as ‘akin to a land scam.’

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A RM6.3 billion project proposed 14 years ago with zero construction is either a failed project or a phantom one. Chow’s insistence that it’s ‘still active’ strains credibility to breaking point. The project was supposed to solve Penang’s traffic crisis — which has only worsened during the 14-year wait. Meanwhile, the former CM who approved the project is on trial for corruption related to it. At what point does ‘still active’ become ‘still pretending’?

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: ‘Technical review and design completion phase’ — after 14 years. For comparison, China built the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge (the world’s longest sea crossing) in 9 years. Singapore built the Thomson-East Coast MRT line in less time. Penang can’t complete the design phase for a tunnel that was supposed to be built a decade ago. The project exists on paper to justify the PTMP’s existence and the land reclamation that was supposed to fund it. The tunnel is Schrödinger’s infrastructure — simultaneously active and non-existent.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The PTMP was criticised from the start for its opaque procurement — using request-for-proposal mechanisms instead of open tenders. The Penang South Islands reclamation was conceived as the funding mechanism for the PTMP. So the logic is: destroy the sea to fund a tunnel that hasn’t been built in 14 years. Each project justifies the other; neither delivers for the public.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Fourteen years. RM6.3 billion. Zero metres built. Former CM on trial for corruption related to the project. Current CM insists it’s ‘still active.’ At what point do you admit this project is a failure? Cancel it, audit the spending, and redirect the funds to transport solutions that can actually be built — bus rapid transit, cycling infrastructure, traffic management. Penang’s commuters need solutions, not phantom tunnels.

US Declares 'Victory Against China' After Securing Unlimited Minerals Pledge From Anwar

Said by: MalaysiaNow report

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 November 2025, MalaysiaNow reported that the United States had “declared victory against China” after securing a pledge from Anwar Ibrahim to not restrict exports of critical minerals and rare earth elements to the US. Under the US-Malaysia ART, Malaysia committed to “refrain from banning, or imposing quotas on, exports to the US of critical minerals or rare earth elements” — a binding trade commitment, not a goodwill gesture. Malaysia also agreed to partner with American firms “to create certainty for businesses to increase production capacity,” essentially guaranteeing US access to Malaysian mineral resources in perpetuity.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Indonesia restricted nickel exports in 2020 and used the leverage to build a domestic processing industry now worth billions of dollars — attracting smelter investments, creating tens of thousands of jobs, and moving up the value chain. Malaysia just agreed to keep sending raw materials to the US. One country used its resources to industrialise. The other agreed to remain a raw material supplier. Rare earth export controls are a key geopolitical tool — perhaps the most powerful one a resource-rich country possesses. China controls 60% of global rare earth mining and 90% of processing, and uses export controls as geopolitical leverage. Malaysia gave up the same strategic tool in exchange for a tariff reduction from 25% to 19%.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: When the US “declares victory against China” using your resources, you are not a partner — you are a pawn. The framing says it all: this was about US strategic interests, and Malaysia was the tool. The long-term cost is incalculable. Critical minerals are finite. Once committed to unrestricted export, Malaysia cannot later impose controls without breaching the ART and triggering US retaliation. The country has locked itself into raw material dependency. Anwar’s defenders argue the deal preserves market access and attracts investment. But market access at the cost of resource sovereignty is a losing trade. Ask any country that exported its resources without adding value — they are all still developing countries.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s resource export history is a cautionary tale. The country was once the world’s largest tin producer — it exported raw tin for decades while others built processing industries. By the time Malaysia tried to add value, the tin market had moved on. The same pattern played out with rubber, palm oil, and petroleum. Each time, Malaysia exported raw commodities while neighbours built processing capacity. Indonesia’s nickel strategy — restrict exports, force domestic processing, capture value — is exactly what Malaysia should have done with rare earths. Instead, Anwar signed away the option.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Indonesia used resource nationalism to build a billion-dollar nickel processing industry. You used your resources to get a 6-percentage-point tariff reduction. One strategy builds industrial capacity. The other locks you into raw material dependency. Renegotiate the minerals clause. Impose a domestic processing requirement before export. Build rare earth refining capacity in Malaysia instead of shipping raw materials to American factories. Or keep letting Washington “declare victory” using your resources — and explain to the next generation why Malaysia is still a raw material supplier.

'Stop Lecturing Us on Sovereign Rights' — US Envoy Told Off Amid Anger Over Trade Deal

Said by: Malaysian public and politicians

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Days after the US-Malaysia trade deal was signed, public anger boiled over. The US envoy was publicly told to “stop lecturing us on sovereign rights” amid growing fury over what critics called Anwar’s “surrender” to Washington. MalaysiaNow reported the confrontation. The deal required Malaysia to align with US export controls, sanctions, and trade enforcement — provisions widely seen as infringing on sovereignty. Adding insult to injury, details of the agreement were published on the White House website before being shared with the Malaysian Parliament, fueling perceptions that Malaysia was an afterthought in its own trade negotiations.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When your own people are telling foreign diplomats to stop lecturing them, the government has a legitimacy problem. The deal triggered a rare moment of bipartisan fury — both opposition and ruling coalition members questioned the terms. A deal signed in haste, published by the other side first, and defended after the fact is not diplomacy — it is capitulation with a press release. Malaysia’s neutral foreign policy — maintained across BN, PH 1.0, and PN — was compromised in one signing ceremony.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government’s defence was that the deal preserved market access and prevented worse tariffs. But “it could have been worse” is not a negotiating triumph — it’s an admission of weakness. Publishing the deal on the White House website before Parliament saw it was not a procedural oversight — it was a power display by Washington showing who controls the narrative. Anwar’s team either agreed to this sequencing (which is humiliating) or didn’t anticipate it (which is incompetent). Either way, the Malaysian public learned about their own trade deal from a foreign government’s website.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a long history of trade agreements that prioritise access over sovereignty. The TPPA under Najib triggered massive protests over investor-state dispute settlement (ISDS) clauses. PH 1.0 withdrew from the TPPA, calling it an infringement on sovereignty. PH 2.0 has now signed a bilateral deal with sovereignty provisions that are arguably worse — and did so without the parliamentary scrutiny that even the TPPA received. The cycle is consistent: opposition parties criticise trade deals as sovereignty surrenders, then sign similar or worse deals when they come to power.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You withdrew from the TPPA because it infringed on sovereignty. Then you signed a bilateral deal that commits Malaysia to US export controls, sanctions alignment, and unrestricted mineral exports — and let Washington publish the terms before your own Parliament saw them. If the TPPA was a sovereignty problem, this deal is worse. Table the full agreement in Parliament for debate. Publish a clause-by-clause comparison with the TPPA you rejected. Explain why this is different — if you can. Or admit that sovereignty concerns were only useful when you were in opposition.

Sources: MalaysiaNow
