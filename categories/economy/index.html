Cabinet Maintains RON95 at RM1.99 — Anwar Calls It a 'Difficult Decision' While Dodging Subsidy Reform for the Third Year Running

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced on March 11, 2026 — following a Cabinet meeting — that the price of RON95 petrol under the Budi95 targeted subsidy mechanism will remain at RM1.99 per litre. He called it a “difficult decision,” noting that the market price of RON95 has risen roughly threefold due to the escalating US-Iran conflict disrupting oil markets and threatening Gulf shipping routes. RON97 had already risen to RM3.25, unsubsidised RON95 to RM2.67, and diesel to RM3.12. Anwar framed the decision as a sacrifice for the rakyat.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not generosity — it is the collapse of reform. Anwar’s government promised targeted fuel subsidy restructuring as a cornerstone of fiscal discipline. The Budi95 mechanism, launched as a watered-down compromise, was supposed to be the first step toward removing blanket subsidies. Instead, a geopolitical crisis has given the government the perfect excuse to shelve reform indefinitely while spending billions on untargeted fuel subsidies ahead of widely expected snap elections. Every week the subsidy is maintained at current market spreads, Malaysia hemorrhages fiscal resources that could go to healthcare, education, or infrastructure.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar has been “about to reform” fuel subsidies since he took office in November 2022. In 2023, it was “next year.” In 2024, it was “soon.” In 2025, the Budi95 mechanism finally launched — then immediately became a political tool rather than a fiscal one. Just five days ago, Anwar admitted he could only hold the RM1.99 price for “one or two months.” Now, with Hari Raya approaching and elections rumoured for mid-2026, the Cabinet “decides” to maintain it — as if there was ever a chance they’d raise fuel prices weeks before a festive season and a potential snap poll. This isn’t a difficult decision. It’s the only decision a government terrified of voters would make.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has been trapped in the fuel subsidy cycle for decades. Najib’s government removed RON95 subsidies in 2014 with a managed float, only for Mahathir’s PH 1.0 government to reintroduce price ceilings. Anwar promised to break the pattern with targeted subsidies — means-tested, fiscally responsible, reform-driven. Instead, Budi95 kept the same RM1.99 price for the vast majority of motorists and excluded only foreign-registered vehicles and luxury cars. The IMF, World Bank, and Malaysia’s own fiscal policy advisors have repeatedly warned that blanket fuel subsidies are regressive, benefiting the wealthy more than the poor. Anwar knows this. He cited these studies himself when he was in opposition.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you’d be different. You said you’d make the hard calls Najib and Muhyiddin wouldn’t. You quoted fiscal responsibility in every speech, promised targeted subsidies in every manifesto, and lectured the opposition about populism from every podium. Now you’re spending billions to keep fuel cheap during Ramadan, ahead of Hari Raya, ahead of elections you’re quietly preparing for. This isn’t reform. This is the oldest trick in Malaysian politics — and you swore you’d never play it. Every ringgit spent on untargeted subsidies is a ringgit stolen from the structural reforms you promised the rakyat.

Oil Past $100, Strait of Hormuz Shut, Rakyat Burning Fuel to Commute — Anwar Won't Mandate WFH, Freeze Tolls, Cap Food Prices, or Cut GLC Fat

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

The Iran-US war has pushed global oil past $100 per barrel, effectively shut down the Strait of Hormuz — through which nearly 80% of Asia’s oil transits — and sent Malaysia’s FBM KLCI plunging 2.55% in a single session on March 9. RON97 is up to RM3.25, diesel to RM3.12, and Anwar himself admitted on March 7 that the government can only hold RON95 at RM1.99 for ‘one or two more months.’ Freight costs are surging, food prices are next, and experts from NST to Hong Leong IB are warning of imported inflation hitting the rakyat’s kitchen table. Anwar’s response so far: a strongly worded tweet about solidarity, an emergency parliamentary motion condemning Israel, and Zafrul saying Malaysia is ‘prepared’ — without naming a single concrete domestic measure to protect workers and families from the economic fallout.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a theoretical crisis. Malaysians are already paying more at the pump for RON97 and diesel. Freight costs feed directly into food, medicine, and essential goods. The stock market is bleeding. And yet the Prime Minister — who is also the Finance Minister — has not announced a single emergency domestic economic measure. No crisis-mode intervention. No relief. Just speeches about geopolitics while the rakyat’s cost of living climbs in real time. Malaysia proved during COVID that the government can act decisively on domestic economic protection when it wants to. The difference is political will — and right now, Anwar’s will is directed at Parliament speeches about Zionist plots, not at shielding the people he governs.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Four things Anwar could do tomorrow with existing executive power — and won’t:

  1. Mandate work-from-home for all WFH-compatible jobs. Malaysia already has a Flexible Work Arrangement framework under the Employment Act. During COVID, the government mandated WFH and it worked. With oil past $100 and commuters burning RM300-500/month on fuel and tolls, a temporary WFH directive for office-based workers would immediately cut household fuel costs, reduce demand on subsidised petrol, and ease the fiscal pressure Anwar says he can only hold for two months. Instead, millions of KL workers sit in traffic burning fuel the government can barely afford to subsidise.

  2. Freeze or waive highway tolls during the crisis. The government already broke its toll abolition promise — paying RM591 million just to freeze hikes rather than remove tolls. A temporary toll waiver during a war-driven energy crisis is basic economic relief. Singapore waived ERP during COVID disruptions. Malaysia won’t even pause toll collection while fuel prices spike.

  3. Impose emergency price controls on essential food items. Freight costs from Hormuz disruptions are already feeding into import prices. Cooking oil, flour, rice, and protein prices will follow. The Price Control and Anti-Profiteering Act 2011 gives the government power to gazette maximum prices for essential goods. Anwar used this power selectively before. Now, when a global war is about to hit Malaysian dinner tables, silence.

  4. Freeze non-essential GLC and political appointment spending. The IDEAS report found 238 political appointments to FSBs and GLCs under Anwar. Every one of those comes with salaries, allowances, and perks funded by public money. During a war-driven fiscal crunch — with the subsidy bill ballooning and oil revenue uncertain — a responsible government would freeze all discretionary patronage spending and redirect it to rakyat relief. Instead, the appointments continue.

📜 Previous Cases

During COVID-19, the Muhyiddin government — for all its democratic illegitimacy — implemented a blanket WFH directive, moratoriums on loan repayments, cash handouts (BPN), and wage subsidies within weeks of the crisis hitting. The Ismail Sabri government continued with hybrid WFH arrangements and extended moratoriums. These were imperfect governments acting under crisis pressure. Anwar, who controls both the PM’s office and the Finance Ministry, has more concentrated fiscal power than either predecessor — and has used less of it in response to a war that is already hitting Malaysian wallets harder than the early weeks of COVID hit fuel prices. The precedent exists. The tools exist. The power exists. The will does not.

🎯 PH Callout

You told us you were different. You told us Madani meant the people come first. You told us having the PM also serve as Finance Minister would mean faster, more decisive economic management. So where is it? Oil is past $100. The Strait of Hormuz is disrupted. Your own deputy economy minister admits growth projections are shaky. Your own mouth admitted the RON95 subsidy has weeks left. And your crisis response is… a parliamentary motion about Iran and a tweet about solidarity? Mandate WFH. Freeze tolls. Cap food prices. Cut the GLC fat. You have the power. You have the precedent. You have the tools. Use them — or admit that ‘Ekonomi Madani’ was never about protecting the rakyat. It was about protecting the coalition.

Anwar Brings Forward RM15 Billion STR Cash Handout — Record Spending, Hari Raya Timing, Snap Election Vibes

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On March 9, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced that Phase 2 of the Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah (STR) cash transfer programme would be brought forward to March 10 — weeks ahead of its originally scheduled date. The early disbursement will benefit 5.2 million recipients.

Anwar framed the move as helping Malaysians prepare for Hari Raya Aidilfitri. The total 2026 allocation for STR and SARA (Sumbangan Asas Rahmah) stands at RM15 billion — the highest in the history of the Federal Government’s direct cash assistance programmes.

This comes on the same day Anwar also announced a separate RM500 special financial aid for civil servants in Grade 15 and below (to be paid March 13) and mosque staff.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Three things make this significant:

  1. Record spending on cash transfers. RM15 billion is not pocket change. This is the largest direct cash handout allocation any Malaysian government has ever made — and it comes from a PM who campaigned on fiscal discipline, subsidy rationalisation, and ending the culture of handouts.
  2. The timing is suspicious. Reuters reported on March 6 that government sources say Anwar could call snap elections as early as July 2026. Bringing forward cash payments right before Hari Raya — the single most politically sensitive festive period in Malaysia — is textbook pre-election cash politics.
  3. Structural reform is nowhere. Cash transfers are not economic policy. They are a painkiller. The RON95 fuel subsidy rationalisation has been delayed repeatedly. The promised targeted subsidy mechanism is still not operational. Instead of fixing the system, the government is writing bigger cheques.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Anwar spent years — decades — attacking BN’s BR1M cash handout programme as vote-buying, populist bribery, and fiscal irresponsibility. He called it a “crutch” that kept the rakyat dependent on government generosity instead of building real economic resilience.

Now he’s running the exact same playbook, except he’s spending more money and bringing payments forward to land right before Hari Raya. STR is BR1M with a rebrand. The only difference is the name on the cheque.

The “highest allocation in history” line is not a flex — it’s a confession. If the economy is performing as well as the government claims (4-4.5% GDP growth, record approved investments), why does the government need to spend a record RM15 billion on cash handouts? Either the growth isn’t reaching ordinary people, or this is about elections, not economics. Probably both.

Anwar urged recipients to “spend wisely.” Here’s a thought: maybe the government should spend wisely too.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

  • BR1M under Najib (2012-2018): Anwar and PH relentlessly attacked Barisan Nasional’s Bantuan Rakyat 1Malaysia (BR1M) as unsustainable vote-buying. DAP called it “irresponsible fiscal policy.” PKR called it “bribing the poor.” Now they run a bigger version of the same programme.
  • 2024 Hari Raya handouts: Anwar brought forward STR payments before Hari Raya 2024 as well. This is now an annual pattern — accelerate cash disbursements to coincide with the festive season for maximum political goodwill.
  • RON95 subsidy delays (2024-2026): The government has repeatedly postponed targeted fuel subsidy reforms. Instead of fixing the subsidy structure, they keep layering cash transfers on top. The fiscal consolidation story — deficit down from 5.5% to 3.5% — looks less impressive when you’re simultaneously running record cash transfer programmes.
  • Snap election signals (March 2026): Reuters reported on March 6 that two government lawmakers confirmed Anwar could call snap polls as early as July. Cash before elections is the oldest trick in Malaysian politics.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You promised “reformasi, bukan derma” — reform, not charity. You told Malaysians that PH would build an economy where people didn’t need handouts. You mocked Najib for BR1M. You called it a plaster on a broken bone.

Now you’re spending RM15 billion on the same plaster — and you’re proud of it. You brought the payment forward to land right before Raya, right before the rumoured snap election window. And you have the audacity to call this “Rahmah” — compassion.

This isn’t compassion. This is campaign spending filed under a different budget line. If you actually believed in reform, you’d be fixing wages, fixing subsidies, and fixing the structural reasons people need these handouts in the first place. Instead, you’re writing record cheques and hoping the rakyat forgets you promised something better.

You say reform is “non-negotiable.” Then why does every reform end up being negotiated away?

The AG-PP separation — delayed. The PM tenure limit — failed on your own attendance. Azam Baki — still in office. RON95 — still subsidised with no timeline. IPCMC — abandoned.

You have the parliamentary majority. You have the institutional mandate. You have the public goodwill — or what remains of it.

What you don’t have is the political will to spend your coalition capital on anything that doesn’t benefit the coalition itself.

Stop telling the Securities Commission audience that reform is non-negotiable. Start telling your own Cabinet.

Anwar's Government Fights 531,976 Retirees in Court — Wins Appeal to Block RM1.7 Billion Pension Payout

Said by: Federal Government / Senior Federal Counsel Sallehuddin Ali

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 What Happened

On 9 March 2026, the Court of Appeal unanimously overturned a High Court order that would have required Anwar Ibrahim’s government to pay RM1.7 billion in pension arrears to 531,976 former civil servants. The three-judge panel — led by Justice Azhahari Kamal Ramli, with Justices Lim Chong Fong and Meor Hashimi Abdul Hamid — ruled that the pensioners’ application was res judicata (already adjudicated) and constituted an “abuse of court process.” The government, represented by Senior Federal Counsel Sallehuddin Ali, successfully argued that the 2016 Public Service Department Circular (PP 1/2016) was an “enhancement” to the civil service scheme, not a mandatory salary revision triggering pension adjustments. The original case was brought by Aminah Ahmad, a retired Ministry of Foreign Affairs officer, on behalf of 56 other retirees in 2017.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Madani government actively going to court to avoid paying half a million retirees money they were legally awarded. The average payout per retiree would have been roughly RM3,195 — not a fortune, but real money for people on fixed incomes. The government chose to fight this rather than honour a High Court order. This is a government that spends billions on Hari Raya cash handouts and election-year STR payments, but when pensioners win a court ruling for pension adjustments based on the government’s own 2016 circular, Putrajaya deploys federal counsel to crush the claim on a technicality. The signal is clear: if you’re politically useful, you get cash. If you’re a retiree with a legal claim, you get lawyers.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim built his career on defending the little guy. His government now argues that a PSD circular meant to adjust pensions in line with civil service salary changes was merely an “enhancement” — bureaucratic doublespeak for “we wrote it, but we didn’t mean it.” The Court of Appeal’s ruling that this was “abuse of process” doesn’t mean the pensioners’ grievance is illegitimate — it means the government found a procedural exit ramp and took it. Meanwhile, Putrajaya approved RM15 billion in STR cash handouts just days earlier. The message: Anwar’s government has money for voters, not for retirees who already served the country.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysian governments have a long history of treating pension adjustments as political favours rather than legal obligations. The 2013 amendment to the Pension Adjustment Act — which provided retirees with a fixed annual 2% increase — was itself invalidated by the Court of Appeal in 2022, stripping retirees of a guaranteed mechanism. Every administration since has treated pension reform as a hot potato: promise it during campaigns, dodge it in office. The Madani government is no different. Anwar’s own 2022 election manifesto spoke of “dignifying” civil servants and retirees, but the legal strategy his government is pursuing tells a different story.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised kerajaan Madani — a civilised government that upholds justice and human dignity. Half a million people who spent their careers serving this country were told by a High Court that they are owed pension adjustments. Your government’s response was not to pay, not to negotiate, but to appeal and win on a technicality. You found RM15 billion for pre-Raya handouts but fought tooth and nail against RM1.7 billion for pensioners. That is not reform. That is not Madani. That is the same old Putrajaya arithmetic: spend where the votes are, litigate where they aren’t.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This was not a casual remark. Anwar made the eradication of hardcore poverty a centrepiece of the Madani economic narrative. No revised timeline. No acknowledgement that the target even exists. This is the same administration that loudly claims credit for every ringgit of GDP growth and every decimal point drop in inflation. But when it comes to a concrete promise with a concrete deadline? Silence. If hardcore poverty has been eradicated, say so. Publish the data. Show the methodology. Let independent economists verify it. If it hasn’t been eradicated, say that too. Explain what went wrong. Revise the timeline. Be honest. What you don’t get to do is make a grand promise, ride the PR wave, and then pretend the deadline doesn’t exist.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: This is not PH’s first vanishing deadline:

  • RON95 fuel subsidy reform has been delayed repeatedly. Anwar himself admitted in early March 2026 that the government could only hold the price for “one or two more months” — yet no concrete reform plan has been announced.
  • The AG-Public Prosecutor separation bill was postponed in March 2026 for a 3-month committee review, after being a signature reform promise.
  • The PM term limit constitutional amendment failed in Parliament in March 2026 — despite PH’s commanding majority — because 8 government MPs didn’t even show up to vote. The pattern is clear: big promises, loud announcements, quiet retreats. The Madani government treats reform pledges as PR events, not binding commitments. Malaysia’s own history is littered with poverty eradication claims that didn’t survive contact with reality. The Najib administration claimed similar progress; independent audits later showed significant undercounting.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You told 33 million Malaysians that hardcore poverty would be gone by Q1 2026. You said it on camera, in Putrajaya, at your own showcase event. Bernama carried it. Every major outlet reported it. It’s March 9. Q1 is almost over. Where’s the announcement? Where’s the data? Where’s the press conference where you pop the champagne and show us the zero? Or did you hope we’d forget? Here’s the thing about specific promises with specific deadlines: people remember. You don’t get to coast on vibes when you gave us a date. Either deliver the results or deliver an explanation. The rakyat who are still living on less than RM1,169 a month deserve more than silence.

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, Senior Political Advisor to PM

Anwar Admits He Can Only Hold RON95 Subsidy for 'One or Two Months' — While Fuel Prices Spike Across the Board

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 What Happened

On March 6, 2026, Prime Minister and Finance Minister Anwar Ibrahim told reporters that Malaysia can maintain the subsidised RON95 petrol price at RM1.99 per litre under the Budi95 scheme — but only for ‘one or two months.’ His exact words: ‘We will try to control the effects of the Iranian conflict, including for RON95, which is RM1.99 per litre. We will monitor this situation because so far, we can still hold off for one or two months.’ This came the same week that RON97 petrol rose 10 sen to RM3.25 per litre, unsubsidised RON95 climbed 8 sen to RM2.67, and diesel in Peninsular Malaysia jumped 8 sen to RM3.12 — all driven by the escalating Middle East conflict and 200 vessels stuck in the Strait of Hormuz.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the first time Anwar has publicly put a time limit on the government’s ability to hold fuel subsidies. The hedging language — ‘we will try,’ ‘hold off,’ ‘monitor’ — is a significant shift from previous assurances. If the Strait of Hormuz disruption continues, and 20% of the world’s oil supply remains at risk, the RM1.99 price becomes fiscally unsustainable. But Anwar cannot politically afford a fuel price hike — especially not with Hari Raya weeks away, snap election rumours swirling for July, and RM15 billion in STR cash handouts already deployed. He’s caught between fiscal reality and electoral survival, and the ‘one or two months’ language tells you which one he’s prioritising.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent three years telling Malaysians that Budi95’s targeted subsidy system was a masterstroke of fiscal reform — smarter, fairer, and more sustainable than blanket subsidies. Now, the moment global oil prices actually test the system, he’s already hedging and saying he can only hold it for a couple of months. What happened to the RM800 million in savings the government claimed from Budi95? What happened to Petronas’s RM32 billion dividend to the government? The truth is that the targeted subsidy was always designed for calm seas. The moment a real shock hits — which anyone tracking Middle East geopolitics could have predicted — the whole system starts to wobble. And instead of a plan, we get ‘we will try.’

📜 Previous Cases

This is a recurring pattern with Anwar on fuel policy: promise stability, deliver uncertainty. In early March, he told Dewan Negara the government would ’try to keep’ RON95 at RM1.99 — vague language that gave no timeline. Fuel prices have been creeping up steadily since January 2026, with diesel alone rising from RM2.84 in mid-January to RM3.12 by early March. Each week brings a new price adjustment upward, while the government insists the situation is ‘under control.’ It mirrors the BN-era playbook: reassure the public while the numbers quietly deteriorate, then act surprised when the dam breaks.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians that Budi95 was reform. You told them targeted subsidies would protect the rakyat while saving the government money. Now you’re admitting, publicly, that the system can only survive ‘one or two months’ of real global pressure. If this is fiscal reform, it’s built on cardboard. Where is the contingency plan? Where is the honest conversation with the public about what happens if oil hits USD100 a barrel and stays there? Stop managing headlines and start managing the economy. The rakyat deserves more than ‘we will try’ from the man who is simultaneously Prime Minister and Finance Minister.
