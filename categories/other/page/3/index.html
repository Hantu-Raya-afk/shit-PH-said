[BM] Penyahutanan Kelapa Sawit Berterusan Tanpa Kawalan — Ikrar Alam Sekitar Lawan Keuntungan Ladang

Said by: Environmental NGOs / SAM

Category: Other

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Walaupun komitmen Malaysia kepada perjanjian iklim antarabangsa dan matlamat pembangunan mampan, penyahutanan untuk ladang kelapa sawit terus merosakkan hutan hujan tropika antara yang tertua dan paling biodiversiti di Bumi. Skim pensijilan Minyak Sawit Mampan Malaysia (MSPO) dikritik NGO antarabangsa sebagai pencucian hijau yang tidak mencukupi. Kualiti air sungai Malaysia merosot — sungai bersih turun dari 486 ke 468 pada 2025. Sungai Perak bertukar biru akibat pelepasan perlombongan nadir bumi pada November 2025. Spesies terancam termasuk orang utan, gajah pygmy, dan harimau Malaya menghadapi habitat yang semakin mengecil.

🔥 KENAPA IA PENTING: Malaysia menyumbang kira-kira 25% pengeluaran minyak sawit global, dan kebergantungan ekonomi terhadap komoditi ini mencipta rintangan politik yang besar terhadap pembaharuan alam sekitar yang tulen. Kos alam sekitar — kehilangan biodiversiti, pencemaran sungai, pelepasan karbon daripada saliran tanah gambut — tidak dikira dalam angka KDNK tetapi ditanggung oleh komuniti yang bergantung pada air bersih, hutan sihat, dan ekosistem yang berfungsi.

💩 KENAPA IA KARUT: Skim pensijilan MSPO adalah pelekat, bukan standard. Ia membenarkan kemerosotan alam sekitar berterusan sambil menyediakan penampilan kelestarian yang memuaskan pembeli antarabangsa. Kerajaan bercakap tentang kelestarian di sidang kemuncak COP dan persidangan antarabangsa sambil meluluskan pembangunan ladang baru di dalam negara.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Di bawah BN, kadar penyahutanan Malaysia antara yang tertinggi di dunia. Era Mahathir menyaksikan penukaran besar-besaran hutan primer kepada ladang. Di bawah PH 1.0, moratorium lesen kelapa sawit baru seketika dibincangkan. Di bawah PH 2.0, MSPO diperluaskan tetapi tidak diperkukuhkan. Setiap kerajaan membayar lip service kepada perlindungan alam sekitar sambil melindungi industri ladang.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Kamu menandatangani perjanjian iklim dan bergambar di sidang kemuncak COP. Kemudian kamu pulang dan luluskan lebih banyak ladang. Skim MSPO kamu hiasan tingkap. Sungai kamu semakin kotor. Hutan kamu semakin kecil. Perkukuhkan MSPO mengikut standard antarabangsa. Kuatkuasakan undang-undang pembalakan haram. Atau terus berucap kelestarian sementara asas alam sekitar negara kamu runtuh.

Climate Action Failure — Malaysia's Paris Agreement Pledges Are Fantasy, Emissions Keep Rising

Said by: Climate Action Tracker / Environmental Groups

Category: Other

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 What Happened

Despite being a signatory to the Paris Agreement and making ambitious pledges on carbon emission reductions, Malaysia’s actual emissions trajectory continues upward. Deforestation continues, coal power plants remain operational, data centre energy consumption grows, and the government has offered no credible pathway to meeting its stated climate commitments.

🔥 Why It Matters

Climate change is already affecting Malaysia through more severe floods (2024 devastation), prolonged droughts, rising sea levels threatening coastal communities, and biodiversity loss. The country is simultaneously a victim and contributor — its palm oil industry and deforestation are significant global emission sources. Failure to act on climate isn’t just bad policy — it’s an existential threat to Malaysia’s future.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH’s 2022 manifesto explicitly promised environmentally friendly governance benchmarked against ‘International Best Practices.’ Instead, the government has approved massive data centres without environmental impact assessments, continued approving logging quotas that exceed sustainability levels, maintained coal-fired power generation, and offered no timeline for renewable energy transition. Anwar lectures the world about moral failing on Gaza while failing the planet on climate. The manifesto promise of strict logging quota enforcement is particularly laughable given continued deforestation.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s climate pledges have been consistently unmatched by action across all administrations. BN treated climate change as an inconvenience to economic growth. PH 1.0 made promises but collapsed before implementation. PH 2.0 is maintaining the tradition of ambitious targets and non-existent action. The Penang South Islands reclamation — a massive environmental destruction project — is being approved under a PH state government.

🎯 PH Callout

Your manifesto promised environmental governance benchmarked against international best practices. Your reality: continued deforestation, rivers turning blue from pollution, floods devastating communities annually, and climate pledges that are pure fiction. You can’t lecture the world about moral hypocrisy on Palestine while being morally bankrupt on the planet. The environment can’t vote — which is why you don’t care about it.

Palm Oil Deforestation Continues Unchecked — Environmental Pledges vs Plantation Profits

Said by: Environmental NGOs / SAM

Category: Other

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Despite Malaysia’s commitments to international climate agreements and sustainable development goals, deforestation for palm oil plantations continues to degrade tropical rainforests among the oldest and most biodiverse on Earth. The Malaysian Sustainable Palm Oil (MSPO) certification scheme has been criticised by international NGOs as inadequate greenwashing. Malaysia’s river water quality is declining — clean rivers dropped from 486 to 468 in 2025. The Perak River turned blue from rare earth mining discharge in November 2025. Endangered species including orangutans, pygmy elephants, and Malayan tigers face shrinking habitats.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia accounts for approximately 25% of global palm oil production, and the economic dependence on this commodity creates enormous political resistance to genuine environmental reform. While the rate of primary forest loss has slowed from its peak, secondary forest clearing and peatland conversion continue. The environmental cost — biodiversity loss, river pollution, carbon emissions from peatland drainage — is not counted in GDP figures but is borne by communities who depend on clean water, healthy forests, and functioning ecosystems.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The MSPO certification scheme is a sticker, not a standard. It allows continued environmental degradation while providing a veneer of sustainability that satisfies international buyers. The government talks about sustainability at COP summits and international conferences while approving new plantation developments at home. When your rivers turn blue from industrial discharge and your forests disappear for palm oil, no amount of international pledges will bring them back. Sustainability isn’t a speech — it’s a policy. Malaysia doesn’t have one.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, Malaysia’s deforestation rate was among the highest in the world. Mahathir’s era saw massive conversion of primary rainforest to plantations. Under PH 1.0, a moratorium on new oil palm licences was briefly discussed. Under PH 2.0, the MSPO was expanded but not strengthened. Each government pays lip service to environmental protection while protecting the plantation industry that funds political parties across the spectrum. The pattern is bipartisan: plantations are more politically valuable than forests.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You sign climate agreements and pose for photos at COP summits. Then you come home and approve more plantations. Your MSPO scheme is window dressing. Your rivers are getting dirtier. Your forests are getting smaller. Strengthen MSPO to meet international standards. Enforce illegal logging laws. Invest in downstream palm oil processing rather than acreage expansion. Or keep giving sustainability speeches while your country’s environmental foundation crumbles — and hope the world doesn’t notice the hypocrisy.

Penang South Islands: $14B Reclamation Project Destroys Fishing Grounds for 6,000 Fishers

Said by: Penang State Government / SAM

Category: Other

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The Penang South Islands (PSI) project — originally three artificial islands costing approximately $14 billion — threatens the livelihoods of 6,000 fishers, 51,184 tonnes of annual marine catch worth RM525 million, and 511 aquaculture farms generating RM1.67 billion per year. After sustained protests from fishing communities and environmental groups including Sahabat Alam Malaysia (SAM), the project was downsized to one island. However, construction proceeds, and the fundamental impact on fishing grounds and marine ecosystems remains.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Penang’s fishing communities have worked these waters for generations. The reclamation destroys their fishing grounds — permanently. No amount of compensation replaces a livelihood that sustains 6,000 families. The marine catch and aquaculture figures represent hundreds of millions in annual economic output that will be lost. The project benefits property developers; the cost is borne by fishing communities who have no political leverage against DAP’s state government.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: DAP-led Penang has governed for over 16 years. The PSI project epitomises the contradiction at the heart of DAP’s governance: progressive rhetoric, developer-friendly reality. Reclaiming thousands of acres of sea to build luxury property while destroying the livelihoods of 6,000 fishers is not sustainable development — it is displacement development. The “downsizing” to one island was a PR concession, not a policy change. The fundamental trade-off — fishing communities sacrificed for developer profits — remains unchanged.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Land reclamation for development has been contentious in Penang for decades. The Gurney Drive reclamation, Tanjung Tokong development, and SRS Consortium controversies all followed the same pattern: developers propose, fishers protest, the state proceeds. DAP — which championed environmental protection in opposition — has greenlit more reclamation in Penang than BN ever did. The PSI project is the largest and most destructive yet.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You’re destroying the livelihoods of 6,000 fishers for a developer’s dream. RM525 million in annual marine catch. RM1.67 billion in aquaculture. Gone — permanently — so developers can sell luxury apartments on artificial islands. This is not development. It is the displacement of working communities for profit. If you can’t develop Penang without destroying the fishing industry, you can’t develop Penang.

[BM] Banjir 2024 Pindahkan Ratusan Ribu — Negeri-Negeri Utara Musnah Sekali Lagi

Said by: ReliefWeb / East Asia Forum

Category: Other

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada Disember 2024, banjir teruk di negeri-negeri utara memindahkan ratusan ribu rakyat Malaysia. Kelantan, Terengganu dan Pahang menanggung beban musim monsun. RM15 bilion telah diluluskan untuk mitigasi banjir selepas bencana dahsyat 2021, tetapi kemajuan sangat perlahan. Negeri-negeri yang sama yang dilanda banjir setiap tahun dilanda banjir semula, dengan komuniti yang sama menanggung kerugian yang sama. East Asia Forum mendokumentasikan bagaimana banjir 2024 mendedahkan kegagalan tadbir urus berterusan dalam kesiapsiagaan bencana dan pelaburan infrastruktur.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Negeri-negeri yang sama dilanda banjir berulang kali — Kelantan, Terengganu, Pahang, dan sebahagian Johor dan Selangor. Masalahnya bukan hujan. Masalahnya ialah kegagalan tadbir urus. Infrastruktur mitigasi banjir telah dijanjikan, dibiayai dan diumumkan di bawah setiap kerajaan. Pelaksanaan secara konsisten tidak mencukupi. Komuniti yang diberitahu “tidak lagi” selepas 2021 mendapati diri mereka di pusat pemindahan sekali lagi pada 2024. Apabila bencana yang sama menimpa orang yang sama di tempat yang sama tahun demi tahun, ia bukan bencana alam — ia pilihan dasar.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: RM15 bilion telah diluluskan untuk mitigasi banjir. Ke mana perginya? Projek mana yang siap? Mana yang ketinggalan jadual? Mana yang tidak pernah dimulakan? Kerajaan tidak menerbitkan laporan pelaksanaan yang komprehensif. Komuniti yang sepatutnya mempunyai saliran baharu, kolam tahanan dan projek pendalaman sungai masih menyusun beg pasir di rumah mereka setiap November. Wang itu ada. Projek telah diumumkan. Banjir datang juga. Jurang antara peruntukan dan pelaksanaan itu adalah ruang di mana kegagalan tadbir urus hidup.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Banjir 2021 membunuh 54 orang dan memindahkan lebih 400,000. Kerajaan meluluskan RM15 bilion untuk mitigasi. Banjir 2022 memindahkan puluhan ribu. Banjir 2023 memindahkan lebih ramai. Banjir 2024 memindahkan ratusan ribu. Setiap tahun, kerajaan mengumumkan bantuan, menjanjikan mitigasi, dan bergerak — sehingga monsun seterusnya. Kitaran ini berterusan di bawah BN, PN dan PH. Tiada kerajaan yang memecahkannya kerana memecahkannya memerlukan pelaburan infrastruktur yang berterusan dan mahal di kawasan yang tidak menjana pulangan politik yang sepadan dengan kos.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: RM15 bilion diluluskan. Ratusan ribu dipindahkan semula. Negeri-negeri yang sama. Komuniti yang sama. Corak yang sama. Terbitkan audit projek demi projek belanjawan mitigasi banjir. Tunjukkan projek mana yang siap, mana yang tertangguh, dan mana yang tidak pernah dimulakan. Pertanggungjawabkan kontraktor atas kelewatan. Posisikan sumber bantuan sebelum musim monsun. Atau terus melayan banjir tahunan sebagai kejutan tahunan — sementara rakyat terus kehilangan segala yang mereka miliki, setiap tahun, di tempat yang sama.

2024 Floods Displaced Hundreds of Thousands — Northern States Devastated Again

Said by: ReliefWeb / East Asia Forum

Category: Other

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In December 2024, severe flooding in northern states displaced hundreds of thousands of Malaysians. Kelantan, Terengganu, and Pahang bore the brunt of the monsoon season. RM15 billion had been approved for flood mitigation after the devastating 2021 disaster, but progress was glacial. The same states that flood every year flooded again, with the same communities bearing the same losses. East Asia Forum documented how the 2024 floods exposed continued governance failures in disaster preparedness and infrastructure investment.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The same states flood repeatedly — Kelantan, Terengganu, Pahang, and parts of Johor and Selangor. The problem is not the rain. The problem is governance failure. Flood mitigation infrastructure has been promised, funded, and announced under every government. Implementation consistently falls short. Communities that were told “never again” after 2021 found themselves in evacuation centres again in 2024. When the same disaster hits the same people in the same places year after year, it is not a natural disaster — it is a policy choice.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: RM15 billion was approved for flood mitigation. Where did it go? Which projects were completed? Which are behind schedule? Which were never started? The government has not published a comprehensive implementation report. Communities that should have new drainage, retention ponds, and river deepening projects are still sandbagging their homes every November. The money exists. The projects were announced. The floods came anyway. That gap between allocation and implementation is the space where governance failure lives.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The 2021 floods killed 54 people and displaced over 400,000. The government approved RM15 billion for mitigation. The 2022 floods displaced tens of thousands. The 2023 floods displaced more. The 2024 floods displaced hundreds of thousands. Each year, the government announces relief, promises mitigation, and moves on — until the next monsoon. The cycle has continued under BN, PN, and PH. No government has broken it because breaking it requires sustained, expensive infrastructure investment in areas that don’t generate political returns proportional to the cost.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: RM15 billion approved. Hundreds of thousands displaced again. The same states. The same communities. The same pattern. Publish a project-by-project audit of the flood mitigation budget. Show which projects were completed, which are delayed, and which were never started. Hold contractors accountable for delays. Pre-position relief resources before monsoon season. Or keep treating annual floods as annual surprises — while the rakyat keeps losing everything they own, every year, in the same places.

[BM] Banjir Terburuk Dalam Sedekad — 90,000 Dipindahkan, Cuti Menteri Dibekukan Terlambat

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2024-11-29

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada akhir November 2024, Malaysia mengalami banjir terburuk dalam sedekad. Lebih 90,000 orang dipindahkan merentasi 9 negeri, dengan Kelantan, Terengganu dan Pahang paling teruk terjejas. Anwar membekukan cuti menteri — tetapi hanya selepas krisis sudah meningkat ke tahap kecemasan. Banjir monsun tahunan, yang berlaku dengan ketepatan jam setiap tahun, sekali lagi mendedahkan infrastruktur yang tidak mencukupi dan kesiapsiagaan bencana yang lemah meskipun berbilion diperuntukkan untuk mitigasi banjir selepas banjir dahsyat 2021.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Banjir monsun bukan kejutan. Ia berlaku setiap tahun, di negeri yang sama, pada bulan yang sama. Kerajaan yang tidak bersedia menghadapi banjir tahunan yang boleh dijangka bukan malang — ia tidak cekap. Angka 90,000 yang dipindahkan mewakili keluarga sebenar yang kehilangan rumah, harta benda dan mata pencarian. Membekukan cuti menteri selepas krisis meningkat adalah pengurusan reaktif, bukan kesiapsiagaan bencana. Kerajaan yang cekap akan menyiapkan sumber pra-posisi, pelan pemindahan dan penyelarasan menteri sebelum monsun tiba.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Selepas banjir dahsyat 2021 di bawah Ismail Sabri, kerajaan meluluskan RM15 bilion untuk mitigasi banjir. Di bawah PH, pelaksanaan sangat perlahan. Projek mitigasi ketinggalan jadual. Infrastruktur saliran di kawasan terdedah banjir masih tidak mencukupi. Komuniti yang sama yang dilanda banjir pada 2021, 2022 dan 2023 dilanda banjir semula pada 2024. Wang telah diluluskan. Projek telah diumumkan. Banjir datang juga. Apabila perbelanjaan mitigasi RM15 bilion tidak menghalang 90,000 pemindahan, sama ada wang itu disalah belanjakan atau projek tidak dibina. Kedua-duanya adalah kegagalan tadbir urus.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia dilanda banjir setiap tahun. Di bawah Najib, banjir 2014 memindahkan lebih 200,000 — dan Najib ditangkap bermain golf di Hawaii. Di bawah Ismail Sabri, banjir 2021 membunuh 54 orang dan memindahkan lebih 400,000. Di bawah PH, banjir 2024 memindahkan 90,000 — lebih baik daripada 2021 tetapi masih dahsyat bagi komuniti yang terjejas. Coraknya sama merentasi pentadbiran: persediaan yang tidak mencukupi, respons yang lambat, janji perbelanjaan mitigasi, pelaksanaan yang perlahan, dan komuniti yang sama dilanda banjir semula tahun depan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Banjir monsun boleh dijangka. Anda mempunyai setahun untuk bersiap sejak banjir tahun lepas. Anda mempunyai RM15 bilion yang diperuntukkan untuk mitigasi. Dan 90,000 orang masih dipindahkan. Membekukan cuti menteri selepas krisis melanda bukan pengurusan bencana — ia kawalan kerosakan. Terbitkan audit peruntukan mitigasi banjir RM15 bilion: berapa banyak yang dibelanjakan, untuk apa, dan mengapa ia tidak menghalang pemindahan tahun ini. Posisikan sumber sebelum monsun tahun depan. Atau terus bertindak balas terhadap bencana yang boleh dijangka dan tertanya-tanya mengapa rakyat tidak mempercayai anda untuk melindungi mereka.

Worst Floods in a Decade — 90,000 Displaced, Ministers' Leave Frozen Too Late

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2024-11-29

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late November 2024, Malaysia experienced its worst floods in a decade. Over 90,000 people were displaced across 9 states, with Kelantan, Terengganu, and Pahang worst affected. Anwar froze ministers’ leave — but only after the crisis had already escalated to emergency levels. The annual monsoon flooding, which occurs with clockwork regularity every year, once again exposed inadequate infrastructure and disaster preparedness despite billions allocated for flood mitigation after the catastrophic 2021 floods.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Monsoon floods are not surprises. They happen every year, in the same states, during the same months. A government that is caught unprepared by predictable annual flooding is not unlucky — it is incompetent. The 90,000 displaced figure represents real families losing homes, possessions, and livelihoods. Freezing ministers’ leave after the crisis escalates is reactive management, not disaster preparedness. A competent government would have had pre-positioned resources, evacuation plans, and ministerial coordination in place before the monsoon arrived.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: After the catastrophic 2021 floods under Ismail Sabri, the government approved RM15 billion for flood mitigation. Under PH, implementation has been glacial. Mitigation projects are behind schedule. Drainage infrastructure in flood-prone areas remains inadequate. The same communities that flooded in 2021, 2022, and 2023 flooded again in 2024. The money was approved. The projects were announced. The floods came anyway. When RM15 billion in mitigation spending doesn’t prevent 90,000 displacements, either the money was misspent or the projects weren’t built. Both are governance failures.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia floods every year. Under Najib, the 2014 floods displaced over 200,000 — and Najib was caught playing golf in Hawaii. Under Ismail Sabri, the 2021 floods killed 54 people and displaced over 400,000. Under PH, the 2024 floods displaced 90,000 — better than 2021 but still catastrophic for the affected communities. The pattern is identical across administrations: inadequate preparation, slow response, promises of mitigation spending, glacial implementation, and the same communities flooding again the next year.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Monsoon floods are predictable. You had a year to prepare since last year’s floods. You had RM15 billion allocated for mitigation. And 90,000 people were still displaced. Freezing ministers’ leave after the crisis hits is not disaster management — it’s damage control. Publish an audit of the RM15 billion flood mitigation allocation: how much was spent, on what, and why it didn’t prevent this year’s displacements. Pre-position resources before next year’s monsoon. Or keep reacting to predictable disasters and wondering why the rakyat doesn’t trust you to protect them.
