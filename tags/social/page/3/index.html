Social Media Platforms Forcibly 'Deemed Licensed' — MCMC Tightens Grip on Internet

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: From 1 January 2026, social media and instant messaging platforms with 8 million or more Malaysian users were automatically “deemed licensed” under MCMC’s regulatory framework — whether they applied for a licence or not. This means platforms like Meta (Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp), TikTok, Telegram, and X are now subject to Malaysian government licensing requirements, content takedown obligations, and penalties for non-compliance. Meta publicly warned the scheme “lacks clarity and threatens innovation.” Soyacincau reported that MCMC’s approach effectively bypassed the normal licensing application process by unilaterally deeming platforms as licensed. Reuters reported Meta’s concerns about the regulatory framework’s vagueness.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Forced licensing gives the Malaysian government regulatory leverage over every major platform used by Malaysians. Licensed entities must comply with government directives — including content removal requests, user data obligations, and operational requirements set by MCMC. This creates a legal framework where the government can threaten to revoke or suspend licences if platforms don’t comply with takedown orders. The scheme bypassed normal regulatory processes: platforms were not asked whether they wanted licences — they were told they had them, along with all the obligations that come with them.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH in opposition criticised the BN government for using MCMC to control online discourse. In government, PH has built the most comprehensive internet regulation framework in Malaysian history: the Online Safety Act with RM10M penalties, the reinstated CMA “grossly offensive” provision, and now forced licensing of every major platform. The “child safety” framing is the Trojan horse — the infrastructure being built gives the government tools to regulate ANY content, not just harmful content. Who decides what content violates licensing conditions? MCMC, which answers to Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil, who answers to Anwar. The same minister who says “free speech doesn’t mean freedom to offend” now has licensing power over every platform Malaysians use.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, MCMC was used to block websites critical of the government — most notably during the 1MDB scandal when The Sarawak Report and other outlets were blocked. Under PH, the tools are more sophisticated but the intent is the same: control the information environment. The deemed licensing approach follows Singapore’s model, where platform licensing has been used to compel content removal on politically sensitive topics. Malaysia is building the same infrastructure while claiming it’s about protecting children.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You forced licensing on every major platform without asking them, without clear rules, and without independent oversight. Meta — one of the largest tech companies on earth — publicly warned your approach “lacks clarity and threatens innovation.” When the platforms you’re regulating tell you your regulation is unclear, that’s not a feature — it’s a design choice. Vague rules give you maximum discretion. Maximum discretion means maximum censorship potential. If this is really about child safety, publish clear, specific, enforceable rules with independent oversight. If you can’t do that, admit this is about control.

TikTok Generation Lost — Malaysian Youth Screen Time Among Highest in the World While Government Plans to Ban Under-16s

Said by: MCMC / Online Safety Act

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysian youth have among the highest screen times and social media usage rates in the world. The government’s response was the Online Safety Act, which bans social media use for under-16s from 2026. The law places enforcement responsibility on platforms, with penalties up to RM10 million for non-compliance. However, implementation details remain vague — no age verification infrastructure exists, no technical framework has been announced, and enforcement mechanisms are untested. Freedom House’s 2024 report flagged Malaysia’s declining internet freedom trajectory under PH.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Excessive social media use among youth is linked to declining mental health, cyberbullying, exposure to inappropriate content, and reduced academic performance. Malaysian PISA scores correlate with screen time data — students spending more hours on devices perform worse academically. The K-pods drug crisis is also facilitated through social media distribution networks. But the issue isn’t just screen time — it’s that the government has failed to provide alternatives: a functional education system, employment prospects, mental health support, or safe public spaces for young people.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The under-16 social media ban is performative legislation — it looks tough but is virtually unenforceable. How do you verify age for social media accounts? VPNs are trivially easy to use. Parental controls are easily circumvented by tech-savvy children. Without robust age verification infrastructure — which doesn’t exist and would raise massive privacy concerns — the law is decoration. Australia passed similar legislation and immediately faced implementation challenges. The EU focused on platform accountability rather than age bans. Malaysia chose the most attention-grabbing but least effective approach. More importantly, the same government building age-verification infrastructure is also forcibly licensing social media platforms and expanding MCMC’s censorship powers — tools that can easily be repurposed for broader internet control.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Previous MCMC “enforcement” of online content has been selective — targeting political critics while struggling to address scams, fraud, and actual harmful content. The government’s track record on tech regulation is one of overreach on speech and underperformance on safety. MCMC can find and arrest social media users who mock politicians within hours, but can’t shut down scam call centres operating from Malaysian soil for years. The priorities tell you everything about what this legislation is really for.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You’re banning TikTok for kids while your education system can’t teach them math. You’re “protecting” them online while bullying cases surge 17% in schools. You’re regulating social media while 10,160 students didn’t even show up for SPM. Maybe address the real-world failures that drive youth to screens in the first place: give them a functional education system, employment prospects, and mental health support. A TikTok ban without systemic reform is a headline, not a policy.

Race-Based Economic Policies NEP Still Untouchable — 'Unanimous' Critics Ignored

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Think Tanks

Category: Social

Date: 2025-12-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Eurasia Review published a comprehensive analysis in December 2025 finding “almost unanimous opinion” among analysts that Malaysia’s race-based economic policies — rooted in the New Economic Policy (NEP) of 1971 — have failed their “overarching goal of national unity.” Yet the analysis concluded the government is “highly unlikely to abandon” them. The NEP and its successors (NDP, NVP, and now Anwar’s various economic frameworks) maintain Bumiputera preferences in government contracts, education, housing, and corporate equity. Despite 50+ years of these policies, Malaysia remains deeply divided along ethnic lines, brain drain continues at alarming rates, and economic inequality persists within the Bumiputera community itself.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The NEP and its successors have been the third rail of Malaysian politics for over 50 years. Every PM — including the “reformist” one — refuses to touch them. When virtually every independent analyst agrees a policy has failed its stated objective, and the government still won’t reform it, the policy exists to serve political interests, not national ones. Anwar’s multiracial “Malaysia Madani” brand is fundamentally incompatible with race-based economic preferences — but admitting that would cost Malay votes. So the contradiction persists and the country pays the price in brain drain, investment distortions, and communal resentment.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The NEP was designed as a 20-year policy (1971-1990). It’s now in its 55th year. The Bumiputera corporate equity target of 30% has been a moving goalpost for decades — with the government periodically changing the methodology to show either progress or continued need, depending on what serves the political moment. Meanwhile, the primary beneficiaries of Bumiputera preferences have been politically connected elites, not ordinary Malays. The wealth gap within the Bumiputera community is enormous. Race-based policies designed to help poor Malays have been captured by rich, connected Malays — and PH, like every government before it, refuses to acknowledge this because the fiction of universal Bumiputera benefit is essential to electoral politics.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Mahathir introduced the Vision 2020 framework that was supposed to move Malaysia beyond race-based economics. It didn’t. Abdullah Badawi promised the NDP would be more “merit-based.” It wasn’t. Najib’s NTP made similar promises. Anwar’s frameworks use different acronyms for the same structure. The NEP is unkillable not because it works, but because dismantling it would require telling millions of Malay voters that the preference system their political identity is built on needs to change. No politician has the courage to do this — least of all one who needs Malay votes to survive.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Analysts — your own think tanks included — say unanimously that race-based economic policies have failed their stated goal of national unity. Brain drain accelerates as non-Bumiputera professionals leave for countries where they’re judged on merit. Investment distortions persist. Communal resentment deepens. And your response is to maintain the policies unchanged because dismantling them would cost votes. “Malaysia Madani” cannot coexist with race-based economic preferences. Choose one. Or admit that “Madani” was always just a slogan — not a policy direction.

The development approvals that created traffic chaos were signed by DAP-appointed officials.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Penang’s development trajectory under DAP has been consistently criticised: the PSI reclamation destroying fishermen’s livelihoods, the undersea tunnel going nowhere, hillside development causing floods, and George Town’s heritage zone under pressure from commercial interests. After 16 years, you own every planning failure in Penang. Commission an independent planning audit. Publish development approval data. Implement a moratorium on hillside development. Or keep blaming others while the traffic gets worse and the green spaces disappear.

Amirudin Rolls Out Flood Recovery Plan — After Selangor's Worst Floods Under His Watch

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Social

Date: 2025-11-29

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 29 November 2025, Amirudin rolled out a ‘major mobilisation’ for post-flood recovery as evacuee numbers surged across Selangor. Water from northern Selangor flooded into Kuala Langat and Sepang. JPS installed four pumps in Kuala Selangor with six more planned. A police officer went missing after a car skidded into a storm drain near KLIA during the floods. The Met Department withdrew severe rain warnings, but damage was extensive.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Selangor experiences catastrophic flooding almost every year. The December 2021 floods killed dozens and displaced hundreds of thousands. Amirudin promised improved mitigation — RM billions in flood infrastructure, better early warning systems, and drainage upgrades. Four years later, evacuees are still being mobilised, pumps are still being installed reactively, and people are still losing their lives. The flood response is reactive, not preventive.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Post-flood recovery is not flood management — it’s disaster response to a management failure. Installing pumps after the water arrives is not mitigation. The question Amirudin never answers: where are the flood retention ponds, upgraded drainage systems, and development controls that would prevent flooding in the first place? Selangor continues to approve hillside and lowland development that worsens flood risk. The state profits from development approvals, then spends public money on cleanup.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The December 2021 Shah Alam floods, the 2022 Taman Sri Muda disaster, the 2023 Klang floods — Selangor floods are now annual events. Each time, Amirudin announces mobilisation, recovery plans, and future improvements. Each year, the same areas flood again. The definition of insanity applies.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Stop announcing reactive flood responses as if they’re achievements. A pump installed after the flood is a failure, not a success. Publish Selangor’s flood mitigation capital expenditure vs. development approval revenue for the last 7 years. Show the rakyat whether the state is spending more on preventing floods or profiting from the development that causes them.

Social Media Ban for Under-16s — Enforcement Plan: TBD

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2025-11-24

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil announced in November 2025 that Malaysia would ban social media access for children under 16, following Australia’s lead. The ban was set for implementation in 2026. However, the NYT noted the announcement was “light on details” — no age verification infrastructure exists, no enforcement mechanism was outlined, and no timeline for implementation was provided. The government framed it as protecting children from online harms, but offered no technical plan for making it work.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A social media ban for under-16s sounds decisive. But without enforcement infrastructure, it’s a headline, not a policy. Age verification online is technically difficult — even Australia, which passed its ban first, has struggled with implementation. Malaysia has neither the digital infrastructure, the regulatory capacity, nor the tech industry cooperation to enforce such a ban. Meanwhile, the announcement distracts from the government’s own record of using social media regulations to suppress dissent — including the arrest of FMT journalist Rex Tan and the expansion of MCMC’s content moderation powers.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government that can’t enforce basic traffic laws, stop illegal waste dumping, or verify the integrity of its own procurement processes is going to verify the age of every social media user in Malaysia? This is performative governance at its worst. Fahmi announced a ban with no enforcement plan — because the plan doesn’t exist. It cannot exist without massive investment in digital ID infrastructure, cooperation from global tech platforms, and privacy safeguards that Malaysia has never implemented. The announcement was designed to generate a positive news cycle, not to protect children.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a long history of announcing tech regulations without enforcement mechanisms. The Anti-Fake News Act was passed and repealed. MCMC’s licensing regime for social media platforms was announced with fanfare but implementation details remain vague. The Online Safety Bill has been in discussion for years. Each announcement generates headlines; none generates meaningful change. Meanwhile, Malaysia’s youth screen time is among the highest in the world, and the government’s response is a ban it cannot enforce rather than investment in digital literacy it could actually deliver.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You announced a ban with no enforcement plan. That’s not policy — it’s a press release. If you’re serious about protecting children online, invest in digital literacy programmes in schools. Fund parental education on screen time management. Build the age verification infrastructure before announcing the ban, not after. Or admit that this was a headline-grabbing announcement designed to match Australia’s news cycle, with no intention of following through.

Anthony Loke Admits Malaysian Society Is 'Polarised' — But Blames Opposition for Playing Up Race

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2025-09-11

Polarised, But Don’t Blame Us

In September 2025, Transport Minister Anthony Loke told Malaysians in Hong Kong that society had become “more polarised” with intensifying race rhetoric. His diagnosis was accurate. His prescription — blame the opposition — was intellectually dishonest.

The Facts

Fact: At an event in Hong Kong, Loke acknowledged that Malaysia was increasingly divided along racial, class, and education lines, with social media amplifying hate speech and misinformation.

Fact: He attributed the polarisation primarily to “the opposition playing up such issues for political capital” — as though the government bore no responsibility for the fractures.

Fact: This came from a government that reshuffled its cabinet along racial lines in December 2025, giving UMNO more positions to shore up Malay support while reducing DAP’s presence.

Fact: JAKIM’s proposed interfaith guidelines in 2024 — which restricted how non-Muslims could engage in religious dialogue — divided communities and were widely criticised by civil society groups as discriminatory.

Fact: The government has made no move to reform race-based affirmative action policies (NEP-era quotas in education, procurement, and housing), despite PH’s manifesto promising a “needs-based” approach.

Fact: Anwar’s government has relied heavily on racial and religious rhetoric when convenient — including Anwar’s own Malay-first speeches at UMNO general assemblies, his defence of Malay-Muslim “special rights,” and his coalition’s use of the Sedition Act against those who question racial policies.

Fact: The SCMP report noted that despite Loke’s optimism about “overcoming challenges,” Malaysia’s racial polarisation metrics — measured by survey data on inter-ethnic trust — have worsened since 2023.

Why It Matters

  • Admitting the country is polarised while blaming only the opposition is the political equivalent of diagnosing the disease and prescribing someone else’s medicine. The government controls policy, appointments, media regulation, and the machinery of state. If polarisation is worsening on your watch, you own it.
  • The government’s own actions have actively contributed to division: a cabinet reshuffle that prioritised racial coalition management over competence, interfaith guidelines that alienated minorities, continued race-based policies that PH promised to reform, and a PM who code-switches between reform rhetoric for international audiences and ethno-religious rhetoric for domestic ones.
  • Loke made these remarks in Hong Kong — not in Malaysia. Malaysian politicians are remarkably candid about domestic failures when speaking to foreign audiences. At home, the same problems are denied or deflected. The gap between what they say abroad and what they do domestically is the real measure of governance.
  • The opposition absolutely plays the race card. PN’s ethno-religious populism is real and dangerous. But blaming PN for polarisation while your own government maintains race-based policies, panders to religious conservatives, and uses sedition laws against those who challenge racial orthodoxy is not analysis — it is deflection.
  • If even a DAP minister — from the party that was supposed to represent multiracial reform — cannot honestly diagnose why Malaysia is polarised, then PH has fully absorbed the BN playbook: acknowledge the symptom, blame the enemy, change nothing. *Source: *SCMP

Penang South Islands Reclamation — Destroying 4,000 Fishermen's Livelihoods for a Developer's Dream

Said by: Chow Kon Yeow, Penang Chief Minister / DAP

Category: Social

Date: 2025-08-20

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The Penang South Islands (PSI) reclamation project — now rebranded as ‘Silicon Island’ — is the DAP-led state government’s mega-development plan to create 930 hectares of artificial land off Penang’s southern coast. As of August 2025, 80 hectares had been reclaimed. The project was originally three islands, later downsized to one after public outcry. More than 4,000 coastal fishermen are affected. Only RM11.7 million has been paid to 791 fishermen under the Social Impact Management Plan, and 118 of 295 active fishermen received new boats and engines worth RM10 million. Fishermen lost their court challenge to the Environmental Impact Assessment approval.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This is the largest land reclamation project in Penang’s history, and it’s being pushed through by DAP — the party that positions itself as pro-people and pro-environment. Academic research confirms the project causes ‘permanent and irreversible loss to the ecosystem.’ Fishermen who have worked these waters for generations are being displaced with minimal compensation. The project is designed to extend Bayan Lepas Free Industrial Zone for semiconductor manufacturing — a corporate objective funded by destroying a community’s livelihood.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: DAP in opposition would have led protests against this. A BN state government reclaiming 930 hectares of sea, displacing thousands of fishermen, and losing court cases over environmental damage would have been DAP’s dream campaign issue. But when DAP does it? It’s ‘development for the people.’ The fishermen who lost their livelihoods are not ’the people’ in DAP’s calculation — they’re collateral damage for FDI numbers.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Penang has a long history of reclamation — Gurney Drive, Tanjung Tokong — but nothing on this scale. Civil society groups including SAM and Penang Forum have documented environmental destruction and community displacement. Fishermen and NGOs were ‘dismayed’ when their court challenge failed. The pattern is clear: DAP governs Penang like a developer-state, prioritising investment over communities.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: 4,000 fishermen displaced. RM11.7 million in compensation for 791 of them — that’s RM14,800 each for losing a livelihood. 930 hectares of seabed destroyed. And you call this development? This is extraction — taking from the sea, taking from the fishermen, and giving to semiconductor companies. If this is PH’s model of governance, the fishermen of Penang now know exactly how much their lives are worth to DAP.

📌 What Happened

The Toda Peace Institute published a comprehensive analysis titled ‘The Many Wars of Malaysia’s Anwar Ibrahim,’ concluding that Anwar ‘isn’t just reluctant to disturb the status quo; he is in fact making it worse by trying to out-Islamize the Islamists with gestures, policies and statements that the minorities find deeply worrying.’

🔥 Why It Matters

This is an international peace research institute saying that Malaysia’s supposedly reformist PM is WORSENING racial discrimination, not reducing it. The institutionalised system of race-based policies — described as ‘killing off merit, stifling competition and innovation, breeding minority resentments, and spawning cronyism by creating a rich Malay elite without doing much for poor Malays’ — is being strengthened rather than reformed under Anwar.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar’s Reformasi movement explicitly ‘sought an end to this system of institutionalized racial discrimination.’ That was the CORE PROMISE. Not a peripheral commitment — the fundamental reason minorities supported him. His ascent ‘understandably triggered hopes of racial justice.’ Instead, he’s competing with PAS on who can be more Malay-Muslim, while minorities who voted PH in droves get empty rhetoric and photo ops with the occasional Chinese school allocation announcement.

📜 Previous Cases

In April 2025, when a UiTM professor suggested opening cardiothoracic surgery training to non-Bumiputera students (because people were dying from surgeon shortages), the government was paralysed. The mandatory Malay language requirement for foreign workers was imposed. Bumiputera policies were declared ’non-negotiable’ in US trade talks. ICERD remains unratified. The civil service remains 90% Bumiputera.

🎯 PH Callout

You asked non-Malay Malaysians to trust you. They did — they voted for PH in overwhelming numbers. In return, you’ve not only maintained every discriminatory policy but, according to independent analysis, made things WORSE. You’ve traded minority rights for Malay-Muslim votes. Every minority Malaysian who voted PH deserves an apology — and an explanation for why their trust was weaponised against their own interests.
