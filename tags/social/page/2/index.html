[BM] Selangor Haramkan Semua Penternakan Babi — Amirudin Tunduk Kepada Titah Sultan, Penternak Cina Tiada Apa-Apa

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-12

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU

Pada 12 Februari 2026, Menteri Besar Selangor Amirudin Shari mengumumkan kerajaan negeri telah berhenti mengeluarkan lesen penternakan babi dan akan mempercepatkan penutupan semua ladang babi sedia ada. Keputusan itu berikutan titah Sultan Sharafuddin Idris Shah, yang memerintahkan tiada ladang babi patut beroperasi di mana-mana daerah di Selangor.

Perancangan awal untuk memindahkan ladang babi ke Bukit Tagar — kawasan berpenduduk jarang di utara Selangor — ditangguhkan selepas penduduk Melayu membantah kerana berdekatan Sungai Selangor, sumber rawatan air utama. Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim campur tangan pada Januari untuk menghentikan pemindahan.

Selangor kini akan mengimport sepenuhnya semua daging babi untuk memenuhi permintaan bukan Muslim. Penternak babi Cina, ramai yang mengoperasikan ladang berbilang generasi di Tanjong Sepat, berdepan kemusnahan sumber pendapatan.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING

Selangor ialah negeri paling kaya dan maju Malaysia — dan ia ditadbir PH. Larangan penternakan babi menjejaskan sumber pendapatan dan keperluan pemakanan komuniti etnik tertentu. Walaupun kebimbangan alam sekitar tentang pencemaran air adalah sah, larangan total tanpa alternatif yang berdaya maju adalah penyerahan, bukan penyelesaian.

Mesej kepada pengundi Cina jelas: apabila istana memerintahkan dan sentimen Melayu menolak, PH tidak akan berjuang untuk anda. Pemindahan ke Bukit Tagar — yang boleh menjadi penyelesaian moden dan terkawal — ditinggalkan sebaik ia menjadi tidak selesa secara politik.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT

PH mentadbir Selangor. PH mempunyai bertahun-tahun untuk membangunkan penyelesaian penternakan babi yang terkawal, moden, dan lestari alam sekitar. Sebaliknya, mereka mencadangkan pemindahan, mendapat bantahan, meninggalkannya, dan kemudian tunduk kepada titah diraja dengan larangan total.

Negeri yang menjenamakan dirinya sebagai progresif dan inklusif baru sahaja menghapuskan seluruh industri yang terutamanya melayani komuniti Cinanya. Pengimport — yang akan meraih keuntungan besar — sudah menyokong larangan. Seorang pengimport menganggarkan import sudah meliputi 80% permintaan. Jadi siapa yang untung? Bukan penternak. Bukan pengguna yang lebih suka daging babi tempatan segar. Pengimport.

📜 KES TERDAHULU

Ini mengikut corak kerajaan negeri PH gagal melindungi komuniti minoriti apabila kos politik terlalu tinggi. Di Pulau Pinang, DAP mentadbir selama 16 tahun tetapi masih berdepan kritikan atas pembangunan yang menyingkirkan komuniti. Di Selangor, Amirudin berulang kali tertangkap tidak bersedia — daripada kegagalan pengurusan banjir hingga krisis kebocoran air. Setiap kali, tindak balas adalah reaktif, tidak pernah proaktif.

Mana kerajaan Madani yang bercerita tentang keterangkuman? Penternak Cina di Tanjong Sepat ingin tahu.

Selangor Bans All Pig Farming — Amirudin Caves to Sultan's Decree, Chinese Farmers Left With Nothing

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-12

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 12 February 2026, Selangor Menteri Besar Amirudin Shari announced that the state government has stopped issuing pig farming licences and will accelerate the closure of all existing pig farms. The decision followed a decree from Sultan Sharafuddin Idris Shah, who declared that no pig farms should operate in any district in Selangor.

An earlier plan to relocate pig farms to Bukit Tagar — a sparsely populated area in northern Selangor — was shelved after Malay residents objected due to proximity to Sungai Selangor, a major water treatment source. Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim intervened in January to halt the relocation.

Selangor will now fully import all pork to meet non-Muslim demand. Chinese pig farmers, many of whom have operated multigenerational farms in Tanjong Sepat, face the destruction of their livelihoods.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Selangor is Malaysia’s richest and most developed state — and it’s governed by PH. The pig farming ban affects a specific ethnic community’s livelihood and dietary needs. While environmental concerns about water pollution are legitimate, the total ban with no viable alternative is a capitulation, not a solution.

The message to Chinese voters is clear: when the palace decrees and Malay sentiment pushes back, PH will not fight for you. The Bukit Tagar relocation — which could have been a modern, regulated solution — was abandoned the moment it became politically uncomfortable.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

PH governs Selangor. PH had years to develop a proper, regulated, environmentally sustainable pig farming solution. Instead, they proposed a relocation, got pushback, abandoned it, and then caved to a royal decree with a total ban.

The state that brands itself as progressive and inclusive just eliminated an entire industry that primarily serves its Chinese community. Pork importers — who stand to profit massively — are already backing the ban. One importer estimated imports already cover 80% of demand. So who benefits? Not the farmers. Not the consumers who prefer fresh local pork. The importers.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

This follows a pattern of PH state governments failing to protect minority communities when the political cost gets too high. In Penang, DAP has governed for 16 years but still faces criticism for development that displaces communities. In Selangor, Amirudin has repeatedly been caught flat-footed — from flood management failures to the water leak crisis. Every time, the response is reactive, never proactive.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You are the government of Selangor. You had the power to regulate, modernise, and relocate pig farming. Instead, you banned it entirely. You didn’t solve a pollution problem — you eliminated a community’s livelihood. Where is the DAP that promised to protect all Malaysians regardless of race? Where is the Madani government that talks about inclusivity? The Chinese farmers in Tanjong Sepat would like to know.

Fahmi Fadzil: Free Speech Doesn't Mean Freedom to Offend Online

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-07

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 7 February 2026, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil told the public that free speech does not mean freedom to offend or defame online. This came after the Federal Court reinstated Section 233 of the Communications and Multimedia Act (CMA), which criminalises content deemed “grossly offensive” — a provision PH once opposed. Fahmi said the 2024 amendments raised the threshold from “offensive” to “grossly offensive,” claiming this made it harder to misuse. The Online Safety Act 2025 (ONSA) took effect on 1 January 2026, giving MCMC powers to penalise platforms up to RM10 million for failing to remove harmful content. MCMC — the body that decides what is “grossly offensive” — reports directly to Fahmi’s ministry.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: “Grossly offensive” is a subjective and vague standard that can mean anything the government wants it to mean. The combination of the reinstated CMA provision and the new ONSA gives the Malaysian government one of the most powerful speech regulation frameworks in Southeast Asia. Platforms face RM10M fines; individuals face prosecution. The chilling effect is by design. PH in opposition repeatedly condemned the CMA as a tool of authoritarian suppression. In 2018, PH’s manifesto called for “reviewing” the CMA. In 2022, PH Youth called Section 233 “draconian.” In government, they amended it slightly, renamed “offensive” to “grossly offensive,” and called it progress.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Who decides what is “grossly offensive”? MCMC, which answers to Fahmi. Fahmi, who answers to Anwar. There is no independent judicial review before content is flagged or removed. The minister who champions “free speech with limits” is also the minister who controls the limits. The fox is guarding the henhouse and telling the chickens it is for their own protection. PH’s own track record demolishes Fahmi’s argument: when the government arrests a journalist for asking a question (Rex Tan), investigates activists for social media posts, and uses the Sedition Act 90+ times in three years, the “we only target real harm” defence collapses. Renaming “offensive” to “grossly offensive” is not reform — it is rebranding.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Since taking power, the Anwar government has used the CMA, Sedition Act, and other speech laws to arrest journalists, activists, and social media users — including FMT journalist Rex Tan, activist Heidy Quah, and multiple online commentators critical of the government. The Sedition Act — which PH promised to repeal — has been used over 90 times since 2024. The CMA was wielded against critics under Najib, under Muhyiddin, and now under Anwar. The tool changes hands; the abuse continues. Every Malaysian government has used speech laws selectively: targeting critics while ignoring allies who say equally provocative things.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You promised to reform the CMA. Instead, you strengthened it. You promised to repeal the Sedition Act. Instead, you used it more than Najib did. You promised press freedom. Instead, your MCMC blocks news outlets and your police arrest journalists at dawn. Fahmi’s statement — “free speech doesn’t mean freedom to offend” — is the same argument made by every government that has ever restricted speech, from Singapore’s POFMA to China’s internet regulations. The formula is universal: affirm the right, define it so narrowly it becomes meaningless. PH voters expected the architecture of speech suppression to be dismantled. Instead, you added new floors to the building.
