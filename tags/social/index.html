[BM] MCMC Fahmi Tingkatkan Sekatan Digital — Grindr Dan Blued Dipadamkan Dari App Store, Kerajaan Ganti 'LGBT' Dengan 'Budaya Songsang'

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister / Marhamah Rosli, Deputy Minister (Religious Affairs)

Category: Social

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Menteri Komunikasi Fahmi Fadzil mengesahkan pada 25 Februari, melalui jawapan bertulis di Parlimen, bahawa Suruhanjaya Komunikasi dan Multimedia Malaysia (SKMM) telah menyekat akses ke laman web temu janji LGBTQ Grindr dan Blued, dan sedang “mengkaji langkah-langkah undang-undang” untuk menyekat aplikasi mudah alih mereka. Menjelang awal Mac, kedua-dua platform dikeluarkan sepenuhnya dari Google Play dan Apple App Store di Malaysia — peningkatan dari sekatan laman web kepada penghapusan digital sepenuhnya. Timbalan Menteri Hal Ehwal Agama Marhamah Rosli pula menggesa rakyat Malaysia berhenti menggunakan istilah “LGBT” dan menggantikannya dengan “budaya songsang” bagi mengelakkan normalisasi, sementara kerajaan melancarkan program peringkat negeri termasuk “kem bimbingan kerohanian,” khutbah Jumaat yang diselaraskan melalui majlis agama Islam negeri, dan jawatankuasa pelbagai agensi untuk menangani isu LGBTQ dalam kalangan Muslim. Timbalan Perdana Menteri Ahmad Zahid Hamidi mengumumkan rang undang-undang Jenayah Siber yang akan memperluaskan kuasa undang-undang kerajaan untuk menyekat platform sebegini.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Ini bukan sekatan satu kali — ini adalah kempen penapisan digital dan penganiayaan institusi yang terancang dan berbilang agensi, menyasarkan kumpulan minoriti, dilaksanakan di bawah kerajaan yang bertanding atas platform reformasi. Penyekatan aplikasi temu janji hanyalah puncak gunung ais; di bawahnya tersembunyi jentera pengawasan, penguatkuasaan agama, dan kejuruteraan bahasa awam yang direka untuk menghapuskan keterlihatan LGBTQ dari masyarakat Malaysia. Apabila kerajaan PH menggunakan SKMM, polis, jabatan agama Islam negeri, dan rang undang-undang Jenayah Siber Timbalan PM untuk menyasarkan komuniti yang sama secara serentak, ini bukan lagi isu “moral” — ini adalah krisis kebebasan sivil yang didalangi oleh negara. Justice for Sisters, ARTICLE 19, dan Human Rights Watch semuanya telah membenderakan ini sebagai corak sistematik diskriminasi tajaan kerajaan.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

PH berkempen sebagai gabungan reformasi, hak asasi manusia, dan akauntabiliti institusi. Anwar Ibrahim sendiri membina imej pluralisme dan toleransi di pentas antarabangsa. Kini Menteri Komunikasinya menyekat aplikasi temu janji atas alasan “moral,” Timbalan Menteri Hal Ehwal Agamanya mengarahkan kawalan bahasa ala Orwell — menyuruh rakyat memanggil sesama warganegara “songsang” — dan Timbalan PMnya menggubal undang-undang untuk memperluaskan kuasa negara ke atas platform digital. Fahmi sempat berkata SKMM belum menerima permohonan rasmi untuk mengeluarkan aplikasi dari app store, tetapi aplikasi itu tetap lenyap dari Google Play dan Apple App Store. Kerajaan bukan sekadar gagal melindungi minoriti; ia sedang aktif membina infrastruktur penguatkuasaan untuk menganiaya mereka. Ini bukan reformasi. Ini Akta Hasutan dalam bungkusan digital baharu, dijual oleh orang yang sama yang pernah berjanji memansuhkannya.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Rekod penganiayaan LGBTQ Malaysia di bawah pengawasan PH sungguh memalukan dan semakin meningkat. Pada Mei 2023, pihak berkuasa menyerbu kedai Swatch di seluruh negara, merampas lebih 160 jam tangan berwarna pelangi. Pada Jun 2025, polis Selangor membuka siasatan terhadap bengkel kesihatan seksual LGBTQ tertutup — mengjenayahkan kerja kesihatan awam. Pada Jun 2025, polis Kelantan menyerbu sebuah harta persendirian, menahan 20 lelaki. Pada 28 November 2025, polis Kuala Lumpur melancarkan serbuan besar-besaran ke tempat-tempat lelaki sahaja, menahan 202 orang — ramai daripada mereka, menurut Justice for Sisters, dinafikan akses peguam, dikenakan layanan menjatuhkan maruah, identiti mereka didedahkan secara terbuka, dan ditahan dalam kenderaan sesak sepanjang malam. Ini bukan masalah baharu. Ini adalah corak peningkatan yang PH telah percepatkan, bukan hentikan.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Korang janji kepada rakyat Malaysia korang akan mansuhkan undang-undang zalim. Korang beritahu dunia korang percaya pada maruah manusia. Sekarang korang sekat aplikasi, kawal bahasa, serbu premis, jalankan “kem bimbingan kerohanian,” dan gubal undang-undang baharu untuk perluaskan penapisan digital. Fahmi, kau ketua kementerian yang mengawasi SKMM — badan yang sama yang padamkan video TikTok kritikan terhadap kerajaan kau minggu ini. Marhamah, kau timbalan menteri dalam gabungan reformis yang suruh rakyat Malaysia panggil sesama warganegara “songsang.” Anwar, kau pernah rujuk Deklarasi Hak Asasi Manusia Sejagat dari dalam penjara. Setiap aplikasi yang disekat, setiap serbuan polis ke majlis persendirian, setiap khutbah arahan negeri yang menyasarkan LGBTQ adalah pengkhianatan janji yang bawa korang ke Putrajaya. Ini bukan tadbir urus. Ini penganiayaan tajaan negara dengan penjenamaan reformis.

Fahmi's MCMC Escalates Digital Crackdown — Grindr and Blued Purged From App Stores as Government Replaces 'LGBT' With 'Deviant Culture'

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister / Marhamah Rosli, Deputy Minister (Religious Affairs)

Category: Social

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil confirmed on February 25, in a written parliamentary reply, that the Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission (MCMC) had blocked access to LGBTQ dating websites Grindr and Blued, and was “studying legal measures” to curb their mobile apps. By early March, both platforms were removed from Google Play and the Apple App Store in Malaysia — an escalation from website blocking to full digital erasure. Deputy Minister for Religious Affairs Marhamah Rosli separately urged Malaysians to stop using the term “LGBT” and replace it with “budaya songsang” (deviant culture) to prevent normalisation, while the government rolled out state-level programs including “spiritual guidance camps,” coordinated Friday sermons via state Islamic councils, and a multi-agency committee to address LGBTQ issues among Muslims. Deputy Prime Minister Ahmad Zahid Hamidi announced a proposed Cyber Crime Bill that would expand the government’s legal powers to restrict such platforms.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a one-off restriction — it is a coordinated, multi-agency campaign of digital censorship and institutional persecution targeting a minority group, carried out under the watch of a government that ran on a reformist platform. The blocking of dating apps is the visible tip; underneath it sits a machinery of surveillance, religious enforcement, and state-mandated language policing designed to erase LGBTQ visibility from Malaysian society. When a PH government wields MCMC, the police, state religious departments, and the Deputy PM’s proposed Cyber Crime Bill to target the same community in lockstep, it is no longer just a “moral” issue — it is a civil liberties crisis being orchestrated by the state. Justice for Sisters, ARTICLE 19, and Human Rights Watch have all flagged this as a systematic pattern of state-sponsored discrimination.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH campaigned as the coalition of reform, human rights, and institutional accountability. Anwar Ibrahim personally cultivated an image of pluralism and tolerance on the international stage. Now his Communications Minister is blocking dating apps on “morality” grounds, his Deputy Religious Affairs Minister is mandating Orwellian language policing — telling the public to call fellow citizens “deviants” — and his Deputy PM is drafting legislation to expand state powers over digital platforms. Fahmi had the gall to say MCMC hadn’t received formal requests to remove the apps from app stores, then the apps disappeared from Google Play and the Apple App Store anyway. The government isn’t just failing to protect minorities; it is actively building the enforcement infrastructure to persecute them. This is not reform. This is the Sedition Act in a new digital wrapper, sold by the same people who once promised to repeal it.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s track record on LGBTQ persecution under PH’s watch is damning and escalating. In May 2023, authorities raided Swatch stores nationwide, seizing over 160 rainbow-coloured watches. In June 2025, Selangor police opened an investigation into a closed-door LGBTQ sexual health workshop — criminalising public health outreach. In June 2025, Kelantan police raided a private property described as a “gay party,” arresting 20 men. On November 28, 2025, Kuala Lumpur police conducted mass raids on men-only venues, detaining 202 people — many of whom, according to Justice for Sisters, were denied legal counsel, subjected to degrading treatment, publicly identified, and held in overcrowded vehicles overnight. This is not a new problem. It is a pattern of escalation that PH has accelerated, not reversed.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would repeal oppressive laws. You told the world you believed in human dignity. Now you’re blocking apps, policing language, raiding venues, running “spiritual guidance camps,” and drafting new legislation to expand digital censorship. Fahmi, you run the ministry that oversees MCMC — the same body that removed TikTok videos critical of your government this very week. Marhamah, you’re a deputy minister in a reformist coalition ordering Malaysians to call their fellow citizens “deviants.” Anwar, you once invoked the Universal Declaration of Human Rights from a prison cell. Every blocked app, every police raid on a private gathering, every state-mandated sermon targeting LGBTQ people is a betrayal of the promises that put you in Putrajaya. This is not governance. This is state-sponsored persecution with reformist branding.

Fahmi Confirms MCMC Blocked Grindr and Blued — PH's 'Reform Government' Now Runs Digital Censorship Against Minorities

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-25

📌 What Happened

On February 25, 2026, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil confirmed in a written parliamentary reply that the Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission (MCMC) has blocked the websites of Grindr and Blued — two of the world’s largest dating and networking platforms used by gay and bisexual men. Fahmi said MCMC is now “studying several legislative measures” to curb the apps from Google Play and the Apple App Store, though he acknowledged that control over foreign-owned platforms is “subject to regulations and policies set by the said platform providers.” By early March, both apps were removed from Malaysian app stores entirely. Deputy Prime Minister Ahmad Zahid Hamidi separately announced a proposed Cyber Crime Bill to expand the government’s digital enforcement powers, including over “immoral” content.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Madani government using state infrastructure to systematically erase digital safe spaces for a persecuted minority community — and doing it quietly through written parliamentary replies and regulatory action rather than open policy debate. Fahmi framed it under the catch-all of “lewd or immoral content, exploitation, abuse, scams, exploiting children or threats towards public safety” — a masterclass in conflating consenting adults with criminals. The blocking comes alongside Deputy Religious Affairs Minister Marhamah Rosli’s directive to replace all references to “LGBT” with “budaya songsang” (deviant culture) across government communications and social media, and a multi-agency committee targeting Muslim LGBTQ individuals with so-called “psychospiritual therapy.” This is not morality policing on the margins — it is a coordinated state apparatus targeting a specific community using censorship, surveillance, and institutional erasure.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Pakatan Harapan ran on civil liberties, institutional reform, and the promise that Malaysia would stop using the state to persecute its own citizens. Fahmi Fadzil — a former civil society figure and one-time press freedom advocate — is now the minister signing off on internet censorship targeting a minority. The same government that screams about “freedom of expression” when its critics get summoned under Section 124B is perfectly comfortable ordering MCMC to block websites and pressure Apple and Google to delist apps. The Cyber Crime Bill that Zahid is pushing will replace the Computer Crimes Act 1997 with broader powers — the exact kind of legislative overreach PH promised to roll back. This is not conservative governance; it is a reformist government that has decided persecuting minorities is electorally convenient.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows a well-documented pattern of escalation under the Anwar administration. In May 2023, the Home Affairs Ministry raided Swatch stores nationwide and confiscated over 160 rainbow-coloured watches. In June 2025, Kelantan police raided a private property described as a “gay party” and arrested 20 men. In November 2025, 202 people were arrested in raids on men-only venues in Kuala Lumpur — detainees reported being denied legal counsel, subjected to degrading treatment, and having their identities publicly exposed. Justice for Sisters documented systematic procedural violations. In January 2026, a community organisation cancelled its “Glamping with Pride” event after receiving death threats. Each time, the government either endorsed the action, stayed silent, or expanded the legal tools available for the next crackdown.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysians a government that would defend civil liberties, roll back repressive laws, and protect the vulnerable from state overreach. Instead, you are building new censorship infrastructure, proposing broader cyber legislation, and running conversion programmes through state religious departments — all while Fahmi Fadzil, who once championed media freedom, signs the blocking orders. Every digital rights group, every international press freedom watchdog, and every human rights organisation monitoring Malaysia is watching this and drawing the obvious conclusion: Madani reform was never meant for everyone. If you cannot even leave people’s phones alone, stop pretending you are a reform government.

Rawang Hindu Temple Demolished at Midnight — No Court Order, Police Assisted, PH State Government Silent

Said by: Selangor PH State Government / Police

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-24

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 24 February 2026, the Sri Uchimalai Muniswaran temple in Rawang, Selangor was demolished in the middle of the night — hours after deities were allegedly relocated following pressure on the temple committee. The demolition was carried out with police assistance and without any court order.

Lawyers Rajesh Nagarajan and Sachpreetraj Singh issued a statement: “We strongly deny the claims by Yayasan Kubra representative Ariff Omar that the temple committee voluntarily removed the deities. That is false and ridiculous since the temple chairman lodged a police report only yesterday complaining that the temple was being pressured to vacate the land without any court order.”

Four men, including activist Tamim Dahri, were arrested but the Shah Alam High Court quashed the remand order and ordered their immediate release. Police classified it as a land dispute, not a racial or religious conflict.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

A Hindu temple was demolished at midnight, with police present, without a court order, in a state governed by PH. This is not something that happens in a country with rule of law. It’s something that happens when institutions have failed and political will is absent.

The timing — amid a broader crackdown on unauthorised Hindu temples in Selangor following the Sultan’s statements — is not coincidental. Malaysia Hindu Sangam identified 687 unauthorised Hindu temples in Selangor alone. The question isn’t whether they’re authorised — it’s whether the government’s response is to demolish community heritage or find solutions.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

PH promised to be different. To protect minorities. To uphold rule of law. Instead, under a PH state government:

  • A temple was demolished without a court order
  • It happened at midnight — the time you choose when you don’t want witnesses
  • Police assisted rather than prevented it
  • The arrested men were released by the High Court, suggesting the lower court’s remand was improper If this happened under BN, DAP would have called it ethnic cleansing. Under PH, it’s a ’land dispute.'

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Temple demolitions have a long and painful history in Malaysia. The Seafield Sri Maha Mariamman temple riot of 2018 occurred during PH 1.0’s watch. The pattern repeats: minority religious sites are treated as disposable when they stand on land someone else wants. The only thing that changes is which coalition looks away.

Controversial Muslim preacher Zamri Vinoth has been targeting Hindu temples with inflammatory rhetoric, seemingly with impunity. Malaysiakini columnist Andrew Sia asked: “Why do agitators like Zamri Vinoth seem to have immunity while insulting Hindu temples?”

🎯 PH CALLOUT

Anwar Ibrahim himself acknowledged the sensitivity: “I know this will invite controversy. Some people have gone to extremes, even launching campaigns to demolish temples.” He warned against vigilantism. Then a temple was demolished at midnight in his coalition’s state. Your warning means nothing if your own state government cannot prevent midnight demolitions without court orders. The Indian community voted for you because they believed you would protect them. This is what that protection looks like?

[BM] Selangor Haramkan Semua Penternakan Babi — Amirudin Tunduk Kepada Titah Sultan, Penternak Cina Tiada Apa-Apa

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-12

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU

Pada 12 Februari 2026, Menteri Besar Selangor Amirudin Shari mengumumkan kerajaan negeri telah berhenti mengeluarkan lesen penternakan babi dan akan mempercepatkan penutupan semua ladang babi sedia ada. Keputusan itu berikutan titah Sultan Sharafuddin Idris Shah, yang memerintahkan tiada ladang babi patut beroperasi di mana-mana daerah di Selangor.

Perancangan awal untuk memindahkan ladang babi ke Bukit Tagar — kawasan berpenduduk jarang di utara Selangor — ditangguhkan selepas penduduk Melayu membantah kerana berdekatan Sungai Selangor, sumber rawatan air utama. Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim campur tangan pada Januari untuk menghentikan pemindahan.

Selangor kini akan mengimport sepenuhnya semua daging babi untuk memenuhi permintaan bukan Muslim. Penternak babi Cina, ramai yang mengoperasikan ladang berbilang generasi di Tanjong Sepat, berdepan kemusnahan sumber pendapatan.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING

Selangor ialah negeri paling kaya dan maju Malaysia — dan ia ditadbir PH. Larangan penternakan babi menjejaskan sumber pendapatan dan keperluan pemakanan komuniti etnik tertentu. Walaupun kebimbangan alam sekitar tentang pencemaran air adalah sah, larangan total tanpa alternatif yang berdaya maju adalah penyerahan, bukan penyelesaian.

Mesej kepada pengundi Cina jelas: apabila istana memerintahkan dan sentimen Melayu menolak, PH tidak akan berjuang untuk anda. Pemindahan ke Bukit Tagar — yang boleh menjadi penyelesaian moden dan terkawal — ditinggalkan sebaik ia menjadi tidak selesa secara politik.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT

PH mentadbir Selangor. PH mempunyai bertahun-tahun untuk membangunkan penyelesaian penternakan babi yang terkawal, moden, dan lestari alam sekitar. Sebaliknya, mereka mencadangkan pemindahan, mendapat bantahan, meninggalkannya, dan kemudian tunduk kepada titah diraja dengan larangan total.

Negeri yang menjenamakan dirinya sebagai progresif dan inklusif baru sahaja menghapuskan seluruh industri yang terutamanya melayani komuniti Cinanya. Pengimport — yang akan meraih keuntungan besar — sudah menyokong larangan. Seorang pengimport menganggarkan import sudah meliputi 80% permintaan. Jadi siapa yang untung? Bukan penternak. Bukan pengguna yang lebih suka daging babi tempatan segar. Pengimport.

📜 KES TERDAHULU

Ini mengikut corak kerajaan negeri PH gagal melindungi komuniti minoriti apabila kos politik terlalu tinggi. Di Pulau Pinang, DAP mentadbir selama 16 tahun tetapi masih berdepan kritikan atas pembangunan yang menyingkirkan komuniti. Di Selangor, Amirudin berulang kali tertangkap tidak bersedia — daripada kegagalan pengurusan banjir hingga krisis kebocoran air. Setiap kali, tindak balas adalah reaktif, tidak pernah proaktif.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH

Anda kerajaan Selangor. Anda mempunyai kuasa mengawal selia, memodenkan, dan memindahkan penternakan babi. Sebaliknya, anda mengharamkannya sepenuhnya. Anda tidak menyelesaikan masalah pencemaran — anda menghapuskan sumber pendapatan sebuah komuniti. Mana DAP yang berjanji melindungi semua rakyat Malaysia tanpa mengira kaum? Mana kerajaan Madani yang bercerita tentang keterangkuman? Penternak Cina di Tanjong Sepat ingin tahu.

Selangor Bans All Pig Farming — Amirudin Caves to Sultan's Decree, Chinese Farmers Left With Nothing

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-12

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 12 February 2026, Selangor Menteri Besar Amirudin Shari announced that the state government has stopped issuing pig farming licences and will accelerate the closure of all existing pig farms. The decision followed a decree from Sultan Sharafuddin Idris Shah, who declared that no pig farms should operate in any district in Selangor.

An earlier plan to relocate pig farms to Bukit Tagar — a sparsely populated area in northern Selangor — was shelved after Malay residents objected due to proximity to Sungai Selangor, a major water treatment source. Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim intervened in January to halt the relocation.

Selangor will now fully import all pork to meet non-Muslim demand. Chinese pig farmers, many of whom have operated multigenerational farms in Tanjong Sepat, face the destruction of their livelihoods.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Selangor is Malaysia’s richest and most developed state — and it’s governed by PH. The pig farming ban affects a specific ethnic community’s livelihood and dietary needs. While environmental concerns about water pollution are legitimate, the total ban with no viable alternative is a capitulation, not a solution.

The message to Chinese voters is clear: when the palace decrees and Malay sentiment pushes back, PH will not fight for you. The Bukit Tagar relocation — which could have been a modern, regulated solution — was abandoned the moment it became politically uncomfortable.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

PH governs Selangor. PH had years to develop a proper, regulated, environmentally sustainable pig farming solution. Instead, they proposed a relocation, got pushback, abandoned it, and then caved to a royal decree with a total ban.

The state that brands itself as progressive and inclusive just eliminated an entire industry that primarily serves its Chinese community. Pork importers — who stand to profit massively — are already backing the ban. One importer estimated imports already cover 80% of demand. So who benefits? Not the farmers. Not the consumers who prefer fresh local pork. The importers.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

This follows a pattern of PH state governments failing to protect minority communities when the political cost gets too high. In Penang, DAP has governed for 16 years but still faces criticism for development that displaces communities. In Selangor, Amirudin has repeatedly been caught flat-footed — from flood management failures to the water leak crisis. Every time, the response is reactive, never proactive.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You are the government of Selangor. You had the power to regulate, modernise, and relocate pig farming. Instead, you banned it entirely. You didn’t solve a pollution problem — you eliminated a community’s livelihood. Where is the DAP that promised to protect all Malaysians regardless of race? Where is the Madani government that talks about inclusivity? The Chinese farmers in Tanjong Sepat would like to know.
