Rawang Hindu Temple Demolished at Midnight — No Court Order, Police Assisted, PH State Government Silent

Said by: Selangor PH State Government / Police

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-24

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 24 February 2026, the Sri Uchimalai Muniswaran temple in Rawang, Selangor was demolished in the middle of the night — hours after deities were allegedly relocated following pressure on the temple committee. The demolition was carried out with police assistance and without any court order.

Lawyers Rajesh Nagarajan and Sachpreetraj Singh issued a statement: “We strongly deny the claims by Yayasan Kubra representative Ariff Omar that the temple committee voluntarily removed the deities. That is false and ridiculous since the temple chairman lodged a police report only yesterday complaining that the temple was being pressured to vacate the land without any court order.”

Four men, including activist Tamim Dahri, were arrested but the Shah Alam High Court quashed the remand order and ordered their immediate release. Police classified it as a land dispute, not a racial or religious conflict.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

A Hindu temple was demolished at midnight, with police present, without a court order, in a state governed by PH. This is not something that happens in a country with rule of law. It’s something that happens when institutions have failed and political will is absent.

The timing — amid a broader crackdown on unauthorised Hindu temples in Selangor following the Sultan’s statements — is not coincidental. Malaysia Hindu Sangam identified 687 unauthorised Hindu temples in Selangor alone. The question isn’t whether they’re authorised — it’s whether the government’s response is to demolish community heritage or find solutions.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

PH promised to be different. To protect minorities. To uphold rule of law. Instead, under a PH state government:

  • A temple was demolished without a court order
  • It happened at midnight — the time you choose when you don’t want witnesses
  • Police assisted rather than prevented it
  • The arrested men were released by the High Court, suggesting the lower court’s remand was improper If this happened under BN, DAP would have called it ethnic cleansing. Under PH, it’s a ’land dispute.'

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Temple demolitions have a long and painful history in Malaysia. The Seafield Sri Maha Mariamman temple riot of 2018 occurred during PH 1.0’s watch. The pattern repeats: minority religious sites are treated as disposable when they stand on land someone else wants. The only thing that changes is which coalition looks away.

Controversial Muslim preacher Zamri Vinoth has been targeting Hindu temples with inflammatory rhetoric, seemingly with impunity. Malaysiakini columnist Andrew Sia asked: “Why do agitators like Zamri Vinoth seem to have immunity while insulting Hindu temples?”

🎯 PH CALLOUT

Anwar Ibrahim himself acknowledged the sensitivity: “I know this will invite controversy. Some people have gone to extremes, even launching campaigns to demolish temples.” He warned against vigilantism. Then a temple was demolished at midnight in his coalition’s state. Your warning means nothing if your own state government cannot prevent midnight demolitions without court orders. The Indian community voted for you because they believed you would protect them. This is what that protection looks like?

Selangor Bans All Pig Farming — Amirudin Caves to Sultan's Decree, Chinese Farmers Left With Nothing

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-12

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 12 February 2026, Selangor Menteri Besar Amirudin Shari announced that the state government has stopped issuing pig farming licences and will accelerate the closure of all existing pig farms. The decision followed a decree from Sultan Sharafuddin Idris Shah, who declared that no pig farms should operate in any district in Selangor.

An earlier plan to relocate pig farms to Bukit Tagar — a sparsely populated area in northern Selangor — was shelved after Malay residents objected due to proximity to Sungai Selangor, a major water treatment source. Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim intervened in January to halt the relocation.

Selangor will now fully import all pork to meet non-Muslim demand. Chinese pig farmers, many of whom have operated multigenerational farms in Tanjong Sepat, face the destruction of their livelihoods.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Selangor is Malaysia’s richest and most developed state — and it’s governed by PH. The pig farming ban affects a specific ethnic community’s livelihood and dietary needs. While environmental concerns about water pollution are legitimate, the total ban with no viable alternative is a capitulation, not a solution.

The message to Chinese voters is clear: when the palace decrees and Malay sentiment pushes back, PH will not fight for you. The Bukit Tagar relocation — which could have been a modern, regulated solution — was abandoned the moment it became politically uncomfortable.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

PH governs Selangor. PH had years to develop a proper, regulated, environmentally sustainable pig farming solution. Instead, they proposed a relocation, got pushback, abandoned it, and then caved to a royal decree with a total ban.

The state that brands itself as progressive and inclusive just eliminated an entire industry that primarily serves its Chinese community. Pork importers — who stand to profit massively — are already backing the ban. One importer estimated imports already cover 80% of demand. So who benefits? Not the farmers. Not the consumers who prefer fresh local pork. The importers.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

This follows a pattern of PH state governments failing to protect minority communities when the political cost gets too high. In Penang, DAP has governed for 16 years but still faces criticism for development that displaces communities. In Selangor, Amirudin has repeatedly been caught flat-footed — from flood management failures to the water leak crisis. Every time, the response is reactive, never proactive.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You are the government of Selangor. You had the power to regulate, modernise, and relocate pig farming. Instead, you banned it entirely. You didn’t solve a pollution problem — you eliminated a community’s livelihood. Where is the DAP that promised to protect all Malaysians regardless of race? Where is the Madani government that talks about inclusivity? The Chinese farmers in Tanjong Sepat would like to know.

Fahmi Fadzil: Free Speech Doesn't Mean Freedom to Offend Online

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-07

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 7 February 2026, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil told the public that free speech does not mean freedom to offend or defame online. This came after the Federal Court reinstated Section 233 of the Communications and Multimedia Act (CMA), which criminalises content deemed “grossly offensive” — a provision PH once opposed. Fahmi said the 2024 amendments raised the threshold from “offensive” to “grossly offensive,” claiming this made it harder to misuse. The Online Safety Act 2025 (ONSA) took effect on 1 January 2026, giving MCMC powers to penalise platforms up to RM10 million for failing to remove harmful content. MCMC — the body that decides what is “grossly offensive” — reports directly to Fahmi’s ministry.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: “Grossly offensive” is a subjective and vague standard that can mean anything the government wants it to mean. The combination of the reinstated CMA provision and the new ONSA gives the Malaysian government one of the most powerful speech regulation frameworks in Southeast Asia. Platforms face RM10M fines; individuals face prosecution. The chilling effect is by design. PH in opposition repeatedly condemned the CMA as a tool of authoritarian suppression. In 2018, PH’s manifesto called for “reviewing” the CMA. In 2022, PH Youth called Section 233 “draconian.” In government, they amended it slightly, renamed “offensive” to “grossly offensive,” and called it progress.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Who decides what is “grossly offensive”? MCMC, which answers to Fahmi. Fahmi, who answers to Anwar. There is no independent judicial review before content is flagged or removed. The minister who champions “free speech with limits” is also the minister who controls the limits. The fox is guarding the henhouse and telling the chickens it is for their own protection. PH’s own track record demolishes Fahmi’s argument: when the government arrests a journalist for asking a question (Rex Tan), investigates activists for social media posts, and uses the Sedition Act 90+ times in three years, the “we only target real harm” defence collapses. Renaming “offensive” to “grossly offensive” is not reform — it is rebranding.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Since taking power, the Anwar government has used the CMA, Sedition Act, and other speech laws to arrest journalists, activists, and social media users — including FMT journalist Rex Tan, activist Heidy Quah, and multiple online commentators critical of the government. The Sedition Act — which PH promised to repeal — has been used over 90 times since 2024. The CMA was wielded against critics under Najib, under Muhyiddin, and now under Anwar. The tool changes hands; the abuse continues. Every Malaysian government has used speech laws selectively: targeting critics while ignoring allies who say equally provocative things.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You promised to reform the CMA. Instead, you strengthened it. You promised to repeal the Sedition Act. Instead, you used it more than Najib did. You promised press freedom. Instead, your MCMC blocks news outlets and your police arrest journalists at dawn. Fahmi’s statement — “free speech doesn’t mean freedom to offend” — is the same argument made by every government that has ever restricted speech, from Singapore’s POFMA to China’s internet regulations. The formula is universal: affirm the right, define it so narrowly it becomes meaningless. PH voters expected the architecture of speech suppression to be dismantled. Instead, you added new floors to the building.

Zulkifli Hasan Says Work Stress Can Make People Gay — International Community Laughs, Malaysia Cringes

Said by: Zulkifli Hasan, Minister in PM’s Department (Religious Affairs)

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On January 26, 2026, Religious Affairs Minister Zulkifli Hasan submitted a written parliamentary reply to PAS MP Siti Zailah Mohd Yusoff about LGBT trends in Malaysia. He cited a 2017 study and listed “societal influence, sexual experiences, work stress, and other personal factors” as possible causes of “LGBT behaviour.” The “work stress” claim went immediately viral — globally. CNA, SCMP, NDTV, Reuters, Hindustan Times, LGBTQ Nation, and them.us all covered it. Malaysians flooded social media with mockery, sarcastically asking whether parliament MPs had “turned gay” since they apparently never work hard enough to experience stress. Zulkifli also disclosed that from 2022 to 2025, 135 cases related to “LGBT activities” were recorded as arrests or prosecutions — in a country where same-sex relations remain criminalised.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This isn’t just embarrassing — it’s damaging. A cabinet minister, speaking in an official parliamentary capacity, cited debunked pseudoscience to explain sexual orientation. This is the government’s official position on record in Hansard. The international coverage turned Malaysia into a global punchline while the country tries to attract foreign investment, host international summits, and position itself as a progressive Muslim-majority democracy. More importantly, the remark normalises the framing of LGBT identity as a pathology — something caused by external factors that can presumably be “fixed.” This framing underpins the continued criminalisation of same-sex relations and the use of state-funded “rehabilitation” programmes.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The 2017 study Zulkifli cited — Sulaiman et al. — is a small-scale Malaysian social study, not a peer-reviewed finding from any recognised international medical or psychological body. Every major medical and psychological organisation in the world — the WHO, APA, AMA, Royal College of Psychiatrists — has stated clearly that sexual orientation is not caused by stress, lifestyle, or environmental factors. The minister later issued a defensive statement saying people should “refer to the full written answer” — as if the problem was context. It wasn’t. The context makes it worse: it was an official, considered, pre-written reply. This wasn’t a slip of the tongue. It was deliberate.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a long, inglorious history of ministers making scientifically illiterate statements about LGBT people. In 2018, then-Deputy PM Wan Azizah said the government would not recognise LGBT rights because they were “against the law of nature.” In 2019, Religious Affairs officials promoted “conversion therapy” camps. Malaysia’s Federal Territories Islamic Department (JAWI) regularly raids private premises suspected of hosting LGBT individuals. PH in opposition criticised BN’s persecution of LGBT Malaysians; in government, PH has continued the same policies and rhetoric. Zulkifli Hasan’s appointment itself was a political concession — he represents the Malay-Muslim establishment within PH’s coalition.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You campaigned on “Malaysia Madani” — a civilised, progressive Malaysia. Your Religious Affairs Minister just told parliament that work stress turns people gay. The world laughed. Malaysia’s reputation took yet another hit. Where was Anwar? Where was the PMO correction? Where was the cabinet disavowal? Silence. Because correcting Zulkifli would upset the conservative base PH desperately needs. So the government let a pseudoscientific claim stand as Malaysia’s official parliamentary position on sexual orientation. Madani means civilised. There is nothing civilised about citing junk science in parliament to justify the criminalisation of who people love.

FMT Journalist Rex Tan Arrested Under Sedition Act for Asking a 'Sensitive Question'

Said by: Royal Malaysia Police

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-17

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2026, Free Malaysia Today journalist Rex Tan was arrested at dawn under the colonial-era Sedition Act 1948 for asking a question at a public debate that linked the Gaza crisis to the treatment of ethnic Chinese in Malaysia. Police arrived at Tan’s home early on a Saturday morning — five days after the debate — and arrested him under Section 4(1) of the Sedition Act. The question was provocative but fell squarely within the bounds of legitimate public discourse. SCMP reported the arrest “casts doubts on Anwar’s reform record.” International press freedom organisations, including RSF and CPJ, condemned the arrest. Over 90 sedition-related arrests have occurred since Anwar took power in late 2022.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A journalist was arrested for asking a question — not for incitement, not for violence, not for defamation. For a question at a public debate. If this is the threshold for sedition, then every Malaysian who has ever asked an uncomfortable question about race, religion, or policy is a potential criminal. The dawn arrest is a deliberate intimidation tactic — police could have summoned Tan to a station during business hours. Instead, they chose the most psychologically coercive approach. This is not law enforcement. It is a message to every other journalist: self-censor or face the same fate. Malaysia’s press freedom ranking has stagnated under Anwar despite reform promises.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar promised to repeal the Sedition Act. In the GE14 manifesto, PH pledged abolition. In GE15, PH reaffirmed the commitment. Instead, the Anwar government has expanded its use — over 90 sedition arrests since taking power. Under Najib — the PM PH overthrew partly because of his authoritarianism — there were fewer sedition prosecutions per year. Anwar is using the Sedition Act more aggressively than the man he replaced. The Sedition Act is a colonial-era law from 1948, enacted by the British to suppress the independence movement. Anwar himself was charged under it in 2014. He called it “a draconian law used to silence dissent.” He spent years in prison as a victim of politically motivated prosecution. He knows exactly what dawn arrests feel like — and he is doing it anyway.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: PH has not only kept every repressive law it promised to repeal — it has used them more frequently and against a wider range of targets. CMA prosecutions, ONSA enforcement, Sedition Act arrests, SOSMA detentions — the reform government has become the enforcement government. Under BN, sedition was used against opposition politicians and activists. Under PH, it is used against journalists asking questions. The target has expanded while the tool remains the same. Activist Heidy Quah was investigated. Social media commentators have been arrested. The pattern is unmistakable.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You arrested a journalist at dawn for asking a question. You promised to repeal the law used to arrest him. Instead, you have used it over 90 times — more than Najib. Anwar, you were a victim of the Sedition Act. You called it draconian. You said it should be abolished. You built a political career on the injustice of politically motivated prosecution. Now your police arrest journalists before sunrise for asking questions at debates. Repeal the Sedition Act or admit that Reformasi was just a slogan you used to get into power — not a principle you intend to govern by.

Social Media Platforms Forcibly 'Deemed Licensed' — MCMC Tightens Grip on Internet

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: From 1 January 2026, social media and instant messaging platforms with 8 million or more Malaysian users were automatically “deemed licensed” under MCMC’s regulatory framework — whether they applied for a licence or not. This means platforms like Meta (Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp), TikTok, Telegram, and X are now subject to Malaysian government licensing requirements, content takedown obligations, and penalties for non-compliance. Meta publicly warned the scheme “lacks clarity and threatens innovation.” Soyacincau reported that MCMC’s approach effectively bypassed the normal licensing application process by unilaterally deeming platforms as licensed. Reuters reported Meta’s concerns about the regulatory framework’s vagueness.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Forced licensing gives the Malaysian government regulatory leverage over every major platform used by Malaysians. Licensed entities must comply with government directives — including content removal requests, user data obligations, and operational requirements set by MCMC. This creates a legal framework where the government can threaten to revoke or suspend licences if platforms don’t comply with takedown orders. The scheme bypassed normal regulatory processes: platforms were not asked whether they wanted licences — they were told they had them, along with all the obligations that come with them.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH in opposition criticised the BN government for using MCMC to control online discourse. In government, PH has built the most comprehensive internet regulation framework in Malaysian history: the Online Safety Act with RM10M penalties, the reinstated CMA “grossly offensive” provision, and now forced licensing of every major platform. The “child safety” framing is the Trojan horse — the infrastructure being built gives the government tools to regulate ANY content, not just harmful content. Who decides what content violates licensing conditions? MCMC, which answers to Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil, who answers to Anwar. The same minister who says “free speech doesn’t mean freedom to offend” now has licensing power over every platform Malaysians use.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, MCMC was used to block websites critical of the government — most notably during the 1MDB scandal when The Sarawak Report and other outlets were blocked. Under PH, the tools are more sophisticated but the intent is the same: control the information environment. The deemed licensing approach follows Singapore’s model, where platform licensing has been used to compel content removal on politically sensitive topics. Malaysia is building the same infrastructure while claiming it’s about protecting children.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You forced licensing on every major platform without asking them, without clear rules, and without independent oversight. Meta — one of the largest tech companies on earth — publicly warned your approach “lacks clarity and threatens innovation.” When the platforms you’re regulating tell you your regulation is unclear, that’s not a feature — it’s a design choice. Vague rules give you maximum discretion. Maximum discretion means maximum censorship potential. If this is really about child safety, publish clear, specific, enforceable rules with independent oversight. If you can’t do that, admit this is about control.

TikTok Generation Lost — Malaysian Youth Screen Time Among Highest in the World While Government Plans to Ban Under-16s

Said by: MCMC / Online Safety Act

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysian youth have among the highest screen times and social media usage rates in the world. The government’s response was the Online Safety Act, which bans social media use for under-16s from 2026. The law places enforcement responsibility on platforms, with penalties up to RM10 million for non-compliance. However, implementation details remain vague — no age verification infrastructure exists, no technical framework has been announced, and enforcement mechanisms are untested. Freedom House’s 2024 report flagged Malaysia’s declining internet freedom trajectory under PH.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Excessive social media use among youth is linked to declining mental health, cyberbullying, exposure to inappropriate content, and reduced academic performance. Malaysian PISA scores correlate with screen time data — students spending more hours on devices perform worse academically. The K-pods drug crisis is also facilitated through social media distribution networks. But the issue isn’t just screen time — it’s that the government has failed to provide alternatives: a functional education system, employment prospects, mental health support, or safe public spaces for young people.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The under-16 social media ban is performative legislation — it looks tough but is virtually unenforceable. How do you verify age for social media accounts? VPNs are trivially easy to use. Parental controls are easily circumvented by tech-savvy children. Without robust age verification infrastructure — which doesn’t exist and would raise massive privacy concerns — the law is decoration. Australia passed similar legislation and immediately faced implementation challenges. The EU focused on platform accountability rather than age bans. Malaysia chose the most attention-grabbing but least effective approach. More importantly, the same government building age-verification infrastructure is also forcibly licensing social media platforms and expanding MCMC’s censorship powers — tools that can easily be repurposed for broader internet control.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Previous MCMC “enforcement” of online content has been selective — targeting political critics while struggling to address scams, fraud, and actual harmful content. The government’s track record on tech regulation is one of overreach on speech and underperformance on safety. MCMC can find and arrest social media users who mock politicians within hours, but can’t shut down scam call centres operating from Malaysian soil for years. The priorities tell you everything about what this legislation is really for.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You’re banning TikTok for kids while your education system can’t teach them math. You’re “protecting” them online while bullying cases surge 17% in schools. You’re regulating social media while 10,160 students didn’t even show up for SPM. Maybe address the real-world failures that drive youth to screens in the first place: give them a functional education system, employment prospects, and mental health support. A TikTok ban without systemic reform is a headline, not a policy.

Race-Based Economic Policies NEP Still Untouchable — 'Unanimous' Critics Ignored

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Think Tanks

Category: Social

Date: 2025-12-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Eurasia Review published a comprehensive analysis in December 2025 finding “almost unanimous opinion” among analysts that Malaysia’s race-based economic policies — rooted in the New Economic Policy (NEP) of 1971 — have failed their “overarching goal of national unity.” Yet the analysis concluded the government is “highly unlikely to abandon” them. The NEP and its successors (NDP, NVP, and now Anwar’s various economic frameworks) maintain Bumiputera preferences in government contracts, education, housing, and corporate equity. Despite 50+ years of these policies, Malaysia remains deeply divided along ethnic lines, brain drain continues at alarming rates, and economic inequality persists within the Bumiputera community itself.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The NEP and its successors have been the third rail of Malaysian politics for over 50 years. Every PM — including the “reformist” one — refuses to touch them. When virtually every independent analyst agrees a policy has failed its stated objective, and the government still won’t reform it, the policy exists to serve political interests, not national ones. Anwar’s multiracial “Malaysia Madani” brand is fundamentally incompatible with race-based economic preferences — but admitting that would cost Malay votes. So the contradiction persists and the country pays the price in brain drain, investment distortions, and communal resentment.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The NEP was designed as a 20-year policy (1971-1990). It’s now in its 55th year. The Bumiputera corporate equity target of 30% has been a moving goalpost for decades — with the government periodically changing the methodology to show either progress or continued need, depending on what serves the political moment. Meanwhile, the primary beneficiaries of Bumiputera preferences have been politically connected elites, not ordinary Malays. The wealth gap within the Bumiputera community is enormous. Race-based policies designed to help poor Malays have been captured by rich, connected Malays — and PH, like every government before it, refuses to acknowledge this because the fiction of universal Bumiputera benefit is essential to electoral politics.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Mahathir introduced the Vision 2020 framework that was supposed to move Malaysia beyond race-based economics. It didn’t. Abdullah Badawi promised the NDP would be more “merit-based.” It wasn’t. Najib’s NTP made similar promises. Anwar’s frameworks use different acronyms for the same structure. The NEP is unkillable not because it works, but because dismantling it would require telling millions of Malay voters that the preference system their political identity is built on needs to change. No politician has the courage to do this — least of all one who needs Malay votes to survive.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Analysts — your own think tanks included — say unanimously that race-based economic policies have failed their stated goal of national unity. Brain drain accelerates as non-Bumiputera professionals leave for countries where they’re judged on merit. Investment distortions persist. Communal resentment deepens. And your response is to maintain the policies unchanged because dismantling them would cost votes. “Malaysia Madani” cannot coexist with race-based economic preferences. Choose one. Or admit that “Madani” was always just a slogan — not a policy direction.
