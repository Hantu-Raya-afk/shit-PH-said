[BM] Fadhlina Kata Kementerian 'Memantau' Sekolah Dari Ekstremisme — Selepas Polis Sudah Tangkap Enam Remaja Berkaitan IS

Said by: Fadhlina Sidek, Education Minister

Category: Education

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 12 Mac, Menteri Pendidikan Fadhlina Sidek memberitahu pemberita di Bukit Mertajam bahawa Kementerian Pendidikan sedang “memantau penglibatan pelajar di institusi pendidikan” untuk menghalang ideologi ekstremis daripada menular ke sekolah. Beliau berkata kementerian telah “menyerahkan perkara itu kepada pihak berkuasa berkaitan untuk siasatan lanjut dan tindakan sewajarnya,” sambil menekankan kerjasama rapat dengan agensi penguatkuasaan untuk melindungi pelajar daripada pengaruh “terutamanya yang berkait rapat dengan Daesh dan keganasan.” Kenyataan ini dibuat berminggu-minggu selepas Cawangan Khas menangkap enam remaja lelaki berusia 16 hingga 21 tahun pada 14–15 Februari dalam operasi khas atas syak terlibat dalam aktiviti IS.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Tangkapan enam remaja kerana kaitan IS sepatutnya menjadi isyarat kecemasan bagi mana-mana Menteri Pendidikan. Sebaliknya, respons Fadhlina — diberi berminggu-minggu kemudian — adalah demonstrasi kepasifan birokrasi: “memantau,” “serahkan kepada pihak berkuasa,” “bekerjasama rapat.” Ini menteri yang sama yang menghabiskan 2025 berhadapan desakan letak jawatan gara-gara kegagalan keselamatan sekolah — kematian akibat buli, serangan seksual, dan budaya menutup kes yang diakui sendiri oleh Ketua Pengarahnya. Coraknya konsisten: ancaman serius timbul, Fadhlina cakap tentang pemantauan, dan kementerian tunggu orang lain bertindak. Kalau perekrut IS menyasarkan kanak-kanak Malaysia, peranan Kementerian Pendidikan bukan memerhati dari tepi — ia adalah merombak cara sekolah mengesan, melaporkan, dan bertindak balas terhadap radikalisasi. Sebaliknya, kita dapat sidang akhbar.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Enam remaja ditangkap kerana kaitan IS, dan respons Menteri Pendidikan — sebulan penuh kemudian — ialah kementerian sedang “memantau.” Bukan lancarkan program deradikalisasi seluruh negara. Bukan umumkan latihan wajib guru untuk mengenal pasti tanda-tanda radikalisasi. Bukan semak semula kurikulum untuk kandungan antiekstremisme. Malah tidak dedahkan berapa ramai pelajar telah dibenderakan. Cuma “memantau” dan “serah kepada pihak berkuasa.” Fadhlina telah menjadikan minimalis reaktif sebagai seni. Bila pelajar dibuli sampai mati, beliau “mengambil serius.” Bila sekolah tutup kes serangan seksual, beliau ikrar “toleransi sifar.” Kini remaja direkrut IS, dan kementerian “memantau.” Pada tahap mana kerajaan mengakui bahawa mod operasi menteri ini adalah pemerhatian berterusan, bukan tindakan tegas?

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Rekod Fadhlina dalam memberi respons lambat dan pasif telah didokumentasikan dengan baik. Pada 2025, gelombang kes buli sekolah — termasuk kematian — mencetuskan kemarahan awam dan memorandum menuntut peletakan jawatannya, yang diterimanya di luar Parlimen pada Oktober 2025. Ketua Pengarah sendiri mengakui kes-kes telah “disapu bawah karpet.” Presiden MCA Wee Ka Siong secara terbuka menuntut pemecatannya pada Disember 2025, memanggil tempoh perkhidmatannya “tidak berkesan.” Penganalisis menamakan beliau ahli Kabinet paling terdedah dalam mana-mana rombakan. Sepanjang setiap krisis, respons Fadhlina mengikut skrip yang sama: akui masalah, ikrar kewaspadaan, rujuk kepada pihak berkuasa, dan tunggu kitaran berita bergerak.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

PH janji kerajaan yang bertindak, bukan yang memantau. Kamu janji pembaharuan institusi, bukan pemerhatian institusi. Bila IS merekrut kanak-kanak di sekolah Malaysia, tugas Menteri Pendidikan bukan memberitahu pemberita bahawa beliau “serahkan kepada pihak berkuasa” — ia adalah memimpin. Fadhlina Sidek telah diberi peluang demi peluang demi peluang. Buli. Serangan seksual. Kini pengambilan keganasan. Setiap kali, respons yang sama: kami memantau. Anak-anak Malaysia layak mendapat kementerian yang bertindak sebelum tangkapan berlaku, bukan yang keluarkan kenyataan akhbar selepas polis sudah buat kerja. Kalau ini tawaran terbaik PH untuk pendidikan, maka janji Madani hanyalah slogan yang dilukis pada kementerian yang enggan bergerak.

Fadhlina Says Ministry Is 'Monitoring' Schools for Extremism — After Police Already Arrested Six Teenagers Linked to IS

Said by: Fadhlina Sidek, Education Minister

Category: Education

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

On March 12, Education Minister Fadhlina Sidek told reporters in Bukit Mertajam that the Ministry of Education is “closely monitoring student involvement in educational institutions” to prevent extremist ideologies from infiltrating schools. She said the ministry has “left the matter to the relevant authorities for further investigation and necessary action,” and emphasized close cooperation with enforcement agencies to protect students from influence “especially those closely linked to Daesh and terrorism.” This came weeks after the Special Branch arrested six teenage boys aged 16 to 21 on February 14–15 in a nationwide operation over suspected involvement in IS activities.

🔥 Why It Matters

The arrests of six teenagers for IS links should have been a five-alarm moment for any Education Minister. Instead, Fadhlina’s response — delivered weeks after the fact — was a masterclass in bureaucratic passivity: “monitoring,” “leaving it to authorities,” “working closely.” This is the same minister who spent 2025 battling calls for her resignation over school safety failures — bullying deaths, sexual assaults, and a culture of concealment that her own Director-General admitted existed. The pattern is consistent: a serious threat emerges, Fadhlina talks about monitoring, and the ministry waits for someone else to act. If IS recruiters are targeting Malaysian schoolchildren, the Education Ministry’s role is not to observe from the sidelines — it is to overhaul how schools detect, report, and respond to radicalization. Instead, we get press conferences.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Six teenagers were arrested for IS links, and the Education Minister’s response — a full month later — is that the ministry is “monitoring.” Not launching a nationwide deradicalization programme. Not announcing mandatory teacher training on identifying radicalization signs. Not reviewing the curriculum for counter-extremism content. Not even disclosing how many students have been flagged. Just “monitoring” and “leaving it to authorities.” Fadhlina has turned reactive minimalism into an art form. When students were bullied to death, she “took it seriously.” When schools covered up sexual assault, she pledged “zero tolerance.” Now teenagers are being recruited by IS, and the ministry is “monitoring.” At what point does the government acknowledge that this minister’s operating mode is perpetual observation, never decisive action?

📜 Previous Cases

Fadhlina’s track record of delayed, passive responses is well-documented. In 2025, a wave of school bullying cases — including deaths — triggered public outrage and a memorandum demanding her resignation, which she received outside Parliament in October 2025. Her Director-General admitted cases had been “swept under the carpet.” MCA president Wee Ka Siong publicly called for her removal in December 2025, calling her tenure “ineffective.” Analysts named her the Cabinet member most exposed in any reshuffle. Throughout every crisis, Fadhlina’s response has followed the same script: acknowledge the problem, pledge vigilance, refer to authorities, and wait for the news cycle to move on.

🎯 PH Callout

PH promised a government that acts, not one that monitors. You promised institutional reform, not institutional observation. When IS is recruiting children in Malaysian schools, the Education Minister’s job is not to tell reporters she’s “left it to authorities” — it is to lead. Fadhlina Sidek has had chance after chance after chance. Bullying. Sexual assault. Now terrorism recruitment. Every time, the same response: we are monitoring. Malaysia’s children deserve a ministry that acts before the arrests happen, not one that issues press statements after the police have already done the work. If this is the best PH can offer for education, then the Madani promise is just a slogan painted on a ministry that refuses to move.

Timbalan Menteri Pengajian Tinggi Adam Adli Abdul Halim — pernah menjadi aktivis pelajar paling terkenal Malaysia, kini penjelmaan hidup janji PH yang dimungkiri. PH 2.0 kini mempunyai lebih tiga tahun dan secara jelas membalikan janji. Corak sama seperti penghapusan tol, pemisahan AG-PP, dan pemansuhan Akta Hasutan: janji berani dalam pembangkang, tangguh dalam kerajaan, diam-diam tinggalkan. AUKU menyertai senarai ikrar manifesto yang merupakan hiasan kempen, bukan komitmen tadbir urus.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Timbalan menteri anda ditahan di bawah undang-undang ini. Beliau membina kerjaya melawannya. Kini beliau mempertahankan kementerian yang menguatkuasakannya. Jika anda tulis ini sebagai fiksyen, ia akan dikritik sebagai terlalu tepat. Mansuhkan AUKU atau pinda untuk membuang sekatan terhadap aktiviti politik pelajar — seperti yang anda janji dua kali. 1.3 juta pelajar di universiti Malaysia sedang memerhati. Dan mereka mengundi.

Students Flash Red Cards at Adam Adli Over Broken UUCA Promise — PH's Own Poster Boy Turns Reneger

Said by: Adam Adli Abdul Halim, Deputy Higher Education Minister

Category: Education

Date: 2026-02-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 9 February 2026, university students marched to Parliament demanding the abolition of the Universities and University Colleges Act 1971 (UUCA). Their target? Deputy Higher Education Minister Adam Adli Abdul Halim — once Malaysia’s most famous student activist, now the living embodiment of PH’s broken promise. Abolishing UUCA was a key manifesto promise by Pakatan Harapan in both GE14 (2018) and GE15 (2022). In late January 2026, Higher Education Minister Zambry Abd Kadir told Parliament the government had “no plans” to abolish UUCA — a policy reversal delivered without fanfare. Students flashed red cards at Adam Adli and called him a “reneger.” Zambry refused to receive their memorandum. Adam Adli, 33, was arrested in 2013 for lowering a flag at a student rally — an act that made him a symbol of the student movement. PH championed his case as proof that UUCA was unjust. Now he sits in the ministry that enforces it.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: PH promised to repeal UUCA in two consecutive elections. Four years into government, the Higher Education Minister says there are “no plans” to abolish it. This is not a delay — it is a lie. The promise was never intended to be kept; it was designed to harvest student votes. Adam Adli’s personal arc is the most devastating metaphor for the PH government: the student who was arrested for lowering a flag in protest now sits in the ministry that maintains the law that criminalises student protest. The students who voted for PH based on this promise are now protesting PH’s own deputy minister. Zambry’s refusal to receive the memorandum is a symbolic gut punch — when the government won’t even accept a piece of paper from student protesters, the message is clear: your vote was useful, your voice is not. UUCA determines whether 1.3 million Malaysian university students can participate in the political life of their country. Keeping it in place means PH believes students should vote for reform but not advocate for it.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Under UUCA, students can face suspension or expulsion for participating in political activities. The law was enacted in 1971 after student protests against rural poverty — it was designed from the start to suppress politically conscious youth. PH knew this. PH campaigned against it. PH’s own deputy minister was a victim of it. And now PH is keeping it. The Straits Times noted the protest “adds to Anwar’s pre-election reform challenges” — framing UUCA as part of a broader pattern of stalled promises eroding youth support. If you cannot keep promises to students who believed in you, the reform brand is dead.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: UUCA reform has been promised by every opposition coalition since the 2008 political tsunami. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) promised reform but collapsed before acting. PH 2.0 has now had over three years and explicitly reversed the promise. The pattern is identical to toll abolition, AG-PP separation, and the Sedition Act repeal: promise boldly in opposition, delay in government, quietly abandon. UUCA joins a growing list of manifesto pledges that were campaign decorations, not governance commitments.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your deputy minister was arrested under this law. He built his career fighting it. Now he defends the ministry that enforces it. If you wrote this as fiction, it would be criticised as too on-the-nose. Abolish UUCA or amend it to remove restrictions on student political activity — as you promised twice. The 1.3 million students in Malaysian universities are watching. And they vote.

[BM] Bahasa Melayu dan Sejarah Wajib Merentas SEMUA Sistem Pendidikan — Pengkritik Kata Ia Menyekat Kepelbagaian

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Education

Date: 2026-01-21

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada Januari 2026, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim mengumumkan bahawa Bahasa Melayu dan Sejarah akan diwajibkan merentas semua sistem pendidikan di Malaysia — termasuk sekolah vernakular (SJKC dan SJKT), sekolah antarabangsa, dan institusi swasta. Anwar berkata keperluan ini bertujuan “mengukuhkan kedudukan Bahasa Melayu sebagai bahasa kebangsaan” dan memberikan “pemahaman bersama tentang sejarah negara.” Pengkritik berhujah bahawa ini menjejaskan perlindungan perlembagaan untuk pendidikan vernakular di bawah Perkara 152(1)(a) dan menyekat kepelbagaian yang menjadikan sistem pendidikan Malaysia unik. Analisis mendalam CNA menyatakan “politik dan kesukaran” pembaharuan pendidikan Malaysia, menggambarkan bagaimana “bahasa dan agama” kekal sebagai bidang dasar paling mudah terbakar.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Kebanyakan pelajar sekolah vernakular sudah pun belajar Bahasa Melayu — ia merupakan mata pelajaran wajib di semua sekolah bantuan kerajaan. Perubahan dasar ini adalah mengenai memperluaskan mata pelajaran wajib ke aliran yang secara tradisinya menetapkan keutamaan akademik sendiri. Sementara itu, krisis pendidikan sebenar langsung tidak ditangani: skor PISA terus merosot, enrolmen pengajian tinggi hanya 42.57% berbanding purata dunia 51.98%, guru melaporkan pelajar “lebih kurang ajar, lebih gelisah, kurang ingin tahu,” kes buli melonjak 17%, dan bahagian belanjawan pendidikan telah jatuh daripada 6.8% kepada 4.3% KDNK. Menambah mata pelajaran wajib kepada kurikulum yang sudah sedia sesak tidak meningkatkan kualiti pendidikan — ia menambah beban kerja tanpa membaiki pedagogi, prasarana, atau latihan guru.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: Perpaduan nasional melalui kurikulum wajib adalah idea era BN yang memakai sut era PH. Mahathir mendesak inisiatif serupa. Begitu juga Muhyiddin. Bingkaian berubah; dasar tidak. Kiraan politik jelas: Bahasa Melayu dan Sejarah wajib bermain baik dengan pangkalan pengundi Melayu-Islam menjelang pilihan raya. Merit pendidikan adalah sekunder kepada isyarat politik. Ini adalah politik identiti yang menyamar sebagai pembaharuan kurikulum. Isu sebenar bukan sama ada pelajar belajar Bahasa Melayu — ia adalah sama ada kerajaan menggunakan retorik “perpaduan” untuk menyeragamkan pendidikan sambil mengabaikan kegagalan sistemik yang benar-benar memusnahkan sistem: kadar kegagalan matematik 25%, 10,160 calon tidak hadir SPM, dan guru yang terlalu terbeban dengan 30+ pelajar setiap kelas tanpa sokongan struktur langsung.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Setiap PM Malaysia telah menggunakan dasar bahasa dan kurikulum sebagai alat politik. Inisiatif Sekolah Wawasan Mahathir pada awal 2000-an cuba menempatkan sekolah vernakular dan kebangsaan bersama. Ia ditentang dan sebahagian besarnya gagal. Dasar PPSMI (mengajar matematik dan sains dalam Bahasa Inggeris) diperkenalkan, ditarik balik, diperkenalkan semula sebagai DLP, dan kekal sebagai bola politik. Coraknya konsisten: gunakan dasar pendidikan untuk isyarat politik, hadapi tentangan, berundur atau lemahkan, kemudian cuba lagi dengan nama berbeza. PH hanyalah kerajaan terkini yang menggunakan tuas “perpaduan nasional melalui kurikulum wajib.”

🎯 TEGURAN PH: Sistem pendidikan anda sedang runtuh — skor PISA merosot, buli melonjak, guru kewalahan, pelajar tidak terlibat dan menatal di bawah meja — dan jawapan anda adalah menambah lebih banyak mata pelajaran wajib? Baiki pengajaran dahulu. Biayai sekolah dengan betul. Tangani 33% pelajar yang gagal sekurang-kurangnya satu mata pelajaran SPM. Kemudian baru bercakap tentang penambahan kurikulum. Menambah Bahasa Melayu dan Sejarah wajib kepada sekolah yang sudah mengajar Bahasa Melayu sambil mengabaikan keruntuhan kualiti pengajaran bukan pembaharuan — ia adalah pengalihan.

Mandatory Malay and History Across ALL Education Systems — Critics Say It Stifles Diversity

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Education

Date: 2026-01-21

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced that Bahasa Melayu and History would be mandatory across all education systems in Malaysia — including vernacular schools (SJKC and SJKT), international schools, and private institutions. Anwar said the requirement aims to “bolster the status of Bahasa Melayu as the national language” and provide “a common understanding of national history.” Critics argued this undermines constitutional protections for vernacular education under Article 152(1)(a) and stifles the diversity that makes Malaysia’s education system unique. CNA’s in-depth analysis noted the “politics and difficulties” of Malaysian education reform, describing how “language and religion” remain the most combustible policy areas.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Most vernacular school students already learn Bahasa Melayu — it is a compulsory subject in all government-aided schools. The policy change is about expanding mandatory subjects into streams that have traditionally set their own academic priorities. Meanwhile, the actual education crisis goes completely unaddressed: PISA scores keep declining, tertiary enrollment is only 42.57% versus the global average of 51.98%, teachers report students are “ruder, more restless, less curious,” bullying cases surged 17%, and the education budget share has dropped from 6.8% to 4.3% of GDP. Adding mandatory subjects to already overcrowded curricula does not improve education quality — it increases workload without fixing pedagogy, infrastructure, or teacher training.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: National unity through compulsory curriculum is a BN-era idea wearing a PH-era suit. Mahathir pushed similar initiatives. So did Muhyiddin. The framing changes; the policy doesn’t. The political calculus is transparent: mandatory Malay and History plays well with the Malay-Muslim base ahead of elections. The educational merit is secondary to the political signalling. This is identity politics disguised as curriculum reform. The real issue isn’t whether students learn Malay — it’s whether the government is using “unity” rhetoric to homogenise education while ignoring the systemic failures that are actually destroying the system: 25% math failure rates, 10,160 SPM no-shows, and teachers overwhelmed by 30+ students per class with zero structural support.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian PM has used language and curriculum policy as a political tool. Mahathir’s Vision Schools initiative in the early 2000s attempted to co-locate vernacular and national schools. It was resisted and largely failed. The PPSMI policy (teaching math and science in English) was introduced, reversed, reintroduced as DLP, and remains a political football. The pattern is consistent: use education policy for political signalling, face backlash, retreat or water down, then try again under a different name. PH is simply the latest government to reach for the “national unity through mandatory curriculum” lever.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your education system is in freefall — declining PISA scores, surging bullying, overwhelmed teachers, students disengaged and scrolling under desks — and your response is to add more mandatory subjects? Fix the teaching first. Fund the schools properly. Address the 33% of students failing at least one SPM subject. Then talk about curriculum additions. Adding mandatory Malay and History to schools that already teach Malay while ignoring the collapse in teaching quality is not reform — it is deflection. Students don’t need more subjects. They need better education.

[BM] Sistem Persekolahan Malaysia Dalam 'Kemerosotan' — Pelajar Lebih Biadap, Kurang Ingin Tahu, Guru Terbeban

Said by: FMT Opinion / Teachers

Category: Education

Date: 2025-10-18

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU

Pada 18 Oktober 2025, Free Malaysia Today menerbitkan rencana pendapat oleh guru-guru barisan hadapan yang menggambarkan sistem persekolahan dalam kemerosotan yang jelas. Penceritaan mereka terus terang: pelajar menatal TikTok di bawah meja semasa pengajaran, membentak guru apabila ditegur, enggan bekerjasama dengan aktiviti kelas, dan menunjukkan sedikit atau tiada rasa ingin tahu tentang apa yang diajar.

Guru-guru menggambarkan perjuangan yang kalah — bukan terhadap pelajar, tetapi terhadap algoritma. Gelung dopamin TikTok telah menjadi pihak berkuasa sebenar bilik darjah. Dan secara struktur, guru tiada apa-apa untuk melawan: tiada pedagogi yang dikemas kini, tiada dasar pengurusan peranti, tiada sokongan kesihatan mental, dan tiada pengakuan institusi bahawa masalah itu wujud.

Rencana itu melukiskan gambaran profesion dalam krisis senyap — bergaji rendah, kurang dihormati, dan kini dikalahkan oleh skrin telefon.

🔥 KENAPA IA PENTING

Sistem pendidikan Malaysia sudah bermasalah sebelum TikTok. Skor PISA telah mendatar atau menurun selama lebih sedekad. Keputusan PISA 2022 meletakkan pelajar Malaysia di bawah purata OECD dalam bacaan, matematik, dan sains — corak yang tidak bertambah baik secara bermakna sejak Malaysia mula menyertainya.

Apa yang diterangkan dalam rencana FMT bukan masalah baru. Ia adalah gejala yang jelas daripada pengabaian sistematik selama berdekad-dekad:

  • Bilik darjah sesak dengan 35–40 pelajar seorang guru di banyak sekolah bandar dan separuh bandar
  • Kurikulum lapuk yang mengutamakan hafalan berbanding pemikiran kritis
  • Keletihan guru didorong oleh beban pentadbiran, gaji rendah, dan sifar autonomi
  • Tiada rangka kerja literasi digital — pelajar ada peranti tetapi tiada panduan berstruktur untuk menggunakannya secara produktif
  • Penyerahan ibu bapa — telefon telah menjadi pengasuh, dan sekolah dijangka membaiki apa yang rumah telah tinggalkan Kemerosotan bukan sedang datang. Ia sudah tiba. Dan tiada siapa di Putrajaya yang melayannya sebagai krisis.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT

Menteri Pendidikan Fadhlina Sidek telah menghabiskan tempoh tugasnya mengumumkan inisiatif yang sedap didengar — program pengekodan, hab STEM, kempen “kegembiraan belajar” — sementara asas struktur sistem reput di bawahnya.

Mengenai kebajikan guru:

  • Guru-guru ditimbus dengan kerja perkeranian, kemasukan data, dan pentadbiran kokurikulum yang tiada kaitan dengan pengajaran. Respons kementerian adalah menjanjikan “mengurangkan beban kerja guru” — janji yang dibuat oleh setiap menteri pendidikan sekurang-kurangnya sejak 2015. Tiada apa yang berubah. Mengenai disiplin bilik darjah:

  • Tiada rangka kerja nasional yang koheren untuk menguruskan penggunaan peranti di sekolah. Sesetengah sekolah mengharamkan telefon sepenuhnya; yang lain mengabaikan isu itu sepenuhnya. Kementerian tidak mengeluarkan arahan mengikat, menyerahkan setiap guru untuk berjuang sendiri terhadap ekonomi perhatian berbilion dolar. Mengenai reformasi kurikulum:

  • Kurikulum kebangsaan (KSSM) terakhir dikemas kini pada 2017. Ia masih menekankan prestasi peperiksaan berbanding pembelajaran tulen. Sistem ini menghasilkan pelajar yang boleh lulus SPM tetapi tidak boleh menulis perenggan yang koheren, menilai sumber, atau mengadakan perbualan berterusan tentang apa-apa yang tiada di skrin. Mengenai pembiayaan:

  • Malaysia membelanjakan kira-kira 3.2% daripada KDNK untuk pendidikan — di bawah purata global 4.3% dan jauh di bawah rakan serantau seperti Vietnam (4.1%) dan Thailand (3.8%). PH berjanji untuk mengutamakan perbelanjaan pendidikan. Bajet 2025 dan 2026 bercerita lain. Pendekatan kementerian adalah kosmetik. Umumkan program rintis. Ambil gambar. Teruskan. Sementara itu, satu generasi pelajar sedang dipinggirkan secara langsung.

📜 KES TERDAHULU

  • Keputusan PISA 2022: Malaysia mendapat 398 dalam matematik, 388 dalam bacaan, dan 398 dalam sains — semuanya di bawah purata OECD. Prestasi mendatar sejak 2012 meskipun berbilion dibelanjakan untuk pendidikan.
  • 2019: Krisis beban kerja guru. Kesatuan Kebangsaan Profesion Perguruan (NUTP) secara terbuka memberi amaran bahawa guru menghabiskan lebih banyak masa dengan kertas kerja daripada mengajar. Kementerian menjanjikan reformasi. Guru melaporkan tiada perubahan bermakna.
  • 2023: Perdebatan larangan telefon. Selepas pelbagai insiden viral pelajar menggunakan telefon di kelas, seruan untuk larangan telefon nasional mendapat momentum. Kementerian memilih “garis panduan” berbanding arahan, menyerahkan penguatkuasaan kepada sekolah individu.
  • 2024: Pengangguran graduan. Data Bank Negara dan DOSM menunjukkan kira-kira 1 daripada 4 graduan Malaysia bawah 30 tahun sama ada menganggur atau kurang guna tenaga. Sistem pendidikan menghasilkan graduan yang ekonomi tidak perlukan dengan kemahiran yang pasaran tidak mahu.
  • Pelan Pembangunan Pendidikan Malaysia 2013–2025. Pelan tindakan itu berjanji untuk membawa Malaysia ke “satu pertiga teratas” penanda aras pendidikan antarabangsa menjelang 2025. Tarikh akhir telah berlalu. Malaysia tidak pun hampir dengan sasaran.

🎯 TEGURAN KEPADA PH

Fadhlina, guru-guru anda memberitahu anda — secara terbuka, dalam media kebangsaan — bahawa mereka kehilangan bilik darjah mereka. Pelajar lebih biadap. Kurang ingin tahu. Kurang terlibat. Dan sistem tidak menawarkan guru apa-apa untuk melawan.

Respons anda adalah program rintis, sesi bergambar, dan janji-janji kabur tentang “pembelajaran abad ke-21.” Tiada satupun yang sampai kepada guru yang berdiri di hadapan 38 remaja yang lebih suka menonton TikTok 15 saat daripada belajar apa-apa.

Anda mewarisi sistem yang rosak. Itu benar. Tetapi anda berjanji untuk membaikinya. Dua tahun berlalu, skor PISA tidak bergerak, guru-guru lebih keletihan daripada sebelumnya, dan kurikulum masih melayan hafalan sebagai penguasaan.

Jika PH mahu mendakwa ia mentadbir secara berbeza, mulakan dengan satu institusi yang menyentuh setiap keluarga Malaysia. Danainya dengan sewajarnya. Bebaskan guru daripada kerja birokrasi. Laksanakan rangka kerja literasi digital dan pengurusan peranti yang sebenar. Reformasikan kurikulum supaya ia memberi ganjaran kepada pemikiran, bukan penghafalan.

Atau teruskan apa yang anda buat — dan saksikan seluruh generasi menatal menuju ketidakrelevanan.

Malaysia's Schooling System in 'Decline' — Students Ruder, Less Curious, Teachers Overwhelmed

Said by: FMT Opinion / Teachers

Category: Education

Date: 2025-10-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 18 October 2025, Free Malaysia Today published an opinion piece by frontline teachers describing a schooling system in visible decline. The accounts were blunt: students scrolling TikTok under desks during lessons, snapping back at teachers when corrected, refusing to cooperate with classroom activities, and showing little to no curiosity about what’s being taught.

Teachers described fighting a losing battle — not against students, but against the algorithm. TikTok’s dopamine loop has become the real classroom authority. And structurally, teachers have nothing to fight back with: no updated pedagogy, no device management policy, no mental health support, and no institutional acknowledgment that the problem even exists.

The piece painted a picture of a profession in quiet crisis — underpaid, under-respected, and now outgunned by a phone screen.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Malaysia’s education system was already in trouble before TikTok. PISA scores have been stagnant or declining for over a decade. The 2022 PISA results placed Malaysian students below the OECD average in reading, mathematics, and science — a pattern that has not meaningfully improved since Malaysia first participated.

What the FMT piece describes is not a new problem. It is the visible symptom of decades of systemic neglect:

  • Overcrowded classrooms with 35–40 students per teacher in many urban and semi-urban schools
  • Outdated curricula that prioritise rote memorisation over critical thinking
  • Teacher burnout driven by administrative overload, low pay, and zero autonomy
  • No digital literacy framework — students have devices but no structured guidance on using them productively
  • Parental abdication — the phone has become the babysitter, and schools are expected to fix what homes have abandoned The decline isn’t coming. It’s here. And no one in Putrajaya is treating it as a crisis.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Education Minister Fadhlina Sidek has spent her tenure announcing feel-good initiatives — coding programmes, STEM hubs, “joy of learning” campaigns — while the structural foundations of the system rot underneath.

On teacher welfare:

  • Teachers are buried in clerical work, data entry, and co-curricular admin that has nothing to do with teaching. The ministry’s response has been to promise “reducing teacher workload” — a promise made by every education minister since at least 2015. Nothing has changed. On classroom discipline:

  • There is no coherent national framework for managing device use in schools. Some schools ban phones outright; others ignore the issue entirely. The ministry has issued no binding directive, leaving every teacher to fend for themselves against a billion-dollar attention economy. On curriculum reform:

  • The national curriculum (KSSM) was last updated in 2017. It still emphasises exam performance over genuine learning. The system produces students who can pass SPM but cannot write a coherent paragraph, evaluate a source, or hold a sustained conversation about anything that isn’t on a screen. On funding:

  • Malaysia spends roughly 3.2% of GDP on education — below the global average of 4.3% and well below regional peers like Vietnam (4.1%) and Thailand (3.8%). PH promised to prioritise education spending. The 2025 and 2026 budgets tell a different story. The ministry’s approach is cosmetic. Announce a pilot programme. Take photos. Move on. Meanwhile, a generation of students is being failed in real time.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

  • 2022 PISA Results: Malaysia scored 398 in mathematics, 388 in reading, and 398 in science — all below the OECD average. Performance has flatlined since 2012 despite billions in education spending.
  • 2019: Teacher workload crisis. The National Union of the Teaching Profession (NUTP) publicly warned that teachers were spending more time on paperwork than teaching. The ministry promised reform. Teachers report no meaningful change.
  • 2023: Phone ban debate. After multiple viral incidents of students using phones in class, calls for a national phone ban gained traction. The ministry opted for “guidelines” rather than a directive, leaving enforcement to individual schools.
  • 2024: Graduate unemployment. Bank Negara and DOSM data showed that roughly 1 in 4 Malaysian graduates under 30 were either unemployed or underemployed. The education system is producing graduates the economy doesn’t need with skills the market doesn’t want.
  • Malaysia Education Blueprint 2013–2025. The blueprint promised to bring Malaysia into the “top third” of international education benchmarks by 2025. The deadline has passed. Malaysia is nowhere near the target.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

Fadhlina, your teachers are telling you — publicly, in national media — that they are losing their classrooms. Students are ruder. Less curious. Less engaged. And the system offers teachers nothing to fight back with.

Your response has been pilot programmes, photo ops, and vague promises about “21st-century learning.” None of it reaches the teacher standing in front of 38 teenagers who would rather watch a 15-second TikTok than learn anything.

You inherited a broken system. That’s true. But you promised to fix it. Two years in, the PISA scores haven’t moved, the teachers are more burned out than ever, and the curriculum still treats memorisation as mastery.

If PH wants to claim it governs differently, start with the one institution that touches every Malaysian family. Fund it properly. Free teachers from bureaucratic busywork. Implement a real digital literacy and device management framework. Reform the curriculum so it rewards thinking, not regurgitation.

Or keep doing what you’re doing — and watch an entire generation scroll its way to irrelevance.
