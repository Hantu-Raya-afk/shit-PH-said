[BM] Fahmi Kata Rang Undang-Undang Pemisahan PG-PP Perlukan 3 Bulan Lagi — Reformasi Utama PH Terus Ditangguhkan

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 4 Mac, Menteri Komunikasi Fahmi Fadzil mengumumkan bahawa rang undang-undang untuk memisahkan peranan Peguam Negara dan Pendakwa Raya memerlukan tiga bulan lagi penambahbaikan sebelum boleh dibentangkan. Jawatankuasa pilihan khas yang dipengerusikan Datuk Seri Azalina Othman Said akan bersidang pada 16 Mac untuk meneliti maklum balas daripada ahli parlimen. Ini berlaku hanya sehari selepas kerajaan gagal meluluskan rang undang-undang had tempoh PM — satu lagi reformasi perlembagaan utama — kekurangan dua undi daripada majoriti dua pertiga yang diperlukan.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Pemisahan PG-PP bukan sesuatu yang bagus untuk ada — ia adalah reformasi struktur yang paling berkait langsung dengan kebebasan pendakwaan dan integriti sistem perundangan Malaysia. Di bawah sistem semasa, Peguam Negara berkhidmat sebagai penasihat undang-undang utama kerajaan dan pendakwa utama negara, mewujudkan konflik kepentingan semula jadi yang telah dieksploitasi oleh setiap pentadbiran. PH berkempen secara jelas untuk memperbaiki ini. Tiga tahun berkuasa, mereka masih membentuk jawatankuasa dan meminta lebih masa.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

PH mempunyai majoriti besar parlimen sejak 2022. Mereka mempunyai tiga tahun untuk merangka, menambah baik dan meluluskan rang undang-undang ini. Sebaliknya, mereka membentuk jawatankuasa pilihan khas, yang kini akan bersidang untuk “meneliti maklum balas” — setara birokrasi dengan menekan butang tidur pada penggera yang kamu sendiri tetapkan. Rang undang-undang had tempoh PM sudah gagal kerana kerajaan tidak dapat mengerahkan AP sendiri untuk datang mengundi. Kini rang undang-undang PG-PP memerlukan “tiga bulan lagi” kerja. Sementara itu, Peguam Negara sebelum ini menarik balik pertuduhan rasuah terhadap Timbalan Perdana Menteri Ahmad Zahid Hamidi walaupun mahkamah mendapati bukti mencukupi untuk meneruskan.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini adalah corak berulang. Rang undang-undang had tempoh PM diumumkan pada Januari 2026, dibentangkan pada Mac, dan gagal pada percubaan pertama. Akta Kebebasan Maklumat, juga dijanjikan, kekal dalam draf. Pemansuhan Akta Hasutan — ikrar tanda tangan PH dari PRU14 — tidak pernah disampaikan semasa kerajaan PH pertama dan kini ditinggalkan secara berkesan untuk diganti dengan undang-undang ucapan yang baru dan lebih ketat.

🎯 Seruan Kepada PH

Kamu telah mempunyai lapan tahun dan dua mandat pilihan raya untuk meluluskan rang undang-undang ini. Kamu mempunyai majoriti besar. Kamu tidak perlukan jawatankuasa tiga bulan. Kamu perlukan kemahuan politik. Sama ada luluskannya sebelum pilihan raya seterusnya atau akui bahawa pemisahan PG/PP sentiasa menjadi hiasan manifesto, bukan keutamaan tadbir urus.

[BM] MB Negeri Sembilan Akui Pemandunya Langgar Lampu Merah — 'Saya Tiada Pilihan, Tengok Jadual Facebook Saya'

Said by: Aminuddin Harun, Menteri Besar of Negri Sembilan (PH-Lenggeng)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 4 Mac 2026, Menteri Besar Negeri Sembilan Datuk Seri Aminuddin Harun secara terbuka mengakui bahawa kereta rasminya secara rutin melanggar lampu merah, selepas video tular menunjukkan sebuah kenderaan yang dipercayai miliknya menggunakan lampu strobo biru dan siren untuk melalui lampu merah di Jalan Rasah. Bercakap kepada wartawan selepas mesyuarat majlis mesyuarat kerajaan negeri, Aminuddin berkata: “Saya akui kami terpaksa melepasi lampu kadangkala, tetapi saya tiada pilihan, kerana kadangkala ada beberapa acara rasmi berturut-turut dalam sehari.” Dia menunjukkan laman Facebooknya sebagai bukti jadualnya yang padat.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Ini bukan tentang kesalahan trafik. Ini tentang pemimpin negeri PH secara terbuka mengisytiharkan bahawa peraturan jalan raya tidak terpakai kepadanya — dan merangkanya sebagai akomodasi yang munasabah untuk khidmat awam. Di negara di mana rakyat biasa menerima saman, mata demerit dan denda untuk kesalahan yang sama, seorang Menteri Besar secara santai memberitahu wartawan dia melakukannya secara kerap dan mengharapkan kefahaman. Mesejnya kepada orang ramai jelas: ada satu set peraturan untuk pemimpin dan satu lagi untuk semua orang lain.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Pembelaan Aminuddin luar biasa dalam keberanian. Dia tidak menafikan. Dia tidak berjanji untuk berhenti. Dia memberitahu rakyat Malaysia untuk semak jadual Facebooknya untuk memahami kenapa dia perlu melanggar undang-undang. Justifikasi ‘ada anggota polis dalam kereta’ bukan pembebasan — ia pengakuan bahawa peralatan negara digunakan untuk memudahkan pelanggaran peraturan oleh pihak berkuasa. Permintaan maaf yang berakhir dengan ’tetapi pemimpin negeri perlukan sedikit kelonggaran’ bukan permintaan maaf. Ia pengisytiharan keistimewaan.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Malaysia mempunyai sejarah panjang elit politik merawat undang-undang trafik sebagai cadangan. Konvoi VIP, penunggang dan siren telah dinormalisasi sehingga pemandu biasa dijangka mengalah. PH, dalam pembangkang, lantang tentang penyalahgunaan kuasa sedemikian. Kini dalam kerajaan, MB mereka sendiri melakukan perkara yang sama dan meminta simpati.

🎯 Seruan Kepada PH

Kamu berkempen atas kerendahan hati. Kamu beritahu rakyat Malaysia era politik feudal dan budaya VIP sudah berakhir. Kini MB Negeri Sembilan kamu melanggar lampu merah dengan perlindungan polis dan memberitahu rakyat untuk tengok Facebook untuk memahami kenapa dia terlalu penting untuk berhenti di lampu trafik. Patuhi peraturan atau ubahnya melalui proses yang betul. Kamu tidak boleh melanggarnya dan memanggilnya khidmat awam.

[BM] Timbalan Menteri Beritahu Parlimen Wakaf Hindu Mesti Di Peringkat Negeri — Perlembagaan Kata Sebaliknya

Said by: R Yuneswaran, Deputy National Unity Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 4 Mac 2026, Timbalan Menteri Perpaduan Negara R Yuneswaran memberitahu Dewan Negara bahawa lembaga wakaf Hindu mesti ditubuhkan di peringkat negeri, mendakwa ini selaras dengan peruntukan perlembagaan di bawah Senarai Negeri Jadual Kesembilan. Beliau menjawab cadangan senator Dr RA Lingeshwaran untuk lembaga wakaf Hindu peringkat kebangsaan. Bekas ahli dewan undangan negeri Pulau Pinang Satees Muniandy dan bekas pengerusi Lembaga Wakaf Hindu Pulau Pinang P Ramasamy segera menegur timbalan menteri, menunjuk kepada Perkara 15(c) Senarai Persekutuan, yang secara jelas meletakkan wakaf Hindu di bawah bidang kuasa persekutuan — bukan negeri.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Seorang timbalan menteri memberikan maklumat yang salah dari segi perlembagaan di Parlimen — dewan atasan badan perundangan negara. Ini bukan soal tafsiran atau undang-undang perlembagaan yang kabur. Perkara 15(c) Senarai Persekutuan adalah jelas: wakaf Hindu terletak di bawah bidang kuasa persekutuan. Apabila menteri yang bertanggungjawab untuk perpaduan negara tidak dapat menyebut peruntukan perlembagaan yang mengawal wakaf agama komuniti minoriti dengan tepat, ia menandakan sama ada ketidakcekapan atau percubaan sengaja mengalihkan tanggungjawab persekutuan kepada kerajaan negeri.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

PH berkempen sebagai gabungan yang akan memulihkan tadbir urus berperlembagaan, melindungi komuniti minoriti dan memastikan kepimpinan yang cekap dalam Kabinet. Sebaliknya, kita mempunyai timbalan menteri — dari portfolio perpaduan negara, tidak kurang — yang tidak ambil peduli membaca Senarai Persekutuan sebelum menjawab soalan di Dewan Negara. Perlembagaan tidak kabur dalam perkara ini. Lembaga Wakaf Hindu Pulau Pinang yang Yuneswaran sebut sebagai model peringkat negeri sebenarnya adalah badan persekutuan yang ditadbir oleh negeri yang melapor kepada Parlimen. Yuneswaran bukan sahaja salah jawapan — dia membalikkan keseluruhan rangka kerja perlembagaan.

🎯 Seruan Kepada PH

Kamu beritahu rakyat Malaysia kamu akan mentadbir dengan kecekapan, integriti dan hormat terhadap perlembagaan. Timbalan menteri kamu baru beritahu Dewan Negara bahawa wakaf Hindu adalah urusan negeri sedangkan Senarai Persekutuan kata sebaliknya — hitam di atas putih. Jika menteri kamu tak boleh baca Jadual Kesembilan sebelum menjawab soalan parlimen, apakah standard tadbir urus yang kamu tawarkan? Komuniti Hindu berhak mendapat kerajaan yang tahu perlembagaannya sendiri. Betulkan rekod dan beritahu rakyat sama ada kamu benar-benar berniat melaksanakan tanggungjawab persekutuan atas wakaf Hindu — atau kamu hanya mengalih tanggungjawab.

Anwar Claims 'Prominent Zionist Group' Behind Plot to Topple Government — Gets Sued by Daim's Family for It

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told Parliament on March 3–4 that an alleged plot to topple his government involved a “prominent Zionist-linked figure,” foreign bankers, and an international media agency. He named no individual directly but said the suspects had engaged a UK-based PR firm to launch a “coordinated attack” on national institutions before the next election. Police had announced a Section 124B investigation on February 27, widely understood to target the family of late former finance minister Daim Zainuddin — specifically his widow Na’imah Abdul Khalid and their sons. On March 6, the Daim family filed a lawsuit at the Kuala Lumpur High Court against Anwar, Inspector-General of Police Mohd Khalid Ismail, and the government, seeking a declaration that the investigation is “unlawful, unconstitutional and tainted by bad faith.”

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not just a legal dispute — it is a stress test for Anwar’s reformist credentials. The man who spent decades denouncing executive overreach, sedition charges, and politically motivated investigations is now deploying the same playbook: vague allegations of foreign subversion, inflammatory labels like “Zionist,” and a police probe under the same Section 124B used against his own allies in previous administrations. The lawsuit forces the courts to adjudicate whether a sitting PM can publicly accuse citizens of treason-level offences without evidence and then use state machinery to investigate them. If Anwar’s government cannot distinguish between legitimate dissent and actual subversion, it has no business calling itself reformist.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The hypocrisy is industrial-grade. Na’imah’s lawyers called the allegations “false, preposterous, and outrageous” — and then MalaysiaNow dropped a bombshell: in 2016, PKR leaders themselves discussed engaging a US consultancy to lobby Washington and pressure the Najib government on Anwar’s behalf. So when Anwar’s own party did it, it was fighting for justice. When someone else allegedly hires a PR firm, it’s a Zionist plot to topple the government. The word “Zionist” is doing heavy rhetorical work here — deployed not as a factual descriptor but as a politically charged label designed to shut down scrutiny in a Muslim-majority country. Consulting a PR firm is not sedition. If it were, half of Putrajaya would be under investigation.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of governments crying “foreign interference” to delegitimise domestic critics. Mahathir’s administration accused reformasi activists of foreign backing in the late 1990s. Najib’s government used the same narrative against Bersih, Sarawak Report, and eventually against Anwar himself. PH promised in its 2018 manifesto to end politically motivated investigations and repeal laws used to silence dissent — including the Sedition Act and Section 124B. Instead, Anwar’s government has now weaponised the very same section against the Daim family, C4 anti-corruption activists, political commentators, and academics. The reformasi generation is watching its own heroes repeat the cycle.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia Madani — a civilisation built on trust, accountability, and the rule of law. Instead, you are using Parliament as a stage to throw around words like “Zionist” and “banker” without producing a shred of evidence. You are suing citizens for questioning you while your own party’s lobbying history is a matter of public record. If engaging a foreign PR firm is sedition, start the investigation with PKR’s 2016 Washington project. If it isn’t, drop the theatrics, withdraw the inflammatory language, and let the courts do their job without the Prime Minister acting as prosecutor, judge, and propagandist from the Dewan Rakyat floor.

Deputy Minister Tells Parliament Hindu Endowments Must Be at State Level — The Constitution Says Otherwise

Said by: R Yuneswaran, Deputy National Unity Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 What Happened

On March 4, 2026, Deputy National Unity Minister R Yuneswaran told the Dewan Negara that Hindu endowments boards must be established at the state level, claiming this was in line with constitutional provisions under the State List of the Ninth Schedule. He was responding to a proposal by senator Dr RA Lingeshwaran for a national-level Hindu endowments board to regulate temple issues. Yuneswaran said the ministry would “fully support” states in setting up their own boards, citing Penang’s model. Former Penang assemblyman Satees Muniandy and former Penang Hindu Endowments Board chairman P Ramasamy immediately called out the deputy minister, pointing to Item 15(c) of the Federal List, which explicitly places Hindu endowments under federal — not state — jurisdiction.

🔥 Why It Matters

A deputy minister gave constitutionally incorrect information in Parliament — the upper house of the country’s legislature. This is not a matter of interpretation or grey-area constitutional law. Item 15(c) of the Federal List is unambiguous: Hindu endowments fall under federal purview. When a minister responsible for national unity cannot accurately cite the constitutional provisions governing a minority community’s religious endowments, it signals either incompetence or a deliberate attempt to deflect federal responsibility onto state governments — either of which erodes trust in PH’s commitment to institutional governance and minority rights.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH campaigned as the coalition that would restore constitutional governance, protect minority communities, and ensure competent leadership in Cabinet. Instead, we have a deputy minister — from the national unity portfolio, no less — who cannot be bothered to read the Federal List before answering a question in the Dewan Negara. The constitution is not ambiguous on this point. The Penang Hindu Endowments Board that Yuneswaran cited as a state-level model is, as Ramasamy pointed out, actually a federal body administered by the state that reports to Parliament. Yuneswaran didn’t just get the answer wrong — he got the entire constitutional framework backwards and then used it to justify inaction at the federal level.

📜 Previous Cases

This is part of a broader pattern of PH ministers and deputy ministers making inaccurate or misleading statements in Parliament. The Madani government has repeatedly shown a tendency to push federal responsibilities down to state governments when it comes to minority community issues, while simultaneously centralising power on matters that serve its political interests. Malaysian parliamentary history is littered with ministerial answers that misstate constitutional provisions — the difference is that PH promised to be better. The national unity ministry, specifically, has been criticised for being more focused on managing optics around racial and religious tensions than on delivering substantive policy outcomes for minority communities.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would govern with competence, integrity, and respect for the constitution. Your deputy minister just told the Dewan Negara that Hindu endowments are a state matter when the Federal List says otherwise — in black and white. If your ministers can’t even read the Ninth Schedule before answering parliamentary questions, what exactly is the standard of governance you’re offering? The Hindu community deserves a government that knows its own constitution. Fix the record, correct the Hansard, and tell the rakyat whether you actually intend to exercise federal responsibility over Hindu endowments — or whether you’re just passing the buck.

Fahmi Says AG-PP Separation Bill Needs 3 More Months — PH's Flagship Reform Keeps Getting Pushed Back

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 What Happened

On March 4, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil announced that the bill to separate the Attorney General and Public Prosecutor roles requires three more months of refinement before it can be tabled. A select committee chaired by Datuk Seri Azalina Othman Said will convene on March 16 to examine feedback from MPs. This came just one day after the government failed to pass the PM tenure limit bill — another flagship constitutional reform — falling two votes short of the required two-thirds majority. The AG-PP separation was one of Anwar’s earliest and most prominent reform pledges, first announced as part of his institutional reform package.

🔥 Why It Matters

The AG-PP separation is not a nice-to-have — it is the structural reform most directly connected to prosecutorial independence and the integrity of Malaysia’s legal system. Under the current system, the Attorney General serves as both the government’s chief legal advisor and the nation’s top prosecutor, creating an inherent conflict of interest that has been exploited by every administration. PH campaigned explicitly on fixing this. Three years into power, they are still forming committees and asking for more time. With elections potentially months away, the window for passing constitutional amendments with a supermajority is closing fast — and the MACC-Azam Baki crisis has made the case for an independent prosecutor more urgent than ever.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH has had a parliamentary supermajority since 2022. They have had three years to draft, refine, and pass this bill. Instead, they formed a select committee, which will now meet to “examine feedback” — the bureaucratic equivalent of hitting the snooze button on the alarm you set yourself. The PM tenure bill already failed because the government couldn’t even whip its own MPs to show up for a vote. Now the AG-PP bill needs “three more months” of work. Meanwhile, Attorney General Mohd Dusuki Mokhtar — the man whose dual role this bill is supposed to reform — previously withdrew corruption charges against Deputy Prime Minister Ahmad Zahid Hamidi despite a court finding enough evidence to proceed. The conflict of interest isn’t theoretical. It is actively shaping Malaysian justice right now, and PH is asking for another quarter to think about it.

📜 Previous Cases

This is a recurring pattern. The PM tenure limit bill was announced in January 2026 as part of a reform package, tabled in March, and failed on its first attempt. The Freedom of Information Act, also promised, remains in draft. The repeal of the Sedition Act — a signature PH pledge from GE14 — was never delivered during the first PH government and has now been effectively abandoned in favour of new restrictive speech laws. The Peaceful Assembly Act reforms are similarly stalled. PH’s reform agenda reads like a graveyard of good intentions: every pledge is announced with fanfare, delegated to a committee, delayed by “consultations,” and eventually overtaken by the election cycle.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians that Madani governance means institutional reform, not just slogans. You said the AG-PP separation would end the conflict of interest that allowed previous governments to weaponise prosecution. Three years and a supermajority later, your Communications Minister is asking for three more months. Three months will become six. Six will become “after the election.” Malaysians have seen this playbook before — they lived through it under BN for six decades. If you cannot deliver the reforms you promised with the largest parliamentary majority in a generation, you do not deserve another mandate to make the same promises again.

Negri Sembilan MB Admits His Driver Runs Red Lights — 'I Have No Choice, Look at My Facebook Schedule'

Said by: Aminuddin Harun, Menteri Besar of Negri Sembilan (PH-Lenggeng)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 What Happened

On March 4, 2026, Negri Sembilan Menteri Besar Datuk Seri Aminuddin Harun publicly admitted that his official car routinely runs red lights, after a viral video showed a vehicle believed to be his using blue strobe lights and a siren to blast through a red light on Jalan Rasah. Speaking to reporters after a state executive council meeting, Aminuddin said: “I admit that we have to beat the light at times, but I have no choice, as there are sometimes several official events lined up in a day.” He pointed to his Facebook page as proof of his hectic schedule. He added that a police officer is always present in the car, and that his office has sought “necessary approvals from the relevant authorities.”

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not about a traffic violation. This is about a PH state leader publicly declaring that the rules of the road do not apply to him — and framing it as a reasonable accommodation for public service. In a country where ordinary Malaysians receive summonses, demerit points, and fines for the same offence, a Menteri Besar is casually telling reporters he does it regularly and expects understanding. The message to the public is unmistakable: there is one set of rules for leaders and another for everyone else. This is the exact culture of elite entitlement PH promised to dismantle.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Aminuddin’s defence is remarkable in its audacity. He didn’t deny it. He didn’t promise to stop. He told Malaysians to check his Facebook schedule to understand why he needs to break the law. The ‘I had a cop in the car’ justification is not exoneration — it’s an admission that the state apparatus is being used to facilitate rule-breaking by the powerful. If the MB’s schedule is so packed that traffic laws become optional, the answer is better scheduling, not blue strobes and sirens. An apology that ends with ‘but state leaders need some leeway’ is not an apology. It is a declaration of privilege.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of political elites treating traffic laws as suggestions. VIP motorcades, outriders, and sirens have been normalised to the point where ordinary motorists are expected to yield or face consequences. In 2022, a viral video of a minister’s convoy forcing vehicles off the road drew widespread criticism. PH, in opposition, was vocal about such abuses of power. Now in government, their own MB is doing the same thing and asking for sympathy. The entitlement transcends party lines — it’s baked into how Malaysian political elites understand their own importance.

🎯 PH Callout

You campaigned on humility. You told Malaysians that the era of feudal politics and VIP culture was over. Your own Prime Minister once made a show of flying economy class and driving himself. Now your Negri Sembilan MB is running red lights with police cover and telling the rakyat to look at his Facebook to understand why he’s too important to stop at a traffic light. If you can’t even hold your state leaders to basic traffic laws, what hope is there for institutional reform? Follow the rules or change them through proper process. You don’t get to break them and call it public service.

[BM] Anwar Guna 'Zionis' Dan 'Bankir' Di Parlimen Untuk Burukkan Balu Daim — Peguam Panggil 'Pembohongan Terang-terangan'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 3 Mac, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim memberitahu Dewan Rakyat bahawa dakwaan komplot untuk menggugat kestabilan kerajaannya — yang dikaitkan dengan Na’imah Abdul Khalid, balu bekas Menteri Kewangan Daim Zainuddin — melibatkan “kumpulan Zionis terkemuka” dan bankir antarabangsa. Beliau mendakwa sebuah firma PR berpusat di UK telah diupah untuk melancarkan serangan terkoordinasi terhadap institusi negara. Ini berlaku sehari selepas MalaysiaNow mendedahkan bahawa pemimpin PKR sendiri pernah mengupah perunding AS pada 2016 untuk membina tekanan Rumah Putih terhadap PM ketika itu Najib Razak. Peguam Na’imah, Rajesh Nagarajan dan Sachpreetraj Singh, mengeluarkan kenyataan pada hari yang sama memanggil dakwaan Anwar “palsu, tidak munasabah, keterlaluan, dan percubaan terang-terangan untuk mengelirukan Parlimen.”

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Anwar menggunakan istilah paling membakar dalam kosa kata politik Malaysia — “Zionis” — di bawah kekebalan parlimen, di mana dia tidak boleh disaman. Ini bukan retorik biasa. Di negara majoriti Islam di mana anti-Zionisme membawa beban emosi yang besar, menggunakan Zionis untuk memburukkan lawan politik adalah penggunaan sentimen awam sebagai senjata secara sengaja. Pemasaan adalah kritikal — Anwar di bawah tekanan atas dakwaan penyalahgunaan SPRM, pendedahan Bloomberg dan desakan yang semakin meningkat dari dalam gabungannya sendiri untuk reformasi sebenar.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Peguam Na’imah menyatakan dengan jelas: “Tiada sepatah perkataan pun tentang Zionisme atau Israel atau apa-apa yang berkaitan dengannya dalam keseluruhan pelan komunikasi.” Dokumen PR itu, kata mereka, adalah tentang meyakinkan bankir tentang keupayaan kewangan keluarga Daim — komunikasi krisis standard untuk keluarga yang menghadapi rampasan aset. Anwar mereka sudut Zionis di Parlimen, di mana dia menikmati kekebalan undang-undang, dengan penuh kesedaran dia tidak akan mengulanginya di luar. Sementara itu, partinya sendiri mengupah perunding asing untuk melobi terhadap PM Malaysia yang memerintah pada 2016. Kemunafikan ini bukan halus — ia struktural. PKR mengupah firma asing adalah strategi politik. Na’imah mengupah firma PR adalah komplot Zionis. Bezanya? Satu melayani kepentingan Anwar. Yang lain mengancamnya.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Anwar mempunyai sejarah panjang menggunakan konspirasi apabila tertekan. Ini adalah taktik yang sama Najib gunakan semasa skandal 1MDB, apabila dia menyalahkan media asing, George Soros dan sabotaj pembangkang atas pendedahan.
