Civil Service is 90% Bumiputera — Malays Hold 80%+ of Top Posts

Said by: Eurasia Review Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: According to Eurasia Review analysis published in December 2025, Malaysia’s civil service of 1.3 to 1.6 million employees is approximately 90% Bumiputera. Malays hold 77.5% of all posts and over 80% of top management positions. Chinese Malaysians hold just 5.7% and Indian Malaysians 3.8% — in a country where Chinese make up 23% and Indians 7% of the population. The Straits Times reported the “Bumiputera-first policy debate” has taken on a “heady mix of health, education and politics.” When a UiTM professor suggested opening Bumiputera-only programmes to other races to address specialist shortages, the backlash was immediate and the idea was shut down.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A civil service that doesn’t reflect the country’s demographics cannot serve all citizens equally. Race-based hiring in public service perpetuates ethnic exclusion and undermines meritocracy — the very principle Anwar campaigned on. The civil service is simultaneously Malaysia’s largest employer and its most racially homogeneous institution. When 90% of government servants come from one ethnic group, the entire apparatus of state — from policy design to implementation to service delivery — is filtered through one cultural lens. Non-Bumiputera citizens interact with a government that barely represents them.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s “Malaysia Madani” brand promises a civilised, meritocratic, multiracial Malaysia. Yet the most visible arm of the Malaysian state — the civil service — remains 90% one race. Under PH, this is untouchable. Not because reform is impossible, but because 1.6 million civil servants are also 1.6 million votes — overwhelmingly Malay votes that Anwar cannot afford to lose ahead of GE16. The “reformist” government has done nothing — not a pilot programme, not a target, not even a public acknowledgment — to address the structural imbalance. Meritocracy applies to everyone except the government’s own workforce.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The NEP’s civil service quotas were introduced in the 1970s as affirmative action. Fifty years later, the “temporary” measures are permanent, the quotas are entrenched, and the racial composition has only become more homogeneous over time. Every PM — Mahathir, Abdullah, Najib, Muhyiddin, Anwar — has left this untouched. The UiTM incident is instructive: even suggesting partial reform triggered enough backlash to kill the idea instantly. The third rail of Malaysian politics isn’t just electrified — it’s surrounded by a moat.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your civil service is 90% one race in a multiracial country. Your “Madani” brand promises meritocracy. These two facts cannot coexist. Set diversity targets. Open recruitment. Create pathways for non-Bumiputera Malaysians to serve their own government. Or admit that “Malaysia Madani” is a slogan for export — a brand you sell to foreign investors while running a race-based state at home. 1.6 million civil servants, 90% Bumiputera. That’s not meritocracy. That’s a monoculture wearing a multiracial mask.

PM's Own Aide Shamsul Iskandar Charged with Bribery — Anti-Corruption Crusade Eats Itself

Said by: Reuters / Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 4 December 2025, Anwar Ibrahim’s senior political secretary Shamsul Iskandar Mohd Akin was charged with receiving bribes totalling RM176,829 from a local businessman. The charges allege Shamsul used his privileged position in the Prime Minister’s office to assist the businessman in obtaining mineral mining licences in Sabah, among other favours. Shamsul resigned just days before being charged, pleaded not guilty, and was granted bail. He called the charges an “attack” against him. Reuters reported the charges “significantly increased scrutiny on the premier’s pledge to crack down on corruption.” CNA reported Anwar now faces a “graft crisis” alongside “PKR party fractures” and “PH coalition jitters.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This is not a minor official in a distant agency. This is the PM’s senior political secretary — someone with direct, daily access to the most powerful office in the country. The charges allege he was running a pay-for-play operation for mining licences from inside the PM’s office. If the allegations are true, the anti-corruption crusade’s own headquarters was a corruption venue. Reuters framing it as increasing “scrutiny on the premier’s pledge to crack down on corruption” is diplomatic understatement — this demolishes the pledge entirely.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: This is the same Anwar who built his entire political career on anti-corruption rhetoric. He spent decades as Malaysia’s moral compass on corruption. He went to prison (on charges he said were politically motivated). He built Reformasi as a movement against the abuse of power. And his closest aide was allegedly selling mining licences from the PM’s office. The irony is suffocating: the man who vowed to clean up Malaysia now has his own office as a crime scene. Shamsul calling the charges an “attack” echoes every corrupt Malaysian politician’s defence — and PH used to mock that defence when BN politicians used it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s history of aide and secretary corruption is extensive. Under Najib, aides and associates were implicated in 1MDB-related schemes. Under Muhyiddin, political secretaries were linked to procurement irregularities. The pattern: powerful aides use proximity to the PM to monetise access, knowing that their position provides both opportunity and protection. Anwar’s office is not different — it is the latest iteration. The Shamsul case is particularly damaging because it involves Sabah mining licences, connecting to the broader Sabah resource corruption ecosystem that PH promised to dismantle.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your closest aide was charged with bribery for selling access to mining licences from YOUR office. Not a distant state official. Not a minor functionary. Your political secretary. The person who sat outside your door. What did you know? When did you know it? Did Shamsul’s activities never come to your attention despite years of proximity? Either you knew and tolerated it, or you didn’t know — in which case your office management is dangerously negligent. Answer the question. The anti-corruption crusade doesn’t survive its own headquarters being a crime scene.

Anwar Defends Support Letters in Parliament — Then Attacks Opposition for Doing the EXACT Same Thing

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim in Parliament / The Star

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-02

📌 What Happened

When confronted in parliament about his defence of ‘recommendation letters’ (surat sokongan) for government tenders, Anwar pivoted to attacking the opposition by revealing that Kelantan and Terengganu state officials had also issued similar letters. He said: ‘I must call out the hypocrisy — there was not a single word on the letters by the Kelantan and Terengganu exco asking for approval.’

🔥 Why It Matters

Instead of addressing whether support letters are appropriate (they aren’t — they undermine competitive procurement), Anwar’s defence was ’the other side does it too.’ This is the political equivalent of a child caught stealing saying ‘but Johnny stole too!’ It’s not a defence — it’s an admission that the practice is universal and nobody, including PH, intends to stop it.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The entire point of electing PH was that they were supposed to be DIFFERENT from BN/PN. ‘They do it too’ is not reform — it’s surrender. Anwar was specifically asked about his OWN government’s practice. Instead of committing to end it, he deflected to the opposition. The man who promised to clean up governance used his parliamentary time to argue that corruption is bipartisan — as if that makes it acceptable.

📜 Previous Cases

Mahathir defended similar patronage practices. Najib’s government was notorious for political interference in procurement. The whole point of Reformasi was to end this cycle. Instead, Anwar is perpetuating it and using ‘whataboutism’ as a governance strategy.

🎯 PH Callout

We didn’t elect you to be equally corrupt as the opposition. We elected you to be BETTER. ‘They do it too’ is not a reform agenda — it’s a confession that you’ve given up on reform. Stop pointing fingers at Kelantan and start cleaning up your own house. Or admit that PH’s anti-corruption platform was always just marketing.

Anwar's Three-Year Report Card: Steady Economics, Broken Promises, Collapsing Reform Credibility

Said by: Multiple analysts and media

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As Anwar Ibrahim marked three years as Prime Minister in late 2025, multiple analysts and media outlets delivered a consistent verdict: steady economics, shattered reform credibility. GDP growth exceeded 5%, foreign investment hit record highs, and unemployment reached decade lows. But on reform — the actual reason PH was elected — the record is dismal: the Sedition Act was expanded with 90 arrests, UUCA remains intact, ICCPR is unratified, AG-PP separation was sent to committee after three years of delay, Zahid’s 47 corruption charges were dropped, MACC is under fire for politicisation, a journalist was arrested for asking a question, and Nurul Izzah was installed as PKR No. 2.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Three years is not “early days.” It is more than half a parliamentary term. If reform was coming, it would have started by now. Instead, what Malaysia has is a government that performs competent economic management while systematically abandoning the democratic promises that brought it to power. The trade-off is now explicit: you get GDP growth, but you lose civil liberties, institutional independence, and accountability. The question is no longer “will Anwar reform?” The question is: was reform ever the goal?

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s defence is always “we inherited a mess” and “give us time.” But they’ve had time — more time than PH 1.0 ever got. The Sedition Act wasn’t just left on the books — it was weaponised more aggressively than under Najib. UUCA wasn’t just kept — students who protested were ignored. The AG-PP separation wasn’t just delayed — it was tabled and then immediately sent to a committee to die quietly. Every single flagship reform promise has been either abandoned, delayed, or reversed. Economic competence is not a substitute for democratic accountability — it’s what authoritarian governments use to justify staying in power.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: PH 1.0 (2018-2020) had 22 months and accomplished partial reforms before collapsing via the Sheraton Move. PH 2.0 has had 36 months with a larger majority and accomplished fewer reforms. Mahathir’s first tenure showed how economic success can coexist with democratic regression for decades. The pattern is clear: Malaysian PMs use economic performance as a shield against reform demands, and it works — until it doesn’t.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You were elected on reform. You delivered economics. Those are not the same thing. BN also delivered economics — for decades — while gutting institutions. You are now doing the same thing with better PR. Repeal the Sedition Act. Abolish UUCA. Ratify ICCPR. Separate the AG and PP. Restore MACC independence. Or stop pretending you’re different from the people you replaced — because after three years, the only difference is the slogan.

Zafrul: The Man Who Served Every PM — From Muhyiddin's Finance Minister to Anwar's Trade Minister to 'Senior Advisor'

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz has achieved what no other Malaysian politician has: serving in senior ministerial roles under three consecutive PMs from three different coalitions. He was Finance Minister under Muhyiddin (PN) and Ismail Sabri (BN-PN), then switched to PKR and became MITI Minister under Anwar (PH). After being reshuffled out in December 2025, he was immediately appointed Senior Political Advisor to the PM and MIDA Chairman. SCMP profiled him as ‘hot property’ — the man every PM wants.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: In a democracy, political leaders are supposed to represent ideological commitments and voter mandates. Zafrul represents neither — he represents technocratic survival. His ability to seamlessly serve Muhyiddin (who came to power via the Sheraton Move that PH calls a betrayal), then Ismail Sabri, then Anwar (who calls the Sheraton Move a coup) raises the question: what does Zafrul actually believe? The answer appears to be: whatever the current PM needs him to believe.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Zafrul is now testifying in Muhyiddin’s corruption trial as a witness — the same Muhyiddin who appointed him Finance Minister. He jumped from UMNO to PKR when the political winds shifted. PH welcomed him because he brings technocratic credibility and banking-sector connections. But Zafrul’s loyalty is to power, not to reform. When the next PM arrives — whoever that is — Zafrul will be ready to serve. That’s not public service; it’s political mercenary work with a good suit.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a long tradition of political chameleons — figures who survive regime changes by being useful to everyone. But Zafrul has elevated it to an art form. His testimony in Muhyiddin’s trial while serving as Anwar’s advisor creates a conflict that would be disqualifying in most democracies.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You spent years condemning the Sheraton Move as a betrayal of democracy. Then you appointed the Sheraton Move’s Finance Minister as your trade minister and now your senior advisor. If Muhyiddin’s government was illegitimate, why is Muhyiddin’s FM your most trusted economic advisor? The answer: principles are for campaigns; power is for governance.

PH Wins 1 Out of 20 Seats in Sabah — Anwar Says Defeat Won't Stop Delivery

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-30

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 30 November 2025, Pakatan Harapan was demolished in the Sabah state election. The coalition won just 1 out of 20 seats it contested — down from 7 previously. That is a 95% loss rate. Sabah-based parties dominated, driven by anti-federal sentiment and demands for greater state autonomy — particularly after a High Court ruling supporting Sabah’s claim to 40% of federal revenue. Political analyst Lee Kuok Tiung said plainly: “The Sabah election outcome shows Anwar’s coalition lost badly.” Anwar’s response: the defeat “won’t stop delivery” of pledges to Sabah.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This was the first electoral test ahead of GE16 — and PH failed it catastrophically. Sabahans didn’t just reject PH — they rejected the entire idea of being governed by Peninsular-based parties. The results exposed a fundamental disconnect: Putrajaya’s priorities do not resonate in East Malaysia. Anwar appealed the High Court ruling on Sabah’s revenue share, directly antagonising the voters he needed. When you fight a state’s legal claim to its own resources and then ask the same state to vote for you, the outcome is predictable.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s response — “defeat won’t stop delivery” — is the political equivalent of losing a match 19-1 and insisting the training programme is working. If PH cannot win seats in Sabah, it cannot claim a mandate to govern Sabah. The promise to “deliver” to a state that overwhelmingly rejected you is not reassurance — it’s tone-deafness. Sabahans told PH exactly what they thought. PH’s response was to ignore the message and repeat the same platitudes. That’s not resilience; it’s denial.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: PH’s East Malaysia problem is not new. In GE15, PH won minimal seats in Sabah and Sarawak, relying on GPS and GRS for coalition numbers. The pattern is consistent: Peninsular parties treat East Malaysia as a vote bank rather than an equal partner. BN did this for decades. PH promised to be different — the Malaysia Agreement 1963 review, devolution of powers, revenue sharing. Three years later, Sabah got a court ruling in its favour and PH appealed it. The lesson from every Sabah election is the same: respect state autonomy or lose.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You won 1 out of 20 seats. One. Sabahans gave you the clearest possible message: you do not represent them. Instead of listening, you said “defeat won’t stop delivery.” Drop the appeal against Sabah’s revenue share ruling. Implement MA63 commitments. Devolve real power to East Malaysian states. Or keep winning 1 out of 20 and pretending it’s fine — because GE16 will deliver the same verdict if nothing changes.

PM's Political Secretary Caught Issuing Support Letters for Hospital Contractors — Anwar Says a Reprimand Is Enough

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 18 November 2025, Anwar confirmed in Parliament that he had reprimanded his senior political secretary, Shamsul Iskandar Mohd Akin, over support letters listing six contractors for a hospital project in Johor. Anwar described the letters as “vague.” On 25 November, Shamsul resigned after mounting media pressure and calls from Rafizi Ramli for a MACC investigation. A Sabah businessman then claimed he provided RM152,000 to Shamsul — money allegedly spent on home furniture, premium cigars, and tailored suits. On 4 December 2025, MACC charged Shamsul with corruption over RM176,829 in gratification related to the hospital project.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The PM’s office was literally issuing contractor support letters. This is the swamp Anwar promised to drain. Shamsul Iskandar was not a minor figure — he was a PKR veteran since the party’s founding, operating within the Prime Minister’s Department with direct influence over government procurement. When your own political secretary is funnelling contractor names to procurement officers and receiving RM152,000 in kickbacks spent on premium cigars and tailored suits, the anti-corruption brand is not just damaged — it is a punchline.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s first instinct was to minimise. “The letters were vague.” “A reprimand is sufficient.” The Edge reported that the support letters listed specific contractors by name — contradicting Anwar’s claim. Naming specific companies in a government support letter is not vague — it is a recommendation, and anyone in procurement knows exactly what it means. The timeline is damning: Shamsul issues letters → Anwar reprimands privately → media reports emerge → Anwar defends the reprimand in Parliament → businessman reveals RM152K payments → Shamsul resigns → MACC charges him. At every stage, Anwar was behind the curve — reacting to pressure rather than leading on accountability. If Rafizi had not publicly demanded action, would Anwar have done anything beyond a private reprimand? The evidence suggests no.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Support letters from political offices are one of Malaysia’s oldest patronage mechanisms. Under BN, political secretaries routinely monetised their proximity to power. Najib’s aide Jho Low operated with impunity for years before 1MDB collapsed. Under Muhyiddin, aide Ahmad Maslan faced corruption charges. The pattern is consistent across administrations: political aides operate in a grey zone of influence, and PMs look the other way until media pressure forces action. PH promised to end this culture. Instead, they replicated it — different party, same premium cigars.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your senior political secretary was charged with corruption for issuing support letters from your office. Your first response was a private reprimand. Your second response was to call the letters “vague.” Your third response — after RM152,000 in kickbacks surfaced — was to accept a resignation you should have demanded weeks earlier. Implement mandatory asset declarations for all political appointees. Ban support letters from ministerial offices. Publish procurement records for every project where support letters were issued. Or keep discovering corruption in your own office after the media does your job for you.

Selangor MB's Aide Accused of Unexplained Wealth — RM3M Bungalow, Luxury Trips, and Too Many GLC Posts

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In November 2025, a newly created WhatsApp group named ‘MB Selangor’ — comprising state assembly members and leaders — triggered controversy by accusing a PKR leader close to Selangor Menteri Besar Amirudin Shari of unexplained wealth. The administrator questioned how the leader could afford a RM3 million bungalow, luxury overseas trips, and high-end hotel stays while holding numerous positions in state-linked agencies. The group asked: ‘Why is one person appointed to so many positions, as if there are no other qualified people in Selangor?’

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The allegations dropped while Amirudin was tabling the Selangor Budget 2026 — timing that suggests deliberate political pressure, but that doesn’t make the questions invalid. When a single aide holds multiple GLC positions and displays a lifestyle inconsistent with public service income, the public deserves answers. The Selangor MB’s office dismissed the claims as ‘baseless’ and ‘politically motivated’ — the standard deflection used by every Malaysian politician since independence.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: ‘We will lodge a report with the authorities’ is not an answer to ‘how did your aide afford a RM3M bungalow.’ Filing a police report against accusers is intimidation, not accountability. If the wealth is legitimate, declare it. If the multiple GLC appointments are merit-based, publish the selection criteria. PH promised transparency — threatening legal action against people who ask questions about wealth is the opposite of transparency.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Unexplained wealth among political aides is a recurring pattern in Malaysian politics. Under BN, political secretaries and officers routinely accumulated wealth through proximity to power. PH criticised this relentlessly in opposition. Under Amirudin, the pattern continues — different coalition, same playbook.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: If the allegations are false, prove it with asset declarations. If they’re true, investigate. But threatening reporters and WhatsApp administrators with legal action while refusing to answer the actual question — ‘how does a public servant afford this lifestyle?’ — is exactly what BN used to do. You called it corruption then. What do you call it now?

Parliament Reduced to Rubber Stamp — PM Whips Ensure Bills Pass Without Genuine Debate

Said by: Opposition MPs / Parliamentary Reform Advocates

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Despite PH’s promise to strengthen parliamentary democracy, the Dewan Rakyat continues to function as a rubber stamp rather than a genuine deliberative body. Government bills pass with minimal amendment, committee work is perfunctory, and the government whip ensures coalition MPs vote in bloc. The PM term limit bill — one of PH’s signature reform promises — failed in Parliament because PH couldn’t even muster enough of its own MPs to support it. Fahmi Fadzil blamed “opposition confusion” for the failure, when 8 of PH’s own MPs didn’t show up to vote.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Parliament is supposed to be the check on executive power. When parliament is reduced to a venue for the PM’s speeches and pro-forma bill passage, democratic governance degrades into elected authoritarianism. Important legislation passes without meaningful debate. Bad policies go unchallenged. The PM term limit bill failure is the most damning example: PH had the numbers, had the mandate, and still couldn’t deliver on one of the simplest democratic reforms imaginable — because its own MPs couldn’t be bothered to attend.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH explicitly promised parliamentary reform: stronger select committees, an independent Speaker, greater allocation for opposition MPs, and meaningful debate on legislation. Some incremental changes were made, but the fundamental dynamics haven’t changed. The government controls the agenda, determines which bills are debated, and uses coalition arithmetic to ensure outcomes. Under BN, Najib’s government once passed 11 bills in a single day without proper debate. PH criticised this as democratic malpractice. Under PH, the pace is slightly slower but the outcomes are equally predetermined.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Parliamentary rubber-stamping is a Malaysian tradition that predates PH. Under Mahathir 1.0, parliament was a formality. Under Najib, the OSA, SOSMA, and NSC Act were rammed through with minimal debate. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) made some improvements — reviving select committees and increasing opposition funding — but these reforms were incomplete and have stalled under PH 2.0. The East Asia Forum documented how “Malaysia’s reform aspirations clashed with political realities in 2024,” noting that parliamentary independence remained a promise rather than a practice.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You control the Dewan Rakyat. You have a supermajority with BN’s support. You couldn’t even pass a PM term limit bill — your own signature reform. You had the numbers, you had the mandate, and you still couldn’t deliver. If you can’t limit your own power when you have the votes to do it, you were never going to limit your own power at all. Strengthen select committees with real investigative authority. Give the Speaker genuine independence. Allow free votes on reform bills. Or keep running a parliament that functions exactly like the one you inherited — just with different faces in the government benches.

RTM Misidentifies THREE ASEAN Leaders on Live TV — Calls Singapore's PM 'Lee Hsien Loong' at Summit Malaysia Hosted

Said by: RTM / Communications Ministry under Fahmi Fadzil

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 26 October 2025, during the live broadcast of the opening ceremony of the 47th ASEAN Summit — which Malaysia was hosting — the national broadcaster Radio Televisyen Malaysia (RTM) misidentified three Southeast Asian leaders:

  • Singapore PM Lawrence Wong was called Lee Hsien Loong (his predecessor)
  • Thailand PM Anutin Charnvirakul was called Srettha Thavisin (his predecessor)
  • Indonesia President Prabowo Subianto was called Jokowi (his predecessor) RTM issued two separate apology statements. The broadcasting department said it “took the matter seriously” and that “appropriate action had been taken.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This was Malaysia’s moment on the world stage. The ASEAN Summit is the region’s premier diplomatic event, and Malaysia was the host. The entire region was watching. And Malaysia’s national broadcaster couldn’t get the names of three neighbouring heads of state right.

This isn’t a minor slip. These weren’t obscure officials — they were the leaders of Singapore, Thailand, and Indonesia, Malaysia’s closest neighbours. The fact that RTM’s commentators were working from outdated information suggests nobody at the broadcaster bothered to update their briefing materials. In the age of Google, this is inexcusable.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

RTM falls under the Communications Ministry, led by Fahmi Fadzil. This is the same ministry that talks about digital transformation, AI regulation, and building Malaysia’s media capabilities. They can’t even brief their commentators on who the current leaders of neighbouring countries are.

The apology — issued in two separate statements because they initially only acknowledged two of the three errors — was the cherry on top. You don’t get to embarrass Malaysia on live television at the summit Malaysia is hosting and then say “appropriate action has been taken” without telling anyone what that action was.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

RTM has been a running joke for decades. Underfunded, poorly managed, and treated as a government mouthpiece rather than a professional broadcaster. Under PH, there was talk of reforming state media. Instead, they got a Communications Minister who focuses on regulating TikTok for teenagers while RTM can’t name three world leaders correctly.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You hosted the ASEAN Summit. You wanted the world to see Malaysia as a serious, modern, competent nation. And your national broadcaster called three neighbouring leaders by their predecessors’ names on live TV. The entire region laughed at us. Mothership in Singapore ran it. CNA ran it. SCMP ran it. This is your communications infrastructure. This is your Madani brand. Fix it.
