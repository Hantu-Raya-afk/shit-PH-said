PM's Political Secretary Caught Issuing Support Letters for Hospital Contractors — Anwar Says a Reprimand Is Enough

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 18 November 2025, Anwar confirmed in Parliament that he had reprimanded his senior political secretary, Shamsul Iskandar Mohd Akin, over support letters listing six contractors for a hospital project in Johor. Anwar described the letters as “vague.” On 25 November, Shamsul resigned after mounting media pressure and calls from Rafizi Ramli for a MACC investigation. A Sabah businessman then claimed he provided RM152,000 to Shamsul — money allegedly spent on home furniture, premium cigars, and tailored suits. On 4 December 2025, MACC charged Shamsul with corruption over RM176,829 in gratification related to the hospital project.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The PM’s office was literally issuing contractor support letters. This is the swamp Anwar promised to drain. Shamsul Iskandar was not a minor figure — he was a PKR veteran since the party’s founding, operating within the Prime Minister’s Department with direct influence over government procurement. When your own political secretary is funnelling contractor names to procurement officers and receiving RM152,000 in kickbacks spent on premium cigars and tailored suits, the anti-corruption brand is not just damaged — it is a punchline.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s first instinct was to minimise. “The letters were vague.” “A reprimand is sufficient.” The Edge reported that the support letters listed specific contractors by name — contradicting Anwar’s claim. Naming specific companies in a government support letter is not vague — it is a recommendation, and anyone in procurement knows exactly what it means. The timeline is damning: Shamsul issues letters → Anwar reprimands privately → media reports emerge → Anwar defends the reprimand in Parliament → businessman reveals RM152K payments → Shamsul resigns → MACC charges him. At every stage, Anwar was behind the curve — reacting to pressure rather than leading on accountability. If Rafizi had not publicly demanded action, would Anwar have done anything beyond a private reprimand? The evidence suggests no.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Support letters from political offices are one of Malaysia’s oldest patronage mechanisms. Under BN, political secretaries routinely monetised their proximity to power. Najib’s aide Jho Low operated with impunity for years before 1MDB collapsed. Under Muhyiddin, aide Ahmad Maslan faced corruption charges. The pattern is consistent across administrations: political aides operate in a grey zone of influence, and PMs look the other way until media pressure forces action. PH promised to end this culture. Instead, they replicated it — different party, same premium cigars.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your senior political secretary was charged with corruption for issuing support letters from your office. Your first response was a private reprimand. Your second response was to call the letters “vague.” Your third response — after RM152,000 in kickbacks surfaced — was to accept a resignation you should have demanded weeks earlier. Implement mandatory asset declarations for all political appointees. Ban support letters from ministerial offices. Publish procurement records for every project where support letters were issued. Or keep discovering corruption in your own office after the media does your job for you.

Selangor MB's Aide Accused of Unexplained Wealth — RM3M Bungalow, Luxury Trips, and Too Many GLC Posts

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In November 2025, a newly created WhatsApp group named ‘MB Selangor’ — comprising state assembly members and leaders — triggered controversy by accusing a PKR leader close to Selangor Menteri Besar Amirudin Shari of unexplained wealth. The administrator questioned how the leader could afford a RM3 million bungalow, luxury overseas trips, and high-end hotel stays while holding numerous positions in state-linked agencies. The group asked: ‘Why is one person appointed to so many positions, as if there are no other qualified people in Selangor?’

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The allegations dropped while Amirudin was tabling the Selangor Budget 2026 — timing that suggests deliberate political pressure, but that doesn’t make the questions invalid. When a single aide holds multiple GLC positions and displays a lifestyle inconsistent with public service income, the public deserves answers. The Selangor MB’s office dismissed the claims as ‘baseless’ and ‘politically motivated’ — the standard deflection used by every Malaysian politician since independence.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: ‘We will lodge a report with the authorities’ is not an answer to ‘how did your aide afford a RM3M bungalow.’ Filing a police report against accusers is intimidation, not accountability. If the wealth is legitimate, declare it. If the multiple GLC appointments are merit-based, publish the selection criteria. PH promised transparency — threatening legal action against people who ask questions about wealth is the opposite of transparency.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Unexplained wealth among political aides is a recurring pattern in Malaysian politics. Under BN, political secretaries and officers routinely accumulated wealth through proximity to power. PH criticised this relentlessly in opposition. Under Amirudin, the pattern continues — different coalition, same playbook.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: If the allegations are false, prove it with asset declarations. If they’re true, investigate. But threatening reporters and WhatsApp administrators with legal action while refusing to answer the actual question — ‘how does a public servant afford this lifestyle?’ — is exactly what BN used to do. You called it corruption then. What do you call it now?

Parliament Reduced to Rubber Stamp — PM Whips Ensure Bills Pass Without Genuine Debate

Said by: Opposition MPs / Parliamentary Reform Advocates

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Despite PH’s promise to strengthen parliamentary democracy, the Dewan Rakyat continues to function as a rubber stamp rather than a genuine deliberative body. Government bills pass with minimal amendment, committee work is perfunctory, and the government whip ensures coalition MPs vote in bloc. The PM term limit bill — one of PH’s signature reform promises — failed in Parliament because PH couldn’t even muster enough of its own MPs to support it. Fahmi Fadzil blamed “opposition confusion” for the failure, when 8 of PH’s own MPs didn’t show up to vote.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Parliament is supposed to be the check on executive power. When parliament is reduced to a venue for the PM’s speeches and pro-forma bill passage, democratic governance degrades into elected authoritarianism. Important legislation passes without meaningful debate. Bad policies go unchallenged. The PM term limit bill failure is the most damning example: PH had the numbers, had the mandate, and still couldn’t deliver on one of the simplest democratic reforms imaginable — because its own MPs couldn’t be bothered to attend.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH explicitly promised parliamentary reform: stronger select committees, an independent Speaker, greater allocation for opposition MPs, and meaningful debate on legislation. Some incremental changes were made, but the fundamental dynamics haven’t changed. The government controls the agenda, determines which bills are debated, and uses coalition arithmetic to ensure outcomes. Under BN, Najib’s government once passed 11 bills in a single day without proper debate. PH criticised this as democratic malpractice. Under PH, the pace is slightly slower but the outcomes are equally predetermined.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Parliamentary rubber-stamping is a Malaysian tradition that predates PH. Under Mahathir 1.0, parliament was a formality. Under Najib, the OSA, SOSMA, and NSC Act were rammed through with minimal debate. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) made some improvements — reviving select committees and increasing opposition funding — but these reforms were incomplete and have stalled under PH 2.0. The East Asia Forum documented how “Malaysia’s reform aspirations clashed with political realities in 2024,” noting that parliamentary independence remained a promise rather than a practice.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You control the Dewan Rakyat. You have a supermajority with BN’s support. You couldn’t even pass a PM term limit bill — your own signature reform. You had the numbers, you had the mandate, and you still couldn’t deliver. If you can’t limit your own power when you have the votes to do it, you were never going to limit your own power at all. Strengthen select committees with real investigative authority. Give the Speaker genuine independence. Allow free votes on reform bills. Or keep running a parliament that functions exactly like the one you inherited — just with different faces in the government benches.

RTM Misidentifies THREE ASEAN Leaders on Live TV — Calls Singapore's PM 'Lee Hsien Loong' at Summit Malaysia Hosted

Said by: RTM / Communications Ministry under Fahmi Fadzil

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 26 October 2025, during the live broadcast of the opening ceremony of the 47th ASEAN Summit — which Malaysia was hosting — the national broadcaster Radio Televisyen Malaysia (RTM) misidentified three Southeast Asian leaders:

  • Singapore PM Lawrence Wong was called Lee Hsien Loong (his predecessor)
  • Thailand PM Anutin Charnvirakul was called Srettha Thavisin (his predecessor)
  • Indonesia President Prabowo Subianto was called Jokowi (his predecessor) RTM issued two separate apology statements. The broadcasting department said it “took the matter seriously” and that “appropriate action had been taken.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This was Malaysia’s moment on the world stage. The ASEAN Summit is the region’s premier diplomatic event, and Malaysia was the host. The entire region was watching. And Malaysia’s national broadcaster couldn’t get the names of three neighbouring heads of state right.

This isn’t a minor slip. These weren’t obscure officials — they were the leaders of Singapore, Thailand, and Indonesia, Malaysia’s closest neighbours. The fact that RTM’s commentators were working from outdated information suggests nobody at the broadcaster bothered to update their briefing materials. In the age of Google, this is inexcusable.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

RTM falls under the Communications Ministry, led by Fahmi Fadzil. This is the same ministry that talks about digital transformation, AI regulation, and building Malaysia’s media capabilities. They can’t even brief their commentators on who the current leaders of neighbouring countries are.

The apology — issued in two separate statements because they initially only acknowledged two of the three errors — was the cherry on top. You don’t get to embarrass Malaysia on live television at the summit Malaysia is hosting and then say “appropriate action has been taken” without telling anyone what that action was.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

RTM has been a running joke for decades. Underfunded, poorly managed, and treated as a government mouthpiece rather than a professional broadcaster. Under PH, there was talk of reforming state media. Instead, they got a Communications Minister who focuses on regulating TikTok for teenagers while RTM can’t name three world leaders correctly.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You hosted the ASEAN Summit. You wanted the world to see Malaysia as a serious, modern, competent nation. And your national broadcaster called three neighbouring leaders by their predecessors’ names on live TV. The entire region laughed at us. Mothership in Singapore ran it. CNA ran it. SCMP ran it. This is your communications infrastructure. This is your Madani brand. Fix it.

RM200 Million SMUE Fund Allegedly Misappropriated — MACC Probes Selangor Senior Citizens' Scheme

Said by: Amirudin Shari / MACC

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-10-16

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission launched an investigation into the alleged misappropriation of RM200 million from Selangor’s Skim Mesra Usia Emas (SMUE) — the state’s elderly welfare scheme. Multiple officials linked to the scheme were questioned. The SMUE fund was designed to provide financial assistance to Selangor’s senior citizens, making the alleged misappropriation particularly egregious — money meant for the elderly allegedly diverted elsewhere under PH’s watch.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: RM200 million is not a rounding error — it’s a massive sum allegedly stolen from a welfare programme for senior citizens. Selangor is PH’s crown jewel, the richest state, run continuously by PKR/PH since 2008. If RM200 million can go missing from an elderly welfare fund in a PH-controlled state, what does that say about PH’s claim to be the ‘clean governance’ coalition? The fund was supposed to help the most vulnerable. Instead, it may have helped insiders.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH has governed Selangor for nearly 18 years. This isn’t an inherited BN problem — this happened on PH’s watch, under PH’s appointees, in PH’s state. The party that built its brand on anti-corruption is facing a corruption probe in its safest state. Amirudin’s office has been conspicuously quiet about the investigation. No public statements demanding accountability. No suspension of officials. No independent audit announced. Silence.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: This joins the sand mining probe, the MBI asset freeze, and the unexplained wealth allegations as part of a pattern. Selangor under PH isn’t a model of clean governance — it’s a state where MACC keeps finding things. At some point, one investigation is an outlier; four investigations is a pattern.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: RM200 million from a senior citizens’ fund. In your state. Under your watch. With your appointees. If BN did this, you’d hold a national rally. Since it’s your state, you hold a press conference about waste management. Demand a full public audit of SMUE. Suspend the officials. And tell the rakyat where the money went.

Anwar Marks His Own Homework — PM Who Is Also Finance Minister Presents Budget and Claims It's Great

Said by: Opposition / Budget Analysts

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-10-10

📌 What Happened

Budget 2026 was presented by Anwar Ibrahim — as Finance Minister — to parliament, where he sits as Prime Minister. He then defended his own budget in parliament, praised its achievements, and dismissed criticism. The person who wrote the exam is also the person who grades it.

🔥 Why It Matters

In any functional democracy, the finance function has oversight independent from the political leadership. When one person controls both the political agenda and the national wallet, there is no meaningful check on fiscal decisions. Budget allocations become political tools without independent scrutiny. Parliament’s role as a budget oversight body is undermined when the PM/FM controls both the executive and the fiscal agenda.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar criticised Najib SPECIFICALLY for holding both PM and FM portfolios, arguing it enabled 1MDB by removing oversight. Then Anwar took BOTH portfolios himself. When asked about this, his response was essentially ’trust me, I’m different.’ That’s not institutional reform — it’s personal exceptionalism. The same structural vulnerability that enabled 1MDB exists under Anwar.

📜 Previous Cases

Najib as PM+FM: 1MDB happened. Mahathir as PM: the Finance Minister role provided some separation. Under good governance principles, fiscal oversight requires institutional independence, not personal trust. Singapore separates the roles. The UK separates the roles. Most functional democracies separate the roles.

🎯 PH Callout

You were elected because the last PM who held both portfolios stole billions. Your solution to preventing the next 1MDB was… to replicate the exact same structural vulnerability. You’re marking your own homework, and unsurprisingly, you keep giving yourself A+. Appoint a separate Finance Minister or stop pretending institutional reform matters to you.

Tiong King Sing Serves Alcohol at Official Tourism Gala, Then Lies About Who Organised It

Said by: Tiong King Sing, Tourism, Arts and Culture Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-10-05

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

Tourism, Arts and Culture Minister Tiong King Sing came under fire in October 2025 after alcoholic beverages were served at a gala dinner during the Global Travel Meet — an event organised under the Tourism Malaysia banner. Video clips showed beer and wine being served to Muslim guests, prompting several to leave early.

Tiong initially denied responsibility, claiming the private sector organised the catering and that it was “not part of Tourism Malaysia’s official programme.” But former Health Minister Khairy Jamaluddin posted screenshots on Instagram showing Tourism Malaysia was listed as the organiser of the gala dinner, mocking Tiong with “Liar, liar pants on fire.”

Anwar eventually issued a “stern warning” to Tiong but did not sack him.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This is a minister lying on record about his own ministry’s event, getting caught with documentary evidence, and facing zero consequences beyond a “stern warning.” In a government that promised integrity and accountability, a minister who lies publicly and gets caught should face real consequences — not a pat on the wrist.

The incident also exposed the deep contradictions in Anwar’s coalition management. Tiong is from GPS (Gabungan Parti Sarawak), a critical coalition partner. Sacking him would risk losing Sarawak’s support. So accountability takes a back seat to coalition arithmetic.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Tiong’s defence was a masterclass in gaslighting. The Tourism Malaysia logo was on the event backdrop. Tourism Malaysia staff were present. But somehow it was the “private sector’s” fault. When Khairy produced the receipts, Tiong pivoted to expressing “deep regret” without actually apologising.

And Anwar’s response? A “stern warning.” The same Anwar who once promised that his ministers would be held to the highest standards. A minister who lies publicly about his own event gets a slap on the wrist. If this were a BN minister under Najib, PH would have demanded a resignation within 24 hours.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Tiong King Sing is the same minister who screamed “Do you know who I am?” at an airport in February 2025 — a pattern of entitlement and arrogance. He was also previously embroiled in controversies linked to alcohol consumption before this gala incident. The man has a track record of embarrassing the government and facing no real consequences.

Under BN, ministers who lied about public events were torn apart by the then-opposition. PH’s selective amnesia about accountability standards is a recurring theme.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

PH promised Kerajaan Madani — a civilised government built on integrity. A minister who lies about who organised an official event, gets caught red-handed, and faces only a “stern warning” is not integrity. It’s impunity. Either your standards apply to all your ministers — including coalition partners from Sarawak — or they mean nothing. Tiong should have been dropped from Cabinet. The fact that he wasn’t tells Malaysians everything they need to know about where coalition survival ranks versus accountability.
