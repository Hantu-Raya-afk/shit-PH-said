Ini bukan serangan pembangkang — ini ahli parlimen belakang PKR yang merekodkan secara terbuka bahawa presiden parti sedang merosakkan kredibilitinya sendiri. Kini ahli partinya sendiri memberitahu akhbar bahawa keengganannya mendengar adalah masalah legitimasi.

Ini bukan kali pertama keretakan dalaman PH menjadi awam. Pada Disember 2025, DAP mengancam “mempertimbangkan semula” kedudukannya dalam kerajaan jika Anwar gagal menyampaikan reformasi dalam enam bulan. Nurul Izzah sendiri telah menyeru jawatankuasa pengawasan SPRM bebas seawal Februari 2026. Coraknya jelas: tangguh sebagai strategi, kesetiaan sebagai kesunyian.

🎯 Teguran PH

Anda berjanji reformasi. Anda berjanji tiada siapa yang kebal daripada penelitian. Kini anak perempuan anda sendiri menyeru pengawasan SPRM, ahli parlimen belakang anda memberi amaran kepada anda dalam akhbar, dan tindak balas anda adalah menyuruh semua orang bertenang dan menunggu jawatankuasa. Inilah realitinya, Perdana Menteri: apabila ahli parti anda terpaksa menggunakan Malaysiakini untuk mendapatkan perhatian anda mengenai rasuah, anda sudah kehilangan kuasa moral yang anda pernah tuntut.

Anwar Condemns Iran's Attacks on Kuwait — Weeks After Parliament Motion Defending Iran Against US-Israel Strikes

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 What Happened

On March 9, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim called the Prime Minister of Kuwait, Sheikh Ahmad Abdullah Al-Ahmad Al-Sabah, to condemn Iranian attacks targeting Kuwait. He expressed condolences for the casualties and affirmed Malaysia’s support for Kuwait’s security and territorial integrity. This phone call came exactly one week after Anwar tabled an emergency motion in Parliament on March 2 condemning US-Israel strikes on Iran, during which he declared that Malaysia could ’no longer swallow’ Western human rights lectures and PH backbencher Syahredzan Johan called on MPs to boycott all US embassy events.

🔥 Why It Matters

The whiplash is the point. Seven days ago, Anwar positioned Malaysia as a defender of Iran against Western aggression, framing the conflict in anti-imperialist terms and using it to score domestic political points with his base. Now Iran is the aggressor striking a Gulf neighbour, and Anwar has to condemn the same country he was defending. This isn’t principled foreign policy — it’s reactive, opportunistic positioning that changes with the news cycle. Malaysia’s stance on the Middle East conflict now reads less like a coherent doctrine and more like whichever statement plays best on the day it is made.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

You cannot table an emergency motion defending Iran on Monday and condemn Iran the following Monday and pretend both positions are part of a coherent foreign policy framework. Either Malaysia has a principled stance on sovereignty and the use of force — in which case it should have been more measured on March 2 instead of turning Parliament into a political rally — or it’s making it up as it goes. The Syahredzan boycott call looks especially absurd now: should MPs also boycott Iranian embassy events? The March 2 motion was not about principle. It was about performance. And this week’s condemnation proves it.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s foreign policy under Anwar has been marked by reactive, headline-driven positioning. The government has oscillated between courting Western investment and denouncing Western hypocrisy, between engaging China on trade and pushing back on South China Sea issues, depending on the domestic audience. Mahathir did the same thing — his famous ‘Jews rule the world’ speech at OIC in 2003 was followed by continued quiet engagement with Western financial institutions. The pattern is Malaysian — grand rhetorical gestures for the gallery, pragmatic retreats behind closed doors.

🎯 PH Callout

PH, if you want Malaysia to be taken seriously on the world stage, pick a lane. You turned Parliament into a solidarity rally for Iran seven days ago. Your own backbencher called for a boycott of US embassy events. Now you’re calling Kuwait to condemn the same country you were defending. What is Malaysia’s actual position? Is it anti-aggression regardless of who the aggressor is? Then say that consistently. Is it pro-Muslim solidarity? Then explain why you’re condemning a Muslim-majority country attacking another Muslim-majority country. The rakyat deserves a foreign policy, not a mood board.

Anwar Repeats 'Aid Based on Need, Not Race' in Dewan Negara — Same Script, Different Session, Zero Structural Change

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim told the Dewan Negara on March 9, 2026, that government aid and assistance are allocated based on need, not race. He affirmed that the Madani government would “remain fair to all states” and that assistance targets those most in need regardless of background.

This is not the first time Anwar has used this exact line. In August 2025, he told the same Dewan Negara the same thing — almost word for word — as reported by The Star and NST. Before that, the 13th Malaysia Plan (13MP) was framed around the same “needs-based, not race-based” rhetoric.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This is one of PH’s most repeated slogans. It was central to the Madani Economy pitch, and it resonates with voters who are tired of race-based distribution systems that benefit political insiders rather than the genuinely poor.

But slogans are not policy. The question is simple: has the government actually restructured any major Bumiputera-specific allocation program since taking power? Has any race-based quota been reformed? Has any procurement preference been revised to be purely needs-based?

The answer, by all available evidence, is no. The 13MP talked about shifting to needs-based frameworks, but core Bumiputera economic programs, GLICs, and procurement carve-outs remain structurally unchanged. The Poverty Line Income (PGK) thresholds have been updated — a technocratic adjustment — but the architecture of race-based distribution is untouched.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Saying “aid is based on need, not race” while leaving every race-based allocation structure intact is not reform. It is branding.

Anwar is not lying — technically, some aid programs like SARA and certain welfare payments do target low-income households regardless of race. But the big-ticket items — Bumiputera equity targets, MARA allocations, Bumiputera-only procurement set-asides, GLC preferential hiring — remain untouched. Nobody in the Madani government has proposed reforming any of these.

What Anwar is doing is classic misdirection: point to the small welfare programs that are needs-based, repeat the slogan, and hope nobody asks about the structural architecture that makes Malaysia’s economy one of the most racially stratified in the region.

Repeat it enough times in enough Dewan Negara sessions and maybe people will believe it. That is the strategy.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

  • August 2025: Anwar told Dewan Negara that “allocations are based on needs, not race.” Same chamber, same script. (The Star, 25 Aug 2025)
  • July 2025: The 13MP was launched with the framing of shifting from race-based to needs-based policies. (Malay Mail, 31 Jul 2025)
  • Deputy Economy Minister Hanifah Hajar Taib (Dec 2025): Told Parliament the government was “reviewing” the use of disposable income as a fairer benchmark for aid eligibility — meaning even the basic measurement tools hadn’t been reformed yet, over two years into the administration. (The Star, 4 Dec 2025)
  • The core contradiction has existed since Anwar took office: the Madani government promises needs-based reform while relying on UMNO — the party whose entire political model depends on race-based distribution — as its coalition anchor.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You ran on “Reformasi.” You promised a Malaysia where race would not determine access to opportunity. You told the rakyat that Madani meant fairness.

Three years later, every single race-based structural program remains intact. Not one has been reformed. Not one has been publicly reviewed. Not one has been opened to needs-based criteria.

And yet you stand in Dewan Negara — for the second time in less than a year — and say the same line: “based on need, not race.”

If that were true, show us the policy paper. Show us the program that was restructured. Show us the quota that was reformed.

Until then, this is not a policy. It is a press release.

Anwar Won't Suspend Azam Baki — Will Just Let His Contract Expire in May While the Country Burns

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Straits Times sources

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The Straits Times reported on March 9, 2026 — citing high-level government and legal sources — that Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim has decided not to renew MACC chief commissioner Tan Sri Azam Baki’s tenure when it expires on May 12, 2026. This would mark the end of Azam’s fourth annual extension since reaching retirement age in 2023. According to the report, Anwar has “repeatedly stressed to Cabinet in recent weeks that Azam won’t be renewed.” But crucially, Anwar is not suspending Azam now. He is not ordering an independent public inquiry. He is not referring the matter to Parliament. He is simply going to let the clock run out — two more months of an anti-corruption chief accused of being the head of a “corporate mafia.” This comes after Bloomberg reported that the MACC under Azam had abused its powers to extort and manipulate corporate figures; Azam was found to have breached civil servant shareholding rules multiple times, most recently in January 2026, holding 17.7 million shares in Velocity Capital Bhd; former Economy Minister Rafizi Ramli and 8 PKR MPs publicly demanded Azam’s immediate suspension; Bersih demanded resignation and organised protests; DAP Secretary-General Anthony Loke called the government’s response “inadequate”; and a Feb 15 rally in Kuala Lumpur demanded Azam’s arrest.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This is not a win. This is damage control dressed up as a decision. Anwar is letting Azam Baki serve out his remaining two months while the MACC remains under a cloud of allegations that it operates as an extortion racket. Every investigation the MACC conducts between now and May 12 is tainted. Every raid, every summons, every prosecution carries the stench of an agency whose credibility has collapsed. The Straits Times quoted advisory firm Viewfinder Global Affairs: “That is a win that DAP will have to take and try to convince their supporters that they are making progress. It won’t be easy as the damage has been done from over three years of stalled reforms.” Risk consultancy KRA Group’s Amir Fareed Rahim was blunter: “Getting to the bottom of issues surrounding the MACC is a baseline requirement before talking about other reforms.” DAP has brought forward its national congress from September to July to decide whether to stay in the Anwar administration — based on whether reform progress accelerates in the first half of 2026. Letting Azam ride out his contract is not acceleration. It is coasting.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Let’s count the ways this is a failure. 1. Anwar renewed Azam three times. Every year since 2023, Anwar personally extended the tenure of a man his own coalition had demanded be suspended back in 2021-2022. He had the power to end this years ago. He chose not to. 2. PH campaigned on parliamentary vetting of the MACC chief. In GE15, Pakatan Harapan promised that the appointment of the MACC chief would be subject to parliamentary scrutiny. It is now 2026. That never happened. Azam was renewed by executive fiat, three times, with zero parliamentary oversight. 3. “Not renewing” is not accountability. Suspension triggers an investigation. Non-renewal just means Azam walks away in May with his pension, his dignity intact, and zero legal consequences for the alleged abuses under his watch. The difference matters. 4. Anwar called the MACC allegations a Zionist conspiracy. Instead of confronting the institutional rot, Anwar told Parliament on March 3 that the criticism of the MACC was part of a “Zionist plot to topple the government.” Police are now investigating critics under Section 124B — a law that carries a 20-year jail term. This is not reform. This is Najib-era playbook. 5. Two more months of a compromised agency. The MACC is currently investigating the Daim family for an alleged “plot to topple the government.” The MACC is investigating critics of Azam Baki under sedition-adjacent laws. And the head of this agency is a man accused of running a corporate shakedown operation. Letting him serve two more months is not prudent. It is reckless.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: 2021 — First shareholding scandal: Azam Baki was found to own over a million shares in a listed company, breaching civil service rules. NGOs and PH parties — including PKR and DAP — demanded action from then-PM Ismail Sabri. Nothing happened. 2022 — PH election promise: PH’s GE15 manifesto included a commitment to have Parliament vet the MACC chief’s appointment. After winning, Anwar quietly shelved the idea and renewed Azam by executive decision — three years running. 2023-2025 — Three renewals: Anwar extended Azam’s tenure annually despite the unresolved 2021 scandal, growing civil society pressure, and reports of MACC overreach. February 2026 — Bloomberg exposé: Bloomberg reported systematic abuses by the MACC, including alleged collusion between MACC top brass and business figures. This triggered street protests, the “Arrest Azam Baki” rally, and demands from within PH for immediate suspension. November 2025 — Sabah wipeout: PH won just 1 of 22 seats in the Sabah state election. DAP lost every seat it contested. Among the issues: perceived selective prosecution linked to MACC investigations.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You promised parliamentary oversight of the MACC chief. You never delivered. You renewed Azam Baki three times. You ignored the 2021 scandal. You ignored the Bloomberg revelations for weeks. When your own MPs demanded action, you called the criticism a Zionist conspiracy. And now your big move is to not renew his contract — the bare minimum — while letting him serve two more months at the helm of an agency accused of operating like a protection racket. This is not reform. This is running out the clock and calling it progress. Anwar Ibrahim once said: “We will not tolerate corruption in any form.” He has now tolerated it in the most visible form imaginable — at the very top of the agency meant to fight it — for over three years. If you want Malaysians to believe you are different from your predecessors, act like it. Suspend Azam Baki now. Establish a royal commission of inquiry. Refer the MACC chief appointment to Parliament, as you promised. Stop hiding behind task forces and conspiracy theories. The rakyat are watching. And they are not impressed.

Daim's Family Sues Anwar's Government Over 'Zionist Plot' Claims — Calls Police Probe Unlawful and Unconstitutional

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Daim Zainuddin’s Family (Respondents)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 What Happened

The family of late former finance minister Daim Zainuddin — his widow Naimah Abdul Khalid, two sons, and two family lawyers — filed a lawsuit at the Kuala Lumpur High Court on March 7, 2026, seeking a declaration that the police investigation into the alleged plot to topple Anwar’s government is unlawful, unconstitutional, and tainted by bad faith. This comes after Anwar told Parliament on March 3 that a suspect in the alleged conspiracy had engaged an international PR firm to launch coordinated attacks on national institutions, claiming the plot involved ‘a prominent Zionist group,’ foreign lobbyists, bankers, and international media agencies.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is no longer just a political narrative — it is now a legal confrontation. Anwar built his case for the ‘Zionist plot’ on parliamentary privilege and police reports, but a court challenge forces his government to produce actual evidence or face the embarrassment of having its probe declared unconstitutional. Critics are already pointing out the hypocrisy: Anwar’s own political camp sought foreign support, engaged international media, and lobbied overseas governments extensively during his decades in opposition. The lawsuit turns Anwar’s own reformist framing against him — if this government truly believes in due process and rule of law, it cannot simultaneously brand overseas lobbying as subversion when it’s done by opponents.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent years as an opposition leader cultivating ties with international media, foreign governments, and global civil society to pressure Malaysian governments. His allies flew to Washington, London, and Geneva to make their case. That was advocacy. But when Daim’s family allegedly does something similar, it’s a ‘Zionist conspiracy to topple the government.’ The double standard is not subtle — it is the entire structure. This is a prime minister who promised accountability and institutional reform, now using the language of conspiracy theories and securitisation to deflect from legitimate questions about MACC misconduct and elite impunity. The ‘Zionist plot’ framing is a political shield, not a legal argument.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysian governments have a long tradition of invoking external threats to shut down domestic dissent. Mahathir blamed ‘Jewish speculators’ for the 1997 financial crisis. Najib’s government accused foreign interference when the 1MDB scandal broke. Anwar himself was the target of politically motivated prosecutions dressed up in legal language. The playbook is familiar: when the pressure mounts, find a foreign enemy. Anwar once stood on the other side of this tactic. Now he is running it.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia Madani — a civilised, reformist, accountable government. You promised that your administration would not criminalise dissent or use state machinery against political opponents. So what is this? A family exercises its legal right to challenge a police investigation and your response is to let the ‘Zionist plot’ narrative hang in the air like a threat. If you have evidence, present it in court. If you don’t, stop using parliamentary privilege to make accusations you can’t prove. The rakyat did not vote for another government that hides behind conspiracy theories when the questions get uncomfortable.

DAP Brings Forward Congress to July — Will Vote on Whether Leaders Should Quit Government, But Promises to Stay Anyway

Said by: Anthony Loke, DAP Secretary-General / Transport Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 What Happened

DAP, the largest component party in Anwar’s Pakatan Harapan coalition by parliamentary seats, has brought forward its national congress from September to July 12, 2026. At the congress, more than 4,000 delegates will vote in an internal referendum on whether party leaders should resign from their government posts. DAP secretary-general and Transport Minister Anthony Loke told Malaysiakini on February 24 that “the government’s response has been inadequate and does not meet public expectations” regarding the MACC scandal. The move comes after DAP lost every seat it contested in November’s Sabah state polls and faces mounting grassroots anger over stalled reforms, the MACC crisis, and the perception that DAP is enabling a government that has abandoned its own platform.

🔥 Why It Matters

DAP’s congress is now the most important internal event on PH’s political calendar before the next general election. If an overwhelming majority of delegates vote for leaders to resign, it would destabilise Anwar’s government at the worst possible time — just as snap election preparations are underway. DAP supplies the single largest bloc of PH MPs, and losing its ministers would leave a vacuum in Cabinet. The referendum is also a proxy vote on whether DAP’s base still believes the unity government is worth supporting. For a party that built its brand on anti-corruption and institutional reform, the MACC scandal — and the government’s failure to act decisively — is existential.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Here’s the tell: DAP says its MPs will continue backing Anwar regardless of the referendum outcome. So the delegates get to feel empowered by voting for a dramatic gesture, while the party leadership has already pre-committed to changing nothing. It’s accountability theatre. Loke says the government’s response is inadequate, then stays in the government. DAP says reforms are a red line, then watches the PM tenure bill fail and the MACC chief remain in office. Bringing forward a congress to threaten to leave while promising to stay is not pressure politics. It’s a performance designed to placate the base while preserving power.

📜 Previous Cases

DAP has a pattern of loudly objecting and quietly complying. In 2023, the party protested UMNO’s influence in the unity government but stayed. In 2024, DAP MPs raised concerns about Najib’s house arrest deal but took no action. When Azam Baki’s shareholding scandal first surfaced in 2021-2022, DAP — then in opposition — demanded his suspension. Now in government, DAP waited years before its own secretary-general called the response “inadequate.” The Sabah wipeout in November 2025 should have been a wake-up call. Instead, DAP’s response is a congress five months away where the outcome has been pre-neutralised.

🎯 PH Callout

DAP, you told your voters you would be the conscience of the unity government. You said joining Anwar meant reform from the inside. Three years in, your own secretary-general admits the government’s response to corruption is inadequate. Your grassroots are furious. Your Sabah results were a humiliation. And your answer is a congress where delegates vote on a question you’ve already decided doesn’t matter? Either the referendum means something or it doesn’t. If your leaders stay regardless of the outcome, stop pretending this is democracy within the party. It’s damage control.

PKR's Own MP Warns Anwar Risks Legitimacy by Ignoring Nurul Izzah and Backbenchers on MACC

Said by: Hassan Abdul Karim, PKR MP for Pasir Gudang

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 What Happened

On 9 March 2026, PKR’s Pasir Gudang MP Hassan Abdul Karim publicly warned Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim — his own party president — that ignoring concerns raised by MPs and PKR deputy president Nurul Izzah Anwar about the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC) puts the PM’s legitimacy at risk. Speaking to Malaysiakini, Hassan said Anwar cannot keep turning a deaf ear to internal party concerns about the MACC’s conduct, particularly after Nurul Izzah called for the agency to be placed under parliamentary oversight and criticised the “high-handed manner” of investigations including the RM1.1 billion Arm Holdings deal probe.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not an opposition attack — it is a PKR backbencher going on record to say the party president is damaging his own credibility. When your own coalition MPs are publicly questioning your legitimacy over an anti-corruption agency that you once promised to reform, the institutional rot is no longer a perception problem — it is an internal party crisis. Nurul Izzah’s March 7 call for MACC reform via Bernama, FMT, and NST was already a rare public break from the PM’s daughter and deputy president. Hassan’s statement escalates it further: from polite disagreement to a warning about the PM’s fitness to lead.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent decades as the face of reformasi. He promised clean governance, institutional independence, and accountability. Three days before Hassan’s warning, Anwar himself dismissed Bersih’s criticism of the MACC as “unfair” and told Malaysians to wait for reports — the same delay tactic every Malaysian PM has used to kill accountability momentum. Now his own party members are telling the press that his refusal to listen is a legitimacy problem. The man who once led street protests against abuse of power is now the man whose backbenchers have to beg him, through the media, to pay attention to abuse of power.

📜 Previous Cases

This is not the first time PH’s internal cracks have gone public. In December 2025, DAP threatened to “reconsider” its position in government if Anwar failed to deliver reforms within six months — a move prompted by DAP’s wipeout in the Sabah state election. Nurul Izzah herself had called for an independent MACC oversight committee as early as February 2026. The MACC crisis — centred on chief commissioner Azam Baki’s unresolved shareholding scandal — has been a slow-burning credibility fire since 2022. Every time a coalition MP speaks up, Anwar’s response is the same: wait, trust the AG, don’t be emotional. The pattern is unmistakable: delay as strategy, loyalty as silence.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised that no one is above scrutiny. You promised institutional independence. Now your own daughter is calling for MACC oversight, your own backbenchers are warning you in the press, and your response is to tell everyone to calm down and wait for a committee. Here is the reality, Prime Minister: when your party members have to use Malaysiakini to get your attention on corruption, you have already lost the moral authority you once claimed. Either act on MACC reform or admit that “reform” was always just a campaign slogan with an expiry date.

Sports Minister Says 'Learn Lessons and Move On' From Heritage Player Scandal — Where's the Government Accountability?

Said by: Dr. Mohammed Taufiq Johari, Youth and Sports Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On March 9, 2026, Youth and Sports Minister Dr. Mohammed Taufiq Johari called on the Football Association of Malaysia (FAM) to “take lessons” from the heritage player scandal and move forward with grassroots football development.

“The legal process has taken place and we accept the decision that has been made. We will now examine what follow-up steps need to be taken,” Taufiq said at an Iftar Ramadan event in Tikam Batu.

This follows the Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS) upholding FIFA’s sanctions against seven players — Facundo Garcés, Rodrigo Holgado, Imanol Machuca, João Figueiredo, Gabriel Felipe, Jon Irazabal, and Héctor Hevel — who were found to have used forged documents to represent Malaysia internationally. All seven received 12-month suspensions. FIFA also overturned the results of three international matches and fined FAM CHF350,000 (RM1.8 million).

Critically: none of the seven players had any Malaysian roots. The “heritage player” label was itself a fiction.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This is not just a football scandal. It is a governance scandal that implicates the Malaysian government at the highest levels.

These seven players were granted Malaysian citizenship through official government channels. The Home Ministry, under Home Minister Datuk Seri Saifuddin Nasution Ismail, previously stated that all citizenship applications were “processed in strict accordance with Malaysian law” under Article 19 of the Federal Constitution and National Registration Department SOPs.

But if the documents were forged — as FIFA found, as CAS confirmed, and as the players were sanctioned for — then either:

  1. The government’s vetting process failed catastrophically, approving citizenship based on documents that could not withstand FIFA’s scrutiny, or
  2. Someone inside the system knew the documents were fraudulent and approved them anyway. Either way, the government has a serious accountability problem. And the Sports Minister’s response? Learn lessons and focus on grassroots.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

“Learn lessons and move on” is the Malaysian government’s universal get-out-of-jail-free card. It is what they say when they want the public to stop asking uncomfortable questions.

Let’s be very clear about what the Sports Minister is not saying:

  • He is not naming who in government approved the fraudulent citizenship applications.
  • He is not calling for an investigation into the Home Ministry’s vetting process.
  • He is not explaining how forged documents passed through NRD’s “standard operating procedures.”
  • He is not demanding accountability from Saifuddin Nasution, whose ministry signed off on these citizenships. Instead, he is telling FAM — an independent sports body — to “learn lessons.” FAM did not grant these men citizenship. The government did.

The Sports Minister is deflecting blame onto football administrators while the actual institutional failure — a government ministry approving citizenship based on forged documents — goes completely unexamined.

And let’s not forget: the Home Ministry’s earlier claim that everything was done “lawfully” is now directly contradicted by an international sports court’s finding of document fraud. Someone is lying. Nobody in government seems interested in finding out who.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

This pattern — government complicity followed by blame-shifting to non-governmental actors — is a PH speciality:

  • MACC/Azam Baki scandal (2025-2026): Bloomberg exposed shareholding irregularities. Anwar’s response? A “special committee” — not a suspension, not an independent inquiry. When DAP pushed for more, they were told to wait.
  • 1MDB under PH: Anwar halved Najib’s sentence while telling the public the debt was being managed. The rakyat still pays RM50 billion.
  • Lynas rare earth (2026): The government renewed Lynas’s license for 10 years despite unresolved radioactive waste issues. When pressed, ministers said the “regulatory process” was followed. In each case, the formula is identical: bad thing happens on government’s watch → government says process was followed → when process is shown to have failed → government says learn lessons and move forward. Rinse. Repeat.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You came to power promising institutional reform and accountability. You said the days of rubber-stamp government approvals were over. You said transparency would be your hallmark.

Seven men with zero Malaysian roots were given Malaysian citizenship under your watch. They played for the national team using forged documents that your Home Ministry certified as lawful. When FIFA caught the fraud, Malaysia was sanctioned, fined, and humiliated on the international stage.

And your response is to tell football administrators to “focus on grassroots”?

Who in the Home Ministry approved these applications? What happened during the vetting process? Will there be an internal investigation? Will Saifuddin Nasution answer for this?

Until you answer those questions, “learn lessons and move on” is just another way of saying: we got caught and we’d rather you forget about it.
