Cabinet Finally Discusses Azam Baki Report — Punts Decision to Chief Secretary, Refuses to Reveal Findings

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister / Cabinet

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil announced that Cabinet had received and discussed the findings of the special committee — chaired by Attorney General Mohd Dusuki Mokhtar — investigating MACC chief commissioner Azam Baki’s shareholding controversy. Cabinet’s decision: hand the matter to Chief Secretary to the Government Tan Sri Shamsul Azri Abu Bakar to determine “next steps.” Fahmi refused to disclose the report’s contents. When asked directly whether Cabinet discussed the extension of Azam’s contract, Fahmi said: “It was not discussed.” Separately, Cabinet instructed the police, Securities Commission, MACC itself, and the Inland Revenue Board to continue investigating Bloomberg’s “corporate mafia” allegations — including seizing proceeds believed to have criminal elements.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Anwar administration’s formal institutional response to months of crisis over the MACC chief — and it amounts to a bureaucratic handoff. The Bloomberg exposé dropped weeks ago. DAP demanded an RCI. PKR backbenchers warned Anwar risks his legitimacy. Civil society has been summoned and interrogated. And after all of that, Cabinet’s big move is to delegate to a civil servant and refuse to tell the public what the AG’s committee actually found. The message is clear: accountability will be managed, not delivered. By routing the decision through the Chief Secretary rather than making a political call, Anwar insulates himself from direct responsibility for whatever happens next — whether Azam stays, goes, or quietly expires in May.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The Cabinet is the highest executive decision-making body in the country. It received the report. It discussed the findings. And then it said: not our call. This is the equivalent of a CEO receiving an internal audit on a compromised compliance officer and telling HR to “handle it.” The Prime Minister has the power to act. He chose not to. The refusal to disclose the findings is especially damning — if the report cleared Azam, they would say so. If it condemned him, they would act. The silence tells you everything. Meanwhile, the instruction to probe “corporate mafia” allegations sounds tough until you remember MACC is being asked to investigate… itself. The fox is auditing the henhouse, and Cabinet just handed it the clipboard.

📜 Previous Cases

Azam Baki’s shareholding scandal has been a slow-rolling crisis since 2022, when the Securities Commission confirmed he held millions of shares in a public company — a potential breach of anti-corruption rules. Anwar, upon taking power, renewed Azam’s contract multiple times while promising reform. In February 2026, Bloomberg published detailed allegations of a “corporate mafia” operating within MACC. Anwar’s response has followed a consistent pattern: acknowledge the problem, set up a committee, delay action, and let the news cycle move on. The Straits Times reported on March 9 that Anwar had privately told Cabinet he would not renew Azam’s contract when it expires May 12 — but still refused to suspend him in the meantime.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia “institutional reform” and “zero tolerance for corruption.” Your own manifesto said MACC would be made independent and accountable. Instead, your Cabinet received a report on the most compromised anti-corruption chief in Malaysian history and responded with: “Ask the Chief Secretary.” That is not reform. That is not accountability. That is not even governance. It is cowardice dressed in procedure. If you cannot fire the man running your anti-corruption agency while he is under investigation for the very corruption he is supposed to fight, then every word of your reform agenda is decoration. Act, or admit you never intended to.

Fahmi Says Cabinet Wants Stricter 3R Laws — PH Promised to Repeal Restrictive Speech Laws, Now They're Writing New Ones

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Communications Minister and government spokesperson Fahmi Fadzil announced that Cabinet has decided firm action must be taken on 3R issues — race, religion, and royalty — including on social media. Fahmi said the government is considering amending existing laws or introducing new legislation to close what it describes as loopholes that allow certain individuals to ignore enforcement measures. He said the Attorney General has been directed to act decisively, and that the review would examine gaps in current laws that prevent early enforcement actions such as detentions.

🔥 Why It Matters

Pakatan Harapan’s 2018 manifesto explicitly pledged to repeal or reform the Sedition Act 1948, the Printing Presses and Publications Act, and Section 233 of the Communications and Multimedia Act — the very tools BN used for decades to police speech on race, religion, and royalty. Eight years later, PH has not repealed a single one. Instead, Fahmi is now announcing the government wants to write new restrictive laws on the same topics. This is not reform. This is a government that went from promising to dismantle the censorship apparatus to expanding it — while using the language of ‘harmony’ and ’national unity’ as cover.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Every authoritarian Malaysian government has used 3R as the justification for silencing critics. Mahathir did it. Najib did it. Now Anwar’s government is doing it — in the same week it’s using Section 124B to interrogate anti-corruption activists like C4’s CEO, and in the same month it invoked a ‘Zionist plot’ narrative to justify investigating civil society. The ’loopholes’ Fahmi wants to close are not loopholes — they are what remains of free expression in a system already stacked with repressive laws. When PH says ‘stricter enforcement,’ they don’t mean protecting minorities. They mean giving the state more power to detain first and ask questions later.

📜 Previous Cases

DAP’s own Ramkarpal Singh questioned the continued use of the Sedition Act as recently as January 2026, noting it contradicts PH’s reform agenda. In 2018, Lim Guan Eng called the Sedition Act ‘draconian.’ In opposition, Fahmi himself criticised BN’s use of speech laws to silence dissent. The Sedition Act remains on the books. The Printing Presses Act remains on the books. SOSMA remains on the books. Not one has been repealed. And now, instead of delivering on those promises, PH is proposing to add to the arsenal. The trajectory is clear: from reformers to enforcers in one parliamentary term.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would tear down the walls BN built around free speech. Instead, you’re adding barbed wire. If you cannot handle criticism about race, religion, and royalty without reaching for new detention powers, you have become the thing you said you were fighting. Every new law you pass to silence 3R speech will be inherited by the next government — including the ones you call extremists. Think about that before you hand them a sharpened tool. Your manifesto said repeal. The rakyat is still waiting.

Mohamad Hasan Says Travel Agencies Must Bring Home Stranded Umrah Pilgrims — 'It Cannot Be Left to the Government Alone'

Said by: Mohamad Hasan, Foreign Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

Foreign Minister Datuk Seri Mohamad Hasan, speaking at a press conference in Putrajaya on March 11, declared that travel companies and agencies managing umrah packages must take full responsibility for bringing home pilgrims stranded by the West Asia conflict. He said airlines must also honour their tickets: ‘If the ticket is with Malaysia Airlines, MAS must bring them home. If it is an AirAsia ticket, AirAsia must do so.’ He added bluntly: ‘It cannot be left to the Malaysian government alone.’ This came the same day the government’s first evacuation flight — a chartered Malaysia Airlines A330-300 — landed at KLIA with just 163 Malaysians from Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Jordan and Syria, after days of airspace closures and flight disruptions across the Gulf.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is a textbook case of the government reframing a crisis-response failure as a question of private-sector accountability. On March 3, Mohamad Hasan told the Dewan Negara that the situation in the Gulf was ‘under control’ and there was ’no need for an evacuation plan.’ Eight days later, his ministry was coordinating emergency repatriation flights while hundreds of Malaysians — including umrah pilgrims who had been stranded for three to five days at airports in Dubai, Doha, Abu Dhabi and Bahrain — waited for someone to get them home. The pivot from ’no evacuation needed’ to ’travel agencies must handle it’ is not reassurance — it is damage control dressed as delegation.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The Foreign Ministry exists precisely for moments like this: when Malaysian citizens are stranded abroad during a conflict and need their government to act. Telling travel agencies and airlines to sort it out is not crisis leadership — it is passing the bill. Many of these pilgrims are elderly, travelling on budget packages, and do not have the leverage to demand rescheduled flights from airlines whose operations are in chaos. The government evacuated 163 people on one chartered flight and then turned to the microphone to say, essentially, ’the rest of you are someone else’s problem.’ Meanwhile, the National Security Council coordinated the evacuation under PM Anwar’s directive — so the government clearly has the machinery. It just chose to limit its use and outsource the rest.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of slow or inadequate evacuation responses. During the 2023 Sudan crisis, Malaysians were stranded for days before Wisma Putra mobilised. During COVID-19, repatriation flights were chaotic, with competing agencies and unclear responsibility chains leaving citizens in limbo. The playbook is familiar: delay, then deflect. Mohamad Hasan’s ‘under control’ assurance on March 3 echoes every Malaysian foreign minister who has ever downplayed a developing crisis until it became undeniable — then blamed someone else for the gaps.

🎯 PH Callout

PH came to power promising a government that puts rakyat first — ‘Kerajaan Madani,’ a compassionate, responsive administration. When your citizens are sleeping on airport floors in the Gulf because airspace is shut and missiles are flying, telling travel agencies to figure it out is not compassion. It is bureaucratic cowardice. Evacuate your people first. Sort out who pays later. That is what a government that means its slogans would do.

PH Backbenchers Call for Snap Election Dressed Up as 'Reform' — Hold GE16 With All State Polls, Save Money, They Say

Said by: Azli Yusof (Amanah-Shah Alam) / Jimmy Puah (PKR-Tebrau)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, two Pakatan Harapan backbenchers — Shah Alam MP Azli Yusof (Amanah) and Tebrau MP Jimmy Puah (PKR) — called for all state assembly elections to be held concurrently with the 16th General Election. Speaking to FMT, both MPs argued that separate elections lead to lower voter turnout, waste public money, and produce state governments with weaker mandates. Azli said separate polls are “unfair to voters, especially those who live far away from where they vote.” Puah called it “simply too tiring for people to come out and vote twice.” Putrajaya spent RM725 million on GE15 in 2022 and a further RM420 million on six subsequent state elections in 2023.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a neutral policy suggestion. It is PH’s latest move in a coordinated signal — following DAP secretary-general Anthony Loke’s identical proposal last year and PH Johor chairman Aminolhuda Hassan’s recent push — to align the political calendar for a snap general election. By calling for simultaneous polls, PH backbenchers are doing the government’s messaging work: normalizing the idea of an early dissolution while packaging it as cost-saving reform. The real audience is not voters who are tired of going to polling stations — it is UMNO, GPS, and other coalition partners who need to be brought on board before the PM pulls the trigger.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH did not care about “voter fatigue” when it suited them. In 2022, six PH-governed states chose to delay their state elections rather than hold them with GE15, citing monsoon concerns. Now, suddenly, the same coalition wants everything held together — but only because the timing benefits them. The opposition is fractured after the Bersatu-PAS split, the economy is holding, and Anwar’s approval is still intact. This is not reform. This is election engineering with a reformist paint job. If PH were serious about alignment, they would legislate fixed election dates — not send backbenchers to float trial balloons in FMT.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of strategic election timing. Mahathir called snap elections when conditions favoured him. Najib dissolved Parliament early in 2013 and 2018. Even within the current cycle, the Sabah, Melaka, and Johor snap elections of 2020–2022 were all called for political advantage, not democratic principle. PH itself benefited from the chaos of misaligned polls — the August 2023 state elections became a de facto referendum on the unity government. The pattern is always the same: whoever controls the timing controls the narrative.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised institutional reform. You promised that governance would no longer be about political convenience. Yet here you are, using backbenchers to soften the ground for a snap election while pretending it is about saving the rakyat money. If you actually believe in synchronized elections, table a constitutional amendment to fix election dates — the way mature democracies do. But you will not, because the whole point is to keep the power to dissolve in the PM’s pocket. Stop dressing up electoral strategy as reform. Malaysians can tell the difference.

Police Question C4 Anti-Corruption CEO Under Section 124B — Anwar's Government Uses 'Plot to Topple' Probe to Interrogate Civil Society

Said by: Bukit Aman (Royal Malaysia Police) / C4 Center (Pushpan Murugiah, CEO)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 10, 2026, the Center to Combat Corruption and Cronyism (C4) CEO Pushpan Murugiah was summoned to Bukit Aman and questioned for approximately 90 minutes under Section 124B of the Penal Code — activities detrimental to parliamentary democracy. The questioning was linked to the police investigation into an alleged plot to topple the government, reportedly because Pushpan’s name appeared in an email deemed relevant to the probe. But the interrogation went far beyond that email. Police questioned C4’s sources of funding, whether the organisation had received instructions to issue statements, and — remarkably — challenged C4’s right to comment on matters of national governance. C4 confirmed that several other political commentators and academics had also been summoned to Bukit Aman under the same investigation.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a routine police inquiry. This is the machinery of state — operating under a government that came to power on a reform mandate — being deployed to scrutinise, pressure, and delegitimise an independent anti-corruption watchdog. Section 124B is a serious charge. Using it to question a civil society leader about their organisation’s funding, autonomy, and standing to comment on public affairs is a textbook chilling effect. It signals to every NGO, academic, and political commentator in Malaysia: criticise the government’s handling of the MACC scandal, and you may find yourself in a police interview room explaining why you think you have the right to speak. This is the same playbook that Malaysians rejected when BN did it. The fact that PH is now running it should alarm every voter who believed the reformasi promise.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim built his entire political career on the claim that he was persecuted by a government that weaponised institutions against critics. He spent years in prison. He rallied millions with the promise that under PH, the police would not be used to silence dissent. Now his government is hauling in the CEO of an anti-corruption NGO and asking — with a straight face — whether C4 has the standing to comment on corruption. The hypocrisy is breathtaking. C4 has been non-partisan since 2014. It criticised BN, it criticised PN, and now it criticises PH — and this is the administration that sends the police. The “plot to topple the government” narrative has become a catch-all justification for intimidating anyone who asks uncomfortable questions about Azam Baki, the MACC, and the corporate mafia allegations. If your anti-corruption credentials can’t survive scrutiny from an anti-corruption NGO, the problem is not the NGO.

📜 Previous Cases

This pattern is not new in Malaysian politics — it is simply new for PH. Under Najib, civil society leaders and journalists were routinely summoned under the Sedition Act, SOSMA, and the Communications and Multimedia Act for criticising 1MDB and government corruption. Under Muhyiddin, the Emergency Ordinance was used to suspend Parliament and silence dissent. Anwar himself was the most prominent victim of institutional weaponisation — twice jailed on charges widely seen as politically motivated. Now, under his own government, Section 124B is being stretched to cover the activities of NGOs whose only offence is demanding accountability from the MACC. Bersih was told to stop criticising MACC “during Ramadan.” DAP’s own youth wing warned of a “climate of fear.” And now C4’s CEO is sitting in Bukit Aman explaining why an anti-corruption organisation dares to talk about corruption.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised institutional independence. You promised that the police would never again be used as a political weapon. Now your police are questioning the CEO of an anti-corruption NGO about their right to speak — under the same “plot to topple” investigation that you’ve used to smear Daim’s family, attack Bloomberg, and deflect from the Azam Baki scandal. Every hour that Anwar’s government spends intimidating civil society instead of reforming the MACC is another hour that proves the Madani brand is hollow. If you want Malaysians to believe in your reform agenda, start by not behaving exactly like the governments you replaced.

Sabah Rejected PH at the Ballot Box — Now Mustapha Sakmud Says He'll Be Their Voice in Putrajaya Anyway

Said by: Mustapha Sakmud, Sabah PH Chairman / Sabah and Sarawak Affairs Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Sabah PH chairman and Sabah and Sarawak Affairs Minister Mustapha Sakmud told FMT that Sabah PH will serve as a “conduit” to ensure state issues are voiced at the federal level. He said he intends to leverage his ministerial appointment — made on February 15 — to bring Sabahan concerns to Cabinet meetings and government agencies. “This is the role we aim to play, and we hope the people of Sabah will appreciate it when their fundamental needs are addressed,” he said.

🔥 Why It Matters

PH was annihilated in the November 29, 2025 Sabah state election. PKR scraped through with a single seat. DAP won nothing. GRS took 29 seats, Warisan took 25, and Sabahans made it abundantly clear they did not want PH representing them at the state level. Yet barely three months later, Anwar handed Mustapha a federal ministerial portfolio — Sabah and Sarawak Affairs — effectively using the federal Cabinet to insert PH into Sabah’s political space through the back door. This is not democratic representation. This is patronage as a substitute for a mandate.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

You cannot lose an election this badly and then claim you are the people’s voice. PH won one seat in the entire Sabah state assembly. That is not a conduit — that is a rejection letter. Mustapha’s argument boils down to: “Sabahans didn’t vote for us, but we got a Cabinet post, so we’ll represent them anyway.” This is the exact kind of unearned political entitlement PH once accused BN of practising. The appointment rewards loyalty to Anwar, not service to Sabah. If Sabahans wanted PH to speak for them, they would have voted for PH. They didn’t.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows a long Malaysian tradition of using federal appointments to compensate for electoral losses at the state level. BN did it for decades in Sabah and Sarawak — parachuting federal figures into state politics through GLCs, agencies, and development corridors. PH, in opposition, railed against this exact practice. Now in government, they’ve adopted the same playbook. Anwar’s December 2025 Cabinet reshuffle — which created the Sabah and Sarawak Affairs portfolio — was widely seen as a damage-control exercise after PH’s Sabah wipeout. The pattern is clear: when voters reject you, buy your way back in through Putrajaya.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised that power would come from the people, not from Putrajaya. You told Sabahans for years that BN was using federal patronage to override their democratic choices. And now you’ve handed a Cabinet seat to a man whose coalition won one seat in an entire state election and told him to go represent the people who rejected him. If “the people of Sabah will appreciate it,” as Mustapha claims, then put it to a test — contest a by-election in Sabah on PH’s record. Otherwise, stop pretending a ministerial appointment is a mandate.

DAP, anda berjanji reformasi. Anda berjanji akauntabiliti institusi. Tetapi apabila Bloomberg mendedahkan cerita antirasuah terbesar dalam politik Malaysia sejak 1MDB, naluri pertama anda adalah melindungi PM, bukan orang awam. Tuntutan RCI yang datang berminggu lewat dan sengaja mengelak peranan PM bukan reformasi — ia teater. Jika anda mahu buktikan PAS salah, mulakan dengan menangani dakwaan Bloomberg tentang campur tangan Anwar dalam SPRM.

[BM] Rafizi Senyap-Senyap Tarik Balik Kes Bankrap NFCorp — Pejuang Antirasuah Berdamai Dengan Orang Yang Pernah Didedahkan

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, Pandan MP / Former Economy Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-10

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Rafizi Ramli, MP Pandan dan bekas Menteri Ekonomi yang membina kerjaya politik atas pendedahan skandal Perbadanan Fidlot Nasional (NFCorp), telah secara senyap menarik balik prosiding kebankrapan dan penggulungannya terhadap pengarah NFCorp Datuk Seri Dr Mohamad Salleh Ismail dan syarikat itu sendiri. Notis Pemberhentian difailkan pada 3 Mac, dan Mahkamah Tinggi membatalkan kes pada 10 Mac tanpa perintah kos. Peguam Rafizi, Razlan Hadri Zulkifli, mengesahkan perkara itu “diselesaikan secara baik.” Kes ini berpunca daripada kos yang diperintahkan mahkamah kepada Rafizi selepas Mahkamah Persekutuan membenarkan rayuannya pada April 2022 — NFCorp dan Salleh membayar RM300,000 pada Disember 2022 tetapi berhenti membayar baki faedah tertunggak.

Seluruh jenama politik Rafizi dibina atas pendedahan NFCorp. Pada 2012, beliau mendedahkan pinjaman lembut RM250 juta kerajaan yang dimaksudkan untuk projek penternakan lembu negara yang sebaliknya dibelanjakan untuk kondominium mewah dan perbelanjaan peribadi. Masa yang mustahil untuk diabaikan.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Inilah lelaki yang memberitahu rakyat Malaysia beliau tidak akan pernah berkompromi terhadap akauntabiliti. Lelaki yang berkata rasuah mesti dikejar tanpa henti. Dan kini beliau meninggalkan wang yang mahkamah katakan miliknya — bukan kerana beliau murah hati, tetapi kerana kalkulus politik telah berubah. Rafizi sedang disiasat SPRM. Bekas pembantunya telah melarikan diri ke UK. Kedudukan politiknya dalam PKR lemah selepas digugurkan daripada Kabinet. Apabila anda tidak lagi dalam kedudukan kuat, anda tidak mencari gaduh — anda berunding. Itu bukan prinsip. Itu kelangsungan hidup.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Rafizi mempunyai corak keagresifan terpilih. Beliau tidak kenal lelah menentang skandal era BN — NFCorp, 1MDB, Tabung Haji — apabila PH dalam pembangkang. Tetapi apabila PH berkuasa, semangat itu pudar. Masalah SPRM terbarunya atas perjanjian cip ARM Holdings, di mana beliau didakwa memudahkan pelaburan RM1.11 bilion dalam syarikat dengan asas yang dipersoalkan, menunjukkan seorang lelaki yang telah menyimpang jauh daripada akar pemberi maklumatnya.

🎯 Teguran PH

PH, anda memberitahu rakyat Malaysia bahawa Reformasi bermakna akauntabiliti tanpa pengecualian. Rafizi adalah anak poster anda — pemberi maklumat berani yang dipenjarakan kerana mendedahkan rasuah. Kini beliau berunding dengan orang yang didedahkan, menggugurkan hutang yang diperintahkan mahkamah, dan membuat perjanjian baik sementara siasatan SPRMnya sendiri berjalan. Jika pejuang antirasuah terbaik anda tidak mampu menyelesaikan kes yang sudah dimenangi, apa sebenarnya pendirian PH?
