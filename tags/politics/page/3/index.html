238 Political Appointments to FSBs and GLCs Under Anwar — IDEAS Report Exposes 'Connections Over Competence'

Said by: IDEAS Report / Anwar Ibrahim Administration

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 What Happened

The Institute for Democracy and Economic Affairs (IDEAS) released a report titled Who Sits at the Table: From Political to Public Appointees in Malaysia’s Federal Statutory Bodies on March 6, 2026, revealing that 238 political appointments were made to federal statutory bodies (FSBs) and government-linked companies (GLCs) under Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim’s administration between November 2022 and October 2025. Of these, 222 were to FSBs and 16 to GLCs. The report reviewed 135 agencies and found that 78% of chairperson positions were filled based on political connections rather than competence. Most lacked clearly defined qualification criteria. The appointments were concentrated in key ministries, and the governance provisions around them were described as inconsistent and fragmented.

DAPSY Chairman Warns MACC Creating 'Climate of Fear' — PH's Own Youth Wing Breaks Ranks on Enforcement Tactics

Said by: Woo Kah Leong, DAPSY National Chairman

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-05

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 5 March 2026, DAPSY National Chairman YB Woo Kah Leong issued a formal seven-point press statement criticising the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC) for conducting what he called disproportionate and intimidating investigations.

The key points of the statement:

  • DAPSY is “deeply concerned” about how MACC has conducted recent high-profile investigations
  • The individual being investigated is “neither a fugitive financier like Jho Low nor a powerful businessman linked to corporate mafia allegations”
  • The high-profile nature of the investigation “appears disproportionate and unnecessary”
  • MACC “must not engage in political witch-hunting against an ordinary citizen”
  • If enforcement powers are exercised selectively, “the Unity Government will inevitably bear the political consequences”
  • Anti-corruption efforts must “strengthen democracy and ensure good governance, instead of weakening it by creating the perception that government machinery is used to pressure or silence individuals” The statement is widely understood to refer to MACC’s search for a former aide to ex-Economy Minister Rafizi Ramli, identified in various reports as Chai. The MACC had launched a high-profile search operation that drew public attention and criticism.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This is not an opposition attack. This is DAP’s own youth wing — a key component of the ruling Pakatan Harapan coalition — publicly accusing the government’s anti-corruption agency of creating a “climate of fear” and engaging in “political witch-hunting.”
