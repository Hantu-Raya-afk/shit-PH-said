Nurul Izzah Defeats Rafizi by 6,000 Votes for PKR Deputy President — Daddy's Party, Daddy's Rules

Said by: PKR Party Elections / Nurul Izzah Anwar

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-05-23

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 23 May 2025, Nurul Izzah Anwar — the eldest daughter of Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim — defeated incumbent deputy president Rafizi Ramli in PKR’s internal elections by a commanding margin: 9,803 votes to 3,866.

The result was never in doubt. Over 200 of PKR’s 222 divisions had declared support for Nurul Izzah before the vote even took place. The election was held in Johor Bahru, with party president Anwar Ibrahim retaining the top post unchallenged.

Days later, Rafizi resigned as Economy Minister, saying he “no longer possesses the party’s mandate.” His ally, Natural Resources Minister Nik Nazmi, also quit.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

The PM’s daughter is now the second-most powerful person in the party that runs Malaysia. This isn’t just a party election — it’s a dynastic consolidation.

Anwar Ibrahim spent 25 years preaching reformasi. He promised to break the cycle of patronage, nepotism, and elite capture that defined Malaysian politics. Now his daughter holds the No. 2 party position, his wife is the president of PKR Wanita, and his political opponents within the party have been systematically sidelined.

The SCMP reported it as a “nepotism row.” Bloomberg called it a “blow to Anwar’s government.” Only in PKR would anyone pretend this was a normal democratic contest.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Let’s be clear about what happened: the PM’s daughter ran against his own Economy Minister for control of the party, and the party apparatus delivered a landslide for the boss’s kid. Over 200 divisions declared for Nurul Izzah before a single ballot was cast. That’s not democracy — that’s a coronation.

Rafizi — the man who built PKR’s policy credibility, who designed PADU, who pushed subsidy rationalisation — was rewarded with humiliation. The message to every reformist in PKR is unmistakable: loyalty to the family trumps competence.

Nurul Izzah was even offered the chance to create a “second deputy president” post for Rafizi. She didn’t deny it. The consolation prize for losing to the boss’s daughter is a made-up title. That’s not a political party — it’s a family firm.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Anwar already appointed Nurul Izzah as Senior Economic Adviser in January 2023, despite her losing her parliamentary seat. He dismissed nepotism accusations by redefining the word. Now the pattern escalates: from unelected adviser to party deputy president. Next stop: Deputy PM?

Malaysia has a long history of political dynasties — Mahathir groomed his son Mukhriz, Najib inherited his father’s legacy. Anwar promised to be different. He isn’t.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You told us reformasi was about breaking the old ways. About meritocracy. About ending the politics of patronage and family. Your daughter now controls your party’s No. 2 slot with a 6,000-vote margin that was engineered before the ballots were printed. Your Economy Minister quit in protest. Your reform agenda lost its most credible champion. And you called it democracy. The only thing reformed in PKR is the family’s grip on power.

Steven Sim's 'Adviser' Turns Out to Be Alleged RM10 Million Fraudster — Minister Claims He Never Appointed Anyone

Said by: Steven Sim, Human Resources Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-03-31

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

In March 2025, a businessman with the title “Tan Sri” was arrested by Bukit Aman’s anti-money laundering team for allegedly defrauding the public of RM10 million. The 59-year-old, who had 30 years of experience in the oil and gas industry, was apprehended upon returning to his home at Jalan U Thant, KL, after a trip to Osaka, Japan.

A news portal then reported that the arrested businessman had been claiming to serve as an adviser to Human Resources Minister Steven Sim. Sim’s press secretary Abdul Hakim Ab Rahman quickly issued a denial, stating that the minister “has never appointed any individual as his advisor.”

The ministry directed all inquiries about positions within the minister’s office to “official channels” but offered no explanation for how an alleged fraudster came to be publicly associated with the minister.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This is a governance failure regardless of whether Sim actually appointed the man or not. If the man was falsely claiming to be the minister’s adviser, it raises questions about why Sim’s office didn’t know about it sooner and why action wasn’t taken before the man was arrested for a completely separate RM10 million fraud.

If the man did have some informal relationship with the minister — even without a formal appointment letter — then Sim’s blanket denial is misleading. In Malaysian politics, “advisers” often operate in grey zones without official documentation, precisely to maintain plausible deniability.

Either way, the fact that an alleged RM10 million fraudster was running around using a PH minister’s name to build credibility is a serious vetting and governance failure.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

The speed of the denial is telling. Within hours of the news breaking, Sim’s office issued a statement saying the minister “has never appointed any individual as his advisor.” No investigation. No explanation. Just a flat denial.

But here’s what the denial doesn’t address: How did a man alleged to have defrauded the public of RM10 million come to be publicly associated with a sitting Cabinet minister? Was there any social contact? Any events attended together? Any photos? The denial addresses the formal appointment question but dodges the substantive question of the relationship.

PH ministers love to talk about transparency and accountability. Here’s a test: release a full accounting of everyone who has access to or works with the minister’s office, formally or informally. Until then, the denial is just PR crisis management.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Malaysia has a long tradition of politically connected fixers, “advisers,” and intermediaries who use their proximity to power to build business credibility. The Nurul Izzah “adviser” appointment controversy was a similar case of a PH figure being linked to questionable appointments. Under BN, the practice was endemic — “political secretaries” and “special officers” operated as unofficial gatekeepers with enormous informal power.

PH promised to clean this up. The fact that alleged fraudsters are still claiming ministerial connections suggests the cleanup hasn’t happened.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You promised Kerajaan Madani would be different — no more cronyism, no more fixers, no more politically connected businessmen using ministerial names for personal gain. Then an alleged RM10 million fraudster turns out to be publicly claiming he’s your minister’s adviser, and the best you can do is a press release saying “we never appointed anyone.” That’s not accountability — that’s damage control. If you can’t even control who’s using your ministers’ names, how are you controlling who’s using their influence?

Najib Gets House Arrest — The World's Most Comfortable 'Punishment' for the World's Biggest Kleptocracy

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Pardons Board

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-02-02

📌 What Happened

Former PM Najib Razak, convicted of corruption in the 1MDB scandal that looted billions from the Malaysian public, had his sentence halved by a royal pardon and was subsequently transferred to house arrest at his luxurious Kuala Lumpur residence. The man convicted of stealing from the rakyat now serves ’time’ in a mansion.

🔥 Why It Matters

1MDB was the largest kleptocracy case in history. US DOJ estimated $4.5 billion was misappropriated. Malaysia is STILL paying the debt — RM42 billion paid so far, RM50 billion total burden. Ordinary Malaysians bear this cost through reduced public services, higher taxes, and fiscal constraints. The man responsible lives in comfort while his victims pay his debts.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent his entire political career criticising Najib and 1MDB. He used 1MDB as his primary argument for why Malaysia needed change. He asked Malaysians to vote PH specifically to ensure accountability for 1MDB. Then, once in power, he allowed — or facilitated — Najib’s sentence reduction and comfortable house arrest. The anti-corruption crusader became the corruption enabler. The reason? UMNO’s parliamentary support requires keeping UMNO’s patron comfortable.

📜 Previous Cases

In 2018, when PH first won, Najib was arrested and charged. The legal process worked. Under PH 2.0, the legal process was undermined by political calculation. Zahid got 47 charges dropped. Najib got his sentence halved. The pattern is clear: PH trades justice for UMNO’s coalition support.

🎯 PH Callout

You asked us to vote for justice. We voted. You then traded that justice for parliamentary seats. Najib lives in a mansion. Zahid walks free. And the rakyat pays RM50 billion in 1MDB debt. This isn’t justice delayed — it’s justice murdered by the very people who promised to deliver it.

CPI Score Flatlines at 50 — Same as During Peak 1MDB Scandal in 2015

Said by: Transparency International / Rafizi Ramli

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-01-28

📌 What Happened

Malaysia’s Corruption Perceptions Index stuck at 50 points and 57th globally in both 2023 and 2024 — identical to the score during the peak 1MDB scandal in 2015. Rafizi himself warned this is ‘happening despite Anwar’s zero-tolerance stance on corruption.’

🔥 Why It Matters

The CPI is the most widely cited international benchmark for corruption. After two years of Anwar’s ‘zero tolerance’ rhetoric, Malaysia’s score has not moved a single point. The country is perceived as exactly as corrupt as it was when Najib was siphoning billions through 1MDB. This is not a lag effect — the CPI reflects current perceptions based on surveys of business leaders and experts.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

They are telling the world that despite the change in government, the corruption environment has not improved. When your own minister (Rafizi) publicly cites this score as evidence of failure, the verdict is unanimous.

🎯 PH Callout

Your CPI score is the same as Najib’s during 1MDB. Not similar — identical. After two years of ‘zero tolerance’ speeches, the international community perceives Malaysia as exactly as corrupt as it was under the most kleptocratic government in its history. That’s not a lag — it’s a verdict.

Jho Low STILL Free After 8 Years — Malaysia's Most Wanted Fugitive Living Comfortably Somewhere

Said by: Interpol / MACC / DOJ

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As of 2025, Low Taek Jho (Jho Low) — the central figure in the 1MDB scandal — remains free despite Interpol red notices, international arrest warrants, and charges in multiple jurisdictions. Three different Malaysian governments have failed to secure his extradition. Anwar made 1MDB accountability his signature promise, yet has made no visible progress in locating or extraditing Jho Low. Reports suggest he has been residing in China or Macau, but the Malaysian government has not disclosed any diplomatic efforts to secure his return.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Jho Low orchestrated the theft of billions from a Malaysian sovereign wealth fund. He is the most wanted fugitive in Malaysian history. His freedom — eight years after charges were filed — represents the single largest accountability failure in Malaysian governance. Every day Jho Low remains free is a day the rakyat services debt he helped create while he lives comfortably beyond the reach of Malaysian law. If Malaysia cannot bring its most-wanted kleptocrat to justice, its entire anti-corruption framework is performative.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar spent years demanding 1MDB accountability. “Where is Jho Low?” was a PH rallying cry. Three years into government, the answer remains the same: somewhere comfortable, apparently beyond Anwar’s reach. The US DOJ recovered over $1 billion in 1MDB-linked assets. Singapore jailed bankers. Switzerland sanctioned institutions. Malaysia — the country that was robbed — has not managed to bring the principal orchestrator to trial. If the political will existed, diplomatic pressure on China (where Jho Low is believed to reside) would be a priority. It clearly isn’t.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under Najib, the 1MDB investigation was actively suppressed — the AG was fired, MACC officers were transferred, and Jho Low was protected. Under Muhyiddin, investigations continued but Jho Low remained free. Under Anwar, investigations concluded with settlements and Najib’s reduced sentence, but Jho Low’s extradition has not progressed. The pattern suggests that every Malaysian government has found it politically convenient to talk about catching Jho Low while doing nothing that would actually bring him back.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: “Where is Jho Low?” — you asked that question for five years. Now you’re the PM. You control the diplomatic apparatus. You have Interpol red notices. You have international cooperation frameworks. And Jho Low is still free. Disclose what diplomatic steps you’ve taken. Publish the extradition requests. Pressure China publicly if necessary. Or admit that catching Jho Low was always a slogan, never a priority — and that the biggest kleptocrat in Malaysian history will die free because no government cared enough to bring him home.

Sabah and Sarawak Autonomy Demands Still Unmet — East Malaysia Remains the Forgotten Stepchild

Said by: Sabah & Sarawak Leaders / GPS

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Despite 2021 constitutional amendments restoring Sabah and Sarawak’s status as equal partners in the Federation, East Malaysia remains structurally marginalised. Sabah is Malaysia’s poorest state. MA63 (Malaysia Agreement 1963) recommendations are gathering dust. Oil and gas royalty disputes remain unresolved. Equal partnership exists on paper but not in budget allocation, infrastructure investment, or political representation. Sarawak’s GPS maintains leverage through coalition politics, but Sabah — where PH won only 1 of 20 seats in the 2025 state election — has almost no bargaining power.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Sabah and Sarawak contributed the resources — oil, gas, timber, land — that built Malaysia’s modern economy. In return, they received the worst infrastructure, healthcare, education, and connectivity in the country. The MA63 framework guarantees equal treatment that has never been delivered. When Sabah remains the poorest state despite sitting on some of Malaysia’s richest natural resources, the federal system has failed East Malaysia. Every year the disparities persist is another year the federation’s promise is broken.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar promised to honour MA63 and treat Sabah and Sarawak as equal partners. Three years later, the specific demands — increased oil royalties, control over immigration, education autonomy, infrastructure investment — remain unresolved. The constitutional amendments were symbolic victories that changed no budget line items. Sabah still has the highest poverty rate. Sarawak still has the worst road infrastructure. Both still have the weakest healthcare coverage. Symbolic equal partnership without fiscal equal partnership is not partnership — it is a rebrand.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, East Malaysian states were coalition partners who received patronage in exchange for parliamentary seats — but never structural reform. Under PH 1.0, a committee was formed to study MA63. Under PH 2.0, the committee’s recommendations remain largely unimplemented. Under every government, the pattern is the same: promise East Malaysian autonomy, study the issue, delay implementation, and rely on coalition arithmetic to avoid accountability. Sixty years after Malaysia Agreement, the agreement’s terms remain unfulfilled.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You amended the Constitution to restore equal partner status. Then you failed to follow through with equal budgets, equal infrastructure, and equal services. Sabah is still the poorest state. Sarawak still has the worst roads. Both still have the worst healthcare access. Constitutional amendments without fiscal commitments are empty gestures. Increase federal allocation to East Malaysia proportional to their resource contribution. Resolve oil royalty disputes. Deliver infrastructure on par with Peninsular Malaysia. Or keep calling them “equal partners” while treating them like subsidiaries.

MCA to PH: 'Don't Blame BN for YOUR Failure to Reform'

Said by: Neow Choo Seong, MCA Youth

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-12-30

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late December 2024, MCA Youth spokesman Neow Choo Seong issued a statement telling PH to stop blaming BN for PH’s failure to implement promised reforms. Neow argued that “power is firmly in PH’s hands” and BN should not be scapegoated for PH’s own inaction. The statement was notable because MCA is a BN component party within the unity government coalition — meaning even PH’s own coalition partners were tired of PH blaming everyone else for its failure to deliver reform.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When your own coalition partner publicly tells you to stop making excuses, the excuse has expired. PH’s default defence for every broken promise has been “coalition constraints” — specifically, UMNO and BN blocking reform. MCA’s statement demolishes that argument from inside the coalition: power is PH’s. The PM is PH. The Finance Minister is PH. The majority of cabinet positions are PH. If reforms haven’t happened, it’s because PH chose not to implement them — not because BN prevented it.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH has used “coalition constraints” as an all-purpose excuse for three years. Can’t repeal the Sedition Act? Coalition constraints. Can’t reform the Printing Presses Act? Coalition constraints. Can’t deliver PM term limits? Coalition constraints. MCA’s statement exposes the fraud: PH controls the government. PH has a parliamentary majority. PH holds the key portfolios. The reforms aren’t blocked — they’re not attempted. Blaming BN is not a political strategy; it’s a confession that PH never intended to deliver the reforms it promised.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Blaming coalition partners for policy failures is a Malaysian political tradition. Under BN, UMNO blamed MCA and MIC for “not understanding Malay sensitivities.” Under PH 1.0, PH blamed Mahathir’s faction for slow reform. Under PH 2.0, PH blames UMNO. The pattern is identical across coalitions: the dominant party uses smaller partners as scapegoats for its own policy failures. When even the scapegoat says “stop blaming us,” the strategy has collapsed.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your own coalition partner is telling you to stop making excuses. Power is in your hands. You hold the PM’s office, the Finance Ministry, and the majority of cabinet seats. If reforms haven’t happened after three years, it’s because you chose not to implement them. Stop blaming BN. Stop blaming “coalition constraints.” Own your record. Deliver the reforms you promised — or admit that the promises were never sincere, and face the voters with that admission.
