Azam Baki's RM100M Defamation Suit Against Bloomberg — MACC Chief Fights Media Instead of Corruption

Said by: Bloomberg / Azam Baki, MACC Chief Commissioner

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-27

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 27 February 2026, it was reported that MACC Chief Commissioner Azam Baki filed a RM100 million defamation suit against Bloomberg. The suit came after Bloomberg published an investigation alleging that Azam owned millions of shares in publicly listed companies — potentially breaching civil servant ownership limits. Bloomberg further alleged that MACC officials had colluded with businessmen to orchestrate hostile takeovers of listed firms, using raids and intimidation to force executives into selling shares to groups dubbed the “corporate mafia.” Rather than resigning or submitting to an independent investigation, Azam chose to sue the messenger. Anwar’s response was to announce a task force — not an independent inquiry, not a Royal Commission. Even DAP, his own coalition partner, said a task force “does not go far enough.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The head of Malaysia’s anti-corruption agency is suing the world’s most prominent financial news outlet for reporting on his alleged financial misconduct. This is not a case of a private citizen defending their reputation — this is the chief enforcer of anti-corruption law using the legal system to intimidate a media organisation that investigated him. If Azam’s role is to fight corruption, and a credible international outlet alleges he is compromised, the correct institutional response is a transparent, independent investigation — not a defamation suit designed to chill future reporting. The RM100M figure is not about compensation; it is about deterrence. Bloomberg has stood by its reporting. The anti-corruption agency fighting corruption journalism instead of actual corruption is peak Madani irony.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s response — a task force — is the bureaucratic equivalent of thoughts and prayers. A task force reports to the government. An independent inquiry reports to the public. Anwar chose the one that protects him. DAP publicly said a task force “does not go far enough,” meaning even Anwar’s own coalition partners don’t trust his anti-corruption mechanisms. CNA reported that Anwar faces a “graft crisis” with “PKR party fractures” and “PH coalition jitters.” The man who built Reformasi on the promise of clean governance now leads a government where the anti-corruption chief sues journalists, the PM’s aide gets charged with bribery, and the DPM’s 47 charges get dropped. At some point, the pattern is the policy.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Azam Baki’s share ownership scandal first surfaced in early 2022, when Malaysiakini reported he held shares in two public-listed companies exceeding the civil service ownership cap. A special parliamentary committee cleared him — prompting widespread outrage. Bloomberg’s 2025-2026 investigation went further, alleging systemic collusion between MACC officials and corporate raiders. The MACC-media adversarial pattern is not new either: the commission has previously threatened legal action against outlets that reported on its internal affairs. Under Azam’s leadership, MACC has become less an anti-corruption body and more a political instrument — selectively prosecuting opposition figures while shielding coalition allies.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You promised MACC independence. Instead, the MACC chief is suing Bloomberg for doing MACC’s job. An independent inquiry — not a task force — is the minimum acceptable response. If Azam is innocent, an inquiry clears him. If he is not, the country’s anti-corruption infrastructure needs to be rebuilt from scratch. Your task force is a stalling mechanism, not an accountability mechanism. Even your own coalition partner sees through it. Do better, or stop pretending Reformasi means anything.

Every Cabinet Member Meeting KPIs, Says Anwar — Critics Ask: What KPIs?

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-25

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 25 February 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told Parliament that every cabinet member is meeting their KPIs and performing with integrity. He did not disclose what those KPIs were, how they were measured, or who evaluated them. Anwar was responding to an opposition MP who asked if ministers would be forced to resign for poor performance. His reply: “Accountability is not only about punishment but also correction, continuous improvement, and the moral responsibility ministers held for the people.” No KPIs have ever been publicly disclosed for any minister in Anwar’s cabinet. No minister has been penalised, demoted, or publicly reprimanded for underperformance. Two ministers were removed in the December 2025 reshuffle — including Rafizi Ramli — but Anwar framed it as “portfolio realignment,” not performance accountability.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Claiming 100% KPI compliance without disclosing the KPIs is the corporate equivalent of giving yourself a perfect performance review and refusing to show the form. It is not accountability — it is self-certification. If every minister is meeting their KPIs, then either the KPIs are so low that failure is impossible, or the assessment is dishonest. Given that 77% of Malaysians report struggling with cost of living, university rankings are dropping, the CPI is stuck at 1MDB-era levels, and reform promises remain unmet, the claim is laughable on its face. Transparency means publishing the KPIs, the methodology, and the results — and letting the public judge. “Trust me, everyone is great” is not reform governance.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: A PM who holds both the PM and Finance Minister portfolios is essentially grading himself on fiscal management. There is no independent check on the most powerful office in the country. The Najib government also claimed ministers were performing. The Mahathir government also claimed results were being delivered. Without published metrics, every government is equally excellent — which means the exercise is meaningless. Singapore publishes ministerial performance metrics and links them to public service delivery targets. South Korea conducts annual government performance evaluations with published results. Malaysia’s approach under the “reformist” PM: trust his word. The Merdeka Center’s February 2026 poll showed public satisfaction with economic management at 38% — suggesting that whatever KPIs exist, the public disagrees they are being met.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian PM has claimed their cabinet is performing well. Najib claimed his ministers delivered results — while 1MDB bled billions. Muhyiddin claimed pandemic management was effective — while thousands died and billions in stimulus were unaccounted for. The pattern is universal: self-assessment without external verification always produces perfect scores. Anwar promised to be different. He isn’t. The KPI charade is a bipartisan tradition that PH has enthusiastically adopted.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Publish the KPIs. Go through every minister, every metric, every target, and every result. Put it online. Let the rakyat see whether their Education Minister’s KPIs include declining PISA scores, whether the Health Minister’s KPIs include 33,000 contract doctors still waiting for permanent posts, whether the Economy Minister’s KPIs include 77% of Malaysians struggling with costs. You won’t — because the scorecard would be devastating. A government that grades itself in secret has no credibility claiming accountability.

MACC 'Corporate Mafia' Allegations — DAP Demands Independent Inquiry, Anwar Resists

Said by: DAP Leaders / SCMP

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-25

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late February 2026, DAP leaders publicly stated that Anwar’s proposed task force to examine alleged misconduct at MACC “does not go far enough.” They demanded an independent inquiry instead. SCMP reported that the push came after Bloomberg’s investigation alleged MACC officials had colluded with businessmen to orchestrate hostile takeovers of listed firms — the so-called “corporate mafia” allegations. Anwar’s response — a government-controlled task force rather than an independent commission — put his “reform agenda under renewed scrutiny.” Even his own coalition allies don’t trust his anti-corruption mechanisms.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When your own coalition partner — the party most associated with governance reform — publicly says your anti-corruption response is inadequate, the credibility gap is no longer deniable. DAP is not the opposition. DAP is Anwar’s ally. If DAP doesn’t trust the task force to deliver accountability, why should anyone else? The distinction between a task force and an independent inquiry is not semantic — it is structural. A task force reports to the PM. An independent inquiry reports to the public. Anwar chose the mechanism that keeps control in his hands, not the one that maximises transparency.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s entire political brand is built on fighting corruption. Bloomberg alleges his anti-corruption chief is compromised. His own coalition partner says his response is too weak. And his answer is… a task force. This is a man who spent years in prison over politically motivated corruption charges. He knows exactly what happens when anti-corruption institutions are captured by politics. Yet when faced with credible allegations of MACC capture, he reaches for the weakest possible tool. The task force is not designed to find the truth — it is designed to manage the news cycle until the story dies.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: This follows a pattern. When Shamsul Iskandar was caught issuing support letters, Anwar’s response was a “reprimand.” When Zahid’s 47 charges were dropped, the response was silence. When Farhash Wafa’s Sabah mining scandal broke, MACC cleared him while Bloomberg alleged Anwar ordered MACC not to investigate. Every time corruption allegations touch Anwar’s inner circle, the response is always the weakest available option — never an independent investigation, never structural reform, never real consequences.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your own coalition partner is telling you publicly that your response is inadequate. Listen to them. Establish an independent inquiry with subpoena powers, public hearings, and a mandate to investigate MACC from top to bottom. If you won’t listen to DAP, listen to the principle you once went to prison for: that anti-corruption institutions must be independent of political control. A task force controlled by the PM investigating the PM’s anti-corruption chief is not independence — it is theatre.

MACC in Crisis with No Credible Leadership — The Anti-Corruption Watchdog Has Become the Scandal

Said by: DAP / Bloomberg / CNA Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-25

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC) is embroiled in its worst-ever credibility crisis. Chief Commissioner Azam Baki faces Bloomberg allegations of owning millions in shares (breaching civil servant limits) and colluding with a ‘corporate mafia.’ Rather than resign, Azam filed a RM100 million defamation suit against Bloomberg. DAP, PH’s own coalition partner, has demanded an independent inquiry, saying the government’s task force ‘does not go far enough.’ CNA called it part of Anwar’s ‘perfect storm.’

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: If the anti-corruption agency itself is corrupt, the entire governance framework collapses. MACC is supposed to be the institution that holds everyone else accountable. When MACC is the subject of corruption allegations — and the government’s response is a toothless task force rather than an independent inquiry — it signals that no institution in Malaysia is trustworthy.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s signature governance promise was strengthening anti-corruption institutions. He proposed separating the AG and Public Prosecutor roles (stalled). He promised MACC independence (the agency’s own chief is now the scandal). He promised zero tolerance for corruption (his own aide was charged with bribery). Every anti-corruption promise has not just been broken — it’s been inverted. The anti-corruption infrastructure is worse under PH, not better.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, MACC was widely perceived as politically compromised, used to target opposition politicians while ignoring ruling party corruption. Under PH 1.0, MACC briefly had credibility when it investigated 1MDB. Under PH 2.0, MACC’s credibility has hit rock bottom — the chief is fighting Bloomberg in court while his own agency faces allegations of running a corporate extortion ring. The trajectory: compromised → briefly credible → worse than ever.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your own coalition partner says your response to the MACC crisis is inadequate. Bloomberg — one of the world’s most respected financial news outlets — is standing by its reporting against Azam Baki. Your anti-corruption commission is running a RM100 million lawsuit against journalism instead of fighting corruption. And your response is a ’task force.’ A task force. For the most serious corruption allegation in MACC’s history. That’s not reform — that’s managing the optics while the institution burns.

Rafizi Alleges Anwar Is Fueling Social Media Attacks Against Him — Rift Goes Public

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, former Economy Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-22

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 22 February 2026, former Economy Minister Rafizi Ramli publicly alleged that Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim was behind coordinated social media campaigns targeting him. Business Today reported Rafizi accused Anwar of “fueling his social media influence” — using coordinated accounts to attack his credibility and undermine his political standing within PKR. SCMP followed up on 18 February, framing Rafizi as seizing “the banner of reform and clean government that was once Anwar’s own” and asking whether he was now “the conscience of the Reformasi movement.” Anwar dismissed the rift as a “distraction.” Rafizi had previously warned Anwar that the CPI flatline at 50 points was “extremely dangerous” for the government’s credibility. His reward: being sidelined from cabinet in the December 2025 reshuffle. The Economy Ministry portfolio sat vacant for six months after his departure.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When your most competent former minister accuses you of running influence operations against him, the reform brand is in terminal decline. This is not opposition noise — this is the former Economy Minister, a PKR founding member, saying the PM is using dirty tricks against his own people. If the allegations are true, the PM’s office is diverting resources to internal political warfare instead of governing. If they are false, the rift is still real and the party is fracturing publicly ahead of the next election. Either way, PKR’s internal democracy is collapsing — the party built on challenging authoritarian leadership is now led by a man who allegedly punishes dissent with coordinated social media attacks.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Rafizi is not opposition. He is PKR. He is Anwar’s own party. If the PM is using social media to discipline internal critics, that is the behaviour of an authoritarian party boss, not a democratic leader. It is the kind of thing Najib was accused of — and which Anwar once condemned. The SCMP framing Rafizi as “the conscience of Reformasi” should alarm Anwar deeply — it means international media has noticed the title no longer fits the PM. When your protégé inherits your brand because you abandoned it, the verdict is clear. The man who told the truth about corruption risks within the government was punished for it. That is not reform — that is retaliation.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Rafizi’s allegations of coordinated social media attacks echo patterns in other authoritarian-leaning governments where leaders use cyber troops to discipline internal dissent. Within PKR itself, Rafizi had previously clashed with the party establishment over candidate selection and economic policy. His warning about the Farhash Wafa scandal was ignored; Farhash was later charged with bribery. His warning about the CPI score was dismissed; the score remains at 1MDB-era levels. The pattern is consistent: Rafizi raises legitimate concerns, the leadership ignores or punishes him, and the concerns later prove justified.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: The minister who warned you about corruption in your own office was right. The minister who told you the CPI score was “extremely dangerous” was right. Your response was to sideline him and allegedly run social media campaigns against him. If you want to prove Rafizi wrong, publish a full accounting of the PMO’s social media spending and contractor relationships. If you can’t do that, the allegation stands — and the “Reformasi” movement’s conscience now belongs to the man you discarded, not the man in Putrajaya.

DAP Special Congress July 12 — Party Questions Its Own Role in Government While Staying in Government

Said by: Chow Kon Yeow, Penang Chief Minister / DAP

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-21

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 21 February 2026, Penang CM Chow Kon Yeow announced that DAP would hold a special congress on 12 July 2026 to decide the party’s role in Anwar’s government. Chow insisted DAP was ’not withdrawing support from the Madani government’ but was discussing ‘whether to continue holding ministerial posts and certain positions.’ DAP’s 40 MPs would continue supporting the government. All members, including leaders, must abide by the congress decision.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: DAP is threatening to leave government without actually leaving government. The special congress is political theatre — a way to pressure Anwar for better terms while maintaining the safety of coalition membership. If DAP genuinely had policy disagreements, they’d articulate them publicly. Instead, they’re holding an internal vote on ‘whether to hold ministerial posts’ — which is a negotiation tactic dressed up as democratic deliberation.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: This is the political equivalent of threatening to quit your job while continuing to cash the paycheck. DAP wants credit for independence (‘we’re questioning our role!’) while enjoying the benefits of power (‘we’re not actually leaving!’). The special congress gives DAP negotiating leverage for the next cabinet reshuffle without any real risk. If the congress votes to stay — which it will — DAP gets to say ’the members decided.’ If they vote to leave cabinet — which they won’t — it would collapse the government. Both outcomes benefit DAP; neither benefits the rakyat.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: DAP has a history of internal drama over its role in coalition governments. In the Mahathir 2.0 era, DAP faced similar tensions over compromises with PPBM. The pattern is consistent: DAP enters coalitions, compromises on principles, faces internal backlash, holds dramatic internal events, then stays. The cycle repeats because the alternative — opposition — is worse for the party apparatus.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: If you have policy disagreements with the government, state them publicly. If you think the coalition isn’t delivering for your voters, say why. But don’t hold a special congress to ‘decide your role’ while simultaneously promising to keep supporting the government. That’s not democratic deliberation — it’s a negotiating ploy. The rakyat can see through it.

CNA’s analysis described DAP’s Penang politics as ‘acrimonious’ and driven by ‘personality politics’ rather than policy.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The Chow-Guan Eng tension has been building since DAP’s party elections in September 2024, when Chow lost the state DAP chairmanship — effectively making him a CM without control of the state party machinery. The Straits Times noted Chow ‘vowed work continues’ even though he’s ’not state’s DAP chief’ — a ’not a lame duck’ declaration that only lame ducks need to make.

Your councillor appointments follow an ’established method’ that nobody outside the party understands. Implement transparent, merit-based appointment criteria. Publish them. And resolve your internal power struggle before it starts affecting planning approvals and development decisions that impact Penangites.

Anwar Defends MACC Chief Azam Baki Over Second Shareholding Scandal, Tells Critics to 'Read His Explanation'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-12

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

In February 2026, Bloomberg reported that MACC chief Azam Baki was a key shareholder in a financial services company — Velocity Capital Partners Berhad — based on corporate filings. This was Azam’s second shareholding scandal, following the 2022 controversy over shares in Excel Force MSC Berhad.

Civil society groups including C4 Center demanded Azam’s immediate suspension pending investigation. They noted concerning links between Velocity Capital Partners and MMAG Holdings, where controversial former Anwar aide Farhash Wafa Salvador had acquired a 20% stake and was appointed chairman.

Anwar’s response? He told critics to “read his [Azam’s] explanation” and said the special committee led by the Attorney General would handle it. As of March 2026, the government had not extended Azam’s contract, but he was never suspended during the investigation.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

The head of Malaysia’s anti-corruption agency is facing allegations that he held shares exceeding limits for public officials — for the second time. The first scandal in 2022 saw massive public protests demanding his removal. PH, then freshly in power, inherited Azam but chose to keep him. Now, under PH’s watch, the same pattern has repeated.

The deeper concern is the Farhash connection. If the MACC chief held shares in a company linked to the PM’s former aide, that raises serious conflict-of-interest questions about why MACC never investigated Farhash despite multiple public calls to do so.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

“Read his explanation.” That’s the Prime Minister’s response to allegations that the head of the anti-corruption commission may be personally corrupt. Not “we take this seriously.” Not “he will be suspended pending investigation.” Just… read his explanation.

This is the same Anwar who, in opposition, demanded Azam’s head during the first shareholding scandal. PH supporters marched in the streets in January 2022 demanding Azam’s removal. Anwar’s allies called it a governance crisis. Now that Anwar is PM, the same governance crisis gets a committee, a delay, and a suggestion to “read his explanation.”

C4 Center called it “Shareholding Scandal 2.0” and demanded immediate suspension. Bersih accused MACC of being used as a political tool. Anwar told Bersih they were being “unfair.” The reformist PM is now defending the institution he once wanted to tear down.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

The first Azam Baki shareholding scandal in early 2022 was one of the biggest anti-corruption protests in Malaysian history. Thousands marched demanding his removal. Azam claimed his brother used his trading account — a defence widely ridiculed. The Securities Commission eventually cleared him in a decision that drew widespread criticism.

PH came to power in November 2022 promising to clean up institutions. They kept Azam. His contract has been extended three times — in 2023, 2024, and 2025. Each extension was a choice. Each extension told Malaysians that MACC independence was less important than political convenience.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You marched against Azam Baki. Your supporters filled the streets demanding his removal. Your allies called the first shareholding scandal a disgrace. Then you took power, kept him, extended his contract three times, and when the second scandal broke, told the public to “read his explanation.” This isn’t reform. This is capture. You own Azam Baki now — every scandal, every conflict of interest, every failure of accountability at MACC. You had the power to replace him and you chose not to. That choice has consequences, and the rakyat can see them clearly.

Tiong King Sing Pulls 'Do You Know Who I Am?' Card While Lecturing Staff About Rudeness

Said by: Tiong King Sing, Tourism, Arts and Culture Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-12

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 12 February 2026, Tourism Minister Tiong King Sing launched the ‘Mesra Malaysia’ (Friendly Malaysia) campaign at KL Sentral — a campaign about being welcoming and courteous to tourists. During the launch, Tiong complained about rudeness from public and private sector employees, saying: “Some officers think they have a lot of power. But when people don’t understand certain issues, we must explain.”

The South China Morning Post ran the story with the devastating headline: “Malaysian minister slams rude staff hindering tourism: ‘do you know who I am?’” — capturing the irony of a powerful politician lecturing others about arrogance while exuding precisely the same energy.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Visit Malaysia Year 2026 is supposed to be a national priority. The tourism sector is critical to Malaysia’s economy. And the minister responsible for making Malaysia welcoming to the world is making headlines for his own attitude problem.

This isn’t Tiong’s first controversy. In 2025, Anwar had to personally warn him after alcohol was served at a Tourism Malaysia gala dinner — a sensitivity failure in a Muslim-majority country. The pattern is a minister who generates more negative headlines than tourist arrivals.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

You’re launching a campaign called ‘Friendly Malaysia’ while acting like the least friendly person in the room. The cognitive dissonance is spectacular. A minister complaining about public servants who “think they have a lot of power” — while wielding the power of a federal ministry to publicly shame unnamed workers — is peak Malaysian political irony.

The ‘do you know who I am’ energy is exactly the attitude tourists and citizens hate about Malaysian bureaucracy. And the minister tasked with fixing it is the embodiment of it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Tiong King Sing is a businessman-politician from Sarawak’s GPS coalition. The alcohol controversy at the gala dinner in 2025 already showed a minister out of touch with sensitivities. Malaysia’s tourism sector has also been plagued by scam taxi drivers, overcharging at tourist spots, and visa problems — systemic issues that require systemic solutions, not press conferences about attitude.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You put Tiong King Sing in charge of tourism. He’s now making international headlines for arrogance while launching a friendliness campaign. The minister responsible for Malaysia’s image is damaging Malaysia’s image. If you want Visit Malaysia Year 2026 to succeed, start by ensuring your ministers don’t become the joke tourists tell when they get home.
