[BM] Anwar Isyarat Keterbukaan Dengan PAS — Orang Yang Lawan Mereka Kini Mendekati Mereka

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 8 Mac 2026, sambil mempertahankan angka pemulihan antirasuah kerajaannya sebanyak RM15.5 bilion, Anwar Ibrahim serentak mengisyaratkan keterbukaan untuk penglibatan lebih rapat dengan PAS — parti Islam yang gabungannya dibina untuk ditentang. Ini datang ketika DAP secara terbuka menyemak ikatan gabungannya dan PH baru sahaja dihapuskan dalam pilihan raya negeri Sabah, hanya memenangi 1 daripada 20 kerusi.

🔥 KENAPA IA PENTING: Keseluruhan premis Pakatan Harapan ialah ia mewakili alternatif kepada ekstremisme agama PAS dan naungan etno-nasionalis UMNO. Kesanggupan Anwar mendekati PAS — walaupun DAP, rakan gabungan paling setia, sedang kehilangan sokongan — mengisyaratkan kelangsungan pilihan raya telah mengatasi ideologi sepenuhnya. Jika PH dibina untuk menentang PAS, dan pemimpin PH kini mendekati PAS, apakah sebenarnya pendirian PH lagi?

💩 KENAPA IA KARUT: Daripada membaiki keretakan dalaman yang menyebabkan kekalahan di Sabah — peminggiran DAP, perpecahan Rafizi PKR, janji manifesto tidak ditunaikan — Anwar sedang mendekati akomodasi politik yang sama yang pernah dikutuknya. Ini bukan pragmatisme. Ia keputusasaan berpakaian kenegarawanan. Anda tidak membaiki gabungan yang dibina atas reformasi dengan menjemput parti anti-reformasi ke meja. Anda membaikinya dengan benar-benar melakukan reformasi.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Mahathir mendekati PAS secara senyap semasa PH 1.0, menyumbang kepada keruntuhan gabungan dalam Langkah Sheraton 2020. UMNO berulang kali bermain-main dengan PAS apabila sesuai secara politik, membentuk Muafakat Nasional pada 2019. Setiap gabungan Malaysia yang mendekati PAS akhirnya membuat konsesi terhadap tadbir urus sekular. Anwar melihat buku panduan ini memusnahkan PH 1.0 — dan kini menjalankannya sendiri.

🎯 PANGGILAN PH: Anda menghabiskan PRU15 memberitahu rakyat Malaysia bahawa PAS adalah ancaman kepada Malaysia pelbagai budaya. Rakan gabungan anda sendiri berarak menentang politik PAS. Kini apabila anda kalah pilihan raya, PAS tiba-tiba layak didekati? Sama ada PAS ancaman kepada demokrasi Malaysia atau tidak. Pilih satu dan teguh. Pengundi anda layak mendapat konsistensi, bukan keputusasaan pilihan raya.

[BM] PKR Keluarkan Surat Tunjuk Sebab Kepada Rafizi, Kemudian Berundur — Parti Tak Mampu Disiplinkan Pengkritik Paling Terkenalnya

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, Pandan MP / Fuziah Salleh, PKR Secretary-General

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 19 Februari, AP Pandan Rafizi Ramli mendedahkan beliau menerima surat tunjuk sebab daripada Setiausaha Agung PKR Fuziah Salleh selepas memberitahu Sin Chew Daily beliau akan bertanding di Pandan pada PRU16 tetapi berkemungkinan bukan sebagai calon PKR. Rafizi menjawab bahawa maksud komennya “boleh difahami oleh sesiapa yang mahir bahasa Melayu” dan beliau bebas membuat rancangan sendiri selepas Parlimen dibubarkan. Pada 6 Mac, PKR menyampaikan keputusannya melalui emel — tiada tindakan diambil, hanya peringatan mematuhi perlembagaan parti dan kod etika. Pada 8 Mac, Rafizi secara terbuka menggambarkan ini sebagai “pusingan U,” berkata surat tunjuk sebab dikeluarkan “dengan niat memberikan beliau tendangan” tetapi parti akhirnya gentar.

🔥 Kenapa Ia Penting

Episod ini mendedahkan kereputan institusi dalam PKR — parti yang pernah melaungkan dirinya sebagai barisan hadapan reformasi. Surat tunjuk sebab sepatutnya menjadi permulaan proses disiplin, bukan ancaman persembahan diikuti undur senyap. Ketidakmampuan PKR sama ada melibatkan kritikannya secara bermakna atau menguatkuasakan mekanisme disiplinnya sendiri menunjukkan parti yang lumpuh — terlalu takut memecat pengkritik paling terkenalnya, tetapi terlalu sombong untuk mengakui dia ada betulnya.

💩 Kenapa Ia Karut

PKR mahu Rafizi pergi tetapi tiada keberanian melakukannya. Seperti yang Rafizi sendiri tunjukkan, memecatnya tidak akan menjejaskan kerusi parlimennya — hanya peletakan jawatan sukarela akan mencetuskan kekosongan. Jadi PKR terperangkap: pecat dia dan dia kekal di Parlimen sebagai agen bebas yang memalukan mereka setiap hari, atau berundur dan kelihatan lemah. Mereka pilih lemah. “Peringatan ikut peraturan parti” adalah setara politik nota Post-it pasif-agresif. Ini parti yang sama yang memberi kuliah negara tentang reformasi institusi dan tadbir urus baik sementara ia tidak mampu menguruskan proses disiplin dalaman asas tanpa tunduk di bawah tekanan.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

PKR mempunyai sejarah panjang dan tidak membanggakan pembersihan puak yang berselindung di sebalik disiplin — dan disiplin yang runtuh menjadi komedi. Parti memecat timbalan presidennya sendiri Azmin Ali pada 2020 selepas Langkah Sheraton, tetapi hanya selepas kerosakan berlaku. Rafizi sendiri pernah menjadi anak emas PKR — pemberi maklumat yang mendedahkan skandal Perbadanan Fidlot Nasional. Kini beliau suara parti yang paling menyusahkan.

🎯 Panggilan PH

Anwar dan PKR menjanjikan rakyat Malaysia politik baru — telus, berprinsip, dan bertanggungjawab. Tetapi apabila bekas timbalan presiden anda sendiri secara terbuka mengatakan beliau tidak akan bertanding di bawah bendera anda, dan yang terbaik parti anda mampu hasilkan ialah surat tunjuk sebab diikuti emel peringatan yang lemah, anda bukan menjalankan parti politik — anda menguruskan situasi tebusan. ‘Reformasi’ pernah bermakna sesuatu. Kini ia bermakna menghantar emel bernada tegas dan berharap masalah hilang sendiri.

[BM] Teresa Kok Kata Perpaduan Gabungan Lebih Penting Daripada RCI Terhadap SPRM — AP DAP Sendiri Jejaskan Desakan Akauntabiliti

Said by: Teresa Kok, MP for Seputeh (DAP)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 8 Mac, AP Seputeh DAP Teresa Kok secara terbuka menyatakan bahawa mengekalkan perpaduan dalam kerajaan gabungan Madani lebih penting daripada desakan partinya sendiri untuk Suruhanjaya Siasatan Diraja (RCI) terhadap Suruhanjaya Pencegahan Rasuah Malaysia (SPRM). Ini datang beberapa hari selepas Setiausaha Agung DAP Anthony Loke memberitahu Malaysiakini respons kerajaan terhadap dakwaan salah laku SPRM adalah “tidak mencukupi” dan menyeru siasatan bebas.

🔥 Kenapa Ia Penting

DAP adalah parti tunggal terbesar dalam gabungan pemerintah Anwar dengan 40 AP, dan keseluruhan identiti politiknya terletak pada reformasi, akauntabiliti, dan antirasuah. Apabila ahli parlimen belakang parti sendiri secara terbuka membelot daripada kepimpinan untuk mengutamakan kelangsungan gabungan berbanding akauntabiliti institusi, ia mengisyaratkan kempen tekanan dalaman sudah retak. Kenyataan Teresa Kok memberikan Anwar perlindungan politik untuk terus mengabaikan seruan RCI.

💩 Kenapa Ia Karut

Ini helah tertua dalam buku panduan gabungan: bingkaikan tuntutan akauntabiliti sebagai ancaman kepada kestabilan. Teresa Kok pada dasarnya berkata kerajaan tidak perlu dipegang kepada piawainya sendiri kerana berbuat demikian mungkin menggoncang perahu. Tetapi DAP tidak memasuki kerajaan untuk menstabilkan perahu — ia masuk untuk membaiki enjin. SPRM di bawah Azam Baki telah didakwa secara boleh dipercayai oleh Bloomberg menjalankan “mafia korporat.” Jika RCI terhadap itu terlalu menggoyahkan untuk gabungan, maka gabungan sudah gagal tujuan yang dinyatakannya. Perpaduan tanpa akauntabiliti hanyalah subahat dengan PR yang lebih baik.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini bukan kali pertama AP DAP secara terbuka melemahkan pendirian reformasi parti untuk melindungi keharmonian gabungan. Coraknya mendalam: DAP berdiam diri berbulan-bulan mengenai skandal pegangan saham pertama Azam Baki pada 2021-2022, kemudian memperbaharui kontraknya tiga kali selepas memasuki kerajaan.

🎯 Panggilan PH

Anda beritahu pengundi Malaysia anda adalah gabungan reformasi. Dan kini, apabila parti anda sendiri menuntut mekanisme akauntabiliti asas — RCI terhadap agensi antirasuah yang didakwa menjadi mafia korporat — AP anda sendiri kata perpaduan lebih penting. Jika DAP tidak mampu mempertahankan garis sendiri mengenai SPRM, untuk apa sebenarnya parti ini?

Anwar Signals Openness to PAS Engagement — The Man Who Ran Against Them Now Courts Them

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 8 March 2026, while defending his government’s RM15.5 billion anti-corruption recovery figure, Anwar Ibrahim simultaneously signalled openness to closer engagement with PAS — the Islamist party his coalition was built to oppose. This came as DAP was publicly reviewing its coalition ties and PH had just been wiped out in the Sabah state election, winning only 1 of 20 seats.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The entire premise of Pakatan Harapan was that it represented the alternative to PAS’s religious extremism and UMNO’s ethno-nationalist patronage. Anwar’s willingness to court PAS — even as DAP, his most loyal coalition partner, is hemorrhaging support — signals that electoral survival has overtaken ideology entirely. If PH was built to oppose PAS, and PH’s leader is now courting PAS, what exactly does PH stand for anymore?

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Rather than fixing the internal fractures that caused the Sabah wipeout — DAP’s marginalisation, PKR’s Rafizi split, manifesto promises left unmet — Anwar is hedging toward the same political accommodation he once condemned. This is not pragmatism. It’s desperation dressed as statesmanship. You don’t fix a coalition built on reform by inviting the anti-reform party to the table. You fix it by actually reforming.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Mahathir courted PAS during PH 1.0 behind the scenes, contributing to the coalition’s collapse in the 2020 Sheraton Move. UMNO has flirted with PAS repeatedly when politically convenient, forming Muafakat Nasional in 2019. Every Malaysian coalition that courts PAS ends up making concessions on secular governance. Anwar watched this playbook destroy PH 1.0 — and is now running it himself.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You spent GE15 telling Malaysians that PAS was a threat to multicultural Malaysia. Your own coalition partners marched against PAS’s brand of politics. Now that you’re losing elections, PAS is suddenly worth engaging? Either PAS is a threat to Malaysian democracy or it isn’t. Pick one and stick with it. Your voters deserve consistency, not electoral desperation.

PKR Issues Show-Cause Letter to Rafizi, Then Backs Down — Party Can't Even Discipline Its Most Prominent Critic

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, Pandan MP / Fuziah Salleh, PKR Secretary-General

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 What Happened

On February 19, Pandan MP Rafizi Ramli revealed he had received a show-cause letter from PKR secretary-general Fuziah Salleh after telling Sin Chew Daily he would contest in Pandan at GE16 but likely not as a PKR candidate. Rafizi responded saying the meaning of his comments was “understandable to anyone with a good command of Malay” and that he was free to make his own plans after Parliament was dissolved. On March 6, PKR conveyed its decision by email — no action taken, just a reminder to adhere to the party constitution and code of ethics. On March 8, Rafizi publicly described this as a “U-turn,” saying the show-cause letter was issued “with the intention to give him the boot” but the party ultimately blinked.

🔥 Why It Matters

This episode exposes the institutional rot inside PKR — the party that once styled itself as the vanguard of reformasi. A show-cause letter is supposed to be the start of a disciplinary process, not a performative threat followed by a quiet retreat. Rafizi has been openly critical of Anwar and the PKR leadership on podcasts, in media interviews, and on social media for weeks. PKR’s inability to either meaningfully engage with his criticisms or enforce its own disciplinary mechanisms shows a party that is paralysed — too afraid to sack its most prominent critic, but too proud to admit he has a point.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PKR wanted Rafizi gone but didn’t have the spine to do it. As Rafizi himself pointed out, expelling him wouldn’t cost him his parliamentary seat — only a voluntary resignation would trigger a vacancy. So PKR was trapped: sack him and he stays in Parliament as a free agent embarrassing them daily, or back down and look weak. They chose weak. The “reminder to follow party rules” is the political equivalent of a passive-aggressive Post-it note. This is the same party that lectures the nation about institutional reform and good governance while it cannot even manage a basic internal disciplinary process without folding under pressure.

📜 Previous Cases

PKR has a long and inglorious history of factional purges disguised as discipline — and discipline that collapses into farce. The party expelled its own deputy president Azmin Ali in 2020 after the Sheraton Move, but only after the damage was done. Before that, leaders like Zuraida Kamaruddin and Baru Bian walked out with minimal consequences. Rafizi himself was once PKR’s golden boy — the whistleblower who exposed the National Feedlot Corporation scandal. Now he is the party’s most inconvenient voice, and PKR’s playbook remains the same: threaten, posture, and then quietly pretend nothing happened when the political cost of following through becomes clear.

🎯 PH Callout

Anwar and PKR promised Malaysians a new politics — transparent, principled, and accountable. But when your own former deputy president publicly says he won’t contest under your banner, and the best your party can muster is a show-cause letter followed by a meek email reminder, you are not running a political party — you are managing a hostage situation. If PKR cannot enforce discipline on a single MP who has openly challenged the president, how does it expect the public to believe it can enforce standards on an entire government? “Reformasi” used to mean something. Now it means sending sternly worded emails and hoping the problem goes away.

Teresa Kok Says Coalition Unity More Important Than RCI Into MACC — DAP's Own MP Undermines the Accountability Push

Said by: Teresa Kok, MP for Seputeh (DAP)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 What Happened

On March 8, DAP’s Seputeh MP Teresa Kok publicly stated that maintaining unity within the Madani coalition government is more important than her own party’s push for a Royal Commission of Inquiry (RCI) into the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC). This came just days after DAP secretary-general Anthony Loke told Malaysiakini the government’s response to MACC misconduct allegations had been “inadequate” and called for an independent inquiry — a position endorsed by DAP’s national leadership and reinforced by the party’s decision to bring forward its special congress to July 2026.

🔥 Why It Matters

DAP is the single largest party in Anwar’s ruling coalition with 40 MPs, and its entire political identity rests on reform, accountability, and anti-corruption. When the party’s own backbenchers publicly break with the leadership to prioritize coalition survival over institutional accountability, it signals that the internal pressure campaign is already fracturing. Teresa Kok’s statement gives Anwar political cover to continue ignoring calls for an RCI — he can point to voices within DAP itself saying unity matters more. It weakens DAP’s negotiating position at the exact moment the party is supposed to be leveraging the July congress as a credible threat.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

This is the oldest trick in the coalition playbook: reframe the demand for accountability as a threat to stability. Teresa Kok is effectively saying the government should not be held to its own standards because doing so might rock the boat. But DAP didn’t enter government to keep the boat steady — it entered to fix the engine. The MACC under Azam Baki has been credibly accused by Bloomberg of running a “corporate mafia,” breaching civil service shareholding rules multiple times, and weaponizing investigations against political targets. If an RCI into that is too destabilizing for the coalition, then the coalition has already failed its stated purpose. Unity without accountability is just complicity with better PR.

📜 Previous Cases

This is not the first time a DAP MP has publicly undercut the party’s reform stance to protect coalition harmony. The pattern runs deep: DAP stayed silent for months on Azam Baki’s first shareholding scandal in 2021-2022 when it was in opposition, then renewed his contract three times after entering government. The party’s leadership accepted the government’s internal task force instead of pushing harder for an RCI when Bloomberg’s “corporate mafia” exposé dropped in February. And in the Sabah state election last November, DAP was wiped out — winning zero seats — partly because voters saw a party that had traded its principles for Cabinet positions.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysian voters you were the reform coalition. You told the Chinese community you were the only credible check on corruption and cronyism. You told civil society that institutional accountability was non-negotiable. And now, when your own party demands a basic accountability mechanism — an RCI into an anti-corruption agency accused of being a corporate mafia — your own MPs say unity is more important. If DAP cannot hold its own line on the MACC, what exactly is the party for? The July congress is supposed to be a reckoning. If this is the signal DAP’s backbench is sending — that keeping ministerial seats matters more than keeping promises — then voters already have their answer.

[BM] Anwar Amaran 'Golongan Kaya' Manipulasi Media — Sementara Kerajaannya Sendiri Lindungi Ketua SPRM Dari Akauntabiliti

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-07

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 7 Mac 2026, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim menggunakan majlis iftar di Kulim untuk menuduh golongan elit Malaysia yang tidak dinamakan “secara rasuah menggunakan kekayaan mereka untuk mempengaruhi media antarabangsa, naratif dalam talian dan politik tempatan.” Beliau berkata beliau bukan merujuk kepada pembangkang sahaja tetapi kepada “semua orang,” dan menggesa rakyat Malaysia “kekal berwaspada” terhadap “skim dan konspirasi asing.”

🔥 Kenapa Ia Penting

Ini bukan kenyataan sekali sahaja. Ia sebahagian daripada naratif yang sengaja dan semakin meningkat oleh Anwar untuk membingkaikan semula penelitian sah terhadap kerajaannya — terutamanya mengenai skandal pegangan saham Azam Baki — sebagai konspirasi yang dibiayai asing oleh elit bayangan. Dengan memastikan tuduhan kabur (“bukan pembangkang sahaja — semua orang”), Anwar memberikan dirinya perlindungan retorik maksimum untuk menolak mana-mana pengkritik, wartawan, atau institusi yang meminta akauntabiliti kerajaannya.

💩 Kenapa Ia Karut

Anwar menghabiskan berdekad dalam pembangkang menuduh PM-PM sebelumnya perkara yang sama — menggunakan retorik “keselamatan negara” yang kabur untuk membungkam pengkritik dan mengelak akauntabiliti. Kini beliau melakukannya sendiri, dari pentas iftar lagi, sementara kerajaannya enggan menggantung ketua SPRM yang didakwa secara boleh dipercayai melanggar undang-undang pegangan saham. Lelaki yang pernah memimpin perhimpunan reformasi kini memberi amaran kepada rakyat Malaysia supaya tidak “terperangkap dan digunakan sebagai alat” — yang bermaksud: berhenti bertanya soalan sukar tentang mengapa agensi antirasuah saya kelihatan terjejas.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini sesuai dengan corak setua politik Malaysia sendiri. Mahathir menyalahkan “spekulator Yahudi” semasa krisis kewangan Asia 1997. Najib menolak laporan Wall Street Journal mengenai 1MDB sebagai konspirasi oleh “orang tertentu” yang mahu “menjatuhkan kerajaan yang dipilih secara demokratik.” Anwar sendiri, ketika pembangkang, memanggil pengelakan ini autoritarian. Kini berkuasa, beliau mengamalkan rangka kerja retorik yang sama.

🎯 Panggilan PH

Anda berlari atas ketelusan, reformasi institusi, dan kebebasan media. Kini Perdana Menteri anda berdiri di podium menuduh elit yang tidak dinamakan mempersenjatai media — sementara laporan Bloomberg mengenai pegangan saham Azam Baki kekal tidak dijawab, sementara Bersih ditegur secara terbuka kerana memanggil SPRM alat politik. Jika “golongan kaya” adalah masalahnya, mulakan dengan mereka dalam Kabinet anda sendiri yang mendapat manfaat daripada sistem yang anda janji untuk runtuhkan.

Secara berasingan, Reuters melaporkan beberapa wakil rakyat PKR mempertimbangkan bertanding sebagai calon bebas, selepas hilang keyakinan terhadap kepimpinan Anwar.

💩 Kenapa Ia Karut

Bukan langkah Rafizi — respons Anwar kepadanya. Daripada menangani rungutan sah yang menolak Rafizi keluar (pilihan raya parti yang dicurangi, keengganan bertindak atas Azam Baki, pengabaian reformasi), kem Anwar dilaporkan telah mempersenjatai SPRM untuk mengganggu bekas pembantu Rafizi James Chai dan menggunakan jentera kerajaan untuk melemahkan AP Pandan ini. Perdana Menteri yang membina kerjayanya atas reformasi kini menggunakan buku panduan yang sama yang Mahathir dan Najib gunakan terhadapnya.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Politik Malaysia pernah menonton filem ini sebelumnya. Pada 1998, Anwar sendiri adalah orang dalam reformis yang berpisah dan membentuk gerakan yang akhirnya menjadi PKR. Pada 2015, Muhyiddin Yassin dipecat kerana mempersoalkan Najib mengenai 1MDB dan kemudian membentuk Bersatu. Coraknya konsisten: perdana menteri Malaysia melayan perbezaan pendapat dalaman sebagai pengkhianatan dan bukannya akauntabiliti.

🎯 Panggilan PH

Anwar, anda yang mencipta Rafizi. Dia mendedahkan skandal yang anda kempen atasnya. Dia membina kempen akar umbi yang membawa anda ke Putrajaya. Dan anda membalasnya dengan mencurangi pilihan raya dalaman parti anda, meminggirkannya dari kabinet, dan menghalakan agensi antirasuah kepada orang-orangnya. Kini dia membina alternatif — dan anda takut, kerana anda tahu lebih baik daripada sesiapa apa yang berlaku apabila seorang reformis popular berpisah daripada penubuhan yang korup. Anda menulis buku panduan itu pada 1998.

Anwar Warns of 'Wealthy Elites' Manipulating Media — While His Own Government Shields the MACC Chief From Accountability

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-07

📌 What Happened

On March 7, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim used an iftar event in Kulim to accuse unnamed Malaysian elites of “corruptly using their wealth to exert influence on international media, online narratives and local politics.” He said he was not referring only to the opposition but to “everyone,” and urged Malaysians to “remain vigilant” against “foreign schemes and conspiracies.” The statement came just four days after he told Parliament about an alleged plot — involving a former senior minister’s wife, foreign PR firms, and Bloomberg — to destabilise his government.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a one-off remark. It is part of a deliberate, escalating narrative by Anwar to reframe legitimate scrutiny of his government — particularly over the Azam Baki shareholding scandal — as a foreign-funded conspiracy by shadowy elites. By keeping the accusation vague (“not just the opposition — everyone”), Anwar gives himself maximum rhetorical cover to dismiss any critic, journalist, or institution that holds his government accountable. This is the same playbook used by Najib Razak during the 1MDB scandal: when the evidence gets uncomfortable, blame foreign interference.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent decades in opposition accusing previous PMs of exactly this — using vague “national security” rhetoric to silence critics and avoid accountability. Now he’s doing it himself, from an iftar stage no less, while his government refuses to suspend an MACC chief credibly accused of breaching shareholding laws. The man who once led reformasi rallies is now warning Malaysians not to be “trapped and used as tools” — by which he means: stop asking hard questions about why my anti-corruption agency looks compromised. The hypocrisy is not subtle. It is structural.

📜 Previous Cases

This fits a pattern as old as Malaysian politics itself. Mahathir blamed “Jewish speculators” during the 1997 Asian financial crisis to deflect from governance failures. Najib dismissed the Wall Street Journal’s 1MDB reporting as a conspiracy by “certain people” who wanted to “topple a democratically elected government.” Anwar himself, when he was in opposition, called these deflections authoritarian. Now in power, he has adopted the identical rhetorical framework — foreign agents, shadowy elites, media manipulation — with only the names changed.

🎯 PH Callout

You ran on transparency, institutional reform, and media freedom. You told Malaysians that under your government, the press would not be intimidated and corruption would be fought without fear or favour. Now your Prime Minister is standing at a podium accusing unnamed elites of weaponising the media — while Bloomberg’s reporting on Azam Baki’s shareholdings remains unanswered, while Bersih gets publicly scolded for calling the MACC a political tool, and while Rafizi — your own former economy minister — has to take to the streets to demand accountability. If “wealthy elites” are the problem, start with the ones in your own Cabinet who benefit from the system you promised to dismantle.
