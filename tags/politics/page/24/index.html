[BM] Kerajaan Sahkan Akta Hasutan TIDAK Akan Dimansuhkan — Janji Tanda Tangan PH Rasmi Mati

Said by: Shamsul Anuar Nasarah, Deputy Home Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-11-14

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 14 November 2024, Timbalan Menteri Dalam Negeri Datuk Seri Dr Shamsul Anuar Nasarah memberitahu Parlimen bahawa kerajaan tiada rancangan untuk meminda atau memansuhkan Akta Hasutan 1948. Beliau berkata Akta itu akan dikekalkan untuk ‘memelihara ketenteraman dan keselamatan awam.’ Kenyataan ini dibuat dalam Dewan Rakyat Anwar sendiri, oleh timbalan menteri Anwar sendiri, menamatkan sebarang lakonan bahawa PH akan menunaikan salah satu janji reformasi paling menentukan mereka. Menjelang awal 2026, sekurang-kurangnya 90 tangkapan telah dibuat di bawah Akta Hasutan sejak 2024 — di bawah pengawasan PH.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Pemansuhan Akta Hasutan bukan tuntutan pinggiran — ia adalah teras platform kebebasan sivil PH. Anwar sendiri panggil keputusan Najib mengekalkan Akta pada 2014 ‘regresif’ dan bukti ‘kepimpinan lemah’ yang ’tunduk kepada tuntutan sayap kanan.’ Setiap manifesto PH, setiap pelan hala tuju reformasi, setiap ceramah pilihan raya merujuk Akta Hasutan sebagai simbol tadbir urus autoritarian. Mengekalkannya bukan sekadar janji dimungkiri — ia penyerahan ideologi. PH kini mentadbir dengan alat tepat yang dikatakan hanya digunakan penguasa autoritarian.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Anwar pada 2014: ‘Ini pusingan U. Najib mengingkari janji awalnya. Kepimpinan lemahnya menyebabkan beliau tunduk kepada tuntutan sayap kanan partinya sendiri.’ Anwar pada 2024: kekalkan Akta Hasutan, gunakannya untuk tangkap 90 orang, dan hantar timbalan menterinya sahkan di Parlimen. Kemunafikan ini bukan halus — ia perkataan demi perkataan. Orang yang sama yang panggil Akta Hasutan pulangan kepada ‘zaman ISA’ kini menggenakannya sebagai PM. Sekurang-kurangnya Najib jujur tentang menjadi autoritarian. Anwar melakukannya sambil masih memanggil dirinya reformis.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Akta Hasutan telah menjadi janji dimungkiri kegemaran PH sejak berkuasa. Chegubard didakwa di bawahnya pada April 2024 kerana melaporkan dakwaan kasino Forest City. Wartawan, aktivis, dan pengulas telah disiasat. ARTICLE 19, Amnesty International, dan organisasi masyarakat sivil Malaysia semuanya menyeru pemansuhannya. Setiap undang-undang represif yang PH janji akan mansuhkan — Akta Hasutan, PPPA, SOSMA, Seksyen 233 AKM — kekal dalam buku statut dan dikuatkuasakan secara aktif. Peralatan legislatif penindasan tidak berubah; hanya tangan yang menggenakannya.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Dalam kata-kata kamu sendiri, mengekalkan Akta Hasutan adalah ‘regresif,’ ‘pusingan U,’ dan bukti ‘kepimpinan lemah yang tunduk kepada sayap kanan.’ Itu kata-kata kamu tentang Najib. Setiap satu kini terpakai kepada kamu. Kamu ada dua tahun dan majoriti besar parlimen. Kamu pilih untuk kekalkan undang-undang era kolonial yang kamu bina kerjaya politik untuk menentang. Jangan beritahu rakyat Malaysia kamu berbeza daripada BN. Soal Akta Hasutan, kamu ADALAH BN.

Government Confirms Sedition Act Will NOT Be Repealed — PH's Signature Promise Officially Dead

Said by: Shamsul Anuar Nasarah, Deputy Home Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-11-14

📌 What Happened

On November 14, 2024, Deputy Home Minister Datuk Seri Dr Shamsul Anuar Nasarah told Parliament that the government has no plans to amend or repeal the Sedition Act 1948. He said the Act would be maintained to ‘preserve public order and security.’ This statement was made in Anwar’s own Dewan Rakyat, by Anwar’s own deputy minister, ending any pretence that PH would deliver on one of its most defining reform promises. By early 2026, at least 90 arrests had been made under the Sedition Act since 2024 — under PH’s watch.

🔥 Why It Matters

The repeal of the Sedition Act wasn’t some fringe demand — it was the centrepiece of PH’s civil liberties platform. Anwar himself called Najib’s decision to keep the Act in 2014 ‘regressive’ and evidence of ‘weak leadership’ that had ‘caved in to demands of right-wingers.’ Every PH manifesto, every reform roadmap, every election ceramah referenced the Sedition Act as a symbol of authoritarian governance. Keeping it isn’t just a broken promise — it’s an ideological surrender. PH is now governing with the exact tools it said only authoritarians use.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar in 2014: ‘This is a flip-flop. Najib reneged on his earlier promise. His weak leadership has resulted in him caving in to demands of the right-wingers of his own party.’ Anwar in 2024: keeps the Sedition Act, uses it to arrest 90 people, and sends his deputy minister to confirm it in Parliament. The hypocrisy isn’t subtle — it’s word-for-word. The same man who called the Sedition Act a return to ‘ISA days’ now wields it as PM. At least Najib was honest about being authoritarian. Anwar does it while still calling himself a reformist.

📜 Previous Cases

The Sedition Act has been PH’s go-to broken promise since taking power. Chegubard was charged under it in April 2024 for reporting Forest City casino allegations. Reporters, activists, and commentators have been investigated. ARTICLE 19, Amnesty International, and Malaysian civil society organisations have all called for its repeal. Every repressive law PH promised to abolish — the Sedition Act, PPPA, SOSMA, Section 233 CMA — remains on the books and actively enforced. The legislative toolkit of repression hasn’t changed; only the hand that wields it.

🎯 PH Callout

In your own words, keeping the Sedition Act is ‘regressive,’ a ‘flip-flop,’ and evidence of ‘weak leadership caving to right-wingers.’ Those were your words about Najib. Every single one now applies to you. You had two years and a parliamentary supermajority. You chose to keep the colonial-era law that you built a political career opposing. Don’t tell Malaysians you’re different from BN. On the Sedition Act, you are BN.

DAP MPs Whipped Into Supporting 'Regressive' Citizenship Amendments — Malaysian Bar Condemns Government 'in the Strongest Possible Terms'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / DAP Leadership

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-10-17

📌 What Happened

On October 17, 2024, Parliament passed the Constitution (Amendment) Bill 2024 with 206 votes — well above the two-thirds majority required. The bill amended citizenship provisions, including changes that civil society groups, rights organisations, and the Malaysian Bar warned would strip protections from abandoned children and foundlings. When the bill was first tabled in March, a split emerged within DAP, with several MPs unhappy about supporting what the Malaysian Bar called ‘regressive’ amendments. Those MPs were warned to toe the government line. The Malaysian Bar passed a unanimous resolution at its AGM condemning the Anwar government ‘in the strongest possible terms’ for the regressive provisions.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is a government that campaigned on expanding rights, not shrinking them. The citizenship amendment exposed a fundamental tension in PH’s identity: when forced to choose between their reform principles and coalition discipline, DAP — PH’s supposed conscience — chose discipline. The Malaysian Bar, Lawyers for Liberty, and international rights bodies all flagged the same concern: that stateless children and foundlings would lose protections. PH whipped its own MPs into voting for a bill that the legal profession and civil society overwhelmingly opposed. This is what reform looks like when it’s subordinated to political convenience.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH spent years in opposition attacking BN for ramming through constitutional amendments without adequate consultation or regard for minority rights. Now PH rams through its own constitutional changes — with internal dissent silenced and external criticism ignored — and calls it ‘bipartisan reform’ because opposition MPs also voted for it. The 206-vote count is being celebrated as consensus. It’s not consensus — it’s whipping. When your own party members are privately objecting and your own legal profession is publicly condemning you, a big vote count doesn’t mean you got it right. It means you got compliance.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows PH’s pattern of abandoning rights commitments when politically inconvenient. The party that promised to ratify ICCPR hasn’t done so. The party that promised to repeal the Sedition Act confirmed in November 2024 it won’t. The party that championed foundling rights in opposition voted to weaken those protections in government. Every constitutional reform under PH has the same flavour: the parts that expand government power pass easily; the parts that protect individual rights get delayed, watered down, or abandoned.

🎯 PH Callout

You told voters you would be different from BN on rights and the constitution. You told DAP supporters specifically that joining a unity government wouldn’t mean abandoning principles. Then you whipped your own MPs into supporting amendments that the Malaysian Bar — not PAS, not PN, but the legal profession — called regressive. If DAP can’t even vote its conscience on children’s citizenship rights without being told to shut up and comply, what exactly is the point of having DAP in government?
