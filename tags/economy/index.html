[BM] Menteri Ekonomi Raikan Angka 2025 Dan Kekal Sasaran Pertumbuhan 4.5% — Pada Hari Sama Harga Minyak Meletup

Said by: Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir, Economy Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Menteri Ekonomi Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir mengekalkan ramalan pertumbuhan Malaysia 2026 pada 4.0% hingga 4.5% pada 12 Mac 2026, sambil melancarkan sistem pemantauan pelaksanaan Rancangan Malaysia Ke-13 (MyRMK) di Putrajaya. Beliau meraikan pertumbuhan KDNK 2025 sebanyak 5.2%, pengangguran pada paras terendah sedekad iaitu 2.9%, dan inflasi menurun kepada 1.4% — mengisytiharkan bahawa ‘usaha berterusan kerajaan dalam mengukuhkan ekonomi dan meningkatkan kesejahteraan rakyat mula menunjukkan keputusan positif.’ Beliau mengakui konflik Asia Barat ‘boleh mengganggu kestabilan bekalan tenaga global dan perdagangan antarabangsa’ tetapi menegaskan sasaran pertumbuhan kekal.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Kenyataan ini dibuat pada hari yang sama Kementerian Kewangan mengumumkan kenaikan harga bahan api paling tajam dalam tempoh seminggu sejak bertahun-tahun: RON97 naik 60 sen kepada RM3.85 (18%), RON95 tak bersubsidi naik 60 sen kepada RM3.27, dan diesel naik 80 sen kepada RM3.92 (26%). Selat Hormuz terganggu, minyak melepasi $100 setong, dan jawatankuasa khas kerajaan sendiri bergegas menilai kesan ekonomi — namun respons Menteri Ekonomi ialah majlis pelancaran papan pemuka pemantauan dan perayaan angka tahun lepas. Apabila rumah terbakar, kamu tidak menunjukkan pelan bangunan kepada tetamu.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Mengekalkan sasaran pertumbuhan 4.0-4.5% sementara harga bahan api meletup, rantaian bekalan retak, dan inflasi bakal melonjak bukanlah keyakinan — ia penafian berselindung di sebalik kestabilan. Bloomberg melaporkan pada 6 Mac bahawa Malaysia sudah ‘menangguhkan peningkatan sasaran pertumbuhan’ kerana ketidaktentuan Timur Tengah. Kini krisis telah meningkat secara dramatik, dan terbaik yang Menteri Ekonomi boleh tawarkan ialah angka KDNK yang melihat ke belakang dan sistem pemantauan baharu. Jurang antara keyakinan Putrajaya dan realiti rakyat di pam minyak tidak pernah selebar ini.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Ini adalah satu corak. Timbalan Menteri Ekonomi Mohd Shahar Abdullah membuat dakwaan 4.0-4.5% yang sama di Sidang Kemuncak Perbankan dan Kewangan ASEAN pada 9 Mac — walaupun pengerusi sidang kemuncak itu sendiri memberi amaran bahawa ketegangan geopolitik sedang ‘menilai semula risiko di seluruh rantau.’ Sebelum krisis Timur Tengah, Anwar sendiri bercakap tentang meningkatkan sasaran pertumbuhan. Kini pendirian kerajaan telah berubah daripada ‘kami akan tingkatkan’ kepada ‘kami akan kekalkan’ kepada ‘kami akan pantau’ — setiap langkah satu undur yang disamarkan sebagai berhemah.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu menjanjikan tadbir urus berasaskan data dan dasar berasaskan bukti. Data menunjukkan bahan api naik 18-26% dalam seminggu, minyak melepasi $100, dan penimbal bekalan kamu habis pada Mei. Jawatankuasa khas kamu sendiri bermesyuarat pada Jumaat kerana keadaan genting. Namun Menteri Ekonomi di atas pentas meraikan angka 2025 dan melancarkan papan pemuka. Rakyat tidak memerlukan sistem pemantauan — mereka memerlukan kerajaan yang berterus terang tentang apa yang bakal datang. Katakan dengan jelas: sasaran pertumbuhan berisiko, kos sara hidup akan bertambah buruk, dan inilah yang akan kami lakukan. Apa-apa yang kurang daripada itu adalah putaran.

[BM] Pelan Krisis Minyak Loke: Biar Syarikat Penerbangan Cas Anda Lebih, Amaran Penerbangan Mungkin Dibatal — Selamat Hari Raya

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 12 Mac 2026, Menteri Pengangkutan Anthony Loke mempengerusikan mesyuarat bersama pengurusan syarikat penerbangan, pengendali lapangan terbang, pembekal bahan api jet dan pihak berkuasa penerbangan untuk membincangkan impak konflik Asia Barat terhadap kos bahan api yang melambung. Loke mengumumkan bahawa Kementerian Pengangkutan telah mencapai “persetujuan prinsip” untuk membenarkan syarikat penerbangan mengenakan bayaran tambahan — surcaj bahan api — kepada pelanggan bagi menampung kos yang meningkat. Beliau juga memberi amaran bahawa “jika harga bahan api terus meningkat, syarikat penerbangan mungkin perlu menggantung operasi penerbangan tertentu.” Kementerian komited untuk mengadakan mesyuarat mingguan dengan pihak berkepentingan penerbangan selagi konflik berterusan. Langkah jangka pendek turut dibincangkan untuk memastikan perkhidmatan kargo udara bagi bekalan makanan terus beroperasi menjelang Aidilfitri.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Ini buku panduan krisis penerbangan kerajaan dalam satu mesyuarat: tiada intervensi, tiada subsidi, tiada penyerapan kos — hanya kebenaran untuk syarikat penerbangan mengenakan bayaran lebih kepada rakyat. Malaysia sedang memasuki musim puncak perjalanan Hari Raya dengan harga minyak melepasi $100 setong dan Selat Hormuz di bawah ancaman tentera. Berjuta-juta rakyat Malaysia bergantung kepada penerbangan domestik untuk balik kampung, terutama dari Sabah dan Sarawak. Jawapan Loke ialah biarkan syarikat penerbangan kenakan surcaj dan bayangkan kemungkinan pembatalan penerbangan. Inilah kerajaan yang mendakwa sedang “melindungi rakyat” daripada krisis.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Anwar menghabiskan minggu ini memberitahu rakyat Malaysia bahawa kerajaan akan “melindungi” mereka daripada kesan ekonomi konflik Timur Tengah. Kabinet kekalkan RON95 pada RM1.99 dan umumkan RM15 bilion bantuan STR. Tetapi bila melibatkan penerbangan — talian hayat keluarga Malaysia Timur dan kos utama perjalanan Hari Raya — jawapan kerajaan ialah serahkan bil kepada penumpang. Tiada subsidi bahan api jet. Tiada had sementara tambang. Tiada peruntukan kecemasan. Hanya “mesyuarat mingguan” dan persetujuan untuk biar syarikat penerbangan cas lebih. Loke pada dasarnya memberitahu rakyat Malaysia: penerbangan Raya anda akan lebih mahal, dan sesetengahnya mungkin tak terbang langsung. Itu bukan pengurusan krisis. Itu penyerahan krisis.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Corak ini sudah biasa. Semasa pemulihan penerbangan pasca-pandemik 2022, syarikat penerbangan menaikkan tambang dan kerajaan tidak campur tangan walaupun menjanjikan perlindungan pengguna. Loke sendiri memperjuangkan pindaan Kod Perlindungan Pengguna Penerbangan Malaysia pada 2024, mewajibkan bayaran balik bagi kelewatan — tetapi perlindungan struktur terhadap penindasan harga semasa krisis masih tiada. Sementara itu, negara seperti Thailand dan Indonesia bergerak lebih pantas dengan intervensi bahan api dan pakej sokongan penerbangan. Buku panduan Malaysia tetap sama: tubuh jawatankuasa, adakan mesyuarat, keluarkan kenyataan akhbar, dan biar pasaran buat sesuka hati.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Korang janji kerajaan yang utamakan rakyat. Korang habiskan minggu ini mengumumkan bantuan tunai dan mendakwa disiplin fiskal. Tapi bila berjuta-juta rakyat Malaysia perlu terbang pulang untuk Hari Raya dan syarikat penerbangan nak hentak mereka dengan surcaj — atau batalkan penerbangan terus — penyelesaian Menteri Pengangkutan korang ialah bagi kebenaran kepada syarikat penerbangan untuk cas lebih. Mana dana kecemasan penerbangan? Mana subsidi sementara bahan api jet? Mana had tambang untuk laluan domestik semasa musim perayaan? Korang ada masa nak haramkan rumah terbuka Raya atas nama “disiplin fiskal” tapi takda masa nak lindungi rakyat daripada dikeruk di lapangan terbang. Reformasi, rupanya, tak sampai ke harga tiket penerbangan.

[BM] Ramanan Kata Kerajaan 'Mengkaji' Kerja Dari Rumah Untuk Sektor Awam — Thailand Bertindak Segera, Malaysia Masih Buat Kajian

Said by: R. Ramanan, Human Resources Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 12 Mac 2026, Menteri Sumber Manusia Ramanan Ramakrishnan memberitahu wartawan di Putrajaya bahawa langkah-langkah penjimatan kos yang mungkin — termasuk kerja dari rumah bagi kakitangan sektor awam dan swasta — sedang ‘dikaji’ dan akan dibentangkan di mesyuarat Kabinet khas pada Jumaat, 14 Mac. ‘Kami telah melihat apa yang Thailand lakukan, dan kami sedang menjalankan kajian mengenai perkara ini,’ kata Ramanan. Beliau menambah bahawa beliau akan menerima taklimat daripada timbalan-timbalan ketua setiausaha dan kemudian membentangkan penemuan kepada Kabinet. Beliau mengakui konflik Timur Tengah yang berpanjangan dan penutupan Selat Hormuz sebagai punca krisis.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Kabinet Thailand meluluskan arahan kerja dari rumah wajib bagi pegawai kerajaan serta-merta apabila krisis tenaga meningkat. Kerajaan Malaysia — menghadapi krisis yang sama, dengan RON97 naik 18%, diesel naik 26%, dan minyak melepasi $100 setong — masih ‘menjalankan kajian.’ Perbezaannya amat memalukan. Setiap hari pekerja Malaysia berulang-alik ke pejabat yang boleh mereka bekerja dari jauh adalah satu hari kerajaan memaksa rakyat menanggung kos bahan api yang boleh dielakkan dengan satu arahan sahaja. Kata-kata Ramanan sendiri mendedahkan hakikat: ‘Jika kita sendiri tidak mengamalkan penjimatan tetapi meminta rakyat berbuat demikian, itu tidak adil.’ Betul. Jadi mengapa kamu masih mengkaji dan bukannya bertindak?

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Pandemik COVID-19 membuktikan — tiga tahun lalu — bahawa kakitangan awam dan pekerja sektor swasta Malaysia boleh bekerja dari rumah dengan berkesan. Infrastrukturnya ada. Duluan sudah ada. Kerangka undang-undang untuk pengaturan kerja fleksibel sudah wujud di bawah Akta Kerja yang dipinda. Tiada apa lagi yang perlu ‘dikaji.’ Apa yang Ramanan sebenarnya katakan ialah kerajaan tidak mahu mengeluarkan arahan tanpa perlindungan politik daripada mesyuarat Kabinet yang belum berlaku. Ini bukan tadbir urus. Ini birokrasi menunggu kebenaran untuk melakukan perkara yang jelas. Thailand bertindak dalam beberapa jam. Malaysia mengambil berhari-hari — dan masih mengira.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Pada 11 Mac, pangkalan data ini mencatat bahawa respons krisis Anwar terhadap minyak melebihi $100 termasuk ciapan solidariti dan ‘pemantauan’ — tetapi tiada arahan kerja dari rumah, tiada pengecualian tol, tiada kawalan harga makanan. Kini, sehari kemudian, kerajaan telah naik tahap daripada ‘memantau’ kepada ‘mengkaji.’ Semasa Sidang Kemuncak ASEAN pada Mei 2025, Fahmi Fadzil menghabiskan berminggu-minggu mengatakan Kabinet sedang ‘membincangkan’ kerja dari rumah sebelum akhirnya melaksanakannya. Coraknya konsisten: kerajaan Malaysia memperlakukan setiap keputusan seolah-olah memerlukan proses maksimum dan kecekapan minimum, walaupun kos kelewatan ditanggung sepenuhnya oleh pekerja biasa.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu memberitahu rakyat Malaysia kamu akan memimpin melalui teladan. Ramanan sendiri berkata: adalah ’tidak adil’ meminta rakyat berjimat sementara kerajaan tidak berbuat demikian. Jadi bertindaklah. Keluarkan arahan hari ini. Thailand tidak memerlukan mesyuarat Kabinet pada hari Jumaat untuk memutuskan pekerja kerajaan patut berhenti membakar bahan api mahal untuk duduk di pejabat yang mempunyai sambungan internet yang berfungsi sempurna. Setiap hari kamu ‘mengkaji’ adalah satu hari kamu memilih proses berbanding rakyat. Rakyat tidak mampu menanggung kelajuan pertimbangan kamu.

[BM] RON97 Naik 18%, Diesel Naik 26% — Kerajaan Biar Harga Minyak Tak Bersubsidi Meletup Sambil Dakwa 'Melindungi Rakyat'

Said by: Ministry of Finance / Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Kementerian Kewangan mengumumkan pada 11 Mac bahawa berkuat kuasa 12-18 Mac, petrol RON97 akan melonjak 60 sen seliter kepada RM3.85 — kenaikan 18% daripada RM3.25. RON95 tak bersubsidi naik 60 sen kepada RM3.27 — lonjakan 22%. Diesel di Semenanjung Malaysia melonjak 80 sen kepada RM3.92 — kenaikan 26% yang mengejutkan. Kerajaan mengekalkan kadar bersubsidi Budi95 pada RM1.99 bagi pemandu yang layak dan menawarkan tambahan RM100 bagi pemilik kenderaan diesel di bawah skim sedia ada, menaikkan bantuan tunai interim daripada RM200 kepada RM300 bagi bulan Mac.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Ini ialah pelarasan harga bahan api mingguan paling tajam sejak krisis Timur Tengah meningkat. Diesel pada RM3.92 secara langsung memukul logistik, pengangkutan, dan rantaian bekalan makanan — kos yang akan dipindahkan kepada pengguna dalam beberapa hari. Sistem dua peringkat bahan api kini teregang sehingga hampir pecah: RON95 bersubsidi pada RM1.99 manakala RON95 tak bersubsidi pada RM3.27, jurang 64% yang menggalakkan penyeludupan. Perniagaan yang bergantung kepada diesel — nelayan, petani, pemandu lori, pengeluar makanan — menanggung lonjakan kos 26% tanpa kawalan harga hiliran.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Pembingkaian kerajaan ialah teater semata-mata. ‘Kerajaan akan terus menanggung sebahagian subsidi untuk melindungi kesan’ — mereka mensubsidi satu gred bahan api dan membiarkan yang lain meletup. Tambahan diesel RM100 adalah penghinaan: jika kamu pengendali lori yang membakar ratusan liter seminggu, RM300 sebulan menampung kira-kira satu isian pada harga baharu. Sementara itu, baris KKM tentang harga ’lebih rendah daripada negara jiran’ ialah pesongan tertua dalam buku subsidi — ia tidak memberitahu kamu sama ada gaji Malaysia mampu menanggung kos ini. Anwar menjanjikan rasionalisasi subsidi bahan api yang ‘adil dan beransur-ansur.’ Lonjakan diesel 26% dalam seminggu bukan adil mahupun beransur-ansur.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Kerajaan ini telah menendang tin subsidi bahan api sejak memegang jawatan. Subsidi bersasar untuk RON95 (Budi95) sepatutnya menggantikan subsidi menyeluruh, tetapi rasionalisasi diesel telah berulang-ulang berhenti-mula sejak Jun 2024. Setiap kali harga global melonjak, kerajaan bergegas dengan bantuan tunai ad hoc dan bukannya reformasi struktur. Minggu lepas sahaja, Anwar mengakui beliau hanya mampu mengekalkan subsidi RON95 untuk ‘satu atau dua bulan’ — dan kini harga di sekelilingnya meletup.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu memberitahu rakyat Malaysia kamu akan merasionalisasi subsidi secara adil. Kamu memberitahu mereka ekonomi kukuh dan diurus dengan baik. Kamu memberitahu mereka kamu ada rancangan. Lonjakan 18% RON97 dan 26% diesel dalam seminggu bukan rancangan — ia penyerahan kepada kuasa pasaran dengan kenyataan akhbar dilampirkan. Rakyat yang menggunakan RON97 atau menjalankan perniagaan bergantung diesel bukan ‘dilindungi’ — mereka terdedah.

Economy Minister Celebrates 2025 Numbers and Maintains 4.5% Growth Target — On the Same Day Fuel Prices Explode

Said by: Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir, Economy Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir maintained Malaysia’s 2026 growth forecast at 4.0% to 4.5% on March 12, 2026, while launching the 13th Malaysia Plan implementation monitoring system (MyRMK) in Putrajaya. He celebrated 2025’s GDP growth of 5.2%, unemployment at a decade-low 2.9%, and inflation moderating to 1.4% — declaring that “the government’s continuous efforts in strengthening the economy and improving the well-being of the people are beginning to show positive results.” He acknowledged West Asia conflicts “could disrupt the stability of global energy supply and international trade” but insisted the growth target stands.

🔥 Why It Matters

This statement was made on the same day the Ministry of Finance announced the sharpest weekly fuel price hike in years: RON97 up 60 sen to RM3.85 (18%), unsubsidised RON95 up 60 sen to RM3.27, and diesel up 80 sen to RM3.92 (26%). The Strait of Hormuz is disrupted, oil is past $100 a barrel, and the government’s own special committee is scrambling to assess economic fallout — yet the Economy Minister’s response is a launch event for a monitoring dashboard and a celebration of last year’s numbers. When the house is on fire, you don’t show guests the blueprints.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Maintaining a 4.0-4.5% growth target while fuel prices explode, supply chains fracture, and inflation is about to surge is not confidence — it’s denial dressed up as stability. Bloomberg reported on March 6 that Malaysia was already “holding off on a growth upgrade” due to Middle East uncertainty. Now the crisis has escalated dramatically, and the best the Economy Minister can offer is backward-looking GDP figures and a new monitoring system. The gap between Putrajaya’s optimism and the rakyat’s reality at the fuel pump has never been wider.

📜 Previous Cases

This is a pattern. Deputy Economy Minister Mohd Shahar Abdullah made the exact same 4.0-4.5% claim at the ASEAN Banking and Finance Summit on March 9 — even as the summit’s own chairman warned that geopolitical tensions were “repricing risk across the entire region.” Before the Middle East crisis, Anwar himself was talking about upgrading the growth target. Now the government’s position has gone from “we’ll upgrade” to “we’ll hold” to “we’ll monitor” — each step a retreat disguised as prudence.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised data-driven governance and evidence-based policy. The data says fuel is up 18-26% in a single week, oil is past $100, and your supply buffer runs out in May. Your own special committee is meeting Friday because the situation is critical. Yet the Economy Minister is on stage celebrating 2025 numbers and launching dashboards. The rakyat don’t need a monitoring system — they need a government that tells the truth about what’s coming. Say it plainly: the growth target is at risk, the cost of living is about to get worse, and here is what we will do about it. Anything less is spin.

Loke's Fuel Crisis Plan: Let Airlines Charge You More, Warn Flights May Be Cancelled — Happy Hari Raya

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

On March 12, 2026, Transport Minister Anthony Loke chaired a meeting with airline management, airport operators, jet fuel suppliers and aviation authorities to discuss the impact of the West Asia conflict on soaring fuel costs. Loke announced that the Transport Ministry had reached an “agreement in principle” to allow airlines to charge customers additional fees — fuel surcharges — to cover rising costs. He also warned that “if fuel prices continue to rise, airlines may need to suspend certain flight operations.” The ministry committed to weekly meetings with aviation stakeholders while the conflict persists. Short-term measures were discussed to keep air cargo services running for food supplies ahead of Aidilfitri.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the government’s aviation crisis playbook in a single meeting: no intervention, no subsidy, no cost absorption — just permission for airlines to charge the rakyat more. Malaysia is heading into the peak Hari Raya travel season with oil past $100 a barrel and the Strait of Hormuz under military threat. Millions of Malaysians rely on domestic flights to balik kampung, especially those from Sabah and Sarawak. Loke’s response is to let airlines impose surcharges and float the possibility of flight cancellations. This is the same government that claims it is “cushioning the rakyat” from the crisis.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent the week telling Malaysians the government would “protect” them from the economic fallout of the Middle East conflict. Cabinet maintained RON95 at RM1.99 and announced RM15 billion in STR handouts. But when it comes to aviation — a lifeline for East Malaysian families and a key cost for Hari Raya travel — the government’s answer is to let airlines pass the bill to passengers. No jet fuel subsidy. No temporary fare cap. No emergency allocation. Just “weekly meetings” and an agreement to let airlines charge more. Loke is essentially telling Malaysians: your Raya flights will cost more, and some may not fly at all. That is not crisis management. That is crisis delegation.

📜 Previous Cases

This pattern is familiar. During the 2022 post-pandemic aviation recovery, airlines hiked fares and the government did nothing to intervene despite promising consumer protections. Loke himself championed the Malaysian Aviation Consumer Protection Code amendments in 2024, requiring refunds for delays — but structural protections against price gouging during crises remain absent. Meanwhile, countries like Thailand and Indonesia have moved faster on fuel interventions and aviation support packages. Malaysia’s playbook remains the same: form a committee, hold a meeting, issue a press statement, and let the market do what it wants.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised a government that puts rakyat first. You spent the week announcing cash handouts and claiming fiscal discipline. But when millions of Malaysians need to fly home for Hari Raya and airlines are about to hit them with surcharges — or cancel flights entirely — your Transport Minister’s solution is to give airlines permission to charge more. Where is the emergency aviation fund? Where is the temporary jet fuel subsidy? Where is the fare cap for domestic routes during festive periods? You had time to ban Raya open houses for “fiscal discipline” but no time to protect the rakyat from being gouged at the airport. Reformasi, apparently, does not extend to airline pricing.

Ramanan Says Government 'Studying' WFH for Public Sector — Thailand Acted Immediately, Malaysia Still Forming a Study

Said by: R. Ramanan, Human Resources Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

On March 12, 2026, Human Resources Minister Ramanan Ramakrishnan told reporters in Putrajaya that possible cost-saving measures — including work-from-home for public sector employees and the private sector — are being “studied” and will be presented at a special Cabinet meeting on Friday, March 14. “We have seen what Thailand has done, and we are currently conducting studies on this,” Ramanan said. He added that he would receive a briefing from deputy secretaries-general and then present findings to Cabinet. He acknowledged the prolonged Middle East conflict and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz as drivers of the crisis.

🔥 Why It Matters

Thailand’s Cabinet approved mandatory work-from-home for government officials immediately upon the escalation of the energy crisis. Malaysia’s government — facing the same crisis, with RON97 up 18%, diesel up 26%, and oil past $100 a barrel — is still “conducting studies.” The contrast is damning. Every day Malaysian workers commute to offices they could work from remotely is a day the government is forcing the rakyat to absorb fuel costs that could be avoided with a single directive. Ramanan’s own framing gives the game away: “If we ourselves do not practise savings but ask the people to do so, that would not be fair.” Correct. So why are you still studying instead of acting?

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The COVID-19 pandemic proved — three years ago — that Malaysian public servants and private sector workers can work from home effectively. The infrastructure exists. The precedent exists. The legal framework for flexible work arrangements already exists under the amended Employment Act. There is nothing left to “study.” What Ramanan is really saying is that the government does not want to issue a directive without political cover from a Cabinet meeting that hasn’t happened yet. This is not governance. This is a bureaucracy waiting for permission to do the obvious. Thailand acted in hours. Malaysia is taking days — and counting.

📜 Previous Cases

On March 11, this database logged an entry noting that Anwar’s crisis response to $100+ oil included solidarity tweets and “monitoring” — but no WFH mandate, no toll waiver, no food price caps. Now, one day later, the government has graduated from “monitoring” to “studying.” During the ASEAN Summit in May 2025, Fahmi Fadzil spent weeks saying the Cabinet was “deliberating” WFH before eventually implementing it. The pattern is consistent: Malaysia’s government treats every decision as requiring maximum process and minimum urgency, even when the cost of delay is borne entirely by ordinary workers.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would lead by example. Ramanan said it himself: it would be “unfair” to ask the people to save while the government doesn’t. So act. Issue the directive today. Thailand didn’t need a Friday Cabinet meeting to decide that government workers should stop burning expensive fuel to sit in offices with perfectly functional internet connections. Every day you “study” is a day you’re choosing process over people. The rakyat cannot afford your deliberation speed.

RON97 Up 18%, Diesel Up 26% — Government Lets Unsubsidised Fuel Prices Explode While Claiming It's 'Cushioning the Rakyat'

Said by: Ministry of Finance / Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

The Ministry of Finance announced on March 11 that effective March 12-18, RON97 petrol will surge 60 sen per litre to RM3.85 — an 18% increase from RM3.25. Unsubsidised RON95 jumps 60 sen to RM3.27 — a 22% spike. Diesel in Peninsular Malaysia leaps 80 sen to RM3.92 — a staggering 26% increase. The government maintained the Budi95 subsidised rate at RM1.99 for eligible motorists and offered a RM100 top-up for diesel vehicle owners under existing schemes, raising interim cash assistance from RM200 to RM300 for March. The MOF said Malaysia’s prices “remain lower than neighbouring countries” and promised “strengthened enforcement” against fuel leakage.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the sharpest single-week fuel price adjustment since the Middle East crisis escalated. Diesel at RM3.92 directly hits logistics, haulage, and food supply chains — costs that will be passed to consumers within days. The two-tier fuel system is now stretched to breaking point: subsidised RON95 sits at RM1.99 while unsubsidised RON95 is at RM3.27, a 64% gap that incentivises smuggling and gaming. The government’s entire fuel strategy is now a subsidy wall protecting one product while everything else burns. Businesses that run on diesel — fishermen, farmers, truckers, food manufacturers — are absorbing a 26% cost spike with no price controls downstream. This will feed directly into food inflation during Ramadan, when household spending is already elevated.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The government’s framing is pure theatre. “The government will continue to bear part of the subsidy to cushion the impact” — they are subsidising one fuel grade and letting everything else rip. The RM100 diesel top-up is an insult: if you’re a lorry operator burning hundreds of litres a week, RM300 a month covers roughly one fill-up at the new price. Meanwhile, the MOF’s line about prices being “lower than neighbouring countries” is the oldest deflection in the subsidy playbook — it tells you nothing about whether Malaysian wages can absorb these costs. Anwar promised fuel subsidy rationalisation that would be “fair and gradual.” A 26% diesel spike in one week is neither fair nor gradual. It’s crisis management dressed up as policy.

📜 Previous Cases

This government has been kicking the fuel subsidy can since taking office. The targeted subsidy for RON95 (Budi95) was meant to replace blanket subsidies, but diesel rationalisation has been stop-start since June 2024 when diesel subsidies were first restructured. Every time global prices spike, the government scrambles with ad hoc cash handouts instead of structural reform. Just last week, Anwar admitted he could only hold the RON95 subsidy for “one or two months” — and now the prices around it are exploding. The pattern is clear: announce reform, delay implementation, throw cash when crisis hits, repeat.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would rationalise subsidies fairly. You told them the economy was strong and well-managed. You told them you had a plan. An 18% spike in RON97 and a 26% spike in diesel in a single week is not a plan — it’s a surrender to market forces with a press release attached. The rakyat who drive RON97 or run diesel-dependent businesses are not “cushioned” — they are exposed. And the fishermen, farmers, and truckers who feed this country are now subsidising your political survival with their margins. If the Budi95 wall cracks — and at these global prices, it will — you have no backup plan. Just more press statements about how prices are lower than Singapore.
