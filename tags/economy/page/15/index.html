[BM] Malaysia Komited Untuk Tidak Pernah Mengharamkan Eksport Nadir Bumi Ke AS — Di Bawah Perjanjian Perdagangan Zafrul

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, MITI Minister / USTR

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Di bawah Perjanjian Perdagangan Timbal Balik AS-Malaysia yang ditandatangani oleh Tengku Zafrul pada 26 Oktober 2025, Malaysia komited untuk tidak mengenakan ‘sekatan kuantitatif’ (larangan atau kuota) ke atas eksport mineral kritikal atau nadir bumi ke Amerika Syarikat. Helaian fakta USTR secara jelas menonjolkan perkara ini sebagai kemenangan AS. Sementara itu, Zafrul menolak dakwaan Mahathir bahawa Malaysia bersetuju membekalkan nadir bumi mentah pada pulangan rendah, dengan mengatakan dasar sedia ada mewajibkan pemprosesan domestik sebelum eksport.

🔥 KENAPA IA PENTING: Mineral nadir bumi adalah sumber paling strategik abad ke-21 — penting untuk semikonduktor, sistem pertahanan, kenderaan elektrik, dan tenaga boleh baharu. China mengawal kira-kira 60% perlombongan nadir bumi global dan 90% pemprosesan. AS sangat memerlukan bekalan alternatif, dan Malaysia — dengan loji Lynas di Kuantan — adalah nod utama. Dengan komited secara bertulis untuk tidak pernah menyekat eksport ke AS, Malaysia menyerahkan salah satu titik leveraj geopolitik paling berkuasa untuk tidak lebih daripada mengekalkan tarif status quo.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: Indonesia, Australia, dan negara kaya mineral lain menggunakan kawalan eksport sebagai leveraj rundingan untuk menarik pemprosesan hiliran dan pemindahan teknologi. Malaysia menyerahkan leveraj itu begitu sahaja. Komitmen ini bermakna walaupun kerajaan masa depan mahu menggunakan nadir bumi sebagai alat tawar-menawar — seperti yang dilakukan China dengan berkesan — Malaysia terikat secara kontrak untuk tidak berbuat demikian. Zafrul menukarkan aset strategik untuk kadar tarif yang tidak pun bertambah baik.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Sekatan eksport nadir bumi China telah menjadi senjata perdagangan paling berkesan selama berdekad-dekad. Larangan eksport nikel Indonesia memaksa syarikat membina kilang lebur secara domestik, mewujudkan pekerjaan dan nilai tambah. Malaysia mempunyai peluang yang sama — dan menandatanganinya begitu sahaja dalam perjanjian perdagangan.

🎯 TEGURAN KEPADA PH: Anda baru memberi Amerika Syarikat jaminan bertulis bahawa Malaysia tidak akan pernah menggunakan sumber nadir buminya sebagai leveraj. Bukan untuk pemindahan teknologi. Bukan untuk pekerjaan hiliran. Bukan untuk terma perdagangan yang lebih baik. Untuk tiada apa-apa. Menteri anda kata dasar pemprosesan-dahulu Malaysia masih utuh — tetapi perjanjian itu kata anda tidak boleh menyekat eksport. Apabila Washington datang mengetuk pintu untuk mineral mentah, ‘dasar’ anda akan tunduk kepada obligasi perjanjian.

[BM] Perjanjian Perdagangan AS-Malaysia Dipanggil 'Perjanjian Terburuk Sejak Merdeka' Dan 'Tindakan Menyerah Kalah'

Said by: Tommy Thomas, Former Attorney General (and critics)

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

Perjanjian Yang Menjual Kedaulatan Malaysia

Pada 26 Oktober 2025, Anwar Ibrahim dan Donald Trump menandatangani Perjanjian Perdagangan Timbal Balik AS-Malaysia (ART) di Sidang Kemuncak ASEAN. Bekas Peguam Negara Tommy Thomas memanggilnya perjanjian terburuk yang Malaysia masuki sejak merdeka pada 1957. Beliau tidak membesar-besarkan.

Fakta-Fakta

Fakta: Perjanjian ini mengekalkan tarif AS 19% ke atas barangan Malaysia — turun dari 25%, tetapi masih menghukum mengikut sebarang standard. Tarif efektif Malaysia ke atas barangan AS sebelum perjanjian ini adalah kira-kira 6%. AS mendapat bahagian lebih baik tiga kali ganda.

Fakta: Malaysia komited untuk: menguatkuasakan peraturan pengelakan tarif AS terhadap negara ketiga, menyelaraskan dengan kawalan eksport dan rejim sekatan AS, tidak mengharamkan atau menyekat eksport mineral kritikal atau nadir bumi ke AS, menahan diri daripada mengenakan cukai perkhidmatan digital ke atas syarikat teknologi AS, dan membeli barangan dan perkhidmatan Amerika tambahan.

Fakta: Klausa penamatan membenarkan AS mengenakan semula tarif asal 25% jika Malaysia memasuki perjanjian perdagangan yang “menjejaskan kepentingan penting AS.” Ini secara efektif memberi Washington kuasa veto ke atas dasar perdagangan bebas Malaysia — termasuk dengan China, rakan dagangan terbesar Malaysia.

Fakta: Teks penuh perjanjian itu pertama kali diterbitkan di laman web Rumah Putih sebelum dikongsi dengan Parlimen Malaysia. Penggubal undang-undang Malaysia mengetahui terma perjanjian perdagangan mereka sendiri daripada kenyataan akhbar kerajaan asing.

Fakta: Bekas PM Mahathir berkata peruntukannya berjumlah “menyerahkan” kemerdekaan negara. Walaupun bagi seorang yang menyelia bahagian perjanjian meragukan sendiri, ini terlalu jauh.

Fakta: Analisis East Asia Forum, bertajuk “The Art of the Deal Lost in US-Malaysia Pact,” berhujah Malaysia menyerahkan kawalan eksport mineral kritikal, hak percukaian perkhidmatan digital, dan dasar perdagangan bebas sebagai pertukaran untuk pemotongan tarif yang masih meletakkan Malaysia membayar tiga kali ganda daripada AS.

Fakta: The Guardian melaporkan Anwar “mempertahankan” perjanjian itu dengan berhujah ia adalah “yang terbaik Malaysia boleh dapat dalam keadaan ini” — hujah yang mengakui perjanjian itu buruk sambil mendakwa ia boleh jadi lebih teruk.

Fakta: SCMP melaporkan Anwar “menghentam” pengkritik kerana menuduhnya menyerahkan kedaulatan — tetapi tidak pernah menangani isi hujah mereka, hanya nada mereka.

Kenapa Ia Penting

  • Ini bukan perjanjian timbal balik mengikut sebarang definisi. Malaysia membuat konsesi luas dan mengikat merentas mineral, percukaian digital, penjajaran sekatan, dan perolehan. AS mengurangkan tarif punitif sebanyak 6 mata peratusan dan mengekalkan hak untuk mengenakan semula bila-bila masa. Dalam mana-mana buku teks rundingan, ini dipanggil penyerahan.
  • Klausa kedaulatan adalah peruntukan paling berbahaya. Jika Malaysia mahu memperdalam hubungan dagangan dengan China — rakan dagangan terbesarnya — AS boleh menamatkan ART dan mengenakan semula tarif 25%. Malaysia secara efektif telah memberi Washington veto ke atas dasar ekonomi luarnya.
  • Menerbitkan butiran di laman web Rumah Putih sebelum memaklumkan Parlimen bukan sekadar memalukan — ia adalah penghinaan terhadap perlembagaan. Perjanjian perdagangan yang mengekang kedaulatan negara sepatutnya memerlukan perbahasan dan kelulusan parlimen sebelum ditandatangani. Anwar memintas badan perundangan mengenai perjanjian ekonomi paling berkesan dalam beberapa dekad.
  • Setiap jiran ASEAN sedang memerhati. Indonesia menolak cadangan AS yang serupa. Vietnam berunding lebih keras. Thailand menangguhkan. Malaysia menandatangani dahulu dan bertanya soalan kemudian. Ini melemahkan posisi rundingan Malaysia dalam setiap perbincangan perdagangan dua hala dan pelbagai hala masa depan.
  • Pembelaan ‘yang terbaik yang kita boleh dapat’ adalah pengakuan kelemahan, bukan justifikasi. Jika ini benar-benar hasil terbaik, bermakna leveraj diplomatik Malaysia lebih lemah daripada yang didakwa kerajaan — yang menimbulkan persoalan tentang keseluruhan jenama dasar luar Madani. *Sumber: The Guardian · East Asia Forum · SCMP · *Straits Times

[BM] Tengku Zafrul Rundingkan Perjanjian Perdagangan AS Yang Pengkritik Panggil 'Perjanjian Terburuk Sejak Merdeka' — Kemudian Dinaikkan Pangkat

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, former MITI Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 26 Oktober 2025, Menteri MITI ketika itu Tengku Zafrul menandatangani Perjanjian Perdagangan Timbal Balik (ART) dengan Amerika Syarikat di Sidang Kemuncak ASEAN ke-47 di Kuala Lumpur. Perjanjian itu mengkomitkan Malaysia untuk menyediakan akses pasaran keutamaan bagi produk AS merentas bahan kimia, jentera, kenderaan, tenusu, ayam, daging babi, beras, dan etanol bahan api. Malaysia juga komited untuk tidak mengenakan larangan atau kuota ke atas eksport mineral kritikal atau nadir bumi ke AS. Sebagai balasan, AS mengekalkan tarif timbal balik 19% ke atas barangan Malaysia — dengan hanya 60.4% baris tarif pada kadar sifar. Pengkritik dari seluruh spektrum politik, termasuk Mahathir dan Rafizi Ramli, memanggilnya penyerahan berat sebelah.

🔥 KENAPA IA PENTING: Helaian fakta USTR diterbitkan di laman web Rumah Putih sebelum dikongsi dengan Parlimen Malaysia — bermakna Washington memberitahu dunia apa yang Malaysia persetujui sebelum penggubal undang-undang Malaysia sendiri tahu. Perjanjian itu memerlukan Malaysia menyelaraskan dengan sekatan AS, bertindak terhadap ‘amalan tidak adil’ oleh firma negara ketiga (baca: China), dan membuka pasaran dalam sektor di mana pengeluar Malaysia terdedah. Eurasia Review menyatakan perjanjian itu ‘disusun tergesa-gesa untuk menyenangkan presiden AS semasa lawatannya’ dan ’tidak bertujuan untuk menarik banyak perhatian.’ Ia menarik banyak perhatian.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: Zafrul mempertahankan perjanjian itu sebagai ‘senario terbaik’ — seolah-olah berunding dari posisi lemah adalah satu pencapaian. Beliau memberitahu Parlimen: ‘Kita tiada pilihan selain berunding.’ Itu bukan pembelaan; itu pengakuan. Dasar luar neutral Malaysia — yang dikekalkan selama berdekad-dekad — terjejas dalam satu majlis tandatangan. Ganjaran Zafrul untuk ini? Kenaikan pangkat kepada Penasihat Politik Kanan PM dan pelantikan sebagai Pengerusi MIDA. Dalam Malaysia PH, anda dinaikkan pangkat kerana menandatangani penyerahan kedaulatan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia pernah menandatangani perjanjian perdagangan sebelum ini tanpa tahap kontroversi ini. CPTPP dirundingkan selama bertahun-tahun dengan penelitian parlimen yang meluas. ART digegas dalam beberapa minggu, ditandatangani di sidang kemuncak, dan dipertahankan selepas fakta. Corak keputusan ekonomi PH — dari rangkaian dwi 5G ke ECRL ke ini — adalah konsisten: buat pilihan yang selesa secara politik, kemudian habiskan berbulan-bulan mempertahankannya.

🎯 TEGURAN KEPADA PH: Menteri anda menandatangani perjanjian yang mengkomitkan Malaysia kepada pematuhan sekatan AS dan jaminan eksport mineral, dan pembelaan anda adalah ‘kita tiada pilihan.’ Anda sentiasa ada pilihan — anda memilih untuk tunduk. Kemudian anda naikkan pangkat orang yang melakukannya. Jika ini benar-benar ‘senario terbaik,’ posisi rundingan Malaysia di bawah PH lebih teruk daripada yang sesiapa bayangkan.

Malaysia Committed to Never Banning Rare Earth Exports to the US — Under Zafrul's Trade Deal

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, MITI Minister / USTR

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Under the US-Malaysia Agreement on Reciprocal Trade signed by Tengku Zafrul on 26 October 2025, Malaysia committed to not imposing ‘quantitative restrictions’ (bans or quotas) on exports of critical or rare-earth minerals to the United States. The USTR fact sheet explicitly highlights this as a US victory. Meanwhile, Zafrul dismissed Mahathir’s claim that Malaysia agreed to supply unprocessed rare earth at low returns, saying existing policy mandates domestic processing before export.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rare earth minerals are the 21st century’s most strategic resource — essential for semiconductors, defence systems, EVs, and renewable energy. China controls roughly 60% of global rare earth mining and 90% of processing. The US desperately needs alternative supplies, and Malaysia — with Lynas’s Kuantan plant — is a key node. By committing in writing to never restrict exports to the US, Malaysia surrendered one of its most powerful geopolitical leverage points for nothing more than maintaining the status quo tariff.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Indonesia, Australia, and other mineral-rich nations are using export controls as negotiating leverage to attract downstream processing and technology transfer. Malaysia gave that leverage away. The commitment means that even if a future government wants to use rare earth as a bargaining chip — as China has done effectively — Malaysia is contractually bound not to. Zafrul traded a strategic asset for a tariff rate that didn’t even improve.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: China’s rare earth export restrictions have been its most effective trade weapon for decades. Indonesia’s nickel export ban forced companies to build smelters domestically, creating jobs and value-add. Malaysia had the same opportunity — and signed it away in a trade deal.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You just gave the United States a written guarantee that Malaysia will never use its rare earth resources as leverage. Not for technology transfer. Not for downstream jobs. Not for better trade terms. For nothing. Your minister says Malaysia’s processing-first policy is intact — but the agreement says you can’t restrict exports. When Washington comes knocking for raw minerals, your ‘policy’ will fold against a treaty obligation.

Tengku Zafrul Negotiated the US Trade Deal Critics Call 'Worst Agreement Since Independence' — Then Got Promoted

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, former MITI Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 26 October 2025, then-MITI Minister Tengku Zafrul signed the Agreement on Reciprocal Trade (ART) with the United States at the 47th ASEAN Summit in Kuala Lumpur. The deal committed Malaysia to providing preferential market access for US products across chemicals, machinery, vehicles, dairy, poultry, pork, rice, and fuel ethanol. Malaysia also committed to not imposing bans or quotas on the export of critical or rare-earth minerals to the US. In return, the US maintained a 19% reciprocal tariff on Malaysian goods — with only 60.4% of tariff lines zero-rated. Critics from across the political spectrum, including Mahathir and Rafizi Ramli, called it a one-sided surrender.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The USTR fact sheet was published on the White House website before it was shared with Malaysia’s Parliament — meaning Washington told the world what Malaysia agreed to before Malaysia’s own legislators knew. The deal requires Malaysia to mirror US sanctions, act against ‘unfair practices’ by third-country firms (read: China), and open markets in sectors where Malaysian producers are vulnerable. Eurasia Review noted the deal was ‘hastily put together to please the US president during his visit’ and was ’not intended to catch much attention.’ It caught plenty.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Zafrul defended the deal as the ‘best case scenario’ — as if negotiating from weakness is an achievement. He told Parliament: ‘We had no choice but to negotiate.’ That is not a defence; it’s a confession. Malaysia’s neutral foreign policy — maintained for decades — was compromised in one signing ceremony. Zafrul’s reward for this? A promotion to Senior Political Advisor to the PM and appointment as MIDA chairman. In PH’s Malaysia, you get promoted for signing away sovereignty.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has signed trade agreements before without this level of controversy. The CPTPP was negotiated over years with extensive parliamentary scrutiny. The ART was rushed through in weeks, signed at a summit, and defended after the fact. The pattern of PH economic decisions — from 5G dual network to ECRL to this — is consistent: make the politically convenient choice, then spend months defending it.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your minister signed a deal that commits Malaysia to US sanctions compliance and mineral export guarantees, and your defence is ‘we had no choice.’ You always have a choice — you chose to capitulate. Then you promoted the man who did it. If this was really the ‘best case scenario,’ Malaysia’s negotiating position under PH is worse than anyone imagined.

Malaysian MPs and the public learned the details of their own country’s trade agreement from an American government website. The deal’s terms were published in Washington before they were presented in Putrajaya.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A sovereign nation’s trade commitments should be communicated to its own parliament and citizens before they are announced by the other party. It also meant the US controlled the narrative — the terms were framed as an American victory before Malaysia could spin its own story.

Under PH, the narrative was literally ceded to the White House. Eurasia Review noted the deal was ‘hastily put together’ and ’not intended to catch much attention.’ The haste is the point — rushed deals favour the stronger party.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You let the White House tell Malaysians what their own government agreed to. That is not a partnership of equals. If you can’t even control the communications around your own trade deals, how do you expect Malaysians to believe you controlled the negotiations?

US-Malaysia Trade Deal Called 'Worst Agreement Since Independence' and 'Act of Surrender'

Said by: Tommy Thomas, Former Attorney General (and critics)

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

The Deal That Sold Malaysia’s Sovereignty

On 26 October 2025, Anwar Ibrahim and Donald Trump signed the US-Malaysia Agreement on Reciprocal Trade (ART) at the ASEAN Summit. Former Attorney-General Tommy Thomas called it the worst agreement Malaysia has entered since independence in 1957. He was not exaggerating.

The Facts

Fact: The deal maintains a 19% US tariff on Malaysian goods — down from 25%, but still punitive by any standard. Malaysia’s effective tariff on US goods before this deal was roughly 6%. The US got the better end by a factor of three.

Fact: Malaysia committed to: enforce US tariff evasion rules against third countries, align with US export controls and sanctions regimes, not ban or restrict exports of critical minerals or rare earths to the US, refrain from imposing digital services taxes on US tech companies, and purchase additional American goods and services.

Fact: A termination clause allows the US to reimpose the original 25% tariff if Malaysia enters a trade deal that “jeopardizes essential US interests.” This effectively gives Washington veto power over Malaysia’s independent trade policy — including with China, Malaysia’s largest trading partner.

Fact: The deal’s full text was first published on the White House website before being shared with Malaysian Parliament. Malaysian lawmakers learned the terms of their own trade agreement from a foreign government’s press release.

Fact: Former PM Mahathir said the provisions amounted to “handing over” the country’s independence. Even for a man who oversaw his own share of questionable deals, this one was a bridge too far.

Fact: East Asia Forum’s analysis, titled “The Art of the Deal Lost in US-Malaysia Pact,” argued Malaysia surrendered critical mineral export controls, digital services taxation rights, and independent trade policy in exchange for a tariff cut that still left Malaysia paying three times what the US pays.

Fact: The Guardian reported that Anwar “defended” the deal by arguing it was “the best Malaysia could get under the circumstances” — an argument that concedes the deal is bad while claiming it could have been worse.

Fact: SCMP reported Anwar “slammed” critics for accusing him of surrendering sovereignty — but never addressed the substance of their arguments, only their tone.

Why It Matters

  • This is not a reciprocal deal by any definition of the word. Malaysia makes extensive, binding concessions across minerals, digital taxation, sanctions alignment, and procurement. The US reduces a punitive tariff by 6 percentage points and retains the right to reimpose it at will. In any negotiation textbook, this is called capitulation.
  • The sovereignty clause is the most dangerous provision. If Malaysia wants to deepen trade ties with China — its largest trading partner — the US can terminate the ART and reimpose 25% tariffs. Malaysia has effectively given Washington a veto over its foreign economic policy.
  • Publishing the details on the White House website before informing Parliament is not just embarrassing — it is a constitutional affront. Trade agreements that constrain national sovereignty should require parliamentary debate and approval before signing. Anwar bypassed the legislature on the most consequential economic agreement in decades.
  • Every ASEAN neighbour is watching. Indonesia walked away from a similar US proposal. Vietnam negotiated harder. Thailand delayed. Malaysia signed first and asked questions later. This weakens Malaysia’s negotiating position in every future bilateral and multilateral trade discussion.
  • The “best we could get” defence is an admission of weakness, not a justification. If this was truly the best outcome, it means Malaysia’s diplomatic leverage is weaker than the government has claimed — which raises questions about the entire Madani foreign policy brand. *Sources: The Guardian · East Asia Forum · SCMP · *Straits Times
