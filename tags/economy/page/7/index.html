MITI Accused of Stealing World Expo Osaka Pavilion Concept — Creative Agency Serves Letter of Demand

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, MITI Minister / Current Media Group

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-08-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In August 2025, MITI under Tengku Zafrul was served a letter of demand by Current Media Group (CMG), a creative consultancy that claimed the ministry stole its concept for Malaysia’s World Expo 2025 Osaka pavilion without providing recognition or payment. The allegation was that CMG’s original pitch was used as the basis for the pavilion design without a contract or credit. Zafrul addressed the matter in a parliamentary reply to Kepong MP Lim Lip Eng.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: If a government ministry used a private company’s intellectual property without payment or credit, that is theft — regardless of whether it’s called ‘inspiration’ or ‘parallel development.’ World Expo pavilions are high-profile national showcases with significant budgets. The allegation that MITI took a concept without paying for it raises serious questions about procurement integrity and intellectual property respect within the ministry.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The ‘reformist’ government that lectures the world about good governance and fair dealing allegedly stole a creative concept from a Malaysian company. PH came to power promising transparent procurement and respect for due process. If MITI can’t even commission a pavilion design without a letter of demand from the original creator, what does that say about how other, larger contracts are handled behind closed doors?

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Government IP theft from private contractors is a recurring complaint in Malaysia. Creative agencies, architects, and consultants have long reported that government pitches are treated as free work — ideas are taken, contracts go elsewhere, and the original creators are ghosted. Under BN, this was standard. Under PH, it was supposed to change.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You represent Malaysia on the world stage at Osaka and you can’t even pay the people who designed it? If the allegation is true, compensate CMG and investigate how this happened. If it’s false, release the procurement documents. Transparency works both ways — you don’t get to lecture the world about governance while your own ministry is served letters of demand for IP theft.

EPF Retirement Crisis — Average Balance of RM47,600 at Age 55 Means POVERTY in Old Age

Said by: EPF Data / Retirement Adequacy Studies

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-06-01

📌 What Happened

EPF data consistently shows that the majority of Malaysians have grossly inadequate retirement savings. The median balance at age 55 (mandatory withdrawal age) is approximately RM47,600 — enough to last roughly 5 years at RM800/month, well below any reasonable poverty line. The government’s policy of allowing repeated EPF withdrawals (Account 3 saw RM14.79 billion withdrawn by 4.63 million contributors) has accelerated retirement fund depletion.

🔥 Why It Matters

Malaysia has no comprehensive social safety net for retirees outside of EPF (for private sector) and pensions (for civil servants). When EPF runs dry — which it does for most retirees within 5-10 years — elderly Malaysians face destitution. There is no universal old-age pension, inadequate public aged care facilities, and limited social assistance. A generation of Malaysians is heading toward retirement poverty.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Instead of strengthening retirement savings, the government has repeatedly raided EPF for short-term political wins. Account 3 — the latest withdrawal scheme — was presented as ‘flexibility’ but in reality it’s the government allowing citizens to spend their future to survive the present. When you can’t fix wages, cost of living, or employment, the politically easy answer is ’let them eat their retirement.’ It’s not a solution — it’s kicking the catastrophe 20 years down the road.

📜 Previous Cases

EPF withdrawals during COVID-19 (under PN/BN) depleted RM145 billion from the fund. PH criticised those withdrawals as irresponsible. Then PH introduced Account 3 with even easier withdrawal terms. Every government — BN, PN, PH — has treated EPF as a piggy bank for political convenience. The retirees of 2040 will pay the price.

🎯 PH Callout

You criticised Muhyiddin for allowing COVID EPF withdrawals. Then you created Account 3, making it even EASIER to withdraw retirement savings. RM14.79 billion gone in months. You’ve traded the financial security of 4.63 million Malaysians’ retirement for short-term political approval. When these people are 70 and destitute, they’ll remember who let them spend their old age today.

FDI Announcements vs Actual Jobs — RM Numbers Look Great, But Where Are the Jobs for Malaysians?

Said by: MIDA / Economic Analysts

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-06-01

📌 What Happened

The government regularly announces massive Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) figures — billions of ringgit in commitments from tech giants, manufacturers, and data centre operators. These announcements generate impressive headlines. However, the gap between announced FDI and actual realised investment, and between investment and actual Malaysian employment, remains significant.

🔥 Why It Matters

FDI announcements are easy PR wins — you stand next to a foreign CEO, sign an MOU, and announce billions. But approved FDI ≠ realised FDI (many projects are delayed or cancelled). Realised FDI ≠ Malaysian jobs (many investments are capital-intensive with minimal employment, or employ foreign specialists). And the jobs that are created are often not the high-quality, well-paying positions that address Malaysia’s unemployment and wage stagnation challenges.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Data centres — the crown jewel of FDI announcements — employ remarkably few people relative to their investment size. A billion-ringgit data centre might employ 50-200 permanent staff, mostly technical specialists. Compare that to a factory employing thousands. The government promotes FDI numbers that look impressive on paper while youth unemployment stays at 12.28% and graduate unemployment remains persistent. The headline numbers don’t translate to the lives of ordinary Malaysians.

📜 Previous Cases

BN also regularly announced massive FDI figures while employment quality stagnated. Najib’s Economic Transformation Programme produced impressive investment statistics but failed to meaningfully raise median wages or reduce inequality. PH promised that their FDI strategy would be different — more quality, more jobs, more technology transfer. The strategy is identical: announce big numbers, hope nobody checks the employment data.

🎯 PH Callout

Stop announcing FDI commitments as if they’re achievements. Announce JOBS. Announce WAGES. Announce how many Malaysians got employed, at what salary, in what roles. A billion ringgit in FDI that creates 100 jobs is not an economic success story — it’s a tax incentive giveaway that benefits foreign shareholders while Malaysians deliver their food on motorcycles for RM6 per trip.

Foreign Labour Dependency Trap — Economy Can't Function Without Workers Malaysia Treats Like Disposable Parts

Said by: Labour Economists / ILO

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-06-01

📌 What Happened

Malaysia remains heavily dependent on foreign workers — estimated at 2-3 million documented and possibly millions more undocumented — in critical sectors including construction, manufacturing, agriculture, and services. The government’s foreign worker policy oscillates between liberalisation and restriction based on political winds rather than economic planning, creating chronic uncertainty for both employers and workers.

🔥 Why It Matters

Foreign worker dependency reveals Malaysia’s failure to move up the value chain — the economy is still structurally reliant on cheap, exploitable labour rather than automation, technology, and skilled domestic workers. This dependency suppresses local wages, enables exploitation, and creates social tensions.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Every government promises to ‘reduce dependency on foreign labour’ and ‘move to a high-value economy.’ Every government then allows millions of foreign workers because the industries that fund political parties can’t function without them. Palm oil plantations, construction companies, and factories NEED cheap foreign labour. Politicians NEED campaign funding from these industries. The result is a permanent cycle of promised reform and actual dependency.

📜 Previous Cases

BN’s foreign worker policies were equally chaotic and exploitation-enabling. The US State Department’s Trafficking in Persons report has flagged Malaysia’s forced labour issues for years — under BN, PN, and PH. The multi-tier foreign worker levy system, repeatedly delayed, was supposed to be PH’s solution. It wasn’t.

🎯 PH Callout

Your economy literally can’t build a building, harvest a palm oil fruit, or run a restaurant without foreign workers — workers who have no rights, no protections, and no voice. Your ‘Madani’ framework apparently doesn’t extend to the millions of human beings who actually make your economy function. Fix the exploitation, fix the wages, fix the dependency — or stop pretending Malaysia is a civilised economy.
