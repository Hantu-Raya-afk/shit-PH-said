Tengku Zafrul Negotiated the US Trade Deal Critics Call 'Worst Agreement Since Independence' — Then Got Promoted

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, former MITI Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 26 October 2025, then-MITI Minister Tengku Zafrul signed the Agreement on Reciprocal Trade (ART) with the United States at the 47th ASEAN Summit in Kuala Lumpur. The deal committed Malaysia to providing preferential market access for US products across chemicals, machinery, vehicles, dairy, poultry, pork, rice, and fuel ethanol. Malaysia also committed to not imposing bans or quotas on the export of critical or rare-earth minerals to the US. In return, the US maintained a 19% reciprocal tariff on Malaysian goods — with only 60.4% of tariff lines zero-rated. Critics from across the political spectrum, including Mahathir and Rafizi Ramli, called it a one-sided surrender.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The USTR fact sheet was published on the White House website before it was shared with Malaysia’s Parliament — meaning Washington told the world what Malaysia agreed to before Malaysia’s own legislators knew. The deal requires Malaysia to mirror US sanctions, act against ‘unfair practices’ by third-country firms (read: China), and open markets in sectors where Malaysian producers are vulnerable. Eurasia Review noted the deal was ‘hastily put together to please the US president during his visit’ and was ’not intended to catch much attention.’ It caught plenty.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Zafrul defended the deal as the ‘best case scenario’ — as if negotiating from weakness is an achievement. He told Parliament: ‘We had no choice but to negotiate.’ That is not a defence; it’s a confession. Malaysia’s neutral foreign policy — maintained for decades — was compromised in one signing ceremony. Zafrul’s reward for this? A promotion to Senior Political Advisor to the PM and appointment as MIDA chairman. In PH’s Malaysia, you get promoted for signing away sovereignty.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has signed trade agreements before without this level of controversy. The CPTPP was negotiated over years with extensive parliamentary scrutiny. The ART was rushed through in weeks, signed at a summit, and defended after the fact. The pattern of PH economic decisions — from 5G dual network to ECRL to this — is consistent: make the politically convenient choice, then spend months defending it.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your minister signed a deal that commits Malaysia to US sanctions compliance and mineral export guarantees, and your defence is ‘we had no choice.’ You always have a choice — you chose to capitulate. Then you promoted the man who did it. If this was really the ‘best case scenario,’ Malaysia’s negotiating position under PH is worse than anyone imagined.

Sources: CNA | USTR | Eurasia Review

US Trade Deal Details Published on White House Website Before Malaysian Parliament Was Told

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, MITI Minister / White House

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: When the US-Malaysia Agreement on Reciprocal Trade was signed on 26 October 2025, the USTR published a detailed fact sheet on the White House website outlining Malaysia’s commitments — including preferential market access for US goods, commitments on rare earth minerals, and tariff schedules. Malaysian MPs and the public learned the details of their own country’s trade agreement from an American government website. The deal’s terms were published in Washington before they were presented in Putrajaya.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A sovereign nation’s trade commitments should be communicated to its own parliament and citizens before they are announced by the other party. The fact that Malaysians had to read the White House website to understand what their government had agreed to is a humiliation of parliamentary sovereignty. It also meant the US controlled the narrative — the terms were framed as an American victory before Malaysia could spin its own story.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH promised transparent governance and parliamentary supremacy. Then Zafrul signed a trade agreement and let Washington publish the terms first. Fahmi Fadzil is the Communications Minister — where was the proactive Malaysian disclosure? The sequence tells you everything about the power dynamics: the US dictated the terms, dictated the timeline, and dictated the narrative. Malaysia’s role was to sign and defend.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s TPPA negotiations under Najib were also criticised for opacity — but at least the government attempted to control its own narrative. Under PH, the narrative was literally ceded to the White House. Eurasia Review noted the deal was ‘hastily put together’ and ’not intended to catch much attention.’ The haste is the point — rushed deals favour the stronger party.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You let the White House tell Malaysians what their own government agreed to. That is not a partnership of equals. That is a relationship where one party writes the press release and the other party nods. If you can’t even control the communications around your own trade deals, how do you expect Malaysians to believe you controlled the negotiations?

Sources: USTR Fact Sheet | Eurasia Review

US-Malaysia Trade Deal Called 'Worst Agreement Since Independence' and 'Act of Surrender'

Said by: Tommy Thomas, Former Attorney General (and critics)

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

The Deal That Sold Malaysia’s Sovereignty

On 26 October 2025, Anwar Ibrahim and Donald Trump signed the US-Malaysia Agreement on Reciprocal Trade (ART) at the ASEAN Summit. Former Attorney-General Tommy Thomas called it the worst agreement Malaysia has entered since independence in 1957. He was not exaggerating.

The Facts

Fact: The deal maintains a 19% US tariff on Malaysian goods — down from 25%, but still punitive by any standard. Malaysia’s effective tariff on US goods before this deal was roughly 6%. The US got the better end by a factor of three.

Fact: Malaysia committed to: enforce US tariff evasion rules against third countries, align with US export controls and sanctions regimes, not ban or restrict exports of critical minerals or rare earths to the US, refrain from imposing digital services taxes on US tech companies, and purchase additional American goods and services.

Fact: A termination clause allows the US to reimpose the original 25% tariff if Malaysia enters a trade deal that “jeopardizes essential US interests.” This effectively gives Washington veto power over Malaysia’s independent trade policy — including with China, Malaysia’s largest trading partner.

Fact: The deal’s full text was first published on the White House website before being shared with Malaysian Parliament. Malaysian lawmakers learned the terms of their own trade agreement from a foreign government’s press release.

Fact: Former PM Mahathir said the provisions amounted to “handing over” the country’s independence. Even for a man who oversaw his own share of questionable deals, this one was a bridge too far.

Fact: East Asia Forum’s analysis, titled “The Art of the Deal Lost in US-Malaysia Pact,” argued Malaysia surrendered critical mineral export controls, digital services taxation rights, and independent trade policy in exchange for a tariff cut that still left Malaysia paying three times what the US pays.

Fact: The Guardian reported that Anwar “defended” the deal by arguing it was “the best Malaysia could get under the circumstances” — an argument that concedes the deal is bad while claiming it could have been worse.

Fact: SCMP reported Anwar “slammed” critics for accusing him of surrendering sovereignty — but never addressed the substance of their arguments, only their tone.

Why It Matters

  • This is not a reciprocal deal by any definition of the word. Malaysia makes extensive, binding concessions across minerals, digital taxation, sanctions alignment, and procurement. The US reduces a punitive tariff by 6 percentage points and retains the right to reimpose it at will. In any negotiation textbook, this is called capitulation.
  • The sovereignty clause is the most dangerous provision. If Malaysia wants to deepen trade ties with China — its largest trading partner — the US can terminate the ART and reimpose 25% tariffs. Malaysia has effectively given Washington a veto over its foreign economic policy.
  • Publishing the details on the White House website before informing Parliament is not just embarrassing — it is a constitutional affront. Trade agreements that constrain national sovereignty should require parliamentary debate and approval before signing. Anwar bypassed the legislature on the most consequential economic agreement in decades.
  • Every ASEAN neighbour is watching. Indonesia walked away from a similar US proposal. Vietnam negotiated harder. Thailand delayed. Malaysia signed first and asked questions later. This weakens Malaysia’s negotiating position in every future bilateral and multilateral trade discussion.
  • The “best we could get” defence is an admission of weakness, not a justification. If this was truly the best outcome, it means Malaysia’s diplomatic leverage is weaker than the government has claimed — which raises questions about the entire Madani foreign policy brand. *Sources: The Guardian · East Asia Forum · SCMP · *Straits Times

Said by: Consumer Frontiers International survey

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-15

77% Berbanding KDNK

  • Apabila tiga perempat rakyat anda berkata mereka bergelut, memetik angka pelaburan adalah pekak nada.
  • Jurang antara statistik kerajaan dan pengalaman hidup adalah jurang yang mewujudkan gerakan bantahan. *Sumber: Business Today · *The Star

77% of Malaysians Struggle With Rising Living Costs — But Anwar Says Economy Is 'Strong'

Said by: Consumer Frontiers International survey

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-15

The 77% vs The GDP

In October 2025, a survey found that 77% of Malaysians said they struggled with rising living costs. In the same month, the government was citing GDP growth and investment figures as evidence that the economy was strong.

The Facts

Fact: Consumer Frontiers International found 77% of Malaysians struggled with rising costs despite rising wages.

Fact: DOSM reported average monthly disposable household income increased 3.2% to RM7,584 in 2024.

Fact: But economist Lee Heng Guie warned: “We must also recognise that the cost of living continues to rise.” Real gains were being eroded by inflation.

Fact: The Star headlined it: “Rising cost of living dampens income gains.”

Why It Matters

  • GDP growth and approved investments are macro numbers. They do not reflect what 77% of the population is experiencing.
  • When three-quarters of your citizens say they’re struggling, citing investment figures is tone-deaf.
  • The gap between government statistics and lived experience is the gap that creates protest movements. *Sources: Business Today · *The Star
