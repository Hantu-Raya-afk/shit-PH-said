[BM] Anwar Megah Pelaburan 'Rekod' RM426.7 Bilion Diluluskan — Tapi 'Diluluskan' Bukan Bermakna 'Tiba'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 6 Mac 2026, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim mengumumkan bahawa Malaysia mencatat pelaburan diluluskan tertinggi sebanyak RM426.7 bilion pada 2025 — peningkatan 11% daripada RM384.4 bilion pada 2024. Menurut MIDA, 8,390 projek diluluskan, dengan potensi mewujudkan 244,902 pekerjaan. Anwar membingkainya sebagai pembuktian agenda reformasinya dan bukti keyakinan global terhadap hala tuju ekonomi Malaysia.

Pengumuman dibuat semasa pelancaran Pelan Induk Pasaran Modal 2026-2030, dengan Anwar juga berkhidmat sebagai Menteri Kewangan — peranan dwi yang dipegangnya sejak berkuasa pada 2022.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Kerana pelaburan diluluskan tidak sama dengan pelaburan direalisasikan, dan rakyat Malaysia telah diberi tajuk utama tanpa pembezaan ini selama berdekad-dekad.

MIDA menjejaki kelulusan — pada dasarnya, surat niat dan sijil pelaburan. Ini adalah komitmen di atas kertas. Wang sebenar yang mengalir ke dalam ekonomi — pelaburan langsung asing (FDI) direalisasikan — secara sejarahnya 50-60% daripada angka diluluskan di Malaysia. Ini bermakna sehingga separuh daripada angka tajuk utama ini mungkin tidak pernah diterjemahkan kepada kilang, pekerjaan, atau aktiviti ekonomi sebenar.

Anwar menggunakan angka pelaburan diluluskan sama seperti setiap PM Malaysia sebelum beliau: sebagai tajuk utama politik, bukan semakan realiti ekonomi. 244,902 pekerjaan itu adalah pekerjaan berpotensi — bukan disahkan, bukan diwujudkan, bukan dijamin.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Mari kita tepat tentang apa yang berlaku: Perdana Menteri berdiri di podium dan meraikan nombor yang beliau tahu tidak mencerminkan pelaburan sebenar.

Setiap laporan tahunan MIDA membezakan antara pelaburan diluluskan dan direalisasikan. Setiap ahli ekonomi serius di Malaysia tahu jurang realisasi. Anwar — seorang yang terlibat dalam penggubalan dasar ekonomi Malaysia selama tiga dekad — tahu ini juga.

Namun: tiada kadar realisasi didedahkan. Tiada pecahan berapa banyak pelaburan diluluskan 2023 atau 2024 benar-benar terwujud. Tiada metrik akauntabiliti. Hanya nombor terbesar, tajuk utama terbaik, dan dakwaan “keyakinan global.”

Ini adalah setara ekonomi mengira RSVP sebagai kehadiran. Anda tidak boleh mendakwa 244,902 pekerjaan apabila pekerjaan itu hanya wujud dalam surat kelulusan. Anda tidak boleh mendakwa RM426.7 bilion apabila sebahagian besar daripadanya mungkin tidak pernah tiba.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini bukan baru. Setiap kerajaan Malaysia telah bermain permainan diluluskan-lawan-direalisasikan:

  • Di bawah Najib, pelaburan diluluskan secara rutin melebihi RM200B+ setiap tahun, tetapi FDI direalisasikan jauh ketinggalan.
  • Di bawah Mahathir 2.0, corak yang sama berterusan — kelulusan besar, prestasi rendah secara senyap.
  • Kerajaan Anwar sendiri tidak menerbitkan audit realisasi komprehensif dan telus sejak berkuasa. Coraknya sentiasa sama: umumkan nombor sebesar mungkin di sidang akhbar, biarkan media jalankan tajuk utama, dan jangan sekali-kali tindak balas dengan apa yang sebenarnya berlaku.

🎯 Seruan PH

Anda menjanjikan ketelusan dan akauntabiliti sebagai jenama anda. Anda berkata anda berbeza daripada BN. Anda berkata anda tidak akan memutar nombor.

Jadi di mana laporan realisasi? Di mana pecahan berapa banyak pelaburan diluluskan 2023 dan 2024 benar-benar muncul sebagai wang sebenar, kilang sebenar, dan pekerjaan sebenar?

Jika anda mahu kredit untuk RM426.7 bilion, tunjukkan resit — bukan jemputan. Sehingga itu, ini adalah teater ekonomi sama yang ditonton rakyat Malaysia sejak 1990-an, cuma dengan cat reformis.

Anwar Admits He Can Only Hold RON95 Subsidy for 'One or Two Months' — While Fuel Prices Spike Across the Board

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 What Happened

On March 6, 2026, Prime Minister and Finance Minister Anwar Ibrahim told reporters that Malaysia can maintain the subsidised RON95 petrol price at RM1.99 per litre under the Budi95 scheme — but only for ‘one or two months.’ His exact words: ‘We will try to control the effects of the Iranian conflict, including for RON95, which is RM1.99 per litre. We will monitor this situation because so far, we can still hold off for one or two months.’ This came the same week that RON97 petrol rose 10 sen to RM3.25 per litre, unsubsidised RON95 climbed 8 sen to RM2.67, and diesel in Peninsular Malaysia jumped 8 sen to RM3.12 — all driven by the escalating Middle East conflict and 200 vessels stuck in the Strait of Hormuz.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the first time Anwar has publicly put a time limit on the government’s ability to hold fuel subsidies. The hedging language — ‘we will try,’ ‘hold off,’ ‘monitor’ — is a significant shift from previous assurances. If the Strait of Hormuz disruption continues, and 20% of the world’s oil supply remains at risk, the RM1.99 price becomes fiscally unsustainable. But Anwar cannot politically afford a fuel price hike — especially not with Hari Raya weeks away, snap election rumours swirling for July, and RM15 billion in STR cash handouts already deployed. He’s caught between fiscal reality and electoral survival, and the ‘one or two months’ language tells you which one he’s prioritising.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent three years telling Malaysians that Budi95’s targeted subsidy system was a masterstroke of fiscal reform — smarter, fairer, and more sustainable than blanket subsidies. Now, the moment global oil prices actually test the system, he’s already hedging and saying he can only hold it for a couple of months. What happened to the RM800 million in savings the government claimed from Budi95? What happened to Petronas’s RM32 billion dividend to the government? The truth is that the targeted subsidy was always designed for calm seas. The moment a real shock hits — which anyone tracking Middle East geopolitics could have predicted — the whole system starts to wobble. And instead of a plan, we get ‘we will try.’

📜 Previous Cases

This is a recurring pattern with Anwar on fuel policy: promise stability, deliver uncertainty. In early March, he told Dewan Negara the government would ’try to keep’ RON95 at RM1.99 — vague language that gave no timeline. Fuel prices have been creeping up steadily since January 2026, with diesel alone rising from RM2.84 in mid-January to RM3.12 by early March. Each week brings a new price adjustment upward, while the government insists the situation is ‘under control.’ It mirrors the BN-era playbook: reassure the public while the numbers quietly deteriorate, then act surprised when the dam breaks.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians that Budi95 was reform. You told them targeted subsidies would protect the rakyat while saving the government money. Now you’re admitting, publicly, that the system can only survive ‘one or two months’ of real global pressure. If this is fiscal reform, it’s built on cardboard. Where is the contingency plan? Where is the honest conversation with the public about what happens if oil hits USD100 a barrel and stays there? Stop managing headlines and start managing the economy. The rakyat deserves more than ‘we will try’ from the man who is simultaneously Prime Minister and Finance Minister.

Anwar Boasts RM426.7B 'Record' Approved Investments — But 'Approved' Doesn't Mean 'Arrived'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On March 6, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced that Malaysia recorded its highest-ever approved investments of RM426.7 billion in 2025 — an 11% increase from RM384.4 billion in 2024. According to MIDA, 8,390 projects were approved, with the potential to create 244,902 jobs. Anwar framed this as a vindication of his reform agenda and evidence of global confidence in Malaysia’s economic direction.

The announcement was made at the launch of the Capital Market Masterplan 2026-2030, with Anwar also serving as Finance Minister — a dual role he has held since taking power in 2022.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Because approved investments are not the same as realized investments, and the Malaysian public has been fed this distinction-free headline for decades.

MIDA tracks approvals — essentially, letters of intent and investment certificates. These are commitments on paper. The actual money flowing into the economy — foreign direct investment (FDI) realized — has historically been 50-60% of approved figures in Malaysia. That means up to half of these headline numbers may never translate into actual factories, jobs, or economic activity.

Anwar is using approved investment figures the same way every Malaysian PM before him has: as a political headline, not an economic reality check. The 244,902 jobs are potential jobs — not confirmed, not created, not guaranteed.

This matters because Malaysians are living through cost-of-living pressures, stagnant real wages, and subsidy rationalization. A headline number that may be half-real does not pay anyone’s bills.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Let’s be precise about what happened here: the Prime Minister stood at a podium and celebrated a number that he knows does not reflect actual investment.

Every MIDA annual report distinguishes between approved and realized investments. Every serious economist in Malaysia knows the realization gap. Anwar — a man who has been in and around Malaysian economic policymaking for three decades — knows this too.

And yet: no realization rate was disclosed. No breakdown of how much of 2023 or 2024’s approved investments actually materialized. No accountability metric. Just the biggest number, the best headline, and a claim of “global confidence.”

This is the economic equivalent of counting RSVPs as attendance. You don’t get to claim 244,902 jobs when those jobs exist only in approval letters. You don’t get to claim RM426.7 billion when a significant chunk of it may never arrive.

The UNCTAD Global Investment Trends Monitor (January 2026) itself noted that much of global FDI growth was concentrated in financial centres rather than productive assets. MIDA’s own press release acknowledged this — then quietly moved on to the headline number.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

This is not new. Every Malaysian government has played the approved-vs-realized game:

  • Under Najib, approved investments regularly topped RM200B+ annually, but realized FDI lagged significantly behind.
  • Under Mahathir 2.0, the same pattern continued — big approvals, quiet underperformance.
  • Anwar’s own government has not published a comprehensive, transparent realization audit since taking office. The pattern is always the same: announce the biggest possible number at a press conference, let the media run the headline, and never follow up with what actually happened. No PM has ever been held accountable for the gap between approved and realized investments.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You promised transparency and accountability as your brand. You said you were different from BN. You said you wouldn’t spin numbers.

So where is the realization report? Where is the breakdown of how much of 2023’s and 2024’s approved investments actually showed up as real money, real factories, and real jobs?

If you want credit for RM426.7 billion, show the receipts — not the invitations. Until then, this is the same economic theatre Malaysians have been watching since the 1990s, just with a reformist coat of paint.

[BM] Hampir RM1 Bilion Dibayar Sebagai Ganti Rugi Mahkamah Atas Kecuaian Kerajaan — Penyelesaian Anwar? Surat Teguran

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Perdana Menteri dan Menteri Kewangan Anwar Ibrahim mendedahkan dalam jawapan parlimen bertulis — menjawab soalan AP PH sendiri William Leong (Selayang) — bahawa kerajaan Malaysia membayar RM933,360,633 sebagai ganti rugi mahkamah antara 2020 dan 2025. Pembayaran itu adalah akibat kecuaian, salah laku, dan kesalahan lain oleh pegawai dan agensi kerajaan. Anwar menggariskan langkah termasuk mengarahkan kementerian menjalankan siasatan dan “mengesyorkan tindakan tatatertib” terhadap mereka yang didapati cuai — dan, secara mengagumkan, mengeluarkan surat teguran.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Hampir RM1 bilion wang awam diserahkan kerana pegawai kerajaan cuai, tidak bertanggungjawab, atau rasuah terang-terangan dalam menjalankan tugas — dan mekanisme akauntabiliti yang dicadangkan kerajaan ialah surat bertegur. Angka ini merangkumi pentadbiran Muhyiddin, Ismail Sabri, dan kini Anwar sendiri. Hakikat pendedahan ini datang terpendam dalam jawapan parlimen bertulis dan bukannya diumumkan secara proaktif memberitahu anda segala-galanya tentang betapa seriusnya kerajaan ini mengambil akauntabiliti fiskal.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Surat teguran. Untuk hampir satu bilion ringgit kerugian akibat kecuaian kerajaan. Biar itu meresap. Dalam sektor swasta, satu kes kecuaian profesional yang mengakibatkan kerugian kewangan besar akan mencetuskan pemecatan, tuntutan mahkamah, dan tindakan kawal selia. Dalam Malaysia Anwar, ia mencetuskan memo tegas. Ini bukan akauntabiliti — ini sandiwara. Jika PH serius tentang reformasi, ia akan menamakan kementerian yang terlibat, mendedahkan kes individu, mengenakan surcaj kepada pegawai bertanggungjawab, dan merujuk kecuaian jenayah kepada pihak berkuasa berkaitan.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Malaysia mempunyai sejarah panjang dan tidak membanggakan kecuaian kerajaan yang membebankan pembayar cukai berbilion — daripada skandal PKFZ (RM12.5 bilion lebihan kos dan pinjaman lembut) kepada saga projek kerajaan terbengkalai dan perolehan gagal di bawah pentadbiran berturut-turut. PH sendiri berkempen untuk menamatkan kitaran ini pada 2018 dan 2022.

🎯 Seruan PH

Anda menjanjikan rakyat anda akan berbeza. Anda berkata akan ada akibat. Anda berkata tadbir urus bersih bukan sekadar slogan — ia komitmen. Jadi di mana akibat untuk hampir RM1 bilion ganti rugi mahkamah akibat jentera kerajaan anda sendiri? Surat teguran adalah apa yang anda beri seseorang yang meletak kereta di tempat salah, bukan pegawai yang kecuaiannya merugikan negara ratusan juta. Namakan kementerian. Terbitkan kes. Kenakan surcaj pegawai. Tunjukkan kepada negara bahawa “Madani” bermakna sesuatu lebih daripada logo pada kepala surat kerajaan.

[BM] SPRM Siasat Rafizi Atas Perjanjian ARM Holdings RM1.1 Bilion — Pembantu Beliau Lari Ke UK

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, Former Economy Minister; Azam Baki, MACC Chief

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada Februari-Mac 2026, SPRM melancarkan siasatan rasmi terhadap perjanjian kerajaan bernilai RM1.1 bilion dengan gergasi cip UK ARM Holdings, ditandatangani pada Mac 2025 semasa Rafizi Ramli menjadi Menteri Ekonomi. Perjanjian itu melibatkan Lembaga Pembangunan Pelaburan Malaysia (MIDA) memperoleh lesen harta intelek dan subsistem pengkomputeran bernilai AS$250 juta, dibayar selama 10 tahun.

Ketua SPRM Azam Baki mengesahkan 12 saksi daripada pelbagai agensi kerajaan telah memberi kenyataan. Lebih dramatik, SPRM mengeluarkan notis awam mencari bekas pembantu Rafizi, Chai Jin Shern (James Chai), yang telah meninggalkan Malaysia dan bekerja di UK. SPRM mengarahkan Chai pulang dan bekerjasama dengan siasatan.

Rafizi menyifatkan siasatan itu “bermotif politik” dan berkata ia “pelik” bahawa SPRM tidak memanggilnya secara langsung walaupun siasatan berjalan lebih sebulan.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Perjanjian RM1.1 bilion adalah wang yang besar. Jika terdapat unsur penyalahgunaan kuasa, penipuan, atau kegagalan tadbir urus dalam cara perjanjian ini distrukturkan, rakyat Malaysia berhak mendapat jawapan. Hakikat bekas pembantu Rafizi telah meninggalkan negara dan tidak responsif terhadap percubaan SPRM untuk menghubunginya menimbulkan persoalan jelas tentang apa yang mungkin diketahuinya.

Tetapi masalah yang lebih mendalam: pemasaan itu mencurigakan. Siasatan ini datang selepas Rafizi kalah dalam perlumbaan timbalan presiden PKR kepada Nurul Izzah dan meletak jawatan daripada Kabinet. Ia datang semasa Azam Baki sendiri di bawah siasatan atas skandal pegangan sahamnya sendiri. Rupa SPRM dipersenjatakan terhadap tokoh politik yang baru sahaja kalah dalam perebutan kuasa dalaman tidak boleh diabaikan.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Karut di sini berlapis. Jika perjanjian itu benar-benar bermasalah, mengapa SPRM tidak menyiasatnya apabila ditandatangani pada Mac 2025? Mengapa tunggu sehingga Rafizi keluar daripada Kabinet dan lemah secara politik? Pemasaan mencadangkan ini sama ada balasan atau oportunistik — kedua-duanya tidak mengilhamkan keyakinan terhadap kebebasan SPRM.

Tetapi Rafizi juga tidak terlepas. Pembelaannya — “ia melalui Kabinet” — adalah tepat apa yang setiap menteri katakan apabila ditangkap. Kelulusan Kabinet tidak mengimunkan perjanjian daripada penelitian. Dan hakikat pembantunya melarikan diri bukan rupa yang baik bagi seseorang yang membina kerjayanya atas ketelusan dan akauntabiliti.

Seluruh sarkis ini adalah kajian kes kegagalan institusi Malaysia: agensi antirasuah yang terjejas menyiasat reformis yang cedera politik, sementara PM memanggil Bloomberg komplot Zionis kerana mendedahkan ketua SPRM.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Malaysia mempunyai sejarah panjang siasatan SPRM bermasa politik. Di bawah Najib, SPRM dituduh mengejar tokoh pembangkang sambil mengabaikan 1MDB. Di bawah PH 1.0, SPRM mengejar tokoh BN. MACC Investigates Rafizi Over RM1.1 Billion ARM Holdings Deal — His Aide Flees to UK

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, Former Economy Minister; Azam Baki, MACC Chief

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

In February-March 2026, MACC launched a formal investigation into a RM1.1 billion government deal with UK chip giant ARM Holdings, signed in March 2025 during Rafizi Ramli’s tenure as Economy Minister. The deal involved Malaysia Investment Development Authority (MIDA) obtaining intellectual property licences and compute subsystems valued at US$250 million, payable over 10 years.

MACC chief Azam Baki confirmed that 12 witnesses from various government agencies had given statements. More dramatically, MACC issued a public notice seeking Rafizi’s former aide, Chai Jin Shern (James Chai), who had left Malaysia and was working in the UK. MACC ordered Chai to return and cooperate with the investigation.

Rafizi called the investigation “politically motivated” and said it was “odd” that MACC hadn’t summoned him directly despite the probe running for over a month. He said both the Silicon Vision Programme and the National Energy Transition Roadmap went through multiple layers of government approval, including Cabinet endorsement.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

A RM1.1 billion deal is serious money. If there were elements of abuse of power, fraud, or governance failures in how this deal was structured, Malaysians deserve answers. The fact that Rafizi’s former aide has left the country and is unresponsive to MACC’s attempts to contact him raises obvious questions about what he might know.

But here’s the deeper problem: the timing stinks. This investigation comes after Rafizi lost his PKR deputy presidency race to Nurul Izzah and resigned from Cabinet. It comes while Azam Baki himself is under investigation for his own shareholding scandal. The appearance of MACC being weaponised against a political figure who just lost an internal power struggle is impossible to ignore.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

The bullshit here is layered. If the deal was genuinely problematic, why didn’t MACC investigate it when it was signed in March 2025? Why wait until Rafizi is out of Cabinet and politically weakened? The timing suggests this is either retaliatory or opportunistic — neither of which inspires confidence in MACC’s independence.

But Rafizi doesn’t get a pass either. His defence — “it went through Cabinet” — is exactly what every minister says when caught out. Cabinet approval doesn’t immunise a deal from scrutiny. And the fact that his aide fled the country is not a great look for someone who built his career on transparency and accountability.

The whole circus is a case study in Malaysian institutional failure: a compromised anti-corruption agency investigating a politically wounded reformist, while the PM calls Bloomberg a Zionist plot for exposing the MACC chief. Nobody comes out clean.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Malaysia has a long history of politically timed MACC investigations. Under Najib, MACC was accused of going after opposition figures while ignoring 1MDB. Under PH 1.0, MACC went after BN figures. Now, under PH 2.0, MACC appears to be going after a fallen PH insider. The institution remains a political tool regardless of who holds power.

Rafizi himself was once MACC’s loudest champion when it investigated BN. Now he’s calling the same agency politically motivated. The irony writes itself.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You promised an independent MACC. You kept Azam Baki — a man with two shareholding scandals — as chief. Now that same compromised MACC chief is investigating your own former minister in what looks like a politically timed probe. If you wanted Malaysians to trust MACC investigations, you should have cleaned house first. You can’t credibly investigate corruption when the head of your anti-corruption agency is himself under investigation. Fix the institution, then go after the wrongdoers. In that order.

Nearly RM1 Billion Paid in Court Damages for Government Negligence — Anwar's Fix? Letters of Reprimand

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister and Finance Minister Anwar Ibrahim disclosed in a written parliamentary reply — responding to PH’s own William Leong (Selayang) — that the Malaysian government paid out RM933,360,633 in court-awarded damages between 2020 and 2025. The payouts were the result of negligence, misconduct, and other wrongdoing by government officials and agencies. Anwar outlined measures including instructing ministries to conduct investigations into the cases and “recommend disciplinary action” against those found negligent — and, spectacularly, issuing letters of reprimand.

🔥 Why It Matters

Nearly RM1 billion of public money was handed over because government officials were negligent, irresponsible, or outright corrupt in carrying out their duties — and the government’s proposed accountability mechanism is a sternly worded letter. This figure spans the administrations of Muhyiddin, Ismail Sabri, and now Anwar himself. The fact that this disclosure came buried in a written parliamentary reply rather than proactively announced tells you everything about how seriously this government takes fiscal accountability. PH promised to end the culture of impunity. Instead, it’s inherited it and furnished it with a fresh coat of bureaucratic language.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Letters of reprimand. For nearly a billion ringgit in losses caused by government negligence. Let that sink in. In the private sector, a single case of professional negligence resulting in significant financial loss would trigger terminations, lawsuits, and regulatory action. In Anwar’s Malaysia, it triggers a stern memo. This isn’t accountability — it’s theatre. If PH were serious about reform, it would name the ministries involved, disclose the individual cases, surcharge the officers responsible, and refer criminal negligence to the relevant authorities. Instead, the Prime Minister filed a quiet written reply and moved on. The rakyat pays the bill; nobody pays the price.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long, inglorious history of government negligence costing taxpayers billions — from the PKFZ scandal (RM12.5 billion in cost overruns and soft loans) to the endless saga of abandoned government projects and botched procurement exercises under successive administrations. PH itself campaigned on ending this cycle in both 2018 and 2022. During PH 1.0 under Mahathir, the government conducted an audit of 1MDB-era spending but quietly shelved follow-up enforcement. Now under PH 2.0, Anwar’s government discloses the losses but proposes remedies so weak they amount to an invitation to keep doing it.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised the rakyat you would be different. You said there would be consequences. You said clean governance wasn’t a slogan — it was a commitment. So where are the consequences for nearly RM1 billion in court-ordered damages caused by your own government machinery? Letters of reprimand are what you give someone who parks in the wrong spot, not officials whose negligence cost the nation hundreds of millions. Name the ministries. Publish the cases. Surcharge the officers. Show the country that “Madani” means something beyond a logo on a government letterhead.
