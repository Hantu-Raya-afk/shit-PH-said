[BM] Sudah Mac 2026 — Mana Janji Anwar Hapuskan Kemiskinan Tegar Menjelang S1?

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 7 Disember 2025, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim mengisytiharkan secara terbuka bahawa Malaysia akan menghapuskan kemiskinan tegar menjelang suku pertama 2026. Berucap di penutupan program Rancakkan Madani di Putrajaya, Anwar memetik kadar kemiskinan tegar 0.09% — kira-kira 7,000 isi rumah — dan berkata baki kes boleh diselesaikan dalam beberapa bulan. “Kita antara negara di dunia… kita berjaya mengurangkan kadar nasional kemiskinan tegar kepada hanya 0.09 peratus,” kata Anwar. “Kita boleh menamatkannya menjelang suku pertama 2026.” Janji itu dilaporkan oleh Bernama, Malay Mail, The Star, New Straits Times, The Edge, dan The Sun. Ia merupakan dakwaan utama — diulang semasa Bajet 2026 dan jerayawara Rancakkan Madani. Kini sudah 9 Mac 2026. S1 berakhir dalam tiga minggu. Tiada pengumuman rasmi bahawa kemiskinan tegar telah dihapuskan. Tiada sidang akhbar. Tiada berita kilat Bernama. Tiada kenyataan PMO yang penuh kemenangan.

🔥 KENAPA IA PENTING: Ini bukan sekadar kenyataan sambil lewa. Anwar menjadikan pembasmian kemiskinan tegar sebagai teras naratif ekonomi Madani. Ia diselitkan dalam pesanan Bajet 2026, dipetik di forum antarabangsa, dan digunakan untuk membezakan Malaysia daripada ekonomi Barat yang dibelenggu gelandangan. Bank Dunia turut memberikan ulasan, memuji kemajuan Malaysia sambil menyatakan bahawa “penambahbaikan selanjutnya dalam pendidikan dan kesihatan amat penting.” Janji Disember 2025 itu direka sebagai detik warisan — PM yang menamatkan kemiskinan melampau. Jika sasaran tercapai, kesunyian ini pelik. Jika tidak tercapai, kesunyian ini memalukan. Walau apa pun, rakyat layak mendapat kemas kini. Dakwaan pembasmian kemiskinan turut membawa risiko metodologi. Ahli ekonomi sebelum ini mempersoalkan sama ada garis kemiskinan tegar Malaysia (RM1,169/bulan seisi rumah) ditetapkan terlalu rendah, menjadikan “pembasmian” satu latihan statistik dan bukannya realiti kehidupan.

💩 KENAPA IA KARUT: Ini kes klasik janji melambung diikuti kempisnya yang senyap. Anwar membuat ikrar apabila angka kelihatan baik dan kamera berputar. Dia menetapkan sasaran khusus dan berjangka masa — S1 2026 — dengan penuh kesedaran ia akan sama ada tercapai atau gagal di hadapan mata umum. Kini apabila tarikh akhir tiba, kerajaan berdiam diri. Tiada kemas kini. Tiada garis masa baharu. Tiada pengakuan bahawa sasaran itu wujud. Ini pentadbiran yang sama yang dengan kuat menuntut penghargaan untuk setiap ringgit pertumbuhan KDNK dan setiap titik perpuluhan penurunan inflasi. Tetapi apabila melibatkan janji konkrit dengan tarikh akhir konkrit? Senyap. Jika kemiskinan tegar sudah dihapuskan, katakanlah. Terbitkan data. Tunjukkan metodologi. Biar ahli ekonomi bebas mengesahkannya. Jika belum dihapuskan, katakan juga. Terangkan apa yang salah. Semak semula garis masa. Jujurlah. Apa yang anda tidak boleh lakukan ialah membuat janji besar, menunggang gelombang PR, dan kemudian berpura-pura tarikh akhir tidak wujud.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Ini bukan tarikh akhir PH yang pertama lenyap:

  • Reformasi subsidi minyak RON95 telah ditangguh berulang kali. Anwar sendiri mengakui pada awal Mac 2026 bahawa kerajaan hanya mampu menahan harga “satu atau dua bulan lagi” — namun tiada pelan reformasi konkrit diumumkan.
  • Rang undang-undang pemisahan AG-Pendakwa Raya ditangguhkan pada Mac 2026 untuk semakan jawatankuasa 3 bulan, selepas menjadi janji reformasi utama.
  • Pindaan perlembagaan had tempoh PM gagal di Parlimen pada Mac 2026 — meskipun majoriti besar PH — kerana 8 AP kerajaan tidak hadir mengundi. Coraknya jelas: janji besar, pengumuman kuat, undur senyap. Kerajaan Madani melayan ikrar reformasi sebagai acara PR, bukan komitmen mengikat.

🎯 PANGGILAN PH: Anda beritahu 33 juta rakyat Malaysia bahawa kemiskinan tegar akan hilang menjelang S1 2026. Anda mengatakannya di depan kamera, di Putrajaya, di acara pameran anda sendiri. Bernama menyiarkannya. Setiap media utama melaporkannya. Sekarang 9 Mac. S1 hampir tamat. Mana pengumuman? Mana data? Mana sidang akhbar yang anda buka champagne dan tunjukkan angka sifar? Atau anda berharap kami lupa? Inilah hakikat janji khusus dengan tarikh akhir khusus: orang ingat. Anda tidak boleh bersenang-lenang atas vibes apabila anda telah memberikan tarikh. Sama ada sampaikan hasilnya atau sampaikan penjelasan. Rakyat yang masih hidup dengan kurang daripada RM1,169 sebulan layak mendapat lebih daripada kesunyian.

Pada 2020, kerajaan bertegas ekonomi “berdaya tahan” beberapa minggu sebelum sekatan COVID menjatuhkan KDNK. Pada 2023, unjuran pertumbuhan disemak turun berkali-kali. Coraknya: umumkan unjuran terbaik, harap dunia bekerjasama, kelam-kabut apabila tidak.

🎯 PANGGILAN PH: Jika anda hendak menganjurkan sidang kemuncak tentang risiko serantau, jangan hantar timbalan menteri anda untuk berkata semuanya baik. Sama ada akui risiko dan kemukakan pelan luar jangka, atau berhenti berpura-pura unjuran pertumbuhan anda lebih daripada sasaran politik.

Anwar Brings Forward RM15 Billion STR Cash Handout — Record Spending, Hari Raya Timing, Snap Election Vibes

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On March 9, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced that Phase 2 of the Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah (STR) cash transfer programme would be brought forward to March 10 — weeks ahead of its originally scheduled date. The early disbursement will benefit 5.2 million recipients.

Anwar framed the move as helping Malaysians prepare for Hari Raya Aidilfitri. The total 2026 allocation for STR and SARA (Sumbangan Asas Rahmah) stands at RM15 billion — the highest in the history of the Federal Government’s direct cash assistance programmes.

This comes on the same day Anwar also announced a separate RM500 special financial aid for civil servants in Grade 15 and below (to be paid March 13) and mosque staff.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Three things make this significant:

  1. Record spending on cash transfers. RM15 billion is not pocket change. This is the largest direct cash handout allocation any Malaysian government has ever made — and it comes from a PM who campaigned on fiscal discipline, subsidy rationalisation, and ending the culture of handouts.
  2. The timing is suspicious. Reuters reported on March 6 that government sources say Anwar could call snap elections as early as July 2026. Bringing forward cash payments right before Hari Raya — the single most politically sensitive festive period in Malaysia — is textbook pre-election cash politics.
  3. Structural reform is nowhere. Cash transfers are not economic policy. They are a painkiller. The RON95 fuel subsidy rationalisation has been delayed repeatedly. The promised targeted subsidy mechanism is still not operational. Instead of fixing the system, the government is writing bigger cheques.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Anwar spent years — decades — attacking BN’s BR1M cash handout programme as vote-buying, populist bribery, and fiscal irresponsibility. He called it a “crutch” that kept the rakyat dependent on government generosity instead of building real economic resilience.

Now he’s running the exact same playbook, except he’s spending more money and bringing payments forward to land right before Hari Raya. STR is BR1M with a rebrand. The only difference is the name on the cheque.

The “highest allocation in history” line is not a flex — it’s a confession. If the economy is performing as well as the government claims (4-4.5% GDP growth, record approved investments), why does the government need to spend a record RM15 billion on cash handouts? Either the growth isn’t reaching ordinary people, or this is about elections, not economics. Probably both.

Anwar urged recipients to “spend wisely.” Here’s a thought: maybe the government should spend wisely too.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

  • BR1M under Najib (2012-2018): Anwar and PH relentlessly attacked Barisan Nasional’s Bantuan Rakyat 1Malaysia (BR1M) as unsustainable vote-buying. DAP called it “irresponsible fiscal policy.” PKR called it “bribing the poor.” Now they run a bigger version of the same programme.
  • 2024 Hari Raya handouts: Anwar brought forward STR payments before Hari Raya 2024 as well. This is now an annual pattern — accelerate cash disbursements to coincide with the festive season for maximum political goodwill.
  • RON95 subsidy delays (2024-2026): The government has repeatedly postponed targeted fuel subsidy reforms. Instead of fixing the subsidy structure, they keep layering cash transfers on top. The fiscal consolidation story — deficit down from 5.5% to 3.5% — looks less impressive when you’re simultaneously running record cash transfer programmes.
  • Snap election signals (March 2026): Reuters reported on March 6 that two government lawmakers confirmed Anwar could call snap polls as early as July. Cash before elections is the oldest trick in Malaysian politics.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You promised “reformasi, bukan derma” — reform, not charity. You told Malaysians that PH would build an economy where people didn’t need handouts. You mocked Najib for BR1M. You called it a plaster on a broken bone.

Now you’re spending RM15 billion on the same plaster — and you’re proud of it. You brought the payment forward to land right before Raya, right before the rumoured snap election window. And you have the audacity to call this “Rahmah” — compassion.

This isn’t compassion. This is campaign spending filed under a different budget line. If you actually believed in reform, you’d be fixing wages, fixing subsidies, and fixing the structural reasons people need these handouts in the first place. Instead, you’re writing record cheques and hoping the rakyat forgets you promised something better.

Anwar Launches Capital Market Masterplan, Says 'Reform Non-Negotiable' — While Every Actual Reform Gets Delayed or Killed

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Prime Minister Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim delivered the keynote address at the launch of the Securities Commission’s Capital Market Masterplan 2026–2030 (CMP4) on March 9, 2026. He declared that “the government has made reform non-negotiable” and that Malaysia’s capital market “must drive shared prosperity, not just profit.”

The CMP4 targets growing Malaysia’s capital market to RM6.3 trillion by 2030 and includes initiatives like the “MY Value Up” program to boost PLC valuations.

Also present were Second Finance Minister Datuk Seri Amir Hamzah Azizan and SC Chairman Datuk Mohammad Faiz Azmi.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

The phrase “reform non-negotiable” would be powerful if it came from a government that was actually delivering reform. Instead, it comes from the same Prime Minister whose government, in the past two weeks alone:

  • Failed to pass the PM tenure limit bill because 8 of its own MPs didn’t show up (March 3)
  • Postponed the AG-Public Prosecutor separation bill for a 3-month committee review — a core reform promise delayed yet again (March 4)
  • Refused to suspend MACC chief Azam Baki despite mounting allegations, opting instead to let his contract quietly expire in May (reported March 9)
  • Delayed RON95 reform repeatedly — Anwar himself admitted he could only hold the price “one or two more months” (March 7) “Reform non-negotiable” is a slogan delivered at a securities industry event while actual institutional reform — the kind that strengthens governance and accountability — keeps getting pushed back, watered down, or abandoned.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Anwar uses “reform” the way a luxury brand uses “exclusive” — it is a marketing word detached from the product.

The Capital Market Masterplan is a Securities Commission document. It is a technocratic roadmap for market growth, IPO pipelines, and PLC valuations. That is fine. But Anwar does not get to stand at a podium, declare “reform non-negotiable,” and receive credit for reform when:

  • The Attorney General still doubles as the Public Prosecutor — a conflict of interest PH has promised to fix since 2018.
  • The MACC chief remains in office despite allegations that would have triggered immediate suspension in any serious anti-corruption jurisdiction.
  • Institutional reforms that require political courage — not just SC planning documents — are systematically delayed. Capital market growth is not reform. Raising PLC valuations is not reform. “Shared prosperity” is not reform. Reform is when you change the rules that protect the powerful. Anwar has not done that.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

  • 2018–2020 (PH 1.0): The first PH government also promised AG-PP separation, IPCMC, and institutional reform. None were completed before the Sheraton Move collapsed the government.
  • March 4, 2026: Fahmi Fadzil announced the AG-PP separation bill would be delayed for a 3-month parliamentary committee review — the latest in a long line of postponements.
  • March 3, 2026: The PM tenure limit constitutional amendment failed because PH couldn’t muster its own full attendance — 8 government and independent MPs were absent.
  • December 2025: Anwar halfed Najib’s sentence while the 1MDB debt — RM50 billion — remained on the rakyat’s books.
  • The pattern is consistent: PH announces bold reform frameworks at formal events, then delays or abandons the actual legislative and institutional changes required to implement them.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You say reform is “non-negotiable.” Then why does every reform end up being negotiated away?

The AG-PP separation — delayed. The PM tenure limit — failed on your own attendance. Azam Baki — still in office. RON95 — still subsidised with no timeline. IPCMC — abandoned.

You have the parliamentary majority. You have the institutional mandate. You have the public goodwill — or what remains of it.

What you don’t have is the political will to spend your coalition capital on anything that doesn’t benefit the coalition itself.

Stop telling the Securities Commission audience that reform is non-negotiable. Start telling your own Cabinet.

Anwar's Government Fights 531,976 Retirees in Court — Wins Appeal to Block RM1.7 Billion Pension Payout

Said by: Federal Government / Senior Federal Counsel Sallehuddin Ali

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 What Happened

On 9 March 2026, the Court of Appeal unanimously overturned a High Court order that would have required Anwar Ibrahim’s government to pay RM1.7 billion in pension arrears to 531,976 former civil servants. The three-judge panel — led by Justice Azhahari Kamal Ramli, with Justices Lim Chong Fong and Meor Hashimi Abdul Hamid — ruled that the pensioners’ application was res judicata (already adjudicated) and constituted an “abuse of court process.” The government, represented by Senior Federal Counsel Sallehuddin Ali, successfully argued that the 2016 Public Service Department Circular (PP 1/2016) was an “enhancement” to the civil service scheme, not a mandatory salary revision triggering pension adjustments. The original case was brought by Aminah Ahmad, a retired Ministry of Foreign Affairs officer, on behalf of 56 other retirees in 2017.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Madani government actively going to court to avoid paying half a million retirees money they were legally awarded. The average payout per retiree would have been roughly RM3,195 — not a fortune, but real money for people on fixed incomes. The government chose to fight this rather than honour a High Court order. This is a government that spends billions on Hari Raya cash handouts and election-year STR payments, but when pensioners win a court ruling for pension adjustments based on the government’s own 2016 circular, Putrajaya deploys federal counsel to crush the claim on a technicality. The signal is clear: if you’re politically useful, you get cash. If you’re a retiree with a legal claim, you get lawyers.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim built his career on defending the little guy. His government now argues that a PSD circular meant to adjust pensions in line with civil service salary changes was merely an “enhancement” — bureaucratic doublespeak for “we wrote it, but we didn’t mean it.” The Court of Appeal’s ruling that this was “abuse of process” doesn’t mean the pensioners’ grievance is illegitimate — it means the government found a procedural exit ramp and took it. Meanwhile, Putrajaya approved RM15 billion in STR cash handouts just days earlier. The message: Anwar’s government has money for voters, not for retirees who already served the country.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysian governments have a long history of treating pension adjustments as political favours rather than legal obligations. The 2013 amendment to the Pension Adjustment Act — which provided retirees with a fixed annual 2% increase — was itself invalidated by the Court of Appeal in 2022, stripping retirees of a guaranteed mechanism. Every administration since has treated pension reform as a hot potato: promise it during campaigns, dodge it in office. The Madani government is no different. Anwar’s own 2022 election manifesto spoke of “dignifying” civil servants and retirees, but the legal strategy his government is pursuing tells a different story.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised kerajaan Madani — a civilised government that upholds justice and human dignity. Half a million people who spent their careers serving this country were told by a High Court that they are owed pension adjustments. Your government’s response was not to pay, not to negotiate, but to appeal and win on a technicality. You found RM15 billion for pre-Raya handouts but fought tooth and nail against RM1.7 billion for pensioners. That is not reform. That is not Madani. That is the same old Putrajaya arithmetic: spend where the votes are, litigate where they aren’t.

Deputy Economy Minister Says Growth Steady at 4-4.5% — Amid a Regional War and Global Volatility

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 9 March 2026, Deputy Economy Minister Mohd Shahar Abdullah projected 4–4.5% GDP growth for Malaysia at the Asean Banking and Finance Summit, citing domestic consumption, infrastructure investment, the digital economy, and Visit Malaysia 2026. He made this statement at the very summit whose chairman, Tan Sri Majid Khan, warned that geopolitical tensions are “repricing risk across our entire region.” The US-Iran conflict was escalating, oil prices were spiking due to Strait of Hormuz disruptions, and GPS jamming was affecting shipping across the Gulf.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A deputy minister projecting steady growth at a summit convened to discuss regional instability suggests a dangerous disconnect between political messaging and economic reality. The growth drivers cited — tourism, digital economy, domestic consumption — are all vulnerable to the very geopolitical risks flagged at the same event. Oil price spikes may temporarily boost petroleum revenue, but they raise input costs across the economy and threaten the government’s already fragile fuel subsidy position. When your own summit chairman is sounding the alarm, doubling down on optimism reads less like confidence and more like denial.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Malaysia’s economy is heavily trade-dependent and exposed to every global shock the deputy minister chose to ignore. Projecting 4-4.5% growth while a regional war threatens shipping lanes, oil markets, and investor confidence is not leadership — it’s PR. The government has repeatedly been caught off-guard by external shocks (COVID, supply chain disruptions, commodity swings) because it prioritises messaging over contingency planning.
