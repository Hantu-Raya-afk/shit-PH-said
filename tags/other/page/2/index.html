[BM] Bajet Sukan Dipotong — Atlet Bergelut Sementara Kerajaan Belanja Berbilion Untuk GLC

Said by: National Sports Council / Athletes

Category: Other

Date: 2025-10-01

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Atlet Malaysia secara konsisten mengadu tentang pembiayaan yang tidak mencukupi, kemudahan latihan yang lemah, dan sistem sokongan yang tidak memadai, walaupun kerajaan menemui berbilion untuk penyelamatan GLC, pelantikan politik, dan faedah perkhidmatan awam. Bajet pembangunan sukan telah dihimpit apabila tekanan fiskal meningkat, menyerahkan atlet dalam sukan Olimpik dan Sukan Asia untuk bergantung kepada penajaan swasta atau pembiayaan sendiri.

🔥 Kenapa Ia Penting

Kemudahan latihan dalam banyak sukan sudah lapuk berbanding pesaing serantau seperti Thailand, Indonesia, dan Vietnam. Prestasi pasukan bola sepak kebangsaan telah menjadi sumber malu negara, dengan kemudahan dan pembangunan belia ketinggalan meskipun sukan ini mempunyai pengikut popular yang besar. Apabila atlet Malaysia berjaya — seperti yang kadangkala berlaku dalam badminton, terjun, atau berbasikal — kerajaan cepat menuntut kredit. Apabila mereka gagal, kekurangan pembiayaan sistematik yang menjadikan kegagalan tidak dapat dielakkan tidak diperiksa.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Perbezaan antara bajet JAKIM RM2.6 bilion untuk hal ehwal agama dan peruntukan yang jauh lebih kecil untuk pembangunan sukan memberitahu anda segala-galanya tentang keutamaan kerajaan. Malaysia bercita-cita menjadi negara sukan tetapi mendanainya seperti perkara sampingan.

🎯 Teguran Kepada PH

Anda mahu pingat emas tetapi mendanai atlet seolah-olah mereka adalah baris perbelanjaan yang perlu diminimumkan. Anda mahu kebanggaan negara dalam sukan tetapi membelanjakan lebih banyak untuk birokrasi agama daripada pembangunan atletik. Pilih satu: sama ada danai sukan dengan sewajarnya atau berhenti berpura-pura peduli apabila pingat tidak datang.

Sports Budget Slashed — Athletes Struggle While Government Spends Billions on GLCs

Said by: National Sports Council / Athletes

Category: Other

Date: 2025-10-01

📌 What Happened

Malaysian athletes consistently complain about inadequate funding, poor training facilities, and insufficient support systems, even as the government finds billions for GLC bailouts, political appointments, and civil service perks. The sports development budget has been squeezed as fiscal pressures mount, leaving athletes in Olympic and Asian Games sports to rely heavily on private sponsorship or self-funding.

🔥 Why It Matters

Training facilities in many sports are outdated compared to regional competitors like Thailand, Indonesia, and Vietnam. The national football team’s performance has been a particular source of national embarrassment, with facilities and youth development lagging despite the sport’s massive popular following. When Malaysian athletes succeed — as they occasionally do in badminton, diving, or cycling — the government is quick to claim credit. When they fail, the systemic underfunding that made failure inevitable goes unexamined.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The contrast between the RM2.6 billion JAKIM budget for religious affairs and the significantly smaller allocation for sports development tells you everything about government priorities. Malaysia aspires to be a sporting nation but funds it like an afterthought.

🎯 PH Callout

You want gold medals but fund athletes like they’re an expense line to be minimised. You want national pride in sports but spend more on religious bureaucracy than athletic development. Pick one: either fund sports properly or stop pretending to care when the medals don’t come.

[BM] Jerebu Merentas Sempadan Terus Kembali — Tindakan Kerajaan: 'Pantau Dan Harap Indonesia Bekerjasama'

Said by: Environment Ministry / ASEAN Coordination

Category: Other

Date: 2025-09-01

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Walaupun Perjanjian ASEAN mengenai Pencemaran Jerebu Merentas Sempadan ditandatangani pada 2002 dan diratifikasi oleh semua negara anggota, Malaysia terus mengalami episod jerebu berkala yang disebabkan terutamanya oleh pembakaran pertanian di Indonesia. Respons kerajaan kekal sebahagian besarnya diplomatik — meminta kerjasama Indonesia dan mengeluarkan nasihat kesihatan — berbanding menangani sumbangan Malaysia sendiri melalui kebakaran tanah gambut domestik dan syarikat ladang milik Malaysia yang beroperasi di Indonesia.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Pemantauan kualiti udara semasa episod jerebu secara rutin menunjukkan bacaan IPU tidak sihat, menjejaskan kesihatan berjuta-juta rakyat Malaysia, menutup sekolah, dan mengurangkan produktiviti. Kos ekonomi jerebu berjumlah berbilion ringgit apabila mengambil kira penjagaan kesihatan, kehilangan produktiviti, kesan pelancongan, dan kesan kesihatan pernafasan jangka panjang. Perubahan iklim menjadikan keadaan lebih kondusif untuk kebakaran, namun penebangan hutan Malaysia sendiri berterusan pada kadar yang membimbangkan.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Isu asas ialah keuntungan kelapa sawit diutamakan berbanding kualiti udara, dan budi bahasa diplomatik menghalang Malaysia daripada mempertanggungjawabkan syarikat Indonesia (dan milik Malaysia). Selepas dua dekad, masalah jerebu kekal pada dasarnya tidak ditangani. Kerajaan bersuara keras semasa musim jerebu dan senyap apabila langit cerah.

🎯 Teguran PH

Anda menghadiri persidangan iklim antarabangsa dan membuat ikrar tentang kelestarian. Tetapi anda tidak akan berdepan dengan syarikat ladang — termasuk yang dimiliki Malaysia — yang menyebabkan jerebu yang rakyat anda sedut. Pemantauan bukan tadbir urus. Harapan bukan dasar. Selepas 20 tahun buku panduan yang sama, sama ada ubah pendekatan atau akui anda telah memilih keuntungan berbanding paru-paru.

Transboundary Haze Keeps Returning — Government's Response: 'Monitor and Hope Indonesia Cooperates'

Said by: Environment Ministry / ASEAN Coordination

Category: Other

Date: 2025-09-01

📌 What Happened

Despite the ASEAN Agreement on Transboundary Haze Pollution signed in 2002 and ratified by all member states, Malaysia continues to suffer periodic haze episodes caused primarily by agricultural burning in Indonesia. The government’s response remains largely diplomatic — requesting Indonesian cooperation and issuing health advisories — rather than addressing Malaysia’s own contribution through domestic peatland fires and Malaysian-owned plantation companies operating in Indonesia.

🔥 Why It Matters

Air quality monitoring during haze episodes routinely shows unhealthy API readings, affecting millions of Malaysians’ health, shutting schools, and reducing productivity. The economic cost of haze runs into billions of ringgit when accounting for healthcare, lost productivity, tourism impacts, and long-term respiratory health effects. Climate change is making conditions more conducive to fires, yet Malaysia’s own deforestation continues at alarming rates.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The fundamental issue is that palm oil profits are prioritised over air quality, and diplomatic niceties prevent Malaysia from holding Indonesian (and Malaysian-owned) companies accountable. After two decades, the haze problem remains essentially unaddressed. The government talks tough during haze season and goes silent when the skies clear.

🎯 PH Callout

You attend international climate conferences and make pledges about sustainability. But you won’t confront the plantation companies — including Malaysian-owned ones — that cause the haze your rakyat breathes. Monitoring is not governance. Hope is not policy. After 20 years of the same playbook, either change the approach or admit you’ve chosen profits over lungs.

Climate Action Failure — Malaysia's Paris Agreement Pledges Are Fantasy, Emissions Keep Rising

Said by: Climate Action Tracker / Environmental Groups

Category: Other

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 What Happened

Despite being a signatory to the Paris Agreement and making ambitious pledges on carbon emission reductions, Malaysia’s actual emissions trajectory continues upward. Deforestation continues, coal power plants remain operational, data centre energy consumption grows, and the government has offered no credible pathway to meeting its stated climate commitments.

🔥 Why It Matters

Climate change is already affecting Malaysia through more severe floods (2024 devastation), prolonged droughts, rising sea levels threatening coastal communities, and biodiversity loss. The country is simultaneously a victim and contributor — its palm oil industry and deforestation are significant global emission sources. Failure to act on climate isn’t just bad policy — it’s an existential threat to Malaysia’s future.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH’s 2022 manifesto explicitly promised environmentally friendly governance benchmarked against ‘International Best Practices.’ Instead, the government has approved massive data centres without environmental impact assessments, continued approving logging quotas that exceed sustainability levels, maintained coal-fired power generation, and offered no timeline for renewable energy transition. Anwar lectures the world about moral failing on Gaza while failing the planet on climate. The manifesto promise of strict logging quota enforcement is particularly laughable given continued deforestation.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s climate pledges have been consistently unmatched by action across all administrations. BN treated climate change as an inconvenience to economic growth. PH 1.0 made promises but collapsed before implementation. PH 2.0 is maintaining the tradition of ambitious targets and non-existent action. The Penang South Islands reclamation — a massive environmental destruction project — is being approved under a PH state government.

🎯 PH Callout

Your manifesto promised environmental governance benchmarked against international best practices. Your reality: continued deforestation, rivers turning blue from pollution, floods devastating communities annually, and climate pledges that are pure fiction. You can’t lecture the world about moral hypocrisy on Palestine while being morally bankrupt on the planet. The environment can’t vote — which is why you don’t care about it.

Palm Oil Deforestation Continues Unchecked — Environmental Pledges vs Plantation Profits

Said by: Environmental NGOs / SAM

Category: Other

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Despite Malaysia’s commitments to international climate agreements and sustainable development goals, deforestation for palm oil plantations continues to degrade tropical rainforests among the oldest and most biodiverse on Earth. The Malaysian Sustainable Palm Oil (MSPO) certification scheme has been criticised by international NGOs as inadequate greenwashing. Malaysia’s river water quality is declining — clean rivers dropped from 486 to 468 in 2025. The Perak River turned blue from rare earth mining discharge in November 2025. Endangered species including orangutans, pygmy elephants, and Malayan tigers face shrinking habitats.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia accounts for approximately 25% of global palm oil production, and the economic dependence on this commodity creates enormous political resistance to genuine environmental reform. While the rate of primary forest loss has slowed from its peak, secondary forest clearing and peatland conversion continue. The environmental cost — biodiversity loss, river pollution, carbon emissions from peatland drainage — is not counted in GDP figures but is borne by communities who depend on clean water, healthy forests, and functioning ecosystems.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The MSPO certification scheme is a sticker, not a standard. It allows continued environmental degradation while providing a veneer of sustainability that satisfies international buyers. The government talks about sustainability at COP summits and international conferences while approving new plantation developments at home. When your rivers turn blue from industrial discharge and your forests disappear for palm oil, no amount of international pledges will bring them back. Sustainability isn’t a speech — it’s a policy. Malaysia doesn’t have one.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, Malaysia’s deforestation rate was among the highest in the world. Mahathir’s era saw massive conversion of primary rainforest to plantations. Under PH 1.0, a moratorium on new oil palm licences was briefly discussed. Under PH 2.0, the MSPO was expanded but not strengthened. Each government pays lip service to environmental protection while protecting the plantation industry that funds political parties across the spectrum. The pattern is bipartisan: plantations are more politically valuable than forests.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You sign climate agreements and pose for photos at COP summits. Then you come home and approve more plantations. Your MSPO scheme is window dressing. Your rivers are getting dirtier. Your forests are getting smaller. Strengthen MSPO to meet international standards. Enforce illegal logging laws. Invest in downstream palm oil processing rather than acreage expansion. Or keep giving sustainability speeches while your country’s environmental foundation crumbles — and hope the world doesn’t notice the hypocrisy.
