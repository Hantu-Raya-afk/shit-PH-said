[BM] Lesen Nadir Bumi Lynas Diperbaharui 10 Tahun — Sisa Radioaktif Masih Di Pahang

Said by: Chang Lih Kang, Science Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 3 Mac 2026, Menteri Sains Chang Lih Kang mengesahkan pembaharuan lesen operasi Lynas Rare Earth selama 10 tahun, walaupun terdapat kebimbangan berterusan tentang penyimpanan sisa radioaktif. Greenpeace Malaysia memberi amaran bahawa negara berisiko menjadi “hab pencemaran.” Kumpulan alam sekitar mengatakan dakwaan kerajaan tentang pengekstrakan torium daripada sisa itu “tidak kukuh dari segi saintifik.” Hampir sejuta tan sisa radioaktif daripada operasi pemprosesan Lynas kekal tersimpan di Pahang, tanpa penyelesaian pelupusan kekal.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Pemprosesan nadir bumi adalah industri strategik yang kritikal dan loji Lynas Malaysia adalah yang terbesar di luar China. Tetapi nilai strategik tidak membenarkan pengabaian risiko alam sekitar dan kesihatan. Pembaharuan 10 tahun tanpa menyelesaikan persoalan pelupusan sisa bermakna Malaysia berkomitmen untuk sedekad lagi mengumpul sisa radioaktif tanpa pelan keluar. Kerajaan mengutamakan kedudukan geopolitik berbanding kebimbangan kesihatan dan alam sekitar penduduk Pahang.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: PH berkempen menentang Lynas dalam PRU14. DAP khususnya menjadikan Lynas isu alam sekitar utama. Fuziah Salleh — kini senator PH — membina kerjaya politiknya dengan menentang loji itu. Kini gabungan yang sama telah memperbaharui lesen untuk satu dekad penuh. Justifikasi — bahawa torium boleh diekstrak secara komersial daripada sisa — telah ditolak oleh saintis alam sekitar sebagai tidak terbukti secara teknikal pada skala. Ini kerajaan yang memilih pelaburan asing berbanding janji yang dibuat kepada komuniti yang diminta mengundi untuknya.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Lynas telah menjadi beracun secara politik sejak loji Kuantan dibuka pada 2012. PH 1.0 mengenakan syarat ketat terhadap pemindahan sisa yang tidak pernah dikuatkuasakan. BN melanjutkan lesen sebelum PRU15. PH 2.0 kini melangkah lebih jauh dengan pembaharuan 10 tahun. Setiap kerajaan berjanji menyelesaikan isu sisa; tiada yang melakukannya. Coraknya: kempen menentang Lynas dalam pembangkang, memuaskan Lynas dalam kerajaan, harap pengundi lupa.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Kamu berkempen menentang loji ini. Ahli kamu sendiri membina kerjaya menentangnya. Pangkalan alam sekitar kamu mengundi untuk kamu sebahagiannya kerana Lynas. Dan kini kamu beri mereka pembaharuan 10 tahun sementara hampir sejuta tan sisa radioaktif duduk di Pahang tanpa pelan pelupusan. Jika sisa boleh diproses, buktikan. Jika tidak boleh, jelaskan kenapa kamu memperbaharui. Pengundi kamu di Pahang berhak mendapat jawapan yang jujur.

Lynas Rare Earth License Renewed 10 Years — Radioactive Waste Still in Pahang

Said by: Chang Lih Kang, Science Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 March 2026, Science Minister Chang Lih Kang confirmed the renewal of Lynas Rare Earth’s operating licence for 10 years, despite ongoing concerns about radioactive waste storage. Greenpeace Malaysia warned that the country risks becoming a “pollution hub.” Environmental groups said government claims about thorium extraction from the waste are “scientifically unsound.” Close to a million tonnes of radioactive waste from Lynas’s processing operations remain stored in Pahang, with no permanent disposal solution in place.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rare earth processing is a critical strategic industry, and Malaysia’s Lynas plant is the largest outside China. But strategic value doesn’t justify ignoring environmental and health risks. A 10-year renewal without resolving the waste disposal question means Malaysia committed to another decade of accumulating radioactive residue with no exit plan. The government is prioritising geopolitical positioning (reducing Western dependence on Chinese rare earths) over the health and environmental concerns of Pahang residents who live near the waste.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH campaigned against Lynas in GE14. DAP in particular made Lynas a flagship environmental issue. Fuziah Salleh — now a PH senator — built her political career on opposing the plant. Now the same coalition has renewed the licence for a full decade. The justification — that thorium can be commercially extracted from the waste — has been dismissed by environmental scientists as technically unproven at scale. This is a government choosing foreign investment over the promises it made to the communities it asked to vote for it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Lynas has been politically toxic since its Kuantan plant opened in 2012. PH 1.0 imposed strict conditions on waste removal that were never enforced. BN extended the licence before GE15. PH 2.0 has now gone further with a 10-year renewal. Every government promises to resolve the waste issue; none have. The pattern: campaign against Lynas in opposition, accommodate Lynas in government, hope voters forget.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You campaigned against this plant. Your own members built careers opposing it. Your environmental base voted for you partly because of Lynas. And now you’ve given them a 10-year renewal while nearly a million tonnes of radioactive waste sits in Pahang with no disposal plan. If the waste can be processed, prove it. If it can’t, explain why you renewed anyway. Your voters in Pahang deserve an honest answer, not strategic doublespeak.

[BM] Kualiti Air Sungai Merosot — Lebih Banyak Sungai Tercemar Pada 2025 Berbanding 2024

Said by: Natural Resources Ministry

Category: Other

Date: 2026-01-28

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Menurut data yang dikeluarkan Kementerian Sumber Asli pada lewat Januari 2026, kualiti air sungai Malaysia merosot tahun ke tahun. Sungai bersih menurun daripada 486 kepada 468. Sungai berkualiti “sederhana” meningkat daripada 161 kepada 171. Sungai “sedikit tercemar” melonjak daripada 25 kepada 33. Angka bergerak ke arah yang salah merentasi setiap kategori. Kemerosotan ini berlaku ketika kerajaan secara agresif mendorong pengembangan perlombongan nadir bumi — satu tapak di Perak sudah menukarkan sungai kepada warna biru akibat pelepasan perlombongan — dan terus mempromosikan Malaysia sebagai peneraju “ekonomi hijau.”

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Sungai ialah penunjuk awal bagi tadbir urus alam sekitar. Jika kualiti air merosot sementara kerajaan mendakwa kemajuan alam sekitar, dakwaan itu kosong. Ini berlaku di bawah kerajaan yang mengumumkan Pelan Ketahanan Iklim, menetapkan sasaran ekonomi hijau, dan meletakkan Malaysia sebagai peka alam sekitar di pentas dunia. Data berkata sebaliknya. Lebih banyak sungai tercemar bermakna sumber air minuman lebih buruk, ekosistem terdegradasi, kos rawatan air lebih tinggi, dan risiko kesihatan bagi komuniti yang tinggal berhampiran laluan air terjejas — terutamanya rakyat Malaysia luar bandar dan berpendapatan rendah.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: Kerajaan bermegah tentang sasaran ekonomi hijau sambil mengetuai kemerosotan alam sekitar yang boleh diukur. Anda tidak boleh serentak mendorong pengembangan perlombongan nadir bumi secara agresif, meluluskan pembangunan pusat data yang membebankan sumber air, dan membiarkan piawaian pelepasan industri tidak dikuatkuasakan — kemudian mendakwa kepimpinan alam sekitar dengan muka serius. Data kualiti sungai bukan subjektif. Ia diukur. Ia diterbitkan. Dan ia menunjukkan kemerosotan. Jurang antara retorik alam sekitar kerajaan dan hasil alam sekitarnya bukan masalah komunikasi — ia kegagalan tadbir urus. Apabila sungai anda semakin kotor di bawah pengawasan anda, sasaran hijau anda adalah rekaan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Penguatkuasaan alam sekitar Malaysia lemah secara kronik merentasi pentadbiran. Pembuangan toksik Sg Kim Kim di Johor (2019) memasukkan ratusan ke hospital. Sungai Perak bertukar biru akibat perlombongan nadir bumi (2025). Banjir monsun tahunan — diburukkan oleh penyahutanan dan perancangan saliran yang lemah — memindahkan puluhan ribu. Jabatan Alam Sekitar secara konsisten kurang dibiayai dan kurang kakitangan. Di bawah BN, perlindungan alam sekitar dikorbankan untuk pembangunan. Di bawah PH, pengorbanan sama berterusan di bawah jenama yang lebih hijau. Coraknya bukan partisan: korek sekarang, bersih tak pernah.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Terbitkan pecahan sungai demi sungai. Namakan pencemar. Kuatkuasakan piawaian pelepasan dengan penalti sebenar — bukan surat amaran yang difailkan dan dilupakan. Jika perlombongan nadir bumi menukarkan sungai biru dan kualiti air merosot secara nasional, naratif “ekonomi hijau” anda adalah pembohongan yang disokong data kementerian anda sendiri. Kerajaan yang mengukur kemerosotan alam sekitar dan tidak berbuat apa-apa mengenainya bukan mentadbir — ia mendokumentasikan kegagalannya sendiri. Biayai Jabatan Alam Sekitar dengan sewajarnya. Kuatkuasakan piawaian pelepasan industri. Atau berhenti berpura-pura anda mengambil berat tentang alam sekitar.

River Water Quality Declining — More Polluted Rivers in 2025 Than 2024

Said by: Natural Resources Ministry

Category: Other

Date: 2026-01-28

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: According to data released by the Natural Resources Ministry in late January 2026, Malaysia’s river water quality deteriorated year-over-year. Clean rivers dropped from 486 to 468. “Moderate” quality rivers rose from 161 to 171. “Slightly polluted” rivers jumped from 25 to 33. The numbers are moving in the wrong direction across every category. This decline occurred as the government aggressively pushed rare earth mining expansion — one site in Perak already turned a river visibly blue from mining discharges — and continued to promote Malaysia as a “green economy” leader.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rivers are the canary in the coal mine for environmental governance. If water quality declines while the government claims environmental progress, the claims are hollow. This is happening under a government that announced a Climate Resilience Plan, set green economy targets, and positioned Malaysia as environmentally conscious on the world stage. The data says otherwise. More polluted rivers mean worse drinking water sources, degraded ecosystems, higher water treatment costs, and health risks for communities living near affected waterways — disproportionately rural and lower-income Malaysians.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government boasts about green economy targets while presiding over measurable environmental degradation. You cannot simultaneously push aggressive rare earth mining expansion, approve data centre developments that strain water resources, and allow industrial discharge standards to go unenforced — then claim environmental leadership with a straight face. The river quality data is not subjective. It is measured. It is published. And it shows decline. The gap between the government’s environmental rhetoric and its environmental outcomes is not a communication problem — it is a governance failure. When your rivers are getting dirtier under your watch, your green targets are fiction.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s environmental enforcement has been chronically weak across administrations. The Sg Kim Kim toxic dumping in Johor (2019) hospitalised hundreds. The Perak River turned blue from rare earth mining (2025). Annual monsoon floods — worsened by deforestation and poor drainage planning — displace tens of thousands. The Department of Environment has been consistently underfunded and understaffed. Under BN, environmental protection was sacrificed for development. Under PH, the same sacrifice continues under a greener brand. The pattern is bipartisan: extract now, clean up never.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Publish the river-by-river breakdown. Name the polluters. Enforce discharge standards with real penalties — not warning letters that get filed and forgotten. If rare earth mining is turning rivers blue and water quality is declining nationally, your “green economy” narrative is a lie backed by your own ministry’s data. A government that measures environmental decline and does nothing about it is not governing — it is documenting its own failure. Fund the Department of Environment properly. Enforce industrial discharge standards. Or stop pretending you care about the environment.

Sabah Timber Corruption — Billions in Illegal Logging While Shamsul Iskandar Got Mining Licences from PMX's Office

Said by: Environmental Groups / MACC Investigations

Category: Other

Date: 2025-12-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Sabah, home to some of Malaysia’s last remaining primary rainforests, continues to face massive illegal and unsustainable logging driven by corruption in licence allocation. The crisis was brought into sharp focus when Anwar’s own senior political secretary, Shamsul Iskandar, was charged on 4 December 2025 with receiving bribes totalling RM176,829 from a local businessman for facilitating mineral mining licences in Sabah. Reuters reported the charges exposed corruption “emanating directly from the PM’s office.” The Shamsul case revealed how natural resource extraction in East Malaysia is systematically corrupted by political patronage — with the corruption pipeline running all the way to the Prime Minister’s doorstep.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Sabah’s forests are among the most biodiverse on Earth, home to orangutans, pygmy elephants, and thousands of endemic species. Illegal and corrupt logging doesn’t just destroy irreplaceable ecosystems — it undermines the livelihoods of indigenous communities, contributes to flooding and soil erosion, robs future generations of natural heritage, and costs the state government billions in lost revenue. The corruption-logging nexus turns public natural resources into private profit for politically connected actors.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The fact that mining and logging licences were allegedly being facilitated through the PM’s own office — through his senior political secretary — means corruption in natural resource allocation goes to the very top of the Madani government. This isn’t a rogue official in a remote district; this is systematic corruption with a direct line to Putrajaya. Shamsul had direct access to the PM. The businessman who allegedly bribed him knew that proximity to power translates to access to resources. PH promised to protect Malaysia’s environment and fight corruption. Instead, PH’s own people were allegedly monetising access to Sabah’s resources from the PM’s office.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Sabah’s timber corruption is decades old. Under BN, logging concessions were distributed as political patronage — most notoriously under former CM Musa Aman, who faced money laundering charges related to timber concessions before his case was dropped. The pattern is bipartisan: whoever controls access to Sabah’s natural resources uses that access for political and personal gain. Different government, same corruption, same forests burning. PH was supposed to break this cycle. The Shamsul charges show PH joined the cycle instead.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your own aide was charged with taking bribes for Sabah mining licences. FROM YOUR OFFICE. The forests of Sabah don’t need another “task force” or “special committee.” They need a government whose own people aren’t facilitating their destruction. Publish every mining and logging licence issued during PH’s tenure. Disclose who applied, who approved, and who benefited. Commission an independent audit of Sabah’s natural resource allocation since 2022. Or admit that Madani’s green rhetoric is as hollow as the logs being illegally shipped out of Sabah’s ports.

Perlis Floods 2025 — 34,000 Evacuated, New Mother Forced to Flee Weeks After Birth

Said by: Government / NADMA

Category: Other

Date: 2025-11-25

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late November 2025, severe flooding across northern Malaysia — particularly in Perlis, Kedah, and Kelantan — displaced over 34,000 people. Reuters described scenes where floodwaters turned entire communities into what looked “like an ocean.” Among those evacuated was a new mother forced to flee just weeks after giving birth. A police officer went missing after a vehicle skidded into a storm drain near KLIA. The government announced a Climate Resilience Plan in response, and NADMA coordinated relief operations across affected states.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia floods catastrophically every year during the monsoon season. Every year, the government announces recovery plans, mobilises resources, and promises better mitigation. Every year, the same communities are displaced again. The 2025 Perlis floods — 34,000 evacuated — came just three years after the 2021 floods that killed dozens and displaced hundreds of thousands. The human cost is real and recurring: a mother with a newborn fleeing her home is not a statistic — it’s a governance failure measured in human suffering.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government’s Climate Resilience Plan was announced after the floods hit — not before. That’s not resilience; that’s reaction. Resilience means infrastructure that prevents flooding: retention ponds, upgraded drainage, enforced development controls, and early warning systems that actually work. Instead, Malaysia spends billions on flood relief every year while continuing to approve development in flood-prone areas, deforest catchment zones, and under-invest in drainage infrastructure. The cycle is predictable because the government chooses not to break it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The December 2021 floods killed over 50 people and displaced 400,000. The 2022 and 2023 monsoon seasons brought more flooding to the same areas. Selangor, Pahang, Kelantan, and Perlis are perennial flood zones — and the government knows exactly which areas will flood every year. After the 2021 disaster, Anwar (then opposition) criticised the Ismail Sabri government for reactive flood management. Now in government, PH’s approach is identical: mobilise after the water rises, announce plans after the damage is done, and promise improvements that never materialise before the next monsoon.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You criticised BN and PN for reactive flood management. Three years later, your flood response is the same: react, mobilise, promise, repeat. Publish a national flood mitigation capital expenditure report showing what has actually been built since GE15. Enforce development controls in flood-prone zones. Fund drainage infrastructure before the monsoon, not after. A new mother fleeing her home with a newborn weeks after birth is not an act of God — it’s a policy failure. Own it.

Perak River Turns BLUE from Rare Earth Mining — Operations Suspended

Said by: Johari Abdul Ghani, Environment Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2025-11-20

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In November 2025, a stretch of the Perak River turned bright blue due to chemical discharges from a rare earth mining site. Environment Minister Johari Abdul Ghani confirmed the contamination and ordered operations suspended pending investigation. The incident occurred as the Malaysian government was aggressively pushing rare earth expansion — signing deals with the US and positioning Malaysia as a rival to China’s dominance in critical minerals. The blue river became a viral symbol of the environmental cost of the rare earth push.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia is betting its economic future on rare earth minerals — a strategic commodity in the US-China tech war. But rare earth processing generates toxic waste: radioactive thorium, heavy metals, and chemical byproducts that contaminate water, soil, and ecosystems. The Perak River incident exposed the gap between the government’s rare earth ambitions and its environmental enforcement capacity. If a single mining site can turn a river blue with impunity, what happens when Malaysia scales up to rival China’s output? The environmental cost could be catastrophic.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government suspended operations after the river turned blue — not before. Environmental enforcement in Malaysia is reactive, not preventive. The same government that renewed Lynas’s licence for 10 years (with radioactive waste still stored in Pahang) is now pushing for more rare earth mining without demonstrating it can regulate the existing operations. Johari’s response — suspend and investigate — is the standard Malaysian playbook: act after the damage, announce an investigation, and hope the news cycle moves on. The river turned blue because enforcement failed. Suspension is not accountability.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The Lynas rare earth plant in Gebeng, Pahang has been controversial since 2012. Radioactive waste remains stored on-site with no permanent disposal solution despite a decade of promises. The Bukit Merah rare earth processing site in Perak (operated by Mitsubishi in the 1980s) left a legacy of elevated cancer rates and birth defects that took decades to clean up. Malaysia’s rare earth history is a history of environmental damage — and the government is now repeating the pattern at a larger scale.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You want Malaysia to be a rare earth superpower. But you can’t stop a single mine from turning a river blue. Before signing more rare earth deals with Washington, demonstrate that you can regulate the industry you already have. Publish environmental monitoring data for every active rare earth site. Resolve the Lynas waste issue. Fund independent environmental audits. Or keep chasing rare earth revenue while rivers change colour — and let the rakyat downstream pay the health costs.

Said by: National Sports Council / Athletes

Category: Other

Date: 2025-10-01

📌 What Happened

Malaysian athletes consistently complain about inadequate funding, poor training facilities, and insufficient support systems, even as the government finds billions for GLC bailouts, political appointments, and civil service perks. The sports development budget has been squeezed as fiscal pressures mount, leaving athletes in Olympic and Asian Games sports to rely heavily on private sponsorship or self-funding.

🎯 PH Callout

You want gold medals but fund athletes like they’re an expense line to be minimised. You want national pride in sports but spend more on religious bureaucracy than athletic development. Pick one: either fund sports properly or stop pretending to care when the medals don’t come.

Transboundary Haze Keeps Returning — Government's Response: 'Monitor and Hope Indonesia Cooperates'

Said by: Environment Ministry / ASEAN Coordination

Category: Other

Date: 2025-09-01

📌 What Happened

Despite the ASEAN Agreement on Transboundary Haze Pollution signed in 2002 and ratified by all member states, Malaysia continues to suffer periodic haze episodes caused primarily by agricultural burning in Indonesia. The government’s response remains largely diplomatic — requesting Indonesian cooperation and issuing health advisories — rather than addressing Malaysia’s own contribution through domestic peatland fires and Malaysian-owned plantation companies operating in Indonesia.

🔥 Why It Matters

Air quality monitoring during haze episodes routinely shows unhealthy API readings, affecting millions of Malaysians’ health, shutting schools, and reducing productivity. The economic cost of haze runs into billions of ringgit when accounting for healthcare, lost productivity, tourism impacts, and long-term respiratory health effects. Climate change is making conditions more conducive to fires, yet Malaysia’s own deforestation continues at alarming rates.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The fundamental issue is that palm oil profits are prioritised over air quality, and diplomatic niceties prevent Malaysia from holding Indonesian (and Malaysian-owned) companies accountable. After two decades, the haze problem remains essentially unaddressed. The government talks tough during haze season and goes silent when the skies clear.

🎯 PH Callout

You attend international climate conferences and make pledges about sustainability. But you won’t confront the plantation companies — including Malaysian-owned ones — that cause the haze your rakyat breathes. Monitoring is not governance. Hope is not policy. After 20 years of the same playbook, either change the approach or admit you’ve chosen profits over lungs.
