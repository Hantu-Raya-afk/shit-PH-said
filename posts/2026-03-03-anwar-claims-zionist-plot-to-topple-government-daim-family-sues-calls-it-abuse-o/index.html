Anwar Claims 'Zionist Plot' to Topple Government — Daim Family Sues, Calls It 'Abuse of Power'

Hantu Raya

March 3, 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 What Happened

On March 3, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told parliament that a suspect in an alleged plot to topple the government had engaged an international public relations firm to launch a coordinated attack aimed at undermining national institutions before the next general election. In a follow-up reported by FMT and Malay Mail, Anwar went further — claiming the alleged conspiracy involved “Zionists,” foreign lobbyist groups, bankers, and international media agencies, all driven by what he described as resistance to his government’s “mass scale” anti-corruption push. On March 6, the family of the late former Finance Minister Daim Zainuddin — whose widow Na’imah Abdul Khalid was widely identified as the investigation’s target — filed a lawsuit against Anwar, Inspector-General of Police Mohd Khalid Ismail, and the government, calling the probe “unlawful” and “tainted with bad faith.”

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not just a legal spat — it is the clearest sign yet that Anwar’s government is willing to deploy the language of existential threat and conspiracy to delegitimise political opponents and civil society scrutiny. By invoking “Zionists” — a loaded term in Malaysian politics — Anwar is borrowing from the same ethno-nationalist playbook he once condemned under Mahathir and Najib. The Daim family’s counter-lawsuit reframes the narrative: what the government calls national security, the targets call political persecution. This sets a dangerous precedent where any form of external engagement — hiring a PR firm, engaging media, consulting lobbyists — can be recast as subversion under Section 124B, which carries up to 20 years in prison.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim — the man who spent decades courting foreign media, international human rights organisations, and Western governments to champion his cause from prison — is now telling Malaysians that engaging a PR firm is tantamount to treason. The hypocrisy is industrial-grade. His own political survival was built on foreign advocacy and international pressure campaigns. Now that he’s in power, the same tools become evidence of a “Zionist” conspiracy. Na’imah Abdul Khalid publicly asked the obvious question: since when does hiring a PR firm constitute overthrowing a government? Anwar has no good answer, because the answer would expose the real purpose of this probe — intimidation.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of prime ministers invoking shadowy foreign plots to silence critics. Mahathir Mohamad blamed “Jewish traders” and George Soros for the 1997 financial crisis. Najib Razak’s allies framed the 1MDB investigations as a foreign-backed attempt to destabilise Malaysia. Anwar himself was the target of politically motivated prosecutions under both Mahathir and Najib, with Sodomy I and II widely seen as tools of political persecution disguised as law enforcement. The “plot to topple” playbook is a staple of Malaysian authoritarian governance — and Anwar, despite all his reformist branding, is now running the same play from the other side of the desk.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised accountability. You promised Malaysia would never again see a government that criminalises dissent and weaponises law enforcement against political opponents. Three years in, your Prime Minister is standing in parliament invoking “Zionists” to explain why his government is being criticised — and your police are using Section 124B to investigate a widow for hiring a PR firm. This is not reform. This is the Najib playbook with better PR. If PH cannot tell the difference between legitimate political opposition and an actual coup, then PH has become exactly what it once fought against.
