MACC in Crisis with No Credible Leadership — The Anti-Corruption Watchdog Has Become the Scandal

Hantu Raya

February 25, 2026

Said by: DAP / Bloomberg / CNA Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-25

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC) is embroiled in its worst-ever credibility crisis. Chief Commissioner Azam Baki faces Bloomberg allegations of owning millions in shares (breaching civil servant limits) and colluding with a ‘corporate mafia.’ Rather than resign, Azam filed a RM100 million defamation suit against Bloomberg. DAP, PH’s own coalition partner, has demanded an independent inquiry, saying the government’s task force ‘does not go far enough.’ CNA called it part of Anwar’s ‘perfect storm.’

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: If the anti-corruption agency itself is corrupt, the entire governance framework collapses. MACC is supposed to be the institution that holds everyone else accountable. When MACC is the subject of corruption allegations — and the government’s response is a toothless task force rather than an independent inquiry — it signals that no institution in Malaysia is trustworthy.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s signature governance promise was strengthening anti-corruption institutions. He proposed separating the AG and Public Prosecutor roles (stalled). He promised MACC independence (the agency’s own chief is now the scandal). He promised zero tolerance for corruption (his own aide was charged with bribery). Every anti-corruption promise has not just been broken — it’s been inverted. The anti-corruption infrastructure is worse under PH, not better.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, MACC was widely perceived as politically compromised, used to target opposition politicians while ignoring ruling party corruption. Under PH 1.0, MACC briefly had credibility when it investigated 1MDB. Under PH 2.0, MACC’s credibility has hit rock bottom — the chief is fighting Bloomberg in court while his own agency faces allegations of running a corporate extortion ring. The trajectory: compromised → briefly credible → worse than ever.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your own coalition partner says your response to the MACC crisis is inadequate. Bloomberg — one of the world’s most respected financial news outlets — is standing by its reporting against Azam Baki. Your anti-corruption commission is running a RM100 million lawsuit against journalism instead of fighting corruption. And your response is a ’task force.’ A task force. For the most serious corruption allegation in MACC’s history. That’s not reform — that’s managing the optics while the institution burns.
