Mat Sabu Tells Ministry Staff to Grow Their Own Vegetables — Government's Food Security Plan Is Now a Backyard Garden

Hantu Raya

March 13, 2026

Said by: Mohamad Sabu, Agriculture and Food Security Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Agriculture and Food Security Minister Mohamad Sabu, speaking after the special Cabinet meeting on March 13, directed ministry staff living in landed properties to start growing their own vegetables. He also encouraged households with available land to cultivate fast-harvesting crops such as chilli, eggplant and chives to supplement food supply. The Cabinet also agreed to increase the national rice stockpile to cover nine months. The ministry later had to clarify the vegetable-growing instruction was “not a directive” after public backlash.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Agriculture Minister’s contribution to Malaysia’s crisis response as oil tops $100 a barrel, the KLCI crashes below 1700, and food inflation accelerates. Instead of announcing emergency food price controls, expanded subsidies for farmers, or concrete supply chain interventions, Mat Sabu’s marquee post-Cabinet announcement was that Malaysians should plant vegetables in their backyards. It signals a government that has run out of institutional tools — or never had them — and is now outsourcing food security to individual households while maintaining a RM3.2 billion fuel subsidy bill.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH came to power promising institutional reform, modern governance, and serious food security policy. Three years later, their Agriculture Minister’s crisis plan is a kampung garden. This is a minister whose portfolio covers a RM5 billion budget, FAMA, MARDI, and the entire national food supply chain — and his response to a global energy crisis disrupting supply chains is “tanam sayur sendiri.” The fact that his own ministry had to clarify it was “not a directive” tells you everything: even the bureaucrats know this is embarrassing. You don’t see Thailand or Vietnam telling citizens to plant eggplant — they mandate WFH, cap prices, and activate strategic reserves.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows Mat Sabu’s March 12 post-Cabinet reassurance that food supply is “sufficient until May or June” while telling Malaysians not to panic-buy — the same minister who has been making identical assurances since the crisis began on February 28. In September 2025, Anwar himself urged Malaysians to “farm at home” and “not leave land idle” at a National Farmers’ Market event. The grow-your-own-food rhetoric is now a recurring PH deflection: when the state can’t deliver food security through policy, it tells citizens to become subsistence farmers.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia Madani — a modern, responsive, competent government. Your Agriculture Minister’s crisis plan is telling civil servants to plant chilli in their garden. This is not food security policy. This is a government that has substituted Instagram-friendly photo ops for institutional capacity. If your best advice to Malaysians facing $100-barrel oil and accelerating food inflation is “grow eggplant,” then you have failed at the most basic function of the ministry. The rakyat didn’t vote for gardening tips — they voted for governance.
