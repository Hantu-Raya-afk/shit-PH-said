Anwar Orders Mass Promotion of 125,000 Civil Servants — During Austerity, Ahead of Elections, After a 2-Week Course

Hantu Raya

March 9, 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 What Happened

On March 9, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced at the Ministry of Domestic Trade and Cost of Living (KPDN) assembly in Putrajaya that 125,000 Grade 1 civil servants — administrative, clerical, and financial assistants — will be promoted to Grade 5 effective January 2027. The only requirement: complete a two-to-three-week training course covering basic IT skills. Anwar directed Chief Secretary Shamsul Azri Abu Bakar and the Public Service Department (PSD) to ensure all 125,000 promotions are processed within six months.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is a mass promotion affecting one of the largest blocks of government employees — and it was announced during the same week the government imposed austerity measures, banned Raya open houses, tripled the RON95 subsidy bill to RM2 billion, and told Malaysians not to panic-buy. The timing is not subtle. With GE16 on the horizon and PH’s approval under pressure from the oil crisis, the Azam Baki scandal, and rising cost of living, 125,000 grateful civil servants — plus their families — represent a significant electoral constituency. This is fiscal patronage dressed as human capital development.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

A two-week IT course to jump four grades is not a promotion — it’s a loyalty programme. Real public service reform means restructuring roles, improving service delivery, and tying advancement to performance and merit. Anwar’s government has spent three years talking about reforming the bloated civil service. Instead of restructuring, they’re inflating grades, expanding the wage bill, and calling it “empowerment.” The announcement came at a ministry assembly — not Parliament, not a formal policy rollout — because this is a gift, not a reform.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s civil service has long been a political tool. Najib’s government introduced massive pay rises under Sistem Saraan Malaysia revisions ahead of GE14. Muhyiddin’s Perikatan Nasional offered special payments and bonuses to shore up support. Anwar himself approved a RM500 Hari Raya bonus for civil servants just days before announcing the Raya open house ban for “fiscal discipline.” The pattern is clear: every government uses the 1.7-million-strong civil service as a vote bank, and the SSPA restructuring — which Anwar promised would be merit-based — is now a conveyor belt for mass upgrades.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised a leaner, more efficient public sector. You promised meritocracy. You promised that SSPA would be different from every previous pay scheme. Instead, you’ve turned a four-grade promotion into a two-week course and timed it to land before the next election. If 125,000 promotions can be processed in six months, why has it taken three years to reform fuel subsidies, implement targeted aid properly, or hold your own anti-corruption chief accountable? The civil service deserves real career pathways — not pre-election sweeteners wrapped in a training brochure.
