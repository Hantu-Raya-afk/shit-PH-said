DAP Finally Demands RCI on MACC — Weeks Late, and PAS Correctly Calls It Political Theatre

Hantu Raya

March 10, 2026

Said by: DAP Leadership / Ahmad Fadhli Shaari, PAS Information Chief

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-10

📌 What Happened

On March 10, DAP — the largest party in the Pakatan Harapan coalition with 40 parliamentary seats — formally called for a Royal Commission of Inquiry (RCI) to investigate the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission over allegations first reported by Bloomberg, including that MACC chief Azam Baki breached civil servant shareholding limits and that the agency was involved in corporate intimidation. PAS information chief Ahmad Fadhli Shaari responded by calling the demand “political theatre,” noting that DAP had remained silent when the same Bloomberg reports alleged that Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim personally interfered in MACC investigations.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the clearest example yet of DAP’s credibility gap on institutional reform. The party spent weeks after the Bloomberg exposé refusing to directly challenge Anwar’s handling of Azam Baki, even as eight PKR MPs led by Rafizi Ramli publicly demanded Azam’s suspension in mid-February. DAP’s belated RCI call comes only after its catastrophic Sabah election loss and growing voter fury — not from genuine conviction. When even PAS can accurately identify your reform posture as performative, something has gone deeply wrong with your party’s moral compass.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

DAP had the numbers to force the issue from day one. Forty seats. The largest bloc in government. Instead, they sat on their hands for weeks while Bloomberg’s reporting exposed potential corruption at the highest levels of the anti-graft agency — and Anwar’s alleged interference in it. Now they want credit for demanding an RCI? That’s not accountability. That’s damage control dressed up as principle. And the most damning part: DAP still hasn’t addressed the Bloomberg allegation that their own Prime Minister interfered in MACC affairs. They’re demanding an investigation into everyone except the man they answer to.

📜 Previous Cases

This pattern is pure DAP under the unity government. After the Sabah election wipeout in November 2025, DAP pledged to “accelerate the reform agenda over the next six months.” Months later, the PM tenure limit bill failed partly because government MPs were absent, and DAP’s response was to blame the opposition rather than hold their own coalition accountable. On the Azam Baki shareholding scandal — which first surfaced in 2021 when DAP was in opposition and demanded action — DAP is now the party in government protecting the same man they once wanted removed. The pattern is clear: loud in opposition, silent in power.

🎯 PH Callout

DAP, you promised reformasi. You promised institutional accountability. You promised you were different. But when Bloomberg dropped the biggest anti-corruption story in Malaysian politics since 1MDB, your first instinct was to protect the Prime Minister, not the public. An RCI demand that comes weeks late and deliberately avoids the PM’s role isn’t reform — it’s theatre. If you want to prove PAS wrong, start by addressing the Bloomberg allegations about Anwar’s interference in MACC. Until then, Ahmad Fadhli is right: you are not qualified to champion this.
