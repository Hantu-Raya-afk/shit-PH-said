Penang Exco Sacked After Charged for Assaulting Wife — How Did DAP Vet This Person?

Hantu Raya

February 24, 2026

Said by: Chow Kon Yeow / Fahmi / Penang DAP

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-24

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 24 February 2026, a Penang executive councillor was detained for allegedly assaulting his wife. He contacted Chow Kon Yeow to apologise. He was subsequently charged in court and sacked from his exco position. The incident drew attention to DAP’s leadership vetting processes — how a state-level cabinet member with alleged domestic violence issues was appointed to a senior position.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: An exco member is a state-level cabinet minister — a person entrusted with public authority and decision-making power. When an exco is charged with domestic violence, it raises two questions: did DAP know about any prior incidents before the appointment, and what vetting process exists to prevent this? Domestic violence is not a private matter for public officials — it speaks to character, judgment, and fitness for office.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: DAP positions itself as progressive, rights-based, and modern. The party has championed women’s rights in opposition. But when one of its own exco members is charged with assaulting his wife, the response was reactive — sack him after the charges, apologise, move on. No public review of how the appointment was made. No announced reforms to the vetting process. No commitment to mandatory background checks for future appointments. The progressivism is for press conferences, not for personnel decisions.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: This isn’t the first DAP appointee controversy. Penang DAP has faced internal conflicts over councillor appointments, with disputes between Chow and Guan Eng’s camps. The party’s appointment processes have been criticised as opaque and personality-driven rather than merit-based. The exco assault case is the most extreme example of what happens when vetting is inadequate.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You champion women’s rights in Parliament. Your exco member was charged with assaulting his wife. You sacked him after the fact — which is the minimum. What are you doing to prevent the next case? Implement mandatory background checks, character references, and a formal vetting committee for all state-level appointments. Or just keep reacting to scandals after they happen.
