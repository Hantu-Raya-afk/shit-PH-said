Loke Admits DAP Failed to Get RCI on MACC 'Corporate Mafia' — The Biggest Party in Government Can't Even Hold Its Own Cabinet Accountable

Hantu Raya

March 13, 2026

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister and DAP Secretary-General

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

DAP secretary-general and Transport Minister Anthony Loke admitted in an interview on the online programme The Keywords — published around March 12–13, 2026 — that DAP was unable to persuade the cabinet to establish a Royal Commission of Inquiry (RCI) into the alleged “corporate mafia” within the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC). Loke said the government’s response to the scandal has been “inadequate” and that the public’s expectations have not been met. He stressed that investigations have been “expanded” but stopped well short of the full independent inquiry DAP had been calling for since late February, when Bloomberg first reported that MACC officers were allegedly helping a network of businessmen seize control of companies through raids and investigations.

🔥 Why It Matters

DAP is the largest party in the unity government. It holds the most seats in the Pakatan Harapan coalition. If DAP cannot even convince its own cabinet colleagues to set up an RCI — a tool commonly used to investigate institutional failures in Malaysia — then the entire reform agenda is performative. This is not an opposition party losing a vote in Parliament. This is a senior coalition partner publicly admitting it was overruled on a matter of institutional accountability inside its own government. The signal to the public is clear: when it comes to holding MACC accountable, even your strongest ally at the table cannot get it done.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Loke’s framing is textbook coalition damage control: “we tried, we couldn’t, but investigations are ongoing.” That’s not accountability — it’s managed disappointment. DAP has 40 MPs and multiple cabinet ministers. If the party cannot win a policy argument this straightforward — should we independently investigate credible allegations of institutional corruption inside our anti-corruption agency? — then what exactly is DAP’s leverage in this government? Loke wants credit for asking. The public wants results. Saying the government response is “inadequate” while remaining in that government and accepting that inadequacy is not a principled stand. It’s a press release.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows a pattern. DAP pushed for an RCI on the Azam Baki share scandal — the AG-led task force found RM14 million in shares across nine companies — and got a referral to PSD’s disciplinary board instead. Before that, DAP publicly called for Azam Baki’s suspension over the original 2022 share ownership controversy under the previous government, then quietly accepted his continued tenure once PH came to power. The party’s record on MACC accountability is a series of loud demands followed by quiet retreats. The “corporate mafia” RCI push is the latest iteration: big rhetoric, no institutional follow-through.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia a new standard of governance. You said the days of institutional capture were over. You said MACC would serve the people, not the powerful. Now your biggest coalition partner is on camera admitting it cannot even get a proper investigation into your own anti-corruption agency. If PH cannot hold MACC accountable — the body literally tasked with holding everyone else accountable — then who is watching the watchers? This is not a policy disagreement. This is a test of whether Madani governance means anything at all. So far, the answer is: not enough.
