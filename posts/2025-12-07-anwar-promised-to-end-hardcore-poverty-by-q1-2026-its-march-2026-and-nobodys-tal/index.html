Anwar Promised to End Hardcore Poverty by Q1 2026 — It's March 2026 and Nobody's Talking About It

Hantu Raya

December 7, 2025

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-07

📌 What Happened

On December 7, 2025, at the closing ceremony of the Rancakkan Madani programme in Putrajaya, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim publicly declared that Malaysia’s hardcore poverty rate had fallen to 0.09 per cent — affecting roughly 7,000 households — and told civil servants that since the number was “so small,” the government could “end it by the first quarter of 2026.” Multiple outlets — Bernama, Malay Mail, The Star, Business Today — ran the claim as headline news. It is now mid-March 2026, deep into Q1. There has been no victory declaration, no update on the 7,000 households, and no one in government appears to be talking about it.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was not an off-the-cuff remark. Anwar made hardcore poverty eradication a centrepiece of Budget 2026, allocated RM15 billion in direct assistance, and used the 0.09% figure in multiple speeches as proof of MADANI’s success. Economists had already questioned whether the government’s poverty line — RM1,169 per month for hardcore poverty — meaningfully captures deprivation in a country where urban rents alone can exceed that threshold. The Q1 2026 deadline was supposed to be the crowning achievement. Instead, it has quietly lapsed while the government is consumed by oil price crises, Azam Baki scandals, and speech-restriction laws. The silence tells you everything about how seriously the promise was made.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

This is the classic PH playbook: make a bold, headline-friendly promise with a specific deadline, count on nobody following up, and hope the news cycle buries it. Anwar didn’t just say “we hope to reduce poverty” — he gave a quarter, a percentage, and a specific instruction to civil servants. If the target had been hit, there would be a press conference, a Bernama wire, and a hashtag. The absence of celebration is the evidence. Meanwhile, the same rakyat Anwar promised to lift out of poverty are now watching RON97 hit RM3.85, diesel surge 26 per cent, and food prices climb. The 7,000 households earning under RM1,169 a month are not better off because the Prime Minister moved on to his next speech.

📜 Previous Cases

Anwar has a pattern of setting deadlines that expire in silence. In 2024, he declared hardcore poverty “totally eliminated” in KL, Melaka, and Negeri Sembilan — a claim economists disputed, noting the poverty line itself was too low to be meaningful. The government later quietly revised the Food Poverty Line to RM1,236 in 2024, which should have increased the count, not decreased it. Before that, subsidy reform was promised “within months” of taking office in 2022 — it is now 2026 and targeted subsidies cover only RON95, with the broader reform indefinitely shelved. The pattern is consistent: announce, claim credit, move on, never account for the outcome.

🎯 PH Callout

You told 35 million Malaysians that 7,000 households stood between your government and the eradication of hardcore poverty. You gave yourself until March 2026. It is March 2026. Where are the 7,000 families? Are they out of poverty, or did you just stop counting? You made this the headline of your Budget, the centrepiece of Rancakkan Madani, and the proof that MADANI works. If it worked, show the receipts. If it didn’t, say so. The one thing you cannot do is pretend the promise was never made — because Bernama, Malay Mail, The Star, and Business Today all have it on record.
