Anwar Claims 'Prominent Zionist Group' Is Plotting to Topple His Government — Daim Family Sues for Abuse of Power

Hantu Raya

March 3, 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 What Happened

On March 3, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told the Dewan Rakyat that a suspect in an alleged plot to topple his government had engaged an international public relations firm to launch what he called a “coordinated attack aimed at undermining national institutions before the next election.” He went further, claiming that a group of Malaysians were working with foreign media and a “prominent Zionist group” to destabilise the country. Neither Anwar nor police identified the suspect or the firm. On March 6, the family of late former finance minister Daim Zainuddin — his widow Na’imah Abdul Khalid, two sons, and two family lawyers — filed a lawsuit at the Kuala Lumpur High Court against Anwar, Inspector-General of Police Mohd Khalid Ismail, and the government, calling the investigation “unlawful, unconstitutional and tainted with bad faith.”

🔥 Why It Matters

This episode crystallises a dangerous pattern in Anwar’s governance playbook: when scrutiny intensifies, invoke existential threats. The “Zionist plot” framing is particularly reckless — it imports the language of global conspiracy into a domestic accountability dispute, weaponises antisemitic tropes for political cover, and signals to the Malaysian public that any criticism of the government can be reframed as foreign subversion. The Daim family lawsuit now forces the courts to examine whether Section 124B — which carries up to 20 years’ imprisonment — is being used as a political tool rather than a genuine security measure. If the courts find the probe unlawful, it would be a devastating blow to Anwar’s credibility as a reformist leader.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim spent decades in opposition courting international media, foreign governments, and global civil society to pressure Malaysian governments. His own political survival depended on foreign advocacy — from Washington think tanks to the Wall Street Journal editorial page. Now that he holds the keys to Putrajaya, engaging a PR firm becomes a plot to topple the government, and foreign media interest becomes a “Zionist conspiracy.” The hypocrisy is industrial-grade. Na’imah Abdul Khalid asked the right question: since when does hiring a PR firm constitute overthrowing a government? If that’s the threshold, half of PH’s own operatives would be in Bukit Aman.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long, ugly history of sitting governments invoking existential threats to justify crackdowns. Mahathir used “foreign interference” to justify Ops Lalang in 1987. Najib’s administration claimed 2015 Bersih rallies were destabilisation attempts funded by foreign agents. Anwar himself was a victim of this playbook — his sodomy charges were widely seen as politically motivated persecution dressed in legal clothing. Now he is running the same play: vague allegations, inflammatory language, Section 124B investigations, and zero transparency about the actual evidence. The playbook hasn’t changed; only the hand holding it has.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia Madani — a government of integrity, transparency, and institutional reform. Instead, you are standing in Parliament invoking “Zionist plots” like a character from a PAS ceramah circa 2013. You are using the same sedition-adjacent laws you once marched against. You are investigating widows and summoning opposition MPs while refusing to name your evidence or your suspects. If the plot is real, show the evidence. If it isn’t, you are doing exactly what Najib did — manufacturing threats to consolidate power. Malaysians didn’t vote for a reformist Najib. They voted for accountability. Deliver it, or stop pretending you’re any different.
