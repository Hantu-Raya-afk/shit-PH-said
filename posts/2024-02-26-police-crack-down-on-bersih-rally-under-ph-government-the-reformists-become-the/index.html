Police Crack Down on Bersih Rally Under PH Government — The Reformists Become the Repressors

Hantu Raya

February 26, 2024

Said by: PH Government; Police (PDRM)

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-02-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 26-27 February 2024, Bersih held a rally calling on the PH government to implement the political reforms it had promised during the 2022 election campaign — including institutional independence, anti-corruption measures, and parliamentary oversight. Police responded with a crackdown that drew immediate condemnation from civil society. Lawyers for Liberty issued a statement condemning the government’s response, criticising the police’s lack of understanding of the Peaceful Assembly Act and the Federal Constitution. Bersih also handed a memorandum to the government urging implementation of long-delayed reforms.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Bersih’s rallies were the oxygen of PH’s rise to power. The Bersih 2.0, 3.0, 4.0, and 5.0 rallies between 2011 and 2016 built the mass movement that eventually toppled BN. PH leaders didn’t just support these rallies — they marched in them. Anwar himself was a beneficiary of Bersih’s advocacy. Now, the same government that rode Bersih’s wave to power is using police to suppress Bersih’s demands for the reforms PH promised. Freedom of assembly is only acceptable when it serves PH’s interests.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH leaders spent a decade demanding the right to peaceful assembly. Nik Nazmi — who was literally charged under the PAA for organising a protest in 2013 — was a PH minister when police cracked down on Bersih. LFL’s condemnation specifically cited a 2014 Court of Appeal ruling from Nik Nazmi’s own case that struck down restrictive provisions of the PAA. PH’s own legal victory against protest suppression is being ignored by PH’s own government. This isn’t about public order — it’s about a government that promised reform and is now using the same repressive tools as its predecessors to avoid being held accountable for broken promises.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, police crackdowns on Bersih rallies were front-page scandals. Tear gas, water cannons, and mass arrests at Bersih 2.0 and 3.0 were cited by international human rights organisations as evidence of Malaysia’s democratic backsliding. PH used these crackdowns to build their narrative as defenders of democracy. Human Rights Watch’s 2024 World Report stated that Malaysia’s new government “largely failed to uphold its pledges to address repression” in its first year — specifically noting the gap between PH’s reform promises and its actual governance record.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You marched with Bersih. You were tear-gassed with Bersih. You used Bersih’s rallies to build the movement that brought you to power. Now you’re sending police after Bersih because they’re demanding you keep the promises you made. The reformists have become the repressors. If you can’t handle peaceful criticism from the very people who put you in power, you don’t deserve that power.
