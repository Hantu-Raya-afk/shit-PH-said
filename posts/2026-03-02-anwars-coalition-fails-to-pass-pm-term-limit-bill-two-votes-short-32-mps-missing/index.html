Anwar's Coalition Fails to Pass PM Term-Limit Bill — Two Votes Short, 32 MPs Missing, Zero Accountability

Hantu Raya

March 2, 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Unity Government Leadership

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 What Happened

On March 2, Anwar Ibrahim’s unity government failed to pass the Constitution (Amendment) Bill 2026, which would have capped the prime minister’s tenure at 10 years or two terms. The bill needed a two-thirds supermajority — 148 votes in the 222-seat Dewan Rakyat — and got 146. Thirty-two MPs were absent, 44 abstained, and none voted against. Among the absentees were several from coalition partner Barisan Nasional, including Sembrong MP Hishammuddin Hussein and Tapah MP Saravanan Murugan. The government has pledged to reintroduce the bill in June.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was supposed to be the easiest reform win in Anwar’s playbook — a popular, symbolically loaded proposal that PH had promised since 2018. Anwar commands a supermajority in Parliament. He failed to pass a bill that no one even voted against. The failure exposes a coalition that cannot enforce discipline on its own members, let alone deliver the institutional reforms it promised Malaysian voters. With a general election due by early 2028, the clock is ticking and the reform scorecard is bleeding red.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

A government with a supermajority that cannot whip its own MPs to show up for a vote it spent weeks championing is not a reform government — it is a performance. Thirty-two MPs did not bother to attend. Forty-four abstained. The opposition did not kill this bill; the coalition’s own inertia did. And the response? “We’ll try again in June.” That is not accountability. That is a government admitting it failed at the most basic function of coalition management and asking for a do-over like a student who forgot to study for the exam.

📜 Previous Cases

This is not the first time Anwar’s coalition has stumbled on its own reform promises. The Anti-Party Hopping Law, passed in 2022, was hailed as a landmark — until it was narrowed in scope and never seriously tested. The promised separation of the Attorney General and Public Prosecutor roles has been delayed repeatedly. The IPCMC (Independent Police Complaints Commission) was watered down into the toothless IPCC. Every time PH promises reform, the pattern is the same: grand announcement, quiet retreat, and a promise to revisit later.

🎯 PH Callout

You ran on reform. You said Mahathir’s 22 years “damaged the country.” You promised term limits in 2018. You promised them again in 2022. You had a supermajority. You had a bill. You had weeks to prepare. And you could not get 148 people to show up. If you cannot pass the easiest reform on your own agenda, what exactly is the unity government for? June is not a second chance — it is a test of whether this coalition has any discipline left at all. The rakyat is watching, and the clock does not reset.
