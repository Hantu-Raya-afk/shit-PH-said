Anwar Calls Corruption 'Treason to the Nation' — While His Government Shields MACC Chief and Interrogates Anti-Corruption NGOs

Hantu Raya

March 14, 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and PKR President

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-14

📌 What Happened

On March 14, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim addressed PKR Sarawak members at a ‘mini convention’ in Kuching, where he backed Yang di-Pertuan Agong Sultan Ibrahim’s call to fight corruption, describing graft as ’treason to the nation.’ He urged party members to remain united and reaffirmed PKR’s commitment to reform, justice, and inclusive progress. The statement came as part of a two-day working visit to Sarawak that included meetings with state leaders and a Madani iftar event.

🔥 Why It Matters

The Prime Minister calling corruption ’treason’ at a party convention would ordinarily be unremarkable — every Malaysian PM says this. What makes it matter is what his government has actually done in the past fortnight. The AG-led task force report on MACC chief Azam Baki’s alleged RM14 million in undisclosed shareholdings was quietly referred to PSD’s disciplinary board instead of being made public. Police questioned C4 Centre CEO Cynthia Gabriel under Section 124B after the anti-corruption NGO published findings critical of government enforcement. And Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil rejected calls for a Royal Commission of Inquiry into MACC. Anwar’s words run directly opposite to his government’s actions.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

You cannot credibly call corruption ’treason’ while your government’s response to the most prominent corruption allegation against your own appointee is to bury the report in a civil service disciplinary process designed for quiet outcomes. The Azam Baki file should be a public test of Madani’s integrity promise — instead, it’s being handled with the same discretion BN used to manage its scandals. Meanwhile, the people actually doing anti-corruption work — NGOs like C4 — are getting police visits. Anwar isn’t fighting corruption. He’s managing the optics of fighting corruption.

📜 Previous Cases

This is a well-worn pattern. At the Selangor PKR Convention in February, Anwar used nearly identical language about the government taking ‘bold and consistent action’ against corruption. In January, he gave enforcement officials a one-week ultimatum to show results — a deadline that came and went without visible consequence. In March 2025, he launched GIACC 2.0 with similar fanfare. Each time, the rhetoric escalates while institutional follow-through stalls. The gap between Anwar’s convention speeches and his government’s actual enforcement record is now one of the defining features of the Madani administration.

🎯 PH Callout

You told us you would be different. You told us Madani meant accountability, not just slogans. You told us the days of protecting cronies and punishing whistleblowers were over. So why is the MACC chief’s RM14 million in shares being handled by a disciplinary board instead of a public inquiry? Why is C4 being questioned by police while Azam Baki is being shielded by process? If corruption is treason, start with your own house. The rakyat didn’t vote for a government that talks like reformists at conventions and governs like the people it replaced.
