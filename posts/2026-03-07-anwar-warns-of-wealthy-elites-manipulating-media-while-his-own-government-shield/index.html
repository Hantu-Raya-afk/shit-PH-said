Anwar Warns of 'Wealthy Elites' Manipulating Media — While His Own Government Shields the MACC Chief From Accountability

Hantu Raya

March 7, 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-07

📌 What Happened

On March 7, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim used an iftar event in Kulim to accuse unnamed Malaysian elites of “corruptly using their wealth to exert influence on international media, online narratives and local politics.” He said he was not referring only to the opposition but to “everyone,” and urged Malaysians to “remain vigilant” against “foreign schemes and conspiracies.” The statement came just four days after he told Parliament about an alleged plot — involving a former senior minister’s wife, foreign PR firms, and Bloomberg — to destabilise his government.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a one-off remark. It is part of a deliberate, escalating narrative by Anwar to reframe legitimate scrutiny of his government — particularly over the Azam Baki shareholding scandal — as a foreign-funded conspiracy by shadowy elites. By keeping the accusation vague (“not just the opposition — everyone”), Anwar gives himself maximum rhetorical cover to dismiss any critic, journalist, or institution that holds his government accountable. This is the same playbook used by Najib Razak during the 1MDB scandal: when the evidence gets uncomfortable, blame foreign interference.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent decades in opposition accusing previous PMs of exactly this — using vague “national security” rhetoric to silence critics and avoid accountability. Now he’s doing it himself, from an iftar stage no less, while his government refuses to suspend an MACC chief credibly accused of breaching shareholding laws. The man who once led reformasi rallies is now warning Malaysians not to be “trapped and used as tools” — by which he means: stop asking hard questions about why my anti-corruption agency looks compromised. The hypocrisy is not subtle. It is structural.

📜 Previous Cases

This fits a pattern as old as Malaysian politics itself. Mahathir blamed “Jewish speculators” during the 1997 Asian financial crisis to deflect from governance failures. Najib dismissed the Wall Street Journal’s 1MDB reporting as a conspiracy by “certain people” who wanted to “topple a democratically elected government.” Anwar himself, when he was in opposition, called these deflections authoritarian. Now in power, he has adopted the identical rhetorical framework — foreign agents, shadowy elites, media manipulation — with only the names changed.

🎯 PH Callout

You ran on transparency, institutional reform, and media freedom. You told Malaysians that under your government, the press would not be intimidated and corruption would be fought without fear or favour. Now your Prime Minister is standing at a podium accusing unnamed elites of weaponising the media — while Bloomberg’s reporting on Azam Baki’s shareholdings remains unanswered, while Bersih gets publicly scolded for calling the MACC a political tool, and while Rafizi — your own former economy minister — has to take to the streets to demand accountability. If “wealthy elites” are the problem, start with the ones in your own Cabinet who benefit from the system you promised to dismantle.
