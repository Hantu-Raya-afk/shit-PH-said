Unity Coalition 'Faltering' — Reform Mandate Unmet, Power Struggles Naked

Hantu Raya

February 15, 2026

Said by: UCA News Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: ‘Malaysia’s Unity Government is beginning to look less like a grand coalition forged in the national interest and more like a fragile ceasefire among parties that no longer trust one another.’ PH campaigned on clear, specific, publicly measurable pledges — most remain unmet.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When even sympathetic international observers describe your coalition as ‘faltering,’ the narrative has shifted from ‘can they deliver?’ to ‘how long can they survive?’ The unity government was sold as a necessary compromise to keep extremists out. But compromise without delivery is just power-sharing for its own sake.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Unmet manifesto pledges — UUCA repeal, toll abolition, anti-hopping law enforcement, institutional reform — are piling up. Each broken promise erodes the moral distinction between PH and the governments it replaced. A coalition that exists only to prevent the alternative from governing is not governing — it is occupying.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: PH 1.0 under Mahathir also promised sweeping reform and delivered almost nothing before collapsing in the Sheraton Move. The pattern is identical: form a coalition, promise reform, get bogged down in internal power struggles, deliver nothing, and hope the fear of the alternative keeps voters loyal. Malaysia’s voters have now watched two PH governments fail to deliver on the same promises. At some point, the excuse runs out.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You told voters the unity government was necessary for stability and reform. You got the stability (barely). Where’s the reform? Every month that passes without delivering on your own manifesto, you look less like a reform government and more like a power-sharing arrangement with no purpose beyond self-preservation.
