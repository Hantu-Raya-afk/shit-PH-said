Penang Undersea Tunnel Project 'Still Active' — 14 Years, Zero Construction, RM6.3B Price Tag

Hantu Raya

November 14, 2025

Said by: Chow Kon Yeow, Penang Chief Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-14

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 14 November 2025, Chow Kon Yeow told media that the Penang Undersea Tunnel Project is ’not an abandoned project but is still active and in the technical review and design completion phase.’ The RM6.3 billion project was proposed in 2011 under the Penang Transport Master Plan (PTMP). Fourteen years later, not a single metre of tunnel has been constructed. Meanwhile, the corruption trial of former CM Lim Guan Eng over the project continues, with MACC witnesses describing it as ‘akin to a land scam.’

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A RM6.3 billion project proposed 14 years ago with zero construction is either a failed project or a phantom one. Chow’s insistence that it’s ‘still active’ strains credibility to breaking point. The project was supposed to solve Penang’s traffic crisis — which has only worsened during the 14-year wait. Meanwhile, the former CM who approved the project is on trial for corruption related to it. At what point does ‘still active’ become ‘still pretending’?

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: ‘Technical review and design completion phase’ — after 14 years. For comparison, China built the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau Bridge (the world’s longest sea crossing) in 9 years. Singapore built the Thomson-East Coast MRT line in less time. Penang can’t complete the design phase for a tunnel that was supposed to be built a decade ago. The project exists on paper to justify the PTMP’s existence and the land reclamation that was supposed to fund it. The tunnel is Schrödinger’s infrastructure — simultaneously active and non-existent.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The PTMP was criticised from the start for its opaque procurement — using request-for-proposal mechanisms instead of open tenders. The Penang South Islands reclamation was conceived as the funding mechanism for the PTMP. So the logic is: destroy the sea to fund a tunnel that hasn’t been built in 14 years. Each project justifies the other; neither delivers for the public.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Fourteen years. RM6.3 billion. Zero metres built. Former CM on trial for corruption related to the project. Current CM insists it’s ‘still active.’ At what point do you admit this project is a failure? Cancel it, audit the spending, and redirect the funds to transport solutions that can actually be built — bus rapid transit, cycling infrastructure, traffic management. Penang’s commuters need solutions, not phantom tunnels.
