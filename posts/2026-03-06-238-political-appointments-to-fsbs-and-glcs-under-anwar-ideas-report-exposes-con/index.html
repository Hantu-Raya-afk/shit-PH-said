238 Political Appointments to FSBs and GLCs Under Anwar — IDEAS Report Exposes 'Connections Over Competence'

Hantu Raya

March 6, 2026

Said by: IDEAS Report / Anwar Ibrahim Administration

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 What Happened

The Institute for Democracy and Economic Affairs (IDEAS) released a report titled Who Sits at the Table: From Political to Public Appointees in Malaysia’s Federal Statutory Bodies on March 6, 2026, revealing that 238 political appointments were made to federal statutory bodies (FSBs) and government-linked companies (GLCs) under Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim’s administration between November 2022 and October 2025. Of these, 222 were to FSBs and 16 to GLCs. The report reviewed 135 agencies and found that 78% of chairperson positions were filled based on political connections rather than competence. Most lacked clearly defined qualification criteria. The appointments were concentrated in key ministries, and the governance provisions around them were described as inconsistent and fragmented.

🔥 Why It Matters

This report is a direct, data-backed indictment of PH’s reform credentials. Anwar came to power promising meritocracy, transparency, and an end to the patronage politics that defined BN-era governance. Three years in, an independent, respected think tank has documented that political appointments haven’t just continued — they dominate. When 78% of the people running your statutory bodies got the job through connections rather than qualifications, you are not reforming governance. You are changing the names on the patronage list. This is not a fringe accusation — IDEAS is one of Malaysia’s most credible policy institutes, and its Pantau Kuasa initiative has tracked appointments across multiple administrations.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH sold voters the idea that they were fundamentally different from UMNO and BN. That the era of politically connected chairs presiding over government agencies was over. IDEAS’ numbers say otherwise: 238 appointments in under three years, with the overwhelming majority driven by political affiliation rather than professional merit. This is not reform — it is rotation. Different coalition, same playbook. The only thing that changed is the party logo on the appointment letters. Anwar has gone from demanding accountability for political appointments in opposition to treating them as a natural entitlement of power in government. If competence mattered, these numbers would look different. They do not.

📜 Previous Cases

Political appointments to statutory bodies and GLCs have been a recurring feature of Malaysian governance for decades. Under Najib Razak, the practice was widely criticised — particularly at 1MDB, Tabung Haji, and FELDA, where politically connected chairs presided over mismanagement and outright corruption. PH’s 2018 manifesto explicitly promised to end this, and the short-lived PH 1.0 government made some initial moves toward meritocratic appointments before collapsing in the Sheraton Move. Under PH 2.0, the pattern has returned with a vengeance. IDEAS’ Pantau Kuasa data shows that Anwar’s government is not the outlier it promised to be — it is the continuation of a structural problem that PH once made its name fighting.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysians a new Malaysia. You said the era of political cronies was over. You told voters that under Madani, competence would matter more than connections. IDEAS just put a number on that promise: 238 political appointments, 78% driven by politics, not merit. That is not reform — that is business as usual with better branding. If PH cannot even clean up the appointment process for statutory bodies — a basic, achievable governance reform with no legal barriers — why should anyone believe the bigger promises about institutional independence, anti-corruption, or meritocracy? The data speaks louder than the slogans.
