River Water Quality Declining — More Polluted Rivers in 2025 Than 2024

Hantu Raya

January 28, 2026

Said by: Natural Resources Ministry

Category: Other

Date: 2026-01-28

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: According to data released by the Natural Resources Ministry in late January 2026, Malaysia’s river water quality deteriorated year-over-year. Clean rivers dropped from 486 to 468. “Moderate” quality rivers rose from 161 to 171. “Slightly polluted” rivers jumped from 25 to 33. The numbers are moving in the wrong direction across every category. This decline occurred as the government aggressively pushed rare earth mining expansion — one site in Perak already turned a river visibly blue from mining discharges — and continued to promote Malaysia as a “green economy” leader.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rivers are the canary in the coal mine for environmental governance. If water quality declines while the government claims environmental progress, the claims are hollow. This is happening under a government that announced a Climate Resilience Plan, set green economy targets, and positioned Malaysia as environmentally conscious on the world stage. The data says otherwise. More polluted rivers mean worse drinking water sources, degraded ecosystems, higher water treatment costs, and health risks for communities living near affected waterways — disproportionately rural and lower-income Malaysians.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government boasts about green economy targets while presiding over measurable environmental degradation. You cannot simultaneously push aggressive rare earth mining expansion, approve data centre developments that strain water resources, and allow industrial discharge standards to go unenforced — then claim environmental leadership with a straight face. The river quality data is not subjective. It is measured. It is published. And it shows decline. The gap between the government’s environmental rhetoric and its environmental outcomes is not a communication problem — it is a governance failure. When your rivers are getting dirtier under your watch, your green targets are fiction.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s environmental enforcement has been chronically weak across administrations. The Sg Kim Kim toxic dumping in Johor (2019) hospitalised hundreds. The Perak River turned blue from rare earth mining (2025). Annual monsoon floods — worsened by deforestation and poor drainage planning — displace tens of thousands. The Department of Environment has been consistently underfunded and understaffed. Under BN, environmental protection was sacrificed for development. Under PH, the same sacrifice continues under a greener brand. The pattern is bipartisan: extract now, clean up never.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Publish the river-by-river breakdown. Name the polluters. Enforce discharge standards with real penalties — not warning letters that get filed and forgotten. If rare earth mining is turning rivers blue and water quality is declining nationally, your “green economy” narrative is a lie backed by your own ministry’s data. A government that measures environmental decline and does nothing about it is not governing — it is documenting its own failure. Fund the Department of Environment properly. Enforce industrial discharge standards. Or stop pretending you care about the environment.
