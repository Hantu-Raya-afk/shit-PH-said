Anwar Announces RM500 Cash Handout for Civil Servants and Mosque Staff — Hari Raya Election Season Begins

Hantu Raya

March 9, 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 9 March 2026, Anwar Ibrahim announced Special Financial Assistance (Bantuan Kewangan Khas, BKK) 2026: RM500 for civil servants Grade 15 and below, RM250 for pensioners and veterans, and RM500 for approximately 70,000 mosque and religious personnel — imams, bilal, siak, marbut, KAFA and takmir teachers. Payments land on 13 March, days before Hari Raya Aidilfitri, when household spending peaks and political goodwill is most visible. Anwar holds both the PM and Finance Minister portfolios, giving him unilateral authority over the timing and targeting of such disbursements.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This is textbook election-cycle spending: targeted, populist, and timed for maximum emotional impact during a religious holiday. Bundling civil servants and mosque personnel in the same announcement shores up two critical vote banks simultaneously — the bureaucracy and the religious establishment. The RM500 for mosque staff is a transparent attempt to undercut PAS’s monopoly on the religious vote, funded not by institutional reform but by direct cash transfers from public funds. When a PM who is also Finance Minister announces pre-Raya handouts with no independent fiscal check, the line between governance and campaigning disappears entirely.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Cash handouts before festive seasons is the BN playbook that PH spent decades criticising. Najib’s BR1M was mocked as “vote buying” by PH in opposition. Anwar’s BKK is the same thing with a different name. The inclusion of mosque personnel is particularly cynical: these roles are traditionally associated with PAS-aligned rural and semi-urban constituencies, making this a direct bid to compete with PAS on religious ground using public money. If you wanted to improve the lives of mosque workers, you’d reform their employment terms and pay scales permanently — not throw them a pre-Raya bonus when you need their votes.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Najib’s BR1M (later BSH) was the original festive cash handout programme, widely criticised by PH as populist vote-buying. Muhyiddin’s BPN during COVID was similarly timed for political impact. Every Malaysian PM discovers the electoral power of cash transfers before major holidays. Anwar promised to be different. He isn’t.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You mocked BR1M for years. Called it bribery. Called it unsustainable. Now you’re doing the exact same thing, timed to the exact same calendar, targeting the exact same vote banks — with the added bonus of buying the mosque vote with taxpayer money. If this is what ‘Malaysia Madani’ looks like, it’s just 1Malaysia with better branding.
