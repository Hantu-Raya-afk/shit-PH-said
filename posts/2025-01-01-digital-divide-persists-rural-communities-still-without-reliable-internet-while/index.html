Digital Divide Persists — Rural Communities Still Without Reliable Internet While Government Talks 5G

Hantu Raya

January 1, 2025

Said by: MCMC / Rural Development Ministry

Category: Education

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As of early 2025, millions of Malaysians in rural Sabah, Sarawak, and the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia still lack reliable internet access. While the government spent billions on the 5G DNB versus Telekom Malaysia dual-network fiasco, the JENDELA programme to improve basic digital infrastructure faced delays and coverage gaps. Students in rural schools were severely disadvantaged during COVID-19 distance learning, and the digital divide has not meaningfully narrowed since. MCMC’s own data shows connectivity gaps between urban and rural areas remain significant, with many rural schools relying on unstable satellite connections or no connectivity at all.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The digital divide compounds every other form of marginalisation. Rural students can’t access online learning resources. Small businesses can’t participate in e-commerce. Healthcare providers can’t use telemedicine. Government services that have moved online are inaccessible to communities without internet. Kids in longhouses and kampungs can’t load a basic web page while KL gets 5G. The irony of spending billions on cutting-edge infrastructure while basic connectivity remains unavailable to millions is not lost on anyone except apparently the government.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government chose to spend billions on a dual 5G network — solving a political problem between telecom companies — while JENDELA, the basic connectivity programme, faces delays. For Orang Asli and indigenous communities in particular, digital exclusion compounds existing educational and economic marginalisation. A government that prioritises 5G headlines over universal basic connectivity has its priorities backwards. A truly “Madani” government would ensure every Malaysian can access basic internet before giving KL residents faster streaming.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Digital infrastructure promises have been made under every Malaysian government. Under BN, the 1Malaysia Internet Centre programme was launched — many centres were built but poorly maintained or unstaffed. Under PH 1.0, digital infrastructure was briefly prioritised before the Sheraton Move. Under PH 2.0, JENDELA has continued but with persistent delays. The pattern is consistent: announce connectivity programmes, build some infrastructure, under-maintain it, and move on to the next headline technology while basic access gaps persist.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You spent billions on 5G so KL can stream faster. Meanwhile, a kid in a Sarawak longhouse can’t submit homework online. That’s not a digital divide — it’s digital apartheid. Prioritise JENDELA completion. Ensure every school in Malaysia has reliable broadband. Fix basic connectivity before chasing 5G headlines. Or keep building luxury infrastructure for urban areas while rural Malaysians — the communities that vote for you in every election — remain disconnected from the digital economy you keep promoting.
