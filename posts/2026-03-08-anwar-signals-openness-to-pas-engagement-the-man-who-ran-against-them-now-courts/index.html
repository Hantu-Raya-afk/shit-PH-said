Anwar Signals Openness to PAS Engagement — The Man Who Ran Against Them Now Courts Them

Hantu Raya

March 8, 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 8 March 2026, while defending his government’s RM15.5 billion anti-corruption recovery figure, Anwar Ibrahim simultaneously signalled openness to closer engagement with PAS — the Islamist party his coalition was built to oppose. This came as DAP was publicly reviewing its coalition ties and PH had just been wiped out in the Sabah state election, winning only 1 of 20 seats.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The entire premise of Pakatan Harapan was that it represented the alternative to PAS’s religious extremism and UMNO’s ethno-nationalist patronage. Anwar’s willingness to court PAS — even as DAP, his most loyal coalition partner, is hemorrhaging support — signals that electoral survival has overtaken ideology entirely. If PH was built to oppose PAS, and PH’s leader is now courting PAS, what exactly does PH stand for anymore?

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Rather than fixing the internal fractures that caused the Sabah wipeout — DAP’s marginalisation, PKR’s Rafizi split, manifesto promises left unmet — Anwar is hedging toward the same political accommodation he once condemned. This is not pragmatism. It’s desperation dressed as statesmanship. You don’t fix a coalition built on reform by inviting the anti-reform party to the table. You fix it by actually reforming.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Mahathir courted PAS during PH 1.0 behind the scenes, contributing to the coalition’s collapse in the 2020 Sheraton Move. UMNO has flirted with PAS repeatedly when politically convenient, forming Muafakat Nasional in 2019. Every Malaysian coalition that courts PAS ends up making concessions on secular governance. Anwar watched this playbook destroy PH 1.0 — and is now running it himself.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You spent GE15 telling Malaysians that PAS was a threat to multicultural Malaysia. Your own coalition partners marched against PAS’s brand of politics. Now that you’re losing elections, PAS is suddenly worth engaging? Either PAS is a threat to Malaysian democracy or it isn’t. Pick one and stick with it. Your voters deserve consistency, not electoral desperation.
