Rosmah Acquitted of All 17 Money Laundering Charges — PH Politicians Who Spent a Decade Attacking Her Go Silent

Hantu Raya

December 19, 2024

Said by: PH Politicians (Tony Pua, Rafizi Ramli, et al.) / High Court

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-12-19

📌 What Happened

On December 19, 2024, High Court judge K. Muniandy acquitted and discharged Datin Seri Rosmah Mansor — wife of jailed former PM Najib Razak — of all 17 charges against her in a money laundering and tax evasion case involving RM7.09 million. The judge ruled the prosecution failed to present key elements of the offence. Rosmah had been charged in October 2018 with 12 counts of money laundering and 5 counts of failing to declare income. MalaysiaNow contacted multiple PH leaders for comment — almost all refused. Tony Pua, who had sarcastically mocked Najib just two months earlier, did not answer calls. Rafizi Ramli, PH’s most vocal anti-corruption crusader, also stayed silent.

🔥 Why It Matters

Rosmah was PH’s ultimate political piñata — the living symbol of BN excess, corruption, and impunity. For over a decade, PH leaders from Anwar to Pua to Rafizi used her name, her handbags, and her alleged crimes to rally voters and justify their existence as a reform movement. When the court acquitted her, the silence was deafening. Not a single senior PH leader issued a statement demanding accountability, questioning the prosecution’s failure, or committing to appeal. This silence tells you everything about what PH’s anti-corruption crusade was always about: opposition theatre, not institutional reform.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH spent years telling Malaysians that Rosmah was the face of kleptocracy. They won elections on that narrative. Now that they’re in government — with control over the AGC, the police, and prosecution strategy — they can’t even muster a press statement when the case collapses? If the prosecution was weak, whose fault is that? PH has been in power since November 2022. They had two years to ensure cases were airtight. Instead, key witnesses reportedly died or went missing, and the AGC later dropped the appeal entirely. This isn’t just silence — it’s complicity in the collapse of accountability.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows the exact pattern of Najib’s pardon in February 2024, where the Pardons Board halved his sentence while Anwar served as PM. It follows the dropping of 47 corruption charges against DPM Zahid Hamidi in September 2023. Every major BN-era corruption case under Anwar’s watch has either been weakened, settled, or collapsed — and every time, PH’s former attack dogs go quiet. The anti-corruption movement that defined Malaysian politics for a decade is being dismantled by the very people who built it.

🎯 PH Callout

You built Pakatan Harapan on the promise that Malaysia deserved better than Najib and Rosmah. You told voters that justice would be served. Now Rosmah walks free, your own prosecutors fumbled the case, and not one of you has the spine to explain what went wrong. If this is what ‘Reformasi’ looks like in practice — silent acceptance when your political enemies go free on your watch — then you have become exactly what you promised to replace.
