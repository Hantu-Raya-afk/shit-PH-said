Tens of Thousands March Demanding Anwar Resign Over Cost of Living — First Major Protest Since GE15

Hantu Raya

July 26, 2025

Said by: Opposition parties and protesters

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-07-26

The Streets Speak

On 26 July 2025, tens of thousands of Malaysians marched to Merdeka Square in Kuala Lumpur, demanding Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim step down. The rally — the first major protest since GE15 — was driven by rising costs and broken reform promises.

The Facts

Fact: Al Jazeera reported thousands gathered at Independence Square, calling for Anwar to resign.

Fact: Bloomberg’s headline was blunt: “Anwar’s Broken Promises Spark Protests Demanding His Ouster.”

Fact: The rally was organized by opposition parties but attracted broad public participation driven by cost of living anger.

Fact: By October 2025, a survey found 77% of Malaysians said they struggled with rising living costs.

Fact: Just days before the protest, Anwar announced a RM100 cash payment to all adult citizens and a further RON95 price cut — widely seen as a pre-emptive measure.

Why It Matters

  • When tens of thousands march against a government that promised “keluarga Malaysia” and reform, it is not a fringe protest. It is a verdict.
  • The RM100 handout was political triage — a band-aid on structural economic pain.
  • 77% struggling with cost of living. That is not an opposition narrative. That is a national reality. *Sources: Al Jazeera · Bloomberg · *Business Today
