Transboundary Haze Keeps Returning — Government's Response: 'Monitor and Hope Indonesia Cooperates'

Hantu Raya

September 1, 2025

Said by: Environment Ministry / ASEAN Coordination

Category: Other

Date: 2025-09-01

📌 What Happened

Despite the ASEAN Agreement on Transboundary Haze Pollution signed in 2002 and ratified by all member states, Malaysia continues to suffer periodic haze episodes caused primarily by agricultural burning in Indonesia. The government’s response remains largely diplomatic — requesting Indonesian cooperation and issuing health advisories — rather than addressing Malaysia’s own contribution through domestic peatland fires and Malaysian-owned plantation companies operating in Indonesia.

🔥 Why It Matters

Air quality monitoring during haze episodes routinely shows unhealthy API readings, affecting millions of Malaysians’ health, shutting schools, and reducing productivity. The economic cost of haze runs into billions of ringgit when accounting for healthcare, lost productivity, tourism impacts, and long-term respiratory health effects. Climate change is making conditions more conducive to fires, yet Malaysia’s own deforestation continues at alarming rates.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The fundamental issue is that palm oil profits are prioritised over air quality, and diplomatic niceties prevent Malaysia from holding Indonesian (and Malaysian-owned) companies accountable. After two decades, the haze problem remains essentially unaddressed. The government talks tough during haze season and goes silent when the skies clear.

🎯 PH Callout

You attend international climate conferences and make pledges about sustainability. But you won’t confront the plantation companies — including Malaysian-owned ones — that cause the haze your rakyat breathes. Monitoring is not governance. Hope is not policy. After 20 years of the same playbook, either change the approach or admit you’ve chosen profits over lungs.
