Lynas Rare Earth License Renewed 10 Years — Radioactive Waste Still in Pahang

Hantu Raya

March 3, 2026

Said by: Chang Lih Kang, Science Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 March 2026, Science Minister Chang Lih Kang confirmed the renewal of Lynas Rare Earth’s operating licence for 10 years, despite ongoing concerns about radioactive waste storage. Greenpeace Malaysia warned that the country risks becoming a “pollution hub.” Environmental groups said government claims about thorium extraction from the waste are “scientifically unsound.” Close to a million tonnes of radioactive waste from Lynas’s processing operations remain stored in Pahang, with no permanent disposal solution in place.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rare earth processing is a critical strategic industry, and Malaysia’s Lynas plant is the largest outside China. But strategic value doesn’t justify ignoring environmental and health risks. A 10-year renewal without resolving the waste disposal question means Malaysia committed to another decade of accumulating radioactive residue with no exit plan. The government is prioritising geopolitical positioning (reducing Western dependence on Chinese rare earths) over the health and environmental concerns of Pahang residents who live near the waste.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH campaigned against Lynas in GE14. DAP in particular made Lynas a flagship environmental issue. Fuziah Salleh — now a PH senator — built her political career on opposing the plant. Now the same coalition has renewed the licence for a full decade. The justification — that thorium can be commercially extracted from the waste — has been dismissed by environmental scientists as technically unproven at scale. This is a government choosing foreign investment over the promises it made to the communities it asked to vote for it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Lynas has been politically toxic since its Kuantan plant opened in 2012. PH 1.0 imposed strict conditions on waste removal that were never enforced. BN extended the licence before GE15. PH 2.0 has now gone further with a 10-year renewal. Every government promises to resolve the waste issue; none have. The pattern: campaign against Lynas in opposition, accommodate Lynas in government, hope voters forget.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You campaigned against this plant. Your own members built careers opposing it. Your environmental base voted for you partly because of Lynas. And now you’ve given them a 10-year renewal while nearly a million tonnes of radioactive waste sits in Pahang with no disposal plan. If the waste can be processed, prove it. If it can’t, explain why you renewed anyway. Your voters in Pahang deserve an honest answer, not strategic doublespeak.
