Loke Admits DAP Failed to Get Cabinet to Agree on MACC Royal Inquiry — Reform Party Can't Reform Its Own Government

Hantu Raya

March 5, 2026

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister and DAP Secretary-General

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-05

📌 What Happened

On March 5, 2026, DAP’s Central Executive Committee reiterated its call for a Royal Commission of Inquiry into allegations that the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission operated as a “corporate mafia” under chief Azam Baki. DAP Secretary-General and Transport Minister Anthony Loke had first proposed the RCI to Cabinet in late February, calling the government’s response to Bloomberg’s February 11 investigative report “inadequate.” But despite repeated public calls and CEC backing, Loke subsequently admitted that DAP could not persuade Cabinet to establish the RCI. Anwar’s political secretary Kamil Azmil Munawar dismissed the proposal, stating any action “must be grounded in established facts and credible evidence” — as if a Royal Commission was not itself a fact-finding body.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is DAP — the party that built its brand on anti-corruption — publicly admitting it cannot convince its own coalition government to investigate its own anti-corruption agency. The Bloomberg report alleged internal MACC documents showed a “corporate mafia” scheme for forced corporate takeovers. The AG’s task force reportedly found Azam Baki holds RM14 million in shares across nine companies. And DAP’s response — the supposed backbone of PH’s reform agenda — amounted to: we tried, we failed, but investigations are ongoing. That is not reform politics. That is coalition captivity.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Loke called the government’s response “inadequate” — and he IS the government. He sits in Cabinet. He is the secretary-general of the second-largest party in the ruling coalition. If DAP cannot secure an RCI with its 40 parliamentary seats and multiple Cabinet positions, then either DAP’s influence in this government is a fiction, or DAP is choosing not to push hard enough because it values coalition stability over institutional accountability. Either way, the reform mandate is dead. Loke’s consolation — that investigations are underway — is meaningless. The government referred the AG’s findings to the PSD disciplinary board, not to a public inquiry. The difference between a disciplinary board and an RCI is the difference between an HR meeting and a courtroom.

📜 Previous Cases

DAP has form on this. In the first Pakatan Harapan government (2018–2020), DAP repeatedly backed down on manifesto promises — ICERD ratification, UEC recognition, the Rome Statute — citing coalition politics. Now in power again with an even larger majority, the pattern repeats. The party that once marched against MACC’s lack of independence now cannot even get its own Cabinet to scrutinise MACC’s alleged corruption. Kit Siang would have written 17 press statements about this by now. Today’s DAP writes one carefully worded CEC resolution and calls it a day.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia that PH would be different. That reform would not be sacrificed on the altar of coalition politics. That MACC would be cleaned up, not covered up. Now Bloomberg has published internal MACC documents alleging a corporate mafia, your own AG’s task force found the chief holding RM14 million in undisclosed shares, and your response is to send it to a disciplinary board and dismiss your own transport minister’s call for an RCI. If DAP cannot secure accountability for the anti-corruption agency, what exactly is DAP for?
