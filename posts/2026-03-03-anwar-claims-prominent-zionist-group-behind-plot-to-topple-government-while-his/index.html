Anwar Claims 'Prominent Zionist Group' Behind Plot to Topple Government — While His Own Anti-Graft Chief Faces RM14 Million Share Scandal

Hantu Raya

March 3, 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 What Happened

On March 3, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told parliament that police were investigating an alleged plot to topple his government, involving what he described as a “group of Malaysians leveraging the influence of the media and international institutions, including involving a prominent Zionist group.” He claimed the alleged conspirators had engaged an international PR firm and targeted Bloomberg as part of a coordinated campaign to undermine national institutions before GE16. He said the plot was triggered by “large-scale corruption investigations” by the MACC and had been running since August 2024. Documents linked to an international firm allegedly outlined strategies to mobilise foreign media and leverage overseas networks to pressure Malaysian institutions.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Prime Minister of a reformist government using parliament — not a party rally — to float a Zionist conspiracy theory as an explanation for why his anti-graft agency is under fire. The MACC scandal is not a foreign plot. It started with a Bloomberg investigative report on February 11 citing internal documents and witnesses, followed by growing public outrage over chief Azam Baki’s alleged RM14 million in undisclosed shareholdings. By redirecting attention to shadowy foreign actors and Zionists, Anwar is borrowing from the same playbook he once condemned under Najib and Mahathir — weaponising the “foreign interference” narrative to shield domestic governance failures.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The “plot” Anwar describes — foreign media reporting on a corruption scandal, civil society demanding accountability, and politicians asking questions in parliament — is called democracy. Bloomberg did not destabilise Malaysia; Azam Baki’s alleged share scandal did. The Zionist angle is particularly cynical: Anwar hosted Donald Trump in Malaysia just weeks after Trump visited Israel to declare “undivided support” for Netanyahu, and Anwar himself dismissed anti-Israel protesters at the time. MalaysiaNow reported that decade-old emails from PKR’s own 2016 “Washington project” showed the party doing exactly what Anwar now accuses others of — using foreign connections to pressure Putrajaya. The hypocrisy is industrial-grade.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysian PMs deploying the “foreign plot” card is a tradition older than Putrajaya itself. Najib blamed “foreign agents” when the 1MDB scandal broke. Mahathir accused “currency speculators” during the 1997 crisis. Anwar himself was the target of such conspiracy-mongering when he was jailed under Mahathir. That he is now the one spinning the same yarn — from the Prime Minister’s chair, no less — shows how quickly the reformist veneer peels off once you are the one being investigated. The Daim family has since sued the government over the Zionist allegations, turning Anwar’s parliamentary gambit into a legal liability.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia Madani — integrity, transparency, accountability. Instead, when a credible international outlet published an investigative report on your anti-graft chief’s alleged corruption, your first instinct was to blame Zionists in parliament. You did not call for an RCI. You did not fire Azam Baki. You blamed Bloomberg and foreign conspiracies. This is not reform. This is Najib cosplay with better English.
