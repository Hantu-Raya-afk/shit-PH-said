Chinese and Tamil Schools Used as Political Football Every Budget — Promised Millions, Delivered Scraps

Hantu Raya

October 1, 2025

Said by: Dong Zong / Tamil Foundation / MCA / MIC

Category: Education

Date: 2025-10-01

📌 What Happened

Every Malaysian budget cycle produces the same theatre: Chinese and Tamil school communities lobby desperately for maintenance, construction, and development funds. Politicians announce allocations with fanfare. Then the actual disbursement falls short, is delayed, or comes with conditions that limit use. The cycle repeats annually, with non-Malay communities performing gratitude for inadequate allocations.

🔥 Why It Matters

1.3 million students attend Chinese-medium schools (SJKC) and hundreds of thousands attend Tamil-medium schools (SJKT). These schools educate a significant portion of the non-Malay population and consistently outperform national schools in certain metrics. Yet they receive systematically less government funding per student, forcing communities to self-fund infrastructure that should be the government’s responsibility.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH, which depends heavily on Chinese and Indian votes, should have resolved this decades-old inequity. Instead, PH plays the same game as BN: use budget announcements as photo opportunities with Chinese and Indian community leaders, generate grateful headlines, then under-deliver. The structural disparity in per-student funding between national schools (SK) and vernacular schools remains because addressing it would require either more money (which the government claims it doesn’t have) or admitting that the current system is discriminatory (which it won’t).

📜 Previous Cases

BN used vernacular school allocations as political patronage — offering funds in exchange for community support. MCA and MIC existed primarily to secure these allocations, perpetuating dependency. PH was supposed to break this cycle by funding schools equitably regardless of medium of instruction. Instead, PH has maintained the BN model: Chinese and Indian schools get what PH decides to give them, when PH decides to give it.

🎯 PH Callout

Non-Malay Malaysians didn’t vote PH to continue BN’s patronage model with better optics. They voted for EQUAL treatment. Fund vernacular schools at the same per-student rate as national schools. Not as a favour. Not as a budget announcement photo op. As a constitutional obligation under Article 152 and basic educational equity. Or admit that ‘Madani for all’ has an asterisk: *terms and conditions apply based on ethnicity.
