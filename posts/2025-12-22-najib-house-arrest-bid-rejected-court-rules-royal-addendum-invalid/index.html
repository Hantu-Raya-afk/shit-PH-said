Najib House Arrest Bid Rejected — Court Rules Royal Addendum Invalid

Hantu Raya

December 22, 2025

Said by: High Court Judge Alice Loke / Najib Razak

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-22

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 22 December 2025, the KL High Court ruled that the royal addendum allowing convicted former PM Najib Razak to serve his remaining 1MDB sentence under house arrest was invalid. Judge Alice Loke said the King’s discretion must be exercised “for the public good” and found the addendum lacked proper legal basis. Al Jazeera and Reuters both reported the ruling. The Selangor Sultan had earlier warned that pardons should not be “arbitrary for certain parties.” Anwar’s government had already halved Najib’s sentence via the Pardons Board in February 2024 — the house arrest bid was the next step in an escalating pattern of leniency for a convicted kleptocrat whose party Anwar now depends on.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The house arrest addendum was widely seen as a backroom deal to keep UMNO in the coalition. Najib’s sentence was first halved (from 12 to 6 years), then the house arrest addendum emerged as a way to get him out of prison entirely while technically still “serving” his sentence. The court’s rejection exposed the political machinery behind it — this was not clemency based on merit, but leniency engineered for coalition management. The judiciary held the line that the executive would not. Judge Alice Loke’s ruling was a rare moment of institutional independence in a system where every other check has failed.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar built his political career opposing Najib. He spent years calling 1MDB the greatest financial scandal in Malaysian history. He demanded accountability. Then he took power, needed UMNO’s votes, and began systematically reducing the consequences for the man who stole billions. First, the Pardons Board halved the sentence. Then, the house arrest addendum appeared. If the court hadn’t intervened, Najib — convicted of stealing from the Malaysian people — would be living in a mansion while the country still owes RM50 billion from his scandal. The man who caused the debt gets a mansion. The rakyat who pays the debt gets toll hikes and diesel price increases. This is Anwar’s Malaysia Madani.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a troubled history with pardons and sentence reductions for politically connected convicts. Anwar himself benefited from a royal pardon after GE14 — which his supporters argued was justified given the politically motivated nature of his conviction. The difference with Najib is that his conviction was for an actual financial crime with billions in documented losses. Halving his sentence and attempting house arrest sets a precedent: if you steal enough and your party is useful enough, the consequences are negotiable. The Selangor Sultan’s warning — that pardons should not be “arbitrary for certain parties” — was a rare royal rebuke that went unheeded by the government.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: The judiciary saved you from yourself. If the court hadn’t ruled the addendum invalid, you would have put a convicted kleptocrat in a mansion while Malaysians pay RM50 billion for his crimes. You already halved his sentence. You already dropped his DPM’s 47 charges. How much more leniency does UMNO need before you admit that Reformasi is dead and coalition survival is all that matters? The court held the line. Will you?
