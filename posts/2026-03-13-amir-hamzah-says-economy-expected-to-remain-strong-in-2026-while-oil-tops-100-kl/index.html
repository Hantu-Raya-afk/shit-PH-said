Amir Hamzah Says Economy 'Expected to Remain Strong' in 2026 — While Oil Tops $100, KLCI Crashes, and Cabinet Announces Austerity

Hantu Raya

March 13, 2026

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Finance Minister II Amir Hamzah Azizan told Bernama on March 13, 2026, that Malaysia’s economic performance in 2025 is “expected to continue driving the country’s economic momentum” in 2026. He said this despite acknowledging that the West Asia war had driven oil past US$100 per barrel, and on the same day the Cabinet announced cost-cutting measures including banning Raya open houses and restricting ministerial overseas travel. The KLCI had crashed below 1700 that same day, and the monthly fuel subsidy bill had ballooned to RM3.2 billion.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the third senior minister in two days to project economic optimism while the country’s finances are visibly deteriorating. Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah said essentially the same thing on March 12. The pattern is clear: the government’s crisis communication strategy is to keep repeating growth targets as if macro numbers insulate the rakyat from fuel price shocks, food inflation, and a tanking stock market. When Finance Minister II says the economy will be “strong” on the same day the Cabinet imposes austerity, the disconnect between governance and messaging becomes a credibility problem.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

If the economy is so strong, why did the Cabinet just ban Hari Raya open houses and restrict overseas travel for fiscal discipline? Why is the government tripling the RON95 subsidy to RM2 billion per month? Why is the KLCI below 1700? Amir Hamzah is not describing reality — he is performing confidence. The job of a Finance Minister is not to be a cheerleader; it is to level with the public about risks and prepare them for trade-offs. Instead, we get the same playbook as always: cite last year’s GDP, project this year’s growth range, and pretend the crisis doesn’t exist.

📜 Previous Cases

This is a recurring pattern. On March 12, Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah maintained the 4.0–4.5% growth target and celebrated 2025’s 5.2% GDP on the same day fuel prices exploded. On March 9, Deputy Economy Minister Mohd Shahar projected “steady” 4–4.5% growth amid a regional war. On March 8, Anwar himself told Malaysians to “be thrifty” — days after committing RM15 billion in cash handouts. The government’s economic messaging exists in a parallel universe from its own policy decisions.

🎯 PH Callout

You campaigned on transparency and competent governance. Your own Cabinet just imposed emergency austerity measures because the fiscal situation is deteriorating. And on the same afternoon, your Finance Minister II tells the press the economy is “expected to remain strong”? Pick one. Either the crisis is serious enough to ban Raya open houses, or the economy is strong. It cannot be both. The rakyat can read headlines — they know when they’re being managed instead of governed.
