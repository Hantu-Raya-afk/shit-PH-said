Anwar's Coalition Contradictions: Reformist PM, Corrupt DPM, and a Party That Enabled 1MDB

Hantu Raya

September 1, 2025

Said by: Multiple analysts and commentators

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-09-01

The Impossible Coalition

Anwar Ibrahim governs Malaysia through a coalition that makes reform structurally impossible. The man who campaigned for decades against corruption now depends on the party that enabled the world’s largest kleptocracy.

The Cast of Characters

  • Anwar Ibrahim (PKR): Built his career on reformasi, anti-corruption, and clean governance.
  • Ahmad Zahid Hamidi (UMNO): Deputy PM. Faced 47 corruption charges. All dropped.
  • DAP: The coalition’s most consistent reform voice. Systematically sidelined in the December 2025 reshuffle.
  • UMNO: Najib’s party. Threatened to review coalition ties when a DAP MP celebrated Najib’s court loss.

The Structural Problem

As Fair Observer noted, Anwar’s coalition is fundamentally unstable because its members have opposing interests:

  • PH voters want reform, accountability, and institutional change.
  • UMNO wants protection for its leaders, patronage for its base, and Malay-first policies.
  • The harder Anwar pushes reform, the more he risks losing UMNO. The more he accommodates UMNO, the more he loses the reform base.

The Result

What you get is not reform. What you get is management. Coalition maintenance disguised as governance. Every major decision — the Zahid NFA, the cabinet reshuffle, the UUCA backtrack, the JAKIM guidelines U-turn — makes sense only through the lens of keeping the coalition together, not making Malaysia better.
