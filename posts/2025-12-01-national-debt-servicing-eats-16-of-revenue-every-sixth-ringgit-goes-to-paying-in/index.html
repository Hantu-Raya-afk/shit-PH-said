National Debt Servicing Eats 16% of Revenue — Every Sixth Ringgit Goes to Paying Interest

Hantu Raya

December 1, 2025

Said by: Ministry of Finance / Bloomberg

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s debt servicing costs consume approximately 16% of federal government revenue — meaning roughly every sixth ringgit collected in taxes goes straight to paying interest on accumulated debt rather than funding schools, hospitals, or infrastructure. With the debt-to-GDP ratio at 64.6% — above the government’s own 60% target — and 1MDB legacy debt still totalling RM50 billion on the books, fiscal space remains severely constrained. Anwar told Bloomberg that debt pressure would only “ease from end-2028.” The Star reported that 1MDB debt remains “Malaysia’s largest fiscal burden.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Debt servicing at 16% of revenue is a silent tax on the future. Every ringgit spent on interest is a ringgit NOT spent on healthcare (where 33,000 doctors are on temporary contracts), education (where PISA scores are declining), infrastructure (where LRT3 is delayed and roads crumble), or social safety nets (where B40 families struggle with costs). The compound effect is devastating: high debt requires high servicing, which reduces fiscal space, which forces more borrowing, which increases future servicing costs. It’s a fiscal doom loop — and PH has done nothing to break it.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government continues to find new spending commitments — subsidies, civil service wages, development projects, 238 political appointments to GLCs — that make fiscal consolidation an ever-receding target. The structural deficit means future generations of Malaysians will inherit a mountain of debt for spending decisions made today, with compound interest ensuring the problem only grows. When Anwar says debt pressure will “ease from end-2028,” he’s asking Malaysians to accept six years of fiscal pain for a promise of eventual improvement. That’s not a plan — it’s a hope, and hope compounds about as well as debt does.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s debt burden is not new, but it has been consistently worsened by political spending. 1MDB added RM50 billion. Pandemic stimulus under Muhyiddin added tens of billions more. Subsidy commitments under every government add annual billions. The Public Finance and Fiscal Responsibility Act was supposed to impose discipline — but the 60% debt-to-GDP target has been breached without consequence. The act has no enforcement mechanism, making it aspirational rather than binding. Every government passes fiscal responsibility laws and then ignores them.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Every sixth ringgit of tax revenue goes to paying interest — not building hospitals, not hiring teachers, not fixing roads. 16% of revenue servicing debt is a governance failure that spans administrations, but you’ve been in power long enough to own it now. Cut wasteful spending: 238 political GLC appointments, RM85.5M propaganda budgets, and discretionary allocations that serve politics rather than people. Enforce corporate tax compliance. Publish a credible fiscal consolidation roadmap with annual targets. Or keep kicking the can to 2028 — and let the next generation pay the bill.
