Rawang Hindu Temple Demolished at Midnight — No Court Order, Police Assisted, PH State Government Silent

Hantu Raya

February 24, 2026

Said by: Selangor PH State Government / Police

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-24

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 24 February 2026, the Sri Uchimalai Muniswaran temple in Rawang, Selangor was demolished in the middle of the night — hours after deities were allegedly relocated following pressure on the temple committee. The demolition was carried out with police assistance and without any court order.

Lawyers Rajesh Nagarajan and Sachpreetraj Singh issued a statement: “We strongly deny the claims by Yayasan Kubra representative Ariff Omar that the temple committee voluntarily removed the deities. That is false and ridiculous since the temple chairman lodged a police report only yesterday complaining that the temple was being pressured to vacate the land without any court order.”

Four men, including activist Tamim Dahri, were arrested but the Shah Alam High Court quashed the remand order and ordered their immediate release. Police classified it as a land dispute, not a racial or religious conflict.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

A Hindu temple was demolished at midnight, with police present, without a court order, in a state governed by PH. This is not something that happens in a country with rule of law. It’s something that happens when institutions have failed and political will is absent.

The timing — amid a broader crackdown on unauthorised Hindu temples in Selangor following the Sultan’s statements — is not coincidental. Malaysia Hindu Sangam identified 687 unauthorised Hindu temples in Selangor alone. The question isn’t whether they’re authorised — it’s whether the government’s response is to demolish community heritage or find solutions.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

PH promised to be different. To protect minorities. To uphold rule of law. Instead, under a PH state government:

  • A temple was demolished without a court order
  • It happened at midnight — the time you choose when you don’t want witnesses
  • Police assisted rather than prevented it
  • The arrested men were released by the High Court, suggesting the lower court’s remand was improper If this happened under BN, DAP would have called it ethnic cleansing. Under PH, it’s a ’land dispute.'

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Temple demolitions have a long and painful history in Malaysia. The Seafield Sri Maha Mariamman temple riot of 2018 occurred during PH 1.0’s watch. The pattern repeats: minority religious sites are treated as disposable when they stand on land someone else wants. The only thing that changes is which coalition looks away.

Controversial Muslim preacher Zamri Vinoth has been targeting Hindu temples with inflammatory rhetoric, seemingly with impunity. Malaysiakini columnist Andrew Sia asked: “Why do agitators like Zamri Vinoth seem to have immunity while insulting Hindu temples?”

🎯 PH CALLOUT

Anwar Ibrahim himself acknowledged the sensitivity: “I know this will invite controversy. Some people have gone to extremes, even launching campaigns to demolish temples.” He warned against vigilantism. Then a temple was demolished at midnight in his coalition’s state. Your warning means nothing if your own state government cannot prevent midnight demolitions without court orders. The Indian community voted for you because they believed you would protect them. This is what that protection looks like?
