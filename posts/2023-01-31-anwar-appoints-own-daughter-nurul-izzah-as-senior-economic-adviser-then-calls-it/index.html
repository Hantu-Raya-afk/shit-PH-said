Anwar Appoints Own Daughter Nurul Izzah as Senior Economic Adviser — Then Calls It Not Nepotism

Hantu Raya

January 31, 2023

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-01-31

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 31 January 2023 — two months into his premiership — Anwar Ibrahim appointed his daughter Nurul Izzah Anwar as Senior Adviser on Economics and Finance in the Prime Minister’s Department. This came after Nurul Izzah lost her Permatang Pauh parliamentary seat in GE15 to Perikatan Nasional by nearly 6,000 votes — a seat that was a traditional PKR stronghold. The appointment was unpaid, which Anwar used as his primary defence. He told reporters: “Nepotism is where a family member is given a position to abuse power, enrich themselves, obtain contracts and get paid a huge sum. This is not the case.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Nepotism is not defined by salary — it is defined by access. An unpaid adviser to the Prime Minister has more institutional influence than most paid civil servants. The role grants proximity to policy, contracts, and decision-making at the highest level. Appointing a candidate who was just rejected by voters to an unelected advisory role is a slap in the face of democratic outcomes. If the PM can appoint family members to unelected advisory roles and call it acceptable, then every future PM can do the same. The precedent is set.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar spent 25 years branding himself as the antidote to Malaysia’s entrenched patronage politics. Two months into office, he appointed his own daughter. The defence — “it’s unpaid, therefore not nepotism” — is a textbook deflection. Was there an open call? A shortlist? A vetting committee? No. She was appointed because she is the PM’s daughter, and every Malaysian knows it. Nurul Izzah holds degrees from Johns Hopkins, but qualification is not the issue — process is. Mahathir appointed family members to business positions. Najib’s circle enriched relatives through 1MDB-adjacent deals. Anwar promised to be different. He wasn’t.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Political dynasties are endemic in Malaysian politics. Mahathir’s sons held business empires built on political connections. Najib’s stepson Riza Aziz was charged in the 1MDB scandal. UMNO’s patronage networks enriched families for decades. PH criticised all of this relentlessly. Anwar’s appointment of Nurul Izzah — within weeks of taking office — signalled that the reformasi playbook had an asterisk: does not apply to the PM’s family. Nurul Izzah has since been installed as PKR deputy president, consolidating the Anwar dynasty within the party.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You spent 25 years preaching against patronage. You appointed your daughter within 60 days. She lost her seat and you gave her an advisory role. She’s now PKR deputy president. This is not meritocracy — it’s dynasty-building with better PR. All PM advisory appointments should go through a transparent vetting process. Family members of the sitting PM should be automatically disqualified from unelected roles in the PMO. Acknowledge the optics instead of redefining nepotism to suit your situation.
