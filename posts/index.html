Anwar Preaches 'Integrity Culture' and Launches CeIO Programme — While His Government Shields MACC Chief and Interrogates Anti-Corruption NGOs

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

On March 12, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim chaired the Special Cabinet Committee on National Governance (JKKTN) and announced the launch of a Certified Integrity Officer (CeIO) programme. In a Facebook post and subsequent Bernama coverage, Anwar declared that “law enforcement alone is insufficient to combat corruption” and called for “a broader culture of integrity and sound governance practices.” He said issues like “leakage of public funds and abuse of power” undermine governance, public health, the environment, and national integrity. The committee agreed to implement CeIO as part of efforts to “strengthen national governance.”

🔥 Why It Matters

This statement lands in the same week that Anwar’s government delegated the Azam Baki shareholding report to the Chief Secretary instead of acting on it, refused to disclose the report’s findings, rejected calls for a Royal Commission of Inquiry into MACC, and allowed police to interrogate C4 Centre’s anti-corruption CEO under Section 124B — a law meant for threats to parliamentary democracy. The gap between Anwar’s rhetoric and his government’s actions on anti-corruption is no longer a gap. It is a canyon. Every new “integrity” initiative announced while the MACC chief remains in post and civil society gets hauled to Bukit Aman makes the rhetoric sound less like conviction and more like performance.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

You do not need a Certified Integrity Officer programme when the head of your anti-corruption commission is accused of breaching shareholding laws and your own coalition partners are publicly demanding an independent inquiry you refuse to grant. Integrity is not a certificate. It is not a programme. It is what you do when the political cost is high — and Anwar has consistently chosen the lowest-cost option on every accountability question put before him. The CeIO programme is bureaucratic window dressing: another acronym, another committee, another layer of process designed to look like action while the actual crisis — an MACC chief credibly accused of misconduct, still drawing a government salary — remains untouched.

📜 Previous Cases

This is not the first time Anwar has deployed reform language as a substitute for reform action. On March 9, he launched the Capital Market Masterplan and declared reform “non-negotiable” — the same week the PM tenure limit bill failed because his own MPs didn’t show up. On March 6, he told Bersih to stop criticising MACC “during Ramadan,” using the holy month as a shield. On March 3, he told Parliament about a “plot to topple the government” — a narrative now being used to justify police interrogation of anti-corruption activists. The pattern is unmistakable: announce a reform initiative, deflect criticism with moral language, and let the underlying problem persist.

🎯 PH Callout

You ran on Reformasi. You promised Malaysians that your government would be different — that integrity would be a practice, not a slogan. Three years in, your anti-corruption chief is credibly accused of misconduct, your police are questioning anti-corruption NGOs under sedition-era laws, and your response is a Facebook post about “integrity culture” and a new certification programme. Malaysians do not need more certificates. They need a Prime Minister who will suspend an MACC chief under investigation, grant the RCI his own coalition demands, and stop using process as a substitute for accountability. Every day Azam Baki remains in post is a day your integrity talk means nothing.

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

He celebrated 2025’s GDP growth of 5.2%, unemployment at a decade-low 2.9%, and inflation moderating to 1.4% — declaring that “the government’s continuous efforts in strengthening the economy and improving the well-being of the people are beginning to show positive results.” He acknowledged West Asia conflicts “could disrupt the stability of global energy supply and international trade” but insisted the growth target stands.

🔥 Why It Matters

This statement was made on the same day the Ministry of Finance announced the sharpest weekly fuel price hike in years: RON97 up 60 sen to RM3.85 (18%), unsubsidised RON95 up 60 sen to RM3.27, and diesel up 80 sen to RM3.92 (26%). The Strait of Hormuz is disrupted, oil is past $100 a barrel, and the government’s own special committee is scrambling to assess economic fallout — yet the Economy Minister’s response is a launch event for a monitoring dashboard and a celebration of last year’s numbers. Now the crisis has escalated dramatically, and the best the Economy Minister can offer is backward-looking GDP figures and a new monitoring system. 📜 Previous Cases

Now the government’s position has gone from “we’ll upgrade” to “we’ll hold” to “we’ll monitor” — each step a retreat disguised as prudence.

🎯 PH Callout

Ramanan Says Government 'Studying' WFH for Public Sector — Thailand Acted Immediately, Malaysia Still Forming a Study

Said by: R. Ramanan, Human Resources Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

On March 12, 2026, Human Resources Minister Ramanan Ramakrishnan told reporters in Putrajaya that possible cost-saving measures — including work-from-home for public sector employees and the private sector — are being “studied” and will be presented at a special Cabinet meeting on Friday, March 14. “We have seen what Thailand has done, and we are currently conducting studies on this,” Ramanan said. He added that he would receive a briefing from deputy secretaries-general and then present findings to Cabinet. He acknowledged the prolonged Middle East conflict and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz as drivers of the crisis.

🔥 Why It Matters

Thailand’s Cabinet approved mandatory work-from-home for government officials immediately upon the escalation of the energy crisis. Malaysia’s government — facing the same crisis, with RON97 up 18%, diesel up 26%, and oil past $100 a barrel — is still “conducting studies.” The contrast is damning. Every day Malaysian workers commute to offices they could work from remotely is a day the government is forcing the rakyat to absorb fuel costs that could be avoided with a single directive. Ramanan’s own framing gives the game away: “If we ourselves do not practise savings but ask the people to do so, that would not be fair.” Correct. So why are you still studying instead of acting?

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The COVID-19 pandemic proved — three years ago — that Malaysian public servants and private sector workers can work from home effectively. The infrastructure exists. The precedent exists. The legal framework for flexible work arrangements already exists under the amended Employment Act. There is nothing left to “study.” What Ramanan is really saying is that the government does not want to issue a directive without political cover from a Cabinet meeting that hasn’t happened yet. This is not governance. This is a bureaucracy waiting for permission to do the obvious. Thailand acted in hours. Malaysia is taking days — and counting.

📜 Previous Cases

On March 11, this database logged an entry noting that Anwar’s crisis response to $100+ oil included solidarity tweets and “monitoring” — but no WFH mandate, no toll waiver, no food price caps. Now, one day later, the government has graduated from “monitoring” to “studying.” During the ASEAN Summit in May 2025, Fahmi Fadzil spent weeks saying the Cabinet was “deliberating” WFH before eventually implementing it. The pattern is consistent: Malaysia’s government treats every decision as requiring maximum process and minimum urgency, even when the cost of delay is borne entirely by ordinary workers.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would lead by example. Ramanan said it himself: it would be “unfair” to ask the people to save while the government doesn’t. So act. Issue the directive today. Thailand didn’t need a Friday Cabinet meeting to decide that government workers should stop burning expensive fuel to sit in offices with perfectly functional internet connections. Every day you “study” is a day you’re choosing process over people. The rakyat cannot afford your deliberation speed.

RON97 Up 18%, Diesel Up 26% — Government Lets Unsubsidised Fuel Prices Explode While Claiming It's 'Cushioning the Rakyat'

Said by: Ministry of Finance / Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

The Ministry of Finance announced on March 11 that effective March 12-18, RON97 petrol will surge 60 sen per litre to RM3.85 — an 18% increase from RM3.25. Unsubsidised RON95 jumps 60 sen to RM3.27 — a 22% spike. Diesel in Peninsular Malaysia leaps 80 sen to RM3.92 — a staggering 26% increase. The government maintained the Budi95 subsidised rate at RM1.99 for eligible motorists and offered a RM100 top-up for diesel vehicle owners under existing schemes, raising interim cash assistance from RM200 to RM300 for March. The MOF said Malaysia’s prices “remain lower than neighbouring countries” and promised “strengthened enforcement” against fuel leakage.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the sharpest single-week fuel price adjustment since the Middle East crisis escalated. Diesel at RM3.92 directly hits logistics, haulage, and food supply chains — costs that will be passed to consumers within days. The two-tier fuel system is now stretched to breaking point: subsidised RON95 sits at RM1.99 while unsubsidised RON95 is at RM3.27, a 64% gap that incentivises smuggling and gaming. The government’s entire fuel strategy is now a subsidy wall protecting one product while everything else burns. Businesses that run on diesel — fishermen, farmers, truckers, food manufacturers — are absorbing a 26% cost spike with no price controls downstream. This will feed directly into food inflation during Ramadan, when household spending is already elevated.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The government’s framing is pure theatre. “The government will continue to bear part of the subsidy to cushion the impact” — they are subsidising one fuel grade and letting everything else rip. The RM100 diesel top-up is an insult: if you’re a lorry operator burning hundreds of litres a week, RM300 a month covers roughly one fill-up at the new price. Meanwhile, the MOF’s line about prices being “lower than neighbouring countries” is the oldest deflection in the subsidy playbook — it tells you nothing about whether Malaysian wages can absorb these costs. Anwar promised fuel subsidy rationalisation that would be “fair and gradual.” A 26% diesel spike in one week is neither fair nor gradual. It’s crisis management dressed up as policy.

📜 Previous Cases

This government has been kicking the fuel subsidy can since taking office. The targeted subsidy for RON95 (Budi95) was meant to replace blanket subsidies, but diesel rationalisation has been stop-start since June 2024 when diesel subsidies were first restructured. Every time global prices spike, the government scrambles with ad hoc cash handouts instead of structural reform. Just last week, Anwar admitted he could only hold the RON95 subsidy for “one or two months” — and now the prices around it are exploding. The pattern is clear: announce reform, delay implementation, throw cash when crisis hits, repeat.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would rationalise subsidies fairly. You told them the economy was strong and well-managed. You told them you had a plan. An 18% spike in RON97 and a 26% spike in diesel in a single week is not a plan — it’s a surrender to market forces with a press release attached. The rakyat who drive RON97 or run diesel-dependent businesses are not “cushioned” — they are exposed. And the fishermen, farmers, and truckers who feed this country are now subsidising your political survival with their margins. If the Budi95 wall cracks — and at these global prices, it will — you have no backup plan. Just more press statements about how prices are lower than Singapore.

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced that all government agencies, GLCs, and GLICs are banned from holding Aidilfitri open house celebrations this year — a decision agreed upon in Cabinet that morning. He framed it as a signal of fiscal discipline amid global uncertainty caused by the Middle East conflict. In the same breath, Anwar also announced tighter restrictions on overseas travel for ministers and government officials, saying only pre-scheduled and essential foreign engagements would be approved. A special Cabinet meeting has been called for March 13 to assess the fiscal situation.

🔥 Why It Matters

This austerity performance arrives just two days after the government announced it would maintain the RON95 subsidy at RM1.99 — and less than a week after Anwar brought forward RM15 billion in Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah (STR) cash handouts plus RM500 Special Financial Assistance for civil servants and mosque personnel, all conveniently timed before Hari Raya. The government is spending record sums on direct cash transfers — the most electorally potent form of spending — while cancelling community open houses that cost a fraction of that. This is not fiscal discipline. This is selective austerity: cut the visible, communal celebrations while flooding individual bank accounts with cash right before a rumoured snap election.

Open houses are community events — they cost millions. The STR programme costs billions. If the Middle East crisis is so dire that civil servants can’t eat ketupat at a government function, why is it not dire enough to pause the RM15 billion in pre-election cash? Because open houses don’t buy votes. Cash in bank accounts before polling day does. The travel restriction is window dressing — ’except for those already scheduled’ is a loophole wide enough to fly the entire Cabinet through.

📜 Previous Cases

This is textbook Malaysian crisis theatre. Mahathir did it during the 1997 Asian financial crisis — austerity for the rakyat, bailouts for cronies. Najib talked fiscal discipline while 1MDB bled billions. Anwar himself mocked BN’s festive-season handouts when he was in opposition, calling them vote-buying. Now he’s doing the same thing, but bigger: record STR payouts, record BKK bonuses, and a Raya ban for optics. The pattern is always the same — cut the things that don’t win elections, spend lavishly on the things that do.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi, not repackaged BN-era electoral spending with a piety overlay. If you’re genuinely worried about the fiscal position, start with the RM15 billion in cash transfers you just announced, not the nasi lemak at government open houses. Cancel the political appointments bleeding FSBs dry. Freeze the patronage spending. Don’t lecture the rakyat about moderation while your government’s chequebook is wide open for every vote bank that matters before GE16. This is not discipline — this is performance.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Prime Minister of an energy-importing nation telling the public not to worry about supply — while the Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly a fifth of the world’s oil transits, is under active military disruption. 💩 Why It’s Bullshit

“Secure until May” is not reassurance — it’s a confession dressed up as comfort. A daily economic impact review panel. A smuggling task force. None of this addresses what happens in June if the Strait stays contested, if Petronas hedges unwind, or if global allocation priorities shift away from Southeast Asia. The press conference was a performance of control, not a demonstration of it.

📜 Previous Cases

The “we checked with Petronas” line echoes previous administrations — Najib’s government used similar assurances during the 2014-2015 oil price crash while raiding Petronas dividends to plug fiscal holes. The playbook is always the same: invoke Petronas, announce a committee, and hope the crisis passes before the next press conference.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysians a government that tells the truth, even when it’s hard. So tell the truth: two months of supply is not security — it’s a ticking clock. Where is the contingency plan for June? Where is the strategic petroleum reserve policy you never built? Where is the long-term energy diversification strategy beyond “Petronas says we’re fine”? You banned Raya open houses for “fiscal discipline” the same week you announced RM15 billion in cash handouts. You formed three new committees in one press conference. Committees don’t produce oil. If the Strait stays shut and supply chains fracture, “we checked with Petronas” will not keep the lights on or the lorries moving. Stop performing calm and start building resilience.

Cabinet’s decision: hand the matter to Chief Secretary to the Government Tan Sri Shamsul Azri Abu Bakar to determine “next steps.” Fahmi refused to disclose the report’s contents. When asked directly whether Cabinet discussed the extension of Azam’s contract, Fahmi said: “It was not discussed.” Separately, Cabinet instructed the police, Securities Commission, MACC itself, and the Inland Revenue Board to continue investigating Bloomberg’s “corporate mafia” allegations — including seizing proceeds believed to have criminal elements.

And after all of that, Cabinet’s big move is to delegate to a civil servant and refuse to tell the public what the AG’s committee actually found. The message is clear: accountability will be managed, not delivered. By routing the decision through the Chief Secretary rather than making a political call, Anwar insulates himself from direct responsibility for whatever happens next — whether Azam stays, goes, or quietly expires in May.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The Cabinet is the highest executive decision-making body in the country. It received the report. It discussed the findings. And then it said: not our call. Meanwhile, the instruction to probe “corporate mafia” allegations sounds tough until you remember MACC is being asked to investigate… itself. The fox is auditing the henhouse, and Cabinet just handed it the clipboard.

📜 Previous Cases

Anwar, upon taking power, renewed Azam’s contract multiple times while promising reform. In February 2026, Bloomberg published detailed allegations of a “corporate mafia” operating within MACC. Anwar’s response has followed a consistent pattern: acknowledge the problem, set up a committee, delay action, and let the news cycle move on. The Straits Times reported on March 9 that Anwar had privately told Cabinet he would not renew Azam’s contract when it expires May 12 — but still refused to suspend him in the meantime.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia “institutional reform” and “zero tolerance for corruption.” Your own manifesto said MACC would be made independent and accountable. Instead, your Cabinet received a report on the most compromised anti-corruption chief in Malaysian history and responded with: “Ask the Chief Secretary.” That is not reform. That is not accountability. That is not even governance. It is cowardice dressed in procedure. If you cannot fire the man running your anti-corruption agency while he is under investigation for the very corruption he is supposed to fight, then every word of your reform agenda is decoration. Act, or admit you never intended to.

🔥 Why It Matters

Anwar promised in December 2025 that the project would proceed “with safeguards for Orang Asli and the environment,” but no independent environmental review or community consent mechanism has been made public. The approval comes amid a Middle East crisis, fiscal austerity theatre (banning Raya open houses), and pre-election positioning — making the timing politically convenient for announcing a mega-development that benefits Selangor’s PH-aligned state machinery.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH ran on transparency, open tenders, and ending GLC capture of public assets. What we got instead is a 5,000-acre megaproject carved up between a PNB-linked plantation giant and the Selangor state GLC before the public even saw a development plan. The Selangor MB’s office admitted the land allocation was “decided a few weeks ago” — before Cabinet even approved the project. That’s not governance; that’s pre-positioning. And the Orang Asli? They get “safeguards” in speeches and displacement in practice. This is exactly the kind of opaque, GLC-driven megaproject that PH promised to end when it was in opposition.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s infrastructure megaproject history is littered with cost overruns, opaque procurement, and GLC enrichment. The East Coast Rail Link (ECRL) was renegotiated but still lacks full transparency. The Penang Transport Master Plan has faced years of environmental and governance controversy. Port Klang itself was the site of the Port Klang Free Zone (PKFZ) scandal — a RM12.5 billion fiasco involving inflated land costs, political patronage, and criminal charges. PH’s own 2018-2020 government inherited that mess and promised never to repeat it. Now they’re greenlighting another massive port project on the same stretch of coast, with pre-allocated GLC land deals and no visible competitive process.

🎯 PH Callout

You told voters you’d end the cosy GLC-state nexus that turns public land into private profit. You said open tenders and institutional reform would replace backroom deals. So where is the open tender for Port Klang Terminal 3? Where is the independent environmental impact assessment? Where is the Orang Asli consultation? A 5,000-acre development involving SD Guthrie, PKNS, and Yayasan Selangor land — all allocated before Cabinet even voted — is not reform. It’s the same game with a Madani label. If you can ban Raya open houses for “fiscal discipline,” you can publish the full procurement framework for a multi-billion ringgit port before you approve it.

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil announced on March 11 that a special committee, led by Petronas chairman Amir Hamzah and including Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir, will evaluate the economic impact of the US-Iran conflict on Malaysia ‘on a daily basis.’ The committee’s findings will be presented at a special Cabinet meeting scheduled for Friday, March 13. Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim said Malaysia’s petroleum supply is ‘sufficient at least until May 2026,’ even as global oil markets reel from the Strait of Hormuz disruption and retaliatory strikes between Iran, the US, and Israel that began on February 28.

🔥 Why It Matters

The government’s instinctive response to a crisis that is already hitting Malaysian wallets — RON97 up 60 sen, diesel up 80 sen, food inflation incoming — is to form a committee to ‘assess’ the damage. Not to act. Not to freeze tolls, cap food prices, mandate work-from-home, or cut wasteful political spending. To study. This is the Madani crisis management playbook distilled to its essence: announce a committee, schedule a meeting, promise a report, and tell the rakyat to wait. The committee doesn’t even meet until March 12, two full weeks after the conflict began. Fuel prices have already spiked. Supply chains are already disrupted. A Thai cargo ship was attacked near the Strait of Hormuz. The time for ‘assessment’ was February 28. The time for action was last week.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

This is not governance. This is bureaucratic theatre dressed up as crisis management. The committee is led by the Petronas chairman — the same entity that benefits from higher oil prices — and includes the Economy Minister, who was publicly projecting 4-4.5% growth just days ago as if the region wasn’t on fire. Fahmi’s announcement came on the same day as a cascade of other Cabinet decisions: banning Raya open houses for ‘fiscal discipline’ while approving RM15 billion in cash handouts, greenlighting a 5,000-acre GLC megaproject, and announcing RM500 bonuses for civil servants and mosque staff. The committee is the government’s way of looking responsive without being responsive. It is a press release pretending to be policy.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long and inglorious history of governance-by-committee. Anwar’s administration has appointed special committees for diesel smuggling, MACC reform, the Azam Baki shareholding issue, and now the economic impact of a war. The pattern is always the same: announce the committee, generate a headline, then quietly let the findings gather dust or punt the decision to someone else — as happened this same week when the Azam Baki committee report was handed to the Chief Secretary with no public disclosure. The committee reflex is not unique to PH, but PH was supposed to be different. They promised responsive, transparent, and accountable governance. Instead they’ve perfected the art of the bureaucratic stall.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised a government that acts, not one that studies the problem while the rakyat burns through their savings at the pump. Two weeks into the worst geopolitical energy crisis since the Gulf War, your response is a committee that hasn’t met yet, a report that hasn’t been written, and a special Cabinet meeting that hasn’t happened. The rakyat doesn’t need a committee to tell them diesel is more expensive. They need a government that acts like it understands urgency. Every day you ‘assess,’ someone skips a meal, absorbs a delivery surcharge, or watches their margins collapse. Stop studying the fire. Put it out.
