Police Threaten Bersih Rally Participants — PH Government Uses Same Tactics It Once Condemned

Hantu Raya

February 26, 2024

Said by: Bersih / Lawyers for Liberty

Category: Social

Date: 2024-02-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In February 2024, the PH government sent police warnings that Bersih’s planned demonstration was illegal. Officers visited participants’ homes and issued warnings ahead of the rally. Lawyers for Liberty (LFL) condemned it as “a betrayal by PH,” citing the government’s use of the same intimidation tactics PH had once fought against. CSIS noted that civil society groups are “torn between their historical alliance with PH and the government’s failure to implement its reform agenda.” Bersih — the electoral reform coalition that was PH’s closest ally during the BN era — was now being treated as a threat.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Bersih’s rallies were the oxygen of PH’s rise to power. The Bersih 2.0 through 5.0 rallies built the mass movement that eventually toppled BN. PH leaders didn’t just support these rallies — they marched in them, were tear-gassed at them, and used them to build their democratic credentials. Now the same government is sending police to people’s homes to warn them against attending a Bersih rally. The message is unmistakable: freedom of assembly is only acceptable when it serves PH’s interests.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: LFL’s condemnation specifically cited a 2014 Court of Appeal ruling — from PKR’s own Nik Nazmi’s case — that struck down restrictive provisions of the Peaceful Assembly Act. PH’s own legal victory against protest suppression is now being ignored by PH’s own government. Nik Nazmi, who was literally charged under the PAA for organising a protest in 2013, was a PH minister when police threatened Bersih participants. The hypocrisy is not subtle — it is the defining feature of PH’s approach to civil liberties: demand them in opposition, suppress them in government.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, police intimidation of rally participants was standard practice. Home visits, phone calls, and social media monitoring were used to discourage attendance at Bersih rallies. PH condemned these tactics as authoritarian. Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International, and every Malaysian civil liberties organisation documented BN’s suppression of assembly rights. Now the same organisations are documenting the same tactics under PH — and the same civil society groups that once marched with PH are being targeted by PH.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You marched with Bersih. You were tear-gassed with Bersih. You built your movement on the right to peaceful assembly. Now you’re sending police to people’s homes to warn them against attending a Bersih rally. Lawyers for Liberty calls it a betrayal. CSIS says civil society is torn. The rakyat who marched for you are now being warned against marching for accountability. Guarantee the right to peaceful assembly without police intimidation. Or admit that freedom of assembly was only useful when you were the ones assembling.
