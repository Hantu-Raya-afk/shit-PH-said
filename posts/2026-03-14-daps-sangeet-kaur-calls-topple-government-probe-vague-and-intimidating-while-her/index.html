DAP's Sangeet Kaur Calls Topple-Government Probe 'Vague' and 'Intimidating' — While Her Own Coalition Built the Narrative

Hantu Raya

March 14, 2026

Said by: Sangeet Kaur Deo, DAP Women’s Wing Legal Bureau Chief

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-14

📌 What Happened

On March 14, 2026, DAP women’s wing legal bureau chief Sangeet Kaur Deo issued a statement criticising the police investigation into the alleged plot to overthrow the government. She called the probe based on ‘vague allegations’ and warned that ‘sweeping calls for interrogation inevitably create an atmosphere of intimidation and raise concerns about the potential misuse of investigative powers.’ She stressed that ‘state machinery must never be used to silence critics’ and said that if genuine crimes exist, authorities should identify them clearly and state the specific conduct said to constitute those offences. This comes after commentators and civil society figures were summoned for questioning in connection with the probe.

Note: This entry is based on a single source (FMT). Cross-referencing found FMT social media reposts but no independent second outlet at time of publication.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is a rare case of a PH figure publicly pushing back against the government’s most politically charged narrative. Since Anwar told Parliament on March 3 about the alleged plot — complete with claims about international PR firms, ‘prominent Zionist groups,’ and foreign media coordination — the government line has been to treat this as a serious national security matter. Police have summoned commentators and civil society figures. The Daim family has sued. And now a DAP legal voice is saying, in public, that the probe looks like intimidation dressed up as law enforcement. That is a significant internal crack in the coalition’s messaging discipline.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Here is the problem: Sangeet Kaur is right about the vagueness, but DAP’s leadership has been conspicuously silent while the probe escalated. Anthony Loke called the allegations ’extremely serious.’ Fahmi Fadzil rejected calls for an RCI. The government’s own communications machinery amplified the ’topple government’ narrative without naming suspects or specifying offences — exactly the vagueness Sangeet Kaur is now criticising. So the question is: where was this concern when the PM stood in Parliament and made sweeping, unsubstantiated claims? Where was it when C4’s CEO was questioned under Section 124B? A backbench critique after the damage is done is not courage — it is a press release.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has been here before. Under BN, ’threats to national security’ were the go-to justification for silencing critics — the Sedition Act sweeps of 2014-2015, the crackdowns on Bersih, the detention of activists under SOSMA. PH, in opposition, condemned all of it. They called it authoritarianism. They promised reform. Now in government, PH has used Section 124B (activities detrimental to parliamentary democracy) against civil society, summoned journalists and commentators, and built an entire parliamentary narrative around an unnamed suspect and an unnamed PR firm. The tools have changed. The playbook has not.

🎯 PH Callout

You built your coalition on the promise that Malaysia would never again see state power weaponised against dissent. Your own people are now telling you — in public — that the topple-government probe looks like exactly that. Listen to Sangeet Kaur. If the plot is real, name the suspects, specify the offences, and let the courts do their work. If it is not, stop using vague allegations to summon critics and cow civil society. You cannot claim to be a reformist government while running BN’s intimidation playbook with better PR. The rakyat did not vote for new management of the same old machine.
