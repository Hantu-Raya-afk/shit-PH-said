Zulkifli Hasan Says Work Stress Can Make People Gay — International Community Laughs, Malaysia Cringes

Hantu Raya

January 26, 2026

Said by: Zulkifli Hasan, Minister in PM’s Department (Religious Affairs)

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On January 26, 2026, Religious Affairs Minister Zulkifli Hasan submitted a written parliamentary reply to PAS MP Siti Zailah Mohd Yusoff about LGBT trends in Malaysia. He cited a 2017 study and listed “societal influence, sexual experiences, work stress, and other personal factors” as possible causes of “LGBT behaviour.” The “work stress” claim went immediately viral — globally. CNA, SCMP, NDTV, Reuters, Hindustan Times, LGBTQ Nation, and them.us all covered it. Malaysians flooded social media with mockery, sarcastically asking whether parliament MPs had “turned gay” since they apparently never work hard enough to experience stress. Zulkifli also disclosed that from 2022 to 2025, 135 cases related to “LGBT activities” were recorded as arrests or prosecutions — in a country where same-sex relations remain criminalised.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This isn’t just embarrassing — it’s damaging. A cabinet minister, speaking in an official parliamentary capacity, cited debunked pseudoscience to explain sexual orientation. This is the government’s official position on record in Hansard. The international coverage turned Malaysia into a global punchline while the country tries to attract foreign investment, host international summits, and position itself as a progressive Muslim-majority democracy. More importantly, the remark normalises the framing of LGBT identity as a pathology — something caused by external factors that can presumably be “fixed.” This framing underpins the continued criminalisation of same-sex relations and the use of state-funded “rehabilitation” programmes.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The 2017 study Zulkifli cited — Sulaiman et al. — is a small-scale Malaysian social study, not a peer-reviewed finding from any recognised international medical or psychological body. Every major medical and psychological organisation in the world — the WHO, APA, AMA, Royal College of Psychiatrists — has stated clearly that sexual orientation is not caused by stress, lifestyle, or environmental factors. The minister later issued a defensive statement saying people should “refer to the full written answer” — as if the problem was context. It wasn’t. The context makes it worse: it was an official, considered, pre-written reply. This wasn’t a slip of the tongue. It was deliberate.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a long, inglorious history of ministers making scientifically illiterate statements about LGBT people. In 2018, then-Deputy PM Wan Azizah said the government would not recognise LGBT rights because they were “against the law of nature.” In 2019, Religious Affairs officials promoted “conversion therapy” camps. Malaysia’s Federal Territories Islamic Department (JAWI) regularly raids private premises suspected of hosting LGBT individuals. PH in opposition criticised BN’s persecution of LGBT Malaysians; in government, PH has continued the same policies and rhetoric. Zulkifli Hasan’s appointment itself was a political concession — he represents the Malay-Muslim establishment within PH’s coalition.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You campaigned on “Malaysia Madani” — a civilised, progressive Malaysia. Your Religious Affairs Minister just told parliament that work stress turns people gay. The world laughed. Malaysia’s reputation took yet another hit. Where was Anwar? Where was the PMO correction? Where was the cabinet disavowal? Silence. Because correcting Zulkifli would upset the conservative base PH desperately needs. So the government let a pseudoscientific claim stand as Malaysia’s official parliamentary position on sexual orientation. Madani means civilised. There is nothing civilised about citing junk science in parliament to justify the criminalisation of who people love.
