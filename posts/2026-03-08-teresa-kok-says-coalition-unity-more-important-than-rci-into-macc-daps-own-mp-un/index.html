Teresa Kok Says Coalition Unity More Important Than RCI Into MACC — DAP's Own MP Undermines the Accountability Push

Hantu Raya

March 8, 2026

Said by: Teresa Kok, MP for Seputeh (DAP)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-08

📌 What Happened

On March 8, DAP’s Seputeh MP Teresa Kok publicly stated that maintaining unity within the Madani coalition government is more important than her own party’s push for a Royal Commission of Inquiry (RCI) into the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC). This came just days after DAP secretary-general Anthony Loke told Malaysiakini the government’s response to MACC misconduct allegations had been “inadequate” and called for an independent inquiry — a position endorsed by DAP’s national leadership and reinforced by the party’s decision to bring forward its special congress to July 2026.

🔥 Why It Matters

DAP is the single largest party in Anwar’s ruling coalition with 40 MPs, and its entire political identity rests on reform, accountability, and anti-corruption. When the party’s own backbenchers publicly break with the leadership to prioritize coalition survival over institutional accountability, it signals that the internal pressure campaign is already fracturing. Teresa Kok’s statement gives Anwar political cover to continue ignoring calls for an RCI — he can point to voices within DAP itself saying unity matters more. It weakens DAP’s negotiating position at the exact moment the party is supposed to be leveraging the July congress as a credible threat.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

This is the oldest trick in the coalition playbook: reframe the demand for accountability as a threat to stability. Teresa Kok is effectively saying the government should not be held to its own standards because doing so might rock the boat. But DAP didn’t enter government to keep the boat steady — it entered to fix the engine. The MACC under Azam Baki has been credibly accused by Bloomberg of running a “corporate mafia,” breaching civil service shareholding rules multiple times, and weaponizing investigations against political targets. If an RCI into that is too destabilizing for the coalition, then the coalition has already failed its stated purpose. Unity without accountability is just complicity with better PR.

📜 Previous Cases

This is not the first time a DAP MP has publicly undercut the party’s reform stance to protect coalition harmony. The pattern runs deep: DAP stayed silent for months on Azam Baki’s first shareholding scandal in 2021-2022 when it was in opposition, then renewed his contract three times after entering government. The party’s leadership accepted the government’s internal task force instead of pushing harder for an RCI when Bloomberg’s “corporate mafia” exposé dropped in February. And in the Sabah state election last November, DAP was wiped out — winning zero seats — partly because voters saw a party that had traded its principles for Cabinet positions.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysian voters you were the reform coalition. You told the Chinese community you were the only credible check on corruption and cronyism. You told civil society that institutional accountability was non-negotiable. And now, when your own party demands a basic accountability mechanism — an RCI into an anti-corruption agency accused of being a corporate mafia — your own MPs say unity is more important. If DAP cannot hold its own line on the MACC, what exactly is the party for? The July congress is supposed to be a reckoning. If this is the signal DAP’s backbench is sending — that keeping ministerial seats matters more than keeping promises — then voters already have their answer.
