Fahmi Announces Special Committee to 'Assess' Economic Impact of US-Iran Conflict — Government's Crisis Playbook Is Another Committee

Hantu Raya

March 11, 2026

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil announced on March 11 that a special committee, led by Petronas chairman Amir Hamzah and including Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir, will evaluate the economic impact of the US-Iran conflict on Malaysia ‘on a daily basis.’ The committee’s findings will be presented at a special Cabinet meeting scheduled for Friday, March 13. Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim said Malaysia’s petroleum supply is ‘sufficient at least until May 2026,’ even as global oil markets reel from the Strait of Hormuz disruption and retaliatory strikes between Iran, the US, and Israel that began on February 28.

🔥 Why It Matters

The government’s instinctive response to a crisis that is already hitting Malaysian wallets — RON97 up 60 sen, diesel up 80 sen, food inflation incoming — is to form a committee to ‘assess’ the damage. Not to act. Not to freeze tolls, cap food prices, mandate work-from-home, or cut wasteful political spending. To study. This is the Madani crisis management playbook distilled to its essence: announce a committee, schedule a meeting, promise a report, and tell the rakyat to wait. The committee doesn’t even meet until March 12, two full weeks after the conflict began. Fuel prices have already spiked. Supply chains are already disrupted. A Thai cargo ship was attacked near the Strait of Hormuz. The time for ‘assessment’ was February 28. The time for action was last week.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

This is not governance. This is bureaucratic theatre dressed up as crisis management. The committee is led by the Petronas chairman — the same entity that benefits from higher oil prices — and includes the Economy Minister, who was publicly projecting 4-4.5% growth just days ago as if the region wasn’t on fire. Fahmi’s announcement came on the same day as a cascade of other Cabinet decisions: banning Raya open houses for ‘fiscal discipline’ while approving RM15 billion in cash handouts, greenlighting a 5,000-acre GLC megaproject, and announcing RM500 bonuses for civil servants and mosque staff. The committee is the government’s way of looking responsive without being responsive. It is a press release pretending to be policy.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long and inglorious history of governance-by-committee. Anwar’s administration has appointed special committees for diesel smuggling, MACC reform, the Azam Baki shareholding issue, and now the economic impact of a war. The pattern is always the same: announce the committee, generate a headline, then quietly let the findings gather dust or punt the decision to someone else — as happened this same week when the Azam Baki committee report was handed to the Chief Secretary with no public disclosure. The committee reflex is not unique to PH, but PH was supposed to be different. They promised responsive, transparent, and accountable governance. Instead they’ve perfected the art of the bureaucratic stall.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised a government that acts, not one that studies the problem while the rakyat burns through their savings at the pump. Two weeks into the worst geopolitical energy crisis since the Gulf War, your response is a committee that hasn’t met yet, a report that hasn’t been written, and a special Cabinet meeting that hasn’t happened. The rakyat doesn’t need a committee to tell them diesel is more expensive. They need a government that acts like it understands urgency. Every day you ‘assess,’ someone skips a meal, absorbs a delivery surcharge, or watches their margins collapse. Stop studying the fire. Put it out.
