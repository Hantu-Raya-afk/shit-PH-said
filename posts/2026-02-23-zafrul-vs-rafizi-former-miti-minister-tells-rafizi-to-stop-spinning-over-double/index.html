Zafrul vs Rafizi — Former MITI Minister Tells Rafizi to 'Stop Spinning' Over Double Tariff Claims

Hantu Raya

February 23, 2026

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, MIDA Chairman / former MITI Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-02-23

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 23 February 2026, Tengku Zafrul — now MIDA chairman — publicly rebuked former Economy Minister Rafizi Ramli for claiming Malaysia faced nearly double the tariff rate under the US trade deal. Zafrul accused Rafizi of ‘distorting the facts’ and told him to ‘stop spinning — it’s exhausting to keep responding.’ Rafizi had argued the ART locked Malaysia into unfavourable terms. Zafrul countered that the 19% tariff no longer applied after the US Supreme Court struck down Trump’s tariff framework.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Two former PH ministers are publicly fighting over a trade deal the government signed. Zafrul negotiated it; Rafizi — who was ousted from Cabinet — is now attacking it. This isn’t policy debate; it’s a proxy war between PH factions using trade policy as ammunition. When your own former ministers can’t agree on whether a deal you signed was good or bad, the rakyat has every right to ask: who was actually in charge of trade policy?

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Both men are right and wrong simultaneously. Zafrul is right that Rafizi’s specific tariff claim was technically inaccurate. Rafizi is right that the broader deal surrendered too much. But neither is addressing the real problem: the deal was rushed, poorly communicated, and published on the White House website before Malaysian MPs saw it. The public spat between two PH elites is entertainment, not accountability.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The Zafrul-Rafizi rivalry predates this deal. Rafizi publicly warned that Farhash allegations undermined the PM’s credibility; Zafrul stayed silent. Now Rafizi attacks Zafrul’s signature achievement. PH’s internal dysfunction is becoming its defining feature — they fight each other more effectively than they fight corruption.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: When your former ministers are publicly calling each other liars over a trade deal you signed four months ago, the problem isn’t the tariff rate — it’s the coalition. Fix your internal communications before you negotiate with superpowers. And stop making the rakyat watch your dirty laundry air on Facebook.
