PKR Accused of Lobbying US Against Najib — The SAME Thing Anwar Calls a 'Plot' When Done to Him

Hantu Raya

March 5, 2026

Said by: Shahidan Kassim (PN-Arau) / Malaysiakini Parliament Report

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-05

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In parliament, opposition MP Shahidan Kassim confronted Anwar Ibrahim with allegations that PKR had communicated with a US firm a decade ago to exert pressure on then-PM Najib Razak. Shahidan demanded Anwar explain the double standard: PKR lobbied foreign powers against Najib, but when Daim Zainuddin’s family allegedly engaged international PR firms, Anwar calls it a ‘plot to topple the government’ and launches police investigations.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This exposes the fundamental hypocrisy at the core of Anwar’s governance: foreign engagement is legitimate activism when PKR does it, but seditious conspiracy when anyone does it against Anwar. The same tactics, rebranded depending on who benefits.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar has spent weeks in parliament talking about an alleged ‘well-funded, strategically planned’ plot to destabilise his government involving international PR firms. He launched criminal investigations. He made dramatic parliamentary speeches. But when confronted with PKR’s own history of engaging foreign firms against a sitting PM, he deflected. The hypocrisy isn’t subtle — it’s structural.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: During the 1MDB scandal, Anwar and PKR actively courted international media and foreign governments to pressure Najib. That was celebrated as brave democracy activism. Now that Anwar IS the PM, the exact same playbook is relabelled as criminal conspiracy. In 2024, Anwar claimed Bersih protests were ‘illegal’ — the same protests that put him in power.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You can’t write the rulebook when you’re in opposition and then burn it when you’re in government. If lobbying foreign powers was legitimate when PKR did it against Najib, it’s legitimate when others do it against you. If it was wrong then, admit it. If it’s wrong now, explain why. You can’t have it both ways — but that’s exactly what PH keeps trying to do.
