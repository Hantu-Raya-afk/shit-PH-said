Data Centre Boom Drains Water and Power — Johor Told to Wait Until 2027 for Water Supply

Said by: Johor state government and Anwar Ibrahim

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s data centre gold rush hit a wall in late 2025 when Johor — ground zero for the country’s silicon valley ambitions — told investors to postpone water-cooled projects for at least 18 months because the state couldn’t supply enough water. By February 2026, Anwar froze all non-AI data centre approvals entirely. Between 2023 and 2025, the government had approved billions in data centre projects from Microsoft, Google, AWS, Oracle, ByteDance, and GDS Holdings — without ensuring the infrastructure could support them. Malaysia’s national non-revenue water rate is 37.1%, meaning over a third of treated water is lost to leaking pipes before reaching consumers.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The government celebrated billions in data centre investment commitments as proof that the Madani economy was attracting global capital. Now it’s telling those same investors to wait because there’s no water and not enough electricity. You cannot approve massive industrial projects that each consume as much water as a small city without ensuring the infrastructure can support them. This is basic governance, and the government failed at it because investment announcements make better headlines than infrastructure audits. The freeze creates investor uncertainty at exactly the wrong moment — companies that committed billions based on government approvals are now being told to wait.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government simultaneously approved a Johor special economic zone designed to attract even more industrial investment, creating a direct conflict between economic ambition and infrastructure reality. Malaysia loses 37.1% of treated water to leaking pipes. States like Perlis and Kelantan waste 50-60%. Building water-intensive industries on top of a crumbling water system is negligence. The irony is suffocating: the government that can’t maintain water pipes for its own citizens approved water-guzzling data centres for foreign corporations, then had to tell both groups to wait. Governance by headline, failure by infrastructure.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a pattern of approving mega-projects before building the infrastructure to support them. The Iskandar Malaysia development zone promised a “world-class city” but still struggles with basic services. Forest City was approved as a RM400 billion development but sits largely empty. Each time, the government prioritises investment announcements over infrastructure planning, then scrambles when reality catches up. The data centre freeze is the latest iteration of the same pattern: approve first, plan never.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You approved billions in data centre investment without ensuring water and electricity supply. Now you’re telling investors to wait 18 months. Fix the pipes first — 37.1% non-revenue water is a national embarrassment. Publish a national infrastructure capacity assessment before approving any new industrial projects. Stop announcing investment wins that you can’t deliver on. Or keep building a silicon valley on a foundation of leaking pipes and overloaded grids — and watch investors move to Thailand and Indonesia instead.

PM's Political Secretary Caught Issuing Support Letters for Hospital Contractors — Anwar Says a Reprimand Is Enough

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 18 November 2025, Anwar confirmed in Parliament that he had reprimanded his senior political secretary, Shamsul Iskandar Mohd Akin, over support letters listing six contractors for a hospital project in Johor. Anwar described the letters as “vague.” On 25 November, Shamsul resigned after mounting media pressure and calls from Rafizi Ramli for a MACC investigation. A Sabah businessman then claimed he provided RM152,000 to Shamsul — money allegedly spent on home furniture, premium cigars, and tailored suits. On 4 December 2025, MACC charged Shamsul with corruption over RM176,829 in gratification related to the hospital project.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The PM’s office was literally issuing contractor support letters. This is the swamp Anwar promised to drain. Shamsul Iskandar was not a minor figure — he was a PKR veteran since the party’s founding, operating within the Prime Minister’s Department with direct influence over government procurement. When your own political secretary is funnelling contractor names to procurement officers and receiving RM152,000 in kickbacks spent on premium cigars and tailored suits, the anti-corruption brand is not just damaged — it is a punchline.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s first instinct was to minimise. “The letters were vague.” “A reprimand is sufficient.” The Edge reported that the support letters listed specific contractors by name — contradicting Anwar’s claim. Naming specific companies in a government support letter is not vague — it is a recommendation, and anyone in procurement knows exactly what it means. The timeline is damning: Shamsul issues letters → Anwar reprimands privately → media reports emerge → Anwar defends the reprimand in Parliament → businessman reveals RM152K payments → Shamsul resigns → MACC charges him. At every stage, Anwar was behind the curve — reacting to pressure rather than leading on accountability. If Rafizi had not publicly demanded action, would Anwar have done anything beyond a private reprimand? The evidence suggests no.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Support letters from political offices are one of Malaysia’s oldest patronage mechanisms. Under BN, political secretaries routinely monetised their proximity to power. Najib’s aide Jho Low operated with impunity for years before 1MDB collapsed. Under Muhyiddin, aide Ahmad Maslan faced corruption charges. The pattern is consistent across administrations: political aides operate in a grey zone of influence, and PMs look the other way until media pressure forces action. PH promised to end this culture. Instead, they replicated it — different party, same premium cigars.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your senior political secretary was charged with corruption for issuing support letters from your office. Your first response was a private reprimand. Your second response was to call the letters “vague.” Your third response — after RM152,000 in kickbacks surfaced — was to accept a resignation you should have demanded weeks earlier. Implement mandatory asset declarations for all political appointees. Ban support letters from ministerial offices. Publish procurement records for every project where support letters were issued. Or keep discovering corruption in your own office after the media does your job for you.

Anda panggil ia rasuah ketika itu. Apa anda panggil ia sekarang?

📜 KES TERDAHULU: PTMP dikritik dari awal kerana perolehan yang tidak telus — menggunakan mekanisme permintaan cadangan dan bukannya tender terbuka. Tebus guna Kepulauan Selatan Pulau Pinang direka sebagai mekanisme pembiayaan untuk PTMP. Jadi logiknya: musnahkan laut untuk membiayai terowong yang belum dibina selama 14 tahun. Batalkannya, audit perbelanjaannya, dan alihkan dana kepada penyelesaian pengangkutan yang benar-benar boleh dibina — transit pantas bas, infrastruktur berbasikal, pengurusan trafik. Meanwhile, the corruption trial of former CM Lim Guan Eng over the project continues, with MACC witnesses describing it as ‘akin to a land scam.’

The project exists on paper to justify the PTMP’s existence and the land reclamation that was supposed to fund it. The tunnel is Schrödinger’s infrastructure — simultaneously active and non-existent.

Current CM insists it’s ‘still active.’ At what point do you admit this project is a failure? Penang’s commuters need solutions, not phantom tunnels.

US Declares 'Victory Against China' After Securing Unlimited Minerals Pledge From Anwar

Said by: MalaysiaNow report

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 November 2025, MalaysiaNow reported that the United States had “declared victory against China” after securing a pledge from Anwar Ibrahim to not restrict exports of critical minerals and rare earth elements to the US. Under the US-Malaysia ART, Malaysia committed to “refrain from banning, or imposing quotas on, exports to the US of critical minerals or rare earth elements” — a binding trade commitment, not a goodwill gesture. Malaysia also agreed to partner with American firms “to create certainty for businesses to increase production capacity,” essentially guaranteeing US access to Malaysian mineral resources in perpetuity.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Indonesia restricted nickel exports in 2020 and used the leverage to build a domestic processing industry now worth billions of dollars — attracting smelter investments, creating tens of thousands of jobs, and moving up the value chain. Malaysia just agreed to keep sending raw materials to the US. One country used its resources to industrialise. The other agreed to remain a raw material supplier. Rare earth export controls are a key geopolitical tool — perhaps the most powerful one a resource-rich country possesses. China controls 60% of global rare earth mining and 90% of processing, and uses export controls as geopolitical leverage. Malaysia gave up the same strategic tool in exchange for a tariff reduction from 25% to 19%.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: When the US “declares victory against China” using your resources, you are not a partner — you are a pawn. The framing says it all: this was about US strategic interests, and Malaysia was the tool. The long-term cost is incalculable. Critical minerals are finite. Once committed to unrestricted export, Malaysia cannot later impose controls without breaching the ART and triggering US retaliation. The country has locked itself into raw material dependency. Anwar’s defenders argue the deal preserves market access and attracts investment. But market access at the cost of resource sovereignty is a losing trade. Ask any country that exported its resources without adding value — they are all still developing countries.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s resource export history is a cautionary tale. The country was once the world’s largest tin producer — it exported raw tin for decades while others built processing industries. By the time Malaysia tried to add value, the tin market had moved on. The same pattern played out with rubber, palm oil, and petroleum. Each time, Malaysia exported raw commodities while neighbours built processing capacity. Indonesia’s nickel strategy — restrict exports, force domestic processing, capture value — is exactly what Malaysia should have done with rare earths. Instead, Anwar signed away the option.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Indonesia used resource nationalism to build a billion-dollar nickel processing industry. You used your resources to get a 6-percentage-point tariff reduction. One strategy builds industrial capacity. The other locks you into raw material dependency. Renegotiate the minerals clause. Impose a domestic processing requirement before export. Build rare earth refining capacity in Malaysia instead of shipping raw materials to American factories. Or keep letting Washington “declare victory” using your resources — and explain to the next generation why Malaysia is still a raw material supplier.

Utusan AS diberitahu secara terbuka untuk “berhenti berkuliah kami soal hak kedaulatan” di tengah kemarahan yang semakin membara terhadap apa yang pengkritik panggil “penyerahan” Anwar kepada Washington. MalaysiaNow melaporkan konfrontasi itu. Perjanjian itu memerlukan Malaysia menyelaraskan dengan kawalan eksport, sekatan, dan penguatkuasaan perdagangan AS — peruntukan yang dilihat secara meluas sebagai mencabuli kedaulatan. Menambah penghinaan, butiran perjanjian diterbitkan di laman web Rumah Putih sebelum dikongsi dengan Parlimen Malaysia, mencetuskan persepsi bahawa Malaysia hanya pertimbangan kemudian dalam rundingnya sendiri.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Apabila rakyat sendiri memberitahu diplomat asing berhenti berkuliah mereka, kerajaan mempunyai masalah legitimasi. Perjanjian ini mencetuskan momen bipartisan yang jarang berlaku — ahli pembangkang dan gabungan pemerintah sama-sama mempersoalkan syaratnya. Perjanjian yang ditandatangani dengan tergesa-gesa, diterbitkan oleh pihak lain terlebih dahulu, dan dipertahankan selepas fakta bukan diplomasi — ia penyerahan dengan kenyataan akhbar. Dasar luar berkecuali Malaysia — yang dikekalkan merentasi BN, PH 1.0, dan PN — dikompromi dalam satu majlis menandatangani.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: Pembelaan kerajaan adalah perjanjian itu memelihara akses pasaran dan mencegah tarif yang lebih teruk. Tetapi “ia boleh jadi lebih teruk” bukan kemenangan rundingan — ia pengakuan kelemahan. Penerbitan perjanjian di laman web Rumah Putih sebelum Parlimen melihatnya bukan kesilapan prosedur — ia pameran kuasa oleh Washington menunjukkan siapa yang mengawal naratif.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia mempunyai sejarah panjang perjanjian perdagangan yang mengutamakan akses berbanding kedaulatan. TPPA di bawah Najib mencetuskan protes besar-besaran atas klausa ISDS. PH 1.0 menarik diri daripada TPPA, memanggilnya pencabulan kedaulatan. PH 2.0 kini menandatangani perjanjian dua hala dengan peruntukan kedaulatan yang boleh dikatakan lebih buruk — dan berbuat demikian tanpa penelitian parlimen yang diberikan kepada TPPA.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Anda menarik diri daripada TPPA kerana ia mencabuli kedaulatan. Kemudian anda menandatangani perjanjian dua hala yang mengkomitkan Malaysia kepada kawalan eksport AS, penyelarasan sekatan, dan eksport mineral tanpa sekatan — dan membiarkan Washington menerbitkan syarat sebelum Parlimen anda melihatnya. Jika TPPA masalah kedaulatan, perjanjian ini lebih teruk. Bentangkan perjanjian penuh di Parlimen untuk perbahasan. Atau akui bahawa kebimbangan kedaulatan hanya berguna apabila anda dalam pembangkang.
