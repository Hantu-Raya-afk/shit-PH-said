Najib Gets House Arrest — The World's Most Comfortable 'Punishment' for the World's Biggest Kleptocracy

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Pardons Board

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-02-02

📌 What Happened

Former PM Najib Razak, convicted of corruption in the 1MDB scandal that looted billions from the Malaysian public, had his sentence halved by a royal pardon and was subsequently transferred to house arrest at his luxurious Kuala Lumpur residence. The man convicted of stealing from the rakyat now serves ’time’ in a mansion.

🔥 Why It Matters

1MDB was the largest kleptocracy case in history. US DOJ estimated $4.5 billion was misappropriated. Malaysia is STILL paying the debt — RM42 billion paid so far, RM50 billion total burden. Ordinary Malaysians bear this cost through reduced public services, higher taxes, and fiscal constraints. The man responsible lives in comfort while his victims pay his debts.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent his entire political career criticising Najib and 1MDB. He used 1MDB as his primary argument for why Malaysia needed change. He asked Malaysians to vote PH specifically to ensure accountability for 1MDB. Then, once in power, he allowed — or facilitated — Najib’s sentence reduction and comfortable house arrest. The anti-corruption crusader became the corruption enabler. The reason? UMNO’s parliamentary support requires keeping UMNO’s patron comfortable.

📜 Previous Cases

In 2018, when PH first won, Najib was arrested and charged. The legal process worked. Under PH 2.0, the legal process was undermined by political calculation. Zahid got 47 charges dropped. Najib got his sentence halved. The pattern is clear: PH trades justice for UMNO’s coalition support.

🎯 PH Callout

You asked us to vote for justice. We voted. You then traded that justice for parliamentary seats. Najib lives in a mansion. Zahid walks free. And the rakyat pays RM50 billion in 1MDB debt. This isn’t justice delayed — it’s justice murdered by the very people who promised to deliver it.

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-01-28

[BM] Jurang Digital Berterusan — Komuniti Luar Bandar Masih Tanpa Internet Yang Boleh Dipercayai Sementara Kerajaan Bercakap 5G

Said by: MCMC / Rural Development Ministry

Category: Education

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Sehingga awal 2025, berjuta-juta rakyat Malaysia di kawasan luar bandar Sabah, Sarawak dan pantai timur Semenanjung Malaysia masih tidak mempunyai akses internet yang boleh dipercayai. Sementara kerajaan membelanjakan berbilion untuk bencana rangkaian dwi 5G DNB lawan Telekom Malaysia, program JENDELA untuk menambah baik infrastruktur digital asas menghadapi kelewatan dan jurang liputan. Pelajar di sekolah luar bandar sangat terpinggir semasa pembelajaran jarak jauh COVID-19, dan jurang digital tidak mengecil secara bermakna sejak itu. Data SKMM sendiri menunjukkan jurang ketersambungan antara kawasan bandar dan luar bandar kekal ketara, dengan banyak sekolah luar bandar bergantung kepada sambungan satelit yang tidak stabil atau tiada ketersambungan langsung.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Jurang digital menggandakan setiap bentuk peminggiran yang lain. Pelajar luar bandar tidak boleh mengakses sumber pembelajaran dalam talian. Perniagaan kecil tidak boleh menyertai e-dagang. Penyedia penjagaan kesihatan tidak boleh menggunakan telemedisin. Perkhidmatan kerajaan yang telah berpindah ke dalam talian tidak boleh diakses oleh komuniti tanpa internet. Kanak-kanak di rumah panjang dan kampung tidak boleh memuat turun laman web asas sementara KL mendapat 5G. Ironi membelanjakan berbilion untuk infrastruktur canggih sementara ketersambungan asas masih tidak tersedia untuk berjuta-juta orang tidak terlepas daripada sesiapa kecuali nampaknya kerajaan.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Kerajaan memilih untuk membelanjakan berbilion untuk rangkaian dwi 5G — menyelesaikan masalah politik antara syarikat telekomunikasi — sementara JENDELA, program ketersambungan asas, menghadapi kelewatan. Bagi Orang Asli dan komuniti asal khususnya, pengecualian digital menggandakan peminggiran pendidikan dan ekonomi yang sedia ada. Kerajaan yang mengutamakan tajuk berita 5G berbanding ketersambungan asas sejagat mempunyai keutamaan yang terbalik. Kerajaan yang benar-benar “Madani” akan memastikan setiap rakyat Malaysia boleh mengakses internet asas sebelum memberi penduduk KL penstriman yang lebih pantas.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Janji infrastruktur digital telah dibuat di bawah setiap kerajaan Malaysia. Di bawah BN, program Pusat Internet 1Malaysia telah dilancarkan — banyak pusat dibina tetapi kurang diselenggara atau tanpa kakitangan. Di bawah PH 1.0, infrastruktur digital secara ringkas diutamakan sebelum Langkah Sheraton. Di bawah PH 2.0, JENDELA diteruskan tetapi dengan kelewatan berterusan. Coraknya konsisten: umumkan program ketersambungan, bina beberapa infrastruktur, kurang selenggara, dan beralih ke teknologi tajuk berita seterusnya sementara jurang akses asas berterusan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda membelanjakan berbilion untuk 5G supaya KL boleh menstrim lebih pantas. Sementara itu, seorang kanak-kanak di rumah panjang Sarawak tidak boleh menghantar kerja sekolah dalam talian. Itu bukan jurang digital — itu apartheid digital. Utamakan penyiapan JENDELA. Pastikan setiap sekolah di Malaysia mempunyai jalur lebar yang boleh dipercayai. Betulkan ketersambungan asas sebelum mengejar tajuk berita 5G. Atau terus membina infrastruktur mewah untuk kawasan bandar sementara rakyat Malaysia luar bandar — komuniti yang mengundi anda dalam setiap pilihan raya — kekal terputus daripada ekonomi digital yang anda terus promosikan.

Child Marriage STILL Legal — PH Promised to End It, Then Did Nothing Because 'Islamic Sensitivities'

Said by: Sisters in Islam / UNICEF / Women’s Rights Groups

Category: Religion

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 What Happened

Despite years of campaigning by women’s rights organisations and explicit promises from PH politicians, child marriage remains legal in Malaysia. Under Islamic family law (state jurisdiction), girls as young as 16 can be married with Shariah court approval, and in practice, younger marriages have been documented.

🔥 Why It Matters

Child marriage robs girls of education, economic independence, and bodily autonomy. It is associated with higher rates of domestic violence, poverty, health complications from early pregnancy, and intergenerational disadvantage. UNICEF has repeatedly urged Malaysia to raise the minimum marriage age to 18 for all.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH has a supermajority in parliament with BN support. They have the numbers to legislate change. They choose not to. The excuse is always ‘Islamic sensitivities’ and ‘state jurisdiction.’ But when PH wants something done — like the Online Safety Act — suddenly state-federal coordination isn’t a barrier. Child marriage persists because the political cost of ending it outweighs the political benefit.

📜 Previous Cases

In 2018, PH 1.0 Deputy PM Wan Azizah pledged to end child marriage. The pledge went nowhere. By 2020, PH 1.0 collapsed without passing any legislation. Anwar’s PH 2.0 hasn’t even raised the issue meaningfully. The promise has been recycled across two PH governments without any legislative action.

🎯 PH Callout

You have the parliamentary numbers. You have the moral argument. You have international pressure. And you STILL won’t act because you’re terrified of upsetting conservative Malay-Muslim voters. You’ve made a political calculation that little girls’ futures are worth less than vote margins. That’s not governance — that’s cowardice.
