[BM] Lesen Nadir Bumi Lynas Diperbaharui 10 Tahun — Sisa Radioaktif Masih Di Pahang

Said by: Chang Lih Kang, Science Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 3 Mac 2026, Menteri Sains Chang Lih Kang mengesahkan pembaharuan lesen operasi Lynas Rare Earth selama 10 tahun, walaupun terdapat kebimbangan berterusan tentang penyimpanan sisa radioaktif. Greenpeace Malaysia memberi amaran bahawa negara berisiko menjadi “hab pencemaran.” Kumpulan alam sekitar mengatakan dakwaan kerajaan tentang pengekstrakan torium daripada sisa itu “tidak kukuh dari segi saintifik.” Hampir sejuta tan sisa radioaktif daripada operasi pemprosesan Lynas kekal tersimpan di Pahang, tanpa penyelesaian pelupusan kekal.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Pemprosesan nadir bumi adalah industri strategik yang kritikal dan loji Lynas Malaysia adalah yang terbesar di luar China. Tetapi nilai strategik tidak membenarkan pengabaian risiko alam sekitar dan kesihatan. Pembaharuan 10 tahun tanpa menyelesaikan persoalan pelupusan sisa bermakna Malaysia berkomitmen untuk sedekad lagi mengumpul sisa radioaktif tanpa pelan keluar. Kerajaan mengutamakan kedudukan geopolitik berbanding kebimbangan kesihatan dan alam sekitar penduduk Pahang.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: PH berkempen menentang Lynas dalam PRU14. DAP khususnya menjadikan Lynas isu alam sekitar utama. Fuziah Salleh — kini senator PH — membina kerjaya politiknya dengan menentang loji itu. Kini gabungan yang sama telah memperbaharui lesen untuk satu dekad penuh. Justifikasi — bahawa torium boleh diekstrak secara komersial daripada sisa — telah ditolak oleh saintis alam sekitar sebagai tidak terbukti secara teknikal pada skala. Ini kerajaan yang memilih pelaburan asing berbanding janji yang dibuat kepada komuniti yang diminta mengundi untuknya.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Lynas telah menjadi beracun secara politik sejak loji Kuantan dibuka pada 2012. PH 1.0 mengenakan syarat ketat terhadap pemindahan sisa yang tidak pernah dikuatkuasakan. BN melanjutkan lesen sebelum PRU15. PH 2.0 kini melangkah lebih jauh dengan pembaharuan 10 tahun. Setiap kerajaan berjanji menyelesaikan isu sisa; tiada yang melakukannya. Coraknya: kempen menentang Lynas dalam pembangkang, memuaskan Lynas dalam kerajaan, harap pengundi lupa.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Kamu berkempen menentang loji ini. Ahli kamu sendiri membina kerjaya menentangnya. Pangkalan alam sekitar kamu mengundi untuk kamu sebahagiannya kerana Lynas. Dan kini kamu beri mereka pembaharuan 10 tahun sementara hampir sejuta tan sisa radioaktif duduk di Pahang tanpa pelan pelupusan. Jika sisa boleh diproses, buktikan. Jika tidak boleh, jelaskan kenapa kamu memperbaharui. Pengundi kamu di Pahang berhak mendapat jawapan yang jujur.

[BM] Rang Undang-Undang Had Penggal PM PH Sendiri Gagal 2 Undi — 32 AP Tidak Hadir, 44 Berkecuali, Sifar Menentang

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Pakatan Harapan Coalition

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 3 Mac 2026, Parlimen Malaysia mengundi Rang Undang-Undang Pindaan Perlembagaan 2026 yang akan mengehadkan penggal Perdana Menteri kepada dua penggal (10 tahun) — salah satu janji reformasi utama Anwar Ibrahim sejak menjadi PM pada November 2022. Rang undang-undang ini memerlukan majoriti dua pertiga sebanyak 148 undi. Ia mendapat 146. Yang di-Pertua Dewan Rakyat Tan Sri Johari Abdul mengumumkan keputusan: 146 menyokong, 32 tidak hadir, 44 berkecuali. Tiada seorang pun AP yang menentang. Ia gagal kerana koalisi kerajaan sendiri tidak mampu mengumpulkan ahlinya ke dalam dewan.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Ini sepatutnya menjadi salah satu reformasi penentu kerajaan Madani — satu semakan struktur terhadap kuasa eksekutif yang PH kempen bertahun-tahun ketika dalam pembangkang. Rang undang-undang ini tidak gagal kerana ditentang secara berprinsip. Ia gagal kerana 76 AP — hampir satu pertiga dewan — sama ada tidak hadir atau tidak kisah untuk mengambil pendirian. Dalam kerajaan yang menguasai majoriti dua pertiga, ini bukan nasib malang. Ini kegagalan kepimpinan, disiplin parti, dan kehendak politik. Ia menandakan agenda reformasi PH bukan sekadar terbantut — ia sedang disabotaj secara senyap dari dalam.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Anwar Ibrahim sudah tiga tahun memberitahu rakyat Malaysia bahawa reformasi perlukan masa, koalisi ini kompleks, kesabaran diperlukan. Dia ada majoriti dua pertiga. Dia sudah draf, bentang, dan bahaskan rang undang-undang ini. Dan apabila tiba masanya, orang dia sendiri tak datang. Koalisi yang sama yang boleh kerahkan penuh barisan untuk undi keyakinan dan kelulusan bajet rupanya tak mampu buat perkara yang sama untuk reformasi demokrasi. Mesejnya jelas: mengehadkan kuasa PM bukan keutamaan bagi mereka yang menikmati kuasa PM tanpa had.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Rekod PH dalam reformasi struktur sangat mengecewakan. Janji mansuh Akta Hasutan tidak pernah dilaksanakan. Rancangan memisahkan kuasa pendakwaan Peguam Negara daripada pelantikan PM tidak ke mana. Cadangan liberalisasi kemasukan universiti dengan mengiktiraf UEC telah ditangguh. Straits Times melaporkan DAP — parti terbesar PH — telah memajukan kongres kebangsaannya ke Julai 2026 khusus untuk menilai sama ada kadar reformasi membenarkan kekal dalam koalisi. Tony Pua, ketua disiplin DAP, memberi amaran bahawa “jika kita tidak keluar, parti akan hilang semua kredibiliti dan boleh jangka keputusan pilihan raya yang menghukum.”

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Anda ada angka. Anda ada rang undang-undang. Anda ada tiga tahun janji di belakang anda. Dan anda tidak mampu kumpulkan 148 orang ke dalam satu bilik. Pakatan Harapan beritahu rakyat Malaysia pada 2022 bahawa undi untuk PH adalah undi untuk reformasi. Had penggal PM adalah Ekshibit A — bukti bahawa Malaysia Madani bermaksud perubahan struktur, bukan sekadar slogan. Pada 3 Mac 2026, dalam dewan di mana tiada seorang pun AP mengundi tidak, reformasi mati kerana ketidakhadiran. Jika anda tidak mampu luluskan reformasi tanda tangan anda sendiri dengan majoriti besar anda sendiri, apa sebenarnya gunanya anda?

5G Dual Network Fiasco — DNB vs TM Contract War Over Termination

Said by: Digital Nasional Bhd / Telekom Malaysia

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 March 2026, Telekom Malaysia (TM) attempted to terminate its 5G wholesale agreement with Digital Nasional Berhad (DNB), the government entity managing Malaysia’s 5G rollout. DNB rejected the termination, saying the contract runs until 2032 and remains binding. Malaysia’s dual 5G network strategy — introduced after Anwar’s government reversed the original single-network plan — has been plagued by policy flip-flops, RM2 billion in DNB debt, and the controversial awarding of the second 5G network to U Mobile.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia’s 5G policy has become a case study in how political interference destroys industrial strategy. The original single-network plan under DNB was already controversial. Anwar’s government then introduced a “dual network” approach — splitting the rollout between DNB and a consortium — which created contractual chaos, duplication of infrastructure, and uncertainty for telcos. Now TM and DNB are fighting in public over who owes what to whom. While politicians argue about contracts, Malaysians still have patchy 5G coverage and telcos still don’t have clarity on their obligations.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The dual network decision was widely seen as a political move to accommodate UMNO-linked business interests rather than a technical improvement. U Mobile’s selection for the second network was questioned by industry analysts. DNB’s RM2 billion debt was accumulated under a plan the government itself designed. Now the government is watching its own creation implode in a contractual dispute — and Malaysians are paying for it through delayed rollout and higher costs. This is what happens when telecoms policy is driven by patronage rather than engineering.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s telecoms sector has a long history of politically motivated decisions. The award of 3G and 4G licences was repeatedly criticised for favouring politically connected firms. DNB itself was created under Muhyiddin’s government with minimal transparency. Anwar’s dual network decision was supposed to fix this — instead, it doubled the problems. The pattern: create a government entity, load it with debt, let it fight with the private sector, blame the previous administration.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You inherited a flawed 5G plan and made it worse. The dual network was your decision. The contractual chaos is your responsibility. While DNB and TM fight over termination clauses, Malaysians are stuck with incomplete 5G infrastructure and a RM2 billion bill. Fix the policy, resolve the contracts, and stop letting political interests dictate telecoms strategy. Or admit that 5G in Malaysia is just another patronage project with better branding.

Anwar Invokes 'Zionists' and 'Bankers' in Parliament to Smear Daim's Widow — Lawyers Call It an 'Outright Lie'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 What Happened

On March 3, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told the Dewan Rakyat that the alleged plot to destabilise his government — attributed to Na’imah Abdul Khalid, the widow of former Finance Minister Daim Zainuddin — involved “prominent Zionist groups” and international bankers. He claimed a UK-based PR firm had been engaged to launch a coordinated attack on national institutions. This came one day after MalaysiaNow revealed that PKR leaders, including figures close to Anwar, had themselves engaged a US consultant in 2016 to build White House pressure on then-PM Najib Razak. Na’imah’s lawyers, Rajesh Nagarajan and Sachpreetraj Singh, issued a statement the same day calling Anwar’s claims “false, preposterous, outrageous, and a blatant attempt to mislead Parliament.”

🔥 Why It Matters

Anwar used the most incendiary term in Malaysian political vocabulary — “Zionist” — under parliamentary immunity, where he cannot be sued. This is not casual rhetoric. In a Muslim-majority country where anti-Zionism carries enormous emotional weight, invoking Zionists to discredit a political opponent is a deliberate weaponisation of public sentiment. It is the same playbook PH once accused UMNO and PAS of running: using fear and identity to distract from institutional accountability. The timing is critical — Anwar is under pressure over the MACC abuse allegations, Bloomberg exposés, and growing calls from within his own coalition for real reform. The “Zionist plot” narrative reframes domestic accountability demands as foreign sabotage.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Na’imah’s lawyers stated unequivocally: “There is not one word about Zionism or Israel or anything related to it in the entire communications plan.” The PR document, they said, was about reassuring bankers of the Daim family’s financial viability — standard crisis communications for a family facing asset seizure. Anwar fabricated the Zionist angle in Parliament, where he enjoys legal immunity, knowing full well he would not repeat it outside. Meanwhile, his own party engaged foreign consultants to lobby against a sitting Malaysian prime minister in 2016. The hypocrisy is not subtle — it is structural. PKR engaging foreign firms to pressure Putrajaya was political strategy. Na’imah engaging a PR firm to protect her family’s banking relationships is a Zionist plot. The difference? One serves Anwar’s interests. The other threatens them.

📜 Previous Cases

Anwar has a long history of invoking conspiracy when cornered. During the MACC crisis in February-March 2026, he accused critics of being part of a plot by “wealthy elites” manipulating media. He dismissed Bersih’s call for MACC accountability as “unfair.” He told the public not to act as “prosecutor or judge” during the KK Mart firebombing crisis. The “foreign plot” narrative is a classic authoritarian deflection — and it is the exact tactic Najib used during the 1MDB scandal, when he blamed foreign media, George Soros, and opposition sabotage for the exposés. Anwar spent a decade mocking that playbook. Now he is running it.

🎯 PH Callout

You campaigned on transparency, accountability, and institutional reform. You told Malaysians that the days of smearing critics as foreign agents were over. You mocked Najib for blaming Soros. You mocked Mahathir for blaming “foreign speculators.” And now, when a widow’s lawyers publish a PR document that contains zero references to Zionism, you stand in Parliament — protected by immunity — and invoke “Zionists” and “bankers” to discredit her. You are not defending Malaysia from foreign interference. You are using the most loaded term in the country’s political lexicon to shut down legitimate scrutiny of your government’s conduct. That is not reform. That is not even competent spin. That is Najib-era politics with a reformasi sticker on it.

Lynas Rare Earth License Renewed 10 Years — Radioactive Waste Still in Pahang

Said by: Chang Lih Kang, Science Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 March 2026, Science Minister Chang Lih Kang confirmed the renewal of Lynas Rare Earth’s operating licence for 10 years, despite ongoing concerns about radioactive waste storage. Greenpeace Malaysia warned that the country risks becoming a “pollution hub.” Environmental groups said government claims about thorium extraction from the waste are “scientifically unsound.” Close to a million tonnes of radioactive waste from Lynas’s processing operations remain stored in Pahang, with no permanent disposal solution in place.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rare earth processing is a critical strategic industry, and Malaysia’s Lynas plant is the largest outside China. But strategic value doesn’t justify ignoring environmental and health risks. A 10-year renewal without resolving the waste disposal question means Malaysia committed to another decade of accumulating radioactive residue with no exit plan. The government is prioritising geopolitical positioning (reducing Western dependence on Chinese rare earths) over the health and environmental concerns of Pahang residents who live near the waste.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH campaigned against Lynas in GE14. DAP in particular made Lynas a flagship environmental issue. Fuziah Salleh — now a PH senator — built her political career on opposing the plant. Now the same coalition has renewed the licence for a full decade. The justification — that thorium can be commercially extracted from the waste — has been dismissed by environmental scientists as technically unproven at scale. This is a government choosing foreign investment over the promises it made to the communities it asked to vote for it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Lynas has been politically toxic since its Kuantan plant opened in 2012. PH 1.0 imposed strict conditions on waste removal that were never enforced. BN extended the licence before GE15. PH 2.0 has now gone further with a 10-year renewal. Every government promises to resolve the waste issue; none have. The pattern: campaign against Lynas in opposition, accommodate Lynas in government, hope voters forget.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You campaigned against this plant. Your own members built careers opposing it. Your environmental base voted for you partly because of Lynas. And now you’ve given them a 10-year renewal while nearly a million tonnes of radioactive waste sits in Pahang with no disposal plan. If the waste can be processed, prove it. If it can’t, explain why you renewed anyway. Your voters in Pahang deserve an honest answer, not strategic doublespeak.

PH's Own Term Limit Bill Falls 2 Votes Short — 32 MPs Absent, 44 Abstained, Zero Voted Against

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Pakatan Harapan Coalition

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 What Happened

On March 3, 2026, Malaysia’s parliament voted on the Constitution (Amendment) Bill 2026, which would have instituted a two-term, 10-year limit for prime ministers — one of Anwar Ibrahim’s flagship reform promises since taking office in November 2022. The bill needed a two-thirds supermajority of 148 votes. It got 146. House Speaker Tan Sri Johari Abdul announced the results: 146 in favor, 32 absent, 44 abstained. Not a single MP voted against the bill. It failed because the government’s own coalition could not get its members into the chamber.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was supposed to be one of the defining reforms of the Madani government — a structural check on executive power that PH campaigned on for years while in opposition. The bill didn’t fail because of principled opposition. It failed because 76 MPs — nearly a third of the house — were either absent or couldn’t be bothered to take a position. In a government that commands a supermajority, that is not bad luck. That is a failure of whipping, leadership, and political will. It signals that PH’s reform agenda is not just stalled — it is being quietly sabotaged from within.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim has spent three years telling Malaysians that reforms take time, that the coalition is complex, that patience is required. He has a two-thirds majority. He had the bill drafted, tabled, and debated. And when the moment came, his own people didn’t show up. This is the same coalition that can mobilize its full bench for confidence votes and budget approvals but apparently cannot do the same for democratic reform. The message is clear: limiting prime ministerial power is not a priority for the people who benefit from unlimited prime ministerial power.

📜 Previous Cases

PH’s record on structural reform is dismal. The promised repeal of the Sedition Act never happened. The plan to separate the Attorney-General’s prosecutorial power from the PM’s appointment authority has gone nowhere. The proposal to liberalize university admissions by recognizing UEC results has been shelved. The Straits Times reported that DAP — PH’s largest party — has brought forward its national congress to July 2026 specifically to evaluate whether the pace of reforms justifies staying in the coalition. Tony Pua, DAP’s disciplinary chief, warned that “if we don’t withdraw, the party will lose all credibility and can expect a punishing outcome from the elections.”

🎯 PH Callout

You had the numbers. You had the bill. You had three years of promises behind you. And you couldn’t get 148 people into a room. Pakatan Harapan told Malaysians in 2022 that a vote for PH was a vote for reform. The term limit was Exhibit A — the proof that Malaysia Madani meant structural change, not just sloganeering. On March 3, 2026, in a chamber where not a single MP voted no, reformasi died of absenteeism. If you cannot pass your own signature reforms with your own supermajority, what exactly is the point of you?

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Syahredzan Johan (PH-Bangi)

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU

Anwar memanggil serangan itu “pelanggaran terang-terangan undang-undang antarabangsa” dan menyifatkan ceramah Barat tentang hak asasi manusia dan demokrasi sebagai kemunafikan:

“Dalam pergaulan saya dengan kepimpinan Amerika dan Eropah, saya tidak selesa dengan ceramah mereka tentang hak asasi manusia dan demokrasi. Kita tidak boleh menelan hujah-hujah sedemikian daripada mereka lagi.”

Usul itu diluluskan sebulat suara dengan sokongan kedua-dua pihak. Tetapi saat paling penting secara politik datang daripada ahli parlimen belakang PH Syahredzan Johan (Bangi), yang menyeru semua AP memboikot acara anjuran kedutaan AS di Kuala Lumpur.

Ketiga, beralih ke arah Iran adalah tidak bertanggungjawab dari segi diplomatik. Mazhab Syiah Iran secara rasmi dianggap “menyimpang” di Malaysia yang majoriti Sunni. Anwar memberi isyarat solidariti dengan negara teokratik yang identiti agamanya dijenayahkan oleh kerajaannya sendiri.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT

Mari kira percanggahan:

Soal kemunafikan hak asasi: Anwar mengatakan dia “tidak boleh menelan lagi” ceramah hak asasi Barat. Ini orang yang sama yang menghabiskan bertahun-tahun melobi kerajaan Barat, NGO dan media untuk hak asasinya sendiri — semasa dipenjarakan di bawah Mahathir dan Najib. Kini penglibatan asing adalah “komplot Zionis” dan piawai hak asasi Barat adalah “kemunafikan.” Satu-satunya yang berubah ialah sebelah mana meja PM dia duduk.

Soal boikot kedutaan AS: Syahredzan Johan menyeru boikot diplomatik sementara kerajaannya sendiri mengekalkan hubungan diplomatik, perdagangan dan ketenteraan penuh dengan AS. Malaysia baru menandatangani Perkongsian Strategik Komprehensif dengan Washington pada Oktober 2025.

Soal solidariti Iran: Malaysia menjenayahkan amalan Syiah. Anwar menyatakan solidariti dengan Iran sambil menganiaya penganut semazhab di tanah air — ini bukan berprinsip, ia oportunistik.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH

Anwar, kamu beritahu Parlimen: “Kita tidak boleh menelan hujah-hujah mereka lagi.”

Adil. Tetapi rakyat juga tidak boleh menelan hujah kamu lagi.

Kamu ceramah Barat tentang kemunafikan sementara ketua SPRM kamu duduk atas RM800,000 saham yang tidak didedahkan. Kamu panggil advokasi asing “komplot Zionis” selepas menghabiskan berdekad melakukan perkara yang sama. Ini bukan dasar luar yang berprinsip. Ini populisme musim Ramadan.

Kamu tak boleh ambil kedua-duanya.

Anwar Condemns US-Israel Strikes on Iran in Parliament — 'We Can No Longer Swallow' Western Human Rights Lectures, PH MP Calls for US Embassy Boycott

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Syahredzan Johan (PH-Bangi)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 2 March 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim tabled an emergency motion in the Dewan Rakyat condemning the US and Israel’s strikes on Iran, which began on 28 February and killed at least 201 people including Iran’s Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei.

Anwar called the strikes a “flagrant violation of international law” and described Western lectures on human rights and democracy as hypocrisy:

“In my engagements with the American and European leadership, I am uneasy about their lectures on human rights and democracy. We can no longer swallow such arguments from them.”

The motion passed unanimously with bipartisan support. Opposition Leader Hamzah Zainudin praised Anwar for a “stern and firm statement.”

But the most politically significant moment came from PH backbencher Syahredzan Johan (Bangi), who called on all MPs to boycott events hosted by the US embassy in Kuala Lumpur:

“Let’s not go. Even though we get invited to their Independence Day celebrations, let’s not go. We need to show that we can no longer accept what they do.”

Anwar also signaled a diplomatic repositioning toward Iran, calling for closer education and trade ties, claiming Malaysia was “the only country to show a firm, brave stance.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

The condemnation itself is unremarkable — Malaysia has consistently supported Palestinian and Iranian sovereignty. What matters is the escalation in rhetoric and the gap between words and actions.

First, Anwar calling Western human rights lectures “hypocrisy” is rich coming from a prime minister whose own government is under fire for:

  • Protecting MACC chief Azam Baki despite multiple corruption allegations
  • Using police investigations to target critics under the guise of a “Zionist plot”
  • Halving Najib’s sentence while the 1MDB debt still runs into billions
  • Failing to pass even basic institutional reforms like the PM tenure bill Anwar is lecturing the West about hypocrisy while running a government that has abandoned virtually every reform promise it made.

Second, Syahredzan’s call to boycott the US embassy is performative populism with real diplomatic consequences. Malaysia’s economy depends on US trade and investment. The US is Malaysia’s third-largest trading partner. Calling for a diplomatic boycott from the floor of Parliament is not a principled stand — it’s cheap political theatre that someone else will have to clean up.

Third, the pivot toward Iran is diplomatically reckless. Iran’s Shia denomination is officially considered “deviant” in Sunni-majority Malaysia. Anwar is signaling solidarity with a theocratic state whose religious identity his own government criminalises. The contradiction is absurd.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Let’s count the contradictions:

On human rights hypocrisy:

  • Anwar says he can “no longer swallow” Western lectures on human rights. This is the same man who spent years lobbying Western governments, NGOs, and media outlets for his own human rights — during his imprisonment under Mahathir and Najib. His entire political comeback was built on international sympathy and foreign advocacy.

  • Now that he’s in power, foreign advocacy is a “Zionist plot” and Western human rights standards are “hypocrisy.” The only thing that changed is which side of the PM’s desk he sits on. On the US embassy boycott:

  • Syahredzan Johan is a PH backbencher calling for a diplomatic boycott while his own government maintains full diplomatic, trade, and military relations with the US. Malaysia just signed a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership with Washington in October 2025.

  • If PH is serious about opposing US actions, why stop at embassy cocktail parties? Cancel the trade deal. Recall the ambassador. Oh wait — that would cost money. On Iran solidarity:

  • Malaysia criminalises Shia practice. Anwar’s own religious affairs apparatus treats Shia Muslims as deviants. Declaring solidarity with Iran while persecuting its co-religionists at home is not principled — it’s opportunistic.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

  • 2018-2022: Anwar’s own foreign lobbying. Anwar and PKR allies engaged Washington consultancies, international media, and foreign governments to pressure the Najib and Muhyiddin administrations. He accepted this support gratefully. Now foreign engagement is subversion.
  • October 2025: Malaysia-US Comprehensive Strategic Partnership. Just five months before calling US human rights lectures “hypocrisy,” Anwar’s government signed an upgraded bilateral deal with Washington covering trade, critical minerals, and market access.
  • Malaysia’s track record on Iran. Malaysia has historically been cautious with Iran due to the Sunni-Shia divide. Anwar’s pivot is a departure from decades of careful diplomatic hedging, driven by the emotional politics of the moment rather than strategic calculation.
  • 2024-2025: Gaza solidarity without substance. Malaysia has been vocal on Palestine but has taken no meaningful economic or diplomatic action against Israel beyond rhetoric. The pattern is clear: loud words, zero follow-through.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

Anwar, you told Parliament: “We can no longer swallow such arguments from them.”

Fair enough. But the rakyat can no longer swallow yours either.

You lecture the West on hypocrisy while your MACC chief sits on RM800,000 in undisclosed shares. You call foreign advocacy a “Zionist plot” after spending decades doing exactly the same thing. You signal solidarity with Iran while your religious authorities criminalise Shia practice at home.

And your backbencher calls for boycotting US embassy parties — the absolute lowest-cost, lowest-risk form of protest imaginable — while your government maintains a full strategic partnership with Washington.

This isn’t principled foreign policy. It’s Ramadan-season populism. You’re performing outrage for domestic consumption while doing nothing that would actually cost you anything.

If you want to be taken seriously on human rights and sovereignty, start at home. Fix your own institutions. Reform your own enforcement agencies. Stop using conspiracy theories to silence critics. Then — and only then — will anyone take your lectures to the West seriously.

PKR Hired US Consultants to Lobby Washington Against Najib — Now Cries Foul When Others Do the Same

Said by: Sivarasa Rasiah, Former PKR Vice-President (confirmed); PKR leadership

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

In March 2026, MalaysiaNow published leaked emails revealing that PKR had engaged a Washington-based political consultancy firm in 2016 to lobby the United States to exert pressure on then-Prime Minister Najib Razak. The emails included an 84-page dossier making serious allegations against Najib, with the goal of getting Washington to publicly rebuke the Malaysian government.

Former PKR vice-president Sivarasa Rasiah confirmed the communications, saying party leaders including himself were involved. He framed it as legitimate advocacy to free Anwar Ibrahim, who was then a political prisoner.

The revelation came as Anwar’s government was simultaneously accusing former finance minister Daim Zainuddin’s family of using foreign agents to destabilise the current administration — calling it a national security threat.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This exposes a massive double standard at the heart of PH’s current political strategy. Anwar is framing Daim’s family’s alleged foreign lobbying as treasonous — a plot to topple the government using foreign influence. But his own party did the exact same thing when they were in opposition.

Opposition MP Shahidan Kassim pressed Anwar in Parliament to explain the difference. The answer matters because the government is using the Daim case to justify expanded national security rhetoric, deflect from the Azam Baki scandal, and paint critics as foreign agents. If PKR’s own lobbying was legitimate, then the same standard must apply to others.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Sivarasa’s defence — “we were trying to free a political prisoner” — is convenient but doesn’t hold up under scrutiny. The 84-page dossier didn’t just ask for Anwar’s release. It made sweeping allegations against Najib and explicitly sought US intervention in Malaysian domestic politics. That’s foreign lobbying. Period.

When PKR does it, it’s “advocacy.” When Daim’s family allegedly does it, it’s a “Zionist conspiracy” and a national security threat. The double standard is so blatant that even a casual observer can see through it.

Anwar’s government is essentially saying: foreign lobbying is patriotic when we do it, and treasonous when our opponents do it. That’s not a principle — it’s a convenience.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Malaysian political parties have a long history of international lobbying. UMNO maintained Washington lobbyists for decades. Anwar himself cultivated deep relationships with US policymakers, think tanks, and media during his years in opposition. None of this was considered problematic when PH was the beneficiary.

The selective outrage follows the same pattern as PH’s treatment of press freedom (Bloomberg good when exposing Najib, Bloomberg bad when exposing Azam Baki) and institutional accountability (MACC is weaponised under BN, MACC is independent under PH).

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You hired a US firm to lobby Washington against a sitting Malaysian PM, produced an 84-page dossier of allegations, and sought American intervention in Malaysian politics. Your own former vice-president confirmed it. Now you’re telling Malaysians that when others do the same thing, it’s a Zionist-backed conspiracy to topple the government? Pick a lane. Either foreign lobbying is a legitimate part of political advocacy — in which case stop criminalising your opponents for it — or it’s a national security threat, in which case PKR’s own leaders should be investigated too. You can’t have it both ways.
