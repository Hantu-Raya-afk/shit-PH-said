5G Dual Network Fiasco — DNB vs TM Contract War Over Termination

Said by: Digital Nasional Bhd / Telekom Malaysia

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 March 2026, Telekom Malaysia (TM) attempted to terminate its 5G wholesale agreement with Digital Nasional Berhad (DNB), the government entity managing Malaysia’s 5G rollout. DNB rejected the termination, saying the contract runs until 2032 and remains binding. Malaysia’s dual 5G network strategy — introduced after Anwar’s government reversed the original single-network plan — has been plagued by policy flip-flops, RM2 billion in DNB debt, and the controversial awarding of the second 5G network to U Mobile.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia’s 5G policy has become a case study in how political interference destroys industrial strategy. The original single-network plan under DNB was already controversial. Anwar’s government then introduced a “dual network” approach — splitting the rollout between DNB and a consortium — which created contractual chaos, duplication of infrastructure, and uncertainty for telcos. Now TM and DNB are fighting in public over who owes what to whom. While politicians argue about contracts, Malaysians still have patchy 5G coverage and telcos still don’t have clarity on their obligations.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The dual network decision was widely seen as a political move to accommodate UMNO-linked business interests rather than a technical improvement. U Mobile’s selection for the second network was questioned by industry analysts. DNB’s RM2 billion debt was accumulated under a plan the government itself designed. Now the government is watching its own creation implode in a contractual dispute — and Malaysians are paying for it through delayed rollout and higher costs. This is what happens when telecoms policy is driven by patronage rather than engineering.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s telecoms sector has a long history of politically motivated decisions. The award of 3G and 4G licences was repeatedly criticised for favouring politically connected firms. DNB itself was created under Muhyiddin’s government with minimal transparency. Anwar’s dual network decision was supposed to fix this — instead, it doubled the problems. The pattern: create a government entity, load it with debt, let it fight with the private sector, blame the previous administration.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You inherited a flawed 5G plan and made it worse. The dual network was your decision. The contractual chaos is your responsibility. While DNB and TM fight over termination clauses, Malaysians are stuck with incomplete 5G infrastructure and a RM2 billion bill. Fix the policy, resolve the contracts, and stop letting political interests dictate telecoms strategy. Or admit that 5G in Malaysia is just another patronage project with better branding.

Anwar Claims 'Zionist Plot' to Topple Government — Daim Family Sues, Calls It 'Abuse of Power'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 What Happened

On March 3, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told parliament that a suspect in an alleged plot to topple the government had engaged an international public relations firm to launch a coordinated attack aimed at undermining national institutions before the next general election. In a follow-up reported by FMT and Malay Mail, Anwar went further — claiming the alleged conspiracy involved “Zionists,” foreign lobbyist groups, bankers, and international media agencies, all driven by what he described as resistance to his government’s “mass scale” anti-corruption push. On March 6, the family of the late former Finance Minister Daim Zainuddin — whose widow Na’imah Abdul Khalid was widely identified as the investigation’s target — filed a lawsuit against Anwar, Inspector-General of Police Mohd Khalid Ismail, and the government, calling the probe “unlawful” and “tainted with bad faith.”

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not just a legal spat — it is the clearest sign yet that Anwar’s government is willing to deploy the language of existential threat and conspiracy to delegitimise political opponents and civil society scrutiny. By invoking “Zionists” — a loaded term in Malaysian politics — Anwar is borrowing from the same ethno-nationalist playbook he once condemned under Mahathir and Najib. The Daim family’s counter-lawsuit reframes the narrative: what the government calls national security, the targets call political persecution. This sets a dangerous precedent where any form of external engagement — hiring a PR firm, engaging media, consulting lobbyists — can be recast as subversion under Section 124B, which carries up to 20 years in prison.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim — the man who spent decades courting foreign media, international human rights organisations, and Western governments to champion his cause from prison — is now telling Malaysians that engaging a PR firm is tantamount to treason. The hypocrisy is industrial-grade. His own political survival was built on foreign advocacy and international pressure campaigns. Now that he’s in power, the same tools become evidence of a “Zionist” conspiracy. Na’imah Abdul Khalid publicly asked the obvious question: since when does hiring a PR firm constitute overthrowing a government? Anwar has no good answer, because the answer would expose the real purpose of this probe — intimidation.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of prime ministers invoking shadowy foreign plots to silence critics. Mahathir Mohamad blamed “Jewish traders” and George Soros for the 1997 financial crisis. Najib Razak’s allies framed the 1MDB investigations as a foreign-backed attempt to destabilise Malaysia. Anwar himself was the target of politically motivated prosecutions under both Mahathir and Najib, with Sodomy I and II widely seen as tools of political persecution disguised as law enforcement. The “plot to topple” playbook is a staple of Malaysian authoritarian governance — and Anwar, despite all his reformist branding, is now running the same play from the other side of the desk.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised accountability. You promised Malaysia would never again see a government that criminalises dissent and weaponises law enforcement against political opponents. Three years in, your Prime Minister is standing in parliament invoking “Zionists” to explain why his government is being criticised — and your police are using Section 124B to investigate a widow for hiring a PR firm. This is not reform. This is the Najib playbook with better PR. If PH cannot tell the difference between legitimate political opposition and an actual coup, then PH has become exactly what it once fought against.

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 What Happened

He claimed a UK-based PR firm had been engaged to launch a coordinated attack on national institutions. This came one day after MalaysiaNow revealed that PKR leaders, including figures close to Anwar, had themselves engaged a US consultant in 2016 to build White House pressure on then-PM Najib Razak. Na’imah’s lawyers, Rajesh Nagarajan and Sachpreetraj Singh, issued a statement the same day calling Anwar’s claims “false, preposterous, outrageous, and a blatant attempt to mislead Parliament.”

🔥 Why It Matters

Anwar used the most incendiary term in Malaysian political vocabulary — “Zionist” — under parliamentary immunity, where he cannot be sued. This is not casual rhetoric. In a Muslim-majority country where anti-Zionism carries enormous emotional weight, invoking Zionists to discredit a political opponent is a deliberate weaponisation of public sentiment. It is the same playbook PH once accused UMNO and PAS of running: using fear and identity to distract from institutional accountability. The timing is critical — Anwar is under pressure over the MACC abuse allegations, Bloomberg exposés, and growing calls from within his own coalition for real reform. The “Zionist plot” narrative reframes domestic accountability demands as foreign sabotage.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Meanwhile, his own party engaged foreign consultants to lobby against a sitting Malaysian prime minister in 2016. The hypocrisy is not subtle — it is structural. PKR engaging foreign firms to pressure Putrajaya was political strategy. Na’imah engaging a PR firm to protect her family’s banking relationships is a Zionist plot. The difference? One serves Anwar’s interests. The other threatens them.

📜 Previous Cases

Anwar has a long history of invoking conspiracy when cornered. During the MACC crisis in February-March 2026, he accused critics of being part of a plot by “wealthy elites” manipulating media. He dismissed Bersih’s call for MACC accountability as “unfair.” He told the public not to act as “prosecutor or judge” during the KK Mart firebombing crisis. The “foreign plot” narrative is a classic authoritarian deflection — and it is the exact tactic Najib used during the 1MDB scandal, when he blamed foreign media, George Soros, and opposition sabotage for the exposés. Anwar spent a decade mocking that playbook. Now he is running it.

🎯 PH Callout

You campaigned on transparency, accountability, and institutional reform. You told Malaysians that the days of smearing critics as foreign agents were over. You mocked Najib for blaming Soros. You mocked Mahathir for blaming “foreign speculators.” And now, when a widow’s lawyers publish a PR document that contains zero references to Zionism, you stand in Parliament — protected by immunity — and invoke “Zionists” and “bankers” to discredit her. You are not defending Malaysia from foreign interference. You are using the most loaded term in the country’s political lexicon to shut down legitimate scrutiny of your government’s conduct. That is not reform. That is not even competent spin. That is Najib-era politics with a reformasi sticker on it.

Lynas Rare Earth License Renewed 10 Years — Radioactive Waste Still in Pahang

Said by: Chang Lih Kang, Science Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 March 2026, Science Minister Chang Lih Kang confirmed the renewal of Lynas Rare Earth’s operating licence for 10 years, despite ongoing concerns about radioactive waste storage. Greenpeace Malaysia warned that the country risks becoming a “pollution hub.” Environmental groups said government claims about thorium extraction from the waste are “scientifically unsound.” Close to a million tonnes of radioactive waste from Lynas’s processing operations remain stored in Pahang, with no permanent disposal solution in place.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rare earth processing is a critical strategic industry, and Malaysia’s Lynas plant is the largest outside China. But strategic value doesn’t justify ignoring environmental and health risks. A 10-year renewal without resolving the waste disposal question means Malaysia committed to another decade of accumulating radioactive residue with no exit plan. The government is prioritising geopolitical positioning (reducing Western dependence on Chinese rare earths) over the health and environmental concerns of Pahang residents who live near the waste.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH campaigned against Lynas in GE14. DAP in particular made Lynas a flagship environmental issue. Fuziah Salleh — now a PH senator — built her political career on opposing the plant. Now the same coalition has renewed the licence for a full decade. The justification — that thorium can be commercially extracted from the waste — has been dismissed by environmental scientists as technically unproven at scale. This is a government choosing foreign investment over the promises it made to the communities it asked to vote for it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Lynas has been politically toxic since its Kuantan plant opened in 2012. PH 1.0 imposed strict conditions on waste removal that were never enforced. BN extended the licence before GE15. PH 2.0 has now gone further with a 10-year renewal. Every government promises to resolve the waste issue; none have. The pattern: campaign against Lynas in opposition, accommodate Lynas in government, hope voters forget.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You campaigned against this plant. Your own members built careers opposing it. Your environmental base voted for you partly because of Lynas. And now you’ve given them a 10-year renewal while nearly a million tonnes of radioactive waste sits in Pahang with no disposal plan. If the waste can be processed, prove it. If it can’t, explain why you renewed anyway. Your voters in Pahang deserve an honest answer, not strategic doublespeak.

PH's Own Term Limit Bill Falls 2 Votes Short — 32 MPs Absent, 44 Abstained, Zero Voted Against

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Pakatan Harapan Coalition

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 What Happened

On March 3, 2026, Malaysia’s parliament voted on the Constitution (Amendment) Bill 2026, which would have instituted a two-term, 10-year limit for prime ministers — one of Anwar Ibrahim’s flagship reform promises since taking office in November 2022. The bill needed a two-thirds supermajority of 148 votes. It got 146. House Speaker Tan Sri Johari Abdul announced the results: 146 in favor, 32 absent, 44 abstained. Not a single MP voted against the bill. It failed because the government’s own coalition could not get its members into the chamber.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was supposed to be one of the defining reforms of the Madani government — a structural check on executive power that PH campaigned on for years while in opposition. The bill didn’t fail because of principled opposition. It failed because 76 MPs — nearly a third of the house — were either absent or couldn’t be bothered to take a position. In a government that commands a supermajority, that is not bad luck. That is a failure of whipping, leadership, and political will. It signals that PH’s reform agenda is not just stalled — it is being quietly sabotaged from within.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim has spent three years telling Malaysians that reforms take time, that the coalition is complex, that patience is required. He has a two-thirds majority. He had the bill drafted, tabled, and debated. And when the moment came, his own people didn’t show up. This is the same coalition that can mobilize its full bench for confidence votes and budget approvals but apparently cannot do the same for democratic reform. The message is clear: limiting prime ministerial power is not a priority for the people who benefit from unlimited prime ministerial power.

📜 Previous Cases

PH’s record on structural reform is dismal. The promised repeal of the Sedition Act never happened. The plan to separate the Attorney-General’s prosecutorial power from the PM’s appointment authority has gone nowhere. The proposal to liberalize university admissions by recognizing UEC results has been shelved. The Straits Times reported that DAP — PH’s largest party — has brought forward its national congress to July 2026 specifically to evaluate whether the pace of reforms justifies staying in the coalition. Tony Pua, DAP’s disciplinary chief, warned that “if we don’t withdraw, the party will lose all credibility and can expect a punishing outcome from the elections.”

🎯 PH Callout

You had the numbers. You had the bill. You had three years of promises behind you. And you couldn’t get 148 people into a room. Pakatan Harapan told Malaysians in 2022 that a vote for PH was a vote for reform. The term limit was Exhibit A — the proof that Malaysia Madani meant structural change, not just sloganeering. On March 3, 2026, in a chamber where not a single MP voted no, reformasi died of absenteeism. If you cannot pass your own signature reforms with your own supermajority, what exactly is the point of you?

Rang undang-undang itu mendapat 146 undi, hanya dua kurang daripada 148 yang diperlukan untuk majoriti dua pertiga. Empat puluh empat AP berkecuali dan 32 tidak hadir. Kerajaan kini berjanji membentangkan semula pada Jun, dengan pemimpin gabungan menjanjikan AP mereka akan “benar-benar hadir kali ini.”

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Ini sepatutnya kemenangan mudah. Had penggal PM popular, sarat simbolik, dan selamat dari segi politik — direka untuk buktikan PH masih mampu laksanakan reformasi. Sebaliknya, gabungan Anwar yang memegang supermajoriti dalam Dewan Rakyat 222 kerusi tidak mampu pastikan ahlinya sendiri hadir mengundi. Kegagalan ini dedahkan kerosakan lebih dalam: tiga tahun di Putrajaya, PH tidak mampu terjemahkan angka parlimen kepada hasil perundangan. Kalau gabungan ini gagal luluskan rang undang-undang yang dipromosikan berminggu-minggu, apa harapan untuk reformasi lebih sukar — penguatkuasaan anti-lompat, IPCMC, ketelusan perolehan, atau kebebasan kehakiman?

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Kerajaan dengan supermajoriti yang kalah undi rang undang-undang reformasi sendiri dengan dua undi bukan nasib malang — ini pengurusan teruk. Sama ada whip parti gagal buat kerja, atau sebahagian gabungan diam-diam sabotaj undian. Seorang AP PH tanpa nama beritahu SCMP: “Rang undang-undang itu sepatutnya mudah lulus. Rasa macam usaha tidak cukup untuk pastikan semua hadir.” Itu bukan penjelasan — itu pengakuan. Dan jawapan kerajaan? “Kami akan cuba lagi pada Jun.” Itu reformasi versi tekan butang snooze. Sementara itu, kamera tangkap Anwar ketawa selepas keputusan undi dibaca, berbual dengan timbalannya seolah-olah rang undang-undang utamanya tidak baru sahaja mati di lantai dewan.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Ini satu corak. PH janji IPCMC — ditangguh. Janji kebebasan maklumat — tiada. Janji penguatkuasaan undang-undang anti-lompat — terpilih sahaja. Janji reformasi pembiayaan politik — terperangkap dalam jawatankuasa. Manifesto 2022 setebal 60 muka surat; tiga tahun kemudian, perkara paling sukar masih tidak disentuh dan yang mudah pun tergagap. Had penggal PM secara literal buah paling rendah — popular merentas parti, disokong sendiri oleh Anwar, dan hanya perlu supermajoriti yang PH sudah ada di atas kertas.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Kamu kata kamu berbeza. Kamu lari atas platform “Reformasi.” Kamu ada angka, mandat, dan sokongan rakyat. Dan kamu tak mampu pastikan AP sendiri masuk dewan untuk undi rang undang-undang yang kamu jual kepada negara berminggu-minggu. Kalau kamu tak boleh luluskan reformasi popular dan simbolik dengan supermajoriti, untuk apa sebenarnya majoriti itu? “Kami akan cuba lagi pada Jun” bukan agenda reformasi — itu pengakuan kamu tak ada kawalan ke atas gabungan sendiri.

[BM] PKR Upah Perunding AS Untuk Lobi Washington Terhadap Najib — Kini Menjerit Salah Bila Orang Lain Buat Sama

Said by: Sivarasa Rasiah, Former PKR Vice-President (confirmed); PKR leadership

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING

Ini mendedahkan piawai berganda yang besar di teras strategi politik PH semasa. 💩 KENAPA INI KARUT

Pembelaan Sivarasa — “kami cuba membebaskan tahanan politik” — mudah tetapi tidak tahan penelitian. Dokumen 84 muka surat itu bukan sahaja meminta pembebasan Anwar. Ia membuat tuduhan menyeluruh terhadap Najib dan secara jelas meminta campur tangan AS dalam politik domestik Malaysia. Itu lobi asing. Noktah.

Apabila PKR melakukannya, ia “advokasi.” Apabila keluarga Daim didakwa melakukannya, ia “konspirasi Zionis” dan ancaman keselamatan negara. Piawai berganda begitu terang-terangan sehingga pemerhati biasa pun boleh nampak. Anwar pada dasarnya mengatakan: lobi asing adalah patriotik apabila kami melakukannya, dan pengkhianatan apabila lawan kami melakukannya. Itu bukan prinsip — ia kemudahan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU

Anwar sendiri memupuk hubungan mendalam dengan pembuat dasar AS, badan pemikir dan media semasa tahun-tahun pembangkangan. Kemarahan terpilih mengikuti corak yang sama seperti layanan PH terhadap kebebasan akhbar (Bloomberg bagus apabila mendedahkan Najib, Bloomberg buruk apabila mendedahkan Azam Baki).

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH

Kamu mengupah firma AS untuk melobi Washington terhadap PM Malaysia yang memerintah, menghasilkan dokumen 84 muka surat tuduhan, dan meminta campur tangan Amerika dalam politik Malaysia. Bekas naib presiden kamu sendiri mengesahkannya. Kini kamu beritahu rakyat Malaysia apabila orang lain melakukan perkara yang sama, ia konspirasi Zionis untuk menjatuhkan kerajaan? Pilih satu laluan. Sama ada lobi asing adalah bahagian sah advokasi politik — maka berhenti menjenayahkan lawan kamu — atau ia ancaman keselamatan negara, maka pemimpin PKR sendiri perlu disiasat juga. Kamu tak boleh ambil kedua-duanya.
