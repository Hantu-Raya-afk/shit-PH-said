PH Wins 1 Out of 20 Seats in Sabah — Anwar Says Defeat Won't Stop Delivery

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-30

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 30 November 2025, Pakatan Harapan was demolished in the Sabah state election. The coalition won just 1 out of 20 seats it contested — down from 7 previously. That is a 95% loss rate. Sabah-based parties dominated, driven by anti-federal sentiment and demands for greater state autonomy — particularly after a High Court ruling supporting Sabah’s claim to 40% of federal revenue. Political analyst Lee Kuok Tiung said plainly: “The Sabah election outcome shows Anwar’s coalition lost badly.” Anwar’s response: the defeat “won’t stop delivery” of pledges to Sabah.

Data Centre Boom Drains Water and Power — Johor Told to Wait Until 2027 for Water Supply

Said by: Johor state government and Anwar Ibrahim

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s data centre gold rush hit a wall in late 2025 when Johor — ground zero for the country’s silicon valley ambitions — told investors to postpone water-cooled projects for at least 18 months because the state couldn’t supply enough water. By February 2026, Anwar froze all non-AI data centre approvals entirely. Between 2023 and 2025, the government had approved billions in data centre projects from Microsoft, Google, AWS, Oracle, ByteDance, and GDS Holdings — without ensuring the infrastructure could support them. Malaysia’s national non-revenue water rate is 37.1%, meaning over a third of treated water is lost to leaking pipes before reaching consumers.

Date: 2025-11-14
