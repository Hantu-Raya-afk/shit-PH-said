Zafrul Appointed MIDA Chairman After Leaving MITI — The Revolving Door Never Stops Spinning

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, MIDA Chairman

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-16

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: When the December 2025 cabinet reshuffle removed Tengku Zafrul from the MITI portfolio, he wasn’t sent to the backbench. Instead, Anwar appointed him as Chairman of MIDA (Malaysian Investment Development Authority) — the very agency that falls under MITI’s purview — and as Senior Political Advisor to the PM. The man who oversaw MIDA as minister now chairs it as a political appointee.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The revolving door between ministerial oversight and agency chairmanship is a textbook governance concern. Zafrul approved MIDA’s policies, budgets, and strategies as MITI Minister. Now he chairs the same agency he regulated. Investment decisions he influenced as minister — including the US trade deal’s implementation — will now be executed under his chairmanship. There is no cooling-off period, no separation, no firewall.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH campaigned on ending the revolving door between politics and GLCs. IDEAS documented 238 political appointments to GLCs and statutory bodies under Anwar — exceeding Mahathir 2.0’s rate. Zafrul’s MIDA appointment is the most brazen example: not even a pretence of distance between the ministerial role and the agency appointment. The same person, the same portfolio, different title. This is not governance reform; it’s musical chairs with name cards.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, ministers who lost portfolios were given GLC sinecures as compensation. PH criticised this practice relentlessly. Under Anwar, the practice continues with better PR — Zafrul’s appointment is framed as ‘continuity and expertise’ rather than what it is: a political reward and soft landing for a reshuffled ally.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You documented 238 political appointments. You campaigned against the GLC revolving door. Then you appointed your former MITI minister as chairman of MIDA — the agency MITI oversees. If this isn’t a political appointment, what is? Implement cooling-off periods. Create independent appointment committees. Or just admit that your patronage system works exactly like BN’s, with slightly better PR.

Civil Service is 90% Bumiputera — Malays Hold 80%+ of Top Posts

Said by: Eurasia Review Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: According to Eurasia Review analysis published in December 2025, Malaysia’s civil service of 1.3 to 1.6 million employees is approximately 90% Bumiputera. Malays hold 77.5% of all posts and over 80% of top management positions. Chinese Malaysians hold just 5.7% and Indian Malaysians 3.8% — in a country where Chinese make up 23% and Indians 7% of the population. The Straits Times reported the “Bumiputera-first policy debate” has taken on a “heady mix of health, education and politics.” When a UiTM professor suggested opening Bumiputera-only programmes to other races to address specialist shortages, the backlash was immediate and the idea was shut down.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A civil service that doesn’t reflect the country’s demographics cannot serve all citizens equally. Race-based hiring in public service perpetuates ethnic exclusion and undermines meritocracy — the very principle Anwar campaigned on. The civil service is simultaneously Malaysia’s largest employer and its most racially homogeneous institution. When 90% of government servants come from one ethnic group, the entire apparatus of state — from policy design to implementation to service delivery — is filtered through one cultural lens. Non-Bumiputera citizens interact with a government that barely represents them.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s “Malaysia Madani” brand promises a civilised, meritocratic, multiracial Malaysia. Yet the most visible arm of the Malaysian state — the civil service — remains 90% one race. Under PH, this is untouchable. Not because reform is impossible, but because 1.6 million civil servants are also 1.6 million votes — overwhelmingly Malay votes that Anwar cannot afford to lose ahead of GE16. The “reformist” government has done nothing — not a pilot programme, not a target, not even a public acknowledgment — to address the structural imbalance. Meritocracy applies to everyone except the government’s own workforce.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The NEP’s civil service quotas were introduced in the 1970s as affirmative action. Fifty years later, the “temporary” measures are permanent, the quotas are entrenched, and the racial composition has only become more homogeneous over time. Every PM — Mahathir, Abdullah, Najib, Muhyiddin, Anwar — has left this untouched. The UiTM incident is instructive: even suggesting partial reform triggered enough backlash to kill the idea instantly. The third rail of Malaysian politics isn’t just electrified — it’s surrounded by a moat.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your civil service is 90% one race in a multiracial country. Your “Madani” brand promises meritocracy. These two facts cannot coexist. Set diversity targets. Open recruitment. Create pathways for non-Bumiputera Malaysians to serve their own government. Or admit that “Malaysia Madani” is a slogan for export — a brand you sell to foreign investors while running a race-based state at home. 1.6 million civil servants, 90% Bumiputera. That’s not meritocracy. That’s a monoculture wearing a multiracial mask.

LRT3 Delayed AGAIN — Software Glitches, Failed Test Runs, RM475M Penalties Looming

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The LRT3 line from Bandar Utama to Johan Setia — a multi-billion ringgit project intended to transform connectivity in the Klang Valley — has been postponed repeatedly due to signalling software failures and the inability to complete fault-free test runs. Transport Minister Anthony Loke warned contractors may face penalties exceeding RM475 million for the delays. The opening date remains unconfirmed. The Edge and Lowyat both reported the latest round of testing setbacks as the system failed to achieve the required reliability benchmarks.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: LRT3 was supposed to be operational years ago. Repeated delays mean commuters continue relying on cars, worsening congestion and pollution in the Klang Valley. Public money is tied up in a system nobody can use — billions invested with zero return to commuters. The signalling software failures suggest deeper issues with project management, contractor capability, and government oversight. Meanwhile, commuters who were told LRT3 would change their lives continue sitting in traffic.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Loke’s penalty threats ring hollow. The government has a track record of renegotiating rather than enforcing consequences on mega-project contractors. Penalties get waived, deadlines get extended, cost overruns get absorbed by the public, and nobody is held accountable. Infrastructure delays under PH are no different from infrastructure delays under BN — the “reform” government delivers the same results with the same contractors and the same excuses. If the RM475M in penalties are actually enforced, it would be the first time in Malaysian infrastructure history. Don’t hold your breath.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s mega-infrastructure project history is a catalogue of delays, cost overruns, and renegotiated contracts. MRT2 was delayed. The ECRL was renegotiated (costs ballooned from RM55B to reportedly higher). The Penang Transport Master Plan is stalled. LRT3 now joins the list. The pattern across administrations: announce ambitious projects, award contracts to politically connected firms, miss deadlines, threaten penalties, renegotiate quietly, and repeat. The contractors get paid regardless. The public gets delays regardless. Only the minister cutting the ribbon changes.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: If RM475M in penalties are owed, collect them. If the contractor can’t deliver, replace them. If the signalling software doesn’t work after years of testing, find out why and publish the findings. Stop threatening consequences you never enforce. Infrastructure accountability starts with one simple principle: contracts that aren’t met should have real consequences. Under BN, they didn’t. Under PH, they still don’t. Prove us wrong. Enforce the RM475M. We dare you.

Race-Based Economic Policies NEP Still Untouchable — 'Unanimous' Critics Ignored

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Think Tanks

Category: Social

Date: 2025-12-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Eurasia Review published a comprehensive analysis in December 2025 finding “almost unanimous opinion” among analysts that Malaysia’s race-based economic policies — rooted in the New Economic Policy (NEP) of 1971 — have failed their “overarching goal of national unity.” Yet the analysis concluded the government is “highly unlikely to abandon” them. The NEP and its successors (NDP, NVP, and now Anwar’s various economic frameworks) maintain Bumiputera preferences in government contracts, education, housing, and corporate equity. Despite 50+ years of these policies, Malaysia remains deeply divided along ethnic lines, brain drain continues at alarming rates, and economic inequality persists within the Bumiputera community itself.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The NEP and its successors have been the third rail of Malaysian politics for over 50 years. Every PM — including the “reformist” one — refuses to touch them. When virtually every independent analyst agrees a policy has failed its stated objective, and the government still won’t reform it, the policy exists to serve political interests, not national ones. Anwar’s multiracial “Malaysia Madani” brand is fundamentally incompatible with race-based economic preferences — but admitting that would cost Malay votes. So the contradiction persists and the country pays the price in brain drain, investment distortions, and communal resentment.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The NEP was designed as a 20-year policy (1971-1990). It’s now in its 55th year. The Bumiputera corporate equity target of 30% has been a moving goalpost for decades — with the government periodically changing the methodology to show either progress or continued need, depending on what serves the political moment. Meanwhile, the primary beneficiaries of Bumiputera preferences have been politically connected elites, not ordinary Malays. The wealth gap within the Bumiputera community is enormous. Race-based policies designed to help poor Malays have been captured by rich, connected Malays — and PH, like every government before it, refuses to acknowledge this because the fiction of universal Bumiputera benefit is essential to electoral politics.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Mahathir introduced the Vision 2020 framework that was supposed to move Malaysia beyond race-based economics. It didn’t. Abdullah Badawi promised the NDP would be more “merit-based.” It wasn’t. Najib’s NTP made similar promises. Anwar’s frameworks use different acronyms for the same structure. The NEP is unkillable not because it works, but because dismantling it would require telling millions of Malay voters that the preference system their political identity is built on needs to change. No politician has the courage to do this — least of all one who needs Malay votes to survive.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Analysts — your own think tanks included — say unanimously that race-based economic policies have failed their stated goal of national unity. Brain drain accelerates as non-Bumiputera professionals leave for countries where they’re judged on merit. Investment distortions persist. Communal resentment deepens. And your response is to maintain the policies unchanged because dismantling them would cost votes. “Malaysia Madani” cannot coexist with race-based economic preferences. Choose one. Or admit that “Madani” was always just a slogan — not a policy direction.

PM's Own Aide Shamsul Iskandar Charged with Bribery — Anti-Corruption Crusade Eats Itself

Said by: Reuters / Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 4 December 2025, Anwar Ibrahim’s senior political secretary Shamsul Iskandar Mohd Akin was charged with receiving bribes totalling RM176,829 from a local businessman. The charges allege Shamsul used his privileged position in the Prime Minister’s office to assist the businessman in obtaining mineral mining licences in Sabah, among other favours. Shamsul resigned just days before being charged, pleaded not guilty, and was granted bail. He called the charges an “attack” against him. Reuters reported the charges “significantly increased scrutiny on the premier’s pledge to crack down on corruption.” CNA reported Anwar now faces a “graft crisis” alongside “PKR party fractures” and “PH coalition jitters.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This is not a minor official in a distant agency. This is the PM’s senior political secretary — someone with direct, daily access to the most powerful office in the country. The charges allege he was running a pay-for-play operation for mining licences from inside the PM’s office. If the allegations are true, the anti-corruption crusade’s own headquarters was a corruption venue. Reuters framing it as increasing “scrutiny on the premier’s pledge to crack down on corruption” is diplomatic understatement — this demolishes the pledge entirely.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: This is the same Anwar who built his entire political career on anti-corruption rhetoric. He spent decades as Malaysia’s moral compass on corruption. He went to prison (on charges he said were politically motivated). He built Reformasi as a movement against the abuse of power. And his closest aide was allegedly selling mining licences from the PM’s office. The irony is suffocating: the man who vowed to clean up Malaysia now has his own office as a crime scene. Shamsul calling the charges an “attack” echoes every corrupt Malaysian politician’s defence — and PH used to mock that defence when BN politicians used it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s history of aide and secretary corruption is extensive. Under Najib, aides and associates were implicated in 1MDB-related schemes. Under Muhyiddin, political secretaries were linked to procurement irregularities. The pattern: powerful aides use proximity to the PM to monetise access, knowing that their position provides both opportunity and protection. Anwar’s office is not different — it is the latest iteration. The Shamsul case is particularly damaging because it involves Sabah mining licences, connecting to the broader Sabah resource corruption ecosystem that PH promised to dismantle.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your closest aide was charged with bribery for selling access to mining licences from YOUR office. Not a distant state official. Not a minor functionary. Your political secretary. The person who sat outside your door. What did you know? When did you know it? Did Shamsul’s activities never come to your attention despite years of proximity? Either you knew and tolerated it, or you didn’t know — in which case your office management is dangerously negligent. Answer the question. The anti-corruption crusade doesn’t survive its own headquarters being a crime scene.

Sabah Timber Corruption — Billions in Illegal Logging While Shamsul Iskandar Got Mining Licences from PMX's Office

Said by: Environmental Groups / MACC Investigations

Category: Other

Date: 2025-12-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Sabah, home to some of Malaysia’s last remaining primary rainforests, continues to face massive illegal and unsustainable logging driven by corruption in licence allocation. The crisis was brought into sharp focus when Anwar’s own senior political secretary, Shamsul Iskandar, was charged on 4 December 2025 with receiving bribes totalling RM176,829 from a local businessman for facilitating mineral mining licences in Sabah. Reuters reported the charges exposed corruption “emanating directly from the PM’s office.” The Shamsul case revealed how natural resource extraction in East Malaysia is systematically corrupted by political patronage — with the corruption pipeline running all the way to the Prime Minister’s doorstep.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Sabah’s forests are among the most biodiverse on Earth, home to orangutans, pygmy elephants, and thousands of endemic species. Illegal and corrupt logging doesn’t just destroy irreplaceable ecosystems — it undermines the livelihoods of indigenous communities, contributes to flooding and soil erosion, robs future generations of natural heritage, and costs the state government billions in lost revenue. The corruption-logging nexus turns public natural resources into private profit for politically connected actors.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The fact that mining and logging licences were allegedly being facilitated through the PM’s own office — through his senior political secretary — means corruption in natural resource allocation goes to the very top of the Madani government. This isn’t a rogue official in a remote district; this is systematic corruption with a direct line to Putrajaya. Shamsul had direct access to the PM. The businessman who allegedly bribed him knew that proximity to power translates to access to resources. PH promised to protect Malaysia’s environment and fight corruption. Instead, PH’s own people were allegedly monetising access to Sabah’s resources from the PM’s office.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Sabah’s timber corruption is decades old. Under BN, logging concessions were distributed as political patronage — most notoriously under former CM Musa Aman, who faced money laundering charges related to timber concessions before his case was dropped. The pattern is bipartisan: whoever controls access to Sabah’s natural resources uses that access for political and personal gain. Different government, same corruption, same forests burning. PH was supposed to break this cycle. The Shamsul charges show PH joined the cycle instead.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your own aide was charged with taking bribes for Sabah mining licences. FROM YOUR OFFICE. The forests of Sabah don’t need another “task force” or “special committee.” They need a government whose own people aren’t facilitating their destruction. Publish every mining and logging licence issued during PH’s tenure. Disclose who applied, who approved, and who benefited. Commission an independent audit of Sabah’s natural resource allocation since 2022. Or admit that Madani’s green rhetoric is as hollow as the logs being illegally shipped out of Sabah’s ports.

1MDB Debt Still RM50 Billion — Malaysia's Largest Fiscal Burden Under Anwar's Watch

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As of December 2025, Malaysia’s 1MDB debt still stands at approximately RM50 billion — the largest single fiscal burden in the country’s history. RM42 billion has already been paid in principal and interest. RM29 billion has been recovered through enforcement actions. Approximately RM13 billion remains to be settled. Under Anwar’s watch, Najib Razak — the PM who presided over 1MDB — had his jail sentence halved by the Pardons Board and was subsequently granted house arrest. The rakyat continues to pay the debt while the man responsible serves a comfortable sentence.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Every ringgit spent servicing 1MDB debt is a ringgit not spent on schools, hospitals, infrastructure, or social protection. The RM50 billion total — principal plus interest — represents the largest single act of kleptocracy in Malaysian history. Anwar made 1MDB accountability his signature promise. Instead, Najib got a halved sentence, house arrest, and whispers of a political return. The debt remains. The accountability was diluted. The rakyat pays.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar built his political career on holding Najib accountable for 1MDB. “Kerajaan Madani” was supposed to mean no impunity for corruption. Instead, under Anwar’s government: the Pardons Board halved Najib’s sentence, the courts granted house arrest, and political allies openly discuss Najib’s “contribution” to UMNO. The man who stole billions lives comfortably while 33 million Malaysians service the debt he created. This is not accountability — it is accommodation.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: 1MDB has been investigated on four continents. The US DOJ recovered over $1 billion in assets. Singapore jailed bankers. Switzerland sanctioned institutions. Goldman Sachs paid $3.9 billion in settlements. Malaysia — the country that was robbed — has done the least of any jurisdiction involved. Under Najib, the investigation was suppressed. Under Muhyiddin, it continued slowly. Under Anwar, the investigation supposedly concluded but the principal figure serves a reduced, comfortable sentence.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You promised 1MDB accountability. You delivered a halved sentence and house arrest for the man who presided over the theft. RM50 billion in total cost. RM13 billion still owed. And Najib may return to politics. If this is what accountability looks like under Kerajaan Madani, the word has lost all meaning. Ensure every ringgit of 1MDB debt recovery is publicly accounted for. Block any attempt at a full pardon. Or admit that 1MDB accountability was a campaign slogan, not a governance commitment.

Debt-to-GDP Still at 64.6% — Anwar Says Wait Until 2028 for Relief

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s debt-to-GDP ratio stood at 64.6% in 2024 — above the government’s own 60% target ceiling set under the Public Finance and Fiscal Responsibility Act. In December 2025, Anwar told Bloomberg that debt pressure would “ease from end-2028” — six years into his tenure before fiscal health even begins to improve. New borrowings were reduced to RM75 billion from RM100 billion but remain massive. The 1MDB legacy alone still accounts for RM50 billion in liabilities. CNA reported on Malaysia’s “increased spending” alongside the “low debt ratio” aspiration that remains unfulfilled.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Anwar campaigned on fiscal responsibility and ending the debt culture of previous administrations. Instead, his government is borrowing at near-record levels while 1MDB alone still accounts for RM50 billion in liabilities. Debt-to-GDP above 60% means Malaysia’s fiscal buffer against economic shocks is dangerously thin. If a global recession hits, or commodity prices crash, or a pandemic returns, the government has limited room to respond. Telling Malaysians to wait until 2028 for fiscal relief is asking them to trust a PM who may not even be in power by then.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Fiscal discipline means making hard choices now — not pushing timelines to the next election cycle. The 2028 target conveniently falls after GE16, meaning Anwar wants voters to re-elect him on the promise that fiscal improvement is “just around the corner” — the same promise he made in 2022. Meanwhile, spending continues to rise: civil service wages (1.6 million employees), subsidies (even after “rationalisation”), development projects (many driven by political rather than economic logic), and debt servicing (16% of revenue). The structural deficit means every year the government spends more than it earns, and borrows the difference. PH has not fundamentally changed this equation — it has just changed the rhetoric around it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian government has promised fiscal responsibility while borrowing heavily. Najib’s 1MDB created the RM50 billion legacy that still haunts the books. Muhyiddin’s pandemic spending was necessary but poorly tracked. Anwar inherited a mess — that’s true. But three years is enough time to demonstrate a credible path to fiscal consolidation. Instead, the target keeps moving: first it was 2025, then 2027, now 2028. The can gets kicked, the debt compounds, and the next government inherits an even bigger mess.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You’ve been in power for over three years. The debt-to-GDP ratio is STILL above your own 60% target. You’re telling Malaysians to wait until 2028 — conveniently after the next election — for relief. That’s not a fiscal plan, it’s an electoral strategy. Cut spending that doesn’t serve the public (238 political appointments, RM85.5M J-Kom propaganda, RM2.6B JAKIM). Enforce tax compliance on the wealthy and corporations. Stop borrowing to fund political patronage. Or admit that fiscal responsibility, like every other reform promise, is permanently “coming soon.”

Anwar's Three-Year Report Card: Steady Economics, Broken Promises, Collapsing Reform Credibility

Said by: Multiple analysts and media

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As Anwar Ibrahim marked three years as Prime Minister in late 2025, multiple analysts and media outlets delivered a consistent verdict: steady economics, shattered reform credibility. GDP growth exceeded 5%, foreign investment hit record highs, and unemployment reached decade lows. But on reform — the actual reason PH was elected — the record is dismal: the Sedition Act was expanded with 90 arrests, UUCA remains intact, ICCPR is unratified, AG-PP separation was sent to committee after three years of delay, Zahid’s 47 corruption charges were dropped, MACC is under fire for politicisation, a journalist was arrested for asking a question, and Nurul Izzah was installed as PKR No. 2.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Three years is not “early days.” It is more than half a parliamentary term. If reform was coming, it would have started by now. Instead, what Malaysia has is a government that performs competent economic management while systematically abandoning the democratic promises that brought it to power. The trade-off is now explicit: you get GDP growth, but you lose civil liberties, institutional independence, and accountability. The question is no longer “will Anwar reform?” The question is: was reform ever the goal?

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s defence is always “we inherited a mess” and “give us time.” But they’ve had time — more time than PH 1.0 ever got. The Sedition Act wasn’t just left on the books — it was weaponised more aggressively than under Najib. UUCA wasn’t just kept — students who protested were ignored. The AG-PP separation wasn’t just delayed — it was tabled and then immediately sent to a committee to die quietly. Every single flagship reform promise has been either abandoned, delayed, or reversed. Economic competence is not a substitute for democratic accountability — it’s what authoritarian governments use to justify staying in power.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: PH 1.0 (2018-2020) had 22 months and accomplished partial reforms before collapsing via the Sheraton Move. PH 2.0 has had 36 months with a larger majority and accomplished fewer reforms. Mahathir’s first tenure showed how economic success can coexist with democratic regression for decades. The pattern is clear: Malaysian PMs use economic performance as a shield against reform demands, and it works — until it doesn’t.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You were elected on reform. You delivered economics. Those are not the same thing. BN also delivered economics — for decades — while gutting institutions. You are now doing the same thing with better PR. Repeal the Sedition Act. Abolish UUCA. Ratify ICCPR. Separate the AG and PP. Restore MACC independence. Or stop pretending you’re different from the people you replaced — because after three years, the only difference is the slogan.
