UMNO's Stranglehold on PH — Zahid Walks Free While Dictating Coalition Policy

Said by: Political Analysts / Opposition

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The single most corrosive dynamic in Malaysian politics today is UMNO’s outsized leverage over PH within the unity government coalition. DPM Ahmad Zahid Hamidi — who had 47 corruption charges dropped in an NFA decision that shocked the legal community — now effectively holds veto power over government policy. UMNO’s support is essential for PH’s parliamentary majority, and UMNO knows it. The result: Zahid walks free, Najib gets sentence reductions and house arrest bids, the AG-PP separation is stalled, DAP is sidelined in the cabinet reshuffle, and the anti-corruption agenda is functionally dead. UCA News described the unity coalition as having a “fractured mandate.” CNA reported Anwar faces a “graft crisis” with “PKR party fractures” and “PH coalition jitters.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This creates a toxic dynamic where Anwar cannot pursue reforms that threaten UMNO’s patronage networks, cannot hold UMNO politicians accountable for past misconduct, and cannot implement the institutional changes PH promised voters. Every major governance failure of the past three years — the Zahid NFA, Najib’s halved sentence, the stalled AG reform, the 238 political appointments, the cabinet reshuffle rewarding UMNO — traces back to one structural reality: UMNO has veto power, and UMNO uses it to protect its own.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH voters who marched for Reformasi got a government that is hostage to the very party they fought against for decades. Anwar’s defenders argue coalition politics requires compromise. But compromising on corruption and accountability isn’t pragmatism — it’s capitulation. The unity government isn’t a grand coalition; it’s UMNO’s rehabilitation programme funded by PH’s credibility. Anwar gets to be PM. UMNO gets its leaders cleared, its patronage restored, and its cabinet positions upgraded. The rakyat gets broken promises. The transaction is transparent to everyone except the people making it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has seen coalition dynamics corrupt governance before. Under BN, component parties traded silence on corruption for patronage allocation. Under Muhyiddin’s Perikatan Nasional, the Sheraton Move coalition was held together by ministerial appointments and GLC posts. Anwar’s unity government follows the same logic — the coalition survives not because it governs well, but because every partner gets something: UMNO gets legal protection, GPS gets Sabah/Sarawak autonomy rhetoric, DAP gets a seat at the table (increasingly a smaller one). The formula is identical across administrations. Only the brand changes.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You told voters UMNO was the enemy. Then you made UMNO your deputy. You told voters corruption must end. Then you dropped Zahid’s charges. You told voters Najib must pay. Then you halved his sentence. At what point does “coalition compromise” become “everything we fought against”? You passed that point when Zahid’s 47 charges were NFA’d. Every day you govern with UMNO holding veto power over reform is another day Reformasi dies — not with a bang, but with a coalition meeting.

Wages Haven't Kept Up with Costs — Real Purchasing Power Has DECLINED Under PH

Said by: DOSM / BNM / Consumer Groups

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: While nominal wages have increased slightly under PH — minimum wage raised to RM1,500, modest salary increments across sectors — the cost of living has risen faster. Diesel is up 56%. SST was expanded to cover more essentials. Food prices are the highest in Southeast Asia at $1,940 per person per year according to USDA data. Electricity costs are up. Property prices are up. The result: real purchasing power — what your ringgit actually buys — has declined for most Malaysians. A 2025 consumer survey found 77% of Malaysians say costs have risen significantly, while official inflation claims just 1.3%.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Purchasing power is the truest measure of economic wellbeing. GDP growth, investment figures, and export numbers are abstractions. What matters to a family is whether they can afford the same basket of groceries this month as last month. Under PH, the answer is increasingly no. When 71.6% of household income goes to daily necessities, disposable income for savings, education, and emergencies shrinks to almost nothing. Families are one medical emergency away from crisis. The government celebrates macro numbers while the rakyat counts sen at the checkout counter.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government points to headline inflation of around 1.3% and claims prices are “stable.” But official CPI calculations underweight items that have risen most sharply — food, fuel, services — and don’t capture the “felt” inflation that families experience. When 77% of Malaysians say costs have risen significantly but official inflation says 1.3%, somebody is lying — and it’s not the 77%. Food away from home inflation runs at 4.3-4.7%, far exceeding the headline rate. The government is using statistical methodology to gaslight the population about their own lived experience. Meanwhile, PH abolished GST (which zero-rated essentials) and replaced it with SST, which taxes MORE daily items. Under BN, diesel was RM2.15. Under PH, it’s RM3.35.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN’s GST system, essentials were zero-rated — consumers paid no tax on basic food, domestic electricity, and public transport. PH abolished GST as a populist move and replaced it with SST, which is less efficient and taxes more daily items. The uncomfortable truth: by many daily-life cost measures, BN’s baseline was better than PH’s performance. Every Malaysian government has faced cost-of-living complaints, but PH uniquely promised to make life cheaper — then made it more expensive through diesel float, SST expansion, and subsidy rationalisation without adequate targeted relief.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You promised to make life better for ordinary Malaysians. Life is MORE expensive. Diesel costs MORE. Food costs MORE. Services cost MORE through expanded SST. Electricity costs MORE. And wages haven’t kept up. Every Malaysian family knows this — they see it every time they buy groceries, fill their tank, or pay their bills. Your 1.3% inflation statistic is an insult to the 77% of Malaysians who know their money buys less than it did three years ago. Stop hiding behind macro numbers and address the lived reality of the rakyat.

[BM] Pengangkutan Awam 'Berfungsi Tetapi Rapuh' — Satu Gangguan dan Semua Orang Memandu

Said by: Khazanah Research Institute (KRI)

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-31

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Kajian Khazanah Research Institute (KRI) yang diterbitkan pada akhir Disember 2025 mendapati bahawa sistem pengangkutan awam Greater KL “berfungsi tetapi rapuh” — gangguan kecil menyebabkan penumpang meninggalkannya sepenuhnya untuk kenderaan persendirian. KRI menyimpulkan bahawa rel “tidak pernah dimaksudkan untuk menyelesaikan masalah trafik Malaysia.” Kajian mendapati bahan api kekal murah secara buatan, tempat letak kereta banyak dan mampu milik, persekitaran berjalan kaki berpecah-pecah dan tidak mesra, dan sambungan jarak terakhir tidak mencukupi. Analisis FMT berhujah ia adalah “sistem yang belum siap, bukan prasarana, yang mengecewakan penumpang Malaysia.” Namun kerajaan terus mengumumkan projek rel mega — MRT3, LRT3, ECRL — seolah-olah membina lebih banyak landasan sahaja akan menyelesaikan mobiliti.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Kerajaan berturut-turut, termasuk PH, terus mengumumkan projek rel berbilion ringgit seolah-olah prasarana sahaja menyelesaikan mobiliti. Kajian KRI sendiri — dibiayai oleh dana kekayaan negara kerajaan sendiri — berkata ia tidak. Tanpa membaharui perancangan bandar, dasar tempat letak kereta, penetapan harga bahan api, dan sambungan jarak terakhir secara serentak, setiap laluan rel baru hanyalah tampalan mahal untuk kegagalan sistemik. Berbilion dibelanjakan untuk landasan manakala sistem kekal satu gangguan dari keruntuhan. Penumpang yang mencuba pengangkutan awam sekali dan terkandas tidak akan kembali.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: Kerajaan mensubsidi bahan api (mengekalkan pemanduan murah secara buatan), membenarkan tempat letak kereta percuma yang banyak di kawasan bandar (menghapuskan insentif kos untuk menggunakan pengangkutan awam), mengabaikan prasarana pejalan kaki (menjadikannya berbahaya untuk berjalan ke stesen), dan gagal melabur dalam penyelesaian jarak terakhir (meninggalkan penumpang terkandas antara stesen dan destinasi). Kemudian ia mengumumkan projek rel RM50 bilion lagi dan mendakwa ia “menyelesaikan trafik.” Ini bukan dasar pengangkutan — ia adalah program perbelanjaan prasarana yang menguntungkan syarikat pembinaan dan ahli politik yang memotong reben, bukan penumpang yang perlu pergi kerja. LRT3 telah ditangguhkan berulang kali. Kos MRT3 terus meningkat. Unjuran penumpang ECRL adalah fantasi. KRI memberitahu kerajaan pendekatan itu tidak berkesan, dan kerajaan membalas dengan mengumumkan lebih banyak perkara yang sama.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia telah membina prasarana rel selama berdekad — KTM Komuter, LRT, Monorel, MRT1, MRT2 — namun kadar pemilikan kereta terus meningkat dan kesesakan trafik terus memburuk. Setiap projek baru diumumkan dengan janji yang sama: yang ini akhirnya akan menyelesaikan trafik. Tidak ada yang berjaya. Sebabnya bersifat struktur: perancangan bandar Malaysia berpusatkan kereta mengikut reka bentuk. Tanpa mengubah reka bentuk — menghadkan tempat letak kereta, menaikkan kos bahan api, memperbaiki kebolehjalanan, membina rangkaian bas — menambah laluan rel adalah seperti membina baldi lebih besar manakala paip terus mengalir.

🎯 TEGURAN PH: Badan pemikir yang dibiayai kerajaan anda sendiri berkata rel sahaja tidak menyelesaikan trafik. Namun dasar pengangkutan anda adalah: bina lebih banyak rel. Baharui dasar tempat letak kereta. Baharui penetapan harga bahan api. Biayai rangkaian bas. Baiki prasarana pejalan kaki. Ini adalah perubahan yang membosankan dan sukar dari segi politik yang sebenarnya akan menjadikan pengangkutan awam berfungsi. Tetapi ia tidak menghasilkan peluang bergambar memotong reben, jadi ia tidak berlaku. Berhenti membina monumen mahal untuk keangkuhan politik dan mula membina SISTEM pengangkutan.

Public Transport 'Functional But Fragile' — One Disruption and Everyone Drives

Said by: Khazanah Research Institute (KRI)

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-31

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: A Khazanah Research Institute (KRI) study published in late December 2025 found that Greater KL’s public transport system is “functional but fragile” — minor disruptions cause commuters to abandon it entirely for private vehicles. KRI concluded that rail was “never meant to fix Malaysia’s traffic problem.” The study found that fuel remains artificially cheap, parking is abundant and affordable, walking environments are fragmented and hostile, and last-mile connectivity is inadequate. FMT’s analysis argued it is “an unfinished system, not infrastructure, that fails Malaysian commuters.” Yet the government continues announcing mega rail projects — MRT3, LRT3, ECRL — as if building more tracks alone will solve mobility.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Successive governments, including PH, keep announcing multi-billion ringgit rail projects as if infrastructure alone solves mobility. KRI’s own study — funded by the government’s own sovereign wealth fund — says it doesn’t. Without simultaneously reforming urban planning, parking policy, fuel pricing, and last-mile connectivity, every new rail line is just an expensive band-aid on a systemic failure. Billions are spent on tracks while the system remains one disruption away from collapse. Commuters who try public transport once and get stranded never come back.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government subsidises fuel (keeping driving artificially cheap), allows abundant free parking in urban areas (removing the cost incentive to use public transport), neglects pedestrian infrastructure (making it dangerous to walk to stations), and fails to invest in last-mile solutions (leaving commuters stranded between stations and destinations). Then it announces another RM50 billion rail project and claims it is “solving traffic.” This is not a transport policy — it is an infrastructure spending programme that benefits construction companies and politicians who cut ribbons, not commuters who need to get to work. LRT3 has been delayed repeatedly. MRT3 costs keep escalating. The ECRL’s ridership projections are fantasy. KRI told the government the approach doesn’t work, and the government responded by announcing more of the same.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has been building rail infrastructure for decades — KTM Komuter, LRT, Monorail, MRT1, MRT2 — yet car ownership rates keep climbing and traffic congestion keeps worsening. Each new project is announced with the same promise: this one will finally solve traffic. None has. The reason is structural: Malaysia’s urban planning is car-centric by design. Without changing the design — restricting parking, raising fuel costs, improving walkability, building bus networks — adding rail lines is like building a bigger bucket while the tap keeps running.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your own government-funded think tank says rail alone doesn’t solve traffic. Yet your transport policy is: build more rail. Reform parking policy. Reform fuel pricing. Fund bus networks. Fix pedestrian infrastructure. These are the boring, politically difficult changes that would actually make public transport work. But they don’t generate ribbon-cutting photo ops, so they don’t happen. Stop building expensive monuments to political vanity and start building a transport SYSTEM.

Said by: High Court Judge Alice Loke / Najib Razak

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-22

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 22 December 2025, the KL High Court ruled that the royal addendum allowing convicted former PM Najib Razak to serve his remaining 1MDB sentence under house arrest was invalid. Judge Alice Loke said the King’s discretion must be exercised “for the public good” and found the addendum lacked proper legal basis. Al Jazeera and Reuters both reported the ruling. The Selangor Sultan had earlier warned that pardons should not be “arbitrary for certain parties.” Anwar’s government had already halved Najib’s sentence via the Pardons Board in February 2024 — the house arrest bid was the next step in an escalating pattern of leniency for a convicted kleptocrat whose party Anwar now depends on.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The house arrest addendum was widely seen as a backroom deal to keep UMNO in the coalition. Najib’s sentence was first halved (from 12 to 6 years), then the house arrest addendum emerged as a way to get him out of prison entirely while technically still “serving” his sentence. The court’s rejection exposed the political machinery behind it — this was not clemency based on merit, but leniency engineered for coalition management. The judiciary held the line that the executive would not. Judge Alice Loke’s ruling was a rare moment of institutional independence in a system where every other check has failed.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar built his political career opposing Najib. He spent years calling 1MDB the greatest financial scandal in Malaysian history. He demanded accountability. Then he took power, needed UMNO’s votes, and began systematically reducing the consequences for the man who stole billions. First, the Pardons Board halved the sentence. Then, the house arrest addendum appeared. If the court hadn’t intervened, Najib — convicted of stealing from the Malaysian people — would be living in a mansion while the country still owes RM50 billion from his scandal. The man who caused the debt gets a mansion. The rakyat who pays the debt gets toll hikes and diesel price increases. This is Anwar’s Malaysia Madani.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a troubled history with pardons and sentence reductions for politically connected convicts. Anwar himself benefited from a royal pardon after GE14 — which his supporters argued was justified given the politically motivated nature of his conviction. The difference with Najib is that his conviction was for an actual financial crime with billions in documented losses. Halving his sentence and attempting house arrest sets a precedent: if you steal enough and your party is useful enough, the consequences are negotiable. The Selangor Sultan’s warning — that pardons should not be “arbitrary for certain parties” — was a rare royal rebuke that went unheeded by the government.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: The judiciary saved you from yourself. If the court hadn’t ruled the addendum invalid, you would have put a convicted kleptocrat in a mansion while Malaysians pay RM50 billion for his crimes. You already halved his sentence. You already dropped his DPM’s 47 charges. How much more leniency does UMNO need before you admit that Reformasi is dead and coalition survival is all that matters? The court held the line. Will you?

Toll Abolition Promise Broken — Govt Pays RM591M to FREEZE Hikes Instead

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: PH promised to abolish highway tolls in both GE14 and GE15. Instead, in December 2025, the government announced it would spend RM591 million just to prevent toll increases on 10 highways in 2026 — paying concessionaires with public money to not raise prices. The Works Minister admitted that fully abolishing tolls would cost “billions.” Gerakan mocked the broken promise. FMT asked whether Anwar would “repeat PH’s pledge to abolish tolls” ahead of GE16. Malaysian drivers continue paying tolls AND their taxes now subsidise the freeze. Festive toll-free periods were also cancelled.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Toll abolition was one of PH’s most concrete, measurable manifesto promises — specific enough that voters could easily verify delivery. The reality: not only were tolls not abolished, the government is paying highway concessionaires hundreds of millions annually just to freeze rate hikes. The RM591M spent on freezing could have funded actual infrastructure improvements. Instead, it flows to concessionaire shareholders — politically connected companies that profit from a captive public. This is the worst possible outcome: broken promise, continued tolls, AND public money transferred to private companies.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The Works Minister’s admission that abolition would cost “billions” is an admission that PH made the promise knowing it was undeliverable. You can’t campaign on toll abolition twice, win government twice, and then say it costs too much. Either you knew the cost before promising (in which case you lied to voters) or you didn’t bother calculating it (in which case you’re incompetent). The toll system represents a regressive tax on mobility — lower-income workers who commute long distances to factory jobs pay a disproportionate share of their income on tolls. PH’s response to this inequality: pay concessionaires with tax money to maintain the status quo.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The toll abolition promise has been made by every opposition coalition since the early 2000s. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) didn’t abolish tolls either — despite 22 months in government. The concession agreements, many awarded under previous administrations through questionable processes, are structured to guarantee private profits at public expense. No government has had the political will to renegotiate or buy back concessions. The concessionaires know this, which is why they keep raising toll rates and governments keep paying them not to.

So the rakyat pays twice: once at the toll booth, once through taxes.

[BM] Anwar Kata 'Masih Jauh' Soal Pilihan Raya Pada Disember 2025 — Kini Sedia PRU16 Kilat Untuk 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-17

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU

Pada 17 Disember 2025, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim memberitahu editor dan wartawan kanan di taklimat di Seri Perdana bahawa beliau menolak sebarang kemungkinan pilihan raya umum kilat pada 2026. “Pilihan raya masih jauh lagi. Saya tidak terfikir tentangnya,” katanya. “Saya menumpukan pada bagaimana kita terus melaksanakan pembaharuan dan meringankan masalah rakyat mengenai kos sara hidup yang lebih tinggi.”

Menjelang Februari 2026, kurang daripada dua bulan kemudian, spekulasi semakin meningkat bahawa PRU16 boleh dipanggil seawal Julai 2026. CNA melaporkan orang dalam parti dan penjawat awam mengesahkan persediaan sedang berjalan. Reuters melaporkan pada Mac 2026 bahawa rakan gabungan sudah mengatur kedudukan untuk pilihan raya kilat.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING

Ini adalah PM yang berbohong kepada media tentang garis masa pilihan rayanya. Pada Disember, beliau berkata pilihan raya “masih jauh lagi” dan beliau langsung tidak terfikir tentangnya. Menjelang Februari, jentera partinya sendiri sedang bersiap sedia untuk pilihan raya kilat. Mandat semasa tidak tamat sehingga Februari 2028 — memanggil pilihan raya hampir dua tahun lebih awal adalah keputusan strategik besar yang tidak berlaku dalam sekelip mata.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT

“Saya tidak terfikir tentangnya.” Itulah yang PM kata pada Disember. PM yang sama yang merupakan pengerusi partinya, ketua gabungannya, dan ahli politik kerjaya yang memikirkan tentang pilihan raya setiap hari selama 30 tahun.

Sudah tentu beliau sedang memikirkannya. Pilihan raya Sabah pada November 2025 — di mana PH dihancurkan — sudah memaksa pertimbangan semula strategik. Kekacauan pilihan raya parti PKR, pemergian Rafizi, dan rombakan kabinet semuanya adalah langkah yang hanya masuk akal jika kamu sedang mengatur kedudukan untuk pilihan raya umum awal.

Anwar memberitahu wartawan apa yang mereka mahu dengar — “tumpuan pada pembaharuan, tumpuan pada kos sara hidup” — sementara operasi politiknya sudah mengira unjuran kerusi dan aritmetik gabungan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU

Muhyiddin menafikan merancang pilihan raya negeri Melaka dan Johor sehingga ia dipanggil. Ia tradisi politik Malaysia: berbohong tentang masa pilihan raya, kemudian panggil pilihan raya apabila ia menguntungkan secara strategik.

Anwar melakukan tepat apa yang dikritiknya tentang pendahulunya.

🎯 SERUAN PH
