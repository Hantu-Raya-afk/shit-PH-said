Malaysia Committed to Never Banning Rare Earth Exports to the US — Under Zafrul's Trade Deal

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, MITI Minister / USTR

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Under the US-Malaysia Agreement on Reciprocal Trade signed by Tengku Zafrul on 26 October 2025, Malaysia committed to not imposing ‘quantitative restrictions’ (bans or quotas) on exports of critical or rare-earth minerals to the United States. RTM Misidentifies THREE ASEAN Leaders on Live TV — Calls Singapore's PM 'Lee Hsien Loong' at Summit Malaysia Hosted

Said by: RTM / Communications Ministry under Fahmi Fadzil

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 26 October 2025, during the live broadcast of the opening ceremony of the 47th ASEAN Summit — which Malaysia was hosting — the national broadcaster Radio Televisyen Malaysia (RTM) misidentified three Southeast Asian leaders:

  • Singapore PM Lawrence Wong was called Lee Hsien Loong (his predecessor)
  • Thailand PM Anutin Charnvirakul was called Srettha Thavisin (his predecessor)
  • Indonesia President Prabowo Subianto was called Jokowi (his predecessor) RTM issued two separate apology statements. The broadcasting department said it “took the matter seriously” and that “appropriate action had been taken.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This was Malaysia’s moment on the world stage. The ASEAN Summit is the region’s premier diplomatic event, and Malaysia was the host. The entire region was watching. And Malaysia’s national broadcaster couldn’t get the names of three neighbouring heads of state right.

Tengku Zafrul Negotiated the US Trade Deal Critics Call 'Worst Agreement Since Independence' — Then Got Promoted

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, former MITI Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 26 October 2025, then-MITI Minister Tengku Zafrul signed the Agreement on Reciprocal Trade (ART) with the United States at the 47th ASEAN Summit in Kuala Lumpur. The deal committed Malaysia to providing preferential market access for US products across chemicals, machinery, vehicles, dairy, poultry, pork, rice, and fuel ethanol. Malaysia also committed to not imposing bans or quotas on the export of critical or rare-earth minerals to the US. In return, the US maintained a 19% reciprocal tariff on Malaysian goods — with only 60.4% of tariff lines zero-rated. Critics from across the political spectrum, including Mahathir and Rafizi Ramli, called it a one-sided surrender.

US-Malaysia Trade Deal Called 'Worst Agreement Since Independence' and 'Act of Surrender'

Said by: Tommy Thomas, Former Attorney General (and critics)

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-10-26

The Deal That Sold Malaysia’s Sovereignty

On 26 October 2025, Anwar Ibrahim and Donald Trump signed the US-Malaysia Agreement on Reciprocal Trade (ART) at the ASEAN Summit. Former Attorney-General Tommy Thomas called it the worst agreement Malaysia has entered since independence in 1957. He was not exaggerating.

The Facts

Fact: The deal maintains a 19% US tariff on Malaysian goods — down from 25%, but still punitive by any standard. Malaysia’s effective tariff on US goods before this deal was roughly 6%. The US got the better end by a factor of three.
