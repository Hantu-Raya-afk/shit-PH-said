[BM] Kajian Malaysia: Analisis Kos-Manfaat Perjanjian Perdagangan AS Tunjuk 'Kebimbangan' Tetapi Kerajaan Kata Jangan Keluar

Said by: Malaysian Government

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-01-27

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada Januari 2026, Bloomberg melaporkan kerajaan Malaysia mengumumkan analisis kos-manfaat rasmi terhadap perjanjian Agreed Reciprocal Trade (ART) AS-Malaysia yang kontroversi — berikutan kritikan bertambah daripada bekas AG Tommy Thomas (yang menyebutnya “perjanjian paling teruk sejak merdeka”), bekas PM Mahathir, kumpulan pemikir, dan ahli politik pembangkang. Tetapi dalam nafas yang sama, kerajaan memberi amaran kepada penggubal undang-undang agar tidak meninggalkan pakatan. East Asia Forum menerbitkan analisis terperinci bertajuk “The Art of the Deal Lost in US-Malaysia Pact,” berhujah Malaysia menyerahkan kawalan eksport mineral kritikal, hak percukaian perkhidmatan digital, dan dasar perdagangan bebas sebagai ganti pengurangan tarif daripada 25% kepada 19% — masih menghukum menurut sebarang piawaian. Butiran perjanjian pertama kali diterbitkan di laman web Rumah Putih sebelum dikongsi dengan Parlimen Malaysia.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Mengumumkan analisis kos-manfaat sambil mendahului kesimpulannya bukan tadbir urus — ia teater politik. “Kaji tetapi jangan keluar” memberitahu anda segalanya tentang pendekatan pentadbiran ini: akui masalah, ubah apa-apa pun tidak. Jika analisis menunjukkan perjanjian itu merugikan Malaysia, adakah kerajaan akan bertindak? Jawapannya sudah ada dalam rangkaian. Kajian itu wujud untuk menyerap tekanan, bukan untuk memaklumkan keputusan. Fakta bahawa Parlimen mengetahui perjanjian itu dari laman web Rumah Putih ialah kegagalan tadbir urus tertib pertama — perjanjian perdagangan yang mengekang kedaulatan negara sepatutnya memerlukan kelulusan parlimen sebelum ditandatangani, bukan selepas.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: “Kaji tetapi jangan keluar” ialah buku panduan Madani dalam bentuk ringkas: akui masalah, janjikan proses, ubah apa-apa pun tidak. Ia pendekatan sama yang digunakan untuk pemisahan AG-PP, pemansuhan AUKU, penghapusan tol, dan setiap reformasi lain yang tergendala. Analisis kos-manfaat sama ada akan dikuburkan, dipetik secara selektif, atau digunakan untuk mewajarkan penyesuaian kecil sementara perjanjian teras kekal tidak disentuh. Anwar melangkau badan perundangan mengenai perjanjian ekonomi paling penting dalam dekad. Apabila bekas AG anda sendiri menyebut perjanjian perdagangan anda yang paling teruk sejak merdeka dan respon anda “kami akan kaji tetapi kami kekalkan,” kajian itu bersifat pertunjukan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia mempunyai sejarah perjanjian perdagangan di mana kerajaan berkomitmen dahulu dan menyemak kemudian. TPPA di bawah Najib berdepan kritikan serupa — Malaysia bersetuju dengan peruntukan yang pengkritik kata menjejaskan kedaulatan, harga farmaseutikal, dan hak buruh. PH 1.0 menarik diri daripada TPPA pada 2018, memanggilnya tidak adil. PH 2.0 menandatangani ART — yang pengkritik kata mengandungi peruntukan lebih ketat — dan kini mahu “mengkaji” sama ada ia idea yang baik. Corak berkomitmen pada perjanjian di bawah tekanan, kemudian menjalankan semakan selepas fakta yang tidak mengubah apa-apa, adalah tradisi Malaysia yang dwipartisan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Jika anda serius tentang analisis kos-manfaat, terbitkan terma penuh ART — bukan ringkasan Rumah Putih, tetapi perjanjian sebenar. Biarkan ahli ekonomi bebas, bukan pasukan petugas yang dilantik kerajaan, menilai perjanjian. Jika analisis menunjukkan Malaysia mendapat perjanjian buruk, beranilah berunding semula. “Kaji tetapi jangan keluar” bukan dasar — ia pengakuan bahawa anda sudah tahu jawapannya dan tidak mahu bertindak atasnya. Parlimen layak mendapat undian mengenai perjanjian perdagangan yang mengekang kedaulatan negara. Berikan mereka satu.

Malaysia Study: Cost-Benefit Analysis of US Trade Deal Shows 'Concerns' But Government Says Don't Quit

Said by: Malaysian Government

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-01-27

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2026, Bloomberg reported that the Malaysian government announced a formal cost-benefit analysis of the controversial US-Malaysia Agreed Reciprocal Trade (ART) agreement — following months of mounting criticism from former AG Tommy Thomas (who called it “the worst agreement since independence”), former PM Mahathir, think tanks, and opposition politicians. But in the same breath, the government warned lawmakers not to quit the pact. East Asia Forum published a detailed analysis titled “The Art of the Deal Lost in US-Malaysia Pact,” arguing Malaysia surrendered critical mineral export controls, digital services taxation rights, and independent trade policy in exchange for a tariff reduction from 25% to 19% — still punitive by any standard. The deal’s details were first published on the White House website before being shared with Malaysian Parliament.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Announcing a cost-benefit analysis while pre-empting its conclusion is not governance — it is political theatre. “Study but don’t quit” tells you everything about this administration’s approach: acknowledge problems, change nothing. If the analysis shows the deal hurts Malaysia, will the government act? The answer is already in the framing. The study exists to absorb pressure, not to inform decisions. The fact that Parliament learned about the deal from the White House website is a governance failure of the first order — trade agreements that constrain national sovereignty should require parliamentary approval before signing, not after.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: “Study but don’t quit” is the Madani playbook in miniature: acknowledge the problem, promise a process, change nothing. It is the same approach applied to the AG-PP separation, UUCA repeal, toll abolition, and every other stalled reform. The cost-benefit analysis will either be buried, selectively quoted, or used to justify minor adjustments while the core agreement remains untouched. Anwar bypassed the legislature on the most consequential economic agreement in decades. When your own former AG calls your trade deal the worst since independence and your response is “we’ll study it but we’re keeping it,” the study is performative.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a history of trade agreements where the government commits first and reviews later. The TPPA under Najib faced similar criticism — Malaysia signed on to provisions critics said undermined sovereignty, pharmaceutical pricing, and labour rights. PH 1.0 withdrew from the TPPA in 2018, calling it unfair. PH 2.0 signed the ART — which critics say contains even more restrictive provisions — and now wants to “study” whether it was a good idea. The pattern of committing to deals under pressure, then conducting post-hoc reviews that change nothing, is a bipartisan Malaysian tradition.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: If you’re serious about the cost-benefit analysis, publish the full terms of the ART — not the White House summary, but the actual agreement. Let independent economists, not government-appointed task forces, evaluate the deal. If the analysis shows Malaysia got a bad deal, have the courage to renegotiate. “Study but don’t quit” is not a policy — it is a confession that you already know the answer and don’t want to act on it. Parliament deserves a vote on trade agreements that constrain national sovereignty. Give them one.

Zulkifli Hasan’s appointment itself was a political concession — he represents the Malay-Muslim establishment within PH’s coalition.

Because correcting Zulkifli would upset the conservative base PH desperately needs. So the government let a pseudoscientific claim stand as Malaysia’s official parliamentary position on sexual orientation. Madani means civilised.
