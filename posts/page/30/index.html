[BM] Setiausaha Politik PM Didapati Keluarkan Surat Sokongan Untuk Kontraktor Hospital — Anwar Kata Teguran Sudah Cukup

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-18

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 18 November 2025, Anwar mengesahkan di Parlimen bahawa beliau telah menegur setiausaha politik kanan, Shamsul Iskandar Mohd Akin, mengenai surat sokongan yang menyenaraikan enam kontraktor untuk projek hospital di Johor. Anwar menggambarkan surat tersebut sebagai “kabur.” Pada 25 November, Shamsul meletak jawatan selepas tekanan media dan desakan Rafizi Ramli untuk siasatan SPRM. Seorang ahli perniagaan Sabah kemudian mendakwa memberikan RM152,000 kepada Shamsul — wang yang didakwa dibelanjakan untuk perabot rumah, cerut premium, dan sut bertempah. Pada 4 Disember 2025, SPRM mendakwa Shamsul dengan rasuah berjumlah RM176,829 berkaitan projek hospital itu.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Pejabat PM sendiri yang mengeluarkan surat sokongan kontraktor. Inilah paya yang Anwar janjikan untuk dikosongkan. Shamsul Iskandar bukan tokoh kecil — beliau veteran PKR sejak penubuhan parti, beroperasi dalam Jabatan Perdana Menteri dengan pengaruh langsung ke atas perolehan kerajaan. Apabila setiausaha politik anda sendiri menyalurkan nama kontraktor kepada pegawai perolehan dan menerima RM152,000 dalam sogokan untuk cerut premium dan sut bertempah, jenama antirasuah bukan lagi rosak — ia menjadi bahan jenaka.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: Naluri pertama Anwar adalah meminimumkan. “Surat itu kabur.” “Teguran sudah memadai.” The Edge melaporkan surat sokongan menyenaraikan kontraktor tertentu mengikut nama — bercanggah dengan dakwaan Anwar. Menamakan syarikat tertentu dalam surat sokongan kerajaan bukan kabur — ia cadangan, dan sesiapa dalam perolehan tahu betul apa maksudnya. Garis masa itu mendakwa: Shamsul keluarkan surat → Anwar tegur secara peribadi → laporan media muncul → Anwar pertahan teguran di Parlimen → ahli perniagaan dedahkan bayaran RM152K → Shamsul letak jawatan → SPRM dakwa. Pada setiap peringkat, Anwar ketinggalan — bertindak balas terhadap tekanan dan bukannya memimpin akauntabiliti.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Surat sokongan daripada pejabat politik adalah salah satu mekanisme naungan tertua Malaysia. Di bawah BN, setiausaha politik secara rutin mengkomersialkan kedekatan mereka dengan kuasa. Di bawah Muhyiddin, pembantu Ahmad Maslan menghadapi tuduhan rasuah. Coraknya konsisten merentasi pentadbiran: pembantu politik beroperasi dalam zon kelabu pengaruh, dan PM memejamkan mata sehingga tekanan media memaksa tindakan.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Setiausaha politik kanan anda didakwa rasuah kerana mengeluarkan surat sokongan daripada pejabat anda. Jawapan pertama anda teguran peribadi. Jawapan kedua anda panggil surat itu “kabur.” Jawapan ketiga anda — selepas sogokan RM152,000 terdedah — menerima peletakan jawatan yang sepatutnya dituntut berminggu sebelumnya. Laksanakan perisytiharan harta wajib untuk semua lantikan politik. Haramkan surat sokongan dari pejabat menteri. Atau terus menemui rasuah dalam pejabat sendiri selepas media melakukan kerja anda.

Data Centre Boom Drains Water and Power — Johor Told to Wait Until 2027 for Water Supply

Said by: Johor state government and Anwar Ibrahim

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s data centre gold rush hit a wall in late 2025 when Johor — ground zero for the country’s silicon valley ambitions — told investors to postpone water-cooled projects for at least 18 months because the state couldn’t supply enough water. By February 2026, Anwar froze all non-AI data centre approvals entirely. Between 2023 and 2025, the government had approved billions in data centre projects from Microsoft, Google, AWS, Oracle, ByteDance, and GDS Holdings — without ensuring the infrastructure could support them. Malaysia’s national non-revenue water rate is 37.1%, meaning over a third of treated water is lost to leaking pipes before reaching consumers.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The government celebrated billions in data centre investment commitments as proof that the Madani economy was attracting global capital. Now it’s telling those same investors to wait because there’s no water and not enough electricity. You cannot approve massive industrial projects that each consume as much water as a small city without ensuring the infrastructure can support them. This is basic governance, and the government failed at it because investment announcements make better headlines than infrastructure audits. The freeze creates investor uncertainty at exactly the wrong moment — companies that committed billions based on government approvals are now being told to wait.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government simultaneously approved a Johor special economic zone designed to attract even more industrial investment, creating a direct conflict between economic ambition and infrastructure reality. Malaysia loses 37.1% of treated water to leaking pipes. States like Perlis and Kelantan waste 50-60%. Building water-intensive industries on top of a crumbling water system is negligence. The irony is suffocating: the government that can’t maintain water pipes for its own citizens approved water-guzzling data centres for foreign corporations, then had to tell both groups to wait. Governance by headline, failure by infrastructure.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a pattern of approving mega-projects before building the infrastructure to support them. The Iskandar Malaysia development zone promised a “world-class city” but still struggles with basic services. Forest City was approved as a RM400 billion development but sits largely empty. Each time, the government prioritises investment announcements over infrastructure planning, then scrambles when reality catches up. The data centre freeze is the latest iteration of the same pattern: approve first, plan never.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You approved billions in data centre investment without ensuring water and electricity supply. Now you’re telling investors to wait 18 months. Fix the pipes first — 37.1% non-revenue water is a national embarrassment. Publish a national infrastructure capacity assessment before approving any new industrial projects. Stop announcing investment wins that you can’t deliver on. Or keep building a silicon valley on a foundation of leaking pipes and overloaded grids — and watch investors move to Thailand and Indonesia instead.

PM's Political Secretary Caught Issuing Support Letters for Hospital Contractors — Anwar Says a Reprimand Is Enough

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 18 November 2025, Anwar confirmed in Parliament that he had reprimanded his senior political secretary, Shamsul Iskandar Mohd Akin, over support letters listing six contractors for a hospital project in Johor. Anwar described the letters as “vague.” On 25 November, Shamsul resigned after mounting media pressure and calls from Rafizi Ramli for a MACC investigation. A Sabah businessman then claimed he provided RM152,000 to Shamsul — money allegedly spent on home furniture, premium cigars, and tailored suits. On 4 December 2025, MACC charged Shamsul with corruption over RM176,829 in gratification related to the hospital project.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The PM’s office was literally issuing contractor support letters. This is the swamp Anwar promised to drain. Shamsul Iskandar was not a minor figure — he was a PKR veteran since the party’s founding, operating within the Prime Minister’s Department with direct influence over government procurement. When your own political secretary is funnelling contractor names to procurement officers and receiving RM152,000 in kickbacks spent on premium cigars and tailored suits, the anti-corruption brand is not just damaged — it is a punchline.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s first instinct was to minimise. “The letters were vague.” “A reprimand is sufficient.” The Edge reported that the support letters listed specific contractors by name — contradicting Anwar’s claim. Naming specific companies in a government support letter is not vague — it is a recommendation, and anyone in procurement knows exactly what it means. The timeline is damning: Shamsul issues letters → Anwar reprimands privately → media reports emerge → Anwar defends the reprimand in Parliament → businessman reveals RM152K payments → Shamsul resigns → MACC charges him. At every stage, Anwar was behind the curve — reacting to pressure rather than leading on accountability. If Rafizi had not publicly demanded action, would Anwar have done anything beyond a private reprimand? The evidence suggests no.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Support letters from political offices are one of Malaysia’s oldest patronage mechanisms. Under BN, political secretaries routinely monetised their proximity to power. Najib’s aide Jho Low operated with impunity for years before 1MDB collapsed. Under Muhyiddin, aide Ahmad Maslan faced corruption charges. The pattern is consistent across administrations: political aides operate in a grey zone of influence, and PMs look the other way until media pressure forces action. PH promised to end this culture. Instead, they replicated it — different party, same premium cigars.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your senior political secretary was charged with corruption for issuing support letters from your office. Your first response was a private reprimand. Your second response was to call the letters “vague.” Your third response — after RM152,000 in kickbacks surfaced — was to accept a resignation you should have demanded weeks earlier. Implement mandatory asset declarations for all political appointees. Ban support letters from ministerial offices. Publish procurement records for every project where support letters were issued. Or keep discovering corruption in your own office after the media does your job for you.

Projek ini wujud di atas kertas untuk mewajarkan kewujudan PTMP dan penambakan tanah yang sepatutnya membiayainya. Terowong ini adalah infrastruktur Schrödinger — serentak aktif dan tidak wujud.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: PTMP dikritik sejak awal kerana perolehan tidak telus — menggunakan mekanisme permintaan cadangan dan bukannya tender terbuka. Penambakan Kepulauan Selatan Pulau Pinang dirancang sebagai mekanisme pembiayaan untuk PTMP. Jadi logiknya: musnahkan laut untuk membiayai terowong yang belum dibina dalam 14 tahun. Setiap projek mewajarkan yang lain; tiada yang menyampaikan untuk orang ramai.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Empat belas tahun. RM6.3 bilion. Sifar meter dibina. Bekas KM dibicarakan atas rasuah berkaitan projek. KM semasa berkeras ia ‘masih aktif.’ Pada tahap mana anda akui projek ini gagal? Batalkan, audit perbelanjaan, dan salurkan semula dana kepada penyelesaian pengangkutan yang boleh dibina — transit bas pantas, infrastruktur berbasikal, pengurusan trafik. Pengunjung Pulau Pinang perlukan penyelesaian, bukan terowong hantu.

Fourteen years later, not a single metre of tunnel has been constructed. Meanwhile, the corruption trial of former CM Lim Guan Eng over the project continues, with MACC witnesses describing it as ‘akin to a land scam.’

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A RM6.3 billion project proposed 14 years ago with zero construction is either a failed project or a phantom one. The project exists on paper to justify the PTMP’s existence and the land reclamation that was supposed to fund it. The tunnel is Schrödinger’s infrastructure — simultaneously active and non-existent.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The PTMP was criticised from the start for its opaque procurement — using request-for-proposal mechanisms instead of open tenders. 🎯 PH CALLOUT: Fourteen years. RM6.3 billion. Zero metres built. Former CM on trial for corruption related to the project. Current CM insists it’s ‘still active.’ At what point do you admit this project is a failure? Cancel it, audit the spending, and redirect the funds to transport solutions that can actually be built — bus rapid transit, cycling infrastructure, traffic management. Penang’s commuters need solutions, not phantom tunnels.

[BM] AS Isytihar 'Kemenangan Terhadap China' Selepas Dapat Ikrar Mineral Tanpa Had Dari Anwar

Said by: MalaysiaNow report

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-03

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 3 November 2025, MalaysiaNow melaporkan bahawa Amerika Syarikat telah “mengisytiharkan kemenangan terhadap China” selepas mendapat ikrar daripada Anwar Ibrahim untuk tidak menyekat eksport mineral kritikal dan unsur nadir bumi ke AS. 🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Indonesia menyekat eksport nikel pada 2020 dan menggunakan leverage itu untuk membina industri pemprosesan domestik yang kini bernilai berbilion dolar — menarik pelaburan pelebur, mencipta puluhan ribu pekerjaan, dan bergerak naik dalam rantaian nilai. Malaysia hanya bersetuju untuk terus menghantar bahan mentah ke AS. Satu negara menggunakan sumbernya untuk industrialisasi. Yang lain bersetuju kekal sebagai pembekal bahan mentah. Kawalan eksport nadir bumi adalah alat geopolitik utama — mungkin yang paling berkuasa yang dimiliki negara kaya sumber. China menguasai 60% perlombongan nadir bumi global dan 90% pemprosesan. Malaysia menyerahkan alat strategik yang sama sebagai pertukaran untuk pengurangan tarif dari 25% kepada 19%.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: Apabila AS “mengisytiharkan kemenangan terhadap China” menggunakan sumber anda, anda bukan rakan kongsi — anda adalah bidak. Pembingkaian itu menjelaskan segala-galanya: ini mengenai kepentingan strategik AS, dan Malaysia adalah alatnya. Kos jangka panjang tidak boleh diukur. Mineral kritikal adalah terhad. Sebaik sahaja komited kepada eksport tanpa sekatan, Malaysia tidak boleh kemudian mengenakan kawalan tanpa melanggar ART dan mencetuskan tindakan balas AS. Negara telah mengunci dirinya ke dalam kebergantungan bahan mentah.
