[BM] Rang Undang-Undang Had Penggal PM PH Sendiri Gagal Dua Undi — 8 AP Kerajaan Sendiri Tidak Hadir

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister / Anthony Loke, DAP Secretary-General

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 2 Mac 2026, Dewan Rakyat Malaysia mengundi Rang Undang-Undang Perlembagaan (Pindaan) 2026 — reformasi lama dijanjikan PH untuk mengehadkan penggal perdana menteri kepada 10 tahun (dua penggal). Rang undang-undang memerlukan 148 undi — majoriti besar dua pertiga — untuk diluluskan. Ia mendapat 146. Dua undi kurang. Yang Dipertua Dewan Tan Sri Johari Abdul mengesahkan 44 AP berkecuali (kebanyakan pembangkang), 32 tidak hadir, dan — dalam butiran yang sangat bermakna — tiada seorang AP pun mengundi menentang. Di antara mereka yang tidak hadir ialah 8 AP penyokong kerajaan. Menteri Komunikasi Fahmi Fadzil kemudian mengesahkan hanya 2 daripada 8 yang mengemukakan penjelasan rasmi; 6 tidak ambil kisah pun. Setiausaha Agung DAP Anthony Loke mengeluarkan kenyataan tegas menekankan kesemua 40 AP DAP hadir, dan secara terang-terangan menegur kerusi kosong.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Ini sepatutnya menjadi reformasi institusi tandatangan PH — tunjang janji manifesto sejak pilihan raya umum 2022, lahir terus daripada trauma pemerintahan 22 tahun Mahathir. Had penggal PM adalah salah satu perlindungan demokrasi paling penting yang boleh digubal oleh mana-mana kerajaan. Dan PH tidak mampu pastikan gabungannya sendiri hadir. Rang undang-undang tidak ditewaskan oleh pembangkang — ia dibunuh oleh sikap tidak peduli, ketidakcekapan, dan ketidakhadiran dari dalam barisan kerajaan sendiri. AP PKR Wong Chen kemudian dedahkan sekurang-kurangnya seorang AP tidak hadir berkata beliau “langsung tidak diberitahu dengan betul untuk hadir” dan tidak menerima panggilan susulan. Ini bukan sekadar kegagalan perundangan — ia kegagalan tadbir urus yang mendedahkan kekosongan jenama reformasi PH.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

PH mempunyai tiga tahun untuk bersiap sedia bagi pengundian ini. Tiga tahun untuk mengawal whip AP sendiri, mengira bilangan, mengesahkan kehadiran, dan menyampaikan keperluan paling asas demokrasi berparlimen — hadir. Timbalan PM Fadillah Yusof adalah ketua whip kerajaan. Tugasnya satu-satunya dalam konteks ini adalah memastikan setiap AP kerajaan hadir. Beliau gagal. Dan alasan AP yang tidak hadir — “saya tidak diberitahu dengan betul” — sangat mengejutkan dengan sikap sambil lewanya. Ini pindaan perlembagaan, bukan taklimat bangku belakang tentang dasar saliran. Jika PH tidak mampu menggerakkan APnya sendiri untuk rang undang-undang reformasi utamanya sendiri, maka agenda reformasi bukan keutamaan — ia slogan. Pembangkang berkecuali, seperti dijangka. Tetapi AP kerajaan yang tidak hadir yang membunuh rang undang-undang ini.

📜 Kes-kes Terdahulu

Malaysia tidak pernah mempunyai had penggal berkanun untuk perdana menteri. Mahathir berkhidmat 22 tahun (1981–2003), kemudian kembali untuk 22 bulan lagi (2018–2020). Kerosakan penggal eksekutif tanpa semakan didokumentasikan dengan baik dalam tadbir urus Malaysia — dari pengukuhan rangkaian naungan hingga penghakisan kebebasan institusi. Manifesto PH 2022 secara nyata menjanjikan reformasi ini. Dalam ucapan Belanjawan 2024, Anwar menegaskan semula komitmen kerajaan terhadap reformasi institusi. Namun apabila tiba masanya menyampaikan, disiplin gabungan PH — kelemahan berterusan sejak Langkah Sheraton — sekali lagi terbukti tidak mencukupi. Ini gabungan yang sama yang kehilangan kerajaannya sendiri pada 2020 kerana tidak mampu mengekalkan barisannya.

🎯 Teguran Kepada PH

Anda membuat janji ini. Anda masukkannya dalam manifesto. Anda beritahu pengundi Malaysia bahawa mengehadkan penggal PM penting untuk mencegah pengukuhan kuasa gaya Mahathir. Dan apabila saat tiba — apabila 148 undi sahaja yang anda perlukan dari dewan 222 kerusi di mana anda menguasai majoriti yang selesa — anda tidak mampu membawa orang anda sendiri masuk ke dewan. Enam daripada lapan AP anda yang tidak hadir tidak menjelaskan mengapa mereka tiada. Fahmi kata “tiada tindakan diambil.” Sudah tentu tidak. Kerana dalam PH, janji reformasi adalah untuk kempen, bukan untuk parlimen. Anda ada satu tugas. Anda gagal. Dan setiap rakyat Malaysia yang percaya retorik reformasi anda kini membayar harga untuk ketidakcekapan anda.

Anwar Condemns US-Israel Strikes on Iran in Parliament — 'We Can No Longer Swallow' Western Human Rights Lectures, PH MP Calls for US Embassy Boycott

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Syahredzan Johan (PH-Bangi)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 2 March 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim tabled an emergency motion in the Dewan Rakyat condemning the US and Israel’s strikes on Iran, which began on 28 February and killed at least 201 people including Iran’s Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei.

Anwar called the strikes a “flagrant violation of international law” and described Western lectures on human rights and democracy as hypocrisy:

“In my engagements with the American and European leadership, I am uneasy about their lectures on human rights and democracy. We can no longer swallow such arguments from them.”

The motion passed unanimously with bipartisan support. Opposition Leader Hamzah Zainudin praised Anwar for a “stern and firm statement.”

But the most politically significant moment came from PH backbencher Syahredzan Johan (Bangi), who called on all MPs to boycott events hosted by the US embassy in Kuala Lumpur:

“Let’s not go. Even though we get invited to their Independence Day celebrations, let’s not go. We need to show that we can no longer accept what they do.”

Anwar also signaled a diplomatic repositioning toward Iran, calling for closer education and trade ties, claiming Malaysia was “the only country to show a firm, brave stance.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

The condemnation itself is unremarkable — Malaysia has consistently supported Palestinian and Iranian sovereignty. What matters is the escalation in rhetoric and the gap between words and actions.

First, Anwar calling Western human rights lectures “hypocrisy” is rich coming from a prime minister whose own government is under fire for:

  • Protecting MACC chief Azam Baki despite multiple corruption allegations
  • Using police investigations to target critics under the guise of a “Zionist plot”
  • Halving Najib’s sentence while the 1MDB debt still runs into billions
  • Failing to pass even basic institutional reforms like the PM tenure bill Anwar is lecturing the West about hypocrisy while running a government that has abandoned virtually every reform promise it made.

Second, Syahredzan’s call to boycott the US embassy is performative populism with real diplomatic consequences. Malaysia’s economy depends on US trade and investment. The US is Malaysia’s third-largest trading partner. Calling for a diplomatic boycott from the floor of Parliament is not a principled stand — it’s cheap political theatre that someone else will have to clean up.

Third, the pivot toward Iran is diplomatically reckless. Iran’s Shia denomination is officially considered “deviant” in Sunni-majority Malaysia. Anwar is signaling solidarity with a theocratic state whose religious identity his own government criminalises. The contradiction is absurd.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Let’s count the contradictions:

On human rights hypocrisy:

  • Anwar says he can “no longer swallow” Western lectures on human rights. This is the same man who spent years lobbying Western governments, NGOs, and media outlets for his own human rights — during his imprisonment under Mahathir and Najib. His entire political comeback was built on international sympathy and foreign advocacy.

  • Now that he’s in power, foreign advocacy is a “Zionist plot” and Western human rights standards are “hypocrisy.” The only thing that changed is which side of the PM’s desk he sits on. On the US embassy boycott:

  • Syahredzan Johan is a PH backbencher calling for a diplomatic boycott while his own government maintains full diplomatic, trade, and military relations with the US. Malaysia just signed a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership with Washington in October 2025.

  • If PH is serious about opposing US actions, why stop at embassy cocktail parties? Cancel the trade deal. Recall the ambassador. Oh wait — that would cost money. On Iran solidarity:

  • Malaysia criminalises Shia practice. Anwar’s own religious affairs apparatus treats Shia Muslims as deviants. Declaring solidarity with Iran while persecuting its co-religionists at home is not principled — it’s opportunistic.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

  • 2018-2022: Anwar’s own foreign lobbying. Anwar and PKR allies engaged Washington consultancies, international media, and foreign governments to pressure the Najib and Muhyiddin administrations. He accepted this support gratefully. Now foreign engagement is subversion.
  • October 2025: Malaysia-US Comprehensive Strategic Partnership. Just five months before calling US human rights lectures “hypocrisy,” Anwar’s government signed an upgraded bilateral deal with Washington covering trade, critical minerals, and market access.
  • Malaysia’s track record on Iran. Malaysia has historically been cautious with Iran due to the Sunni-Shia divide. Anwar’s pivot is a departure from decades of careful diplomatic hedging, driven by the emotional politics of the moment rather than strategic calculation.
  • 2024-2025: Gaza solidarity without substance. Malaysia has been vocal on Palestine but has taken no meaningful economic or diplomatic action against Israel beyond rhetoric. The pattern is clear: loud words, zero follow-through.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

Anwar, you told Parliament: “We can no longer swallow such arguments from them.”

Fair enough. But the rakyat can no longer swallow yours either.

You lecture the West on hypocrisy while your MACC chief sits on RM800,000 in undisclosed shares. You call foreign advocacy a “Zionist plot” after spending decades doing exactly the same thing. You signal solidarity with Iran while your religious authorities criminalise Shia practice at home.

And your backbencher calls for boycotting US embassy parties — the absolute lowest-cost, lowest-risk form of protest imaginable — while your government maintains a full strategic partnership with Washington.

This isn’t principled foreign policy. It’s Ramadan-season populism. You’re performing outrage for domestic consumption while doing nothing that would actually cost you anything.

If you want to be taken seriously on human rights and sovereignty, start at home. Fix your own institutions. Reform your own enforcement agencies. Stop using conspiracy theories to silence critics. Then — and only then — will anyone take your lectures to the West seriously.

Anwar's Coalition Can't Pass Its Own Reform Bill — Loses PM Term Limit Vote by Two, Promises to 'Try Again in June'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Pakatan Harapan Coalition

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 What Happened

On March 2, Anwar Ibrahim’s unity government failed to pass a constitutional amendment that would have limited the prime minister’s tenure to 10 years — a flagship reform promise from PH’s 2022 election manifesto. The bill received 146 votes, just two short of the 148 needed for a two-thirds majority. Forty-four MPs abstained and 32 were absent. Deputy Speaker Alice Lau confirmed eight government and independent MPs did not participate. The government has since pledged to reintroduce the bill in June, with coalition leaders promising their MPs will “actually attend this time.”

🔥 Why It Matters

This was supposed to be the easy win. The PM term limit was popular, symbolically loaded, and politically safe — designed to show that PH can still deliver on reform. Instead, Anwar’s coalition, which holds a supermajority in the 222-seat Dewan Rakyat, couldn’t even whip its own members into the chamber. The failure exposes a deeper rot: three years into government, PH cannot translate its parliamentary numbers into legislative outcomes. If the coalition can’t pass a bill it spent weeks championing, what hope is there for harder reforms — anti-hopping enforcement, IPCMC, procurement transparency, or judicial independence?

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

A government with a supermajority losing its own reform bill by two votes is not bad luck — it’s bad management. Either the whips failed to do their jobs, or parts of the coalition quietly sabotaged the vote. An unnamed PH lawmaker told the SCMP: “That bill should have easily passed. It feels like there was not enough effort to make sure everyone turned up.” That’s not an explanation — it’s a confession. And the government’s response? “We’ll try again in June.” That’s the reform equivalent of hitting snooze. Meanwhile, cameras caught Anwar mid-laugh after the vote was read out, chatting with his deputy as if his flagship bill hadn’t just died on the floor.

📜 Previous Cases

This is a pattern. PH promised IPCMC — shelved. Promised freedom of information — nothing. Promised anti-hopping law enforcement — selective at best. Promised political funding reform — in committee limbo. The 2022 manifesto was a 60-page wishlist; three years on, the hardest items remain untouched and even the easy ones are fumbled. The PM term limit was literally the lowest-hanging fruit — popular across party lines, personally backed by Anwar, and requiring only a supermajority PH already had on paper.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysia you were different. You ran on “Reformasi.” You had the numbers, the mandate, and the public goodwill. And you couldn’t get your own MPs into the room to vote for a bill you spent weeks selling to the country. If you can’t pass popular, symbolic reforms with a supermajority, what exactly are you using that majority for? “We’ll try again in June” is not a reform agenda — it’s an admission that you don’t have control of your own coalition.

PH's Own PM Term Limit Bill Fails by Two Votes — 8 Government MPs Didn't Even Show Up

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister / Anthony Loke, DAP Secretary-General

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 What Happened

On March 2, 2026, Malaysia’s Dewan Rakyat voted on the Constitution (Amendment) Bill 2026 — PH’s long-promised reform to limit the prime minister’s tenure to 10 years (two terms). The bill needed 148 votes — a two-thirds supermajority — to pass. It got 146. Two votes short. Speaker Tan Sri Johari Abdul confirmed that 44 MPs abstained (mostly opposition), 32 were absent, and — in a telling detail — not a single MP voted against it. Among the absentees were 8 government-supporting MPs. Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil later confirmed that only 2 of the 8 had submitted formal explanations; 6 had not even bothered. DAP Secretary-General Anthony Loke issued a strongly worded statement emphasising that all 40 DAP MPs were present, and pointedly called out the empty seats.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was supposed to be PH’s signature institutional reform — the centrepiece of a manifesto promise dating back to the 2022 general election, born directly from the trauma of Mahathir’s 22-year reign. A PM term limit is one of the most consequential democratic safeguards any government can enact. And PH couldn’t even get its own coalition to show up. The bill wasn’t defeated by opposition — it was killed by indifference, incompetence, and absenteeism from within the government’s own ranks. PKR MP Wong Chen later revealed that at least one absent MP said he “was simply not properly informed to turn up” and received no follow-up calls. This is not just a legislative failure — it is a governance failure that exposes the hollowness of PH’s reform brand.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH had three years to prepare for this vote. Three years to whip their own MPs, count heads, confirm attendance, and deliver the most basic requirement of parliamentary democracy — showing up. Deputy PM Fadillah Yusof is the government chief whip. His one job in this context was to ensure every government MP was present. He failed. And the excuse from absent MPs — “I wasn’t properly informed” — is breathtaking in its casualness. This was a constitutional amendment, not a backbench briefing on drainage policy. If PH cannot mobilise its own MPs for its own flagship reform bill, then the reform agenda is not a priority — it is a slogan. The opposition abstained, as expected. But the government’s absentees are the ones who killed this bill.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has never had a statutory term limit for the prime minister. Mahathir served 22 years (1981–2003), then returned for another 22 months (2018–2020). The damage of unchecked executive tenure is well-documented in Malaysian governance — from the entrenchment of patronage networks to the erosion of institutional independence. PH’s 2022 manifesto explicitly promised this reform. In the 2024 Budget speech, Anwar reaffirmed the government’s commitment to institutional reform. Yet when it came time to deliver, PH’s coalition discipline — a perennial weakness since the Sheraton Move — once again proved inadequate. This is the same coalition that lost its own government in 2020 because it couldn’t hold its ranks together.

🎯 PH Callout

You made this promise. You put it in your manifesto. You told Malaysian voters that limiting PM tenure was essential to prevent another Mahathir-style power entrenchment. And when the moment came — when 148 votes were all you needed from a 222-seat house where you command a comfortable majority — you couldn’t even get your own people to walk into the chamber. Six of your eight absent MPs haven’t even explained why they weren’t there. Fahmi says “no action has been taken.” Of course it hasn’t. Because in PH, reform promises are for campaigns, not for parliament. You had one job. You failed. And every Malaysian who believed your reformasi rhetoric is now paying the price for your incompetence.

PKR Hired US Consultants to Lobby Washington Against Najib — Now Cries Foul When Others Do the Same

Said by: Sivarasa Rasiah, Former PKR Vice-President (confirmed); PKR leadership

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

In March 2026, MalaysiaNow published leaked emails revealing that PKR had engaged a Washington-based political consultancy firm in 2016 to lobby the United States to exert pressure on then-Prime Minister Najib Razak. The emails included an 84-page dossier making serious allegations against Najib, with the goal of getting Washington to publicly rebuke the Malaysian government.

Former PKR vice-president Sivarasa Rasiah confirmed the communications, saying party leaders including himself were involved. He framed it as legitimate advocacy to free Anwar Ibrahim, who was then a political prisoner.

The revelation came as Anwar’s government was simultaneously accusing former finance minister Daim Zainuddin’s family of using foreign agents to destabilise the current administration — calling it a national security threat.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This exposes a massive double standard at the heart of PH’s current political strategy. Anwar is framing Daim’s family’s alleged foreign lobbying as treasonous — a plot to topple the government using foreign influence. But his own party did the exact same thing when they were in opposition.

Opposition MP Shahidan Kassim pressed Anwar in Parliament to explain the difference. The answer matters because the government is using the Daim case to justify expanded national security rhetoric, deflect from the Azam Baki scandal, and paint critics as foreign agents. If PKR’s own lobbying was legitimate, then the same standard must apply to others.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Sivarasa’s defence — “we were trying to free a political prisoner” — is convenient but doesn’t hold up under scrutiny. The 84-page dossier didn’t just ask for Anwar’s release. It made sweeping allegations against Najib and explicitly sought US intervention in Malaysian domestic politics. That’s foreign lobbying. Period.

When PKR does it, it’s “advocacy.” When Daim’s family allegedly does it, it’s a “Zionist conspiracy” and a national security threat. The double standard is so blatant that even a casual observer can see through it.

Anwar’s government is essentially saying: foreign lobbying is patriotic when we do it, and treasonous when our opponents do it. That’s not a principle — it’s a convenience.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Malaysian political parties have a long history of international lobbying. UMNO maintained Washington lobbyists for decades. Anwar himself cultivated deep relationships with US policymakers, think tanks, and media during his years in opposition. None of this was considered problematic when PH was the beneficiary.

The selective outrage follows the same pattern as PH’s treatment of press freedom (Bloomberg good when exposing Najib, Bloomberg bad when exposing Azam Baki) and institutional accountability (MACC is weaponised under BN, MACC is independent under PH).

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You hired a US firm to lobby Washington against a sitting Malaysian PM, produced an 84-page dossier of allegations, and sought American intervention in Malaysian politics. Your own former vice-president confirmed it. Now you’re telling Malaysians that when others do the same thing, it’s a Zionist-backed conspiracy to topple the government? Pick a lane. Either foreign lobbying is a legitimate part of political advocacy — in which case stop criminalising your opponents for it — or it’s a national security threat, in which case PKR’s own leaders should be investigated too. You can’t have it both ways.

Said by: Political Analysts / ISEAS / Toda Peace Institute / CSIS

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pelbagai analisis bebas daripada ISEAS, CSIS, Institut Keamanan Toda dan East Asia Forum berkumpul pada kesimpulan yang sama: gaya pemerintahan Anwar Ibrahim semakin mencerminkan gaya Mahathir Mohamad — orang yang memenjarakannya. 🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Anwar menghabiskan kehidupan politiknya sebagai ANTI-Mahathir. Seluruh naratifnya adalah: saya menderita di bawah sistem autokratik Mahathir, saya faham kejahatannya, dan saya akan membina sesuatu yang berbeza. Konvergensi antara gaya pemerintahan Anwar dan Mahathir bukan sekadar ironi — ia bukti muktamad bahawa budaya politik Malaysia, bukannya pemimpin individu, menghasilkan pemerintahan autokratik.
