[BM] Janji Mansuh Tol Dimungkiri — Kerajaan Bayar RM591J Untuk BEKU Kenaikan Sahaja

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-18

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: PH berjanji untuk memansuhkan tol lebuhraya dalam kedua-dua PRU14 dan PRU15. Sebaliknya, pada Disember 2025, kerajaan mengumumkan ia akan membelanjakan RM591 juta semata-mata untuk menghalang kenaikan tol di 10 lebuhraya pada 2026 — membayar syarikat konsesi dengan wang awam supaya tidak menaikkan harga. Menteri Kerja Raya mengakui memansuhkan tol sepenuhnya akan menelan belanja “berbilion.” Gerakan mengejek janji yang dimungkiri. FMT bertanya sama ada Anwar akan “mengulangi ikrar PH untuk memansuhkan tol” menjelang PRU16. Pemandu Malaysia terus membayar tol DAN cukai mereka kini mensubsidi pembekuan itu. Tempoh bebas tol perayaan juga dibatalkan.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Pemansuhan tol adalah salah satu janji manifesto PH yang paling konkrit dan boleh diukur — cukup spesifik sehingga pengundi boleh mengesahkan penyampaian dengan mudah. Realitinya: bukan sahaja tol tidak dimansuhkan, kerajaan membayar syarikat konsesi lebuhraya ratusan juta setiap tahun semata-mata untuk membekukan kenaikan kadar. RM591J yang dibelanjakan untuk pembekuan boleh membiayai penambahbaikan infrastruktur sebenar. Sebaliknya, ia mengalir kepada pemegang saham konsesi — syarikat yang mempunyai hubungan politik yang mengaut keuntungan daripada orang awam yang terperangkap.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: Pengakuan Menteri Kerja Raya bahawa pemansuhan akan menelan belanja “berbilion” adalah pengakuan bahawa PH membuat janji itu dengan mengetahui ia tidak boleh ditunaikan. Kamu tidak boleh berkempen atas pemansuhan tol dua kali, memenangi kerajaan dua kali, dan kemudian kata ia terlalu mahal. Sama ada kamu tahu kosnya sebelum berjanji (dalam kes itu kamu berbohong kepada pengundi) atau kamu tidak ambil peduli untuk mengiranya (dalam kes itu kamu tidak cekap). Sistem tol mewakili cukai regresif terhadap mobiliti — pekerja berpendapatan rendah yang berulang-alik jarak jauh ke kilang membayar bahagian pendapatan yang tidak seimbang untuk tol. Jawapan PH terhadap ketidaksamaan ini: bayar konsesi dengan wang cukai untuk mengekalkan status quo.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Janji pemansuhan tol telah dibuat oleh setiap gabungan pembangkang sejak awal 2000-an. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) juga tidak memansuhkan tol — walaupun 22 bulan berkuasa. Perjanjian konsesi, yang banyak dianugerahkan di bawah pentadbiran terdahulu melalui proses yang dipersoalkan, distrukturkan untuk menjamin keuntungan swasta dengan belanja awam. Tiada kerajaan yang mempunyai kehendak politik untuk berunding semula atau membeli balik konsesi.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Kamu berjanji untuk memansuhkan tol. Dua kali. Dalam dua pilihan raya umum. Kamu kini sudah berkuasa lebih tiga tahun. Tol masih ada. Kamu membelanjakan RM591J wang awam untuk MEMBEKUKAN mereka — bukan memansuhkan, membekukan. RM591J itu pergi kepada pemegang saham konsesi, bukan kepada rakyat. Berhenti membuat janji yang kamu tahu tidak boleh ditepati. Jika pemansuhan tol tidak mampu ditanggung, katakan secara jujur — dan terangkan pelan alternatif kamu. Atau lebih baik lagi, rundingan semula perjanjian konsesi yang mengunci Malaysia untuk membayar syarikat swasta selama-lamanya.

Toll Abolition Promise Broken — Govt Pays RM591M to FREEZE Hikes Instead

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: PH promised to abolish highway tolls in both GE14 and GE15. Instead, in December 2025, the government announced it would spend RM591 million just to prevent toll increases on 10 highways in 2026 — paying concessionaires with public money to not raise prices. The Works Minister admitted that fully abolishing tolls would cost “billions.” Gerakan mocked the broken promise. FMT asked whether Anwar would “repeat PH’s pledge to abolish tolls” ahead of GE16. Malaysian drivers continue paying tolls AND their taxes now subsidise the freeze. Festive toll-free periods were also cancelled.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Toll abolition was one of PH’s most concrete, measurable manifesto promises — specific enough that voters could easily verify delivery. The reality: not only were tolls not abolished, the government is paying highway concessionaires hundreds of millions annually just to freeze rate hikes. The RM591M spent on freezing could have funded actual infrastructure improvements. Instead, it flows to concessionaire shareholders — politically connected companies that profit from a captive public. This is the worst possible outcome: broken promise, continued tolls, AND public money transferred to private companies.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The Works Minister’s admission that abolition would cost “billions” is an admission that PH made the promise knowing it was undeliverable. You can’t campaign on toll abolition twice, win government twice, and then say it costs too much. Either you knew the cost before promising (in which case you lied to voters) or you didn’t bother calculating it (in which case you’re incompetent). The toll system represents a regressive tax on mobility — lower-income workers who commute long distances to factory jobs pay a disproportionate share of their income on tolls. PH’s response to this inequality: pay concessionaires with tax money to maintain the status quo.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The toll abolition promise has been made by every opposition coalition since the early 2000s. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) didn’t abolish tolls either — despite 22 months in government. The concession agreements, many awarded under previous administrations through questionable processes, are structured to guarantee private profits at public expense. No government has had the political will to renegotiate or buy back concessions. The concessionaires know this, which is why they keep raising toll rates and governments keep paying them not to.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You promised to abolish tolls. Twice. In two general elections. You’ve now been in power for over three years. Tolls are still there. You’re spending RM591M of public money to FREEZE them — not abolish them, freeze them. That RM591M goes to concessionaire shareholders, not to the rakyat. Stop making promises you know you can’t keep. If toll abolition is unaffordable, say so honestly — and explain your alternative plan. Or better yet, renegotiate the concession agreements that lock Malaysia into paying private companies forever. You won’t, because the concessionaires are politically connected. So the rakyat pays twice: once at the toll booth, once through taxes.

Date: 2025-12-17

Menjelang Februari 2026, kurang daripada dua bulan kemudian, spekulasi semakin meningkat bahawa PRU16 boleh dipanggil seawal Julai 2026. Sudah tentu beliau sedang memikirkannya. Pilihan raya Sabah pada November 2025 — di mana PH dihancurkan — sudah memaksa pertimbangan semula strategik. Kekacauan pilihan raya parti PKR, pemergian Rafizi, dan rombakan kabinet semuanya adalah langkah yang hanya masuk akal jika kamu sedang mengatur kedudukan untuk pilihan raya umum awal.

Anwar memberitahu wartawan apa yang mereka mahu dengar — “tumpuan pada pembaharuan, tumpuan pada kos sara hidup” — sementara operasi politiknya sudah mengira unjuran kerusi dan aritmetik gabungan.

Muhyiddin menafikan merancang pilihan raya negeri Melaka dan Johor sehingga ia dipanggil. Ia tradisi politik Malaysia: berbohong tentang masa pilihan raya, kemudian panggil pilihan raya apabila ia menguntungkan secara strategik.

Anwar melakukan tepat apa yang dikritiknya tentang pendahulunya.

🎯 SERUAN PH

Kamu beritahu media pada Disember bahawa pilihan raya “masih jauh lagi.” Dua bulan kemudian, gabungan kamu bersiap sedia untuk pilihan raya kilat. Sama ada kamu berbohong pada Disember atau kamu membuat keputusan demokratik besar secara tergesa-gesa. Kedua-duanya tidak boleh diterima untuk PM yang mendakwa berbeza daripada pendahulunya.

Anwar Says 'Long Way to Go' on Elections in December 2025 — Now Preparing Snap GE16 for 2026

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-17

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On December 17, 2025, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told editors and senior journalists at a briefing in Seri Perdana that he had ruled out any possibility of snap general elections in 2026. “The election still has a long way to go. I have not thought about it,” he said. “I am focusing on how we continue to implement reforms and alleviate the problems people face regarding the higher cost of living.”

By February 2026, less than two months later, speculation was mounting that GE16 could be called as early as July 2026. CNA reported that party insiders and civil servants confirmed preparations were underway. Reuters reported in March 2026 that coalition partners were already positioning themselves for snap polls.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This is a PM lying to the press about his election timeline. In December, he said elections were “a long way to go” and he hadn’t even thought about it. By February, his own party machinery was gearing up for a snap election. The current mandate doesn’t expire until February 2028 — calling elections nearly two years early is a major strategic decision that doesn’t happen overnight.

Anwar either lied to the press in December, or he made a radical strategic shift in under two months. Neither explanation inspires confidence. If he lied, it’s calculated deception of the public. If he genuinely hadn’t considered it, then the snap election push was driven by panic over the Azam Baki scandal, the PKR internal fractures, and coalition instability — not strategic planning.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

“I have not thought about it.” That’s what the PM said in December. The same PM who is the chairman of his party, the head of his coalition, and a career politician who has been thinking about elections every day for 30 years.

Of course he was thinking about it. The Sabah election in November 2025 — where PH was routed — had already forced a strategic reckoning. The PKR party election mess, Rafizi’s departure, and the cabinet reshuffle were all moves that only make sense if you’re positioning for an early general election.

Anwar told journalists what they wanted to hear — “focus on reforms, focus on cost of living” — while his political operation was already calculating seat projections and coalition arithmetic. This is not a man who hasn’t thought about elections. This is a man who doesn’t want you to know he’s thinking about elections.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Every Malaysian PM who has called snap elections has denied planning them right up until the announcement. Najib denied planning GE14 until he dissolved Parliament. Muhyiddin denied planning the Melaka and Johor state elections until they were called. It’s a Malaysian political tradition: lie about election timing, then call elections when it’s strategically advantageous.

Anwar is doing exactly what he criticised his predecessors for doing. The reformist is following the BN playbook.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You told the press in December that elections were “a long way to go.” Two months later, your coalition is gearing up for snap polls. Either you lied in December or you’re making major democratic decisions on the fly. Neither is acceptable for a PM who claims to be different from his predecessors. If you’re going to call an early election, at least be honest about why — don’t pretend it’s about reform when it’s about survival.

Hannah Yeoh Appointment Sparks Race-Based Backlash — PAS and UMNO Both Object

Said by: Takiyuddin Hassan, PAS Secretary-General

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-17

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In December 2025, Anwar appointed Hannah Yeoh as Federal Territories Minister in his cabinet reshuffle. PAS Secretary-General Takiyuddin Hassan immediately objected — not to Yeoh’s qualifications, but to her race. He argued the appointment would “strengthen DAP’s influence” in urban areas and disadvantage Malays. Rightist Malay groups warned that DAP was now “controlling Malay landholding” through the Federal Territories portfolio, which oversees KL, Putrajaya, and Labuan. Even UMNO leaders quietly raised concerns. Anwar hit back: “To reject a person because of her colour and race, this is atrocious in this day and age.” Social media reactions were polarised.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The backlash exposes how normalised ethno-religious gatekeeping remains in Malaysian politics — even under a supposedly reformist government. A minister was judged not by competence, track record, or policy vision, but by ethnicity. This is the structural racism PH was elected to dismantle. Instead, the party that appointed Yeoh simultaneously downgraded other DAP portfolios in the same reshuffle to signal “Malay strength” to voters. Yeoh’s appointment was brave — but it was also the exception that proved the rule. The rest of the reshuffle was racial calculus.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The contradiction is structural: Anwar wants to appear multiracial while managing a coalition that requires Malay-first optics. He appoints one Chinese-Malaysian woman to a prominent portfolio, then compensates by downgrading DAP elsewhere and elevating UMNO ministers. The multiracial gesture is real but it is hedged — designed to be just bold enough for DAP voters, but not so bold that Malay voters feel threatened. Analyst Azmi Hassan said the reshuffle “can only be read as sending a signal to Malays that Anwar is pushing for stronger Malay presence in government.” Yeoh’s appointment is the progressive leaf on an ethno-nationalist tree.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every DAP appointment to a senior portfolio triggers the same race-based backlash. When Lim Guan Eng became Finance Minister in PH 1.0, Malay groups protested. When DAP won state seats, opponents framed it as Chinese encroachment. The cycle is depressingly predictable: appoint a non-Malay, face racial backlash, either stand firm (briefly) or retreat. Anwar stood firm on Yeoh — which is commendable — but his overall cabinet strategy still operates within the racial framework rather than dismantling it. A government that makes one multiracial appointment while conducting the rest of its governance on racial lines is not reforming race relations — it is managing optics.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Defending Hannah Yeoh’s appointment was the right thing to do. Now defend the principle behind it — that competence matters more than ethnicity — across your ENTIRE government. Stop making one multiracial appointment while conducting racial calculus everywhere else. If you believe race shouldn’t determine cabinet positions, prove it with every appointment — not just the one that gets you international praise. And if PAS objects because a minister is Chinese, call it what it is: racism. Not “cultural concern,” not “community sensitivity” — racism. You said it was “atrocious.” Now act like you mean it.

On December 16, 2025, Anwar Ibrahim announced his second cabinet reshuffle, involving 28 ministerial and deputy ministerial changes. Among the most controversial appointments:

Hannah Yeoh was named Minister in the Prime Minister’s Department (Federal Territories) — the first non-Malay to hold the portfolio. PAS and right-wing Malay groups immediately attacked the appointment, claiming it would strengthen “DAP dominance” in KL and threaten Malay interests. Anwar called the criticism “atrocious” and said race should not determine ministerial capability.

Adam Adli — a former student activist — was appointed Deputy Minister in the Prime Minister’s Department (Religious Affairs), sparking questions about whether he had the credentials or background to oversee Islamic governance matters.

The reshuffle came after months of vacancies following Rafizi and Nik Nazmi’s resignations, and was widely seen as positioning for a potential snap election.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

The Hannah Yeoh appointment exposed the fundamental contradiction of Malaysian politics: a government that claims to be multiracial but operates in a system where race determines who can hold which portfolio. The FT Minister portfolio had always been held by a senior UMNO Malay. Anwar’s decision to give it to a Chinese DAP leader was either a bold reform move or a political miscalculation that handed ammunition to the opposition.

The Adam Adli appointment raised legitimate questions about competence. Religious affairs governance requires specific knowledge of Islamic jurisprudence, state-federal religious dynamics, and the politics of religious authorities. Appointing a former student activist with no demonstrated expertise in these areas looked like a political reward, not a merit-based decision.

The Straits Times noted that “controversies cloud Malaysian PM Anwar’s new Cabinet line-up” — not the kind of headline you want when you’re positioning for a snap election.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

There’s bullshit on multiple sides here. The race-based attacks on Hannah Yeoh are deplorable — she has a strong track record across multiple portfolios. Her race should be irrelevant to her competence.

But Anwar walked into this controversy with his eyes open. He knew appointing a non-Malay FT minister would trigger a racial backlash. If he wanted to make that stand on principle, fine — but then commit to it. Don’t make the appointment and then have UMNO’s Zahid Hamidi rush out to reassure Malays that it won’t undermine the “Malay agenda.” That sends the message that the appointment needs defending on racial grounds, which undermines the very principle Anwar claimed to be championing.

And the Adam Adli appointment? That’s just patronage. Reward a loyal activist with a portfolio he’s not qualified for. It’s exactly what PH criticised BN for doing.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Every Malaysian cabinet reshuffle generates racial controversy — it’s baked into the system. But PH was supposed to transcend this. In 2018, the appointment of Lim Guan Eng as Finance Minister was attacked on racial grounds. PH defended it then on merit. Seven years later, the same party is still fighting the same battles — suggesting that PH’s “multiracial” vision has not actually shifted the political landscape.

The pattern of rewarding loyalty over competence is also not new for PH. The Nurul Izzah adviser appointment, various political secretary scandals, and now Adam Adli all follow the same logic: political loyalty first, governance competence second.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You said you’d appoint ministers based on merit, not race. Then you made an appointment that triggered a racial firestorm, and instead of standing firm, your coalition partner UMNO rushed to reassure Malays. Pick a lane. Either you believe in multiracial meritocracy — and defend it without qualifiers — or admit that race still drives your Cabinet math. And while you’re at it, explain why a former student activist is overseeing religious affairs governance. Because “loyalty” is not a qualification.

Zafrul Appointed MIDA Chairman After Leaving MITI — The Revolving Door Never Stops Spinning

Said by: Tengku Zafrul Abdul Aziz, MIDA Chairman

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-16

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: When the December 2025 cabinet reshuffle removed Tengku Zafrul from the MITI portfolio, he wasn’t sent to the backbench. Instead, Anwar appointed him as Chairman of MIDA (Malaysian Investment Development Authority) — the very agency that falls under MITI’s purview — and as Senior Political Advisor to the PM. The man who oversaw MIDA as minister now chairs it as a political appointee.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The revolving door between ministerial oversight and agency chairmanship is a textbook governance concern. Zafrul approved MIDA’s policies, budgets, and strategies as MITI Minister. Now he chairs the same agency he regulated. Investment decisions he influenced as minister — including the US trade deal’s implementation — will now be executed under his chairmanship. There is no cooling-off period, no separation, no firewall.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH campaigned on ending the revolving door between politics and GLCs. IDEAS documented 238 political appointments to GLCs and statutory bodies under Anwar — exceeding Mahathir 2.0’s rate. Zafrul’s MIDA appointment is the most brazen example: not even a pretence of distance between the ministerial role and the agency appointment. The same person, the same portfolio, different title. This is not governance reform; it’s musical chairs with name cards.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, ministers who lost portfolios were given GLC sinecures as compensation. PH criticised this practice relentlessly. Under Anwar, the practice continues with better PR — Zafrul’s appointment is framed as ‘continuity and expertise’ rather than what it is: a political reward and soft landing for a reshuffled ally.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You documented 238 political appointments. You campaigned against the GLC revolving door. Then you appointed your former MITI minister as chairman of MIDA — the agency MITI oversees. If this isn’t a political appointment, what is? Implement cooling-off periods. Create independent appointment committees. Or just admit that your patronage system works exactly like BN’s, with slightly better PR.
