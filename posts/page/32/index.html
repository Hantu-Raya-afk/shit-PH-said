Said by: Opposition MPs / Parliamentary Reform Advocates

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-01

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Parlimen sepatutnya menjadi semakan terhadap kuasa eksekutif. Kegagalan rang undang-undang had tempoh PM adalah contoh paling mendedahkan: PH mempunyai bilangan, mempunyai mandat, dan masih tidak mampu menyampaikan salah satu reformasi demokratik paling mudah — kerana AP sendiri tidak sudi hadir.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: PH secara eksplisit menjanjikan reformasi parlimen: jawatankuasa pilihan lebih kuat, Speaker bebas, peruntukan lebih besar untuk AP pembangkang, dan perbahasan bermakna ke atas undang-undang. Beberapa perubahan kecil dibuat, tetapi dinamik asas tidak berubah. Kerajaan mengawal agenda, menentukan rang undang-undang mana yang dibahas, dan menggunakan aritmetik gabungan untuk memastikan keputusan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Cap getah parlimen adalah tradisi Malaysia yang mendahului PH. Di bawah Mahathir 1.0, parlimen bersifat formaliti. [BM] Pengangguran Belia Kekal Pada 12.28% — Satu Daripada Lapan Anak Muda Malaysia Tidak Dapat Kerja

Said by: DOSM / ILO / World Bank Data

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Sehingga akhir 2025, kadar pengangguran belia Malaysia (umur 15-24) kekal terlekat pada 12.28% — kira-kira 297,500 anak muda Malaysia menganggur. Ini tiga hingga empat kali lebih tinggi daripada kadar pengangguran keseluruhan negara 3.2%. Data DOSM, ILO, dan Bank Dunia semuanya mengesahkan sifat struktur masalah ini: Malaysia menghasilkan graduan yang tidak diperlukan ekonomi, sementara industri yang merekrut tidak menemui pekerja berkemahiran yang tepat. “Ekonomi Madani” yang diraikan Anwar nampaknya tidak merangkumi golongan muda.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Satu daripada lapan anak muda Malaysia tidak dapat kerja. Ini bukan kemerosotan kitaran — ia kegagalan struktur saluran pendidikan-ke-pekerjaan. Universiti menghasilkan graduan dalam bidang permintaan terhad sementara industri menghadapi kekurangan akut dalam bidang teknikal, digital, dan tred mahir. Hasilnya: generasi anak muda Malaysia berpendidikan yang terlalu layak untuk pekerjaan tersedia dan tidak cukup layak untuk pekerjaan yang wujud. Pengangguran belia pada 12.28% adalah bom masa sosial.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: Kerajaan menonjolkan pertumbuhan KDNK melebihi 5% dan rekod FDI sebagai bukti ekonomi berkembang maju. Tetapi ekonomi yang berkembang sambil meninggalkan satu daripada lapan anak muda menganggur bukan berkembang maju — ia mengekstrak nilai tanpa mengedarkan peluang. PH berjanji reformasi TVET, program kebolehkerjaan graduan, dan pendidikan sejajar industri. Tiga tahun kemudian, nombor tidak bergerak.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Pengangguran belia secara struktur telah tinggi di Malaysia selama lebih satu dekad. Di bawah BN, kadar itu sekitar 10-11%. Di bawah PH 2.0, ia telah menetap pada 12.28% — lebih baik daripada kemuncak pandemik tetapi lebih teruk daripada tahap pra-pandemik. Tiada kerajaan menyelesaikannya kerana tiada kerajaan sanggup mereformasi sistem pendidikan yang menghasilkan ketidakpadanan itu.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Satu daripada lapan anak muda Malaysia tidak dapat kerja. KDNK anda berkembang melebihi 5%. FDI anda pada rekod tertinggi. Dan belia anda menganggur. Jurang itu bersifat struktur — dan ia tanggungjawab anda sekarang. Reformasi pembiayaan TVET supaya sepadan dengan permintaan industri. Terbitkan data kebolehkerjaan graduan mengikut universiti dan program. Atau terus meraikan pertumbuhan KDNK sementara satu generasi anak muda Malaysia meninggalkan negara untuk mencari kerja di tempat lain.

[BM] Rakyat Kata Inflasi 1.3% Adalah Pembohongan — 'Tak Tahu Dari Mana Madanon Dapat Nombor Ni'

Said by: Public Sentiment / Social Media

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Kadar inflasi rasmi Malaysia untuk Oktober 2025 dilaporkan pada 1.3% oleh Jabatan Perangkaan (DOSM). Rakyat Malaysia bertindak balas dengan ejekan terbuka di media sosial, dengan komen seperti “tak tahu dari mana Madanon dapat nombor ni.” Jurang antara inflasi tajuk dan pengalaman sebenar amat ketara: inflasi makanan luar rumah berjalan pada 4.3-4.7%, jauh melebihi kadar tajuk. Kos sewa, penjagaan kesihatan, dan perbelanjaan pendidikan — kategori yang secara tidak seimbang menjejaskan isi rumah berpendapatan sederhana dan rendah — semuanya meningkat lebih cepat daripada angka komposit rasmi.

🔥 MENGAPA IA PENTING: Apabila rakyat secara terbuka mengejek statistik rasmi kerajaan mereka, ia menandakan keruntuhan kredibiliti institusi. Angka tajuk 1.3% secara teknikal tepat — ia mengukur bakul IHP seperti yang ditakrifkan DOSM. Tetapi bakul IHP tidak mencerminkan cara rakyat Malaysia biasa sebenarnya membelanjakan wang mereka. Hidangan penjaja yang berharga RM5 dua tahun lalu kini berharga RM7-8. Teh tarik mamak telah naik dari RM1.80 kepada RM2.50. Ini harga yang dirasai rakyat setiap hari — dan ia langsung tidak menyerupai 1.3%.

💩 MENGAPA IA KARUT: Kerajaan menggunakan angka 1.3% untuk mendakwa kestabilan ekonomi sambil mengabaikan komposisi angka itu. IHP tajuk ditarik turun oleh harga terkawal (subsidi bahan api, kawalan harga) yang menyembunyikan inflasi asas. Apabila anda mensubsidi RON95 dan mengawal harga tepung dan gula, inflasi tajuk kelihatan rendah — tetapi sebaik sahaja subsidi ditarik, inflasi yang ditahan menghentam sekaligus. Kerajaan bukan mengawal inflasi — ia menyembunyikannya di sebalik subsidi yang tidak mampu dikekalkan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Setiap kerajaan Malaysia telah menggunakan angka inflasi tajuk untuk melukis gambaran lebih cerah daripada realiti. PH menjanjikan ketelusan dan kejujuran dalam tadbir urus — tetapi pengendalian data inflasinya tidak dapat dibezakan daripada pendahulunya.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Rakyat ketawa melihat nombor inflasi anda. Itu sepatutnya membimbangkan anda lebih daripada nombor itu sendiri. Terbitkan “indeks kos sara hidup” yang menjejak bakul barangan dan perkhidmatan sebenar yang digunakan isi rumah B40 dan M40 — bukan komposit IHP yang merangkumi barangan import mewah dan harga terkawal. Tunjukkan rakyat Malaysia anda faham apa yang mereka sebenarnya bayar. Atau terus petik 1.3% sementara hidangan mamak berharga RM8 — dan tertanya mengapa tiada siapa percaya apa sahaja yang anda kata tentang ekonomi.

Lowy Institute: 'Anwar's International Success Sours at Home' — US Trade Deal Backlash

Said by: Lowy Institute Analysis

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In November 2025, the Lowy Institute published an analysis of the US-Malaysia trade deal (ART), concluding that “Anwar’s international success sours at home.” The analysis identified ‘poison pill’ clauses that function as a “loyalty test” requiring Malaysia to align with US strategic interests — including export controls, sanctions enforcement, and unrestricted critical mineral exports. The Lowy Institute noted that the 19% tariff on Malaysian goods was maintained, not reduced to zero as Anwar’s public framing implied. The deal was characterised as a US strategic win dressed up as a bilateral partnership.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When an independent, respected international think tank describes your trade deal as containing “poison pills” and “loyalty tests,” the government’s spin that this was a diplomatic triumph collapses. The Lowy analysis confirmed what domestic critics had been saying: the ART prioritises US interests over Malaysian sovereignty. The 19% tariff — still among the highest imposed on any US trading partner — was presented domestically as a negotiating win, when in reality it represents a failure to secure the zero-tariff access that competitors like Vietnam achieved.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar framed the trade deal as proof of Malaysia’s elevated international standing. The Lowy Institute framed it as proof that Malaysia got outmanoeuvred. The gap between the government’s narrative and independent analysis is enormous. The “poison pill” clauses — requiring alignment with US export controls and sanctions — effectively subordinate Malaysia’s foreign policy to Washington’s strategic agenda. For a PM who champions non-alignment and Global South solidarity, signing a deal that an international think tank describes as a “loyalty test” is a contradiction that cannot be spun away.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s trade deal history is littered with agreements that looked good in press conferences and bad in practice. The TPPA under Najib was shelved after public fury over sovereignty clauses. The RCEP was signed but implementation has been slow. Each agreement follows the same pattern: government celebrates the signing, analysts identify the problems, public anger builds, and the government pivots to damage control. The ART follows this pattern exactly — except this time, the critics include a respected international institution, not just domestic opposition.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: The Lowy Institute called your trade deal a “loyalty test.” Independent analysts identified “poison pill” clauses. The tariff stayed at 19%, not zero. Your own narrative doesn’t match the analysis. Publish the full text of the ART with a clause-by-clause explanation of what Malaysia committed to. Commission an independent economic impact assessment. Explain why Malaysia accepted “loyalty test” provisions that other countries rejected. Or keep celebrating a deal that international analysts describe as a strategic defeat.

Parliament Reduced to Rubber Stamp — PM Whips Ensure Bills Pass Without Genuine Debate

Said by: Opposition MPs / Parliamentary Reform Advocates

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-11-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Despite PH’s promise to strengthen parliamentary democracy, the Dewan Rakyat continues to function as a rubber stamp rather than a genuine deliberative body. Government bills pass with minimal amendment, committee work is perfunctory, and the government whip ensures coalition MPs vote in bloc. The PM term limit bill — one of PH’s signature reform promises — failed in Parliament because PH couldn’t even muster enough of its own MPs to support it. Fahmi Fadzil blamed “opposition confusion” for the failure, when 8 of PH’s own MPs didn’t show up to vote.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Parliament is supposed to be the check on executive power. When parliament is reduced to a venue for the PM’s speeches and pro-forma bill passage, democratic governance degrades into elected authoritarianism. Important legislation passes without meaningful debate. Bad policies go unchallenged. The PM term limit bill failure is the most damning example: PH had the numbers, had the mandate, and still couldn’t deliver on one of the simplest democratic reforms imaginable — because its own MPs couldn’t be bothered to attend.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH explicitly promised parliamentary reform: stronger select committees, an independent Speaker, greater allocation for opposition MPs, and meaningful debate on legislation. Some incremental changes were made, but the fundamental dynamics haven’t changed. The government controls the agenda, determines which bills are debated, and uses coalition arithmetic to ensure outcomes. Under BN, Najib’s government once passed 11 bills in a single day without proper debate. PH criticised this as democratic malpractice. Under PH, the pace is slightly slower but the outcomes are equally predetermined.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Parliamentary rubber-stamping is a Malaysian tradition that predates PH. Under Mahathir 1.0, parliament was a formality. Under Najib, the OSA, SOSMA, and NSC Act were rammed through with minimal debate. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) made some improvements — reviving select committees and increasing opposition funding — but these reforms were incomplete and have stalled under PH 2.0. The East Asia Forum documented how “Malaysia’s reform aspirations clashed with political realities in 2024,” noting that parliamentary independence remained a promise rather than a practice.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You control the Dewan Rakyat. You have a supermajority with BN’s support. You couldn’t even pass a PM term limit bill — your own signature reform. You had the numbers, you had the mandate, and you still couldn’t deliver. If you can’t limit your own power when you have the votes to do it, you were never going to limit your own power at all. Strengthen select committees with real investigative authority. Give the Speaker genuine independence. Allow free votes on reform bills. Or keep running a parliament that functions exactly like the one you inherited — just with different faces in the government benches.

Personal Bankruptcies Rise 5.7% — Self-Declared Bankruptcy Trend 'Growing Concern'

Said by: Department of Insolvency Malaysia

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In the first nine months of 2025, Malaysia recorded 4,875 personal bankruptcy cases — a 5.7% increase from the same period in 2024. The Insolvency Department Director-General flagged a “growing concern”: the rise in self-declared bankruptcies, where Malaysians are so financially distressed they voluntarily declare themselves bankrupt rather than waiting for creditors to force the process. The data indicates that financial distress is not concentrated among business owners — it is spreading to salaried workers, young professionals, and gig economy participants.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When people voluntarily declare bankruptcy, it means they have given up hope of recovery. Self-declared bankruptcy is an act of financial surrender — it signals that the individual sees no path to repaying debts through income alone. A 5.7% rise in total cases, combined with a rising self-declared trend, suggests that Malaysia’s cost-of-living crisis is pushing ordinary households past breaking point. Official inflation is 1.3%. But bankruptcies are rising 5.7%. Those two numbers cannot both be telling the truth about the economy.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government celebrates GDP growth, FDI records, and low headline inflation while an increasing number of Malaysians are literally declaring themselves bankrupt. The disconnect between macro indicators and micro reality is the defining feature of the Madani economy: the numbers look good in Putrajaya, but the rakyat is drowning in debt. The bankruptcy threshold was raised from RM50,000 to RM100,000 in 2020 — meaning current figures actually undercount the problem, since people with debts under RM100,000 can no longer be declared bankrupt by creditors. The real financial distress is worse than the data shows.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Personal bankruptcy has been rising in Malaysia for years. Under BN, the government raised the bankruptcy threshold to mask the trend. Under PH 1.0, a moratorium on bankruptcy proceedings was introduced during COVID. Under PH 2.0, the threshold remains at RM100,000 and bankruptcy cases are climbing again. Each government has managed the optics rather than addressing the root causes: stagnant real wages, rising living costs, easy access to consumer credit, and inadequate financial literacy. The cycle continues because addressing it would require admitting that the economy is not working for ordinary people.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Bankruptcies are up 5.7% while you claim inflation is 1.3%. Self-declared bankruptcies are rising — people are so desperate they’re surrendering voluntarily. Your GDP growth is meaningless if it doesn’t translate into household financial security. Expand financial counselling services through AKPK. Regulate predatory lending practices, especially in the buy-now-pay-later sector. Publish household debt-to-income data by income quintile so the rakyat can see the real picture. Or keep quoting macro numbers while more Malaysians walk into the Insolvency Department to declare themselves bankrupt.

The government is not controlling inflation — it is hiding it behind subsidies it can’t afford to maintain. The pattern is consistent: governments manipulate the visible number while ordinary households bear invisible cost increases. PH promised transparency and honesty in governance — but its handling of inflation data is indistinguishable from its predecessors.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: The rakyat is laughing at your inflation numbers. That should worry you more than the numbers themselves. Publish a “cost of living index” that tracks the actual basket of goods and services used by B40 and M40 households — not the CPI composite that includes imported luxury goods and administered prices. Show Malaysians you understand what they’re actually paying. Or keep quoting 1.3% while mamak meals cost RM8 — and wonder why nobody believes anything you say about the economy.
