Najib's Jail Sentence Halved — Pardons Board Under Anwar's Watch

Said by: Pardons Board / King of Malaysia

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-02-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 2 February 2024, the Pardons Board — which operates under the constitutional authority of the King, with the PM as a member — halved Najib Razak’s 12-year jail sentence to 6 years for his role in the 1MDB scandal. Najib’s RM210 million fine was also reduced to RM50 million. The decision came amid a “royal addendum” controversy, with Najib’s camp claiming an additional document existed granting house arrest. Reuters reported the pardon came “amid accusations of backsliding on reforms.” Critics called it a backroom deal to keep UMNO in the coalition — UMNO had made Najib’s release a precondition for continued support of Anwar’s government.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Najib was convicted of the largest financial scandal in Malaysian history — billions stolen from a sovereign wealth fund, laundered through global banks, spent on yachts, jewellery, and Hollywood films. The conviction was PH’s signature achievement under PH 1.0. Halving the sentence under PH 2.0 sent a devastating message: in Malaysia, corruption penalties are negotiable if you have enough political leverage. The pardon was not based on new evidence or legal merit — it was based on coalition arithmetic. UMNO’s support for PH came with an implicit price, and the rakyat is watching that price get paid.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar spent his entire political career demanding accountability for Najib. “Malu Apa Bossku” was the slogan PH mocked relentlessly. Now Najib’s sentence is halved, his fine reduced by 76%, and his supporters are pushing for house arrest — all under Anwar’s watch. The Pardons Board includes the PM. The decision cannot be separated from the political context: UMNO wanted Najib’s sentence reduced, UMNO is essential to PH’s majority, and the sentence was reduced. Calling this anything other than a political deal requires a level of naivety that no Malaysian possesses.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a history of politically convenient pardons. Anwar himself was pardoned by the King after PH 1.0’s victory in 2018 — a pardon that was celebrated as justice. The difference: Anwar’s conviction was widely seen as politically motivated. Najib’s conviction was upheld by every level of the judiciary based on overwhelming evidence. Pardoning a political prisoner and reducing the sentence of a convicted kleptocrat are fundamentally different acts — but PH’s willingness to facilitate both suggests that the pardons process is a political tool, not a justice mechanism.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You built your movement on holding Najib accountable. Then you halved his sentence to keep UMNO happy. The rakyat marched in Bersih rallies, voted in GE14 and GE15, and waited years for justice — and you traded it for coalition stability. If Najib’s crime was worth 12 years when PH was in opposition, it doesn’t become worth 6 years just because UMNO is in your coalition. Every ringgit Najib stole is still missing. Every year removed from his sentence is a discount on accountability. The rakyat will remember.

[BM] Skor PISA Malaysia Merosot Paling Teruk Di Kalangan Negara Asia Tenggara — Anwar Kata 'Tidak Boleh Nafikan'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Education

Date: 2024-01-15

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Keputusan PISA 2022 Malaysia — mengukur kualiti pendidikan pelajar berumur 15 tahun — menunjukkan kemerosotan terbesar di kalangan semua negara Asia Tenggara yang diuji. Skor jatuh merentas matematik, sains, dan bacaan. Anwar merespons dengan mengatakan Malaysia “mesti mengetahui keadaan sebenar dan tidak boleh dalam keadaan menafikan.” Menteri Pendidikan mengakui sistem itu “mengecewakan guru dan pelajar.” CNA melaporkan bahawa politik — terutamanya ketegangan antara keperluan bahasa Melayu dan kecekapan bahasa Inggeris — berada di sebalik kesukaran reformasi pendidikan. Tiga tahun kemudian, pakar mengatakan tiada apa yang berubah di lapangan.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Pendidikan menentukan masa depan sesebuah negara. Sistem pendidikan Malaysia sedang gagal — dan kerajaan tahu. Keputusan PISA telah bertakung selama tiga kitaran penilaian berturut-turut sementara pesaing serantau seperti Vietnam dan Indonesia telah meningkat. FMT memerhatikan: “Setiap beberapa tahun, kementerian melancarkan pelan induk pendidikan baharu. Ia mempunyai logo yang kemas dan halaman penuh jargon. Tetapi di lapangan, tiada apa yang berubah.” Jurang antara Malaysia dan Singapura dalam matematik kini bersamaan kira-kira 8 tahun pembelajaran. Satu generasi pelajar Malaysia sedang ditinggalkan oleh sistem yang mengutamakan pertimbangan politik berbanding hasil pendidikan.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: “Tidak boleh nafikan” adalah pengakuan, bukan dasar. Anwar mengakui masalah pada Januari 2024. Kini sudah Mac 2026. Mana hasilnya? Politik bahasa, agama, dan kaum menjadikan reformasi pendidikan hampir mustahil dalam sistem gabungan Malaysia — dan PH menunjukkan sifar kesediaan untuk menghadapi politik tersebut. Keperluan wajib Bahasa Melayu dan Sejarah merentas semua sistem pendidikan diperluaskan bukannya direformasi. AUKU — yang menyekat penyertaan politik pelajar — masih kekal meskipun ia janji manifesto. Sistem pendidikan gagal kerana membaikinya memerlukan menghadapi kepentingan yang mengakar yang PH bergantung padanya untuk kelangsungan politik.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Kemerosotan pendidikan Malaysia bukan baharu. Skor PISA telah sederhana sejak Malaysia mula menyertai pada 2009. Setiap menteri pendidikan melancarkan pelan induk baharu: Pelan Pembangunan Pendidikan Malaysia 2013-2025, pelbagai inisiatif STEM, pengembangan TVET. Setiap satu menjana laporan dan sidang akhbar. Tiada yang menjana peningkatan yang boleh diukur dalam hasil pelajar. Masalah asas — sistem yang direka berdasarkan pertimbangan politik dan etnik bukannya kecemerlangan pendidikan — kekal tidak disentuh kerana tiada kerajaan yang sanggup membayar kos politik memreformasinya.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda berkata Malaysia “tidak boleh nafikan.” Kemudian anda terus menafikan. Skor PISA merosot. Jurang pendidikan dengan Singapura adalah 8 tahun pembelajaran. Respons anda ialah mengakui masalah dan tidak melakukan apa-apa. Reformasi kurikulum untuk mengutamakan pemikiran kritis berbanding hafalan. Laburkan dalam latihan dan pampasan guru. Tangani kesesakan dasar bahasa dengan jujur. Atau terus melancarkan pelan induk pendidikan dengan logo kemas sementara pelajar Malaysia semakin ketinggalan berbanding rakan serantau mereka — dan tertanya-tanya mengapa penghijrahan otak semakin pantas.

Malaysia's PISA Scores Decline Most Among Southeast Asian Countries — Anwar Says 'Cannot Be in Denial'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Education

Date: 2024-01-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s 2022 PISA results — measuring education quality among 15-year-olds — showed the biggest decline among all Southeast Asian countries tested. Scores dropped across mathematics, science, and reading. Anwar responded by saying Malaysia “must know the state of our affairs and cannot be in a state of denial.” The Education Minister admitted the system “failed both teachers and students.” CNA reported that politics — particularly the tension between Malay language requirements and English proficiency — is behind the difficulty of education reform. Three years later, experts say nothing has changed on the ground.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Education determines a nation’s future. Malaysia’s education system is failing — and the government knows it. PISA results have stagnated for three consecutive assessment cycles while regional competitors like Vietnam and Indonesia have improved. FMT observed: “Every few years, the ministry launches a new education blueprint. It’s got a sleek logo and pages of jargon. But on the ground, nothing changes.” The gap between Malaysia and Singapore in mathematics is now equivalent to approximately 8 years of learning. A generation of Malaysian students is being left behind by a system that prioritises political considerations over educational outcomes.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: “Cannot be in denial” is an admission, not a policy. Anwar acknowledged the problem in January 2024. It is now March 2026. Where are the results? The politics of language, religion, and race make education reform nearly impossible in Malaysia’s coalition system — and PH has shown zero willingness to confront those politics. Mandatory Malay and History requirements across all education systems were expanded rather than reformed. UUCA — which restricts student political participation — remains intact despite being a manifesto promise. The education system is failing because fixing it requires confronting entrenched interests that PH depends on for political survival.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s education decline is not new. PISA scores have been mediocre since Malaysia first participated in 2009. Every education minister launches a new blueprint: the National Education Blueprint 2013-2025, various STEM initiatives, TVET expansions. Each generates reports and press conferences. None generates measurable improvement in student outcomes. The fundamental problem — a system designed around political and ethnic considerations rather than educational excellence — remains untouched because no government has been willing to pay the political cost of reforming it.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You said Malaysia “cannot be in denial.” Then you stayed in denial. PISA scores are declining. The education gap with Singapore is 8 years of learning. Your response was to acknowledge the problem and do nothing about it. Reform the curriculum to prioritise critical thinking over rote memorisation. Invest in teacher training and compensation. Address the language policy bottleneck honestly. Or keep launching education blueprints with sleek logos while Malaysian students fall further behind their regional peers — and wonder why the brain drain accelerates.

[BM] Amnesty International 2024: Anwar 'Mengkhianati Janji Reformasi Yang Dibuat Kepada Pengundi Malaysia'

Said by: Amnesty International / Shayna Bauchner

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-01-11

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada Januari 2024, laporan tahunan Amnesty International menyatakan bahawa Anwar Ibrahim telah “mengkhianati janji reformasi yang dibuatnya kepada pengundi Malaysia supaya beliau boleh mengekalkan gabungannya.” Laporan itu, ditulis oleh penyelidik Shayna Bauchner, mengenal pasti tujuh bidang di mana Malaysia gagal memenuhi tanggungjawab hak asasi manusianya, termasuk kebebasan bersuara, kebebasan berhimpun, kebebasan media, hak pendatang, hak LGBTQ+, dan hukuman mati. Amnesty menyatakan bahawa kerajaan bukan sahaja gagal memajukan reformasi tetapi secara aktif menggunakan undang-undang menindas yang dijanjikan untuk dimansuhkan.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Apabila Amnesty International — pertubuhan hak asasi manusia terbesar dunia — menggunakan perkataan “mengkhianati,” ia bukan membuat kritikan biasa. Ia mendokumentasikan kerajaan yang secara eksplisit menjanjikan reformasi tertentu, menerima undi atas dasar itu, dan kemudian berpatah balik. Pilihan perkataan itu penting: “terbantut” membawa makna kelewatan; “mengkhianati” membawa makna niat. Amnesty mengatakan bahawa peninggalan reformasi oleh PH bukan disebabkan kekangan atau kerumitan — ia adalah pilihan sengaja untuk mengutamakan kestabilan gabungan berbanding komitmen hak asasi manusia.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Pembelaan PH — “kekangan gabungan memerlukan kompromi” — adalah tepat-tepat hujah yang digunakan BN selama berdekad. UMNO berkata ia tidak boleh mereformasi kerana rakan gabungan. PAS berkata ia tidak boleh bersederhana kerana asasnya. Kini PH berkata ia tidak boleh menyampaikan hak kerana UMNO ada dalam gabungan. Alasan itu serupa; hanya nama parti yang bertukar. Jika anda tahu UMNO akan menyekat reformasi apabila anda membentuk gabungan, maka janji manifesto anda adalah palsu dengan sengaja. Jika anda tidak menjangkakannya, anda tidak cekap. Walau bagaimanapun, penggunaan “mengkhianati” oleh Amnesty adalah tepat.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Amnesty telah mendokumentasikan kegagalan hak asasi manusia Malaysia merentas setiap pentadbiran. Di bawah Mahathir 1.0, tumpuan adalah penahanan ISA dan tindakan keras terhadap perbezaan pendapat. Di bawah Najib, ia adalah penindasan berkaitan 1MDB dan Akta Hasutan. Di bawah PH 1.0, Amnesty mencatatkan kemajuan separa sebelum Langkah Sheraton. Di bawah PH 2.0, keputusan adalah yang paling keras — kerana jurang antara janji dan penyampaian tidak pernah lebih lebar. PH membuat komitmen hak asasi manusia paling bercita-cita tinggi dalam sejarah Malaysia, dan memungkirinya secara lebih menyeluruh daripada mana-mana pendahulu.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Amnesty International mengatakan anda “mengkhianati” pengundi anda. Bukan “mengecewakan.” Bukan “tidak memenuhi.” Mengkhianati. Perkataan itu akan mengikut kerajaan ini ke setiap forum antarabangsa, setiap pertemuan diplomatik, dan setiap pilihan raya masa depan. Anda masih boleh mengubah trajektori: mansuhkan Akta Hasutan, lindungi hak berhimpun, jamin kebebasan akhbar, dan berhenti menggunakan undang-undang menindas terhadap pengkritik. Atau biarkan “mengkhianati” menjadi ringkasan satu perkataan era Anwar — kerana sekarang, Amnesty dan HRW bersetuju bahawa ia memang begitu.

Tahun 3: kawalan internet yang diperluaskan, 90+ tangkapan hasutan, dan tangkapan wartawan. Apabila pertubuhan hak asasi manusia paling terkenal dunia mengatakan agenda reformasi anda telah terbantut — dan kemudian mengatakan ia secara aktif mundur — masalahnya bukan persepsi. Ia dasar. Di bawah Muhyiddin dan Ismail Sabri, tumpuan adalah sekatan era pandemik dan kemunduran demokrasi. Tetapi PH sepatutnya berbeza — ia adalah kerajaan yang cadangan HRW sejajar dengannya. Apabila pertubuhan hak asasi manusia yang sepatutnya menjadi sekutu semula jadi anda mengatakan anda telah terbantut dan mundur, masalahnya bukan pengkritik. Ia kerajaan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Human Rights Watch memberikan anda setahun. Kemudian setahun lagi. Keputusan menjadi lebih teruk, bukan lebih baik. “Terbantut” menjadi “kemunduran.” Jika trajektori berterusan, Tahun 4 akan menjadi “kemerosotan.” Laksanakan reformasi yang dikenal pasti HRW: mansuhkan Akta Hasutan, kukuhkan perlindungan kebebasan media, jamin hak berhimpun, dan pulihkan kebebasan SPRM. Atau terus menolak kritikan antarabangsa sebagai “tidak memahami konteks” — sementara konteksnya ialah anda melakukan tepat apa yang BN lakukan, dengan bahasa Inggeris yang lebih baik.

Amnesty International 2024: Anwar 'Betrayed the Reform Pledges He Made to Malaysian Voters'

Said by: Amnesty International / Shayna Bauchner

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-01-11

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2024, Amnesty International’s annual report stated that Anwar Ibrahim had “betrayed the reform pledges he made to Malaysian voters so he could hold his coalition together.” The report, authored by researcher Shayna Bauchner, identified seven areas where Malaysia fell short of its human rights obligations, including freedom of expression, freedom of assembly, media freedom, migrant rights, LGBTQ+ rights, and the death penalty. Amnesty noted that the government had not only failed to advance reforms but had actively used repressive laws it had promised to repeal.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When Amnesty International — the world’s largest human rights organisation — uses the word “betrayed,” it is not making a casual criticism. It is documenting a government that explicitly promised specific reforms, received votes on that basis, and then reversed course. The word choice matters: “stalled” implies delay; “betrayed” implies intent. Amnesty is saying that PH’s abandonment of reform was not due to constraints or complexity — it was a deliberate choice to prioritise coalition stability over human rights commitments.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s defence — “coalition constraints require compromise” — is exactly the argument BN used for decades. UMNO said it couldn’t reform because of coalition partners. PAS said it couldn’t moderate because of its base. Now PH says it can’t deliver rights because UMNO is in the coalition. The excuse is identical; only the party name has changed. If you knew UMNO would block reforms when you formed the coalition, then your manifesto promises were knowingly false. If you didn’t anticipate it, you’re incompetent. Either way, Amnesty’s use of “betrayed” is accurate.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Amnesty has documented Malaysian human rights failures across every administration. Under Mahathir 1.0, the focus was ISA detentions and crackdowns on dissent. Under Najib, it was 1MDB-related repression and the Sedition Act. Under PH 1.0, Amnesty noted partial progress before the Sheraton Move. Under PH 2.0, the verdict is the harshest yet — because the gap between promise and delivery has never been wider. PH made the most ambitious human rights commitments in Malaysian history, and broke them more comprehensively than any predecessor.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Amnesty International says you “betrayed” your voters. Not “disappointed.” Not “fell short.” Betrayed. That word will follow this government into every international forum, every diplomatic meeting, and every future election. You can still change the trajectory: repeal the Sedition Act, protect assembly rights, guarantee press freedom, and stop using repressive laws against critics. Or let “betrayed” become the one-word summary of the Anwar era — because right now, Amnesty and HRW agree that it is.

HRW 2024: 'Reform Agenda Stalled' — Government Failed to Uphold Pledges in First Year

Said by: Human Rights Watch

Category: Social

Date: 2024-01-11

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2024, Human Rights Watch released its 2024 World Report, delivering a damning verdict on Anwar Ibrahim’s first year in office: “Reform Agenda Stalled.” HRW stated that Anwar had “largely failed to uphold his pledges to address repression and corruption” and called on the government to “recommit to a rights-respecting agenda by turning its sidelined campaign promises into action.” The report documented failures across freedom of expression, assembly, media freedom, and institutional reform. A year later, HRW’s 2025 assessment was even harsher: “Rights Backslide.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Two consecutive years of HRW condemnation is not a bump in the road — it is a trajectory. Year 1: stalled. Year 2: backslide. Year 3: expanded internet controls, 90+ sedition arrests, and journalist arrests. When the world’s most prominent human rights organisation says your reform agenda has stalled — and then says it’s actively going backwards — the problem is not perception. It is policy. PH was elected on a reform mandate. The international human rights community is documenting, in real time, the abandonment of that mandate.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s response to HRW’s assessment was predictable: “They don’t understand our context.” “Coalition constraints.” “We need time.” But HRW gave PH a year before issuing the “stalled” verdict. Then another year before escalating to “backslide.” The organisation is not being impatient — it is documenting a government that promised reform and delivered regression. The Sedition Act was not just left on the books — it was expanded. Media freedom was not just neglected — journalists were arrested. Assembly rights were not just unprotected — Bersih rallies were suppressed. This is not stalling. This is reversal.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: HRW has criticised every Malaysian government. Under Najib, the assessments focused on 1MDB, repressive laws, and crackdowns on civil society. Under Muhyiddin and Ismail Sabri, the focus was pandemic-era restrictions and democratic backsliding. But PH was supposed to be different — it was the government that HRW’s recommendations aligned with. When the human rights organisation that should be your natural ally says you’ve stalled and backslid, the problem is not the critic. It’s the government.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Human Rights Watch gave you a year. Then another year. The verdict got worse, not better. “Stalled” became “backslide.” If the trajectory continues, Year 4 will be “regression.” Implement the reforms HRW identified: repeal the Sedition Act, strengthen media freedom protections, guarantee assembly rights, and restore MACC independence. Or keep dismissing international criticism as “not understanding context” — while the context is that you’re doing exactly what BN did, with better English.

[BM] Pangkalan Data PADU Dilancar Sebagai Mimpi Ngeri Privasi — Sesiapa Dengan Nombor IC Anda Boleh Akses Data Anda

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2024-01-02

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 2 Januari 2024, Anwar melancarkan Hab Pangkalan Data Pusat (PADU) — sistem yang bertujuan merevolusikan penyampaian subsidi dengan menyasarkan bantuan kepada mereka yang memerlukan. Lebih 233,000 pengguna mendaftar pada hari pelancaran. Dalam 48 jam, penyelidik keselamatan mendapati pengesahan itu sangat lemah: sesiapa yang mempunyai nombor IC dan poskod rumah orang lain boleh mendaftar dan mengakses data mereka. CybersecurityAsia memanggilnya “mimpi ngeri privasi.” Sarawak menghentikan penyertaannya sepenuhnya pada Mac 2024, memetik kebimbangan mengenai autonomi negeri dan data peribadi rakyat. Menjelang pertengahan 2025, PADU senyap-senyap diketepikan — reformasi subsidi RON95 langsung tidak menggunakannya, menggunakan data pendaftaran kenderaan JPJ sebaliknya.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Kerajaan melancarkan pangkalan data kebangsaan dengan pengesahan yang sangat lemah sehingga ia pada dasarnya akses terbuka. Nombor IC bukan rahsia — ia muncul pada setiap borang kerajaan dan kerap bocor dalam pelanggaran data. Menggunakannya sebagai faktor pengesahan tunggal untuk pangkalan data yang mengandungi data pendapatan, isi rumah, dan pekerjaan bukan kecacatan reka bentuk — ia adalah kegagalan sistemik tadbir urus digital. ISEAS menyatakan PADU “mencetuskan ribut perdebatan mengenai isu privasi, federalisme, dan sifat kontrak sosial antara rakyat dan negara.” Apabila Sarawak enggan menyertai dan membina sistemnya sendiri, mesej tersirat itu dahsyat: kami tidak mempercayai anda dengan data rakyat kami.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: PADU sepatutnya menjadi tulang belakang keseluruhan agenda reformasi subsidi Malaysia. Kerajaan menghabiskan bertahun berhujah bahawa subsidi sejagat perlu digantikan dengan yang bersasar, dan PADU adalah mekanismenya. Apabila ujian terbesar tiba — reformasi RON95 — PADU langsung tidak digunakan. Berbilion dibelanjakan membina sistem yang ditinggalkan tanpa pengakuan atau akauntabiliti awam. Tiada bedah siasat diterbitkan. Tiada akauntabiliti diberikan. Tiada perakaunan kos disediakan. Inilah yang berlaku apabila dasar didorong oleh pengumuman bukannya pelaksanaan — PADU menghasilkan tajuk utama yang hebat pada hari pelancaran, dan hakikat bahawa ia tidak selamat, tidak lengkap, dan akhirnya tidak boleh digunakan dilayan sebagai masalah yang semua orang lupakan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Rekod IT kerajaan Malaysia adalah kubur kegagalan yang mahal. Sistem MyKad telah dibelenggu isu data selama bertahun. Aplikasi COVID MySejahtera dibelitkan kontroversi pemilikan dan privasi data. Laman web kerajaan kerap digodam atau dirosakkan. Setiap projek mengikut corak yang sama: lancar dengan gembar-gembur, temui kecacatan, tampung secara reaktif, dan akhirnya tinggalkan. PADU adalah iterasi paling mahal kitaran ini — pangkalan data kebangsaan yang dibina tanpa keselamatan yang mencukupi, dilancarkan tanpa ujian yang mencukupi, dan diketepikan tanpa penjelasan yang mencukupi.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda membelanjakan ratusan juta membina pangkalan data kebangsaan. Ia dilancarkan dengan keselamatan yang sangat lemah sehingga sesiapa boleh mengakses data sesiapa. Sarawak enggan menyertai. Dan apabila reformasi subsidi terbesar anda tiba, anda langsung tidak menggunakannya. Terbitkan jumlah kos PADU. Jelaskan mengapa ia diketepikan. Jalankan bedah siasat awam mengenai apa yang salah. Atau terus melancarkan inisiatif digital yang menjadi tajuk utama yang berkos berbilion dan tidak menyampaikan apa-apa — sementara data peribadi rakyat duduk dalam pangkalan data yang tidak mampu mengesahkan siapa yang mengaksesnya.

PADU Database Launches as Privacy Nightmare — Anyone With Your IC Number Can Access Your Data

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2024-01-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 2 January 2024, Anwar launched the Central Database Hub (PADU) — a system meant to revolutionise subsidy delivery by targeting aid to those who need it. Over 233,000 users registered on launch day. Within 48 hours, security researchers discovered the authentication was laughably weak: anyone armed with another person’s IC number and home postcode could register and access their data. CybersecurityAsia called it a “privacy nightmare.” Sarawak halted its participation entirely in March 2024, citing concerns over state autonomy and citizens’ personal data. By mid-2025, PADU was quietly sidelined — the RON95 subsidy reform bypassed it entirely, using JPJ vehicle registration data instead.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The government launched a national database with authentication so weak it was effectively open access. IC numbers are not secrets — they appear on every government form and are routinely leaked in data breaches. Using them as the sole authentication factor for a database containing income, household, and employment data is not a design flaw — it is a systemic failure of digital governance. ISEAS noted that PADU “ignited a firestorm of debate over issues of privacy, federalism, and the very nature of the social contract between citizens and the state.” When Sarawak refused to participate and built its own system, the implied message was devastating: we don’t trust you with our citizens’ data.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PADU was meant to be the backbone of Malaysia’s entire subsidy reform agenda. The government spent years arguing that blanket subsidies needed to be replaced with targeted ones, and PADU was the mechanism. When the biggest test came — RON95 reform — PADU was bypassed entirely. Billions were spent building a system that was abandoned without public acknowledgment or accountability. No postmortem was published. No accountability was assigned. No cost accounting was provided. This is what happens when policy is announcement-driven rather than implementation-driven — PADU made a great headline on launch day, and the fact that it was insecure, incomplete, and ultimately unusable was treated as everyone’s forgotten problem.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s government IT track record is a graveyard of expensive failures. The MyKad system has been plagued by data issues for years. The MySejahtera COVID app was mired in ownership and data privacy controversies. Government websites are routinely hacked or defaced. Each project follows the same pattern: launch with fanfare, discover flaws, patch reactively, and eventually abandon. PADU is the most expensive iteration of this cycle — a national database built without adequate security, launched without adequate testing, and shelved without adequate explanation.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You spent hundreds of millions building a national database. It launched with security so weak anyone could access anyone’s data. Sarawak refused to participate. And when your biggest subsidy reform came, you didn’t even use it. Publish the total cost of PADU. Explain why it was sidelined. Conduct a public postmortem on what went wrong. Or keep launching headline-grabbing digital initiatives that cost billions and deliver nothing — while the rakyat’s personal data sits in a database that couldn’t even verify who was accessing it.

[BM] SPRM Rampas Ilham Tower Daim Dalam Siasatan Pengubahan Wang Haram — Pendakwaan Terpilih Atau Akauntabiliti Tulen?

Said by: Azam Baki, MACC Chief Commissioner / Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-12-21

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 21 Disember 2023, SPRM merampas Ilham Tower 60 tingkat di Kuala Lumpur — bernilai anggaran RM1 hingga RM1.5 bilion — di bawah Seksyen 51 Akta Pencegahan Pengubahan Wang Haram (AMLA). Rampasan ini sebahagian daripada siasatan yang semakin meluas terhadap bekas menteri kewangan Tun Daim Zainuddin berhubung urus niaga korporat 1997 bernilai RM2.3 bilion melibatkan Renong Bhd dan United Engineers Malaysia (UEM), kedua-duanya dikaitkan dengan UMNO. Ketua Pesuruhjaya SPRM Tan Sri Azam Baki mengesahkan rampasan itu. Daim menafikan sebarang salah laku dan mengekalkan pendirian bahawa siasatan bermotif politik. SPRM juga mendakwa Daim dan isterinya Naimah Abdul Khalid kerana gagal mengisytiharkan harta, dan mengeluarkan notis untuk mengesan empat orang anak Daim.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Di atas kertas, menyiasat kekayaan tersembunyi bekas menteri kewangan adalah tepat apa yang kerajaan antirasuah patut buat. Tetapi konteksnya penting: Daim adalah sekutu utama Mahathir, yang kini menentang Anwar secara politik. Sekutu Mahathir — Daim, pelbagai tokoh pembangkang — menghadapi penelitian SPRM di bawah Anwar, manakala rakan gabungan Anwar sendiri Zahid mempunyai 47 pertuduhan digugurkan beberapa bulan sebelum itu. Coraknya tidak boleh disangkal: SPRM keras terhadap lawan politik dan lembut terhadap sekutu politik.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Ketua SPRM yang sama, Azam Baki, yang agresif mengejar Daim adalah orang yang sama yang menghadapi skandal pegangan saham sendiri dan dilindungi kerajaan Anwar. Pejabat AG yang sama yang meluluskan siasatan Daim juga menandatangani penggugaran 47 pertuduhan Zahid. Kalau SPRM benar-benar bebas, rakyat mungkin meraikan siasatan Daim sebagai kemenangan. Tetapi apabila ketua agensi antirasuah sendiri dibelenggu kontroversi, dan kerajaan memilih rasuah mana untuk didakwa berdasarkan penjajaran politik, seluruh latihan ini kelihatan seperti kuasa negara berpakaian akauntabiliti.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Pendakwaan terpilih mempunyai sejarah panjang di Malaysia. Di bawah Najib, SPRM dituduh menyasar tokoh pembangkang sambil mengabaikan rasuah berkaitan BN. Di bawah Mahathir 2.0, pemimpin UMNO didakwa mengikut kepentingan politik. Di bawah Anwar, senarai sasaran berubah lagi: sekutu Mahathir diserbu, sekutu Anwar dapat DNAA. Siasatan ICIJ Pandora Papers mengaitkan Daim dengan amanah luar pesisir $52 juta — jadi mungkin ada substansi dalam siasatan. Tetapi aplikasi keadilan yang terpilih memastikan siasatan yang sah pun dicemari syak politik.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Korang nak kredit kejar Daim? Boleh. Tapi jelaskan kenapa korang gugurkan 47 pertuduhan Zahid tiga bulan sebelum tu. Jelaskan kenapa Azam Baki — orang yang ada skandal aset sendiri — yang ketuai siasatan ini. Korang tak boleh dakwa lawan dan lindungi kawan dan panggil ia Reformasi. Rakyat nampak coraknya. Sama ada semua orang tertakluk kepada undang-undang, atau tiada siapa. Sekarang ni, nampak macam undang-undang hanya terpakai kepada orang yang menyusahkan politik Anwar Ibrahim.
