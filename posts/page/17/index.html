Anwar Marks His Own Homework — PM Who Is Also Finance Minister Presents Budget and Claims It's Great

Said by: Opposition / Budget Analysts

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-10-10

📌 What Happened

Budget 2026 was presented by Anwar Ibrahim — as Finance Minister — to parliament, where he sits as Prime Minister. He then defended his own budget in parliament, praised its achievements, and dismissed criticism. The person who wrote the exam is also the person who grades it.

🔥 Why It Matters

In any functional democracy, the finance function has oversight independent from the political leadership. When one person controls both the political agenda and the national wallet, there is no meaningful check on fiscal decisions. Budget allocations become political tools without independent scrutiny. Parliament’s role as a budget oversight body is undermined when the PM/FM controls both the executive and the fiscal agenda.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar criticised Najib SPECIFICALLY for holding both PM and FM portfolios, arguing it enabled 1MDB by removing oversight. Then Anwar took BOTH portfolios himself. When asked about this, his response was essentially ’trust me, I’m different.’ That’s not institutional reform — it’s personal exceptionalism. The same structural vulnerability that enabled 1MDB exists under Anwar.

📜 Previous Cases

Najib as PM+FM: 1MDB happened. Mahathir as PM: the Finance Minister role provided some separation. Under good governance principles, fiscal oversight requires institutional independence, not personal trust. Singapore separates the roles. The UK separates the roles. Most functional democracies separate the roles.

🎯 PH Callout

You were elected because the last PM who held both portfolios stole billions. Your solution to preventing the next 1MDB was… to replicate the exact same structural vulnerability. You’re marking your own homework, and unsurprisingly, you keep giving yourself A+. Appoint a separate Finance Minister or stop pretending institutional reform matters to you.

Housing Affordability Crisis: B40 and M40 Locked Out as Prices Remain Beyond Reach

Said by: Multiple economists and NAPIC

Category: Housing

Date: 2025-10-07

The Promise of a Roof That Never Comes

Despite Anwar’s government capping affordable housing at RM300,000, the reality for B40 and M40 Malaysians remains bleak. The housing affordability crisis — one of the most serious socio-economic problems of the 21st century — has only deepened under PH.

The Facts

Fact: Using B40 median income as a benchmark, homes need to be priced below RM300,000 to be considered affordable. The government set this as the ceiling price.

Fact: National house prices increased by a cumulative 15.2% from 2019 to Q3 2025, but in real (inflation-adjusted) terms, the increase was just 4.2%.

Fact: 54.3% of KL residential transactions were below RM500,000, while 22.8% were above RM1 million — showing a deeply split market.

Fact: Research describes an “unsustainable dynamic, locking up a significant portion of the people — particularly the M40 and higher B40 — out of homeownership.”

Why It Matters

  • Setting a price cap is not the same as building homes at that price. Where are the units?
  • Young Malaysians are being priced out of their own cities. This is a generational crisis.
  • PH’s housing promises — like so many others — exist on paper but not on the ground. *Sources: The Edge · *Research

Tiong King Sing Serves Alcohol at Official Tourism Gala, Then Lies About Who Organised It

Said by: Tiong King Sing, Tourism, Arts and Culture Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-10-05

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

Tourism, Arts and Culture Minister Tiong King Sing came under fire in October 2025 after alcoholic beverages were served at a gala dinner during the Global Travel Meet — an event organised under the Tourism Malaysia banner. Video clips showed beer and wine being served to Muslim guests, prompting several to leave early.

Tiong initially denied responsibility, claiming the private sector organised the catering and that it was “not part of Tourism Malaysia’s official programme.” But former Health Minister Khairy Jamaluddin posted screenshots on Instagram showing Tourism Malaysia was listed as the organiser of the gala dinner, mocking Tiong with “Liar, liar pants on fire.”

Anwar eventually issued a “stern warning” to Tiong but did not sack him.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This is a minister lying on record about his own ministry’s event, getting caught with documentary evidence, and facing zero consequences beyond a “stern warning.” In a government that promised integrity and accountability, a minister who lies publicly and gets caught should face real consequences — not a pat on the wrist.

The incident also exposed the deep contradictions in Anwar’s coalition management. Tiong is from GPS (Gabungan Parti Sarawak), a critical coalition partner. Sacking him would risk losing Sarawak’s support. So accountability takes a back seat to coalition arithmetic.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Tiong’s defence was a masterclass in gaslighting. The Tourism Malaysia logo was on the event backdrop. Tourism Malaysia staff were present. But somehow it was the “private sector’s” fault. When Khairy produced the receipts, Tiong pivoted to expressing “deep regret” without actually apologising.

And Anwar’s response? A “stern warning.” The same Anwar who once promised that his ministers would be held to the highest standards. A minister who lies publicly about his own event gets a slap on the wrist. If this were a BN minister under Najib, PH would have demanded a resignation within 24 hours.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Tiong King Sing is the same minister who screamed “Do you know who I am?” at an airport in February 2025 — a pattern of entitlement and arrogance. He was also previously embroiled in controversies linked to alcohol consumption before this gala incident. The man has a track record of embarrassing the government and facing no real consequences.

Under BN, ministers who lied about public events were torn apart by the then-opposition. PH’s selective amnesia about accountability standards is a recurring theme.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

PH promised Kerajaan Madani — a civilised government built on integrity. A minister who lies about who organised an official event, gets caught red-handed, and faces only a “stern warning” is not integrity. It’s impunity. Either your standards apply to all your ministers — including coalition partners from Sarawak — or they mean nothing. Tiong should have been dropped from Cabinet. The fact that he wasn’t tells Malaysians everything they need to know about where coalition survival ranks versus accountability.

Chinese and Tamil Schools Used as Political Football Every Budget — Promised Millions, Delivered Scraps

Said by: Dong Zong / Tamil Foundation / MCA / MIC

Category: Education

Date: 2025-10-01

📌 What Happened

Every Malaysian budget cycle produces the same theatre: Chinese and Tamil school communities lobby desperately for maintenance, construction, and development funds. Politicians announce allocations with fanfare. Then the actual disbursement falls short, is delayed, or comes with conditions that limit use. The cycle repeats annually, with non-Malay communities performing gratitude for inadequate allocations.

🔥 Why It Matters

1.3 million students attend Chinese-medium schools (SJKC) and hundreds of thousands attend Tamil-medium schools (SJKT). These schools educate a significant portion of the non-Malay population and consistently outperform national schools in certain metrics. Yet they receive systematically less government funding per student, forcing communities to self-fund infrastructure that should be the government’s responsibility.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH, which depends heavily on Chinese and Indian votes, should have resolved this decades-old inequity. Instead, PH plays the same game as BN: use budget announcements as photo opportunities with Chinese and Indian community leaders, generate grateful headlines, then under-deliver. The structural disparity in per-student funding between national schools (SK) and vernacular schools remains because addressing it would require either more money (which the government claims it doesn’t have) or admitting that the current system is discriminatory (which it won’t).

📜 Previous Cases

BN used vernacular school allocations as political patronage — offering funds in exchange for community support. MCA and MIC existed primarily to secure these allocations, perpetuating dependency. PH was supposed to break this cycle by funding schools equitably regardless of medium of instruction. Instead, PH has maintained the BN model: Chinese and Indian schools get what PH decides to give them, when PH decides to give it.

🎯 PH Callout

Non-Malay Malaysians didn’t vote PH to continue BN’s patronage model with better optics. They voted for EQUAL treatment. Fund vernacular schools at the same per-student rate as national schools. Not as a favour. Not as a budget announcement photo op. As a constitutional obligation under Article 152 and basic educational equity. Or admit that ‘Madani for all’ has an asterisk: *terms and conditions apply based on ethnicity.

Category: Other

📌 What Happened

Training facilities in many sports are outdated compared to regional competitors like Thailand, Indonesia, and Vietnam. The contrast between the RM2.6 billion JAKIM budget for religious affairs and the significantly smaller allocation for sports development tells you everything about government priorities. Malaysia aspires to be a sporting nation but funds it like an afterthought.

🎯 PH Callout

You want gold medals but fund athletes like they’re an expense line to be minimised. You want national pride in sports but spend more on religious bureaucracy than athletic development. Pick one: either fund sports properly or stop pretending to care when the medals don’t come.

Anthony Loke Admits Malaysian Society Is 'Polarised' — But Blames Opposition for Playing Up Race

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2025-09-11

Polarised, But Don’t Blame Us

In September 2025, Transport Minister Anthony Loke told Malaysians in Hong Kong that society had become “more polarised” with intensifying race rhetoric. His diagnosis was accurate. His prescription — blame the opposition — was intellectually dishonest.

The Facts

Fact: At an event in Hong Kong, Loke acknowledged that Malaysia was increasingly divided along racial, class, and education lines, with social media amplifying hate speech and misinformation.

Fact: He attributed the polarisation primarily to “the opposition playing up such issues for political capital” — as though the government bore no responsibility for the fractures.

Fact: This came from a government that reshuffled its cabinet along racial lines in December 2025, giving UMNO more positions to shore up Malay support while reducing DAP’s presence.

Fact: JAKIM’s proposed interfaith guidelines in 2024 — which restricted how non-Muslims could engage in religious dialogue — divided communities and were widely criticised by civil society groups as discriminatory.

Fact: The government has made no move to reform race-based affirmative action policies (NEP-era quotas in education, procurement, and housing), despite PH’s manifesto promising a “needs-based” approach.

Fact: Anwar’s government has relied heavily on racial and religious rhetoric when convenient — including Anwar’s own Malay-first speeches at UMNO general assemblies, his defence of Malay-Muslim “special rights,” and his coalition’s use of the Sedition Act against those who question racial policies.

Fact: The SCMP report noted that despite Loke’s optimism about “overcoming challenges,” Malaysia’s racial polarisation metrics — measured by survey data on inter-ethnic trust — have worsened since 2023.

Why It Matters

  • Admitting the country is polarised while blaming only the opposition is the political equivalent of diagnosing the disease and prescribing someone else’s medicine. The government controls policy, appointments, media regulation, and the machinery of state. If polarisation is worsening on your watch, you own it.
  • The government’s own actions have actively contributed to division: a cabinet reshuffle that prioritised racial coalition management over competence, interfaith guidelines that alienated minorities, continued race-based policies that PH promised to reform, and a PM who code-switches between reform rhetoric for international audiences and ethno-religious rhetoric for domestic ones.
  • Loke made these remarks in Hong Kong — not in Malaysia. Malaysian politicians are remarkably candid about domestic failures when speaking to foreign audiences. At home, the same problems are denied or deflected. The gap between what they say abroad and what they do domestically is the real measure of governance.
  • The opposition absolutely plays the race card. PN’s ethno-religious populism is real and dangerous. But blaming PN for polarisation while your own government maintains race-based policies, panders to religious conservatives, and uses sedition laws against those who challenge racial orthodoxy is not analysis — it is deflection.
  • If even a DAP minister — from the party that was supposed to represent multiracial reform — cannot honestly diagnose why Malaysia is polarised, then PH has fully absorbed the BN playbook: acknowledge the symptom, blame the enemy, change nothing. *Source: *SCMP
