[BM] Setiap Ahli Kabinet Capai KPI, Kata Anwar — Pengkritik Tanya: KPI Apa?

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-25

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 25 Februari 2026, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim memberitahu Parlimen bahawa setiap ahli kabinet memenuhi KPI mereka dan menunjukkan prestasi berintegriti. Dia tidak mendedahkan apa KPI tersebut, bagaimana ia diukur atau siapa yang menilainya. Anwar menjawab ahli parlimen pembangkang yang bertanya sama ada menteri akan dipaksa meletak jawatan kerana prestasi lemah. Jawapannya: “Akauntabiliti bukan sahaja tentang hukuman tetapi juga pembetulan, penambahbaikan berterusan, dan tanggungjawab moral yang dipegang menteri untuk rakyat.” Tiada KPI pernah didedahkan secara awam untuk mana-mana menteri dalam kabinet Anwar. Tiada menteri pernah dikenakan hukuman, diturunkan pangkat atau ditegur secara awam kerana prestasi lemah.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Mendakwa pematuhan KPI 100% tanpa mendedahkan KPI adalah setara korporat memberi diri sendiri ulasan prestasi yang sempurna dan enggan tunjukkan borangnya. Ia bukan akauntabiliti — ia pensijilan sendiri. Jika setiap menteri memenuhi KPI mereka, maka sama ada KPI begitu rendah sehingga kegagalan mustahil, atau penilaian itu tidak jujur. Memandangkan 77% rakyat Malaysia melaporkan bergelut dengan kos sara hidup, kedudukan universiti merosot dan janji reformasi kekal tidak dipenuhi, dakwaan itu menggelikan.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: PM yang memegang kedua-dua portfolio PM dan Menteri Kewangan pada dasarnya menilai dirinya sendiri atas pengurusan fiskal. Tiada semakan bebas terhadap pejabat paling berkuasa di negara ini. Singapura menerbitkan metrik prestasi menteri dan mengaitkannya dengan sasaran penyampaian khidmat awam. Korea Selatan menjalankan penilaian prestasi kerajaan tahunan dengan keputusan yang diterbitkan. Pendekatan Malaysia di bawah PM “reformis”: percaya katanya. Tinjauan Pusat Merdeka Februari 2026 menunjukkan kepuasan orang ramai terhadap pengurusan ekonomi pada 38% — mencadangkan bahawa apa sahaja KPI yang wujud, orang ramai tidak bersetuju ia dipenuhi.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Setiap PM Malaysia pernah mendakwa kabinetnya berprestasi baik. Najib mendakwa menterinya menyampaikan keputusan — sementara 1MDB berdarah berbilion. Muhyiddin mendakwa pengurusan pandemik berkesan — sementara ribuan mati. Coraknya sejagat: penilaian sendiri tanpa pengesahan luaran sentiasa menghasilkan skor sempurna.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Terbitkan KPI. Teliti setiap menteri, setiap metrik, setiap sasaran dan setiap keputusan. Letakkan dalam talian. Biar rakyat lihat sama ada KPI Menteri Pendidikan termasuk skor PISA yang merosot, sama ada KPI Menteri Kesihatan termasuk 33,000 doktor kontrak masih menunggu jawatan tetap, sama ada KPI Menteri Ekonomi termasuk 77% rakyat Malaysia bergelut dengan kos. Kamu takkan — kerana kad skor itu akan menghancurkan. Kerajaan yang menilai diri sendiri secara rahsia tiada kredibiliti mendakwa akauntabiliti.

[BM] SPRM Dalam Krisis Tanpa Kepimpinan Boleh Dipercayai — Badan Pemerhati Antirasuah Kini Menjadi Skandal

Said by: DAP / Bloomberg / CNA Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-25

Ketua Pesuruhjaya Azam Baki berdepan dakwaan Bloomberg memiliki berjuta-juta ringgit saham (melanggar had pegawai awam) dan bersubahat dengan ‘mafia korporat.’ Bukannya meletak jawatan, Azam memfailkan saman fitnah RM100 juta terhadap Bloomberg. DAP, rakan gabungan PH sendiri, mendesak siasatan bebas, kata pasukan petugas kerajaan ’tidak cukup jauh.’ CNA menyifatkannya sebahagian daripada ‘ribut sempurna’ Anwar.

🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Jika agensi antirasuah sendiri korup, keseluruhan kerangka tadbir urus runtuh. SPRM sepatutnya menjadi institusi yang mempertanggungjawabkan semua pihak lain. Apabila SPRM sendiri menjadi subjek dakwaan rasuah — dan tindak balas kerajaan hanya pasukan petugas yang tidak berkesan berbanding siasatan bebas — ia mengisyaratkan tiada institusi di Malaysia yang boleh dipercayai.

💩 KENAPA INI KARUT: Janji tadbir urus utama Anwar ialah mengukuhkan institusi antirasuah. Beliau mencadangkan memisahkan peranan AG dan Pendakwa Raya (tergendala). Beliau menjanjikan kebebasan SPRM (ketua agensi itu sendiri kini menjadi skandal). Beliau menjanjikan toleransi sifar terhadap rasuah (pembantu sendiri didakwa rasuah). Setiap janji antirasuah bukan sahaja dimungkiri — ia diterbalikkan. Infrastruktur antirasuah lebih teruk di bawah PH, bukan lebih baik.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Di bawah BN, SPRM dilihat ramai sebagai terjejas secara politik, digunakan menyasarkan ahli politik pembangkang sambil mengabaikan rasuah parti pemerintah. Di bawah PH 1.0, SPRM pernah memiliki kredibiliti singkat ketika menyiasat 1MDB. Di bawah PH 2.0, kredibiliti SPRM jatuh ke paras paling rendah — ketua berperang dengan Bloomberg di mahkamah sementara agensinya sendiri berdepan dakwaan menjalankan rangkaian pemerasan korporat. Perjalanannya: terjejas → seketika dipercayai → lebih teruk dari sebelumnya.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Rakan gabungan anda sendiri kata tindak balas anda terhadap krisis SPRM tidak mencukupi. Bloomberg — salah satu saluran berita kewangan paling dihormati dunia — berpegang pada laporannya mengenai Azam Baki. Suruhanjaya antirasuah anda menjalankan saman RM100 juta terhadap kewartawanan berbanding melawan rasuah. Dan tindak balas anda? Pasukan petugas. Untuk dakwaan rasuah paling serius dalam sejarah SPRM. Itu bukan reformasi — itu mengurus optik sementara institusi terbakar.

Every Cabinet Member Meeting KPIs, Says Anwar — Critics Ask: What KPIs?

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-25

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 25 February 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told Parliament that every cabinet member is meeting their KPIs and performing with integrity. He did not disclose what those KPIs were, how they were measured, or who evaluated them. Anwar was responding to an opposition MP who asked if ministers would be forced to resign for poor performance. His reply: “Accountability is not only about punishment but also correction, continuous improvement, and the moral responsibility ministers held for the people.” No KPIs have ever been publicly disclosed for any minister in Anwar’s cabinet. No minister has been penalised, demoted, or publicly reprimanded for underperformance. Two ministers were removed in the December 2025 reshuffle — including Rafizi Ramli — but Anwar framed it as “portfolio realignment,” not performance accountability.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Claiming 100% KPI compliance without disclosing the KPIs is the corporate equivalent of giving yourself a perfect performance review and refusing to show the form. It is not accountability — it is self-certification. If every minister is meeting their KPIs, then either the KPIs are so low that failure is impossible, or the assessment is dishonest. Given that 77% of Malaysians report struggling with cost of living, university rankings are dropping, the CPI is stuck at 1MDB-era levels, and reform promises remain unmet, the claim is laughable on its face. Transparency means publishing the KPIs, the methodology, and the results — and letting the public judge. “Trust me, everyone is great” is not reform governance.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: A PM who holds both the PM and Finance Minister portfolios is essentially grading himself on fiscal management. There is no independent check on the most powerful office in the country. The Najib government also claimed ministers were performing. The Mahathir government also claimed results were being delivered. Without published metrics, every government is equally excellent — which means the exercise is meaningless. Singapore publishes ministerial performance metrics and links them to public service delivery targets. South Korea conducts annual government performance evaluations with published results. Malaysia’s approach under the “reformist” PM: trust his word. The Merdeka Center’s February 2026 poll showed public satisfaction with economic management at 38% — suggesting that whatever KPIs exist, the public disagrees they are being met.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian PM has claimed their cabinet is performing well. Najib claimed his ministers delivered results — while 1MDB bled billions. Muhyiddin claimed pandemic management was effective — while thousands died and billions in stimulus were unaccounted for. The pattern is universal: self-assessment without external verification always produces perfect scores. Anwar promised to be different. He isn’t. The KPI charade is a bipartisan tradition that PH has enthusiastically adopted.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Publish the KPIs. Go through every minister, every metric, every target, and every result. Put it online. Let the rakyat see whether their Education Minister’s KPIs include declining PISA scores, whether the Health Minister’s KPIs include 33,000 contract doctors still waiting for permanent posts, whether the Economy Minister’s KPIs include 77% of Malaysians struggling with costs. You won’t — because the scorecard would be devastating. A government that grades itself in secret has no credibility claiming accountability.

Fahmi Confirms MCMC Blocked Grindr and Blued — PH's 'Reform Government' Now Runs Digital Censorship Against Minorities

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-25

📌 What Happened

On February 25, 2026, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil confirmed in a written parliamentary reply that the Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission (MCMC) has blocked the websites of Grindr and Blued — two of the world’s largest dating and networking platforms used by gay and bisexual men. Fahmi said MCMC is now “studying several legislative measures” to curb the apps from Google Play and the Apple App Store, though he acknowledged that control over foreign-owned platforms is “subject to regulations and policies set by the said platform providers.” By early March, both apps were removed from Malaysian app stores entirely. Deputy Prime Minister Ahmad Zahid Hamidi separately announced a proposed Cyber Crime Bill to expand the government’s digital enforcement powers, including over “immoral” content.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Madani government using state infrastructure to systematically erase digital safe spaces for a persecuted minority community — and doing it quietly through written parliamentary replies and regulatory action rather than open policy debate. Fahmi framed it under the catch-all of “lewd or immoral content, exploitation, abuse, scams, exploiting children or threats towards public safety” — a masterclass in conflating consenting adults with criminals. The blocking comes alongside Deputy Religious Affairs Minister Marhamah Rosli’s directive to replace all references to “LGBT” with “budaya songsang” (deviant culture) across government communications and social media, and a multi-agency committee targeting Muslim LGBTQ individuals with so-called “psychospiritual therapy.” This is not morality policing on the margins — it is a coordinated state apparatus targeting a specific community using censorship, surveillance, and institutional erasure.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Pakatan Harapan ran on civil liberties, institutional reform, and the promise that Malaysia would stop using the state to persecute its own citizens. Fahmi Fadzil — a former civil society figure and one-time press freedom advocate — is now the minister signing off on internet censorship targeting a minority. The same government that screams about “freedom of expression” when its critics get summoned under Section 124B is perfectly comfortable ordering MCMC to block websites and pressure Apple and Google to delist apps. The Cyber Crime Bill that Zahid is pushing will replace the Computer Crimes Act 1997 with broader powers — the exact kind of legislative overreach PH promised to roll back. This is not conservative governance; it is a reformist government that has decided persecuting minorities is electorally convenient.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows a well-documented pattern of escalation under the Anwar administration. In May 2023, the Home Affairs Ministry raided Swatch stores nationwide and confiscated over 160 rainbow-coloured watches. In June 2025, Kelantan police raided a private property described as a “gay party” and arrested 20 men. In November 2025, 202 people were arrested in raids on men-only venues in Kuala Lumpur — detainees reported being denied legal counsel, subjected to degrading treatment, and having their identities publicly exposed. Justice for Sisters documented systematic procedural violations. In January 2026, a community organisation cancelled its “Glamping with Pride” event after receiving death threats. Each time, the government either endorsed the action, stayed silent, or expanded the legal tools available for the next crackdown.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysians a government that would defend civil liberties, roll back repressive laws, and protect the vulnerable from state overreach. Instead, you are building new censorship infrastructure, proposing broader cyber legislation, and running conversion programmes through state religious departments — all while Fahmi Fadzil, who once championed media freedom, signs the blocking orders. Every digital rights group, every international press freedom watchdog, and every human rights organisation monitoring Malaysia is watching this and drawing the obvious conclusion: Madani reform was never meant for everyone. If you cannot even leave people’s phones alone, stop pretending you are a reform government.

MACC 'Corporate Mafia' Allegations — DAP Demands Independent Inquiry, Anwar Resists

Said by: DAP Leaders / SCMP

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-25

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late February 2026, DAP leaders publicly stated that Anwar’s proposed task force to examine alleged misconduct at MACC “does not go far enough.” They demanded an independent inquiry instead. SCMP reported that the push came after Bloomberg’s investigation alleged MACC officials had colluded with businessmen to orchestrate hostile takeovers of listed firms — the so-called “corporate mafia” allegations. Anwar’s response — a government-controlled task force rather than an independent commission — put his “reform agenda under renewed scrutiny.” Even his own coalition allies don’t trust his anti-corruption mechanisms.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When your own coalition partner — the party most associated with governance reform — publicly says your anti-corruption response is inadequate, the credibility gap is no longer deniable. DAP is not the opposition. DAP is Anwar’s ally. If DAP doesn’t trust the task force to deliver accountability, why should anyone else? The distinction between a task force and an independent inquiry is not semantic — it is structural. A task force reports to the PM. An independent inquiry reports to the public. Anwar chose the mechanism that keeps control in his hands, not the one that maximises transparency.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s entire political brand is built on fighting corruption. Bloomberg alleges his anti-corruption chief is compromised. His own coalition partner says his response is too weak. And his answer is… a task force. This is a man who spent years in prison over politically motivated corruption charges. He knows exactly what happens when anti-corruption institutions are captured by politics. Yet when faced with credible allegations of MACC capture, he reaches for the weakest possible tool. The task force is not designed to find the truth — it is designed to manage the news cycle until the story dies.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: This follows a pattern. When Shamsul Iskandar was caught issuing support letters, Anwar’s response was a “reprimand.” When Zahid’s 47 charges were dropped, the response was silence. When Farhash Wafa’s Sabah mining scandal broke, MACC cleared him while Bloomberg alleged Anwar ordered MACC not to investigate. Every time corruption allegations touch Anwar’s inner circle, the response is always the weakest available option — never an independent investigation, never structural reform, never real consequences.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your own coalition partner is telling you publicly that your response is inadequate. Listen to them. Establish an independent inquiry with subpoena powers, public hearings, and a mandate to investigate MACC from top to bottom. If you won’t listen to DAP, listen to the principle you once went to prison for: that anti-corruption institutions must be independent of political control. A task force controlled by the PM investigating the PM’s anti-corruption chief is not independence — it is theatre.

Your anti-corruption commission is running a RM100 million lawsuit against journalism instead of fighting corruption. And your response is a ’task force.’ A task force. For the most serious corruption allegation in MACC’s history. That’s not reform — that’s managing the optics while the institution burns.
