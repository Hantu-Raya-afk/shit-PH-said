MACC Probes Senior Penang State Official Over Land Deal — Chow Says 'We Are Not Investigators'

Said by: Chow Kon Yeow, Penang Chief Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-01-31

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 31 January 2026, Penang CM Chow Kon Yeow confirmed that MACC was investigating allegations of abuse of power involving a senior state official over a land deal. Chow said the state government would ‘give full space’ to MACC and would not conduct its own internal probe. ‘We are not investigators, but we will provide full cooperation,’ he told reporters. The official was reportedly continuing to perform duties as usual during the investigation.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A senior state official is under MACC investigation for abuse of power over a land deal — and the Penang government’s response is to let the official keep working while refusing to conduct any internal review. ‘We are not investigators’ is technically true but morally bankrupt. The state government is the employer. It has a duty to ensure its officials aren’t abusing their positions. Outsourcing accountability entirely to MACC while the official keeps making decisions is not cooperation — it’s wilful blindness.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Every private company in Malaysia would suspend an employee under MACC investigation pending the outcome — especially if the investigation involves the employee’s job functions. DAP’s Penang government lets the official keep working. The justification — ‘we are not investigators’ — is a cop-out. You don’t need to be investigators to implement basic governance protocols: suspend pending investigation, secure documents, conduct an internal review. These are standard corporate governance practices that the ‘competent administration’ party apparently hasn’t heard of.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: This is not Penang’s first MACC investigation under DAP. The Guan Eng tunnel trial is ongoing. Zakat fund allegations were investigated. Land deals have been questioned repeatedly. The pattern is consistent: MACC investigates, Penang says ‘we’re cooperating,’ officials keep working, and nothing changes internally until (and unless) charges are filed.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your senior official is under MACC investigation for abuse of power over a land deal and you won’t even suspend them? ‘We are not investigators’ is not a governance philosophy — it’s an abdication of responsibility. Implement an internal review. Suspend the official pending investigation. Secure the relevant land deal documents. Or admit that DAP’s Penang is no better at self-policing than any BN state.

River Water Quality Declining — More Polluted Rivers in 2025 Than 2024

Said by: Natural Resources Ministry

Category: Other

Date: 2026-01-28

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: According to data released by the Natural Resources Ministry in late January 2026, Malaysia’s river water quality deteriorated year-over-year. Clean rivers dropped from 486 to 468. “Moderate” quality rivers rose from 161 to 171. “Slightly polluted” rivers jumped from 25 to 33. The numbers are moving in the wrong direction across every category. This decline occurred as the government aggressively pushed rare earth mining expansion — one site in Perak already turned a river visibly blue from mining discharges — and continued to promote Malaysia as a “green economy” leader.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rivers are the canary in the coal mine for environmental governance. If water quality declines while the government claims environmental progress, the claims are hollow. This is happening under a government that announced a Climate Resilience Plan, set green economy targets, and positioned Malaysia as environmentally conscious on the world stage. The data says otherwise. More polluted rivers mean worse drinking water sources, degraded ecosystems, higher water treatment costs, and health risks for communities living near affected waterways — disproportionately rural and lower-income Malaysians.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government boasts about green economy targets while presiding over measurable environmental degradation. You cannot simultaneously push aggressive rare earth mining expansion, approve data centre developments that strain water resources, and allow industrial discharge standards to go unenforced — then claim environmental leadership with a straight face. The river quality data is not subjective. It is measured. It is published. And it shows decline. The gap between the government’s environmental rhetoric and its environmental outcomes is not a communication problem — it is a governance failure. When your rivers are getting dirtier under your watch, your green targets are fiction.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s environmental enforcement has been chronically weak across administrations. The Sg Kim Kim toxic dumping in Johor (2019) hospitalised hundreds. The Perak River turned blue from rare earth mining (2025). Annual monsoon floods — worsened by deforestation and poor drainage planning — displace tens of thousands. The Department of Environment has been consistently underfunded and understaffed. Under BN, environmental protection was sacrificed for development. Under PH, the same sacrifice continues under a greener brand. The pattern is bipartisan: extract now, clean up never.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Publish the river-by-river breakdown. Name the polluters. Enforce discharge standards with real penalties — not warning letters that get filed and forgotten. If rare earth mining is turning rivers blue and water quality is declining nationally, your “green economy” narrative is a lie backed by your own ministry’s data. A government that measures environmental decline and does nothing about it is not governing — it is documenting its own failure. Fund the Department of Environment properly. Enforce industrial discharge standards. Or stop pretending you care about the environment.

Malaysia Study: Cost-Benefit Analysis of US Trade Deal Shows 'Concerns' But Government Says Don't Quit

Said by: Malaysian Government

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-01-27

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2026, Bloomberg reported that the Malaysian government announced a formal cost-benefit analysis of the controversial US-Malaysia Agreed Reciprocal Trade (ART) agreement — following months of mounting criticism from former AG Tommy Thomas (who called it “the worst agreement since independence”), former PM Mahathir, think tanks, and opposition politicians. But in the same breath, the government warned lawmakers not to quit the pact. East Asia Forum published a detailed analysis titled “The Art of the Deal Lost in US-Malaysia Pact,” arguing Malaysia surrendered critical mineral export controls, digital services taxation rights, and independent trade policy in exchange for a tariff reduction from 25% to 19% — still punitive by any standard. The deal’s details were first published on the White House website before being shared with Malaysian Parliament.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Announcing a cost-benefit analysis while pre-empting its conclusion is not governance — it is political theatre. “Study but don’t quit” tells you everything about this administration’s approach: acknowledge problems, change nothing. If the analysis shows the deal hurts Malaysia, will the government act? The answer is already in the framing. The study exists to absorb pressure, not to inform decisions. The fact that Parliament learned about the deal from the White House website is a governance failure of the first order — trade agreements that constrain national sovereignty should require parliamentary approval before signing, not after.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: “Study but don’t quit” is the Madani playbook in miniature: acknowledge the problem, promise a process, change nothing. It is the same approach applied to the AG-PP separation, UUCA repeal, toll abolition, and every other stalled reform. The cost-benefit analysis will either be buried, selectively quoted, or used to justify minor adjustments while the core agreement remains untouched. Anwar bypassed the legislature on the most consequential economic agreement in decades. When your own former AG calls your trade deal the worst since independence and your response is “we’ll study it but we’re keeping it,” the study is performative.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a history of trade agreements where the government commits first and reviews later. The TPPA under Najib faced similar criticism — Malaysia signed on to provisions critics said undermined sovereignty, pharmaceutical pricing, and labour rights. PH 1.0 withdrew from the TPPA in 2018, calling it unfair. PH 2.0 signed the ART — which critics say contains even more restrictive provisions — and now wants to “study” whether it was a good idea. The pattern of committing to deals under pressure, then conducting post-hoc reviews that change nothing, is a bipartisan Malaysian tradition.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: If you’re serious about the cost-benefit analysis, publish the full terms of the ART — not the White House summary, but the actual agreement. Let independent economists, not government-appointed task forces, evaluate the deal. If the analysis shows Malaysia got a bad deal, have the courage to renegotiate. “Study but don’t quit” is not a policy — it is a confession that you already know the answer and don’t want to act on it. Parliament deserves a vote on trade agreements that constrain national sovereignty. Give them one.

Selangor's Illegal Waste Dumping Crisis — Years of Promises, Zero Results Under Amirudin

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 26 January 2026, Amirudin Shari declared ’no more finger pointing’ and ordered a ’total crackdown’ on illegal waste dumping across Selangor. He said he would personally visit sites with local authorities and ’take firm action.’ He instructed the Land Office and local authorities to focus on cleanliness. This follows a similar declaration in early 2026 where the Selangor government announced intensified efforts to ’eradicate’ illegal waste dumping.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Amirudin has been Menteri Besar since July 2018. He has made similar promises about illegal dumping every single year. Each year, the announcements get bolder — ’total crackdown,’ ‘firm action,’ ’no more excuses.’ Each year, illegal dump sites proliferate. Residents in areas like Kuala Langat, Jenjarom, and Ijok continue to suffer from toxic waste, contaminated water, and respiratory problems. The gap between Amirudin’s rhetoric and Selangor’s reality is now a canyon.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: ‘I will personally go to the ground’ is what politicians say when they’ve run out of policy ideas. After 7+ years as MB, if Amirudin hasn’t fixed illegal dumping, it’s not because he hasn’t visited enough sites — it’s because the enforcement infrastructure, penalties, and political will don’t exist. Or worse, because the illegal dumping networks have political connections that protect them. ‘No more finger pointing’ is itself finger pointing — at previous finger pointers.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: In 2020, Amirudin promised to tackle illegal dumping. In 2022, he promised again. In 2024, he declared it a priority. In 2026, he’s still promising. The waste is still there. At some point, the promises themselves become pollution — filling the public discourse with noise while the actual waste piles up.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Seven years. Same problem. Same promises. New adjectives. If your enforcement agencies can’t stop illegal dumping after seven years of your leadership, the problem is the leader, not the agencies. Commission an independent audit of every illegal dump site, publish the landowner details, and explain why enforcement orders aren’t being executed. Or just make the same speech next January.

Zulkifli Hasan Says Work Stress Can Make People Gay — International Community Laughs, Malaysia Cringes

Said by: Zulkifli Hasan, Minister in PM’s Department (Religious Affairs)

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On January 26, 2026, Religious Affairs Minister Zulkifli Hasan submitted a written parliamentary reply to PAS MP Siti Zailah Mohd Yusoff about LGBT trends in Malaysia. He cited a 2017 study and listed “societal influence, sexual experiences, work stress, and other personal factors” as possible causes of “LGBT behaviour.” The “work stress” claim went immediately viral — globally. CNA, SCMP, NDTV, Reuters, Hindustan Times, LGBTQ Nation, and them.us all covered it. Malaysians flooded social media with mockery, sarcastically asking whether parliament MPs had “turned gay” since they apparently never work hard enough to experience stress. Zulkifli also disclosed that from 2022 to 2025, 135 cases related to “LGBT activities” were recorded as arrests or prosecutions — in a country where same-sex relations remain criminalised.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This isn’t just embarrassing — it’s damaging. A cabinet minister, speaking in an official parliamentary capacity, cited debunked pseudoscience to explain sexual orientation. This is the government’s official position on record in Hansard. The international coverage turned Malaysia into a global punchline while the country tries to attract foreign investment, host international summits, and position itself as a progressive Muslim-majority democracy. More importantly, the remark normalises the framing of LGBT identity as a pathology — something caused by external factors that can presumably be “fixed.” This framing underpins the continued criminalisation of same-sex relations and the use of state-funded “rehabilitation” programmes.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The 2017 study Zulkifli cited — Sulaiman et al. — is a small-scale Malaysian social study, not a peer-reviewed finding from any recognised international medical or psychological body. Every major medical and psychological organisation in the world — the WHO, APA, AMA, Royal College of Psychiatrists — has stated clearly that sexual orientation is not caused by stress, lifestyle, or environmental factors. The minister later issued a defensive statement saying people should “refer to the full written answer” — as if the problem was context. It wasn’t. The context makes it worse: it was an official, considered, pre-written reply. This wasn’t a slip of the tongue. It was deliberate.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a long, inglorious history of ministers making scientifically illiterate statements about LGBT people. In 2018, then-Deputy PM Wan Azizah said the government would not recognise LGBT rights because they were “against the law of nature.” In 2019, Religious Affairs officials promoted “conversion therapy” camps. Malaysia’s Federal Territories Islamic Department (JAWI) regularly raids private premises suspected of hosting LGBT individuals. PH in opposition criticised BN’s persecution of LGBT Malaysians; in government, PH has continued the same policies and rhetoric. Zulkifli Hasan’s appointment itself was a political concession — he represents the Malay-Muslim establishment within PH’s coalition.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You campaigned on “Malaysia Madani” — a civilised, progressive Malaysia. Your Religious Affairs Minister just told parliament that work stress turns people gay. The world laughed. Malaysia’s reputation took yet another hit. Where was Anwar? Where was the PMO correction? Where was the cabinet disavowal? Silence. Because correcting Zulkifli would upset the conservative base PH desperately needs. So the government let a pseudoscientific claim stand as Malaysia’s official parliamentary position on sexual orientation. Madani means civilised. There is nothing civilised about citing junk science in parliament to justify the criminalisation of who people love.

Mandatory Malay and History Across ALL Education Systems — Critics Say It Stifles Diversity

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Education

Date: 2026-01-21

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced that Bahasa Melayu and History would be mandatory across all education systems in Malaysia — including vernacular schools (SJKC and SJKT), international schools, and private institutions. Anwar said the requirement aims to “bolster the status of Bahasa Melayu as the national language” and provide “a common understanding of national history.” Critics argued this undermines constitutional protections for vernacular education under Article 152(1)(a) and stifles the diversity that makes Malaysia’s education system unique. CNA’s in-depth analysis noted the “politics and difficulties” of Malaysian education reform, describing how “language and religion” remain the most combustible policy areas.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Most vernacular school students already learn Bahasa Melayu — it is a compulsory subject in all government-aided schools. The policy change is about expanding mandatory subjects into streams that have traditionally set their own academic priorities. Meanwhile, the actual education crisis goes completely unaddressed: PISA scores keep declining, tertiary enrollment is only 42.57% versus the global average of 51.98%, teachers report students are “ruder, more restless, less curious,” bullying cases surged 17%, and the education budget share has dropped from 6.8% to 4.3% of GDP. Adding mandatory subjects to already overcrowded curricula does not improve education quality — it increases workload without fixing pedagogy, infrastructure, or teacher training.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: National unity through compulsory curriculum is a BN-era idea wearing a PH-era suit. Mahathir pushed similar initiatives. So did Muhyiddin. The framing changes; the policy doesn’t. The political calculus is transparent: mandatory Malay and History plays well with the Malay-Muslim base ahead of elections. The educational merit is secondary to the political signalling. This is identity politics disguised as curriculum reform. The real issue isn’t whether students learn Malay — it’s whether the government is using “unity” rhetoric to homogenise education while ignoring the systemic failures that are actually destroying the system: 25% math failure rates, 10,160 SPM no-shows, and teachers overwhelmed by 30+ students per class with zero structural support.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian PM has used language and curriculum policy as a political tool. Mahathir’s Vision Schools initiative in the early 2000s attempted to co-locate vernacular and national schools. It was resisted and largely failed. The PPSMI policy (teaching math and science in English) was introduced, reversed, reintroduced as DLP, and remains a political football. The pattern is consistent: use education policy for political signalling, face backlash, retreat or water down, then try again under a different name. PH is simply the latest government to reach for the “national unity through mandatory curriculum” lever.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your education system is in freefall — declining PISA scores, surging bullying, overwhelmed teachers, students disengaged and scrolling under desks — and your response is to add more mandatory subjects? Fix the teaching first. Fund the schools properly. Address the 33% of students failing at least one SPM subject. Then talk about curriculum additions. Adding mandatory Malay and History to schools that already teach Malay while ignoring the collapse in teaching quality is not reform — it is deflection. Students don’t need more subjects. They need better education.

FMT Journalist Rex Tan Arrested Under Sedition Act for Asking a 'Sensitive Question'

Said by: Royal Malaysia Police

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-17

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2026, Free Malaysia Today journalist Rex Tan was arrested at dawn under the colonial-era Sedition Act 1948 for asking a question at a public debate that linked the Gaza crisis to the treatment of ethnic Chinese in Malaysia. Police arrived at Tan’s home early on a Saturday morning — five days after the debate — and arrested him under Section 4(1) of the Sedition Act. The question was provocative but fell squarely within the bounds of legitimate public discourse. SCMP reported the arrest “casts doubts on Anwar’s reform record.” International press freedom organisations, including RSF and CPJ, condemned the arrest. Over 90 sedition-related arrests have occurred since Anwar took power in late 2022.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A journalist was arrested for asking a question — not for incitement, not for violence, not for defamation. For a question at a public debate. If this is the threshold for sedition, then every Malaysian who has ever asked an uncomfortable question about race, religion, or policy is a potential criminal. The dawn arrest is a deliberate intimidation tactic — police could have summoned Tan to a station during business hours. Instead, they chose the most psychologically coercive approach. This is not law enforcement. It is a message to every other journalist: self-censor or face the same fate. Malaysia’s press freedom ranking has stagnated under Anwar despite reform promises.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar promised to repeal the Sedition Act. In the GE14 manifesto, PH pledged abolition. In GE15, PH reaffirmed the commitment. Instead, the Anwar government has expanded its use — over 90 sedition arrests since taking power. Under Najib — the PM PH overthrew partly because of his authoritarianism — there were fewer sedition prosecutions per year. Anwar is using the Sedition Act more aggressively than the man he replaced. The Sedition Act is a colonial-era law from 1948, enacted by the British to suppress the independence movement. Anwar himself was charged under it in 2014. He called it “a draconian law used to silence dissent.” He spent years in prison as a victim of politically motivated prosecution. He knows exactly what dawn arrests feel like — and he is doing it anyway.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: PH has not only kept every repressive law it promised to repeal — it has used them more frequently and against a wider range of targets. CMA prosecutions, ONSA enforcement, Sedition Act arrests, SOSMA detentions — the reform government has become the enforcement government. Under BN, sedition was used against opposition politicians and activists. Under PH, it is used against journalists asking questions. The target has expanded while the tool remains the same. Activist Heidy Quah was investigated. Social media commentators have been arrested. The pattern is unmistakable.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You arrested a journalist at dawn for asking a question. You promised to repeal the law used to arrest him. Instead, you have used it over 90 times — more than Najib. Anwar, you were a victim of the Sedition Act. You called it draconian. You said it should be abolished. You built a political career on the injustice of politically motivated prosecution. Now your police arrest journalists before sunrise for asking questions at debates. Repeal the Sedition Act or admit that Reformasi was just a slogan you used to get into power — not a principle you intend to govern by.

Anwar Calls for 'Patience and Wisdom' After Every Crisis — But Never Accountability

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-01-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Across every major crisis from 2024 to 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim’s response has followed the same template: acknowledge the issue, call for “patience and wisdom,” dismiss critics, and move on without structural change. When Zahid’s 47 charges were dropped: “I don’t interfere in AG decisions.” When Shamsul Iskandar was caught issuing support letters: “The letters were vague” — reprimand only. When Najib’s house arrest bid was raised: “Respond with patience and wisdom.” When Bersih criticised MACC: “Unfair” — told them to wait. When diesel prices hiked: “We will be criticised with slander and lies.” When KK Mart stores were firebombed: “Don’t be prosecutor or judge.” When PH was wiped out in Sabah: “Defeat won’t stop delivery.” When Rafizi accused him of social media attacks: called it a “distraction.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Calling for calm is not governance. It is conflict avoidance dressed as statesmanship. In every single case, the underlying structural issue — prosecutorial independence, patronage, coalition contradictions, institutional accountability — remains unaddressed. The script never changes because the strategy never changes: absorb the blow, issue platitudes, wait for the news cycle to move on, and avoid making any decision that might upset any faction of the coalition. A PM who responds to every crisis with the same emotional appeal is not managing crises — he is postponing reckonings.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: “Patience and wisdom” is not a governance philosophy — it is a deflection technique. When your DPM’s 47 corruption charges get dropped and your response is “I don’t interfere,” you are not respecting institutional independence — you are hiding behind it. When stores are firebombed and you tell the public not to be “prosecutor or judge,” you are not calming tensions — you are failing to protect citizens. When Bersih — the movement that helped put you in power — calls MACC a political tool and you call it “unfair,” you are not defending institutions — you are defending yourself. The “patience and wisdom” playbook works because it sounds reasonable. But reasonableness is not the same as accountability.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The “call for calm” strategy is not unique to Anwar — it is the default response of every Malaysian PM facing uncomfortable scrutiny. Najib called for “patience” during 1MDB. Muhyiddin called for “unity” during the Sheraton Move. Ismail Sabri called for “stability” during his backdoor government. The language is interchangeable because the purpose is identical: buy time, change nothing. What makes Anwar’s version more offensive is that he built his entire career promising to be different. He was the one who said accountability should be immediate, transparent, and structural. In power, he reaches for the same script as every PM before him.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Stop managing feelings and start fixing systems. Every crisis you’ve responded to with “patience and wisdom” has the same root cause: institutions that answer to politics instead of the public. Separate the AG and PP. Establish IPCMC. Give MACC real independence. Reform the Pardons Board. These are the structural changes you promised. Instead, you offer platitudes — because structural change would constrain your own power. A PM who manages feelings instead of fixing systems is not governing. He is postponing reckonings — and the bill comes due at GE16.

Palestine Solidarity But Silence on Uyghurs — Double Standards Exposed

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Critics

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-01-13

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Throughout 2025 and into 2026, Malaysia under Anwar Ibrahim maintained one of the most vocal stances globally on Palestinian rights — cutting diplomatic ties with Israel, boycotting Israeli-linked companies, and lecturing world leaders about “moral hypocrisy.” However, critics pointed out a glaring double standard: Malaysia’s silence on China’s persecution of Uyghur Muslims in Xinjiang, Iran’s brutal crackdown on internal dissent, the Yemen humanitarian crisis, and Myanmar’s genocide against Rohingya Muslims. Reddit discussions among Malaysians accused the government of “selective activism.” The Hindu reported Malaysia “slams double standards on Gaza atrocities” — while practising its own double standards on other Muslim-majority crises.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Foreign policy credibility depends on consistency. When a government loudly champions one Muslim cause (Palestine) while staying silent on other Muslim causes (Uyghurs, Yemen, Rohingya), the activism looks strategic rather than principled. Malaysia’s silence on China is particularly damning because China is Malaysia’s largest trading partner — worth over RM400B annually. The calculus is transparent: champion Palestine (which costs nothing economically) while ignoring Uyghur persecution (which would cost Chinese investment). Principled advocacy doesn’t have a price tag. Selective advocacy does — and the price is credibility.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar personally asked world leaders to condemn Israeli atrocities in Gaza. When asked about Uyghur concentration camps — which the UN Human Rights Office itself described as “serious human rights violations” — Malaysia’s response has been near-silence. The government has not recalled its ambassador to China. It has not imposed sanctions. It has not even issued a strongly worded statement. The reason is obvious: China’s investment in Malaysia is too valuable to jeopardise with human rights criticism. Supporters argue Palestine is a “clear-cut occupation” and therefore different. But the Uyghur crisis involves documented mass detention, forced sterilisation, and cultural erasure of Muslims — by any measure, a human rights catastrophe. The distinction is not moral; it is economic.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysian selective activism on Muslim causes is a bipartisan tradition. Mahathir championed Palestine and Bosnia while maintaining warm ties with China during the early stages of Uyghur persecution. Najib deepened Chinese economic ties through 1MDB-related deals while Muslim Rohingya refugees drowned in the Andaman Sea. Anwar has simply continued the pattern with more eloquent rhetoric. The formula: champion causes that are geopolitically cheap, ignore causes that are geopolitically expensive.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You lecture the world about double standards on Gaza. But your silence on Uyghur Muslims — imprisoned, sterilised, and culturally erased by China — is the loudest double standard of all. If Palestinian lives matter (and they do), then Uyghur lives matter too. Rohingya lives matter too. Yemeni lives matter too. You can’t pick which Muslim crises to care about based on trade balances. Either human rights are universal or they are bargaining chips. Right now, under your government, they are bargaining chips.

Free the press you promised to free. Fight the corruption you promised to fight. Then — and only then — will your moral authority on the world stage be anything more than theatre. Until then, every Gaza speech is a reminder of what you promised Malaysia and didn’t deliver.
