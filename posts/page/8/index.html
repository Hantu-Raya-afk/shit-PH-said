[BM] Sabah Tolak PH Di Peti Undi — Kini Mustapha Sakmud Kata Dia Suara Mereka Di Putrajaya

Said by: Mustapha Sakmud, Sabah PH Chairman / Sabah and Sarawak Affairs Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 11 Mac 2026, Pengerusi PH Sabah dan Menteri Hal Ehwal Sabah dan Sarawak Mustapha Sakmud memberitahu FMT bahawa PH Sabah akan berperanan sebagai ‘saluran’ untuk memastikan isu negeri disuarakan di peringkat persekutuan. Beliau berkata beliau berhasrat memanfaatkan pelantikan menteri — dibuat pada 15 Februari — untuk membawa kebimbangan rakyat Sabah ke mesyuarat Kabinet.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

PH dihancurkan dalam pilihan raya negeri Sabah pada 29 November 2025. PKR hanya berjaya dengan satu kerusi. DAP tidak menang langsung. GRS meraih 29 kerusi, Warisan meraih 25, dan rakyat Sabah menjelaskan dengan nyata mereka tidak mahu PH mewakili mereka di peringkat negeri. Namun tiga bulan kemudian, Anwar memberikan Mustapha portfolio menteri persekutuan — memasukkan PH ke dalam ruang politik Sabah melalui pintu belakang. Ini bukan perwakilan demokratik. Ini naungan sebagai pengganti mandat.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Kamu tidak boleh kalah pilihan raya seteruk ini dan kemudian mendakwa kamu suara rakyat. PH menang satu kerusi di seluruh dewan undangan negeri Sabah. Itu bukan saluran — itu surat penolakan. Hujah Mustapha berbunyi: ‘Rakyat Sabah tidak mengundi kami, tetapi kami dapat jawatan Kabinet, jadi kami akan wakili mereka.’ Ini tepat jenis keistimewaan politik tanpa usaha yang PH pernah tuduh BN amalkan.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Ini mengikuti tradisi panjang Malaysia menggunakan pelantikan persekutuan untuk mengimbangi kekalahan pilihan raya di peringkat negeri. BN melakukannya selama berdekad-dekad di Sabah dan Sarawak. PH, sebagai pembangkang, mengecam amalan yang sama ini. Kini dalam kerajaan, mereka mengamalkan buku permainan yang sama.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu menjanjikan reformasi. Kamu menjanjikan kuasa datang daripada rakyat, bukan daripada Putrajaya. Dan kini kamu memberikan kerusi Kabinet kepada seorang yang gabungannya menang satu kerusi di seluruh pilihan raya negeri dan menyuruhnya mewakili rakyat yang menolaknya. Jika ‘rakyat Sabah akan menghargainya,’ seperti dakwaan Mustapha, maka ujilah — bertanding pilihan raya kecil di Sabah atas rekod PH.

Anwar Bans Raya Open Houses for 'Fiscal Discipline' — Days After Announcing RM15 Billion in Cash Handouts and RM500 Bonuses

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced that all government agencies, GLCs, and GLICs are banned from holding Aidilfitri open house celebrations this year — a decision agreed upon in Cabinet that morning. He framed it as a signal of fiscal discipline amid global uncertainty caused by the Middle East conflict. In the same breath, Anwar also announced tighter restrictions on overseas travel for ministers and government officials, saying only pre-scheduled and essential foreign engagements would be approved. A special Cabinet meeting has been called for March 13 to assess the fiscal situation.

🔥 Why It Matters

This austerity performance arrives just two days after the government announced it would maintain the RON95 subsidy at RM1.99 — and less than a week after Anwar brought forward RM15 billion in Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah (STR) cash handouts plus RM500 Special Financial Assistance for civil servants and mosque personnel, all conveniently timed before Hari Raya. The government is spending record sums on direct cash transfers — the most electorally potent form of spending — while cancelling community open houses that cost a fraction of that. This is not fiscal discipline. This is selective austerity: cut the visible, communal celebrations while flooding individual bank accounts with cash right before a rumoured snap election.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

You cannot announce the largest cash transfer programme in Malaysian history one week, then cancel Raya open houses the next week and call it prudence. Open houses are community events — they cost millions. The STR programme costs billions. If the Middle East crisis is so dire that civil servants can’t eat ketupat at a government function, why is it not dire enough to pause the RM15 billion in pre-election cash? Because open houses don’t buy votes. Cash in bank accounts before polling day does. The travel restriction is window dressing — ’except for those already scheduled’ is a loophole wide enough to fly the entire Cabinet through.

📜 Previous Cases

This is textbook Malaysian crisis theatre. Mahathir did it during the 1997 Asian financial crisis — austerity for the rakyat, bailouts for cronies. Najib talked fiscal discipline while 1MDB bled billions. Anwar himself mocked BN’s festive-season handouts when he was in opposition, calling them vote-buying. Now he’s doing the same thing, but bigger: record STR payouts, record BKK bonuses, and a Raya ban for optics. The pattern is always the same — cut the things that don’t win elections, spend lavishly on the things that do.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi, not repackaged BN-era electoral spending with a piety overlay. If you’re genuinely worried about the fiscal position, start with the RM15 billion in cash transfers you just announced, not the nasi lemak at government open houses. Cancel the political appointments bleeding FSBs dry. Freeze the patronage spending. Don’t lecture the rakyat about moderation while your government’s chequebook is wide open for every vote bank that matters before GE16. This is not discipline — this is performance.

Anwar Says Petroleum Supply 'Secure Until May' — While Strait of Hormuz Burns and Ships Get Attacked

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim held a live-streamed press conference in Putrajaya to address the escalating Middle East energy crisis. He claimed that Malaysia’s petroleum product supplies are “sufficient at least until May 2026,” citing checks with Petronas. He reaffirmed the RM1.99 subsidised RON95 price under Budi Madani, called it a “difficult decision,” and announced a special task force of ministers to review economic impacts daily. He also tasked the Deputy Prime Minister and KPDN with cracking down on fuel smuggling.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Prime Minister of an energy-importing nation telling the public not to worry about supply — while the Strait of Hormuz, through which roughly a fifth of the world’s oil transits, is under active military disruption. A Thai cargo ship was attacked near the Strait on the same day. Brent crude hit US$119 per barrel days earlier before easing to around US$90. Airlines across Asia are hiking fares. The IEA is recommending a record 400-million-barrel emergency reserve release. Anwar’s reassurance lands in a context where every major energy market signal is flashing red — and “secure until May” is only two months away.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

“Secure until May” is not reassurance — it’s a confession dressed up as comfort. Two months of supply in the middle of a shooting war near the world’s most important oil chokepoint is not energy security. It’s a countdown. Just days ago, on March 6, Anwar himself admitted he could only hold the RON95 subsidy for “one or two months.” Now the supply itself has a two-month shelf life. And the solution? More committees. A daily economic impact review panel. A smuggling task force. None of this addresses what happens in June if the Strait stays contested, if Petronas hedges unwind, or if global allocation priorities shift away from Southeast Asia. The press conference was a performance of control, not a demonstration of it.

📜 Previous Cases

Anwar has a well-documented pattern of using Petronas as a rhetorical shield. In 2023, he promised fuel subsidy rationalisation “within months” — it took until late 2024 to even introduce Budi Madani, and the targeted subsidy still doesn’t cover diesel properly. In 2024, he told Malaysians the economy was “on track” while inflation ate into household budgets. The “we checked with Petronas” line echoes previous administrations — Najib’s government used similar assurances during the 2014-2015 oil price crash while raiding Petronas dividends to plug fiscal holes. The playbook is always the same: invoke Petronas, announce a committee, and hope the crisis passes before the next press conference.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysians a government that tells the truth, even when it’s hard. So tell the truth: two months of supply is not security — it’s a ticking clock. Where is the contingency plan for June? Where is the strategic petroleum reserve policy you never built? Where is the long-term energy diversification strategy beyond “Petronas says we’re fine”? You banned Raya open houses for “fiscal discipline” the same week you announced RM15 billion in cash handouts. You formed three new committees in one press conference. Committees don’t produce oil. If the Strait stays shut and supply chains fracture, “we checked with Petronas” will not keep the lights on or the lorries moving. Stop performing calm and start building resilience.

Cabinet Finally Discusses Azam Baki Report — Punts Decision to Chief Secretary, Refuses to Reveal Findings

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister / Cabinet

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil announced that Cabinet had received and discussed the findings of the special committee — chaired by Attorney General Mohd Dusuki Mokhtar — investigating MACC chief commissioner Azam Baki’s shareholding controversy. Cabinet’s decision: hand the matter to Chief Secretary to the Government Tan Sri Shamsul Azri Abu Bakar to determine “next steps.” Fahmi refused to disclose the report’s contents. When asked directly whether Cabinet discussed the extension of Azam’s contract, Fahmi said: “It was not discussed.” Separately, Cabinet instructed the police, Securities Commission, MACC itself, and the Inland Revenue Board to continue investigating Bloomberg’s “corporate mafia” allegations — including seizing proceeds believed to have criminal elements.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Anwar administration’s formal institutional response to months of crisis over the MACC chief — and it amounts to a bureaucratic handoff. The Bloomberg exposé dropped weeks ago. DAP demanded an RCI. PKR backbenchers warned Anwar risks his legitimacy. Civil society has been summoned and interrogated. And after all of that, Cabinet’s big move is to delegate to a civil servant and refuse to tell the public what the AG’s committee actually found. The message is clear: accountability will be managed, not delivered. By routing the decision through the Chief Secretary rather than making a political call, Anwar insulates himself from direct responsibility for whatever happens next — whether Azam stays, goes, or quietly expires in May.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The Cabinet is the highest executive decision-making body in the country. It received the report. It discussed the findings. And then it said: not our call. This is the equivalent of a CEO receiving an internal audit on a compromised compliance officer and telling HR to “handle it.” The Prime Minister has the power to act. He chose not to. The refusal to disclose the findings is especially damning — if the report cleared Azam, they would say so. If it condemned him, they would act. The silence tells you everything. Meanwhile, the instruction to probe “corporate mafia” allegations sounds tough until you remember MACC is being asked to investigate… itself. The fox is auditing the henhouse, and Cabinet just handed it the clipboard.

📜 Previous Cases

Azam Baki’s shareholding scandal has been a slow-rolling crisis since 2022, when the Securities Commission confirmed he held millions of shares in a public company — a potential breach of anti-corruption rules. Anwar, upon taking power, renewed Azam’s contract multiple times while promising reform. In February 2026, Bloomberg published detailed allegations of a “corporate mafia” operating within MACC. Anwar’s response has followed a consistent pattern: acknowledge the problem, set up a committee, delay action, and let the news cycle move on. The Straits Times reported on March 9 that Anwar had privately told Cabinet he would not renew Azam’s contract when it expires May 12 — but still refused to suspend him in the meantime.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia “institutional reform” and “zero tolerance for corruption.” Your own manifesto said MACC would be made independent and accountable. Instead, your Cabinet received a report on the most compromised anti-corruption chief in Malaysian history and responded with: “Ask the Chief Secretary.” That is not reform. That is not accountability. That is not even governance. It is cowardice dressed in procedure. If you cannot fire the man running your anti-corruption agency while he is under investigation for the very corruption he is supposed to fight, then every word of your reform agenda is decoration. Act, or admit you never intended to.

Cabinet Greenlights Port Klang Third Terminal on Carey Island — A 5,000-Acre Megaproject With GLC Fingerprints All Over It

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Transport Minister Anthony Loke announced that Cabinet has approved the development of Port Klang’s Third Terminal on Carey Island — a megaproject that has been in limbo for years. The project involves over 5,000 acres of land, with SD Guthrie (a GLC under PNB) and the Selangor State Development Corporation (PKNS) already staking claims on thousands of hectares of land and seabed. PKNS was allocated 1,011 hectares of seabed land and a 688-hectare Yayasan Selangor plot was placed under joint PKNS management. PM Anwar issued a directive in December 2025 to accelerate the project, and now Cabinet has formally greenlit it.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is one of the largest infrastructure approvals of the Anwar administration — and it has GLC and state-linked corporation fingerprints all over it. The land allocation to PKNS and the SD Guthrie partnership were locked in before any competitive tender for the port itself. Carey Island is home to the Mah Meri Orang Asli community, and environmental and displacement concerns have dogged this project for years. Anwar promised in December 2025 that the project would proceed “with safeguards for Orang Asli and the environment,” but no independent environmental review or community consent mechanism has been made public. The approval comes amid a Middle East crisis, fiscal austerity theatre (banning Raya open houses), and pre-election positioning — making the timing politically convenient for announcing a mega-development that benefits Selangor’s PH-aligned state machinery.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH ran on transparency, open tenders, and ending GLC capture of public assets. What we got instead is a 5,000-acre megaproject carved up between a PNB-linked plantation giant and the Selangor state GLC before the public even saw a development plan. The Selangor MB’s office admitted the land allocation was “decided a few weeks ago” — before Cabinet even approved the project. That’s not governance; that’s pre-positioning. And the Orang Asli? They get “safeguards” in speeches and displacement in practice. This is exactly the kind of opaque, GLC-driven megaproject that PH promised to end when it was in opposition.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s infrastructure megaproject history is littered with cost overruns, opaque procurement, and GLC enrichment. The East Coast Rail Link (ECRL) was renegotiated but still lacks full transparency. The Penang Transport Master Plan has faced years of environmental and governance controversy. Port Klang itself was the site of the Port Klang Free Zone (PKFZ) scandal — a RM12.5 billion fiasco involving inflated land costs, political patronage, and criminal charges. PH’s own 2018-2020 government inherited that mess and promised never to repeat it. Now they’re greenlighting another massive port project on the same stretch of coast, with pre-allocated GLC land deals and no visible competitive process.

🎯 PH Callout

You told voters you’d end the cosy GLC-state nexus that turns public land into private profit. You said open tenders and institutional reform would replace backroom deals. So where is the open tender for Port Klang Terminal 3? Where is the independent environmental impact assessment? Where is the Orang Asli consultation? A 5,000-acre development involving SD Guthrie, PKNS, and Yayasan Selangor land — all allocated before Cabinet even voted — is not reform. It’s the same game with a Madani label. If you can ban Raya open houses for “fiscal discipline,” you can publish the full procurement framework for a multi-billion ringgit port before you approve it.

Cabinet Maintains RON95 at RM1.99 — Anwar Calls It a 'Difficult Decision' While Dodging Subsidy Reform for the Third Year Running

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced on March 11, 2026 — following a Cabinet meeting — that the price of RON95 petrol under the Budi95 targeted subsidy mechanism will remain at RM1.99 per litre. He called it a “difficult decision,” noting that the market price of RON95 has risen roughly threefold due to the escalating US-Iran conflict disrupting oil markets and threatening Gulf shipping routes. RON97 had already risen to RM3.25, unsubsidised RON95 to RM2.67, and diesel to RM3.12. Anwar framed the decision as a sacrifice for the rakyat.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not generosity — it is the collapse of reform. Anwar’s government promised targeted fuel subsidy restructuring as a cornerstone of fiscal discipline. The Budi95 mechanism, launched as a watered-down compromise, was supposed to be the first step toward removing blanket subsidies. Instead, a geopolitical crisis has given the government the perfect excuse to shelve reform indefinitely while spending billions on untargeted fuel subsidies ahead of widely expected snap elections. Every week the subsidy is maintained at current market spreads, Malaysia hemorrhages fiscal resources that could go to healthcare, education, or infrastructure.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar has been “about to reform” fuel subsidies since he took office in November 2022. In 2023, it was “next year.” In 2024, it was “soon.” In 2025, the Budi95 mechanism finally launched — then immediately became a political tool rather than a fiscal one. Just five days ago, Anwar admitted he could only hold the RM1.99 price for “one or two months.” Now, with Hari Raya approaching and elections rumoured for mid-2026, the Cabinet “decides” to maintain it — as if there was ever a chance they’d raise fuel prices weeks before a festive season and a potential snap poll. This isn’t a difficult decision. It’s the only decision a government terrified of voters would make.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has been trapped in the fuel subsidy cycle for decades. Najib’s government removed RON95 subsidies in 2014 with a managed float, only for Mahathir’s PH 1.0 government to reintroduce price ceilings. Anwar promised to break the pattern with targeted subsidies — means-tested, fiscally responsible, reform-driven. Instead, Budi95 kept the same RM1.99 price for the vast majority of motorists and excluded only foreign-registered vehicles and luxury cars. The IMF, World Bank, and Malaysia’s own fiscal policy advisors have repeatedly warned that blanket fuel subsidies are regressive, benefiting the wealthy more than the poor. Anwar knows this. He cited these studies himself when he was in opposition.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you’d be different. You said you’d make the hard calls Najib and Muhyiddin wouldn’t. You quoted fiscal responsibility in every speech, promised targeted subsidies in every manifesto, and lectured the opposition about populism from every podium. Now you’re spending billions to keep fuel cheap during Ramadan, ahead of Hari Raya, ahead of elections you’re quietly preparing for. This isn’t reform. This is the oldest trick in Malaysian politics — and you swore you’d never play it. Every ringgit spent on untargeted subsidies is a ringgit stolen from the structural reforms you promised the rakyat.

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim said Malaysia’s petroleum supply is ‘sufficient at least until May 2026,’ even as global oil markets reel from the Strait of Hormuz disruption and retaliatory strikes between Iran, the US, and Israel that began on February 28.

🔥 Why It Matters

The government’s instinctive response to a crisis that is already hitting Malaysian wallets — RON97 up 60 sen, diesel up 80 sen, food inflation incoming — is to form a committee to ‘assess’ the damage. Not to act. Not to freeze tolls, cap food prices, mandate work-from-home, or cut wasteful political spending. To study. This is the Madani crisis management playbook distilled to its essence: announce a committee, schedule a meeting, promise a report, and tell the rakyat to wait. The committee doesn’t even meet until March 12, two full weeks after the conflict began. Fuel prices have already spiked. Supply chains are already disrupted. 💩 Why It’s Bullshit

This is not governance. This is bureaucratic theatre dressed up as crisis management. The committee is led by the Petronas chairman — the same entity that benefits from higher oil prices — and includes the Economy Minister, who was publicly projecting 4-4.5% growth just days ago as if the region wasn’t on fire. The committee reflex is not unique to PH, but PH was supposed to be different. They promised responsive, transparent, and accountable governance. Instead they’ve perfected the art of the bureaucratic stall.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised a government that acts, not one that studies the problem while the rakyat burns through their savings at the pump. Two weeks into the worst geopolitical energy crisis since the Gulf War, your response is a committee that hasn’t met yet, a report that hasn’t been written, and a special Cabinet meeting that hasn’t happened. The rakyat doesn’t need a committee to tell them diesel is more expensive. They need a government that acts like it understands urgency. Every day you ‘assess,’ someone skips a meal, absorbs a delivery surcharge, or watches their margins collapse. Stop studying the fire. Put it out.

The ’loopholes’ Fahmi wants to close are not loopholes — they are what remains of free expression in a system already stacked with repressive laws. When PH says ‘stricter enforcement,’ they don’t mean protecting minorities. They mean giving the state more power to detain first and ask questions later.

📜 Previous Cases

DAP’s own Ramkarpal Singh questioned the continued use of the Sedition Act as recently as January 2026, noting it contradicts PH’s reform agenda. In 2018, Lim Guan Eng called the Sedition Act ‘draconian.’ In opposition, Fahmi himself criticised BN’s use of speech laws to silence dissent. The Sedition Act remains on the books. The Printing Presses Act remains on the books. SOSMA remains on the books. Not one has been repealed. And now, instead of delivering on those promises, PH is proposing to add to the arsenal. The trajectory is clear: from reformers to enforcers in one parliamentary term.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would tear down the walls BN built around free speech. Instead, you’re adding barbed wire. If you cannot handle criticism about race, religion, and royalty without reaching for new detention powers, you have become the thing you said you were fighting. Every new law you pass to silence 3R speech will be inherited by the next government — including the ones you call extremists. Think about that before you hand them a sharpened tool. Your manifesto said repeal. The rakyat is still waiting.

KPDN Scrambles on Fuel Smuggling After Oil Tops $100 — Armizan Promises Enforcement That Should Have Been in Place for Years

Said by: Armizan Mohd Ali, Minister of Domestic Trade and Cost of Living (KPDN)

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 10–11, KPDN Minister Armizan Mohd Ali announced that his ministry would “strengthen enforcement” against fuel smuggling and intensify Ops Tiris operations at national borders, after global oil prices topped US$100 per barrel due to the escalating US-Israel-Iran conflict. Armizan acknowledged that the widening gap between Malaysia’s subsidised fuel prices (RON95 at RM1.99) and market prices in neighbouring countries creates “high risk” of smuggling. He told the public there was “no need for concern or panic buying” and claimed contingency plans were in place. Separately, NST reported that Anwar appointed Deputy Prime Minister Fadillah Yusof to chair a special enforcement committee specifically targeting diesel smuggling (single-source: NST).

🔥 Why It Matters

Malaysia has known about fuel smuggling for years. The government restructured diesel subsidies in 2024 partly because of cross-border leakage. Op Tiris has been running since at least 2023. Yet when the biggest energy crisis in a decade hits — with oil past $100 and the Strait of Hormuz under threat — the government’s response is to announce that it will “strengthen” enforcement it should have had in place all along, and form yet another committee. This is reactive governance dressed up as decisive action. The gap between Malaysia’s RM1.99 RON95 and unsubsidised market prices of RM2.67+ makes Malaysia a fuel smuggling magnet, and every week of delay costs the rakyat.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Armizan’s reassurance that supply is “sufficient” and that “contingency plans are in place” is the same script every KPDN minister reads when things go sideways. No contingency plan has ever been made public. No enforcement statistics on Op Tiris outcomes have been shared. And the formation of a special committee chaired by the DPM — on a problem that already has an existing task force — is textbook bureaucratic theatre. If your enforcement was adequate, you wouldn’t need a new committee every time oil prices spike. The government is spending more time announcing what it will do than actually doing it.

📜 Previous Cases

This pattern goes back decades. In 2022–2023, diesel smuggling across the Thai and Singapore borders was so rampant that KPDN itself admitted losses of hundreds of millions of ringgit annually. The targeted diesel subsidy implemented in June 2024 was supposed to solve the problem — but Armizan’s own admission that “the risk of smuggling remains high” proves it didn’t. In 2025, Anwar boasted about saving RM4 billion in subsidy leakage — a claim that was never independently verified. Now, with oil at $100+ and the subsidy gap wider than ever, the same enforcement gaps are back, and the government’s playbook hasn’t changed.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised subsidy reform would be smart, targeted, and leak-proof. It’s none of those things. You’ve had three years to build border enforcement infrastructure, digitise fuel distribution tracking, and close the smuggling loopholes that every analyst warned you about. Instead, the moment a crisis hits, you scramble to announce “strengthened enforcement” that should already exist and form a committee that duplicates existing task forces. Armizan telling Malaysians “don’t panic buy” while admitting smuggling risk is “high” is not reassurance — it’s a contradiction. Stop forming committees. Start governing.

Mohamad Hasan Says Travel Agencies Must Bring Home Stranded Umrah Pilgrims — 'It Cannot Be Left to the Government Alone'

Said by: Mohamad Hasan, Foreign Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

Foreign Minister Datuk Seri Mohamad Hasan, speaking at a press conference in Putrajaya on March 11, declared that travel companies and agencies managing umrah packages must take full responsibility for bringing home pilgrims stranded by the West Asia conflict. He said airlines must also honour their tickets: ‘If the ticket is with Malaysia Airlines, MAS must bring them home. If it is an AirAsia ticket, AirAsia must do so.’ He added bluntly: ‘It cannot be left to the Malaysian government alone.’ This came the same day the government’s first evacuation flight — a chartered Malaysia Airlines A330-300 — landed at KLIA with just 163 Malaysians from Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Jordan and Syria, after days of airspace closures and flight disruptions across the Gulf.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is a textbook case of the government reframing a crisis-response failure as a question of private-sector accountability. On March 3, Mohamad Hasan told the Dewan Negara that the situation in the Gulf was ‘under control’ and there was ’no need for an evacuation plan.’ Eight days later, his ministry was coordinating emergency repatriation flights while hundreds of Malaysians — including umrah pilgrims who had been stranded for three to five days at airports in Dubai, Doha, Abu Dhabi and Bahrain — waited for someone to get them home. The pivot from ’no evacuation needed’ to ’travel agencies must handle it’ is not reassurance — it is damage control dressed as delegation.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The Foreign Ministry exists precisely for moments like this: when Malaysian citizens are stranded abroad during a conflict and need their government to act. Telling travel agencies and airlines to sort it out is not crisis leadership — it is passing the bill. Many of these pilgrims are elderly, travelling on budget packages, and do not have the leverage to demand rescheduled flights from airlines whose operations are in chaos. The government evacuated 163 people on one chartered flight and then turned to the microphone to say, essentially, ’the rest of you are someone else’s problem.’ Meanwhile, the National Security Council coordinated the evacuation under PM Anwar’s directive — so the government clearly has the machinery. It just chose to limit its use and outsource the rest.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of slow or inadequate evacuation responses. During the 2023 Sudan crisis, Malaysians were stranded for days before Wisma Putra mobilised. During COVID-19, repatriation flights were chaotic, with competing agencies and unclear responsibility chains leaving citizens in limbo. The playbook is familiar: delay, then deflect. Mohamad Hasan’s ‘under control’ assurance on March 3 echoes every Malaysian foreign minister who has ever downplayed a developing crisis until it became undeniable — then blamed someone else for the gaps.

🎯 PH Callout

PH came to power promising a government that puts rakyat first — ‘Kerajaan Madani,’ a compassionate, responsive administration. When your citizens are sleeping on airport floors in the Gulf because airspace is shut and missiles are flying, telling travel agencies to figure it out is not compassion. It is bureaucratic cowardice. Evacuate your people first. Sort out who pays later. That is what a government that means its slogans would do.
