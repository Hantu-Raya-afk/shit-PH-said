Negri Sembilan MB Admits His Driver Runs Red Lights — 'I Have No Choice, Look at My Facebook Schedule'

Said by: Aminuddin Harun, Menteri Besar of Negri Sembilan (PH-Lenggeng)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-04

📌 What Happened

On March 4, 2026, Negri Sembilan Menteri Besar Datuk Seri Aminuddin Harun publicly admitted that his official car routinely runs red lights, after a viral video showed a vehicle believed to be his using blue strobe lights and a siren to blast through a red light on Jalan Rasah. Speaking to reporters after a state executive council meeting, Aminuddin said: “I admit that we have to beat the light at times, but I have no choice, as there are sometimes several official events lined up in a day.” He pointed to his Facebook page as proof of his hectic schedule. He added that a police officer is always present in the car, and that his office has sought “necessary approvals from the relevant authorities.”

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not about a traffic violation. This is about a PH state leader publicly declaring that the rules of the road do not apply to him — and framing it as a reasonable accommodation for public service. In a country where ordinary Malaysians receive summonses, demerit points, and fines for the same offence, a Menteri Besar is casually telling reporters he does it regularly and expects understanding. The message to the public is unmistakable: there is one set of rules for leaders and another for everyone else. This is the exact culture of elite entitlement PH promised to dismantle.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Aminuddin’s defence is remarkable in its audacity. He didn’t deny it. He didn’t promise to stop. He told Malaysians to check his Facebook schedule to understand why he needs to break the law. The ‘I had a cop in the car’ justification is not exoneration — it’s an admission that the state apparatus is being used to facilitate rule-breaking by the powerful. If the MB’s schedule is so packed that traffic laws become optional, the answer is better scheduling, not blue strobes and sirens. An apology that ends with ‘but state leaders need some leeway’ is not an apology. It is a declaration of privilege.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of political elites treating traffic laws as suggestions. VIP motorcades, outriders, and sirens have been normalised to the point where ordinary motorists are expected to yield or face consequences. In 2022, a viral video of a minister’s convoy forcing vehicles off the road drew widespread criticism. PH, in opposition, was vocal about such abuses of power. Now in government, their own MB is doing the same thing and asking for sympathy. The entitlement transcends party lines — it’s baked into how Malaysian political elites understand their own importance.

🎯 PH Callout

You campaigned on humility. You told Malaysians that the era of feudal politics and VIP culture was over. Your own Prime Minister once made a show of flying economy class and driving himself. Now your Negri Sembilan MB is running red lights with police cover and telling the rakyat to look at his Facebook to understand why he’s too important to stop at a traffic light. If you can’t even hold your state leaders to basic traffic laws, what hope is there for institutional reform? Follow the rules or change them through proper process. You don’t get to break them and call it public service.

5G Dual Network Fiasco — DNB vs TM Contract War Over Termination

Said by: Digital Nasional Bhd / Telekom Malaysia

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 March 2026, Telekom Malaysia (TM) attempted to terminate its 5G wholesale agreement with Digital Nasional Berhad (DNB), the government entity managing Malaysia’s 5G rollout. DNB rejected the termination, saying the contract runs until 2032 and remains binding. Malaysia’s dual 5G network strategy — introduced after Anwar’s government reversed the original single-network plan — has been plagued by policy flip-flops, RM2 billion in DNB debt, and the controversial awarding of the second 5G network to U Mobile.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia’s 5G policy has become a case study in how political interference destroys industrial strategy. The original single-network plan under DNB was already controversial. Anwar’s government then introduced a “dual network” approach — splitting the rollout between DNB and a consortium — which created contractual chaos, duplication of infrastructure, and uncertainty for telcos. Now TM and DNB are fighting in public over who owes what to whom. While politicians argue about contracts, Malaysians still have patchy 5G coverage and telcos still don’t have clarity on their obligations.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The dual network decision was widely seen as a political move to accommodate UMNO-linked business interests rather than a technical improvement. U Mobile’s selection for the second network was questioned by industry analysts. DNB’s RM2 billion debt was accumulated under a plan the government itself designed. Now the government is watching its own creation implode in a contractual dispute — and Malaysians are paying for it through delayed rollout and higher costs. This is what happens when telecoms policy is driven by patronage rather than engineering.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s telecoms sector has a long history of politically motivated decisions. The award of 3G and 4G licences was repeatedly criticised for favouring politically connected firms. DNB itself was created under Muhyiddin’s government with minimal transparency. Anwar’s dual network decision was supposed to fix this — instead, it doubled the problems. The pattern: create a government entity, load it with debt, let it fight with the private sector, blame the previous administration.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You inherited a flawed 5G plan and made it worse. The dual network was your decision. The contractual chaos is your responsibility. While DNB and TM fight over termination clauses, Malaysians are stuck with incomplete 5G infrastructure and a RM2 billion bill. Fix the policy, resolve the contracts, and stop letting political interests dictate telecoms strategy. Or admit that 5G in Malaysia is just another patronage project with better branding.

Category: Other

Date: 2026-03-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 March 2026, Science Minister Chang Lih Kang confirmed the renewal of Lynas Rare Earth’s operating licence for 10 years, despite ongoing concerns about radioactive waste storage. Greenpeace Malaysia warned that the country risks becoming a “pollution hub.” Environmental groups said government claims about thorium extraction from the waste are “scientifically unsound.” Close to a million tonnes of radioactive waste from Lynas’s processing operations remain stored in Pahang, with no permanent disposal solution in place.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rare earth processing is a critical strategic industry, and Malaysia’s Lynas plant is the largest outside China. But strategic value doesn’t justify ignoring environmental and health risks. A 10-year renewal without resolving the waste disposal question means Malaysia committed to another decade of accumulating radioactive residue with no exit plan. The government is prioritising geopolitical positioning (reducing Western dependence on Chinese rare earths) over the health and environmental concerns of Pahang residents who live near the waste.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH campaigned against Lynas in GE14. DAP in particular made Lynas a flagship environmental issue. Fuziah Salleh — now a PH senator — built her political career on opposing the plant. Now the same coalition has renewed the licence for a full decade. The justification — that thorium can be commercially extracted from the waste — has been dismissed by environmental scientists as technically unproven at scale. This is a government choosing foreign investment over the promises it made to the communities it asked to vote for it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Lynas has been politically toxic since its Kuantan plant opened in 2012. PH 1.0 imposed strict conditions on waste removal that were never enforced. BN extended the licence before GE15. PH 2.0 has now gone further with a 10-year renewal. Every government promises to resolve the waste issue; none have. The pattern: campaign against Lynas in opposition, accommodate Lynas in government, hope voters forget.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You campaigned against this plant. Your own members built careers opposing it. Your environmental base voted for you partly because of Lynas. And now you’ve given them a 10-year renewal while nearly a million tonnes of radioactive waste sits in Pahang with no disposal plan. If the waste can be processed, prove it. If it can’t, explain why you renewed anyway. Your voters in Pahang deserve an honest answer, not strategic doublespeak.

Anwar Condemns US-Israel Strikes on Iran in Parliament — 'We Can No Longer Swallow' Western Human Rights Lectures, PH MP Calls for US Embassy Boycott

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Syahredzan Johan (PH-Bangi)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 2 March 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim tabled an emergency motion in the Dewan Rakyat condemning the US and Israel’s strikes on Iran, which began on 28 February and killed at least 201 people including Iran’s Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei.

Anwar called the strikes a “flagrant violation of international law” and described Western lectures on human rights and democracy as hypocrisy:

“In my engagements with the American and European leadership, I am uneasy about their lectures on human rights and democracy. We can no longer swallow such arguments from them.”

The motion passed unanimously with bipartisan support. Opposition Leader Hamzah Zainudin praised Anwar for a “stern and firm statement.”

But the most politically significant moment came from PH backbencher Syahredzan Johan (Bangi), who called on all MPs to boycott events hosted by the US embassy in Kuala Lumpur:

“Let’s not go. Even though we get invited to their Independence Day celebrations, let’s not go. We need to show that we can no longer accept what they do.”

Anwar also signaled a diplomatic repositioning toward Iran, calling for closer education and trade ties, claiming Malaysia was “the only country to show a firm, brave stance.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

The condemnation itself is unremarkable — Malaysia has consistently supported Palestinian and Iranian sovereignty. What matters is the escalation in rhetoric and the gap between words and actions.

First, Anwar calling Western human rights lectures “hypocrisy” is rich coming from a prime minister whose own government is under fire for:

  • Protecting MACC chief Azam Baki despite multiple corruption allegations
  • Using police investigations to target critics under the guise of a “Zionist plot”
  • Halving Najib’s sentence while the 1MDB debt still runs into billions
  • Failing to pass even basic institutional reforms like the PM tenure bill Anwar is lecturing the West about hypocrisy while running a government that has abandoned virtually every reform promise it made.

Second, Syahredzan’s call to boycott the US embassy is performative populism with real diplomatic consequences. Malaysia’s economy depends on US trade and investment. The US is Malaysia’s third-largest trading partner. Calling for a diplomatic boycott from the floor of Parliament is not a principled stand — it’s cheap political theatre that someone else will have to clean up.

Third, the pivot toward Iran is diplomatically reckless. Iran’s Shia denomination is officially considered “deviant” in Sunni-majority Malaysia. Anwar is signaling solidarity with a theocratic state whose religious identity his own government criminalises. The contradiction is absurd.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Let’s count the contradictions:

On human rights hypocrisy:

  • Anwar says he can “no longer swallow” Western lectures on human rights. This is the same man who spent years lobbying Western governments, NGOs, and media outlets for his own human rights — during his imprisonment under Mahathir and Najib. His entire political comeback was built on international sympathy and foreign advocacy.

  • Now that he’s in power, foreign advocacy is a “Zionist plot” and Western human rights standards are “hypocrisy.” The only thing that changed is which side of the PM’s desk he sits on. On the US embassy boycott:

  • Syahredzan Johan is a PH backbencher calling for a diplomatic boycott while his own government maintains full diplomatic, trade, and military relations with the US. Malaysia just signed a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership with Washington in October 2025.

  • If PH is serious about opposing US actions, why stop at embassy cocktail parties? Cancel the trade deal. Recall the ambassador. Oh wait — that would cost money. On Iran solidarity:

  • Malaysia criminalises Shia practice. Anwar’s own religious affairs apparatus treats Shia Muslims as deviants. Declaring solidarity with Iran while persecuting its co-religionists at home is not principled — it’s opportunistic.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

  • 2018-2022: Anwar’s own foreign lobbying. Anwar and PKR allies engaged Washington consultancies, international media, and foreign governments to pressure the Najib and Muhyiddin administrations. He accepted this support gratefully. Now foreign engagement is subversion.
  • October 2025: Malaysia-US Comprehensive Strategic Partnership. Just five months before calling US human rights lectures “hypocrisy,” Anwar’s government signed an upgraded bilateral deal with Washington covering trade, critical minerals, and market access.
  • Malaysia’s track record on Iran. Malaysia has historically been cautious with Iran due to the Sunni-Shia divide. Anwar’s pivot is a departure from decades of careful diplomatic hedging, driven by the emotional politics of the moment rather than strategic calculation.
  • 2024-2025: Gaza solidarity without substance. Malaysia has been vocal on Palestine but has taken no meaningful economic or diplomatic action against Israel beyond rhetoric. The pattern is clear: loud words, zero follow-through.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

Anwar, you told Parliament: “We can no longer swallow such arguments from them.”

Fair enough. But the rakyat can no longer swallow yours either.

You lecture the West on hypocrisy while your MACC chief sits on RM800,000 in undisclosed shares. You call foreign advocacy a “Zionist plot” after spending decades doing exactly the same thing. You signal solidarity with Iran while your religious authorities criminalise Shia practice at home.

And your backbencher calls for boycotting US embassy parties — the absolute lowest-cost, lowest-risk form of protest imaginable — while your government maintains a full strategic partnership with Washington.

This isn’t principled foreign policy. It’s Ramadan-season populism. You’re performing outrage for domestic consumption while doing nothing that would actually cost you anything.

If you want to be taken seriously on human rights and sovereignty, start at home. Fix your own institutions. Reform your own enforcement agencies. Stop using conspiracy theories to silence critics. Then — and only then — will anyone take your lectures to the West seriously.

Transparency means publishing the KPIs, the methodology, and the results — and letting the public judge. “Trust me, everyone is great” is not reform governance.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: A PM who holds both the PM and Finance Minister portfolios is essentially grading himself on fiscal management. There is no independent check on the most powerful office in the country. The Najib government also claimed ministers were performing. The Mahathir government also claimed results were being delivered. Without published metrics, every government is equally excellent — which means the exercise is meaningless. Singapore publishes ministerial performance metrics and links them to public service delivery targets. South Korea conducts annual government performance evaluations with published results. Malaysia’s approach under the “reformist” PM: trust his word. The Merdeka Center’s February 2026 poll showed public satisfaction with economic management at 38% — suggesting that whatever KPIs exist, the public disagrees they are being met.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian PM has claimed their cabinet is performing well. Najib claimed his ministers delivered results — while 1MDB bled billions. Muhyiddin claimed pandemic management was effective — while thousands died and billions in stimulus were unaccounted for. The pattern is universal: self-assessment without external verification always produces perfect scores. Anwar promised to be different. He isn’t. The KPI charade is a bipartisan tradition that PH has enthusiastically adopted.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Publish the KPIs. Go through every minister, every metric, every target, and every result. Put it online. Let the rakyat see whether their Education Minister’s KPIs include declining PISA scores, whether the Health Minister’s KPIs include 33,000 contract doctors still waiting for permanent posts, whether the Economy Minister’s KPIs include 77% of Malaysians struggling with costs. You won’t — because the scorecard would be devastating. A government that grades itself in secret has no credibility claiming accountability.
