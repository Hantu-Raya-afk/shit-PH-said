MACC Seizes RM6M and Freezes Assets Linked to Selangor Menteri Besar Inc in Sand Mining Corruption Probe

Said by: Amirudin Shari / MACC

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-10-18

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In October 2024, the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC) seized RM6 million and froze assets linked to Selangor Menteri Besar Incorporated (MBI) in connection with a sand mining corruption probe. MACC Chief Commissioner Azam Baki confirmed that five firms linked to a Selangor politician were under investigation for alleged corruption involving a sand mining concession. The probe uncovered a network of contracts and payments that raised questions about the integrity of Selangor’s sand mining approvals.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Selangor MBI is the state’s investment arm — directly under the Menteri Besar’s purview. When MACC seizes millions linked to the MB’s own corporate vehicle, it implicates the governance of the state’s highest office. Sand mining is one of Selangor’s most corruption-prone sectors, involving environmental destruction, river pollution, and billions in revenue. The fact that MACC had to intervene means internal checks failed.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Amirudin has been MB since 2018. Sand mining corruption in Selangor has been documented for years. Environmental groups have warned about illegal mining destroying riverbanks and poisoning water sources. Under Amirudin’s watch, the problem grew until MACC had to step in. That’s not governance — that’s negligence. The MB either didn’t know (incompetence) or didn’t act (complicity). Neither is acceptable.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: In September 2025, MACC confirmed new developments in the MBI corruption probe. A former Selangor GLC officer was charged over RM1.7 million in graft. Earlier, activist Chegubard lodged a MACC report alleging RM10 billion in irregularities linked to the Selangor Maritime Gateway project. The pattern is consistent: Selangor under PH is a corruption hotspot that the government refuses to acknowledge.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: MACC is investigating your state investment arm for corruption. Your own GLC officers have been charged. Your sand mining sector is a mess. And your response is silence. If this happened under a BN menteri besar, PKR would be holding nightly ceramah demanding resignation. Under Amirudin? Crickets.

Forest City Special Financial Zone — 0% Tax for the Ultra-Rich While Rakyat Pays 8% SST

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Securities Commission

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-10-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In October 2024, the PH government launched the Forest City Special Financial Zone (SFZ) in Johor, offering 0% corporate tax for up to 15 years for qualifying businesses, 0% tax for ultra-high-net-worth family offices, and incentives for fintech, fund management, and digital asset companies. The SFZ was designed to rescue Forest City — a RM400 billion Chinese-backed property development that sat largely empty — by transforming it into a financial hub. Meanwhile, ordinary Malaysians pay 8% SST on everyday services including healthcare, education, and rent.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The SFZ creates a two-tier tax system: 0% for the ultra-rich who park their wealth in Forest City, and 8% SST for ordinary Malaysians buying groceries, seeing a doctor, or sending their children to school. The optics are devastating — the government is simultaneously expanding SST to cover basic necessities while offering tax-free status to billionaires’ family offices. This is not investment attraction — it is a wealth shelter branded as economic development.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Forest City was a failed property project. Billions of dollars in Chinese investment produced a ghost city that Mahathir himself mocked. Instead of letting the market price in the failure, the Anwar government is using public policy — tax exemptions, regulatory incentives, infrastructure spending — to rescue a private development. The beneficiaries are property developers and ultra-wealthy investors. The cost is borne by Malaysian taxpayers who lose the revenue that tax-exempt businesses would otherwise contribute. Socialising losses and privatising gains is the definition of crony capitalism — and PH promised to end it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a long history of special economic zones that promised transformation and delivered tax giveaways. The Iskandar Development Region offered tax incentives for over a decade — Johor’s inequality worsened. Labuan’s offshore financial centre attracted shell companies more than genuine investment. Cyberjaya was supposed to be Malaysia’s Silicon Valley — it became an office park. Each zone follows the same pattern: generous tax incentives, minimal clawback mechanisms, and benefits that accrue to capital rather than labour. Forest City SFZ adds another chapter to this pattern.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You’re offering 0% tax to billionaires’ family offices while charging 8% SST on the rakyat’s healthcare and education. You’re rescuing a failed Chinese property project with public policy incentives. You’re socialising the losses and privatising the gains — exactly what you accused BN of doing. Publish a cost-benefit analysis of the SFZ. Show how many Malaysian jobs it will create versus how much tax revenue Malaysia loses. Explain why ultra-wealthy family offices deserve 0% tax when hawkers pay SST. Or admit that the SFZ is a bailout for developers, not an economic strategy for Malaysia.

GISBH Child Abuse Scandal — Amirudin Says Abuses Were 'Concealed by Shifting to Business'

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Social

Date: 2024-09-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Following the explosive GISBH (Global Ikhwan Services and Business Holdings) scandal involving sexual and physical abuse of children in welfare homes, Amirudin stated that the organisation had ‘concealed abuses by shifting to business’ — suggesting that GISBH’s transition from the banned Al-Arqam movement into a legitimate business empire allowed it to hide criminal activity. Hundreds of children were rescued from GISBH-linked premises across Malaysia, with many found in Selangor.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: GISBH operated openly in Selangor for years. Its premises were known. Its business operations were visible. Its welfare homes housed hundreds of children. The Selangor state government — which oversees local authority licensing, land use, welfare inspections, and Islamic affairs — either failed to detect the abuse or failed to act on it. Amirudin’s explanation that GISBH ‘concealed’ itself by becoming a business is an admission that his state government was unable to monitor organisations operating openly within its borders.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: You don’t get to govern Selangor for six years, have a massive child abuse ring operating in your state, and then say ’they hid it well.’ State welfare departments are supposed to inspect children’s homes. Local authorities issue business licences. JAIS oversees Islamic organisations. If all these agencies missed what was happening in GISBH homes, that’s not concealment — that’s institutional failure. And institutional failure in Selangor is Amirudin’s responsibility.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Al-Arqam was banned in 1994 — thirty years ago. Its transformation into GISBH was well-documented. Journalists and activists had raised concerns about the organisation for years. The pattern of authorities ignoring well-documented abuses until they explode into public scandal is a Malaysian governance tradition that PH was supposed to end.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Children were abused in welfare homes in your state, under your governance, by an organisation that has been on the radar for thirty years. Your excuse is that they ‘concealed’ themselves by becoming a business. That is not an explanation — it is a confession that your welfare inspection, licensing, and monitoring systems are broken. Fix them, or the next GISBH is already operating in Selangor while you make speeches about development.

Hannah Yeoh Fangirls Over Thai Shuttler Who Just Eliminated Malaysia's Lee Zii Jia — Tone-Deaf at the Olympics

Said by: Hannah Yeoh, Youth and Sports Minister (then)

Category: Social

Date: 2024-08-07

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In August 2024 at the Paris Olympics, Malaysian shuttler Lee Zii Jia was knocked out in the men’s singles semi-final by Thailand’s Kunlavut Vitidsarn — a devastating loss for Malaysian fans hoping for an Olympic medal. Days later, Youth and Sports Minister Hannah Yeoh posted selfies with Kunlavut on Instagram, praising him. SCMP reported she was “smashed on social media for ‘fangirl’ post.” Mothership called it “insensitive.” Yeoh defended her post, saying Malaysia is “not a sore loser” and that it was her duty to “instil a culture of healthy competition.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Timing matters in politics. A Sports Minister’s job at the Olympics is to support the national team. Taking selfies with the athlete who just crushed your country’s medal hopes — and posting them on social media — shows a fundamental lack of political instinct. Nobody would have objected if Yeoh had congratulated Kunlavut privately or weeks later. The issue is posting fangirl selfies while the wound was still fresh, while millions of Malaysians were still processing the disappointment. Read the room.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Yeoh’s defence — promoting sportsmanship — is a classic politician’s deflection. Nobody accused her of lacking sportsmanship. They accused her of lacking sensitivity. There’s a difference. Imagine a Japanese Sports Minister posting selfies with an American swimmer who just eliminated Japan’s favourite — it wouldn’t happen, because competent politicians understand that the public’s emotions matter during the Olympics. Yeoh’s tone-deafness isn’t new. It’s a pattern of a politician who confuses personal branding with ministerial responsibilities.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: This wasn’t Yeoh’s only Olympics controversy. The Malaysian Olympic attire design for Paris 2024 also sparked public criticism, with many questioning why the national kit looked uninspired. Yeoh, as Sports Minister, bore responsibility for the overall presentation of Team Malaysia. Instead of a proud Olympic moment, Malaysia got two controversies. Yeoh’s tenure as Sports Minister was marked by a series of tone-deaf moments that suggested disconnection from public sentiment rather than deliberate insensitivity.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You sent Hannah Yeoh to Paris to support Malaysia’s Olympic athletes. She came back with selfies with the guy who beat our best shuttler. The Sports Minister’s job is to champion Malaysian sport, not audition for a Thai fan club. The fact that she couldn’t understand why Malaysians were upset tells you everything about how disconnected PH ministers are from public sentiment. Political instinct isn’t optional — it’s the job.

Fahmi Fadzil Forces Social Media Licensing on Platforms — Critics Call It PH's Version of Internet Censorship

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2024-07-28

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 28 July 2024, the PH government announced that social media platforms with more than 8 million users in Malaysia would be required to apply for a regulatory licence from MCMC, effective 1 August. Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil said the measure was needed to combat cyber offences including online scams, child exploitation, and deepfakes. By late 2025, Fahmi was threatening legal action against non-compliant platforms. In January 2026, MCMC temporarily blocked X’s Grok AI feature over deepfake concerns. ARTICLE 19 called on Malaysia to “halt hasty imposition of mandatory electronic verification,” warning about implications for privacy and free speech.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Social media licensing gives the government a lever to pressure platforms into compliance with content removal requests. While the stated goals — combating scams, protecting children — are legitimate, the mechanism creates obvious censorship risks. When a government can threaten to revoke a platform’s operating licence, it has enormous power over what content stays up and what comes down. In a country with a history of using the Communications and Multimedia Act to prosecute critics, handing MCMC more regulatory power over social media is a loaded gun.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH rode social media to power. Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, and WhatsApp were the platforms that allowed PH to circumvent BN’s control of mainstream media. Without social media, the 1MDB scandal might never have reached the Malaysian public at the scale it did. Now in power, PH is licensing the same platforms, threatening legal action against those that don’t comply, and blocking AI features. The “child safety” framing is clever but transparent — the licensing framework applies to all content, not just child safety. PH repealed the Anti-Fake News Act and called themselves defenders of internet freedom. Then they introduced a licensing regime that gives MCMC even more power than the Act they repealed.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, the Anti-Fake News Act 2018 was PH’s exhibit A for authoritarian internet control. PH repealed it in 2018. Now PH has replaced it with something arguably more powerful: a licensing regime that gives MCMC ongoing regulatory leverage over platforms, rather than just post-hoc prosecution. PH also opposed MCMC’s use of Section 233 of the CMA to prosecute critics under BN. Under PH, the same section continues to be used, and platforms themselves can now be held liable through the licensing framework.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You repealed the Anti-Fake News Act and called yourselves defenders of internet freedom. Then you introduced a licensing regime that gives MCMC even more power over social media than BN ever had. You’re licensing the internet while claiming to protect it. The child safety argument is legitimate — but the licensing framework goes far beyond child safety. Be honest about what you’re building: a regulatory tool that can pressure platforms into compliance with government content demands. That’s not reform. That’s BN’s internet controls with a PH label.

Diesel Price Jumps 56% Overnight — Anwar Says Critics Will Attack Him Regardless

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-06-10

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 10 June 2024, diesel prices in Peninsular Malaysia jumped from RM2.15 to RM3.35 per litre — a 56% increase overnight. Finance Minister II Amir Hamzah Azizan announced the price float on 9 June, effective the next morning — one day’s notice for a shock that rippled through logistics, food prices, and small businesses. Sabah, Sarawak, and Labuan remained at RM2.15. Anwar told reporters: “We must also know that whatever we do, we will be severely criticised with all sorts of slander and lies.” His response to citizens facing a 56% cost increase was to complain about the quality of criticism he was receiving.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The diesel subsidy bill had ballooned to RM14.3 billion annually, and most economists agreed the blanket subsidy was regressive. The policy may be economically necessary, but the execution was politically negligent. A 56% price hike with one day’s notice is a shock to households, small businesses, fishermen, and farmers who cannot absorb overnight cost increases. Phased implementation over 6-12 months would have achieved the same fiscal goal with a fraction of the pain. The Diplomat noted that diesel increases have cascading effects — logistics, food transport, construction, agriculture, and fishing all depend on diesel.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s dismissal of all criticism as “slander and lies” is not just tone-deaf — it is contemptuous. Many of the people struggling with higher diesel costs are PH voters. They are not slandering the PM — they are telling him they cannot afford to fill their trucks. The two-tier pricing system created an immediate arbitrage problem — diesel smuggling from Sabah and Sarawak increased, adding enforcement costs. The government promised RM14.3 billion in savings would fund public transport and targeted aid. But PADU was sidelined. Public transport expansion outside KL remains negligible. The money was saved, but the promised reinvestment is largely invisible to the people who sacrificed.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: When Najib attempted fuel subsidy reform in 2014, PH was among the loudest critics. When petrol prices fluctuated under the managed float system, PH accused BN of burdening the people. Now in government, PH implemented an even more aggressive subsidy cut — 56% overnight versus Najib’s gradual float — and called critics slanderers. The hypocrisy of implementing tougher measures than those you attacked your predecessors for is the defining feature of PH’s economic governance.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You raised diesel by 56% overnight with one day’s notice. Your response to criticism was to call it slander. Your promised reinvestment — public transport, targeted aid — hasn’t materialised. PADU was sidelined. Buses didn’t appear. If the rakyat must tighten their belts, show them you’re tightening yours first. Publish where the RM14.3 billion in savings went. Deliver the public transport improvements you promised. Phase future subsidy reforms gradually. Or keep imposing overnight shocks and dismissing the pain as slander.

Diesel Price Jumps 56% Overnight as PH Removes Subsidies — Rakyat Told to 'Tighten Belts'

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II; Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-06-10

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 10 June 2024, the PH government removed diesel subsidies in Peninsular Malaysia, causing the price to jump from RM2.15 to RM3.35 per litre overnight — a 56% increase. Finance Minister II Amir Hamzah Azizan framed it as “targeted subsidy rationalisation” and announced a Subsidised Diesel Control System for eligible sectors. The impact was immediate: transport costs surged, food prices climbed, and businesses passed the costs on to consumers. The government promised B40 and M40 households would be protected through BUDI MADANI cash assistance, but payments were widely criticised as insufficient.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Subsidy reform is necessary — Malaysia was spending RM14.3 billion on diesel subsidies in 2023, much of which leaked to smuggling and foreign vehicles. The fiscal case is strong. But the execution was brutal. A 56% overnight price increase hits working Malaysians hard, especially in logistics-dependent sectors. Research showed businesses across multiple industries faced immediate cost pressures, passing costs directly to consumers — exactly the outcome PH promised to prevent. Sabah and Sarawak were exempted, creating a two-tier system that was as much about coalition management as regional sensitivity.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH spent years attacking BN’s subsidy policies — both for being too generous (fiscal waste) and for any attempt to reform them (burdening the rakyat). Classic opposition have-your-cake-and-eat-it. Now in power, PH removed diesel subsidies with a 56% overnight hike and told Malaysians it was for their own good. The BUDI MADANI cash assistance was modest compared to cascading cost increases. The savings were supposed to fund public transport and targeted transfers. By 2026, public transport outside KL remains minimal, and PADU — the targeting mechanism — was sidelined. The money was saved. The reinvestment was not delivered.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: When Najib attempted fuel subsidy reform in 2014, PH was among the loudest critics. The broader pattern is consistent: PH promises populist policies in opposition, then implements the same tough economic measures in government that they attacked their predecessors for. Subsidy reform, GST (abolished, replaced with less efficient SST), and toll abolition (still hasn’t happened) all follow the same trajectory. AMRO’s analysis recommended a phased approach — PH chose shock therapy instead.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Subsidy reform was necessary. Nobody disputes that. But you could have done it gradually, transparently, and with adequate cushioning. Instead, you did it fast and hard, while your ministers still enjoy subsidised fuel cards. Publish where the RM14.3 billion in annual savings went. Deliver the public transport improvements you promised. If the rakyat must tighten their belts, show them you’re tightening yours first. Or keep implementing overnight shocks while promising reinvestment that never arrives.

Dual 5G Network: Billions Wasted on Political Compromise — One Network Would Have Sufficed

Said by: Telecom Analysts / Consumers

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-06-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In mid-2024, the PH government shifted Malaysia’s 5G rollout from a single wholesale network (Digital Nasional Berhad, DNB) to a dual-network model — allowing a second 5G network to be built alongside DNB. The move was widely seen as a political compromise to accommodate major telecom operators with political connections who had opposed the single-network model. The dual-network approach doubles infrastructure costs, splits spectrum efficiency, and delays rural coverage expansion — all to solve a political problem, not a technical one.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: 5G infrastructure costs billions. A single wholesale network — the model adopted by countries like South Korea and parts of Europe — is more spectrum-efficient and avoids duplicating expensive infrastructure. The dual-network model means Malaysia will build two parallel 5G networks where one would have sufficed. The additional cost — estimated in the billions — comes from public funds and consumer pricing. Rural Malaysians, who were supposed to benefit from 5G coverage expansion, will wait longer because resources are now split between two networks instead of concentrated on reaching underserved areas.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The single-network model was working. DNB had achieved over 80% population coverage by mid-2024. The dual-network shift was not driven by technical failure or consumer demand — it was driven by telecom operators who wanted to maintain their competitive advantage and the political actors who supported them. The government framed it as “promoting competition.” But you don’t need two 5G networks to have competition — you need one efficient network with fair wholesale access for all operators. Duplication is waste, not competition.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s telecom sector has a long history of political patronage. Under Mahathir 1.0, telecom licences were distributed to politically connected companies. Under BN, spectrum allocation benefited government-linked entities. The dual-network decision fits the same pattern: technical policy shaped by political interests rather than engineering logic. South Korea built one of the world’s fastest 5G networks using a competitive but coordinated approach. Malaysia is building two slower networks to keep politically connected telcos happy.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You chose politics over engineering. One 5G network would have cost less, delivered more coverage, and reached rural areas faster. Instead, you approved a second network to accommodate telecom operators with political leverage. Publish a cost-benefit analysis comparing single vs. dual networks. Disclose which operators lobbied for the change and what access they were promised. Or keep spending billions on duplicated infrastructure while rural Malaysians wait for connectivity that one network could have delivered years ago.

MCMC Blocks Critical News Sites — MalaysiaNow, Asia Sentinel, UtusanTV Censored

Said by: Freedom House Report 2024

Category: Social

Date: 2024-06-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In mid-2024, MCMC blocked news outlets critical of the government including MalaysiaNow, UtusanTV, TV Pertiwi, and Asia Sentinel. A new Code of Ethics effectively grants or bars media access based on compliance — creating a de facto licensing system for journalism. A man was sentenced to 6 months for a Facebook post insulting a Sultan. Freedom House’s 2024 report documented Malaysia’s internet freedom as declining under PH, flagging the blocking of news sites and the expansion of MCMC’s content moderation powers as key concerns.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Blocking entire news outlets — not individual articles, but entire publications — is authoritarian behaviour regardless of which party does it. MalaysiaNow, Asia Sentinel, and UtusanTV were blocked because they published content the government found inconvenient, not because they were publishing illegal material. When the government controls which news outlets Malaysians can access, it controls the information environment. Freedom House explicitly flagged this as a decline in internet freedom — under a government that promised to protect it.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar once relied on independent media to survive politically. Without Malaysiakini, Sarawak Report, and international outlets covering 1MDB, his political comeback would not have been possible. Now his government is blocking news sites that publish critical coverage. The Code of Ethics creates a carrot-and-stick system where compliant media gets access and critical media gets blocked. This is not regulation — it is state management of the press. The irony of a former political prisoner censoring the press that helped free him is beyond parody.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, Sarawak Report was blocked for its 1MDB coverage. The Malaysian Insider was shut down. The Printing Presses and Publications Act required annual licences for print media. PH condemned all of this as authoritarian. Under PH, the approach is subtler but equally effective: instead of banning publications through licensing, you block them through MCMC and control access through the Code of Ethics. The end result is identical — the government decides which media the public can consume.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You survived because independent media told your story when the government wanted to silence you. Now you’re the government, and you’re silencing independent media. Unblock MalaysiaNow, Asia Sentinel, and UtusanTV. Withdraw the Code of Ethics that functions as a gatekeeping mechanism. Let journalists do their jobs without government approval. Or keep blocking the press while claiming to protect press freedom — and hope Malaysians don’t notice the contradiction.

Sources: Freedom House
