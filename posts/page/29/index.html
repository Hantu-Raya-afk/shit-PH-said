Anwar's Three-Year Report Card: Steady Economics, Broken Promises, Collapsing Reform Credibility

Said by: Multiple analysts and media

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As Anwar Ibrahim marked three years as Prime Minister in late 2025, multiple analysts and media outlets delivered a consistent verdict: steady economics, shattered reform credibility. GDP growth exceeded 5%, foreign investment hit record highs, and unemployment reached decade lows. But on reform — the actual reason PH was elected — the record is dismal: the Sedition Act was expanded with 90 arrests, UUCA remains intact, ICCPR is unratified, AG-PP separation was sent to committee after three years of delay, Zahid’s 47 corruption charges were dropped, MACC is under fire for politicisation, a journalist was arrested for asking a question, and Nurul Izzah was installed as PKR No. 2.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Three years is not “early days.” It is more than half a parliamentary term. If reform was coming, it would have started by now. Instead, what Malaysia has is a government that performs competent economic management while systematically abandoning the democratic promises that brought it to power. The trade-off is now explicit: you get GDP growth, but you lose civil liberties, institutional independence, and accountability. The question is no longer “will Anwar reform?” The question is: was reform ever the goal?

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s defence is always “we inherited a mess” and “give us time.” But they’ve had time — more time than PH 1.0 ever got. The Sedition Act wasn’t just left on the books — it was weaponised more aggressively than under Najib. UUCA wasn’t just kept — students who protested were ignored. The AG-PP separation wasn’t just delayed — it was tabled and then immediately sent to a committee to die quietly. Every single flagship reform promise has been either abandoned, delayed, or reversed. Economic competence is not a substitute for democratic accountability — it’s what authoritarian governments use to justify staying in power.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: PH 1.0 (2018-2020) had 22 months and accomplished partial reforms before collapsing via the Sheraton Move. PH 2.0 has had 36 months with a larger majority and accomplished fewer reforms. Mahathir’s first tenure showed how economic success can coexist with democratic regression for decades. The pattern is clear: Malaysian PMs use economic performance as a shield against reform demands, and it works — until it doesn’t.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You were elected on reform. You delivered economics. Those are not the same thing. BN also delivered economics — for decades — while gutting institutions. You are now doing the same thing with better PR. Repeal the Sedition Act. Abolish UUCA. Ratify ICCPR. Separate the AG and PP. Restore MACC independence. Or stop pretending you’re different from the people you replaced — because after three years, the only difference is the slogan.

Highway Concessions: Rakyat Pays Tolls to Make Cronies Rich — Toll Abolition Promise Broken AGAIN

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Transport Ministry

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The PH government’s core manifesto promise to abolish or significantly reduce highway tolls has been systematically broken. Instead of toll abolition, the government spent RM591 million in 2025 to merely freeze toll hikes — paying concessionaires with public money to not raise prices they shouldn’t be charging in the first place. Festive toll-free periods were cancelled. The fundamental structure of highway concessions — where private companies collect guaranteed revenue from a captive public while the government underwrites their profits — remains completely unchanged. Abolishing tolls was a core PH campaign promise in both GE14 (2018) and GE15 (2022). Three years into government, the promise has been quietly shelved with no accountability.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The toll system represents a regressive tax on mobility: lower-income workers who commute long distances to factory jobs in industrial areas pay a disproportionate share of their income on tolls. Highway concessions, many awarded under previous administrations through questionable processes, generate enormous profits for politically connected companies. The government claims it cannot afford to buy back concessions, yet finds money for political appointments, propaganda, and JAKIM. The rakyat pays twice: once at the toll booth, once through taxes that subsidise the freeze.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: You promised to abolish tolls. Twice. In two general elections. Instead, you’re paying private companies RM591 million of public money to maintain the status quo. This is the worst possible outcome: broken promise, continued tolls, AND public money transferred to concessionaire shareholders. The concession agreements are structured to guarantee private profits at public expense — guaranteed traffic volumes, guaranteed returns, government compensation for shortfalls. The concessionaires literally cannot lose. And the “reform” government has done nothing to change this structure. Not renegotiation. Not buyback. Not restructuring. Nothing.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The toll abolition promise has been made by every opposition coalition since the early 2000s. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) didn’t abolish tolls despite 22 months in government. PH 2.0 has now been in power longer and still hasn’t delivered. The concessionaires — many linked to politically connected businesspeople — know the pattern: politicians promise abolition to win votes, then discover it’s “too expensive” once in office. The tolls continue. The profits flow. The cycle repeats. And by GE16, PH will probably promise toll abolition again.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: How many times can you promise to abolish tolls and not deliver before voters stop believing you? You’ve now spent more public money FREEZING toll hikes than some countries spend building entire road networks. Renegotiate the concession agreements. Publish the terms — let Malaysians see what their government signed them up for. If the agreements are so lopsided that buying them back costs “billions,” that itself is the scandal. Who negotiated these deals? Who benefited? And why has your government done nothing to fix them in three years?

National Debt Servicing Eats 16% of Revenue — Every Sixth Ringgit Goes to Paying Interest

Said by: Ministry of Finance / Bloomberg

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s debt servicing costs consume approximately 16% of federal government revenue — meaning roughly every sixth ringgit collected in taxes goes straight to paying interest on accumulated debt rather than funding schools, hospitals, or infrastructure. With the debt-to-GDP ratio at 64.6% — above the government’s own 60% target — and 1MDB legacy debt still totalling RM50 billion on the books, fiscal space remains severely constrained. Anwar told Bloomberg that debt pressure would only “ease from end-2028.” The Star reported that 1MDB debt remains “Malaysia’s largest fiscal burden.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Debt servicing at 16% of revenue is a silent tax on the future. Every ringgit spent on interest is a ringgit NOT spent on healthcare (where 33,000 doctors are on temporary contracts), education (where PISA scores are declining), infrastructure (where LRT3 is delayed and roads crumble), or social safety nets (where B40 families struggle with costs). The compound effect is devastating: high debt requires high servicing, which reduces fiscal space, which forces more borrowing, which increases future servicing costs. It’s a fiscal doom loop — and PH has done nothing to break it.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government continues to find new spending commitments — subsidies, civil service wages, development projects, 238 political appointments to GLCs — that make fiscal consolidation an ever-receding target. The structural deficit means future generations of Malaysians will inherit a mountain of debt for spending decisions made today, with compound interest ensuring the problem only grows. When Anwar says debt pressure will “ease from end-2028,” he’s asking Malaysians to accept six years of fiscal pain for a promise of eventual improvement. That’s not a plan — it’s a hope, and hope compounds about as well as debt does.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s debt burden is not new, but it has been consistently worsened by political spending. 1MDB added RM50 billion. Pandemic stimulus under Muhyiddin added tens of billions more. Subsidy commitments under every government add annual billions. The Public Finance and Fiscal Responsibility Act was supposed to impose discipline — but the 60% debt-to-GDP target has been breached without consequence. The act has no enforcement mechanism, making it aspirational rather than binding. Every government passes fiscal responsibility laws and then ignores them.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Every sixth ringgit of tax revenue goes to paying interest — not building hospitals, not hiring teachers, not fixing roads. 16% of revenue servicing debt is a governance failure that spans administrations, but you’ve been in power long enough to own it now. Cut wasteful spending: 238 political GLC appointments, RM85.5M propaganda budgets, and discretionary allocations that serve politics rather than people. Enforce corporate tax compliance. Publish a credible fiscal consolidation roadmap with annual targets. Or keep kicking the can to 2028 — and let the next generation pay the bill.
