Press Cards as Weapons — New Code of Ethics Lets Government Decide Who Is a 'Real' Journalist

Said by: Freedom House / Journalists’ Union

Category: Social

Date: 2024-06-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In mid-2024, the government released an updated Code of Ethics for Journalists that effectively creates a de facto licensing system for journalism in Malaysia. Media access to government events, press conferences, and official sources is increasingly dependent on compliance with the code — meaning outlets that publish critical reporting risk losing access. Freedom House documented this as a form of soft censorship. Self-censorship becomes the mechanism: critical stories risk loss of access, advertising boycotts from government-linked companies, or MCMC investigations.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When the government decides who qualifies as a “real” journalist, it controls the information environment without needing to formally censor anyone. The Code of Ethics doesn’t ban publications — it gates access. Journalists who comply get press cards, access to ministers, and invitations to official events. Journalists who don’t comply lose all of this. The result is soft censorship: editors learn which stories cost them access, and they adjust accordingly. No law is broken. No outlet is formally banned. The press is simply managed.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH campaigned explicitly on press freedom. Anwar, a political prisoner whose case was partly championed by press freedom organisations worldwide, should understand better than anyone the importance of a free press. Instead, his government has created a code of ethics that functions as a gatekeeping mechanism — deciding who gets to be a journalist and who doesn’t, based on compliance with government-set standards. Every journalist who self-censors to keep their access is a journalist PH has silenced without the bad optics of a formal ban. Subtle censorship is still censorship.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, the Printing Presses and Publications Act required annual licences for print media — direct censorship that PH promised to repeal. They didn’t repeal it. Under PH, the approach is subtler but equally effective: instead of requiring licences for publications, you require compliance for access. The Sedition Act, the PPPA, and now the Code of Ethics form a layered system of media control that is more sophisticated than BN’s but achieves the same outcome — a press that knows the boundaries and stays within them.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: A free press protects democracy. You know this — you were a political prisoner partly rescued by press freedom. Now you’ve created a system where journalists need government approval to be “real” journalists. Withdraw the Code of Ethics as a gatekeeping mechanism. Guarantee access to all accredited journalists regardless of editorial line. Repeal the PPPA as promised. Or keep managing the press through access control — and accept that your press freedom record is indistinguishable from BN’s.

30% of SPM Candidates FAIL At Least One Subject — A Pass Grade of 20% in Math Is Not Education, It's a Lie

Said by: Education Ministry Data / Independent Analysis

Category: Education

Date: 2024-05-27

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In SPM 2023 results released in May 2024, approximately 30% of candidates failed at least one subject. Mathematics had the highest failure rate at 25.9%, followed by science at 25%. The best-performing subject was Quran and Sunnah at 84%. Reports indicate the pass threshold for mathematics may be as low as 20% — meaning a student who gets 4 out of 5 questions wrong still “passes.” The government had previously abolished UPSR (Year 6) and PMR (Year 9) examinations, removing the only diagnostic checkpoints that could identify struggling students early.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When you set the pass mark at 20%, you’re not measuring education — you’re manufacturing the illusion of it. A student who “passes” mathematics with a 20% score cannot do basic arithmetic reliably. These students enter the workforce, attempt further education, or become statistics in the 12.28% youth unemployment figure. The education system is producing graduates with certificates but without competence. Under BN, Malaysia maintained mediocre but stable PISA scores (440 in math in 2018). Under PH, scores crashed 31 points to 409. The percentage of students below basic proficiency increased from 41% to 59%.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government abolished UPSR and PMR — removing the diagnostic checkpoints that would catch struggling students at ages 12 and 15 — then acts surprised when 30% fail at SPM. As one analyst put it: “The journey seems to be just to promote incompetence from one stage to the other until students reach SPM.” When the government removes tests and lowers pass marks, it’s not reforming education — it’s hiding failure. The education system is producing religious knowledge better than mathematics, science, or English — and the government’s response is more blueprints, more jargon, and more press conferences.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The Malaysia Education Blueprint 2013-2025, continued by PH, has been a decade-long failure by its own metrics. PISA scores have declined. SPM failure rates remain high. Youth unemployment suggests the system doesn’t prepare students for work. Every education minister launches a “transformation.” None transforms anything. The fundamental problem — a curriculum designed around political and ethnic considerations rather than educational excellence — remains untouched because reforming it would require confronting interests no government has been willing to challenge.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your education system produces religious knowledge better than mathematics. Your students are 8 years behind Singapore and 3 years behind Vietnam. You abolished the tests that would have caught this earlier. You lowered the pass mark to hide the failure. And you call this “Madani civilisation”? A civilisation that can’t teach its children to count is not a civilisation — it’s a civilisational collapse in slow motion. Restore diagnostic checkpoints. Raise pass standards to meaningful levels. Reform the curriculum for competence, not compliance. Or keep producing SPM certificates that employers don’t trust and the world doesn’t recognise.

KFC Closes 100+ Outlets Amid Pro-Palestine Boycott — Government Watches From the Sidelines

Said by: QSR Brands Holdings (KFC operator)

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-05-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In May 2024, KFC Malaysia temporarily closed over 100 outlets amid boycotts linked to the Gaza conflict. Thousands of Malaysian workers — mostly low-income B40 families — faced reduced hours or outright job losses. McDonald’s global sales fell 0.1% in 2024 and 1% in Q1 2025, driven largely by boycotts in Muslim-majority countries including Malaysia. The boycott movement was fueled by social media activists and opposition politicians — particularly PAS and PN — who framed consumption at Western fast-food chains as complicity in the Gaza conflict. The government’s response was conspicuously, strategically absent — no minister endorsed or opposed the boycott.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The government’s silence was calculated cowardice. Supporting the boycott would anger investors, franchise operators, and the business community. Opposing it would anger the Malay-Muslim base and hand PN a political weapon. So the government said nothing while thousands of its own citizens lost income. QSR Brands — the KFC operator — is a Malaysian-owned company that employs Malaysian workers, pays Malaysian taxes, and sources from Malaysian suppliers. The boycott punished Malaysian businesses and workers for the perceived sins of a foreign brand.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: No government relief programme was targeted at affected F&B workers. Madani’s promise to prioritise B40 welfare evaporated the moment it conflicted with political convenience. These workers — cashiers, cooks, delivery riders — were collateral damage in a geopolitical conflict they had no role in. Opposition politicians who championed the boycott faced no scrutiny for the economic damage they encouraged. PAS MPs who called for boycotts offered no alternative employment plan for displaced workers. The government, by staying silent, let the opposition set the narrative and absorbed none of the accountability.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Consumer boycotts in Malaysia have historically been driven by ethno-religious mobilisation rather than economic analysis. The 1969 “Buy British Last” campaign, various anti-Chinese boycotts, and periodic anti-Israel product campaigns have all followed the same pattern: moral outrage drives consumer action, workers bear the cost, and politicians exploit the sentiment without managing the consequences. Moral clarity on Palestine does not require destroying the livelihoods of your own citizens. A responsible government would have separated legitimate solidarity from economic self-harm.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Thousands of B40 workers lost income because you were too afraid to take a position. Supporting Palestine is a moral imperative. Destroying Malaysian livelihoods is not. You could have funded Palestinian aid while protecting Malaysian workers. You could have spoken clearly: “boycott is your right, but these are Malaysian businesses employing Malaysian families.” Instead, you chose silence — the path of least political resistance. Cowardice is not governance.

Press Freedom Plunges 34 Spots to 107th — 'Difficult Situation' Under PH

Said by: Reporters Without Borders (RSF)

Category: Social

Date: 2024-05-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In May 2024, Reporters Without Borders (RSF) released the 2024 World Press Freedom Index, in which Malaysia plunged 34 places — from 73rd to 107th — under the PH government. RSF classified Malaysia as a “difficult situation” for press freedom. The collapse was driven by sedition charges against journalists and activists, MCMC content blocking, social media licensing requirements, and the arrest of FMT journalist Rex Tan. Even after a partial recovery to 88th in 2025, Malaysia still trails its 2023 ranking under PH.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A 34-place drop in press freedom under a government that promised media reform is not a statistical fluctuation — it is an indictment. PH’s 2022 manifesto included commitments to repeal the Sedition Act, reform the Printing Presses and Publications Act, and protect press freedom. Instead, under PH, journalists have been arrested, news sites blocked, and a social media licensing regime introduced. RSF’s “difficult situation” classification places Malaysia alongside countries PH claims to be morally superior to. The ranking reflects not just specific incidents but a systemic deterioration in the media environment.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s response to the ranking drop was to blame “inherited problems” and promise improvement. But the specific incidents RSF cited — Chegubard’s sedition charge, Rex Tan’s arrest, MCMC blocking MalaysiaNow and Asia Sentinel — all occurred under PH’s watch. These are not inherited problems; they are PH-created problems. The government that promised to protect journalists is now prosecuting them. The government that promised to unblock websites is now blocking more. Every press freedom commitment has been reversed.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under Najib, Malaysia’s press freedom ranking hovered around 140th — driven by 1MDB-related media suppression, the blocking of Sarawak Report, and sedition charges against editors. PH 1.0 achieved a dramatic improvement to 101st in 2019. PH 2.0 initially maintained the improvement before the 2024 collapse. The trajectory is clear: PH delivers press freedom gains in opposition and early government, then reverts to suppression once media scrutiny becomes inconvenient. The 34-place drop happened in a single year — suggesting a deliberate policy shift, not gradual decline.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You dropped 34 places in press freedom in a single year. RSF calls Malaysia a “difficult situation.” Your manifesto promised media freedom. Your government delivered media suppression. Unblock MalaysiaNow and Asia Sentinel. Drop charges against journalists. Repeal the Sedition Act as promised. Withdraw the social media licensing regime. Or keep falling in the rankings — and stop pretending Malaysia is a democracy when the world’s press freedom watchdog classifies it as difficult.

Forest City Casino Saga — Anwar Threatens JAIL for Those Who Report on It

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-05-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In May 2024, Anwar Ibrahim called allegations about a casino at Forest City in Johor “malicious” and suggested those spreading the rumours could face jail. SCMP reported that Anwar and tycoons connected to the Forest City Special Financial Zone were “urged to walk back threats” after the aggressive response drew backlash. Chegubard — the activist who reported on the casino plan — was actually charged with sedition for his social media post. The government’s response to journalism about Forest City was not transparency or denial with evidence — it was threats of imprisonment.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When a Prime Minister threatens jail for people who report on a development project, it sends a devastating signal about press freedom and public accountability. Forest City is a politically sensitive project — a Chinese-backed development in Johor that the government is promoting as a Special Financial Zone with 0% tax incentives. Questions about what activities will be permitted there are legitimate public interest journalism, not sedition. The threat of jail for reporting is not a response to misinformation — it is a suppression of scrutiny.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: If the casino allegations were false, the government had every tool available to debunk them: issue a formal denial, publish the SFZ regulations, show that gaming is excluded. Instead, Anwar chose threats. That’s not the response of a government with nothing to hide — it’s the response of a government that wants questions to stop. SCMP’s reporting — that Anwar and tycoons were “urged to walk back threats” — suggests even allies recognised the response was disproportionate. When your reaction to journalism is more aggressive than your reaction to corruption, your priorities are transparent.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysian PMs have a long history of threatening critics instead of addressing allegations. Mahathir threatened journalists who questioned his policies. Najib’s government used the Official Secrets Act, Sedition Act, and MACC against critics of 1MDB. Muhyiddin used emergency ordinances to suppress dissent during COVID. PH promised to break this pattern. Instead, Anwar’s response to Forest City reporting was indistinguishable from his predecessors: threaten jail, charge the critic, and hope the story dies.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: The correct response to false allegations is evidence, not threats. If there’s no casino planned at Forest City, publish the SFZ regulations and prove it. If there is, own it and defend the policy. But threatening jail for reporters asking questions about a publicly funded special zone is authoritarian — and you know it, because you spent 25 years on the receiving end of exactly this tactic. Stop threatening journalists. Start answering questions.

Chegubard Charged with SEDITION for Reporting Forest City Casino Claims — Reformasi's Free Speech Promise Dies

Said by: Johor Sessions Court / Badrul Hisham (Chegubard)

Category: Social

Date: 2024-04-30

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 30 April 2024, activist Badrul Hisham Shaharin (Chegubard) was charged under Malaysia’s colonial-era Sedition Act for a social media post about casino plans at Forest City in Johor. The Sedition Act is the same law PH promised to repeal during its 2018 and 2022 election campaigns. As of March 2026, Chegubard’s appeal is still pending at the Johor Bahru Sessions Court. Instead of repealing the Sedition Act, the Anwar government is now weaponising it against critics — the exact same playbook used by BN against Anwar himself in the 1990s.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Every repressive law PH promised to abolish — the Sedition Act, the Printing Presses and Publications Act, SOSMA, Section 233 of the Communications and Multimedia Act — is still on the books and being actively used by PH. Press freedom organisations condemned the prosecution as a chilling signal to journalists and activists: in Anwar’s Malaysia, questioning powerful interests — especially those connected to the Forest City special economic zone — can land you in court. The message is clear: free speech ends where powerful business interests begin.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar Ibrahim was himself charged under the Sedition Act. He knows what it feels like to face prosecution for speech. He built an entire political movement — Reformasi — on the injustice of being silenced by the state. Now his government is using the same law against a man who reported on casino plans at a development the government is actively promoting. The Sedition Act wasn’t just left unrepealed — it was expanded, with over 90 sedition-related arrests under PH. The man who was once a victim of these laws is now wielding them.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, the Sedition Act was used against opposition politicians, academics, lawyers, and journalists. Anwar, Karpal Singh, Azmi Sharom, and dozens of others were charged or investigated. PH condemned every single prosecution as authoritarian overreach. Under PH 2.0, the same law has been used against Chegubard, social media commentators, and critics of government policy. The only difference is the name on the charge sheet — the law, the process, and the chilling effect are identical.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You were jailed under these laws. You know what they feel like. You promised to repeal them. And now you’re using them against the same kind of activists who marched for your freedom. Repeal the Sedition Act — as you promised in 2018 and 2022. Drop the charges against Chegubard. Or admit that Reformasi was never about free speech — it was about freeing one man, not the system.

Electricity Tariff Hike + SST on Electricity — Double Whammy on Rakyat's Monthly Bills

Said by: Energy Commission / Consumer Groups

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-03-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In March 2024, the government imposed 8% SST on commercial electricity, adding costs that businesses pass directly to consumers through higher prices for goods and services. Combined with the broader SST expansion and diesel subsidy removal, Malaysians faced a triple cost shock: higher fuel, higher electricity costs, and higher prices for everything that uses fuel and electricity — which is everything. Under BN’s GST system, electricity for domestic use was zero-rated, meaning consumers paid no consumption tax on it.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Electricity is a basic utility that affects every household and business. SST on electricity is effectively a tax on living — you can’t opt out of using electricity. For manufacturing SMEs already struggling with rising costs, electricity tariff increases erode already-thin margins. For households, it’s another line item squeezing budgets that are already stretched by food inflation, diesel hikes, and stagnant wages. The cumulative effect — diesel hike plus SST expansion plus electricity tariff increase plus RON95 reform uncertainty — creates a “hotpot of bad economic news” that the Straits Times documented in detail.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH won power partly by criticising BN’s cost-of-living policies. They promised to reduce the burden on ordinary Malaysians. Instead, they’ve imposed SST on electricity (which BN didn’t do under GST — electricity was zero-rated), raised diesel by 56%, and presided over food prices becoming the highest in Southeast Asia. Every cost-of-living metric has gotten worse under PH, not better. The irony is devastating: PH abolished GST because it “hurt the rakyat,” then replaced it with SST that taxes basic utilities GST didn’t touch. The rakyat is literally paying more under the system PH created.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN’s GST system, basic utilities including electricity for domestic use were zero-rated, meaning consumers paid no consumption tax. PH abolished GST in 2018 and replaced it with SST, which doesn’t have the same zero-rating mechanism for essentials. The result: PH’s tax system is more burdensome on daily necessities than the BN system they demonised. Each subsequent SST expansion has moved the system closer to GST-level coverage — raising the obvious question: why did PH abolish GST only to recreate it in a less efficient form?

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You abolished GST because you said it hurt the rakyat. Then you imposed SST on electricity, which GST didn’t tax. You raised diesel prices. You presided over the highest food costs in Southeast Asia. By every measurable cost-of-living indicator, Malaysians were better off under the GST system you demonised. You broke a working system for political points and replaced it with something worse. The rakyat is literally paying for your populism.

Over 10,000 SPM Students Didn't Even Sit for the Exam — Education System Haemorrhaging

Said by: Education Ministry / DOSM

Category: Education

Date: 2024-03-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: More than 10,160 registered SPM candidates — 2.6% of the total — did not sit for Malaysia’s most important school examination in 2023. This is not a rounding error; it represents over ten thousand young Malaysians who disengaged from the education system entirely. Combined with a tertiary enrollment rate of just 42.57% (versus Singapore’s 93% and the global average of 51.98%), youth unemployment stuck at 12.28%, a higher education budget share that dropped from 6.8% in 2010 to 4.3% in 2025, and 7,681 reported bullying cases, the system is haemorrhaging students at every level.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When over 10,000 students don’t even show up for SPM, it signals a system that has lost legitimacy in the eyes of the people it’s supposed to serve. These are not students who failed — they are students who decided the system wasn’t worth showing up for. The reasons are multi-layered: financial hardship forcing teenagers into the workforce, mental health struggles, disillusionment with an education system perceived as irrelevant, and the growing sense that a Malaysian degree may not lead to meaningful employment given 12.28% youth unemployment.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government allocated a declining share of the national budget to education — down to 4.3% for higher education in 2025 from 6.8% in 2010. When the Education Minister reports 7,681 bullying cases and rising student disengagement, the response should be structural reform, not communication plans. But structural reform requires money and political will — both of which are directed elsewhere. The civil service budget grows every year. The education budget shrinks. When your budget priorities tell students they matter less than bureaucrats, students respond by walking away.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Student dropout and disengagement have been rising in Malaysia for years. The COVID pandemic accelerated the trend — thousands of students from B40 families never returned to school after lockdowns. The government’s response has been programmes and initiatives that generate press releases but don’t address root causes: poverty, irrelevant curricula, teacher quality, and a qualification system that employers don’t value. Every education minister announces a “transformation.” None transforms anything.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Ten thousand students gave up on your education system. They didn’t fail — they decided it wasn’t worth showing up for. When your budget for education keeps shrinking while your budget for civil service salaries keeps growing, you’re telling the next generation exactly where your priorities are. And they’re responding by walking away. Reverse the education budget decline. Reform the curriculum for relevance. Address bullying as a systemic crisis, not a statistic. Or keep losing students — and wonder why the brain drain starts before they even graduate.

SST Hiked From 6% to 8%, Then Expanded to Healthcare, Rent, Education — 'Necessary' Tax on the Rakyat

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim (Finance Minister) and MOF

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-03-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In March 2024, Malaysia’s service tax was hiked from 6% to 8%. Then in July 2025, the SST was expanded to cover six new categories: rental and leasing, construction, financial services, private healthcare, education, and beauty services. Sales tax of 5-10% was also imposed on imported fruits and “premium seafood.” Anwar, who also holds the Finance Minister portfolio, described the expansion as “necessary” for fiscal sustainability. The Straits Times described it as part of a “hotpot of bad economic news” alongside electricity tariff hikes, subsidy cuts, and cooking gas changes. CNA asked: “Necessary, but at what cost to businesses and consumers?”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: PH opposed GST and abolished it in 2018, calling it a burden on the rakyat. Now they’re expanding SST so aggressively that it’s approaching GST-level coverage anyway — but in a less efficient form. Taxing private healthcare and education directly hits the middle class who use these services because public alternatives are overwhelmed. The cumulative effect of diesel hikes, SST expansion, electricity tariff increases, and RON95 reform uncertainty creates a cost-of-living squeeze that no amount of GDP growth statistics can mask.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The SST expansion contradicts everything PH said about taxation when they were in opposition. GST was a broad-based consumption tax with input tax credits and zero-rating for essentials. SST is a narrower tax without input credits — meaning it cascades through the supply chain, raising costs at each stage. By expanding SST to cover healthcare, education, and rent, PH has created a tax system that is both less efficient than GST and more burdensome on essential services. The rakyat didn’t vote for a different tax system — they voted for a less expensive one. They got the opposite.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s tax policy has been a political football since 2015. BN introduced GST at 6%. PH abolished it and replaced it with SST at 6%. PH then raised SST to 8% and expanded its coverage. Each step has moved the system closer to the GST PH destroyed — but without the structural advantages (input tax credits, broader base, lower rates) that made GST more economically efficient. Economists across the political spectrum have noted that Malaysia will eventually need to return to a broad-based consumption tax — which means PH’s abolition of GST was a populist detour that cost the country billions in lost revenue and now requires aggressive SST expansion to compensate.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You abolished GST to win votes. Now you’re expanding SST to replace the revenue you lost. SST on healthcare. SST on education. SST on rent. These are not luxury goods — they are basic necessities for millions of Malaysians. If you’re going to tax at GST levels, bring back GST with proper zero-rating for essentials. If you’re not, stop expanding SST into every corner of Malaysian life while pretending it’s different from the tax you abolished. The rakyat can do the math even if the government can’t.

Sources: Straits Times | CNA
