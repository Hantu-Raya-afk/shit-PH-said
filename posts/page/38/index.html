🔥 KENAPA INI PENTING: Ini bukan pertikaian birokrasi kecil — ia kes agensi persekutuan mengekstrak berbilion daripada sebuah negeri selama empat dekad tanpa pampasan. Negeri seperti Pahang dan Sabah telah menerima bayaran mereka — Terengganu semata-mata diabaikan. Layanan terpilih menimbulkan persoalan: adakah kerajaan persekutuan melayan negeri yang ditadbir PN secara berbeza daripada negeri PH?

Kerajaan persekutuan — di bawah pengawasan PH — membenarkan GLC untuk menumpang percuma di tanah negeri selama empat dekad sementara peneroka menanggung kos reputasi. Hanya apabila Terengganu mengancam pengusiran barulah TPM Zahid masuk campur. Itu bukan tadbir urus — ia kawalan kerosakan yang dicetuskan oleh rasa malu. Jika ini negeri yang ditadbir PH, tindak balas persekutuan akan segera.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Pertikaian tanah persekutuan-negeri adalah tema berulang dalam politik Malaysia, dan ia hampir sentiasa berpecah mengikut garis politik. Felda sendiri mempunyai sejarah panjang salah urus — RM4 bilion dalam pelaburan dipersoalkan di bawah FGV.

🎯 SERUAN PH: Kamu berkhotbah tentang federalisme dan layanan adil kepada semua negeri. Tetapi apabila negeri yang ditadbir PN dieksploitasi oleh agensi persekutuan, kamu memandang ke arah lain selama bertahun-tahun. Kelakuan Felda di bawah pengawasan kamu tidak boleh dibezakan daripada pengekstrakan zaman penjajah — ambil sumber, abaikan penduduk tempatan, dan berlindung di sebalik peneroka apabila ditangkap. Bayar Terengganu apa yang terhutang. Audit setiap operasi Felda di tanah negeri di seluruh negara.

Turunkan kuasa sebenar kepada negeri-negeri Malaysia Timur. Atau terus menang 1 daripada 20 dan berpura-pura semuanya baik — kerana PRU16 akan memberikan keputusan sama jika tiada perubahan.

Felda-Terengganu Land War — 15,000 Hectares Exploited for 40 Years Without Paying the State a Sen

Said by: Terengganu State Government / Felda

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-30

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: A decades-long land dispute between the Federal Land Development Authority (Felda) and the Terengganu state government exploded in late November 2025 when the state issued trespass notices covering 10 plantations totalling approximately 15,000 hectares. Terengganu accused Felda of developing state land for over 40 years without paying premiums, taxes, or profit shares — essentially extracting billions in palm oil revenue from state land for free. FGV Holdings was also served notices. DPM Zahid eventually stepped in for negotiations.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This is not a minor bureaucratic dispute — it is a case of a federal agency extracting billions from a state for four decades without compensation. The Terengganu state Exco accused Felda of using settlers as “human shields,” placing ordinary farmers in the middle of what is fundamentally a corporate land grab. States like Pahang and Sabah had received their payments — Terengganu was simply being ignored. The selective treatment raises the question: does the federal government treat PN-governed states differently from PH-governed ones?

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Terengganu demanded 30% of Felda’s gross profits in 2024 but Felda refused to even propose a counter-figure. The federal government — under PH oversight — allowed a GLC to freeload on state land for four decades while settlers bore the reputational cost. Only when Terengganu threatened eviction did DPM Zahid step in. That’s not governance — that’s damage control triggered by embarrassment. If this were a PH-governed state, the federal response would have been immediate. The delay tells you everything about how this government treats opposition states.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Federal-state land disputes are a recurring theme in Malaysian politics, and they almost always break along political lines. Kelantan’s oil royalty dispute with Petronas dragged on for decades under BN. Sabah and Sarawak have fought for resource rights since Malaysia’s formation. The pattern is consistent: federal agencies extract, states complain, and resolution only comes when political pressure becomes unbearable. Felda itself has a long history of mismanagement — RM4 billion in questionable investments under FGV, settler land schemes that enriched intermediaries rather than farmers.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You preach federalism and fair treatment of all states. But when it’s a PN-governed state being exploited by a federal agency, you looked the other way for years. Felda’s behaviour under your watch is indistinguishable from colonial extraction — take the resources, ignore the locals, and hide behind the settlers when caught. Pay Terengganu what it is owed. Audit every Felda operation on state land nationwide. Publish the revenue extracted versus premiums paid for every state. Or admit that your federalism only applies to states that voted for you.

Political analyst Lee Kuok Tiung said plainly: “The Sabah election outcome shows Anwar’s coalition lost badly.” Anwar’s response: the defeat “won’t stop delivery” of pledges to Sabah.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s response — “defeat won’t stop delivery” — is the political equivalent of losing a match 19-1 and insisting the training programme is working. 🎯 PH CALLOUT: You won 1 out of 20 seats. One. Sabahans gave you the clearest possible message: you do not represent them. Instead of listening, you said “defeat won’t stop delivery.” Drop the appeal against Sabah’s revenue share ruling. Implement MA63 commitments. Devolve real power to East Malaysian states. The cycle is predictable because the government chooses not to break it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The December 2021 floods killed over 50 people and displaced 400,000. The 2022 and 2023 monsoon seasons brought more flooding to the same areas. Publish a national flood mitigation capital expenditure report showing what has actually been built since GE15. Enforce development controls in flood-prone zones. Social Media Ban for Under-16s — Enforcement Plan: TBD

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2025-11-24

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil announced in November 2025 that Malaysia would ban social media access for children under 16, following Australia’s lead. The ban was set for implementation in 2026. However, the NYT noted the announcement was “light on details” — no age verification infrastructure exists, no enforcement mechanism was outlined, and no timeline for implementation was provided. The government framed it as protecting children from online harms, but offered no technical plan for making it work.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A social media ban for under-16s sounds decisive. But without enforcement infrastructure, it’s a headline, not a policy. Age verification online is technically difficult — even Australia, which passed its ban first, has struggled with implementation. Malaysia has neither the digital infrastructure, the regulatory capacity, nor the tech industry cooperation to enforce such a ban. Meanwhile, the announcement distracts from the government’s own record of using social media regulations to suppress dissent — including the arrest of FMT journalist Rex Tan and the expansion of MCMC’s content moderation powers.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government that can’t enforce basic traffic laws, stop illegal waste dumping, or verify the integrity of its own procurement processes is going to verify the age of every social media user in Malaysia? This is performative governance at its worst. Fahmi announced a ban with no enforcement plan — because the plan doesn’t exist. It cannot exist without massive investment in digital ID infrastructure, cooperation from global tech platforms, and privacy safeguards that Malaysia has never implemented. The announcement was designed to generate a positive news cycle, not to protect children.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a long history of announcing tech regulations without enforcement mechanisms. The Anti-Fake News Act was passed and repealed. MCMC’s licensing regime for social media platforms was announced with fanfare but implementation details remain vague. The Online Safety Bill has been in discussion for years. Each announcement generates headlines; none generates meaningful change. Meanwhile, Malaysia’s youth screen time is among the highest in the world, and the government’s response is a ban it cannot enforce rather than investment in digital literacy it could actually deliver.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You announced a ban with no enforcement plan. That’s not policy — it’s a press release. If you’re serious about protecting children online, invest in digital literacy programmes in schools. Fund parental education on screen time management. Build the age verification infrastructure before announcing the ban, not after. Or admit that this was a headline-grabbing announcement designed to match Australia’s news cycle, with no intention of following through.
