[BM] MCA Kepada PH: 'Jangan Salahkan BN Untuk Kegagalan ANDA Mereformasi'

Said by: Neow Choo Seong, MCA Youth

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-12-30

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada akhir Disember 2024, jurucakap Pemuda MCA Neow Choo Seong mengeluarkan kenyataan memberitahu PH supaya berhenti menyalahkan BN atas kegagalan PH melaksanakan reformasi yang dijanjikan. Neow berhujah bahawa “kuasa berada sepenuhnya di tangan PH” dan BN tidak patut dijadikan kambing hitam atas keengganan PH sendiri bertindak. Kenyataan ini ketara kerana MCA adalah parti komponen BN dalam gabungan kerajaan perpaduan — bermakna rakan gabungan PH sendiri sudah jemu PH menyalahkan semua orang lain atas kegagalannya menyampaikan reformasi.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Apabila rakan gabungan anda sendiri secara terbuka memberitahu anda untuk berhenti memberi alasan, alasan itu sudah tamat tempoh. Pembelaan lalai PH untuk setiap janji yang dimungkiri ialah “kekangan gabungan” — khususnya, UMNO dan BN menyekat reformasi. Kenyataan MCA meruntuhkan hujah itu dari dalam gabungan: kuasa adalah milik PH. PM adalah PH. Menteri Kewangan adalah PH. Majoriti jawatan kabinet adalah PH. Jika reformasi tidak berlaku, ia kerana PH memilih untuk tidak melaksanakannya — bukan kerana BN menghalangnya.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: PH telah menggunakan “kekangan gabungan” sebagai alasan serba guna selama tiga tahun. Tidak boleh mansuhkan Akta Hasutan? Kekangan gabungan. Tidak boleh mereformasi Akta Mesin Cetak? Kekangan gabungan. Tidak boleh luluskan had tempoh PM? Kekangan gabungan. Kenyataan MCA mendedahkan penipuan: PH mengawal kerajaan. PH mempunyai majoriti parlimen. PH memegang portfolio utama. Reformasi bukan disekat — ia tidak dicuba. Menyalahkan BN bukan strategi politik; ia pengakuan bahawa PH tidak pernah berniat menyampaikan reformasi yang dijanjikan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Menyalahkan rakan gabungan atas kegagalan dasar adalah tradisi politik Malaysia. Di bawah BN, UMNO menyalahkan MCA dan MIC kerana “tidak memahami sensitiviti Melayu.” Di bawah PH 1.0, PH menyalahkan puak Mahathir atas reformasi yang lambat. Di bawah PH 2.0, PH menyalahkan UMNO. Coraknya sama merentas gabungan: parti dominan menggunakan rakan yang lebih kecil sebagai kambing hitam bagi kegagalan dasarnya sendiri. Apabila kambing hitam itu sendiri berkata “berhenti salahkan kami,” strategi itu telah runtuh.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Rakan gabungan anda sendiri memberitahu anda untuk berhenti memberi alasan. Kuasa ada di tangan anda. Anda memegang pejabat PM, Kementerian Kewangan, dan majoriti kerusi kabinet. Jika reformasi tidak berlaku selepas tiga tahun, ia kerana anda memilih untuk tidak melaksanakannya. Berhenti menyalahkan BN. Berhenti menyalahkan “kekangan gabungan.” Akui rekod anda. Sampaikan reformasi yang anda janjikan — atau akui bahawa janji-janji itu tidak pernah ikhlas, dan hadapi pengundi dengan pengakuan itu.

MCA to PH: 'Don't Blame BN for YOUR Failure to Reform'

Said by: Neow Choo Seong, MCA Youth

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-12-30

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late December 2024, MCA Youth spokesman Neow Choo Seong issued a statement telling PH to stop blaming BN for PH’s failure to implement promised reforms. Neow argued that “power is firmly in PH’s hands” and BN should not be scapegoated for PH’s own inaction. The statement was notable because MCA is a BN component party within the unity government coalition — meaning even PH’s own coalition partners were tired of PH blaming everyone else for its failure to deliver reform.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When your own coalition partner publicly tells you to stop making excuses, the excuse has expired. PH’s default defence for every broken promise has been “coalition constraints” — specifically, UMNO and BN blocking reform. MCA’s statement demolishes that argument from inside the coalition: power is PH’s. The PM is PH. The Finance Minister is PH. The majority of cabinet positions are PH. If reforms haven’t happened, it’s because PH chose not to implement them — not because BN prevented it.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH has used “coalition constraints” as an all-purpose excuse for three years. Can’t repeal the Sedition Act? Coalition constraints. Can’t reform the Printing Presses Act? Coalition constraints. Can’t deliver PM term limits? Coalition constraints. MCA’s statement exposes the fraud: PH controls the government. PH has a parliamentary majority. PH holds the key portfolios. The reforms aren’t blocked — they’re not attempted. Blaming BN is not a political strategy; it’s a confession that PH never intended to deliver the reforms it promised.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Blaming coalition partners for policy failures is a Malaysian political tradition. Under BN, UMNO blamed MCA and MIC for “not understanding Malay sensitivities.” Under PH 1.0, PH blamed Mahathir’s faction for slow reform. Under PH 2.0, PH blames UMNO. The pattern is identical across coalitions: the dominant party uses smaller partners as scapegoats for its own policy failures. When even the scapegoat says “stop blaming us,” the strategy has collapsed.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your own coalition partner is telling you to stop making excuses. Power is in your hands. You hold the PM’s office, the Finance Ministry, and the majority of cabinet seats. If reforms haven’t happened after three years, it’s because you chose not to implement them. Stop blaming BN. Stop blaming “coalition constraints.” Own your record. Deliver the reforms you promised — or admit that the promises were never sincere, and face the voters with that admission.

[BM] Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian Diluluskan — SKMM Dapat Kuasa Penapisan Menyeluruh, Tiada Pengawasan

Said by: Parliament / Fahmi Fadzil

Category: Social

Date: 2024-12-20

Perundangan ini memberi kuasa kepada SKMM untuk mengarahkan penyingkiran kandungan, menyekat laman web dan menghukum syarikat media sosial — semuanya tanpa pengawasan kehakiman bebas. Tiada tribunal bebas, tiada keperluan semakan kehakiman sebelum penyingkiran kandungan, dan tiada mekanisme ketelusan untuk kandungan yang disekat. Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar mempunyai denda sehingga RM500,000. Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian mempunyai penalti sehingga RM10 juta. Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar menyasarkan penceramah individu. Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian menyasarkan platform — bermakna SKMM boleh mengancam menutup keseluruhan perkhidmatan melainkan mereka mematuhi. PH telah menggantikan undang-undang buruk dengan yang lebih buruk dan memanggilnya reformasi.

PH memansuhkan Akta Antiberita Tidak Benar pada 2018 dan berjanji mereformasi AKM. Di bawah PH 2.0, AKM diperkuatkan (bukan direformasi), Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian diluluskan (menambah kuasa baharu), dan SKMM diberi alat penguatkuasaan yang melebihi apa yang BN pernah miliki. Infrastruktur penapisan yang PH bina dalam tiga tahun lebih komprehensif daripada apa yang BN bina dalam 60 tahun.

Kemudian anda meluluskan Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian, yang memberi kerajaan lebih banyak kuasa lagi dengan penalti lebih besar dan tiada pengawasan bebas. Anda tidak mereformasi peraturan internet — anda menguatkannya secara turbo. Masukkan pengawasan kehakiman bebas untuk semua perintah penyingkiran kandungan. Hadkan penalti pada tahap yang tidak mewujudkan insentif untuk pematuhan berlebihan. Terbitkan laporan ketelusan tentang semua permintaan penyingkiran kandungan SKMM. Atau terima bahawa anda telah membina rangka kerja penapisan internet paling komprehensif dalam sejarah Malaysia — dan akui ia.

Online Safety Act Passes — MCMC Gets Sweeping Censorship Powers, No Oversight

Said by: Parliament / Fahmi Fadzil

Category: Social

Date: 2024-12-20

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In December 2024, Parliament passed the Online Safety Act and CMA amendments giving MCMC sweeping censorship powers with penalties up to RM10 million for non-compliant platforms. The legislation empowers MCMC to order content removal, block websites, and penalise social media companies — all without independent judicial oversight. Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil described it as necessary to protect children and combat cyber threats. Freedom House flagged it as a major regression in internet freedom. No independent oversight mechanism was included.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The Online Safety Act gives a government agency — MCMC, which reports to the Communications Minister — the power to determine what Malaysians can see online. There is no independent tribunal, no judicial review requirement before content removal, and no transparency mechanism for blocked content. When a government agency can remove content and penalise platforms up to RM10 million without independent oversight, every platform has an incentive to over-comply — removing borderline content preemptively rather than risking penalties. The result is censorship by incentive, not just censorship by order.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH in opposition fought against exactly these kinds of laws. The Anti-Fake News Act was PH’s exhibit A for BN’s authoritarianism — and that Act was milder than what PH has now passed. The Anti-Fake News Act had fines of up to RM500,000. The Online Safety Act has penalties of up to RM10 million. The Anti-Fake News Act targeted individual speakers. The Online Safety Act targets platforms — meaning MCMC can threaten to shut down entire services unless they comply. PH has replaced a bad law with a worse one and called it reform.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, the Anti-Fake News Act (2018), Sedition Act, and Section 233 CMA formed the internet censorship toolkit. PH repealed the Anti-Fake News Act in 2018 and promised to reform the CMA. Under PH 2.0, the CMA was strengthened (not reformed), the Online Safety Act was passed (adding new powers), and MCMC was given enforcement tools that exceed anything BN had. The censorship infrastructure PH built in three years is more comprehensive than what BN built in 60 years.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You repealed the Anti-Fake News Act because it gave the government too much power over online speech. Then you passed the Online Safety Act, which gives the government even more power with even bigger penalties and no independent oversight. You didn’t reform internet regulation — you supercharged it. Include independent judicial oversight for all content removal orders. Cap penalties at levels that don’t create incentives for over-compliance. Publish transparency reports on all MCMC content removal requests. Or accept that you’ve built the most comprehensive internet censorship framework in Malaysian history — and own it.

Rosmah Acquitted of All 17 Money Laundering Charges — PH Politicians Who Spent a Decade Attacking Her Go Silent

Said by: PH Politicians (Tony Pua, Rafizi Ramli, et al.) / High Court

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-12-19

📌 What Happened

On December 19, 2024, High Court judge K. Muniandy acquitted and discharged Datin Seri Rosmah Mansor — wife of jailed former PM Najib Razak — of all 17 charges against her in a money laundering and tax evasion case involving RM7.09 million. The judge ruled the prosecution failed to present key elements of the offence. Rosmah had been charged in October 2018 with 12 counts of money laundering and 5 counts of failing to declare income. MalaysiaNow contacted multiple PH leaders for comment — almost all refused. Tony Pua, who had sarcastically mocked Najib just two months earlier, did not answer calls. Rafizi Ramli, PH’s most vocal anti-corruption crusader, also stayed silent.

🔥 Why It Matters

Rosmah was PH’s ultimate political piñata — the living symbol of BN excess, corruption, and impunity. For over a decade, PH leaders from Anwar to Pua to Rafizi used her name, her handbags, and her alleged crimes to rally voters and justify their existence as a reform movement. When the court acquitted her, the silence was deafening. Not a single senior PH leader issued a statement demanding accountability, questioning the prosecution’s failure, or committing to appeal. This silence tells you everything about what PH’s anti-corruption crusade was always about: opposition theatre, not institutional reform.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH spent years telling Malaysians that Rosmah was the face of kleptocracy. They won elections on that narrative. Now that they’re in government — with control over the AGC, the police, and prosecution strategy — they can’t even muster a press statement when the case collapses? If the prosecution was weak, whose fault is that? PH has been in power since November 2022. They had two years to ensure cases were airtight. Instead, key witnesses reportedly died or went missing, and the AGC later dropped the appeal entirely. This isn’t just silence — it’s complicity in the collapse of accountability.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows the exact pattern of Najib’s pardon in February 2024, where the Pardons Board halved his sentence while Anwar served as PM. It follows the dropping of 47 corruption charges against DPM Zahid Hamidi in September 2023. Every major BN-era corruption case under Anwar’s watch has either been weakened, settled, or collapsed — and every time, PH’s former attack dogs go quiet. The anti-corruption movement that defined Malaysian politics for a decade is being dismantled by the very people who built it.

🎯 PH Callout

You built Pakatan Harapan on the promise that Malaysia deserved better than Najib and Rosmah. You told voters that justice would be served. Now Rosmah walks free, your own prosecutors fumbled the case, and not one of you has the spine to explain what went wrong. If this is what ‘Reformasi’ looks like in practice — silent acceptance when your political enemies go free on your watch — then you have become exactly what you promised to replace.

Penurunan ini tidak seragam: pelajar luar bandar, keluarga B40, dan pelajar di negeri yang kurang berprestasi menanggung kesan terburuk. Pandemik mempercepatkan kereputan yang sudah ada — sekolah yang kekurangan dana, guru yang tidak terlatih dengan baik, dan kurikulum yang mengutamakan hafalan berbanding pemikiran kritis. Malaysia menarik diri daripada PISA sepenuhnya pada 2003 dan 2006 kerana keputusan yang memalukan. Pelan Pembangunan Pendidikan Malaysia 2013-2025 sepatutnya membaiki segalanya — ia tidak membaiki apa-apa. Sedekad “pelan induk” dan “pelan transformasi” menghasilkan keputusan terburuk dalam sejarah.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Di bawah BN, Malaysia mengekalkan skor PISA yang sederhana tetapi stabil. Di bawah PH, skor runtuh. Peratusan pelajar di bawah kecekapan asas meningkat daripada 41% kepada 59%. Setiap menteri pendidikan melancarkan pelan induk. Tiada yang menghasilkan peningkatan. Masalah asas — kurikulum yang direka berdasarkan pertimbangan politik dan etnik bukan kecemerlangan pendidikan — kekal tidak disentuh kerana tiada kerajaan yang sanggup membayar kos politik reformasi tulen. JAKIM menerima RM2.6 bilion. Pendidikan tinggi mendapat 4.3% belanjawan.

Pelajar anda 8 tahun di belakang Singapura. 59% tidak boleh buat matematik asas. Hanya 1% mencapai kecekapan tinggi. Keutamaan anda jelas — dan pendidikan bukan salah satunya. Balikkan penurunan belanjawan pendidikan. Laburkan dalam kualiti guru. Reformasi kurikulum untuk pemikiran kritis. Atau terus menghasilkan keputusan PISA terburuk dalam sejarah Malaysia dan tertanya-tanya mengapa penghijrahan otak semakin pantas.

PISA Scores Collapse — Malaysia Loses 1.6 YEARS of Learning, Now 8 Years Behind Singapore in Math

Said by: OECD PISA 2022 Report / ISIS Malaysia Analysis

Category: Education

Date: 2024-12-06

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The 2022 PISA report, released in December 2024, revealed Malaysia suffered among the top five largest declines globally across all three subjects. Mathematics dropped 31 points (440 to 409), reading fell 27 points (415 to 388), and science declined 22 points (438 to 416). ISIS Malaysia estimated this equates to 1.6 years of lost learning in mathematics alone. 59% of Malaysian students are now below basic proficiency in math — up from 41%. Only 1% of Malaysian students achieved high proficiency. The socioeconomic gap is widening, with wealthy students outperforming disadvantaged ones by 82 points.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia is now 8 years behind Singapore and 3 years behind Vietnam in mathematics educational attainment. This isn’t just a statistic — it’s a generation of young Malaysians who cannot compete in the global knowledge economy. The decline is not evenly distributed: rural students, B40 families, and students in underperforming states bear the worst impact. When 59% of your 15-year-olds can’t do basic math, the consequences ripple through the economy for decades — in lower productivity, higher unemployment, and reduced innovation capacity.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government blamed COVID-19 school closures, but the OECD average only dropped 15 points in math — Malaysia dropped double at 31 points. Other countries with similar closures performed better. The decline predates COVID: Malaysia has been falling since 2015. The pandemic accelerated a rot that was already there — underfunded schools, poorly trained teachers, and a curriculum that prioritises rote memorisation over critical thinking. Malaysia pulled out of PISA entirely in 2003 and 2006 because the results were embarrassing. The Malaysia Education Blueprint 2013-2025 was supposed to fix everything — it fixed nothing. A decade of “blueprints” and “transformation plans” produced the worst results in history.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, Malaysia maintained mediocre but stable PISA scores. Under PH, scores collapsed. The percentage of students below basic proficiency increased from 41% to 59%. Every education minister launches a blueprint. None delivers improvement. The fundamental problem — a curriculum designed around political and ethnic considerations rather than educational excellence — remains untouched because no government has been willing to pay the political cost of genuine reform. JAKIM receives RM2.6 billion. Higher education gets 4.3% of the budget.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your own minister admitted the system “failed both teachers and students.” You allocate RM2.6 billion to JAKIM for religious affairs but cut higher education to 4.3% of the budget. Your students are 8 years behind Singapore. 59% can’t do basic math. Only 1% achieve high proficiency. Your priorities are clear — and education isn’t one of them. Reverse the education budget decline. Invest in teacher quality. Reform the curriculum for critical thinking. Or keep producing the worst PISA results in Malaysian history and wonder why the brain drain accelerates.

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada Disember 2024, Bloomberg melaporkan bahawa Malaysia menghadapi tentangan atas rancangan mengetatkan cengkaman ke atas internet. Kerajaan mencari kuasa dan penalti yang lebih luas untuk penyalahgunaan internet melalui peraturan baharu. SKMM diberi kuasa mengenakan denda ke atas platform sehingga RM10 juta.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Internet Malaysia pernah menjadi antara yang paling bebas di rantau ini — dilindungi oleh Jaminan MSC Malaysia yang berjanji tiada penapisan internet. PH secara sistematik membongkar jaminan itu. Apabila pencipta kandungan menapis sendiri, kesan menakutkan sudah berjaya — tiada pendakwaan diperlukan. Gabungan penyekatan laman web, pelesenan platform, penalti media sosial dan peruntukan AKM “sangat menyinggung” yang dikembalikan mewujudkan infrastruktur penapisan komprehensif yang tidak wujud ketika PH berkuasa.

Di bawah PH 2.0, rejim pengganti lebih komprehensif: penyekatan laman web melalui SKMM, pelesenan platform, Akta Keselamatan Dalam Talian, pindaan AKM dengan penalti RM10 juta, dan penapisan sendiri yang didorong oleh ketidakpastian pengawalseliaan. Setiap langkah individu dibingkaikan sebagai melindungi kanak-kanak, membanteras penipuan, atau melawan maklumat palsu. Tarik balik penalti berlebihan. Nyahsekat laman berita kritikal. Lindungi internet bebas yang memberi anda kuasa. Atau terima bahawa anda telah membina infrastruktur penapisan yang setiap kerajaan autoritarian masa depan akan berterima kasih kepada anda.

Bloomberg: Malaysia 'Faces Backlash Over Plan to Broaden Internet Controls'

Said by: Bloomberg and critics

Category: Social

Date: 2024-12-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In December 2024, Bloomberg reported that Malaysia faced backlash over plans to tighten its grip on the internet. The government sought broader powers and penalties for internet misuse through new regulations. Rest of World reported that creators in Malaysia were self-censoring to avoid crackdowns — “The new rules make it harder to make a living,” they said. ARTICLE 19 called the Federal Court’s CMA decision reinstating “grossly offensive” provisions “a blow to freedom of expression.” Critical news websites were blocked. MCMC was given powers to penalise platforms up to RM10 million.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia’s internet was once one of the freest in the region — protected by the MSC Malaysia Bill of Guarantees that promised no internet censorship. PH is systematically dismantling that guarantee. When content creators self-censor, the chilling effect has already succeeded — no prosecution needed. The combination of website blocking, platform licensing, social media penalties, and the reinstated “grossly offensive” CMA provision creates a comprehensive censorship infrastructure that didn’t exist when PH took power.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH rode the free internet to power. Facebook, YouTube, and WhatsApp were the platforms that broke BN’s media monopoly. Malaysiakini and independent blogs were PH’s lifeline when mainstream media was controlled. Now PH is blocking websites, licensing platforms, penalising content, and creating self-censorship through regulatory threat. The MSC Bill of Guarantees — a founding promise of Malaysia’s digital economy — is dead. PH didn’t just break the promise; it buried it under multiple layers of new regulation that give MCMC more power than it ever had under BN.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, the Anti-Fake News Act was PH’s primary evidence of authoritarian internet control. PH repealed it in 2018. Under PH 2.0, the replacement regime is more comprehensive: website blocking through MCMC, platform licensing, the Online Safety Act, CMA amendments with RM10 million penalties, and self-censorship driven by regulatory uncertainty. Each individual measure is framed as protecting children, combating scams, or fighting misinformation. Collectively, they constitute a censorship apparatus that exceeds anything BN built.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Bloomberg, Rest of World, ARTICLE 19, and Freedom House all agree: you’re building an internet censorship regime. Creators are self-censoring. Websites are blocked. Platforms face RM10 million penalties. The MSC Bill of Guarantees promised no internet censorship. You’ve broken that promise more comprehensively than any previous government. Withdraw the excessive penalties. Unblock critical news sites. Protect the free internet that gave you power. Or accept that you’ve built the censorship infrastructure every future authoritarian government will thank you for.
