[BM] Rafizi Dakwa Azam Baki Pegang Saham RM14 Juta — Desak Kerajaan Dedahkan Penemuan Pasukan Petugas Yang Disimpan Rapi

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, Pandan MP and Former Economy Minister (PKR)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 13 Mac 2026, Ahli Parlimen Pandan Rafizi Ramli — Naib Presiden PKR dan bekas Menteri Ekonomi — mengeluarkan kenyataan awam mendesak kerajaan mendedahkan sepenuhnya penemuan siasatan pasukan petugas khas terhadap pegangan saham ketua SPRM Azam Baki. Rafizi mendakwa, merujuk sumber kerajaan, bahawa siasatan menemui Azam memegang saham bernilai RM14 juta merentasi sembilan syarikat — jauh melebihi apa yang gaji penjawat awam kanan mampu jelaskan. Setakat ini, jawapan kerajaan: serahkan kepada lembaga tatatertib Jabatan Perkhidmatan Awam, dengan Ketua Setiausaha Negara Shamsul Azri Abu Bakar mengendalikan langkah seterusnya. Tiada pendedahan awam tentang kandungan laporan.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Ini ahli politik PH yang secara terbuka memecah barisan dengan strategi kerajaan sendiri yang mahu menyembunyikan penemuan kes Azam Baki. Campur tangan Rafizi mendedahkan jurang yang semakin lebar antara retorik antirasuah PH dengan cara mereka mengendalikan skandal integriti paling besar melibatkan ketua SPRM yang masih berkhidmat. Jika Azam benar-benar memegang saham bernilai RM14 juta — 17.7 juta unit saham dalam Velocity Capital sahaja, menurut laporan Bloomberg sebelum ini — ini bukan masalah sumber manusia untuk JPA uruskan secara senyap. Ini potensi kes jenayah melibatkan kekayaan tidak dapat dijelaskan oleh ketua agensi antirasuah negara, dan kerajaan tahu perkara ini.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Kerajaan terima laporan pasukan petugas, simpan rapi, serahkan kepada Ketua Setiausaha Negara, tolak gesaan tubuhkan RCI, dan sekarang mahu kita percaya bahawa perbicaraan lembaga tatatertib sudah memadai sebagai akauntabiliti bagi seorang lelaki yang didakwa memegang jutaan ringgit saham tidak diisytiharkan sambil mengetuai SPRM. Rafizi betul mendesak ini — tapi jangan lupa, beliau pernah duduk dalam Kabinet yang sama yang biarkan isu ini berlarutan bertahun-tahun. Menteri DAP Loke dan Gobind juga gesakan RCI, namun mereka kekal dalam Kabinet yang secara jelas menolaknya. Percanggahan ini jelas kepada semua: menteri PH secara terbuka menuntut ketelusan daripada kerajaan mereka sendiri, bermakna sama ada Kabinet mengabaikan ahli sendiri, atau ini perbezaan pendapat lakonan yang direka untuk memberi PH alasan sambil kedudukan Azam kekal tidak tersentuh.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Ini mengikut corak Malaysia yang sudah usang. Kontroversi pegangan saham Azam Baki pertama kali terbongkar pada penghujung 2021, apabila pemberi maklumat Lalitha Kunaratnam mendedahkan pegangannya dalam syarikat tersenarai awam. Di bawah kerajaan PN sebelum ini, tiada apa berlaku. Di bawah kerajaan perpaduan pimpinan PH, pasukan petugas ditubuhkan — hanya selepas pendedahan Bloomberg 2025 mengenai dakwaan rangkaian “mafia korporat” memaksa Putrajaya bertindak. Pasukan petugas melapor kepada Kabinet pada 11 Mac 2026, tetapi Fahmi enggan dedahkan penemuan dan tolak gesaan RCI. Sementara itu, CEO C4 diheret ke Bukit Aman di bawah Seksyen 124B kerana mengulas isu ini, dan dua rakaman TikTok MalaysiaNow mengenai topik ini dipadamkan. Coraknya jelas: siasat secara senyap, dedahkan apa-apa pun tidak, hukum mereka yang bertanya soalan.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

Kamu janji Malaysia Madani — kerajaan yang dibina atas integriti, ketelusan, dan tadbir urus baik. Kamu beritahu pengundi pada 2022 bahawa kamu akan bersihkan rumah. Ketua agensi antirasuah kamu didakwa memegang saham bernilai RM14 juta merentasi sembilan syarikat, dan jawapan kamu lembaga tatatertib? Bukan RCI. Bukan laporan awam. Bukan pun sidang akhbar dengan butiran sebenar. Apabila bekas menteri kamu sendiri terpaksa keluarkan kenyataan awam mendesak kamu buat perkara yang betul, itu bukan ketelusan — itu memalukan. Dedahkan laporan penuh pasukan petugas. Jelaskan RM14 juta itu. Beritahu rakyat Malaysia kenapa lelaki yang menyiasat rasuah di negara ini nampaknya memiliki kekayaan tidak dapat dijelaskan. Atau akui bahawa Azam Baki tidak boleh disentuh, dan biarkan rakyat Malaysia buat kesimpulan sendiri tentang apa sebenarnya makna Madani.

Amir Hamzah Says Economy 'Expected to Remain Strong' in 2026 — While Oil Tops $100, KLCI Crashes, and Cabinet Announces Austerity

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Finance Minister II Amir Hamzah Azizan told Bernama on March 13, 2026, that Malaysia’s economic performance in 2025 is “expected to continue driving the country’s economic momentum” in 2026. He said this despite acknowledging that the West Asia war had driven oil past US$100 per barrel, and on the same day the Cabinet announced cost-cutting measures including banning Raya open houses and restricting ministerial overseas travel. The KLCI had crashed below 1700 that same day, and the monthly fuel subsidy bill had ballooned to RM3.2 billion.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the third senior minister in two days to project economic optimism while the country’s finances are visibly deteriorating. Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah said essentially the same thing on March 12. The pattern is clear: the government’s crisis communication strategy is to keep repeating growth targets as if macro numbers insulate the rakyat from fuel price shocks, food inflation, and a tanking stock market. When Finance Minister II says the economy will be “strong” on the same day the Cabinet imposes austerity, the disconnect between governance and messaging becomes a credibility problem.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

If the economy is so strong, why did the Cabinet just ban Hari Raya open houses and restrict overseas travel for fiscal discipline? Why is the government tripling the RON95 subsidy to RM2 billion per month? Why is the KLCI below 1700? Amir Hamzah is not describing reality — he is performing confidence. The job of a Finance Minister is not to be a cheerleader; it is to level with the public about risks and prepare them for trade-offs. Instead, we get the same playbook as always: cite last year’s GDP, project this year’s growth range, and pretend the crisis doesn’t exist.

📜 Previous Cases

This is a recurring pattern. On March 12, Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah maintained the 4.0–4.5% growth target and celebrated 2025’s 5.2% GDP on the same day fuel prices exploded. On March 9, Deputy Economy Minister Mohd Shahar projected “steady” 4–4.5% growth amid a regional war. On March 8, Anwar himself told Malaysians to “be thrifty” — days after committing RM15 billion in cash handouts. The government’s economic messaging exists in a parallel universe from its own policy decisions.

🎯 PH Callout

You campaigned on transparency and competent governance. Your own Cabinet just imposed emergency austerity measures because the fiscal situation is deteriorating. And on the same afternoon, your Finance Minister II tells the press the economy is “expected to remain strong”? Pick one. Either the crisis is serious enough to ban Raya open houses, or the economy is strong. It cannot be both. The rakyat can read headlines — they know when they’re being managed instead of governed.

Amir Hamzah Tells Malaysians 'Don't Speculate' on Fuel After Special Cabinet — While KLCI Crashes Below 1700 and Subsidy Bill Balloons to RM3.2 Billion

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Finance Minister II Datuk Seri Amir Hamzah Azizan held a press conference after the special Cabinet meeting on March 13 — the meeting that was supposed to deliver the government’s comprehensive crisis response to the West Asia conflict. His headline message: the Malaysian economy “remains resilient,” the government is sourcing additional oil, and Malaysians should stop spreading or acting on speculation about fuel availability because it “could create unnecessary negative consequences.” No new economic relief measures, no targeted aid packages, no toll freezes, no food price interventions — just reassurance.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was the special Cabinet meeting that was supposed to matter — the one Fahmi previewed on March 11, the one Fadillah built up on March 12, the one the whole country was watching. And the outcome? A Finance Minister telling citizens not to worry and not to talk about it. Meanwhile, the FBM KLCI crashed below 1,700 for the first time in years, the monthly fuel subsidy bill has ballooned to RM3.2 billion, and economists are warning of a potential global recession triggered by the oil shock. The gap between what the government says and what the market shows has never been wider.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

When your special Cabinet meeting produces nothing except “don’t speculate” and “economy is resilient,” you don’t have a crisis response — you have a PR operation. Amir Hamzah is literally telling Malaysians to stop talking about the problem instead of solving it. This is the same government that promised fiscal reform, subsidy rationalization, and institutional capacity. Three years in, their crisis playbook is: hold a special meeting, issue reassuring soundbites, tell people to stay calm, and defer every hard decision. Thailand mandated WFH immediately. Malaysia formed a committee, deferred to Tuesday, held a special meeting, and told citizens to shut up.

📜 Previous Cases

This is not new. When the ringgit fell past 4.80 to the dollar in early 2024, Anwar’s response was to blame speculators and tell Malaysians to have confidence. When inflation concerns mounted post-SST reintroduction, the messaging was “everything is under control.” When Bloomberg reported MACC misconduct, Fahmi’s first instinct was to question the source, not the substance. PH’s pattern is consistent: when the facts are bad, attack the people pointing out the facts. Amir Hamzah’s “don’t speculate” is just the latest variation.

🎯 PH Callout

You called a special Cabinet meeting — the whole country waited. And your answer is “don’t speculate”? The rakyat are paying RM3.85 for RON97, RM3.92 for diesel, watching their portfolios bleed, and wondering how long the RM1.99 RON95 lifeline will hold. They don’t need to be told to calm down. They need a government that shows up with a plan — not a press conference full of reassurance and empty of action. You promised competence, reform, and fiscal discipline. Deliver, or admit you can’t.

Anwar Admits Reforms 'Not As Quick As We'd Like' — After Three Years of Broken Promises, That's the Best He's Got

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and PKR President

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

On March 13, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim addressed Sarawak PKR members at a “mini convention” in Kuching and admitted that PKR is “not perfect” and that reforms are not being implemented “as quickly as we would like.” He told party members to “close ranks” and continue the reform struggle, declaring: “The true test of executing change or reforms is when one is in the seat of power, not after losing power or having been removed.” This was said on the same day his special Cabinet meeting deferred the WFH decision to yet another review, tripled the RON95 subsidy to RM2 billion instead of reforming it, and had the Agriculture Minister telling Malaysians to grow their own vegetables.

🔥 Why It Matters

Anwar’s admission is significant because it is the closest he has come to acknowledging the yawning gap between Pakatan Harapan’s reform promises and its delivery in government. After more than three years in power, the reform scorecard is dismal: the PM term limit bill failed in Parliament with 32 MPs absent and 44 abstaining, targeted fuel subsidy reform has been abandoned in favour of a ballooning blanket subsidy, the MACC chief remains protected despite credible misconduct allegations, and anti-corruption civil society organisations are being interrogated under Section 124B. The fact that Anwar chose to make this admission at a closed party event in Sarawak — far from the KL press corps — rather than at the post-Cabinet press conference tells you everything about who this message was really for: the faithful, not the public.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Admitting you’re “not perfect” is the political equivalent of a corporate apology that takes no responsibility. Anwar’s framing — that the “true test” of reform is when you’re in power — is supposed to sound humble and honest. But it’s actually an argument against his own government: if the true test is in the seat of power, and you’ve been in it for three years, then you are being graded right now, and you are failing. The PM term limit bill collapsed on his watch. Fuel subsidy reform, promised since 2023, is dead. The MACC scandal festers while his Communications Minister blocks transparency. “Not as quick as we’d like” is a masterclass in managed expectations — lowering the bar so the base accepts less while the patronage networks keep running.

📜 Previous Cases

Anwar has a long pattern of promising reform and delivering incrementalism or retreat. In January 2026, he pushed the PM term limit bill as a signature reform — it failed in March. In 2023-2024, targeted fuel subsidy reform was the centrepiece of fiscal policy — it has been shelved indefinitely. The CeIO “integrity culture” programme launched on March 12, 2026, the same week his government shielded Azam Baki and interrogated C4. PKR’s own internal reform credentials are thin: the Rafizi faction of 10 MPs pushing for parliamentary oversight of the public prosecutor has been sidelined. The party’s reform DNA has been diluted by three years of coalition management with UMNO.

🎯 PH Callout

You told the rakyat in 2022 that you were different. That you would reform the system, not just occupy it. Three years later, your own president stands before party faithful in Kuching and admits reforms aren’t happening fast enough — and your answer is to “close ranks.” Close ranks around what, exactly? A tripled fuel subsidy with no reform timeline? A protected MACC chief? An Agriculture Minister telling people to plant chilli in their backyards? The rakyat didn’t vote for managed expectations. They voted for Reformasi. If the true test of reform is when you’re in power, you are failing your own exam. Stop asking for patience and start delivering results — or admit that PH in government is just BN with better PR.

Anwar Says RON95 Subsidy Could Hit RM24 Billion — His Solution? 'Pray for Peace'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim revealed on March 13 that the government’s subsidy bill to maintain RON95 at RM1.99 per litre under the BUDI95 scheme could reach RM24 billion by year-end if the Middle East conflict persists. Speaking after the special Cabinet meeting, Anwar said the calculation was based on a monthly RON95 subsidy cost of RM2 billion. His proposed solution: “We must pray for a swift end to this conflict so that we won’t be burdened any further.” He added that the government would look for “other ways” to fund the subsidy through cost-cutting.

🔥 Why It Matters

The RM24 billion figure is a fiscal bombshell. The entire 2026 fuel subsidy allocation was originally a fraction of this. The number lays bare the total failure of subsidy reform — a policy Anwar promised since taking office in November 2022. Instead of a targeted subsidy mechanism, Malaysia now has a blanket subsidy that costs three times what it did a month ago, with no exit plan beyond hoping that a war ends. This is not fiscal management. This is fiscal surrender dressed up as compassion.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar had three years to implement targeted fuel subsidies. He launched BUDI95 in November 2025, but it was designed for stable oil prices, not a crisis. When the crisis hit, instead of activating the targeting mechanism he built, he froze RON95 at RM1.99 for everyone — including wealthy Malaysians driving luxury cars — and his solution is prayer. “Pray for peace” is not a fiscal strategy. It is the response of a government that spent its reform capital on election-cycle handouts and is now trapped by its own populism.

📜 Previous Cases

This echoes the BN playbook Anwar once campaigned against. Under Najib Razak, blanket fuel subsidies ballooned and were used as a political tool to buy loyalty before elections. Anwar explicitly promised to end this cycle. In 2023, he said targeted subsidies would be implemented “within months.” In 2024, he said “soon.” In 2025, he launched BUDI95 — and immediately undermined it by freezing the price floor when prices spiked. The RM24 billion figure is the cost of three years of broken promises.

🎯 PH Callout

You said you were different from BN. You said blanket subsidies were wasteful. You said targeting was the answer. Now you’re spending more on blanket subsidies than Najib ever did, and your backup plan is prayer. If RM24 billion doesn’t buy reform, what does it buy? It buys votes. And the rakyat — the ones you claim to protect — will pay for it in inflation, debt, and austerity down the road. This is not Madani. This is Najib with a better vocabulary.

Government Triples RON95 Subsidy to RM2 Billion — Three Years of 'Reform' Ends With a Bigger Blanket

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

On Friday, March 13, Finance Minister II Amir Hamzah Azizan announced that the government would increase its spending on RON95 petrol subsidies from RM700 million to RM2 billion — nearly tripling the allocation — to maintain the fixed retail price of RM1.99 per litre amid surging global oil prices past $100/barrel. The announcement came after the Special Cabinet Meeting convened to address the West Asia conflict’s economic impact. The subsidy covers RON95, the most widely used transport fuel in Malaysia, consumed by the vast majority of motorists.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the final burial of Anwar’s subsidy reform agenda. Since taking office in late 2022, the Madani government has repeatedly promised to move from blanket to targeted fuel subsidies — a reform the World Bank, IMF, and Malaysia’s own fiscal advisors have urged for years. The diesel subsidy was partially rationalised in mid-2024. RON95 was supposed to follow. Instead, with a global energy crisis exposing every weakness in the current system, the government’s response is to pour three times more money into the same untargeted mechanism. The RM2 billion figure reveals the true fiscal cost of inaction: had targeted subsidies been implemented on schedule, the government would be spending a fraction of this amount while still protecting B40 households.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Three years. That’s how long this government has had to implement targeted fuel subsidies. Every budget speech since 2023 has included some version of “subsidy rationalisation will proceed in phases.” The T20 income verification system was supposedly ready. The Budi mechanism for diesel was supposed to be the template. And yet here we are in March 2026, and the only tool in the government’s kit is to triple the blanket subsidy — meaning T20 earners driving luxury SUVs get the same RM1.99 petrol as B40 workers on motorcycles. This is not crisis management. This is the consequence of three years of delayed reform catching up with a government that was too afraid of electoral backlash to act when oil was cheap.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has been here before. Najib’s government removed fuel subsidies in 2014, then brought them back when oil prices dropped, missing the reform window entirely. Anwar criticised that flip-flop from the opposition bench. Now he’s doing the same thing with extra steps. The diesel subsidy reform of June 2024 was supposed to be Phase 1 of a broader rationalisation. Phase 2 (RON95) was initially targeted for late 2024, then pushed to 2025, then to “after economic conditions stabilise.” The conditions never stabilise because this government keeps waiting for the perfect moment that never comes.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reform. You promised targeted subsidies. You had years of relatively stable oil prices to implement them. You didn’t. Now you’re tripling the blanket subsidy and calling it crisis management. Every ringgit of that RM2 billion that flows to T20 drivers is money that could have gone to B40 cash transfers, public transport, or food price interventions. You didn’t build the targeted system when you could. Now the rakyat pays for your procrastination — twice: once through inflation, and again through a fiscal bill that crowds out spending on everything else.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is performative austerity wrapped in reform language. The Raya open house ban was announced the same week the government rolled out RM15 billion in STR cash handouts and RM500 civil servant bonuses — classic pre-election spending. Hannah Yeoh framing the open house ban as fiscal discipline is spin at its most transparent: the government isn’t saving money, it’s redirecting spending to vote-buying mechanisms while cancelling the one thing that costs ministries relatively little but generates public visibility. The “people-centred” claim rings hollow when the same minister was notably absent from the Kampung Lee Kong Chian eviction crisis days earlier, where 146 families in KL’s last private Chinese village were told to pack up for RM50,000 each.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Hannah Yeoh praising a Raya open house ban as evidence of good governance is like praising a shoplifter for returning the plastic bag. The open houses were never the fiscal problem — the RM15 billion in election-timed handouts are. And the optics of praising “cost-cutting” while personally distributing RM150 handouts at a private-company-sponsored event in your own constituency is not fiscal discipline; it’s constituency service dressed up as national policy. Hannah Yeoh has form in this space. As Women, Family and Community Development Minister in the first Anwar cabinet, she was known for symbolic announcements that rarely translated into structural reform. Her track record in Federal Territories has been similarly optics-heavy: she launched Yayasan Wilayah Persekutuan initiatives with great fanfare but faced criticism over the Kampung Lee Kong Chian evictions, rising rents in KL, and the lack of affordable housing delivery. PH’s broader pattern is the same — announce savings in visible, low-cost areas while maintaining or expanding spending in areas that benefit their political base.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised a government that would be different. You promised transparency, reform, and fiscal responsibility. Instead, you cancel Raya open houses — a gesture worth maybe a few million ringgit across all ministries — while spending RM15 billion on cash handouts timed for maximum electoral impact. Hannah Yeoh, you were in Segambut handing out RM150 bags while 146 families in Kampung Lee Kong Chian, in your own Federal Territories jurisdiction, are being evicted for RM50,000 each. That is not “people-centred.” That is camera-centred. If the Madani government wants Malaysians to believe in fiscal discipline, start with the RM15 billion — not the open houses.

If DAP cannot manage an open, credible leadership transition in Penang — the one state they’ve governed for nearly two decades — why should voters trust PH to reform national institutions? The rakyat deserves better than dynasty politics and CEC backroom deals. Show us the process, or admit you don’t have one.

Mat Sabu Says Food Supply 'Sufficient Until June' and Tells Rakyat Not to Panic — While Food Prices Spike and Oil Tops $100

Said by: Mohamad Sabu, Agriculture and Food Security Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Agriculture and Food Security Minister Mohamad Sabu told a post-Cabinet press conference on March 13, 2026 that Malaysia’s food supply — rice, chicken, eggs, fish, beef, buffalo meat, fresh milk, vegetables and fruits — is ‘sufficient at least until May or June.’ He urged Malaysians not to panic-buy ahead of Hari Raya and to avoid food wastage. This came after the special Cabinet meeting convened to assess the fallout from the West Asia crisis, with oil past $100/barrel and the subsidy bill ballooning to RM3.2 billion monthly.

🔥 Why It Matters

Mat Sabu’s reassurance follows the same crisis-management playbook the entire Cabinet has adopted this week: tell Malaysians not to worry, don’t speculate, don’t panic. But food inflation is not a future risk — it is already happening. Transport costs feed directly into food prices. The government has announced no food price caps, no targeted subsidies for essential goods, and no concrete intervention beyond monitoring. ‘Sufficient supply’ and ‘affordable prices’ are not the same thing, and Mat Sabu conflated the two.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Telling Malaysians not to panic while offering zero new measures to cushion food price inflation is not governance — it is vibes. Thailand has mandated WFH and capped select food prices. Vietnam acted immediately on energy-saving directives. Malaysia’s Agriculture Minister is telling people the fridge is full while ignoring that the price tags on everything inside it have gone up. ‘Sufficient until June’ is also a conveniently short timeline — what happens after June when Hari Raya is over and the crisis hasn’t resolved? The government is managing news cycles, not food security.

📜 Previous Cases

Mat Sabu has been making the same ‘food supply is stable’ reassurances since the crisis escalated in early March 2026, telling reporters on March 5 and again on March 6 that imports are diversified and supply chains are intact. He said the same things during the 2022 chicken shortage crisis and the 2023 egg price spikes. The pattern is clear: announce stability, avoid price controls, and hope the market sorts itself out before the political cost hits.

Mat Sabu Tells Ministry Staff to Grow Their Own Vegetables — Government's Food Security Plan Is Now a Backyard Garden

Said by: Mohamad Sabu, Agriculture and Food Security Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Agriculture and Food Security Minister Mohamad Sabu, speaking after the special Cabinet meeting on March 13, directed ministry staff living in landed properties to start growing their own vegetables. He also encouraged households with available land to cultivate fast-harvesting crops such as chilli, eggplant and chives to supplement food supply. The Cabinet also agreed to increase the national rice stockpile to cover nine months. The ministry later had to clarify the vegetable-growing instruction was “not a directive” after public backlash.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the Agriculture Minister’s contribution to Malaysia’s crisis response as oil tops $100 a barrel, the KLCI crashes below 1700, and food inflation accelerates. Instead of announcing emergency food price controls, expanded subsidies for farmers, or concrete supply chain interventions, Mat Sabu’s marquee post-Cabinet announcement was that Malaysians should plant vegetables in their backyards. It signals a government that has run out of institutional tools — or never had them — and is now outsourcing food security to individual households while maintaining a RM3.2 billion fuel subsidy bill.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH came to power promising institutional reform, modern governance, and serious food security policy. Three years later, their Agriculture Minister’s crisis plan is a kampung garden. This is a minister whose portfolio covers a RM5 billion budget, FAMA, MARDI, and the entire national food supply chain — and his response to a global energy crisis disrupting supply chains is “tanam sayur sendiri.” The fact that his own ministry had to clarify it was “not a directive” tells you everything: even the bureaucrats know this is embarrassing. You don’t see Thailand or Vietnam telling citizens to plant eggplant — they mandate WFH, cap prices, and activate strategic reserves.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows Mat Sabu’s March 12 post-Cabinet reassurance that food supply is “sufficient until May or June” while telling Malaysians not to panic-buy — the same minister who has been making identical assurances since the crisis began on February 28. In September 2025, Anwar himself urged Malaysians to “farm at home” and “not leave land idle” at a National Farmers’ Market event. The grow-your-own-food rhetoric is now a recurring PH deflection: when the state can’t deliver food security through policy, it tells citizens to become subsistence farmers.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia Madani — a modern, responsive, competent government. Your Agriculture Minister’s crisis plan is telling civil servants to plant chilli in their garden. This is not food security policy. This is a government that has substituted Instagram-friendly photo ops for institutional capacity. If your best advice to Malaysians facing $100-barrel oil and accelerating food inflation is “grow eggplant,” then you have failed at the most basic function of the ministry. The rakyat didn’t vote for gardening tips — they voted for governance.
