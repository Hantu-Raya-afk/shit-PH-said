Cabinet Greenlights Port Klang Third Terminal on Carey Island — A 5,000-Acre Megaproject With GLC Fingerprints All Over It

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Transport Minister Anthony Loke announced that Cabinet has approved the development of Port Klang’s Third Terminal on Carey Island — a megaproject that has been in limbo for years. The project involves over 5,000 acres of land, with SD Guthrie (a GLC under PNB) and the Selangor State Development Corporation (PKNS) already staking claims on thousands of hectares of land and seabed. PKNS was allocated 1,011 hectares of seabed land and a 688-hectare Yayasan Selangor plot was placed under joint PKNS management. PM Anwar issued a directive in December 2025 to accelerate the project, and now Cabinet has formally greenlit it.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is one of the largest infrastructure approvals of the Anwar administration — and it has GLC and state-linked corporation fingerprints all over it. The land allocation to PKNS and the SD Guthrie partnership were locked in before any competitive tender for the port itself. Carey Island is home to the Mah Meri Orang Asli community, and environmental and displacement concerns have dogged this project for years. Anwar promised in December 2025 that the project would proceed “with safeguards for Orang Asli and the environment,” but no independent environmental review or community consent mechanism has been made public. The approval comes amid a Middle East crisis, fiscal austerity theatre (banning Raya open houses), and pre-election positioning — making the timing politically convenient for announcing a mega-development that benefits Selangor’s PH-aligned state machinery.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH ran on transparency, open tenders, and ending GLC capture of public assets. What we got instead is a 5,000-acre megaproject carved up between a PNB-linked plantation giant and the Selangor state GLC before the public even saw a development plan. The Selangor MB’s office admitted the land allocation was “decided a few weeks ago” — before Cabinet even approved the project. That’s not governance; that’s pre-positioning. And the Orang Asli? They get “safeguards” in speeches and displacement in practice. This is exactly the kind of opaque, GLC-driven megaproject that PH promised to end when it was in opposition.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s infrastructure megaproject history is littered with cost overruns, opaque procurement, and GLC enrichment. The East Coast Rail Link (ECRL) was renegotiated but still lacks full transparency. The Penang Transport Master Plan has faced years of environmental and governance controversy. Port Klang itself was the site of the Port Klang Free Zone (PKFZ) scandal — a RM12.5 billion fiasco involving inflated land costs, political patronage, and criminal charges. PH’s own 2018-2020 government inherited that mess and promised never to repeat it. Now they’re greenlighting another massive port project on the same stretch of coast, with pre-allocated GLC land deals and no visible competitive process.

🎯 PH Callout

You told voters you’d end the cosy GLC-state nexus that turns public land into private profit. You said open tenders and institutional reform would replace backroom deals. So where is the open tender for Port Klang Terminal 3? Where is the independent environmental impact assessment? Where is the Orang Asli consultation? A 5,000-acre development involving SD Guthrie, PKNS, and Yayasan Selangor land — all allocated before Cabinet even voted — is not reform. It’s the same game with a Madani label. If you can ban Raya open houses for “fiscal discipline,” you can publish the full procurement framework for a multi-billion ringgit port before you approve it.

Cabinet Maintains RON95 at RM1.99 — Anwar Calls It a 'Difficult Decision' While Dodging Subsidy Reform for the Third Year Running

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced on March 11, 2026 — following a Cabinet meeting — that the price of RON95 petrol under the Budi95 targeted subsidy mechanism will remain at RM1.99 per litre. He called it a “difficult decision,” noting that the market price of RON95 has risen roughly threefold due to the escalating US-Iran conflict disrupting oil markets and threatening Gulf shipping routes. RON97 had already risen to RM3.25, unsubsidised RON95 to RM2.67, and diesel to RM3.12. Anwar framed the decision as a sacrifice for the rakyat.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not generosity — it is the collapse of reform. Anwar’s government promised targeted fuel subsidy restructuring as a cornerstone of fiscal discipline. The Budi95 mechanism, launched as a watered-down compromise, was supposed to be the first step toward removing blanket subsidies. Instead, a geopolitical crisis has given the government the perfect excuse to shelve reform indefinitely while spending billions on untargeted fuel subsidies ahead of widely expected snap elections. Every week the subsidy is maintained at current market spreads, Malaysia hemorrhages fiscal resources that could go to healthcare, education, or infrastructure.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar has been “about to reform” fuel subsidies since he took office in November 2022. In 2023, it was “next year.” In 2024, it was “soon.” In 2025, the Budi95 mechanism finally launched — then immediately became a political tool rather than a fiscal one. Just five days ago, Anwar admitted he could only hold the RM1.99 price for “one or two months.” Now, with Hari Raya approaching and elections rumoured for mid-2026, the Cabinet “decides” to maintain it — as if there was ever a chance they’d raise fuel prices weeks before a festive season and a potential snap poll. This isn’t a difficult decision. It’s the only decision a government terrified of voters would make.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has been trapped in the fuel subsidy cycle for decades. Najib’s government removed RON95 subsidies in 2014 with a managed float, only for Mahathir’s PH 1.0 government to reintroduce price ceilings. Anwar promised to break the pattern with targeted subsidies — means-tested, fiscally responsible, reform-driven. Instead, Budi95 kept the same RM1.99 price for the vast majority of motorists and excluded only foreign-registered vehicles and luxury cars. The IMF, World Bank, and Malaysia’s own fiscal policy advisors have repeatedly warned that blanket fuel subsidies are regressive, benefiting the wealthy more than the poor. Anwar knows this. He cited these studies himself when he was in opposition.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you’d be different. You said you’d make the hard calls Najib and Muhyiddin wouldn’t. You quoted fiscal responsibility in every speech, promised targeted subsidies in every manifesto, and lectured the opposition about populism from every podium. Now you’re spending billions to keep fuel cheap during Ramadan, ahead of Hari Raya, ahead of elections you’re quietly preparing for. This isn’t reform. This is the oldest trick in Malaysian politics — and you swore you’d never play it. Every ringgit spent on untargeted subsidies is a ringgit stolen from the structural reforms you promised the rakyat.

Fahmi Announces Special Committee to 'Assess' Economic Impact of US-Iran Conflict — Government's Crisis Playbook Is Another Committee

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil announced on March 11 that a special committee, led by Petronas chairman Amir Hamzah and including Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir, will evaluate the economic impact of the US-Iran conflict on Malaysia ‘on a daily basis.’ The committee’s findings will be presented at a special Cabinet meeting scheduled for Friday, March 13. Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim said Malaysia’s petroleum supply is ‘sufficient at least until May 2026,’ even as global oil markets reel from the Strait of Hormuz disruption and retaliatory strikes between Iran, the US, and Israel that began on February 28.

🔥 Why It Matters

The government’s instinctive response to a crisis that is already hitting Malaysian wallets — RON97 up 60 sen, diesel up 80 sen, food inflation incoming — is to form a committee to ‘assess’ the damage. Not to act. Not to freeze tolls, cap food prices, mandate work-from-home, or cut wasteful political spending. To study. This is the Madani crisis management playbook distilled to its essence: announce a committee, schedule a meeting, promise a report, and tell the rakyat to wait. The committee doesn’t even meet until March 12, two full weeks after the conflict began. Fuel prices have already spiked. Supply chains are already disrupted. A Thai cargo ship was attacked near the Strait of Hormuz. The time for ‘assessment’ was February 28. The time for action was last week.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

This is not governance. This is bureaucratic theatre dressed up as crisis management. The committee is led by the Petronas chairman — the same entity that benefits from higher oil prices — and includes the Economy Minister, who was publicly projecting 4-4.5% growth just days ago as if the region wasn’t on fire. Fahmi’s announcement came on the same day as a cascade of other Cabinet decisions: banning Raya open houses for ‘fiscal discipline’ while approving RM15 billion in cash handouts, greenlighting a 5,000-acre GLC megaproject, and announcing RM500 bonuses for civil servants and mosque staff. The committee is the government’s way of looking responsive without being responsive. It is a press release pretending to be policy.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long and inglorious history of governance-by-committee. Anwar’s administration has appointed special committees for diesel smuggling, MACC reform, the Azam Baki shareholding issue, and now the economic impact of a war. The pattern is always the same: announce the committee, generate a headline, then quietly let the findings gather dust or punt the decision to someone else — as happened this same week when the Azam Baki committee report was handed to the Chief Secretary with no public disclosure. The committee reflex is not unique to PH, but PH was supposed to be different. They promised responsive, transparent, and accountable governance. Instead they’ve perfected the art of the bureaucratic stall.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised a government that acts, not one that studies the problem while the rakyat burns through their savings at the pump. Two weeks into the worst geopolitical energy crisis since the Gulf War, your response is a committee that hasn’t met yet, a report that hasn’t been written, and a special Cabinet meeting that hasn’t happened. The rakyat doesn’t need a committee to tell them diesel is more expensive. They need a government that acts like it understands urgency. Every day you ‘assess,’ someone skips a meal, absorbs a delivery surcharge, or watches their margins collapse. Stop studying the fire. Put it out.

Fahmi Says Cabinet Wants Stricter 3R Laws — PH Promised to Repeal Restrictive Speech Laws, Now They're Writing New Ones

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Communications Minister and government spokesperson Fahmi Fadzil announced that Cabinet has decided firm action must be taken on 3R issues — race, religion, and royalty — including on social media. Fahmi said the government is considering amending existing laws or introducing new legislation to close what it describes as loopholes that allow certain individuals to ignore enforcement measures. He said the Attorney General has been directed to act decisively, and that the review would examine gaps in current laws that prevent early enforcement actions such as detentions.

🔥 Why It Matters

Pakatan Harapan’s 2018 manifesto explicitly pledged to repeal or reform the Sedition Act 1948, the Printing Presses and Publications Act, and Section 233 of the Communications and Multimedia Act — the very tools BN used for decades to police speech on race, religion, and royalty. Eight years later, PH has not repealed a single one. Instead, Fahmi is now announcing the government wants to write new restrictive laws on the same topics. This is not reform. This is a government that went from promising to dismantle the censorship apparatus to expanding it — while using the language of ‘harmony’ and ’national unity’ as cover.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Every authoritarian Malaysian government has used 3R as the justification for silencing critics. Mahathir did it. Najib did it. Now Anwar’s government is doing it — in the same week it’s using Section 124B to interrogate anti-corruption activists like C4’s CEO, and in the same month it invoked a ‘Zionist plot’ narrative to justify investigating civil society. The ’loopholes’ Fahmi wants to close are not loopholes — they are what remains of free expression in a system already stacked with repressive laws. When PH says ‘stricter enforcement,’ they don’t mean protecting minorities. They mean giving the state more power to detain first and ask questions later.

📜 Previous Cases

DAP’s own Ramkarpal Singh questioned the continued use of the Sedition Act as recently as January 2026, noting it contradicts PH’s reform agenda. In 2018, Lim Guan Eng called the Sedition Act ‘draconian.’ In opposition, Fahmi himself criticised BN’s use of speech laws to silence dissent. The Sedition Act remains on the books. The Printing Presses Act remains on the books. SOSMA remains on the books. Not one has been repealed. And now, instead of delivering on those promises, PH is proposing to add to the arsenal. The trajectory is clear: from reformers to enforcers in one parliamentary term.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would tear down the walls BN built around free speech. Instead, you’re adding barbed wire. If you cannot handle criticism about race, religion, and royalty without reaching for new detention powers, you have become the thing you said you were fighting. Every new law you pass to silence 3R speech will be inherited by the next government — including the ones you call extremists. Think about that before you hand them a sharpened tool. Your manifesto said repeal. The rakyat is still waiting.

KPDN Scrambles on Fuel Smuggling After Oil Tops $100 — Armizan Promises Enforcement That Should Have Been in Place for Years

Said by: Armizan Mohd Ali, Minister of Domestic Trade and Cost of Living (KPDN)

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 10–11, KPDN Minister Armizan Mohd Ali announced that his ministry would “strengthen enforcement” against fuel smuggling and intensify Ops Tiris operations at national borders, after global oil prices topped US$100 per barrel due to the escalating US-Israel-Iran conflict. Armizan acknowledged that the widening gap between Malaysia’s subsidised fuel prices (RON95 at RM1.99) and market prices in neighbouring countries creates “high risk” of smuggling. He told the public there was “no need for concern or panic buying” and claimed contingency plans were in place. Separately, NST reported that Anwar appointed Deputy Prime Minister Fadillah Yusof to chair a special enforcement committee specifically targeting diesel smuggling (single-source: NST).

🔥 Why It Matters

Malaysia has known about fuel smuggling for years. The government restructured diesel subsidies in 2024 partly because of cross-border leakage. Op Tiris has been running since at least 2023. Yet when the biggest energy crisis in a decade hits — with oil past $100 and the Strait of Hormuz under threat — the government’s response is to announce that it will “strengthen” enforcement it should have had in place all along, and form yet another committee. This is reactive governance dressed up as decisive action. The gap between Malaysia’s RM1.99 RON95 and unsubsidised market prices of RM2.67+ makes Malaysia a fuel smuggling magnet, and every week of delay costs the rakyat.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Armizan’s reassurance that supply is “sufficient” and that “contingency plans are in place” is the same script every KPDN minister reads when things go sideways. No contingency plan has ever been made public. No enforcement statistics on Op Tiris outcomes have been shared. And the formation of a special committee chaired by the DPM — on a problem that already has an existing task force — is textbook bureaucratic theatre. If your enforcement was adequate, you wouldn’t need a new committee every time oil prices spike. The government is spending more time announcing what it will do than actually doing it.

📜 Previous Cases

This pattern goes back decades. In 2022–2023, diesel smuggling across the Thai and Singapore borders was so rampant that KPDN itself admitted losses of hundreds of millions of ringgit annually. The targeted diesel subsidy implemented in June 2024 was supposed to solve the problem — but Armizan’s own admission that “the risk of smuggling remains high” proves it didn’t. In 2025, Anwar boasted about saving RM4 billion in subsidy leakage — a claim that was never independently verified. Now, with oil at $100+ and the subsidy gap wider than ever, the same enforcement gaps are back, and the government’s playbook hasn’t changed.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised subsidy reform would be smart, targeted, and leak-proof. It’s none of those things. You’ve had three years to build border enforcement infrastructure, digitise fuel distribution tracking, and close the smuggling loopholes that every analyst warned you about. Instead, the moment a crisis hits, you scramble to announce “strengthened enforcement” that should already exist and form a committee that duplicates existing task forces. Armizan telling Malaysians “don’t panic buy” while admitting smuggling risk is “high” is not reassurance — it’s a contradiction. Stop forming committees. Start governing.

Mohamad Hasan Says Travel Agencies Must Bring Home Stranded Umrah Pilgrims — 'It Cannot Be Left to the Government Alone'

Said by: Mohamad Hasan, Foreign Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

Foreign Minister Datuk Seri Mohamad Hasan, speaking at a press conference in Putrajaya on March 11, declared that travel companies and agencies managing umrah packages must take full responsibility for bringing home pilgrims stranded by the West Asia conflict. He said airlines must also honour their tickets: ‘If the ticket is with Malaysia Airlines, MAS must bring them home. If it is an AirAsia ticket, AirAsia must do so.’ He added bluntly: ‘It cannot be left to the Malaysian government alone.’ This came the same day the government’s first evacuation flight — a chartered Malaysia Airlines A330-300 — landed at KLIA with just 163 Malaysians from Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Jordan and Syria, after days of airspace closures and flight disruptions across the Gulf.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is a textbook case of the government reframing a crisis-response failure as a question of private-sector accountability. On March 3, Mohamad Hasan told the Dewan Negara that the situation in the Gulf was ‘under control’ and there was ’no need for an evacuation plan.’ Eight days later, his ministry was coordinating emergency repatriation flights while hundreds of Malaysians — including umrah pilgrims who had been stranded for three to five days at airports in Dubai, Doha, Abu Dhabi and Bahrain — waited for someone to get them home. The pivot from ’no evacuation needed’ to ’travel agencies must handle it’ is not reassurance — it is damage control dressed as delegation.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The Foreign Ministry exists precisely for moments like this: when Malaysian citizens are stranded abroad during a conflict and need their government to act. Telling travel agencies and airlines to sort it out is not crisis leadership — it is passing the bill. Many of these pilgrims are elderly, travelling on budget packages, and do not have the leverage to demand rescheduled flights from airlines whose operations are in chaos. The government evacuated 163 people on one chartered flight and then turned to the microphone to say, essentially, ’the rest of you are someone else’s problem.’ Meanwhile, the National Security Council coordinated the evacuation under PM Anwar’s directive — so the government clearly has the machinery. It just chose to limit its use and outsource the rest.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of slow or inadequate evacuation responses. During the 2023 Sudan crisis, Malaysians were stranded for days before Wisma Putra mobilised. During COVID-19, repatriation flights were chaotic, with competing agencies and unclear responsibility chains leaving citizens in limbo. The playbook is familiar: delay, then deflect. Mohamad Hasan’s ‘under control’ assurance on March 3 echoes every Malaysian foreign minister who has ever downplayed a developing crisis until it became undeniable — then blamed someone else for the gaps.

🎯 PH Callout

PH came to power promising a government that puts rakyat first — ‘Kerajaan Madani,’ a compassionate, responsive administration. When your citizens are sleeping on airport floors in the Gulf because airspace is shut and missiles are flying, telling travel agencies to figure it out is not compassion. It is bureaucratic cowardice. Evacuate your people first. Sort out who pays later. That is what a government that means its slogans would do.

Oil Past $100, Strait of Hormuz Shut, Rakyat Burning Fuel to Commute — Anwar Won't Mandate WFH, Freeze Tolls, Cap Food Prices, or Cut GLC Fat

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

The Iran-US war has pushed global oil past $100 per barrel, effectively shut down the Strait of Hormuz — through which nearly 80% of Asia’s oil transits — and sent Malaysia’s FBM KLCI plunging 2.55% in a single session on March 9. RON97 is up to RM3.25, diesel to RM3.12, and Anwar himself admitted on March 7 that the government can only hold RON95 at RM1.99 for ‘one or two more months.’ Freight costs are surging, food prices are next, and experts from NST to Hong Leong IB are warning of imported inflation hitting the rakyat’s kitchen table. Anwar’s response so far: a strongly worded tweet about solidarity, an emergency parliamentary motion condemning Israel, and Zafrul saying Malaysia is ‘prepared’ — without naming a single concrete domestic measure to protect workers and families from the economic fallout.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a theoretical crisis. Malaysians are already paying more at the pump for RON97 and diesel. Freight costs feed directly into food, medicine, and essential goods. The stock market is bleeding. And yet the Prime Minister — who is also the Finance Minister — has not announced a single emergency domestic economic measure. No crisis-mode intervention. No relief. Just speeches about geopolitics while the rakyat’s cost of living climbs in real time. Malaysia proved during COVID that the government can act decisively on domestic economic protection when it wants to. The difference is political will — and right now, Anwar’s will is directed at Parliament speeches about Zionist plots, not at shielding the people he governs.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Four things Anwar could do tomorrow with existing executive power — and won’t:

1. Mandate work-from-home for all WFH-compatible jobs. Malaysia already has a Flexible Work Arrangement framework under the Employment Act. During COVID, the government mandated WFH and it worked. With oil past $100 and commuters burning RM300-500/month on fuel and tolls, a temporary WFH directive for office-based workers would immediately cut household fuel costs, reduce demand on subsidised petrol, and ease the fiscal pressure Anwar says he can only hold for two months. Instead, millions of KL workers sit in traffic burning fuel the government can barely afford to subsidise.

2. Freeze or waive highway tolls during the crisis. The government already broke its toll abolition promise — paying RM591 million just to freeze hikes rather than remove tolls. A temporary toll waiver during a war-driven energy crisis is basic economic relief. Singapore waived ERP during COVID disruptions. Malaysia won’t even pause toll collection while fuel prices spike.

3. Impose emergency price controls on essential food items. Freight costs from Hormuz disruptions are already feeding into import prices. Cooking oil, flour, rice, and protein prices will follow. The Price Control and Anti-Profiteering Act 2011 gives the government power to gazette maximum prices for essential goods. Anwar used this power selectively before. Now, when a global war is about to hit Malaysian dinner tables, silence.

4. Freeze non-essential GLC and political appointment spending. The IDEAS report found 238 political appointments to FSBs and GLCs under Anwar. Every one of those comes with salaries, allowances, and perks funded by public money. During a war-driven fiscal crunch — with the subsidy bill ballooning and oil revenue uncertain — a responsible government would freeze all discretionary patronage spending and redirect it to rakyat relief. Instead, the appointments continue.

📜 Previous Cases

During COVID-19, the Muhyiddin government — for all its democratic illegitimacy — implemented a blanket WFH directive, moratoriums on loan repayments, cash handouts (BPN), and wage subsidies within weeks of the crisis hitting. The Ismail Sabri government continued with hybrid WFH arrangements and extended moratoriums. These were imperfect governments acting under crisis pressure. Anwar, who controls both the PM’s office and the Finance Ministry, has more concentrated fiscal power than either predecessor — and has used less of it in response to a war that is already hitting Malaysian wallets harder than the early weeks of COVID hit fuel prices. The precedent exists. The tools exist. The power exists. The will does not.

🎯 PH Callout

You told us you were different. You told us Madani meant the people come first. You told us having the PM also serve as Finance Minister would mean faster, more decisive economic management. So where is it? Oil is past $100. The Strait of Hormuz is disrupted. Your own deputy economy minister admits growth projections are shaky. Your own mouth admitted the RON95 subsidy has weeks left. And your crisis response is… a parliamentary motion about Iran and a tweet about solidarity? Mandate WFH. Freeze tolls. Cap food prices. Cut the GLC fat. You have the power. You have the precedent. You have the tools. Use them — or admit that ‘Ekonomi Madani’ was never about protecting the rakyat. It was about protecting the coalition.

PH Backbenchers Call for Snap Election Dressed Up as 'Reform' — Hold GE16 With All State Polls, Save Money, They Say

Said by: Azli Yusof (Amanah-Shah Alam) / Jimmy Puah (PKR-Tebrau)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, two Pakatan Harapan backbenchers — Shah Alam MP Azli Yusof (Amanah) and Tebrau MP Jimmy Puah (PKR) — called for all state assembly elections to be held concurrently with the 16th General Election. Speaking to FMT, both MPs argued that separate elections lead to lower voter turnout, waste public money, and produce state governments with weaker mandates. Azli said separate polls are “unfair to voters, especially those who live far away from where they vote.” Puah called it “simply too tiring for people to come out and vote twice.” Putrajaya spent RM725 million on GE15 in 2022 and a further RM420 million on six subsequent state elections in 2023.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a neutral policy suggestion. It is PH’s latest move in a coordinated signal — following DAP secretary-general Anthony Loke’s identical proposal last year and PH Johor chairman Aminolhuda Hassan’s recent push — to align the political calendar for a snap general election. By calling for simultaneous polls, PH backbenchers are doing the government’s messaging work: normalizing the idea of an early dissolution while packaging it as cost-saving reform. The real audience is not voters who are tired of going to polling stations — it is UMNO, GPS, and other coalition partners who need to be brought on board before the PM pulls the trigger.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH did not care about “voter fatigue” when it suited them. In 2022, six PH-governed states chose to delay their state elections rather than hold them with GE15, citing monsoon concerns. Now, suddenly, the same coalition wants everything held together — but only because the timing benefits them. The opposition is fractured after the Bersatu-PAS split, the economy is holding, and Anwar’s approval is still intact. This is not reform. This is election engineering with a reformist paint job. If PH were serious about alignment, they would legislate fixed election dates — not send backbenchers to float trial balloons in FMT.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of strategic election timing. Mahathir called snap elections when conditions favoured him. Najib dissolved Parliament early in 2013 and 2018. Even within the current cycle, the Sabah, Melaka, and Johor snap elections of 2020–2022 were all called for political advantage, not democratic principle. PH itself benefited from the chaos of misaligned polls — the August 2023 state elections became a de facto referendum on the unity government. The pattern is always the same: whoever controls the timing controls the narrative.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised institutional reform. You promised that governance would no longer be about political convenience. Yet here you are, using backbenchers to soften the ground for a snap election while pretending it is about saving the rakyat money. If you actually believe in synchronized elections, table a constitutional amendment to fix election dates — the way mature democracies do. But you will not, because the whole point is to keep the power to dissolve in the PM’s pocket. Stop dressing up electoral strategy as reform. Malaysians can tell the difference.

Police Question C4 Anti-Corruption CEO Under Section 124B — Anwar's Government Uses 'Plot to Topple' Probe to Interrogate Civil Society

Said by: Bukit Aman (Royal Malaysia Police) / C4 Center (Pushpan Murugiah, CEO)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 10, 2026, the Center to Combat Corruption and Cronyism (C4) CEO Pushpan Murugiah was summoned to Bukit Aman and questioned for approximately 90 minutes under Section 124B of the Penal Code — activities detrimental to parliamentary democracy. The questioning was linked to the police investigation into an alleged plot to topple the government, reportedly because Pushpan’s name appeared in an email deemed relevant to the probe. But the interrogation went far beyond that email. Police questioned C4’s sources of funding, whether the organisation had received instructions to issue statements, and — remarkably — challenged C4’s right to comment on matters of national governance. C4 confirmed that several other political commentators and academics had also been summoned to Bukit Aman under the same investigation.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a routine police inquiry. This is the machinery of state — operating under a government that came to power on a reform mandate — being deployed to scrutinise, pressure, and delegitimise an independent anti-corruption watchdog. Section 124B is a serious charge. Using it to question a civil society leader about their organisation’s funding, autonomy, and standing to comment on public affairs is a textbook chilling effect. It signals to every NGO, academic, and political commentator in Malaysia: criticise the government’s handling of the MACC scandal, and you may find yourself in a police interview room explaining why you think you have the right to speak. This is the same playbook that Malaysians rejected when BN did it. The fact that PH is now running it should alarm every voter who believed the reformasi promise.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim built his entire political career on the claim that he was persecuted by a government that weaponised institutions against critics. He spent years in prison. He rallied millions with the promise that under PH, the police would not be used to silence dissent. Now his government is hauling in the CEO of an anti-corruption NGO and asking — with a straight face — whether C4 has the standing to comment on corruption. The hypocrisy is breathtaking. Sabah Rejected PH at the Ballot Box — Now Mustapha Sakmud Says He'll Be Their Voice in Putrajaya Anyway

Said by: Mustapha Sakmud, Sabah PH Chairman / Sabah and Sarawak Affairs Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Sabah PH chairman and Sabah and Sarawak Affairs Minister Mustapha Sakmud told FMT that Sabah PH will serve as a “conduit” to ensure state issues are voiced at the federal level. He said he intends to leverage his ministerial appointment — made on February 15 — to bring Sabahan concerns to Cabinet meetings and government agencies. “This is the role we aim to play, and we hope the people of Sabah will appreciate it when their fundamental needs are addressed,” he said.

🔥 Why It Matters

PH was annihilated in the November 29, 2025 Sabah state election. PKR scraped through with a single seat. DAP won nothing. GRS took 29 seats, Warisan took 25, and Sabahans made it abundantly clear they did not want PH representing them at the state level. Yet barely three months later, Anwar handed Mustapha a federal ministerial portfolio — Sabah and Sarawak Affairs — effectively using the federal Cabinet to insert PH into Sabah’s political space through the back door. This is not democratic representation. This is patronage as a substitute for a mandate.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

You cannot lose an election this badly and then claim you are the people’s voice. PH won one seat in the entire Sabah state assembly. That is not a conduit — that is a rejection letter. Mustapha’s argument boils down to: “Sabahans didn’t vote for us, but we got a Cabinet post, so we’ll represent them anyway.” This is the exact kind of unearned political entitlement PH once accused BN of practising. The appointment rewards loyalty to Anwar, not service to Sabah. If Sabahans wanted PH to speak for them, they would have voted for PH. They didn’t.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows a long Malaysian tradition of using federal appointments to compensate for electoral losses at the state level. BN did it for decades in Sabah and Sarawak — parachuting federal figures into state politics through GLCs, agencies, and development corridors. PH, in opposition, railed against this exact practice. Now in government, they’ve adopted the same playbook. Anwar’s December 2025 Cabinet reshuffle — which created the Sabah and Sarawak Affairs portfolio — was widely seen as a damage-control exercise after PH’s Sabah wipeout. The pattern is clear: when voters reject you, buy your way back in through Putrajaya.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised that power would come from the people, not from Putrajaya. You told Sabahans for years that BN was using federal patronage to override their democratic choices. And now you’ve handed a Cabinet seat to a man whose coalition won one seat in an entire state election and told him to go represent the people who rejected him. If “the people of Sabah will appreciate it,” as Mustapha claims, then put it to a test — contest a by-election in Sabah on PH’s record. Otherwise, stop pretending a ministerial appointment is a mandate.
