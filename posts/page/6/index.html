Anwar Preaches 'Integrity Culture' and Launches CeIO Programme — While His Government Shields MACC Chief and Interrogates Anti-Corruption NGOs

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

On March 12, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim chaired the Special Cabinet Committee on National Governance (JKKTN) and announced the launch of a Certified Integrity Officer (CeIO) programme. In a Facebook post and subsequent Bernama coverage, Anwar declared that “law enforcement alone is insufficient to combat corruption” and called for “a broader culture of integrity and sound governance practices.” He said issues like “leakage of public funds and abuse of power” undermine governance, public health, the environment, and national integrity. The committee agreed to implement CeIO as part of efforts to “strengthen national governance.”

🔥 Why It Matters

This statement lands in the same week that Anwar’s government delegated the Azam Baki shareholding report to the Chief Secretary instead of acting on it, refused to disclose the report’s findings, rejected calls for a Royal Commission of Inquiry into MACC, and allowed police to interrogate C4 Centre’s anti-corruption CEO under Section 124B — a law meant for threats to parliamentary democracy. The gap between Anwar’s rhetoric and his government’s actions on anti-corruption is no longer a gap. It is a canyon. Every new “integrity” initiative announced while the MACC chief remains in post and civil society gets hauled to Bukit Aman makes the rhetoric sound less like conviction and more like performance.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

You do not need a Certified Integrity Officer programme when the head of your anti-corruption commission is accused of breaching shareholding laws and your own coalition partners are publicly demanding an independent inquiry you refuse to grant. Integrity is not a certificate. It is not a programme. It is what you do when the political cost is high — and Anwar has consistently chosen the lowest-cost option on every accountability question put before him. The CeIO programme is bureaucratic window dressing: another acronym, another committee, another layer of process designed to look like action while the actual crisis — an MACC chief credibly accused of misconduct, still drawing a government salary — remains untouched.

📜 Previous Cases

This is not the first time Anwar has deployed reform language as a substitute for reform action. On March 9, he launched the Capital Market Masterplan and declared reform “non-negotiable” — the same week the PM tenure limit bill failed because his own MPs didn’t show up. On March 6, he told Bersih to stop criticising MACC “during Ramadan,” using the holy month as a shield. On March 3, he told Parliament about a “plot to topple the government” — a narrative now being used to justify police interrogation of anti-corruption activists. The pattern is unmistakable: announce a reform initiative, deflect criticism with moral language, and let the underlying problem persist.

🎯 PH Callout

You ran on Reformasi. You promised Malaysians that your government would be different — that integrity would be a practice, not a slogan. Three years in, your anti-corruption chief is credibly accused of misconduct, your police are questioning anti-corruption NGOs under sedition-era laws, and your response is a Facebook post about “integrity culture” and a new certification programme. Malaysians do not need more certificates. They need a Prime Minister who will suspend an MACC chief under investigation, grant the RCI his own coalition demands, and stop using process as a substitute for accountability. Every day Azam Baki remains in post is a day your integrity talk means nothing.

Economy Minister Celebrates 2025 Numbers and Maintains 4.5% Growth Target — On the Same Day Fuel Prices Explode

Said by: Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir, Economy Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

Economy Minister Akmal Nasrullah Mohd Nasir maintained Malaysia’s 2026 growth forecast at 4.0% to 4.5% on March 12, 2026, while launching the 13th Malaysia Plan implementation monitoring system (MyRMK) in Putrajaya. He celebrated 2025’s GDP growth of 5.2%, unemployment at a decade-low 2.9%, and inflation moderating to 1.4% — declaring that “the government’s continuous efforts in strengthening the economy and improving the well-being of the people are beginning to show positive results.” He acknowledged West Asia conflicts “could disrupt the stability of global energy supply and international trade” but insisted the growth target stands.

🔥 Why It Matters

This statement was made on the same day the Ministry of Finance announced the sharpest weekly fuel price hike in years: RON97 up 60 sen to RM3.85 (18%), unsubsidised RON95 up 60 sen to RM3.27, and diesel up 80 sen to RM3.92 (26%). The Strait of Hormuz is disrupted, oil is past $100 a barrel, and the government’s own special committee is scrambling to assess economic fallout — yet the Economy Minister’s response is a launch event for a monitoring dashboard and a celebration of last year’s numbers. When the house is on fire, you don’t show guests the blueprints.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Maintaining a 4.0-4.5% growth target while fuel prices explode, supply chains fracture, and inflation is about to surge is not confidence — it’s denial dressed up as stability. Bloomberg reported on March 6 that Malaysia was already “holding off on a growth upgrade” due to Middle East uncertainty. Now the crisis has escalated dramatically, and the best the Economy Minister can offer is backward-looking GDP figures and a new monitoring system. The gap between Putrajaya’s optimism and the rakyat’s reality at the fuel pump has never been wider.

📜 Previous Cases

This is a pattern. Deputy Economy Minister Mohd Shahar Abdullah made the exact same 4.0-4.5% claim at the ASEAN Banking and Finance Summit on March 9 — even as the summit’s own chairman warned that geopolitical tensions were “repricing risk across the entire region.” Before the Middle East crisis, Anwar himself was talking about upgrading the growth target. Now the government’s position has gone from “we’ll upgrade” to “we’ll hold” to “we’ll monitor” — each step a retreat disguised as prudence.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised data-driven governance and evidence-based policy. The data says fuel is up 18-26% in a single week, oil is past $100, and your supply buffer runs out in May. Your own special committee is meeting Friday because the situation is critical. Yet the Economy Minister is on stage celebrating 2025 numbers and launching dashboards. The rakyat don’t need a monitoring system — they need a government that tells the truth about what’s coming. Say it plainly: the growth target is at risk, the cost of living is about to get worse, and here is what we will do about it. Anything less is spin.

Fadhlina Says Ministry Is 'Monitoring' Schools for Extremism — After Police Already Arrested Six Teenagers Linked to IS

Said by: Fadhlina Sidek, Education Minister

Category: Education

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

On March 12, Education Minister Fadhlina Sidek told reporters in Bukit Mertajam that the Ministry of Education is “closely monitoring student involvement in educational institutions” to prevent extremist ideologies from infiltrating schools. She said the ministry has “left the matter to the relevant authorities for further investigation and necessary action,” and emphasized close cooperation with enforcement agencies to protect students from influence “especially those closely linked to Daesh and terrorism.” This came weeks after the Special Branch arrested six teenage boys aged 16 to 21 on February 14–15 in a nationwide operation over suspected involvement in IS activities.

🔥 Why It Matters

The arrests of six teenagers for IS links should have been a five-alarm moment for any Education Minister. Instead, Fadhlina’s response — delivered weeks after the fact — was a masterclass in bureaucratic passivity: “monitoring,” “leaving it to authorities,” “working closely.” This is the same minister who spent 2025 battling calls for her resignation over school safety failures — bullying deaths, sexual assaults, and a culture of concealment that her own Director-General admitted existed. The pattern is consistent: a serious threat emerges, Fadhlina talks about monitoring, and the ministry waits for someone else to act. If IS recruiters are targeting Malaysian schoolchildren, the Education Ministry’s role is not to observe from the sidelines — it is to overhaul how schools detect, report, and respond to radicalization. Instead, we get press conferences.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Six teenagers were arrested for IS links, and the Education Minister’s response — a full month later — is that the ministry is “monitoring.” Not launching a nationwide deradicalization programme. Not announcing mandatory teacher training on identifying radicalization signs. Not reviewing the curriculum for counter-extremism content. Not even disclosing how many students have been flagged. Just “monitoring” and “leaving it to authorities.” Fadhlina has turned reactive minimalism into an art form. When students were bullied to death, she “took it seriously.” When schools covered up sexual assault, she pledged “zero tolerance.” Now teenagers are being recruited by IS, and the ministry is “monitoring.” At what point does the government acknowledge that this minister’s operating mode is perpetual observation, never decisive action?

📜 Previous Cases

Fadhlina’s track record of delayed, passive responses is well-documented. In 2025, a wave of school bullying cases — including deaths — triggered public outrage and a memorandum demanding her resignation, which she received outside Parliament in October 2025. Her Director-General admitted cases had been “swept under the carpet.” MCA president Wee Ka Siong publicly called for her removal in December 2025, calling her tenure “ineffective.” Analysts named her the Cabinet member most exposed in any reshuffle. Throughout every crisis, Fadhlina’s response has followed the same script: acknowledge the problem, pledge vigilance, refer to authorities, and wait for the news cycle to move on.

🎯 PH Callout

PH promised a government that acts, not one that monitors. You promised institutional reform, not institutional observation. When IS is recruiting children in Malaysian schools, the Education Minister’s job is not to tell reporters she’s “left it to authorities” — it is to lead. Fadhlina Sidek has had chance after chance after chance. Bullying. Sexual assault. Now terrorism recruitment. Every time, the same response: we are monitoring. Malaysia’s children deserve a ministry that acts before the arrests happen, not one that issues press statements after the police have already done the work. If this is the best PH can offer for education, then the Madani promise is just a slogan painted on a ministry that refuses to move.

Fahmi's MCMC Escalates Digital Crackdown — Grindr and Blued Purged From App Stores as Government Replaces 'LGBT' With 'Deviant Culture'

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister / Marhamah Rosli, Deputy Minister (Religious Affairs)

Category: Social

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil confirmed on February 25, in a written parliamentary reply, that the Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission (MCMC) had blocked access to LGBTQ dating websites Grindr and Blued, and was “studying legal measures” to curb their mobile apps. By early March, both platforms were removed from Google Play and the Apple App Store in Malaysia — an escalation from website blocking to full digital erasure. Deputy Minister for Religious Affairs Marhamah Rosli separately urged Malaysians to stop using the term “LGBT” and replace it with “budaya songsang” (deviant culture) to prevent normalisation, while the government rolled out state-level programs including “spiritual guidance camps,” coordinated Friday sermons via state Islamic councils, and a multi-agency committee to address LGBTQ issues among Muslims. Deputy Prime Minister Ahmad Zahid Hamidi announced a proposed Cyber Crime Bill that would expand the government’s legal powers to restrict such platforms.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a one-off restriction — it is a coordinated, multi-agency campaign of digital censorship and institutional persecution targeting a minority group, carried out under the watch of a government that ran on a reformist platform. The blocking of dating apps is the visible tip; underneath it sits a machinery of surveillance, religious enforcement, and state-mandated language policing designed to erase LGBTQ visibility from Malaysian society. When a PH government wields MCMC, the police, state religious departments, and the Deputy PM’s proposed Cyber Crime Bill to target the same community in lockstep, it is no longer just a “moral” issue — it is a civil liberties crisis being orchestrated by the state. Justice for Sisters, ARTICLE 19, and Human Rights Watch have all flagged this as a systematic pattern of state-sponsored discrimination.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH campaigned as the coalition of reform, human rights, and institutional accountability. Anwar Ibrahim personally cultivated an image of pluralism and tolerance on the international stage. Now his Communications Minister is blocking dating apps on “morality” grounds, his Deputy Religious Affairs Minister is mandating Orwellian language policing — telling the public to call fellow citizens “deviants” — and his Deputy PM is drafting legislation to expand state powers over digital platforms. Fahmi had the gall to say MCMC hadn’t received formal requests to remove the apps from app stores, then the apps disappeared from Google Play and the Apple App Store anyway. The government isn’t just failing to protect minorities; it is actively building the enforcement infrastructure to persecute them. This is not reform. This is the Sedition Act in a new digital wrapper, sold by the same people who once promised to repeal it.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s track record on LGBTQ persecution under PH’s watch is damning and escalating. In May 2023, authorities raided Swatch stores nationwide, seizing over 160 rainbow-coloured watches. In June 2025, Selangor police opened an investigation into a closed-door LGBTQ sexual health workshop — criminalising public health outreach. In June 2025, Kelantan police raided a private property described as a “gay party,” arresting 20 men. On November 28, 2025, Kuala Lumpur police conducted mass raids on men-only venues, detaining 202 people — many of whom, according to Justice for Sisters, were denied legal counsel, subjected to degrading treatment, publicly identified, and held in overcrowded vehicles overnight. This is not a new problem. It is a pattern of escalation that PH has accelerated, not reversed.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would repeal oppressive laws. You told the world you believed in human dignity. Now you’re blocking apps, policing language, raiding venues, running “spiritual guidance camps,” and drafting new legislation to expand digital censorship. Fahmi, you run the ministry that oversees MCMC — the same body that removed TikTok videos critical of your government this very week. Marhamah, you’re a deputy minister in a reformist coalition ordering Malaysians to call their fellow citizens “deviants.” Anwar, you once invoked the Universal Declaration of Human Rights from a prison cell. Every blocked app, every police raid on a private gathering, every state-mandated sermon targeting LGBTQ people is a betrayal of the promises that put you in Putrajaya. This is not governance. This is state-sponsored persecution with reformist branding.

Loke's Fuel Crisis Plan: Let Airlines Charge You More, Warn Flights May Be Cancelled — Happy Hari Raya

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

On March 12, 2026, Transport Minister Anthony Loke chaired a meeting with airline management, airport operators, jet fuel suppliers and aviation authorities to discuss the impact of the West Asia conflict on soaring fuel costs. Loke announced that the Transport Ministry had reached an “agreement in principle” to allow airlines to charge customers additional fees — fuel surcharges — to cover rising costs. He also warned that “if fuel prices continue to rise, airlines may need to suspend certain flight operations.” The ministry committed to weekly meetings with aviation stakeholders while the conflict persists. Short-term measures were discussed to keep air cargo services running for food supplies ahead of Aidilfitri.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the government’s aviation crisis playbook in a single meeting: no intervention, no subsidy, no cost absorption — just permission for airlines to charge the rakyat more. Malaysia is heading into the peak Hari Raya travel season with oil past $100 a barrel and the Strait of Hormuz under military threat. Millions of Malaysians rely on domestic flights to balik kampung, especially those from Sabah and Sarawak. Loke’s response is to let airlines impose surcharges and float the possibility of flight cancellations. This is the same government that claims it is “cushioning the rakyat” from the crisis.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent the week telling Malaysians the government would “protect” them from the economic fallout of the Middle East conflict. Cabinet maintained RON95 at RM1.99 and announced RM15 billion in STR handouts. But when it comes to aviation — a lifeline for East Malaysian families and a key cost for Hari Raya travel — the government’s answer is to let airlines pass the bill to passengers. No jet fuel subsidy. No temporary fare cap. No emergency allocation. Just “weekly meetings” and an agreement to let airlines charge more. Loke is essentially telling Malaysians: your Raya flights will cost more, and some may not fly at all. That is not crisis management. That is crisis delegation.

📜 Previous Cases

This pattern is familiar. During the 2022 post-pandemic aviation recovery, airlines hiked fares and the government did nothing to intervene despite promising consumer protections. Loke himself championed the Malaysian Aviation Consumer Protection Code amendments in 2024, requiring refunds for delays — but structural protections against price gouging during crises remain absent. Meanwhile, countries like Thailand and Indonesia have moved faster on fuel interventions and aviation support packages. Malaysia’s playbook remains the same: form a committee, hold a meeting, issue a press statement, and let the market do what it wants.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised a government that puts rakyat first. You spent the week announcing cash handouts and claiming fiscal discipline. But when millions of Malaysians need to fly home for Hari Raya and airlines are about to hit them with surcharges — or cancel flights entirely — your Transport Minister’s solution is to give airlines permission to charge more. Where is the emergency aviation fund? Where is the temporary jet fuel subsidy? Where is the fare cap for domestic routes during festive periods? You had time to ban Raya open houses for “fiscal discipline” but no time to protect the rakyat from being gouged at the airport. Reformasi, apparently, does not extend to airline pricing.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would lead by example. Ramanan said it himself: it would be “unfair” to ask the people to save while the government doesn’t. So act. Issue the directive today. Thailand didn’t need a Friday Cabinet meeting to decide that government workers should stop burning expensive fuel to sit in offices with perfectly functional internet connections. Every day you “study” is a day you’re choosing process over people. The rakyat cannot afford your deliberation speed.

RON97 Up 18%, Diesel Up 26% — Government Lets Unsubsidised Fuel Prices Explode While Claiming It's 'Cushioning the Rakyat'

Said by: Ministry of Finance / Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

The Ministry of Finance announced on March 11 that effective March 12-18, RON97 petrol will surge 60 sen per litre to RM3.85 — an 18% increase from RM3.25. Unsubsidised RON95 jumps 60 sen to RM3.27 — a 22% spike. Diesel in Peninsular Malaysia leaps 80 sen to RM3.92 — a staggering 26% increase. The government maintained the Budi95 subsidised rate at RM1.99 for eligible motorists and offered a RM100 top-up for diesel vehicle owners under existing schemes, raising interim cash assistance from RM200 to RM300 for March. The MOF said Malaysia’s prices “remain lower than neighbouring countries” and promised “strengthened enforcement” against fuel leakage.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the sharpest single-week fuel price adjustment since the Middle East crisis escalated. Diesel at RM3.92 directly hits logistics, haulage, and food supply chains — costs that will be passed to consumers within days. The two-tier fuel system is now stretched to breaking point: subsidised RON95 sits at RM1.99 while unsubsidised RON95 is at RM3.27, a 64% gap that incentivises smuggling and gaming. The government’s entire fuel strategy is now a subsidy wall protecting one product while everything else burns. Businesses that run on diesel — fishermen, farmers, truckers, food manufacturers — are absorbing a 26% cost spike with no price controls downstream. This will feed directly into food inflation during Ramadan, when household spending is already elevated.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The government’s framing is pure theatre. “The government will continue to bear part of the subsidy to cushion the impact” — they are subsidising one fuel grade and letting everything else rip. The RM100 diesel top-up is an insult: if you’re a lorry operator burning hundreds of litres a week, RM300 a month covers roughly one fill-up at the new price. Meanwhile, the MOF’s line about prices being “lower than neighbouring countries” is the oldest deflection in the subsidy playbook — it tells you nothing about whether Malaysian wages can absorb these costs. Anwar promised fuel subsidy rationalisation that would be “fair and gradual.” A 26% diesel spike in one week is neither fair nor gradual. It’s crisis management dressed up as policy.

📜 Previous Cases

This government has been kicking the fuel subsidy can since taking office. The targeted subsidy for RON95 (Budi95) was meant to replace blanket subsidies, but diesel rationalisation has been stop-start since June 2024 when diesel subsidies were first restructured. Every time global prices spike, the government scrambles with ad hoc cash handouts instead of structural reform. Just last week, Anwar admitted he could only hold the RON95 subsidy for “one or two months” — and now the prices around it are exploding. The pattern is clear: announce reform, delay implementation, throw cash when crisis hits, repeat.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians you would rationalise subsidies fairly. You told them the economy was strong and well-managed. You told them you had a plan. An 18% spike in RON97 and a 26% spike in diesel in a single week is not a plan — it’s a surrender to market forces with a press release attached. The rakyat who drive RON97 or run diesel-dependent businesses are not “cushioned” — they are exposed. And the fishermen, farmers, and truckers who feed this country are now subsidising your political survival with their margins. If the Budi95 wall cracks — and at these global prices, it will — you have no backup plan. Just more press statements about how prices are lower than Singapore.

Instead, they built a licensing apparatus that lets a conflicted appointee pressure platforms into silencing critical coverage of the government’s own anti-corruption failures.

📜 Previous Cases

Now PH is using the same tools, with additional legal teeth via the licensing regime they introduced. The pattern is bipartisan: every Malaysian government, regardless of coalition, reaches for the censorship lever when the pressure builds. PH simply formalized it.

🎯 PH Callout

You campaigned on repealing the Sedition Act, reforming MCMC, and defending press freedom. You said Malaysians deserved a government that could withstand scrutiny. Instead, you appointed a conflicted corporate figure to run MCMC and the MACC advisory board simultaneously, introduced a licensing regime that turns social media platforms into compliance arms of the state, and used it to suppress coverage of your own anti-corruption scandal. The fact that you are censoring opposition press conferences about your own failures tells the rakyat everything they need to know about what ‘Madani’ means in practice.

[BM] Ahli Parlimen Belakang PH Desak Pilihan Raya Kilat Berselindung Di Sebalik 'Reformasi' — Gabung PRU16 Dengan Semua PRN, Jimat Duit Kata Mereka

Said by: Azli Yusof (Amanah-Shah Alam) / Jimmy Puah (PKR-Tebrau)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 11 Mac, dua ahli parlimen belakang Pakatan Harapan — Ahli Parlimen Shah Alam Azli Yusof (Amanah) dan Ahli Parlimen Tebrau Jimmy Puah (PKR) — menyeru agar semua pilihan raya dewan undangan negeri diadakan serentak dengan Pilihan Raya Umum ke-16. Azli berkata pilihan raya berasingan ’tidak adil kepada pengundi, terutamanya mereka yang tinggal jauh dari tempat mengundi.’ Putrajaya membelanjakan RM725 juta untuk PRU15 pada 2022 dan RM420 juta lagi untuk enam pilihan raya negeri seterusnya pada 2023.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Ini bukan cadangan dasar neutral. Ia langkah terbaharu PH dalam isyarat terselaras — berikutan cadangan serupa oleh Setiausaha Agung DAP Anthony Loke tahun lepas dan desakan terbaharu pengerusi PH Johor Aminolhuda Hassan — untuk menyelaraskan kalendar politik bagi pilihan raya umum awal. 💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

PH tidak mengambil peduli tentang ‘keletihan pengundi’ apabila ia sesuai bagi mereka. Pada 2022, enam negeri yang ditadbir PH memilih untuk menangguhkan pilihan raya negeri dan bukannya mengadakannya bersama PRU15, dengan alasan kebimbangan monsun. Kini, tiba-tiba, gabungan yang sama mahu semuanya diadakan bersama — tetapi hanya kerana masanya menguntungkan mereka. Pembangkang berpecah selepas perpecahan Bersatu-PAS, ekonomi bertahan, dan kelulusan Anwar masih utuh. Ini bukan reformasi. Ini kejuruteraan pilihan raya dengan cat reformis.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Malaysia mempunyai sejarah panjang pemasaan pilihan raya strategik. Mahathir memanggil pilihan raya kilat apabila keadaan memihak kepadanya. Najib membubarkan Parlimen awal pada 2013 dan 2018. Coraknya sentiasa sama: sesiapa yang mengawal masa mengawal naratif.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu menjanjikan reformasi institusi. Kamu menjanjikan tadbir urus tidak lagi mengenai kemudahan politik. Namun kamu menggunakan ahli parlimen belakang untuk melembutkan tapak bagi pilihan raya kilat sambil berlakon ia mengenai penjimatan wang rakyat. Jika kamu benar-benar percaya pada pilihan raya serentak, bentangkan pindaan perlembagaan untuk menetapkan tarikh pilihan raya — seperti yang dilakukan demokrasi matang. Tetapi kamu tidak akan, kerana seluruh tujuannya ialah mengekalkan kuasa membubarkan di dalam poket PM. Berhenti menyamarkan strategi pilihan raya sebagai reformasi.

[BM] Anwar Haramkan Rumah Terbuka Raya Atas Nama 'Disiplin Fiskal' — Selepas Baru Umumkan RM15 Bilion Bantuan Tunai Dan Bonus RM500

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 11 Mac 2026, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim mengumumkan bahawa semua agensi kerajaan, GLC, dan GLIC dilarang mengadakan majlis rumah terbuka Aidilfitri tahun ini — keputusan yang dipersetujui dalam Kabinet pagi itu. Beliau membingkainya sebagai isyarat disiplin fiskal di tengah ketidaktentuan global akibat konflik Timur Tengah. Pada masa yang sama, Anwar juga mengumumkan sekatan lebih ketat terhadap perjalanan luar negara bagi menteri dan pegawai kerajaan.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Persembahan berjimat cermat ini tiba hanya dua hari selepas kerajaan mengumumkan akan mengekalkan subsidi RON95 pada RM1.99 — dan kurang seminggu selepas Anwar mempercepatkan RM15 bilion bantuan tunai Sumbangan Tunai Rahmah (STR) serta Bantuan Kewangan Khas RM500 bagi kakitangan awam dan kakitangan masjid, semuanya tepat pada masanya sebelum Hari Raya. Kerajaan membelanjakan jumlah rekod untuk pemindahan tunai langsung — bentuk perbelanjaan paling berkuasa secara pilihan raya — sambil membatalkan sambutan rumah terbuka komuniti yang kosnya hanya sebahagian kecil. Ini bukan disiplin fiskal. Ini berjimat cermat terpilih.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Kamu tidak boleh mengumumkan program pemindahan tunai terbesar dalam sejarah Malaysia satu minggu, kemudian membatalkan rumah terbuka Raya minggu berikutnya dan memanggilnya berhemah. Rumah terbuka ialah acara komuniti — kosnya berjuta-juta. Program STR berharga berbilion. Jika krisis Timur Tengah begitu teruk sehingga kakitangan awam tidak boleh makan ketupat di majlis kerajaan, mengapa ia tidak cukup teruk untuk menghentikan RM15 bilion tunai sebelum pilihan raya? Kerana rumah terbuka tidak membeli undi. Tunai dalam akaun bank sebelum hari mengundi boleh.

📜 Kes-Kes Terdahulu

Ini ialah teater krisis Malaysia yang biasa. Mahathir melakukannya semasa krisis kewangan Asia 1997 — berjimat untuk rakyat, bailout untuk kroni. Najib bercakap disiplin fiskal sementara 1MDB menghembus berbilion. Anwar sendiri mengejek pemberian musim perayaan BN semasa beliau dalam pembangkang, memanggilnya pembelian undi. Kini beliau melakukan perkara yang sama, tetapi lebih besar.

🎯 Seruan Terhadap PH

Kamu menjanjikan reformasi, bukan perbelanjaan pilihan raya era BN yang dibungkus semula dengan lapisan kesalehan. Jika kamu benar-benar bimbang tentang kedudukan fiskal, mulakan dengan RM15 bilion pemindahan tunai yang baru kamu umumkan, bukan nasi lemak di rumah terbuka kerajaan. Batalkan pelantikan politik yang menguras FSB. Bekukan perbelanjaan naungan. Jangan berkuliah kepada rakyat tentang kesederhanaan sementara buku cek kerajaan terbuka luas bagi setiap kumpulan pengundi yang penting sebelum PRU16.
