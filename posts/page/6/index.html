Anwar Is Running Mahathir's Playbook 2.0 — Different Man, Same Authoritarian Instincts

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Multiple independent analyses from ISEAS, CSIS, Toda Peace Institute, and East Asia Forum converge on the same conclusion: Anwar Ibrahim’s governance style increasingly mirrors that of Mahathir Mohamad — the man who imprisoned him. Centralisation of power (PM+FM), use of repressive laws, crackdown on media, selective prosecution, race-based politics, foreign policy grandstanding as domestic distraction, and coalition management through patronage.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Anwar spent his political life as the ANTI-Mahathir. His entire narrative was: I suffered under Mahathir’s authoritarian system, I understand its evils, and I will build something different. The convergence between Anwar’s governing style and Mahathir’s is not just ironic — it’s the final proof that Malaysian political culture, rather than individual leaders, produces authoritarian governance. Changing the face at the top doesn’t change the system.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Repressive laws? Mahathir had ISA, Anwar has SOSMA and the Sedition Act. Media control? Mahathir had PPPA, Anwar has MCMC blocks and journalist codes of ethics. Patronage? Mahathir had cronies, Anwar has 47.5 GLC appointees/year. Race politics? Mahathir had NEP, Anwar has ’non-negotiable’ Bumiputera policies. Concentration of power? Mahathir held PM+FM, Anwar holds PM+FM. Foreign policy preening? Mahathir had ‘Asian values,’ Anwar has Palestine speeches. The checklist is identical.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysian democracy has a pattern: opposition leaders promise reform, win power, then govern like their predecessors. Mahathir in 1981 promised reform, then built the system Anwar fought against. Anwar in 2022 promised to dismantle that system, then adopted it wholesale. The CSIS analysis titled it perfectly: ‘The Death of Reformasi.’ Not because the movement failed from outside — but because its leader killed it from within.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You are Mahathir 2.0 with better English and a more sympathetic backstory. The policies are the same. The power structures are the same. The patronage is the same. The authoritarianism is the same. The only difference is you KNOW it’s wrong because you spent 11 years in prison because of it. And you chose to do it anyway. That’s not just hypocrisy — it’s a betrayal of everyone who believed you were different.

The Scoreboard: Things That Were BETTER Under BN Than PH — An Uncomfortable Truth

Said by: Comparative Economic Data / Various Sources

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: By multiple measurable metrics, ordinary Malaysians were objectively better off under certain BN policies than under PH: (1) GST was more efficient and less regressive than SST; (2) Diesel was RM2.15 vs RM3.35; (3) Festive toll-free periods existed; (4) PISA scores were 31 points higher in math; (5) Food prices were lower; (6) RON95 subsidy was universal without the anxiety of ’targeted’ reform; (7) Electricity for domestic use was GST-exempt.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The fundamental promise of changing government is that things will get BETTER, not worse. When multiple cost-of-living and quality-of-life indicators are worse under PH than under the government they replaced, the entire rationale for PH’s existence comes into question. Voters didn’t march for Reformasi so they could pay more for diesel.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH will argue context — global inflation, inherited debt, COVID aftermath. These are real factors. But they don’t explain why Malaysia’s food prices are the HIGHEST in Southeast Asia (surpassing richer Singapore), why PISA decline was DOUBLE the OECD average, or why diesel had to rise 56% in one shot while JAKIM kept RM2.6 billion. Context explains some decline; it doesn’t excuse all of it. Choices were made, and those choices made life worse.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: BN was voted out for corruption, abuse of power, and 1MDB — all legitimate reasons. But the implicit promise was that PH would maintain or improve the baseline while cleaning up governance. Instead, governance remains corrupt (Shamsul Iskandar, MACC scandal, GLC patronage) AND daily life costs more. You got the worst of both worlds: BN-level corruption with higher costs.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Here’s your report card. Diesel: WORSE. Food prices: WORSE. Education outcomes: WORSE. Press freedom: SAME or WORSE. Corruption: SAME. Cost of living: WORSE. Toll abolition: BROKEN. EPF protection: WEAKER. Healthcare: WORSE. The one metric where you’re better? PR and speechwriting. Congratulations — your press conferences are excellent. Too bad Malaysians can’t eat press conferences.

Azam Baki's RM100M Defamation Suit Against Bloomberg — MACC Chief Fights Media Instead of Corruption

Said by: Bloomberg / Azam Baki, MACC Chief Commissioner

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-27

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 27 February 2026, it was reported that MACC Chief Commissioner Azam Baki filed a RM100 million defamation suit against Bloomberg. The suit came after Bloomberg published an investigation alleging that Azam owned millions of shares in publicly listed companies — potentially breaching civil servant ownership limits. Bloomberg further alleged that MACC officials had colluded with businessmen to orchestrate hostile takeovers of listed firms, using raids and intimidation to force executives into selling shares to groups dubbed the “corporate mafia.” Rather than resigning or submitting to an independent investigation, Azam chose to sue the messenger. Anwar’s response was to announce a task force — not an independent inquiry, not a Royal Commission. Even DAP, his own coalition partner, said a task force “does not go far enough.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The head of Malaysia’s anti-corruption agency is suing the world’s most prominent financial news outlet for reporting on his alleged financial misconduct. This is not a case of a private citizen defending their reputation — this is the chief enforcer of anti-corruption law using the legal system to intimidate a media organisation that investigated him. If Azam’s role is to fight corruption, and a credible international outlet alleges he is compromised, the correct institutional response is a transparent, independent investigation — not a defamation suit designed to chill future reporting. The RM100M figure is not about compensation; it is about deterrence. Bloomberg has stood by its reporting. The anti-corruption agency fighting corruption journalism instead of actual corruption is peak Madani irony.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s response — a task force — is the bureaucratic equivalent of thoughts and prayers. A task force reports to the government. An independent inquiry reports to the public. Anwar chose the one that protects him. DAP publicly said a task force “does not go far enough,” meaning even Anwar’s own coalition partners don’t trust his anti-corruption mechanisms. CNA reported that Anwar faces a “graft crisis” with “PKR party fractures” and “PH coalition jitters.” The man who built Reformasi on the promise of clean governance now leads a government where the anti-corruption chief sues journalists, the PM’s aide gets charged with bribery, and the DPM’s 47 charges get dropped. At some point, the pattern is the policy.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Azam Baki’s share ownership scandal first surfaced in early 2022, when Malaysiakini reported he held shares in two public-listed companies exceeding the civil service ownership cap. A special parliamentary committee cleared him — prompting widespread outrage. Bloomberg’s 2025-2026 investigation went further, alleging systemic collusion between MACC officials and corporate raiders. The MACC-media adversarial pattern is not new either: the commission has previously threatened legal action against outlets that reported on its internal affairs. Under Azam’s leadership, MACC has become less an anti-corruption body and more a political instrument — selectively prosecuting opposition figures while shielding coalition allies.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You promised MACC independence. Instead, the MACC chief is suing Bloomberg for doing MACC’s job. An independent inquiry — not a task force — is the minimum acceptable response. If Azam is innocent, an inquiry clears him. If he is not, the country’s anti-corruption infrastructure needs to be rebuilt from scratch. Your task force is a stalling mechanism, not an accountability mechanism. Even your own coalition partner sees through it. Do better, or stop pretending Reformasi means anything.

Every Cabinet Member Meeting KPIs, Says Anwar — Critics Ask: What KPIs?

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-25

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 25 February 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim told Parliament that every cabinet member is meeting their KPIs and performing with integrity. He did not disclose what those KPIs were, how they were measured, or who evaluated them. Anwar was responding to an opposition MP who asked if ministers would be forced to resign for poor performance. His reply: “Accountability is not only about punishment but also correction, continuous improvement, and the moral responsibility ministers held for the people.” No KPIs have ever been publicly disclosed for any minister in Anwar’s cabinet. No minister has been penalised, demoted, or publicly reprimanded for underperformance. Two ministers were removed in the December 2025 reshuffle — including Rafizi Ramli — but Anwar framed it as “portfolio realignment,” not performance accountability.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Claiming 100% KPI compliance without disclosing the KPIs is the corporate equivalent of giving yourself a perfect performance review and refusing to show the form. It is not accountability — it is self-certification. If every minister is meeting their KPIs, then either the KPIs are so low that failure is impossible, or the assessment is dishonest. Given that 77% of Malaysians report struggling with cost of living, university rankings are dropping, the CPI is stuck at 1MDB-era levels, and reform promises remain unmet, the claim is laughable on its face. Transparency means publishing the KPIs, the methodology, and the results — and letting the public judge. “Trust me, everyone is great” is not reform governance.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: A PM who holds both the PM and Finance Minister portfolios is essentially grading himself on fiscal management. There is no independent check on the most powerful office in the country. The Najib government also claimed ministers were performing. The Mahathir government also claimed results were being delivered. Without published metrics, every government is equally excellent — which means the exercise is meaningless. Singapore publishes ministerial performance metrics and links them to public service delivery targets. South Korea conducts annual government performance evaluations with published results. Malaysia’s approach under the “reformist” PM: trust his word. The Merdeka Center’s February 2026 poll showed public satisfaction with economic management at 38% — suggesting that whatever KPIs exist, the public disagrees they are being met.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian PM has claimed their cabinet is performing well. Najib claimed his ministers delivered results — while 1MDB bled billions. Muhyiddin claimed pandemic management was effective — while thousands died and billions in stimulus were unaccounted for. The pattern is universal: self-assessment without external verification always produces perfect scores. Anwar promised to be different. He isn’t. The KPI charade is a bipartisan tradition that PH has enthusiastically adopted.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Publish the KPIs. Go through every minister, every metric, every target, and every result. Put it online. Let the rakyat see whether their Education Minister’s KPIs include declining PISA scores, whether the Health Minister’s KPIs include 33,000 contract doctors still waiting for permanent posts, whether the Economy Minister’s KPIs include 77% of Malaysians struggling with costs. You won’t — because the scorecard would be devastating. A government that grades itself in secret has no credibility claiming accountability.

Rawang Hindu Temple Demolished at Midnight — No Court Order, Police Assisted, PH State Government Silent

Said by: Selangor PH State Government / Police

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-24

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 24 February 2026, the Sri Uchimalai Muniswaran temple in Rawang, Selangor was demolished in the middle of the night — hours after deities were allegedly relocated following pressure on the temple committee. The demolition was carried out with police assistance and without any court order.

Lawyers Rajesh Nagarajan and Sachpreetraj Singh issued a statement: “We strongly deny the claims by Yayasan Kubra representative Ariff Omar that the temple committee voluntarily removed the deities. That is false and ridiculous since the temple chairman lodged a police report only yesterday complaining that the temple was being pressured to vacate the land without any court order.”

Four men, including activist Tamim Dahri, were arrested but the Shah Alam High Court quashed the remand order and ordered their immediate release. Police classified it as a land dispute, not a racial or religious conflict.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

A Hindu temple was demolished at midnight, with police present, without a court order, in a state governed by PH. This is not something that happens in a country with rule of law. It’s something that happens when institutions have failed and political will is absent.

The timing — amid a broader crackdown on unauthorised Hindu temples in Selangor following the Sultan’s statements — is not coincidental. Malaysia Hindu Sangam identified 687 unauthorised Hindu temples in Selangor alone. The question isn’t whether they’re authorised — it’s whether the government’s response is to demolish community heritage or find solutions.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

PH promised to be different. To protect minorities. To uphold rule of law. Instead, under a PH state government:

  • A temple was demolished without a court order
  • It happened at midnight — the time you choose when you don’t want witnesses
  • Police assisted rather than prevented it
  • The arrested men were released by the High Court, suggesting the lower court’s remand was improper If this happened under BN, DAP would have called it ethnic cleansing. Under PH, it’s a ’land dispute.'

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Temple demolitions have a long and painful history in Malaysia. The Seafield Sri Maha Mariamman temple riot of 2018 occurred during PH 1.0’s watch. The pattern repeats: minority religious sites are treated as disposable when they stand on land someone else wants. The only thing that changes is which coalition looks away.

Controversial Muslim preacher Zamri Vinoth has been targeting Hindu temples with inflammatory rhetoric, seemingly with impunity. Malaysiakini columnist Andrew Sia asked: “Why do agitators like Zamri Vinoth seem to have immunity while insulting Hindu temples?”

🎯 PH CALLOUT

Anwar Ibrahim himself acknowledged the sensitivity: “I know this will invite controversy. Some people have gone to extremes, even launching campaigns to demolish temples.” He warned against vigilantism. Then a temple was demolished at midnight in his coalition’s state. Your warning means nothing if your own state government cannot prevent midnight demolitions without court orders. The Indian community voted for you because they believed you would protect them. This is what that protection looks like?

The pattern is consistent: Rafizi raises legitimate concerns, the leadership ignores or punishes him, and the concerns later prove justified.
