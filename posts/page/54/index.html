Digital Divide Persists — Rural Communities Still Without Reliable Internet While Government Talks 5G

Said by: MCMC / Rural Development Ministry

Category: Education

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As of early 2025, millions of Malaysians in rural Sabah, Sarawak, and the east coast of Peninsular Malaysia still lack reliable internet access. While the government spent billions on the 5G DNB versus Telekom Malaysia dual-network fiasco, the JENDELA programme to improve basic digital infrastructure faced delays and coverage gaps. Students in rural schools were severely disadvantaged during COVID-19 distance learning, and the digital divide has not meaningfully narrowed since. MCMC’s own data shows connectivity gaps between urban and rural areas remain significant, with many rural schools relying on unstable satellite connections or no connectivity at all.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The digital divide compounds every other form of marginalisation. Rural students can’t access online learning resources. Small businesses can’t participate in e-commerce. Healthcare providers can’t use telemedicine. Government services that have moved online are inaccessible to communities without internet. Kids in longhouses and kampungs can’t load a basic web page while KL gets 5G. The irony of spending billions on cutting-edge infrastructure while basic connectivity remains unavailable to millions is not lost on anyone except apparently the government.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government chose to spend billions on a dual 5G network — solving a political problem between telecom companies — while JENDELA, the basic connectivity programme, faces delays. For Orang Asli and indigenous communities in particular, digital exclusion compounds existing educational and economic marginalisation. A government that prioritises 5G headlines over universal basic connectivity has its priorities backwards. A truly “Madani” government would ensure every Malaysian can access basic internet before giving KL residents faster streaming.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Digital infrastructure promises have been made under every Malaysian government. Under BN, the 1Malaysia Internet Centre programme was launched — many centres were built but poorly maintained or unstaffed. Under PH 1.0, digital infrastructure was briefly prioritised before the Sheraton Move. Under PH 2.0, JENDELA has continued but with persistent delays. The pattern is consistent: announce connectivity programmes, build some infrastructure, under-maintain it, and move on to the next headline technology while basic access gaps persist.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You spent billions on 5G so KL can stream faster. Meanwhile, a kid in a Sarawak longhouse can’t submit homework online. That’s not a digital divide — it’s digital apartheid. Prioritise JENDELA completion. Ensure every school in Malaysia has reliable broadband. Fix basic connectivity before chasing 5G headlines. Or keep building luxury infrastructure for urban areas while rural Malaysians — the communities that vote for you in every election — remain disconnected from the digital economy you keep promoting.

Gambling Addiction Crisis — 4D, Toto, and Online Betting Destroy Families While Government Collects Revenue

Said by: Health Ministry / NGOs

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia faces a growing gambling addiction crisis driven by legal 4D and Toto outlets, sports betting, and an explosion of illegal online gambling platforms. The government profits from gambling through licensing fees and gaming taxes — collecting billions annually from licensed operators. Meanwhile, gambling addiction destroys lower-income families, with treatment services remaining minimal compared to the scale of the problem. Online gambling has exploded beyond MCMC’s enforcement capacity, with estimates suggesting billions of ringgit flow through illegal platforms annually.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Gambling addiction disproportionately affects B40 families who can least afford losses. The social costs — family breakdown, debt, crime, mental health deterioration — are borne by communities while the government collects revenue. Licensed gambling operators contribute to political campaigns and enjoy regulatory protection. The government has a financial incentive to maintain gambling revenue and a social responsibility to address addiction. It consistently chooses revenue over responsibility.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government simultaneously profits from gambling and claims to combat it. Licensing fees and gaming taxes generate billions for the Treasury. Online gambling enforcement is performative — MCMC blocks a few sites while thousands more operate freely through VPNs and messaging apps. Treatment services for gambling addiction are chronically underfunded. The conflict of interest is structural: the government is both regulator and beneficiary of the industry it’s supposed to control. That’s not governance — it’s a revenue addiction masquerading as regulation.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Gambling regulation in Malaysia has always been politically convenient. Under BN, licensed operators flourished with political connections. Under PH 1.0, no changes were made. Under PH 2.0, the same operators continue with the same political connections, and online gambling has grown unchecked. Each government collects the revenue, underfunds treatment, and claims to be fighting the problem. The Forest City SFZ’s potential for gaming activities has raised further concerns about the government’s relationship with gambling revenue.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You collect billions from gambling while addiction destroys families. Your enforcement against online gambling is performative. Your treatment services are underfunded. The conflict of interest is obvious: you profit from the industry you’re supposed to regulate. Either significantly increase gambling addiction treatment funding — proportional to the revenue you collect — or admit that gambling regulation is a revenue operation, not a public health programme.

Gender Pay Gap Persists at 6-9% — Women Do More Work for Less Pay, Government Does Nothing

Said by: DOSM / Talentcorp Data

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 What Happened

Despite Anwar’s government having prominent women in cabinet and occasional rhetoric about gender equality, Malaysia’s gender pay gap remains stubbornly persistent at an estimated 6-9% across sectors, with significantly larger gaps in certain industries. Women bear a disproportionate burden of unpaid care work, face career penalties for motherhood, and are underrepresented in senior management and board positions.

🔥 Why It Matters

The female labour force participation rate, while improving, still lags regional peers. Maternity protection remains inadequate for gig economy workers. Sexual harassment laws, while strengthened, face enforcement challenges.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The government has not introduced pay transparency legislation, gender-disaggregated budgeting, or mandatory gender pay gap reporting for companies — measures that have proven effective in other countries. For a PM who claims to champion a civilisational approach to governance, the failure to meaningfully address gender economic inequality represents a significant blind spot.

🎯 PH Callout

Half of Malaysia’s population deserves more than tokenistic representation and empty rhetoric. Put women in cabinet for the photos, then ignore the structural inequality that holds women back economically. Pay transparency costs nothing to legislate. Gender budgeting is best practice globally. If you won’t do the easy things, you certainly won’t do the hard ones.

Higher Education Budget Keeps Shrinking — From 6.8% in 2010 to Just 4.3% in 2025

Said by: New Mandala / Academic Analysis

Category: Education

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s higher education budget has dropped from 6.8% of the total national budget in 2010 to just 4.3% in 2025, with the lowest point at 3.9% in 2023. Meanwhile, tertiary enrollment stands at only 42.57% — far below Singapore’s 93% and the global average of 51.98%. Academic analysis describes a “widening disjuncture between reformist discourse and actual financial commitment” to education under PH. While the government talks about the knowledge economy and human capital development, the budget tells a different story.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A country that reduces education spending while talking about becoming a knowledge economy is lying to itself. The 4.3% allocation means fewer university places, less research funding, lower academic salaries, and deteriorating campus infrastructure. The tertiary enrollment gap — Malaysia at 42.57% vs Singapore at 93% — represents millions of young Malaysians who could be in higher education but aren’t, because the system doesn’t have the capacity to accommodate them. Every percentage point of budget decline translates into fewer graduates, less innovation, and slower economic growth.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The same government that cut higher education to 4.3% allocated RM2.6 billion to JAKIM — the highest allocation in JAKIM’s history. The civil service budget grows every year while education shrinks. When your budget priorities tell students they matter less than bureaucrats and religious administration, students respond by leaving the country. The brain drain — 1.86 million Malaysians abroad — is directly connected to an education system that receives declining investment. You can’t build human capital while cutting the budget for building it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, education received a larger budget share but was plagued by inefficiency and political management. Under PH 1.0, education reform was briefly prioritised. Under PH 2.0, the budget has consistently declined. New Mandala’s analysis noted that PH’s education rhetoric is more progressive than any previous government but its funding commitment is among the worst. The gap between words and money is the gap between aspiration and reality.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You talk about human capital. You talk about the knowledge economy. You talk about innovation and competitiveness. Then you cut higher education to 4.3% of the budget — the lowest in modern Malaysian history. Singapore spends three times as much proportionally and has 93% tertiary enrollment. You have 42.57%. The numbers don’t lie. Reverse the education budget decline. Commit to reaching 6% within three years. Or stop talking about the knowledge economy while defunding the institutions that are supposed to build it.

Jho Low STILL Free After 8 Years — Malaysia's Most Wanted Fugitive Living Comfortably Somewhere

Said by: Interpol / MACC / DOJ

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As of 2025, Low Taek Jho (Jho Low) — the central figure in the 1MDB scandal — remains free despite Interpol red notices, international arrest warrants, and charges in multiple jurisdictions. Three different Malaysian governments have failed to secure his extradition. Anwar made 1MDB accountability his signature promise, yet has made no visible progress in locating or extraditing Jho Low. Reports suggest he has been residing in China or Macau, but the Malaysian government has not disclosed any diplomatic efforts to secure his return.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Jho Low orchestrated the theft of billions from a Malaysian sovereign wealth fund. He is the most wanted fugitive in Malaysian history. His freedom — eight years after charges were filed — represents the single largest accountability failure in Malaysian governance. Every day Jho Low remains free is a day the rakyat services debt he helped create while he lives comfortably beyond the reach of Malaysian law. If Malaysia cannot bring its most-wanted kleptocrat to justice, its entire anti-corruption framework is performative.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar spent years demanding 1MDB accountability. “Where is Jho Low?” was a PH rallying cry. Three years into government, the answer remains the same: somewhere comfortable, apparently beyond Anwar’s reach. The US DOJ recovered over $1 billion in 1MDB-linked assets. Singapore jailed bankers. Switzerland sanctioned institutions. Malaysia — the country that was robbed — has not managed to bring the principal orchestrator to trial. If the political will existed, diplomatic pressure on China (where Jho Low is believed to reside) would be a priority. It clearly isn’t.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under Najib, the 1MDB investigation was actively suppressed — the AG was fired, MACC officers were transferred, and Jho Low was protected. Under Muhyiddin, investigations continued but Jho Low remained free. Under Anwar, investigations concluded with settlements and Najib’s reduced sentence, but Jho Low’s extradition has not progressed. The pattern suggests that every Malaysian government has found it politically convenient to talk about catching Jho Low while doing nothing that would actually bring him back.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: “Where is Jho Low?” — you asked that question for five years. Now you’re the PM. You control the diplomatic apparatus. You have Interpol red notices. You have international cooperation frameworks. And Jho Low is still free. Disclose what diplomatic steps you’ve taken. Publish the extradition requests. Pressure China publicly if necessary. Or admit that catching Jho Low was always a slogan, never a priority — and that the biggest kleptocrat in Malaysian history will die free because no government cared enough to bring him home.

Malaysia Refuses to Ratify ICERD — Anwar Caves to Racial Politics on International Human Rights Treaty

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration / Malay-Muslim Groups

Category: Social

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia remains one of the few countries in the world that has not ratified the International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination (ICERD). In 2018, PH’s attempt to ratify ICERD was abandoned after mass protests led by Malay-Muslim groups who argued it would undermine Bumiputera privileges. Anwar, once a progressive leader committed to equality, has not revisited the issue since becoming PM. The “reformist” Prime Minister has accepted the status quo on racial discrimination — ICERD ratification is not on the agenda.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: ICERD ratification would commit Malaysia to eliminating racial discrimination in law and practice. Malaysia’s refusal to ratify — alongside countries like North Korea and Myanmar — places it in embarrassing company. The refusal signals to the international community that Malaysia is unwilling to subject its race-based policies to international scrutiny. For non-Bumiputera Malaysians, the refusal to ratify confirms that their rights are subordinate to racial politics.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar spent decades advocating for a “Malaysian Malaysia” — equality regardless of race. He founded the multi-racial PH coalition on the principle that all Malaysians deserve equal treatment. ICERD ratification would be the natural expression of that principle. Instead, Anwar has accepted the 2018 defeat and moved on. The man who was imprisoned partly for challenging racial politics has, as PM, decided that racial politics are too powerful to challenge. The reformist has capitulated to the very forces he built his career opposing.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia signed ICERD in 1966 but never ratified it. Under Mahathir 1.0, ratification was never considered. Under Najib, it was ignored. Under PH 1.0, ratification was announced and then abandoned after protests. Under PH 2.0, it hasn’t even been raised. Each government decides that the political cost of challenging racial discrimination is higher than the cost of maintaining it. ICERD joins the Sedition Act, the PPPA, and SOSMA on the list of reforms PH promised and abandoned.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You built a coalition on multiracial equality. Your manifesto promised a “New Malaysia” where race wouldn’t determine rights. Ratifying ICERD would be the simplest, most direct expression of that promise. Instead, you’ve accepted the 2018 defeat and moved on — just like every government before you. Either ICERD is consistent with the Malaysia you promised, or the Malaysia you promised was a lie. Which is it?

Mental Health Crisis Spiralling — Youth Suicide and Depression Surge While Government Underfunds Services

Said by: Health Ministry / WHO Data

Category: Health

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 What Happened

Malaysia is facing a deepening mental health crisis, particularly among young people, with rising rates of depression, anxiety, and suicide. The National Health and Morbidity Survey has consistently shown alarming increases in mental health issues, with nearly 3 in 10 Malaysians experiencing some form of mental health problem.

🔥 Why It Matters

Youth are disproportionately affected: social media pressure, academic stress, economic uncertainty with 12.28% youth unemployment, and the COVID-19 pandemic’s lingering psychological effects have created a perfect storm. Yet government mental health services remain woefully underfunded and understaffed. Public hospitals face severe shortages of psychiatrists and psychologists, with waiting times stretching weeks or months.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

The government allocated increased budgets for healthcare but mental health remains a fraction of overall health spending. School counsellors are often undertrained and overwhelmed. Helplines like Befrienders are largely volunteer-run. For a government that talks about ‘Madani’ (civilisational) values, allowing an entire generation to suffer in silence is a civilisational failure.

🎯 PH Callout

Three in ten Malaysians are struggling mentally and your response is underfunded. You talk about ‘Madani’ values but won’t fund the psychiatrists, counsellors, and helplines that could actually save lives. Mental health isn’t a soft issue — it’s a crisis. Fund it like one.

Palm Oil Deforestation Continues Unchecked — Environmental Pledges vs Plantation Profits

Said by: Environmental NGOs / SAM

Category: Other

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Despite Malaysia’s commitments to international climate agreements and sustainable development goals, deforestation for palm oil plantations continues to degrade tropical rainforests among the oldest and most biodiverse on Earth. The Malaysian Sustainable Palm Oil (MSPO) certification scheme has been criticised by international NGOs as inadequate greenwashing. Malaysia’s river water quality is declining — clean rivers dropped from 486 to 468 in 2025. The Perak River turned blue from rare earth mining discharge in November 2025. Endangered species including orangutans, pygmy elephants, and Malayan tigers face shrinking habitats.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Malaysia accounts for approximately 25% of global palm oil production, and the economic dependence on this commodity creates enormous political resistance to genuine environmental reform. While the rate of primary forest loss has slowed from its peak, secondary forest clearing and peatland conversion continue. The environmental cost — biodiversity loss, river pollution, carbon emissions from peatland drainage — is not counted in GDP figures but is borne by communities who depend on clean water, healthy forests, and functioning ecosystems.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The MSPO certification scheme is a sticker, not a standard. It allows continued environmental degradation while providing a veneer of sustainability that satisfies international buyers. The government talks about sustainability at COP summits and international conferences while approving new plantation developments at home. When your rivers turn blue from industrial discharge and your forests disappear for palm oil, no amount of international pledges will bring them back. Sustainability isn’t a speech — it’s a policy. Malaysia doesn’t have one.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under BN, Malaysia’s deforestation rate was among the highest in the world. Mahathir’s era saw massive conversion of primary rainforest to plantations. Under PH 1.0, a moratorium on new oil palm licences was briefly discussed. Under PH 2.0, the MSPO was expanded but not strengthened. Each government pays lip service to environmental protection while protecting the plantation industry that funds political parties across the spectrum. The pattern is bipartisan: plantations are more politically valuable than forests.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You sign climate agreements and pose for photos at COP summits. Then you come home and approve more plantations. Your MSPO scheme is window dressing. Your rivers are getting dirtier. Your forests are getting smaller. Strengthen MSPO to meet international standards. Enforce illegal logging laws. Invest in downstream palm oil processing rather than acreage expansion. Or keep giving sustainability speeches while your country’s environmental foundation crumbles — and hope the world doesn’t notice the hypocrisy.

Penang South Islands: $14B Reclamation Project Destroys Fishing Grounds for 6,000 Fishers

Said by: Penang State Government / SAM

Category: Other

Date: 2025-01-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: The Penang South Islands (PSI) project — originally three artificial islands costing approximately $14 billion — threatens the livelihoods of 6,000 fishers, 51,184 tonnes of annual marine catch worth RM525 million, and 511 aquaculture farms generating RM1.67 billion per year. After sustained protests from fishing communities and environmental groups including Sahabat Alam Malaysia (SAM), the project was downsized to one island. However, construction proceeds, and the fundamental impact on fishing grounds and marine ecosystems remains.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Penang’s fishing communities have worked these waters for generations. The reclamation destroys their fishing grounds — permanently. No amount of compensation replaces a livelihood that sustains 6,000 families. The marine catch and aquaculture figures represent hundreds of millions in annual economic output that will be lost. The project benefits property developers; the cost is borne by fishing communities who have no political leverage against DAP’s state government.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: DAP-led Penang has governed for over 16 years. The PSI project epitomises the contradiction at the heart of DAP’s governance: progressive rhetoric, developer-friendly reality. Reclaiming thousands of acres of sea to build luxury property while destroying the livelihoods of 6,000 fishers is not sustainable development — it is displacement development. The “downsizing” to one island was a PR concession, not a policy change. The fundamental trade-off — fishing communities sacrificed for developer profits — remains unchanged.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Land reclamation for development has been contentious in Penang for decades. The Gurney Drive reclamation, Tanjung Tokong development, and SRS Consortium controversies all followed the same pattern: developers propose, fishers protest, the state proceeds. DAP — which championed environmental protection in opposition — has greenlit more reclamation in Penang than BN ever did. The PSI project is the largest and most destructive yet.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You’re destroying the livelihoods of 6,000 fishers for a developer’s dream. RM525 million in annual marine catch. RM1.67 billion in aquaculture. Gone — permanently — so developers can sell luxury apartments on artificial islands. This is not development. It is the displacement of working communities for profit. If you can’t develop Penang without destroying the fishing industry, you can’t develop Penang.
