[BM] Pelanggaran Hak Tanah Orang Asli Berterusan — Komuniti Asal Dipindahkan Untuk Pembalakan Dan Perladangan

Said by: Amnesty International / Indigenous Rights Groups

Category: Social

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Sepanjang 2024, Orang Asli dan komuniti asal di Semenanjung Malaysia terus menghadapi pelanggaran sistematik hak tanah adat. Hutan datuk nenek moyang ditebang untuk konsesi pembalakan dan perladangan kelapa sawit, sering kali tanpa persetujuan bebas, termaklum dan terdahulu. Amnesty International membenderakan hak orang asal sebagai satu daripada tujuh bidang di mana Malaysia gagal memenuhi obligasi hak asasi manusia. Orang Asli membentuk hanya 0.2% perkhidmatan awam Malaysia walaupun merangkumi kira-kira 0.7% populasi — malah perwakilan berkadar pun dinafikan.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Orang Asli adalah komuniti paling terpinggir di Malaysia. Hak tanah adat mereka — diiktiraf oleh mahkamah tetapi secara sistematik dilanggar oleh kerajaan negeri — adalah asas identiti, mata pencarian dan kelangsungan budaya mereka. Apabila hutan datuk nenek moyang ditebang untuk perladangan, Orang Asli bukan sahaja kehilangan tanah — mereka kehilangan sumber makanan, kawasan tadahan air, tumbuhan ubatan dan tanah perkuburan. Kerajaan PH, yang mendakwa memperjuangkan hak asasi manusia dan keadilan sosial, tidak melakukan apa-apa untuk mengukuhkan perlindungan undang-undang bagi hak tanah orang asal atau menguatkuasakan keputusan mahkamah sedia ada.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: PH termasuk ahli politik yang memperjuangkan hak orang asal semasa pembangkang. Baru Bian — peguam hak tanah terkemuka dan AP PH — telah melawan kes tanah orang asal selama berdekad. Namun dalam kerajaan, PH tidak memperkenalkan undang-undang untuk mengkodifikasikan hak tanah adat, tidak menguatkuasakan keputusan mahkamah sedia ada yang melindungi wilayah orang asal, dan tidak meningkatkan perwakilan Orang Asli dalam perkhidmatan awam atau badan pembuat keputusan. Angka perwakilan perkhidmatan awam 0.2% bermakna Orang Asli hampir tidak kelihatan dalam kerajaan yang mendakwa bercakap bagi pihak semua rakyat Malaysia.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Perampasan tanah orang asal telah berterusan sejak kemerdekaan. Di bawah BN, konsesi pembalakan dikeluarkan kepada syarikat yang berhubungan politik tanpa perundingan. Di bawah PH 1.0, moratorium ringkas ke atas konsesi tertentu diumumkan sebelum Langkah Sheraton. Di bawah PH 2.0, corak ini disambung semula. Keputusan mahkamah yang mengiktiraf hak tanah adat — termasuk keputusan penting Mahkamah Persekutuan — secara rutin diabaikan oleh kerajaan negeri. Rangka kerja undang-undang wujud untuk melindungi hak orang asal. Kemauan politik untuk menguatkuasakannya tiada.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Tanah Orang Asli masih diambil. Keputusan mahkamah masih diabaikan. Perkhidmatan awam anda mempunyai 0.2% perwakilan Orang Asli. Amnesty membenderakannya. Kumpulan hak orang asal mendokumentasikannya. Dan kerajaan anda berdiam diri. Kodifikasikan hak tanah adat dalam perundangan persekutuan. Kuatkuasakan keputusan mahkamah sedia ada. Tingkatkan perwakilan orang asal dalam kerajaan. Atau terus bercakap tentang ’tamadun Madani’ sementara penduduk asal Malaysia menyaksikan hutan datuk nenek moyang mereka dijadikan perladangan kelapa sawit — di bawah pengawasan anda.

[BM] Yayasan Rosa Luxemburg: 'Rasuah, Jurang Kaum, Rasa Tidak Puas Hati Ekonomi' Di Bawah Anwar

Said by: Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada akhir 2024, Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung (RLS), sebuah yayasan politik Jerman, menerbitkan penilaian terperinci tentang Malaysia di bawah Anwar Ibrahim. Laporan itu menyimpulkan: “Rasuah peringkat tinggi berterusan, ketegangan kaum meningkat dengan ahli politik mengeksploitasi isu sensitif, respons kerajaan tertumpu kepada mengurus insiden melalui undang-undang usang berbanding menggalakkan dialog terbuka.” RLS mendokumentasikan bagaimana kerajaan Anwar mengekalkan struktur naungan dan rangka kerja etnik yang sama yang dijanjikan untuk dibongkar, sambil menggunakan undang-undang penindas untuk mengurus bantahan berbanding menangani punca.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Apabila yayasan politik antarabangsa tanpa kepentingan partisan dalam politik Malaysia menggambarkan kerajaan anda sebagai mengekalkan “rasuah peringkat tinggi” dan mengurus ketegangan kaum melalui “undang-undang usang,” ia adalah diagnosis klinikal kegagalan tadbir urus. RLS bukan corong pembangkang — ia adalah institusi akademik yang menganalisis ekonomi politik secara global. Penilaiannya sejajar dengan HRW, Amnesty International, Asia Maior dan East Asia Forum — konsensus di kalangan pemerhati antarabangsa bahawa agenda reformasi PH telah ditinggalkan.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Pembelaan PH terhadap setiap penilaian antarabangsa adalah sama: “mereka tidak faham konteks Malaysia.” Tetapi apabila lima institusi antarabangsa berbeza — dari benua yang berbeza, dengan metodologi yang berbeza — semua mencapai kesimpulan yang sama, masalahnya bukan pemahaman mereka. Ia tadbir urus anda. RLS secara khusus menyatakan bahawa respons kerajaan adalah mengurus insiden bukan menggalakkan dialog terbuka. Itu bukan reformasi — itu kawalan orang ramai. Mengurus ketegangan kaum melalui Akta Hasutan bukan menangani perkauman. Ia menyekat perbualan sementara masalah yang mendasari membusuk.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Penilaian antarabangsa terhadap tadbir urus Malaysia telah mengikut lengkungan yang konsisten di bawah PH 2.0. HRW: “Agenda Reformasi Terhenti” (2024), “Kemunduran Hak” (2025). Amnesty: “Mengkhianati ikrar reformasi” (2024). RSF: penurunan 34 tempat dalam kebebasan akhbar (2024). Asia Maior: mengelak “perbincangan terbuka tentang pelbagai garis peminggiran.” East Asia Forum: “aspirasi reformasi bercanggah dengan realiti politik.” RLS: “rasuah, jurang kaum, rasa tidak puas hati ekonomi.” Setiap penilaian tiba secara bebas di destinasi yang sama: PH bercakap reformasi tetapi mentadbir seperti BN.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Pemerhati antarabangsa telah mencapai konsensus: agenda reformasi anda telah ditinggalkan. Rasuah berterusan. Ketegangan kaum diurus melalui undang-undang usang, bukan ditangani melalui dialog terbuka. Rasa tidak puas hati ekonomi semakin meningkat. Anda boleh menolak satu laporan kritikal. Anda tidak boleh menolak lima daripada lima institusi di lima benua berbeza. Sama ada komit semula kepada agenda reformasi yang membawa anda berkuasa — atau terima bahawa masyarakat antarabangsa telah mengklasifikasikan kerajaan anda sebagai apa adanya: kesinambungan status quo dengan Bahasa Inggeris yang lebih baik.

2024 Floods Displaced Hundreds of Thousands — Northern States Devastated Again

Said by: ReliefWeb / East Asia Forum

Category: Other

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In December 2024, severe flooding in northern states displaced hundreds of thousands of Malaysians. Kelantan, Terengganu, and Pahang bore the brunt of the monsoon season. RM15 billion had been approved for flood mitigation after the devastating 2021 disaster, but progress was glacial. The same states that flood every year flooded again, with the same communities bearing the same losses. East Asia Forum documented how the 2024 floods exposed continued governance failures in disaster preparedness and infrastructure investment.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The same states flood repeatedly — Kelantan, Terengganu, Pahang, and parts of Johor and Selangor. The problem is not the rain. The problem is governance failure. Flood mitigation infrastructure has been promised, funded, and announced under every government. Implementation consistently falls short. Communities that were told “never again” after 2021 found themselves in evacuation centres again in 2024. When the same disaster hits the same people in the same places year after year, it is not a natural disaster — it is a policy choice.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: RM15 billion was approved for flood mitigation. Where did it go? Which projects were completed? Which are behind schedule? Which were never started? The government has not published a comprehensive implementation report. Communities that should have new drainage, retention ponds, and river deepening projects are still sandbagging their homes every November. The money exists. The projects were announced. The floods came anyway. That gap between allocation and implementation is the space where governance failure lives.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The 2021 floods killed 54 people and displaced over 400,000. The government approved RM15 billion for mitigation. The 2022 floods displaced tens of thousands. The 2023 floods displaced more. The 2024 floods displaced hundreds of thousands. Each year, the government announces relief, promises mitigation, and moves on — until the next monsoon. The cycle has continued under BN, PN, and PH. No government has broken it because breaking it requires sustained, expensive infrastructure investment in areas that don’t generate political returns proportional to the cost.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: RM15 billion approved. Hundreds of thousands displaced again. The same states. The same communities. The same pattern. Publish a project-by-project audit of the flood mitigation budget. Show which projects were completed, which are delayed, and which were never started. Hold contractors accountable for delays. Pre-position relief resources before monsoon season. Or keep treating annual floods as annual surprises — while the rakyat keeps losing everything they own, every year, in the same places.

Asia Maior: Anwar Sidelines 'Open Discussion on Multiple Lines of Disenfranchisement'

Said by: Asia Maior Academic Journal

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late 2024, the academic journal Asia Maior published an assessment of Anwar Ibrahim’s governance, concluding that he faced challenges “without substantially interrogating a public discourse that has historically sidelined open discussion on multiple socio-economic lines of disenfranchisement.” The analysis documented how Anwar’s reformist rhetoric coexisted with a status quo reality — maintaining the same patronage structures, ethnic frameworks, and institutional arrangements that he had spent decades promising to dismantle.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When an academic journal assesses that your government avoids “open discussion on multiple lines of disenfranchisement,” it is saying that you have chosen political safety over substantive reform. The lines of disenfranchisement in Malaysia are well-documented: ethnic quotas in education and public sector employment, gender inequality in legal rights, wealth concentration among politically connected elites, and East Malaysian marginalisation. PH promised to address all of these. Asia Maior’s assessment suggests none have been substantively confronted.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar built his political career on challenging the very structures Asia Maior identifies as untouched. Reformasi was supposed to mean reforming the ethnic patronage system, opening public discourse, and creating a Malaysia based on merit rather than identity. Three years into government, the discourse hasn’t opened — it has been managed. The patronage system hasn’t been dismantled — it has been rebranded. PH’s approach to disenfranchisement is identical to BN’s: acknowledge it in speeches, avoid it in policy, and hope the economy grows fast enough that nobody notices.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian academic assessment follows a similar trajectory. International scholars note the gap between PH’s rhetoric and reality with increasing precision. The Rosa Luxemburg Foundation documented “corruption, racial divides, and economic discontent.” East Asia Forum documented reform aspirations clashing with political realities. Asia Maior documented the avoidance of open discourse. Each assessment comes from a different angle but reaches the same conclusion: PH talks reform but practices status quo governance.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Academic journals are documenting what Malaysians already know: your reform rhetoric doesn’t match your governance reality. “Multiple lines of disenfranchisement” remain unaddressed because addressing them would cost you politically. Open the discourse Anwar promised. Commission an independent review of ethnic quotas, public sector hiring, and wealth distribution. Publish the data. Let Malaysians debate the findings. Or keep avoiding the conversation while scholars document your avoidance — and watch the reform mandate evaporate.

Orang Asli Land Rights Violations Continue — Indigenous Communities Displaced for Logging and Plantations

Said by: Amnesty International / Indigenous Rights Groups

Category: Social

Date: 2024-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Throughout 2024, Orang Asli and indigenous communities in Peninsular Malaysia continued to face systematic violations of customary land rights. Ancestral forests were cleared for logging concessions and palm oil plantations, often without free, prior, and informed consent. Amnesty International flagged indigenous rights as one of seven areas where Malaysia fell short of its human rights obligations. Orang Asli make up just 0.2% of Malaysia’s civil service despite comprising approximately 0.7% of the population — even proportional representation is denied.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Orang Asli are Malaysia’s most marginalised community. Their customary land rights — recognised by courts but systematically violated by state governments — are the foundation of their identity, livelihood, and cultural survival. When ancestral forests are cleared for plantations, Orang Asli don’t just lose land — they lose food sources, water catchments, medicinal plants, and burial grounds. The PH government, which claims to champion human rights and social justice, has done nothing to strengthen legal protections for indigenous land rights or enforce existing court rulings.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH includes politicians who have advocated for indigenous rights in opposition. Baru Bian — a prominent land rights lawyer and PH MP — has fought indigenous land cases for decades. Yet in government, PH has not introduced legislation to codify customary land rights, has not enforced existing court rulings protecting indigenous territories, and has not increased Orang Asli representation in the civil service or decision-making bodies. The 0.2% civil service representation figure means Orang Asli are virtually invisible in the government that claims to speak for all Malaysians.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Indigenous land dispossession has been ongoing since independence. Under BN, logging concessions were issued to politically connected companies with no consultation. Under PH 1.0, a brief moratorium on certain concessions was announced before the Sheraton Move. Under PH 2.0, the pattern has resumed. Court rulings recognising customary land rights — including landmark Federal Court decisions — are routinely ignored by state governments. The legal framework exists to protect indigenous rights. The political will to enforce it does not.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Orang Asli land is still being taken. Court rulings are still being ignored. Your civil service has 0.2% Orang Asli representation. Amnesty flagged it. Indigenous rights groups are documenting it. And your government is silent. Codify customary land rights in federal legislation. Enforce existing court rulings. Increase indigenous representation in government. Or keep talking about “Madani civilisation” while Malaysia’s original inhabitants watch their ancestral forests being turned into palm oil plantations — under your watch.

[BM] Banjir Terburuk Dalam Sedekad — 90,000 Dipindahkan, Cuti Menteri Dibekukan Terlambat

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2024-11-29

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada akhir November 2024, Malaysia mengalami banjir terburuk dalam sedekad. Lebih 90,000 orang dipindahkan merentasi 9 negeri, dengan Kelantan, Terengganu dan Pahang paling teruk terjejas. Anwar membekukan cuti menteri — tetapi hanya selepas krisis sudah meningkat ke tahap kecemasan. Banjir monsun tahunan, yang berlaku dengan ketepatan jam setiap tahun, sekali lagi mendedahkan infrastruktur yang tidak mencukupi dan kesiapsiagaan bencana yang lemah meskipun berbilion diperuntukkan untuk mitigasi banjir selepas banjir dahsyat 2021.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: Banjir monsun bukan kejutan. Ia berlaku setiap tahun, di negeri yang sama, pada bulan yang sama. Kerajaan yang tidak bersedia menghadapi banjir tahunan yang boleh dijangka bukan malang — ia tidak cekap. Angka 90,000 yang dipindahkan mewakili keluarga sebenar yang kehilangan rumah, harta benda dan mata pencarian. Membekukan cuti menteri selepas krisis meningkat adalah pengurusan reaktif, bukan kesiapsiagaan bencana. Kerajaan yang cekap akan menyiapkan sumber pra-posisi, pelan pemindahan dan penyelarasan menteri sebelum monsun tiba.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Selepas banjir dahsyat 2021 di bawah Ismail Sabri, kerajaan meluluskan RM15 bilion untuk mitigasi banjir. Di bawah PH, pelaksanaan sangat perlahan. Projek mitigasi ketinggalan jadual. Infrastruktur saliran di kawasan terdedah banjir masih tidak mencukupi. Komuniti yang sama yang dilanda banjir pada 2021, 2022 dan 2023 dilanda banjir semula pada 2024. Wang telah diluluskan. Projek telah diumumkan. Banjir datang juga. Apabila perbelanjaan mitigasi RM15 bilion tidak menghalang 90,000 pemindahan, sama ada wang itu disalah belanjakan atau projek tidak dibina. Kedua-duanya adalah kegagalan tadbir urus.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia dilanda banjir setiap tahun. Di bawah Najib, banjir 2014 memindahkan lebih 200,000 — dan Najib ditangkap bermain golf di Hawaii. Di bawah Ismail Sabri, banjir 2021 membunuh 54 orang dan memindahkan lebih 400,000. Di bawah PH, banjir 2024 memindahkan 90,000 — lebih baik daripada 2021 tetapi masih dahsyat bagi komuniti yang terjejas. Coraknya sama merentasi pentadbiran: persediaan yang tidak mencukupi, respons yang lambat, janji perbelanjaan mitigasi, pelaksanaan yang perlahan, dan komuniti yang sama dilanda banjir semula tahun depan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Banjir monsun boleh dijangka. Anda mempunyai setahun untuk bersiap sejak banjir tahun lepas. Anda mempunyai RM15 bilion yang diperuntukkan untuk mitigasi. Dan 90,000 orang masih dipindahkan. Membekukan cuti menteri selepas krisis melanda bukan pengurusan bencana — ia kawalan kerosakan. Terbitkan audit peruntukan mitigasi banjir RM15 bilion: berapa banyak yang dibelanjakan, untuk apa, dan mengapa ia tidak menghalang pemindahan tahun ini. Posisikan sumber sebelum monsun tahun depan. Atau terus bertindak balas terhadap bencana yang boleh dijangka dan tertanya-tanya mengapa rakyat tidak mempercayai anda untuk melindungi mereka.

Worst Floods in a Decade — 90,000 Displaced, Ministers' Leave Frozen Too Late

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Other

Date: 2024-11-29

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In late November 2024, Malaysia experienced its worst floods in a decade. Over 90,000 people were displaced across 9 states, with Kelantan, Terengganu, and Pahang worst affected. Anwar froze ministers’ leave — but only after the crisis had already escalated to emergency levels. The annual monsoon flooding, which occurs with clockwork regularity every year, once again exposed inadequate infrastructure and disaster preparedness despite billions allocated for flood mitigation after the catastrophic 2021 floods.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Monsoon floods are not surprises. They happen every year, in the same states, during the same months. A government that is caught unprepared by predictable annual flooding is not unlucky — it is incompetent. The 90,000 displaced figure represents real families losing homes, possessions, and livelihoods. Freezing ministers’ leave after the crisis escalates is reactive management, not disaster preparedness. A competent government would have had pre-positioned resources, evacuation plans, and ministerial coordination in place before the monsoon arrived.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: After the catastrophic 2021 floods under Ismail Sabri, the government approved RM15 billion for flood mitigation. Under PH, implementation has been glacial. Mitigation projects are behind schedule. Drainage infrastructure in flood-prone areas remains inadequate. The same communities that flooded in 2021, 2022, and 2023 flooded again in 2024. The money was approved. The projects were announced. The floods came anyway. When RM15 billion in mitigation spending doesn’t prevent 90,000 displacements, either the money was misspent or the projects weren’t built. Both are governance failures.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia floods every year. Under Najib, the 2014 floods displaced over 200,000 — and Najib was caught playing golf in Hawaii. Under Ismail Sabri, the 2021 floods killed 54 people and displaced over 400,000. Under PH, the 2024 floods displaced 90,000 — better than 2021 but still catastrophic for the affected communities. The pattern is identical across administrations: inadequate preparation, slow response, promises of mitigation spending, glacial implementation, and the same communities flooding again the next year.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Monsoon floods are predictable. You had a year to prepare since last year’s floods. You had RM15 billion allocated for mitigation. And 90,000 people were still displaced. Freezing ministers’ leave after the crisis hits is not disaster management — it’s damage control. Publish an audit of the RM15 billion flood mitigation allocation: how much was spent, on what, and why it didn’t prevent this year’s displacements. Pre-position resources before next year’s monsoon. Or keep reacting to predictable disasters and wondering why the rakyat doesn’t trust you to protect them.

[BM] Lawatan Luar Negara Anwar: 75% Dibiayai Syarikat Swasta — Kemudian Kerajaan Kata Sebenarnya Tidak

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister (and Fahmi Fadzil)

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-11-22

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada November 2024, Anwar memberitahu Parlimen bahawa syarikat swasta seperti Petronas dan Proton menanggung kira-kira 75% kos lawatan luar negaranya di bawah strategi perkongsian kos baru. Keesokan harinya, Menteri Komunikasi Fahmi Fadzil “menjelaskan” bahawa eksekutif korporat “membayar sendiri” manakala kerajaan menanggung delegasi Anwar. Kerajaan telah membelanjakan RM1.6 juta untuk mencarter pesawat bagi lawatan empat negara. Apabila disoal lebih lanjut, Anwar memanggilnya “zaman gila” bahawa ketelusan dihukum. Penganalisis CNA memberi amaran amalan itu “boleh mendedahkan kelemahan dalam rangka kerja pembiayaan politik Malaysia.”

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: PM berkata satu perkara di Parlimen — syarikat swasta menanggung 75% kos lawatan. Menteri Komunikasinya berkata sesuatu yang berbeza keesokan harinya — syarikat membayar untuk eksekutif mereka sendiri, bukan delegasi Anwar. Yang mana satu betul? Jika syarikat swasta turut membiayai lawatan PM, terdapat persoalan sah tentang akses dan pengaruh. Syarikat yang terbang bersama PM mendapat masa bersemuka, pembinaan hubungan dan sokongan kerajaan yang dilihat. Akses itu ada nilainya — dan apabila ia dibeli melalui pembiayaan lawatan, ia menimbulkan kebimbangan konflik kepentingan.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Memanggil pengkritik tidak bersyukur kerana mempersoalkan perbelanjaan kerajaan bukan ketelusan — ia pengelakan. Kerajaan yang tidak mampu memastikan ceritanya konsisten mengenai perbelanjaan lawatan untuk satu kitaran berita tidak menunjukkan ketelusan — ia menunjukkan disfungsi. Dan RM1.6 juta untuk mencarter pesawat untuk empat negara bukanlah mengetatkan tali pinggang, tanpa mengira siapa yang membayar.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Perbelanjaan lawatan PM menjadi pertikaian di bawah setiap pentadbiran. Penggunaan jet kerajaan oleh Najib untuk lawatan peribadi dan pembelian jet mewah adalah skandal yang menjadi tanda dagangan. PH mengkritik setiap ringgit perbelanjaan lawatan Najib. Di bawah PH 2.0, Anwar telah melakukan lebih 100 lawatan luar negara dalam tiga tahun — lebih daripada mana-mana PM Malaysia terdahulu dalam tempoh yang sama. Pengaturan perkongsian kos sepatutnya menjawab kritikan tentang kekerapan lawatan. Sebaliknya, ia mewujudkan kontroversi baru tentang pengaruh korporat.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: PM anda memberitahu Parlimen satu perkara. Menteri Komunikasi anda berkata sesuatu yang berbeza keesokan harinya. Kemudian PM anda mengadu bahawa ketelusan dihukum. Itu bukan ketelusan — itu pemesejan bercanggah diikuti rasa kasihan diri. Terbitkan perakaunan yang jelas dan terperinci bagi setiap lawatan luar negara: kos kerajaan, penyertaan korporat, dan hasil perniagaan apa yang terhasil. Jika perkongsian kos itu sah, buktikan dengan data. Jika tidak, berhenti melakukannya. Tetapi jangan beritahu Parlimen satu angka dan suruh menteri anda bercanggah dalam 24 jam — dan jangkakan rakyat Malaysia memanggil itu tadbir urus.

Anwar's Overseas Trips: 75% Funded by Private Companies — Then Government Says Actually No

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister (and Fahmi Fadzil)

Category: Politics

Date: 2024-11-22

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In November 2024, Anwar told Parliament that private companies like Petronas and Proton covered approximately 75% of his overseas travel costs under a new cost-sharing strategy. The next day, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil “clarified” that corporate executives “paid their own way” while the government covered Anwar’s delegation. The government had spent RM1.6 million to charter a plane for a four-country trip. When questioned further, Anwar called it a “crazy era” that transparency was being punished. CNA analysts warned the practice “could expose vulnerabilities in Malaysia’s political financing framework.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The PM said one thing in Parliament — private companies covered 75% of travel costs. His Communications Minister said something different the next day — companies paid for their own executives, not Anwar’s delegation. Which is it? If private companies are co-funding PM travel, there are legitimate questions about access and influence. Companies that fly with the PM get face time, relationship-building, and perceived government endorsement. That access has value — and when it’s purchased through travel funding, it raises conflict-of-interest concerns.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Calling critics ungrateful for questioning government spending is not transparency — it is deflection. Anwar styled the cost-sharing as fiscal responsibility, but the messaging collapse within 24 hours suggests the arrangement was either poorly structured or poorly understood by his own cabinet. A government that can’t keep its story straight on travel expenses for one news cycle is not demonstrating transparency — it is demonstrating dysfunction. And RM1.6 million to charter a plane for four countries is not exactly belt-tightening, regardless of who pays.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: PM travel expenses have been contentious under every administration. Najib’s use of a government jet for personal trips and the purchase of a luxury jet were signature scandals. PH criticised every ringgit of Najib’s travel spending. Under PH 2.0, Anwar has taken over 100 overseas trips in three years — more than any previous Malaysian PM in the same period. The cost-sharing arrangement was supposed to answer criticism about travel frequency. Instead, it created a new controversy about corporate influence.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your PM told Parliament one thing. Your Communications Minister said something different the next day. Then your PM complained that transparency is being punished. That’s not transparency — that’s contradictory messaging followed by self-pity. Publish a clear, detailed accounting of every overseas trip: government cost, corporate participation, and what business outcomes resulted. If the cost-sharing is legitimate, prove it with data. If it’s not, stop doing it. But don’t tell Parliament one figure and have your minister contradict it within 24 hours — and expect Malaysians to call that governance.
