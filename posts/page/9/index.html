Selangor Bans All Pig Farming — Amirudin Caves to Sultan's Decree, Chinese Farmers Left With Nothing

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-12

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 12 February 2026, Selangor Menteri Besar Amirudin Shari announced that the state government has stopped issuing pig farming licences and will accelerate the closure of all existing pig farms. The decision followed a decree from Sultan Sharafuddin Idris Shah, who declared that no pig farms should operate in any district in Selangor.

An earlier plan to relocate pig farms to Bukit Tagar — a sparsely populated area in northern Selangor — was shelved after Malay residents objected due to proximity to Sungai Selangor, a major water treatment source. Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim intervened in January to halt the relocation.

Selangor will now fully import all pork to meet non-Muslim demand. Chinese pig farmers, many of whom have operated multigenerational farms in Tanjong Sepat, face the destruction of their livelihoods.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Selangor is Malaysia’s richest and most developed state — and it’s governed by PH. The pig farming ban affects a specific ethnic community’s livelihood and dietary needs. While environmental concerns about water pollution are legitimate, the total ban with no viable alternative is a capitulation, not a solution.

The message to Chinese voters is clear: when the palace decrees and Malay sentiment pushes back, PH will not fight for you. The Bukit Tagar relocation — which could have been a modern, regulated solution — was abandoned the moment it became politically uncomfortable.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

PH governs Selangor. PH had years to develop a proper, regulated, environmentally sustainable pig farming solution. Instead, they proposed a relocation, got pushback, abandoned it, and then caved to a royal decree with a total ban.

The state that brands itself as progressive and inclusive just eliminated an entire industry that primarily serves its Chinese community. Pork importers — who stand to profit massively — are already backing the ban. One importer estimated imports already cover 80% of demand. So who benefits? Not the farmers. Not the consumers who prefer fresh local pork. The importers.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

This follows a pattern of PH state governments failing to protect minority communities when the political cost gets too high. In Penang, DAP has governed for 16 years but still faces criticism for development that displaces communities. In Selangor, Amirudin has repeatedly been caught flat-footed — from flood management failures to the water leak crisis. Every time, the response is reactive, never proactive.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You are the government of Selangor. You had the power to regulate, modernise, and relocate pig farming. Instead, you banned it entirely. You didn’t solve a pollution problem — you eliminated a community’s livelihood. Where is the DAP that promised to protect all Malaysians regardless of race? Where is the Madani government that talks about inclusivity? The Chinese farmers in Tanjong Sepat would like to know.

Tiong King Sing Pulls 'Do You Know Who I Am?' Card While Lecturing Staff About Rudeness

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-02-12

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 12 February 2026, Tourism Minister Tiong King Sing launched the ‘Mesra Malaysia’ (Friendly Malaysia) campaign at KL Sentral — a campaign about being welcoming and courteous to tourists. During the launch, Tiong complained about rudeness from public and private sector employees, saying: “Some officers think they have a lot of power. But when people don’t understand certain issues, we must explain.”

The South China Morning Post ran the story with the devastating headline: “Malaysian minister slams rude staff hindering tourism: ‘do you know who I am?’” — capturing the irony of a powerful politician lecturing others about arrogance while exuding precisely the same energy.

Visit Malaysia Year 2026 is supposed to be a national priority. The tourism sector is critical to Malaysia’s economy. And the minister responsible for making Malaysia welcoming to the world is making headlines for his own attitude problem.

You’re launching a campaign called ‘Friendly Malaysia’ while acting like the least friendly person in the room. The cognitive dissonance is spectacular. A minister complaining about public servants who “think they have a lot of power” — while wielding the power of a federal ministry to publicly shame unnamed workers — is peak Malaysian political irony.

The ‘do you know who I am’ energy is exactly the attitude tourists and citizens hate about Malaysian bureaucracy. And the minister tasked with fixing it is the embodiment of it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Tiong King Sing is a businessman-politician from Sarawak’s GPS coalition. The alcohol controversy at the gala dinner in 2025 already showed a minister out of touch with sensitivities. Malaysia’s tourism sector has also been plagued by scam taxi drivers, overcharging at tourist spots, and visa problems — systemic issues that require systemic solutions, not press conferences about attitude.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You put Tiong King Sing in charge of tourism. He’s now making international headlines for arrogance while launching a friendliness campaign. The minister responsible for Malaysia’s image is damaging Malaysia’s image. If you want Visit Malaysia Year 2026 to succeed, start by ensuring your ministers don’t become the joke tourists tell when they get home.

Nga Kor Ming's MyKiosk Project: No Needs Assessment, No KPIs, MACC Calls It a Governance Failure

Said by: Nga Kor Ming, Housing and Local Government Minister

Category: Housing

Date: 2026-02-10

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 10 February 2026, the Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission (MACC) revealed that the Housing and Local Government Ministry (KPKT) under Nga Kor Ming failed to carry out a proper needs assessment before launching the MyKiosk initiative — a nationwide programme to provide traders with modern kiosks.

MACC issued five governance improvement recommendations and stated: “The absence of a needs assessment and a comprehensive outcome-monitoring mechanism, including KPIs, hindered the evaluation of the project’s impact at the local authority level.”

Former MCA vice-president Ti Lian Ker immediately called for Nga’s resignation, saying: “When billions in public funds are at stake, failing to ask fundamental questions — who needs this, where it should be placed, and how success will be measured — is not just careless but irresponsible.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

This is basic governance 101. You don’t launch a nationwide programme with public money without first asking: Does anyone need this? The MyKiosk project was described as a white elephant after failing to meet government objectives, with poorly placed kiosks causing public inconvenience rather than helping traders.

Ti Lian Ker noted this added to “a growing record of questionable judgments and misplaced priorities” — including Nga’s directive to turn four public toilets into smart toilets and public unease over the Urban Renewal Bill. The pattern is clear: tone-deaf decision-making, weak policy grounding, and public backlash after the fact.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Here’s the best part. In July 2025, when the MyKiosk project was first flagged as a white elephant, Nga invited MACC to investigate and then demanded an apology from critics when MACC found no corruption. He said: “To those who threw stones, don’t hide your hands.”

But no corruption doesn’t mean no failure. The MACC’s February 2026 report confirmed what everyone already knew: the project was poorly planned, poorly monitored, and poorly executed. Nga celebrated the absence of criminal intent while ignoring the presence of spectacular incompetence. That’s not governance — that’s a participation trophy.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

Nga’s ministry has a pattern. The smart toilet initiative was mocked nationally. The Urban Renewal Bill drew public protest. Each episode follows the same arc: launch first, study later, apologise when caught, then demand credit for not being corrupt. PAS researcher Dr Nurul Islam called it the “execute first, study later” work culture of the Madani Government.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You promised clean, competent governance. The MACC just told your ministry it didn’t even bother to check if anyone needed MyKiosk before spending public money on it. That’s not reform — that’s the same BN-era waste you used to condemn from the opposition bench. If a BN minister had done this, DAP would have held press conferences for weeks. When it’s your own minister? Silence.

Students Flash Red Cards at Adam Adli Over Broken UUCA Promise — PH's Own Poster Boy Turns Reneger

Said by: Adam Adli Abdul Halim, Deputy Higher Education Minister

Category: Education

Date: 2026-02-09

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 9 February 2026, university students marched to Parliament demanding the abolition of the Universities and University Colleges Act 1971 (UUCA). Their target? Deputy Higher Education Minister Adam Adli Abdul Halim — once Malaysia’s most famous student activist, now the living embodiment of PH’s broken promise. Abolishing UUCA was a key manifesto promise by Pakatan Harapan in both GE14 (2018) and GE15 (2022). In late January 2026, Higher Education Minister Zambry Abd Kadir told Parliament the government had “no plans” to abolish UUCA — a policy reversal delivered without fanfare. Students flashed red cards at Adam Adli and called him a “reneger.” Zambry refused to receive their memorandum. Adam Adli, 33, was arrested in 2013 for lowering a flag at a student rally — an act that made him a symbol of the student movement. PH championed his case as proof that UUCA was unjust. Now he sits in the ministry that enforces it.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: PH promised to repeal UUCA in two consecutive elections. Four years into government, the Higher Education Minister says there are “no plans” to abolish it. This is not a delay — it is a lie. The promise was never intended to be kept; it was designed to harvest student votes. Adam Adli’s personal arc is the most devastating metaphor for the PH government: the student who was arrested for lowering a flag in protest now sits in the ministry that maintains the law that criminalises student protest. The students who voted for PH based on this promise are now protesting PH’s own deputy minister. Zambry’s refusal to receive the memorandum is a symbolic gut punch — when the government won’t even accept a piece of paper from student protesters, the message is clear: your vote was useful, your voice is not. UUCA determines whether 1.3 million Malaysian university students can participate in the political life of their country. Keeping it in place means PH believes students should vote for reform but not advocate for it.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Under UUCA, students can face suspension or expulsion for participating in political activities. The law was enacted in 1971 after student protests against rural poverty — it was designed from the start to suppress politically conscious youth. PH knew this. PH campaigned against it. PH’s own deputy minister was a victim of it. And now PH is keeping it. The Straits Times noted the protest “adds to Anwar’s pre-election reform challenges” — framing UUCA as part of a broader pattern of stalled promises eroding youth support. If you cannot keep promises to students who believed in you, the reform brand is dead.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: UUCA reform has been promised by every opposition coalition since the 2008 political tsunami. PH 1.0 (2018-2020) promised reform but collapsed before acting. PH 2.0 has now had over three years and explicitly reversed the promise. The pattern is identical to toll abolition, AG-PP separation, and the Sedition Act repeal: promise boldly in opposition, delay in government, quietly abandon. UUCA joins a growing list of manifesto pledges that were campaign decorations, not governance commitments.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Your deputy minister was arrested under this law. He built his career fighting it. Now he defends the ministry that enforces it. If you wrote this as fiction, it would be criticised as too on-the-nose. Abolish UUCA or amend it to remove restrictions on student political activity — as you promised twice. The 1.3 million students in Malaysian universities are watching. And they vote.

Fahmi Fadzil: Free Speech Doesn't Mean Freedom to Offend Online

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-07

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 7 February 2026, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil told the public that free speech does not mean freedom to offend or defame online. This came after the Federal Court reinstated Section 233 of the Communications and Multimedia Act (CMA), which criminalises content deemed “grossly offensive” — a provision PH once opposed. Fahmi said the 2024 amendments raised the threshold from “offensive” to “grossly offensive,” claiming this made it harder to misuse. The Online Safety Act 2025 (ONSA) took effect on 1 January 2026, giving MCMC powers to penalise platforms up to RM10 million for failing to remove harmful content. MCMC — the body that decides what is “grossly offensive” — reports directly to Fahmi’s ministry.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: “Grossly offensive” is a subjective and vague standard that can mean anything the government wants it to mean. The combination of the reinstated CMA provision and the new ONSA gives the Malaysian government one of the most powerful speech regulation frameworks in Southeast Asia. Platforms face RM10M fines; individuals face prosecution. The chilling effect is by design. PH in opposition repeatedly condemned the CMA as a tool of authoritarian suppression. In 2018, PH’s manifesto called for “reviewing” the CMA. In 2022, PH Youth called Section 233 “draconian.” In government, they amended it slightly, renamed “offensive” to “grossly offensive,” and called it progress.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Who decides what is “grossly offensive”? MCMC, which answers to Fahmi. Fahmi, who answers to Anwar. There is no independent judicial review before content is flagged or removed. The minister who champions “free speech with limits” is also the minister who controls the limits. The fox is guarding the henhouse and telling the chickens it is for their own protection. PH’s own track record demolishes Fahmi’s argument: when the government arrests a journalist for asking a question (Rex Tan), investigates activists for social media posts, and uses the Sedition Act 90+ times in three years, the “we only target real harm” defence collapses. Renaming “offensive” to “grossly offensive” is not reform — it is rebranding.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Since taking power, the Anwar government has used the CMA, Sedition Act, and other speech laws to arrest journalists, activists, and social media users — including FMT journalist Rex Tan, activist Heidy Quah, and multiple online commentators critical of the government. The Sedition Act — which PH promised to repeal — has been used over 90 times since 2024. The CMA was wielded against critics under Najib, under Muhyiddin, and now under Anwar. The tool changes hands; the abuse continues. Every Malaysian government has used speech laws selectively: targeting critics while ignoring allies who say equally provocative things.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You promised to reform the CMA. Instead, you strengthened it. You promised to repeal the Sedition Act. Instead, you used it more than Najib did. You promised press freedom. Instead, your MCMC blocks news outlets and your police arrest journalists at dawn. Fahmi’s statement — “free speech doesn’t mean freedom to offend” — is the same argument made by every government that has ever restricted speech, from Singapore’s POFMA to China’s internet regulations. The formula is universal: affirm the right, define it so narrowly it becomes meaningless. PH voters expected the architecture of speech suppression to be dismantled. Instead, you added new floors to the building.

River Water Quality Declining — More Polluted Rivers in 2025 Than 2024

Said by: Natural Resources Ministry

Category: Other

Date: 2026-01-28

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: According to data released by the Natural Resources Ministry in late January 2026, Malaysia’s river water quality deteriorated year-over-year. Clean rivers dropped from 486 to 468. “Moderate” quality rivers rose from 161 to 171. “Slightly polluted” rivers jumped from 25 to 33. The numbers are moving in the wrong direction across every category. This decline occurred as the government aggressively pushed rare earth mining expansion — one site in Perak already turned a river visibly blue from mining discharges — and continued to promote Malaysia as a “green economy” leader.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Rivers are the canary in the coal mine for environmental governance. If water quality declines while the government claims environmental progress, the claims are hollow. This is happening under a government that announced a Climate Resilience Plan, set green economy targets, and positioned Malaysia as environmentally conscious on the world stage. The data says otherwise. More polluted rivers mean worse drinking water sources, degraded ecosystems, higher water treatment costs, and health risks for communities living near affected waterways — disproportionately rural and lower-income Malaysians.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The government boasts about green economy targets while presiding over measurable environmental degradation. You cannot simultaneously push aggressive rare earth mining expansion, approve data centre developments that strain water resources, and allow industrial discharge standards to go unenforced — then claim environmental leadership with a straight face. The river quality data is not subjective. It is measured. It is published. And it shows decline. The gap between the government’s environmental rhetoric and its environmental outcomes is not a communication problem — it is a governance failure. When your rivers are getting dirtier under your watch, your green targets are fiction.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia’s environmental enforcement has been chronically weak across administrations. The Sg Kim Kim toxic dumping in Johor (2019) hospitalised hundreds. The Perak River turned blue from rare earth mining (2025). Annual monsoon floods — worsened by deforestation and poor drainage planning — displace tens of thousands. The Department of Environment has been consistently underfunded and understaffed. Under BN, environmental protection was sacrificed for development. Under PH, the same sacrifice continues under a greener brand. The pattern is bipartisan: extract now, clean up never.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Publish the river-by-river breakdown. Name the polluters. Enforce discharge standards with real penalties — not warning letters that get filed and forgotten. If rare earth mining is turning rivers blue and water quality is declining nationally, your “green economy” narrative is a lie backed by your own ministry’s data. A government that measures environmental decline and does nothing about it is not governing — it is documenting its own failure. Fund the Department of Environment properly. Enforce industrial discharge standards. Or stop pretending you care about the environment.

Malaysia Study: Cost-Benefit Analysis of US Trade Deal Shows 'Concerns' But Government Says Don't Quit

Said by: Malaysian Government

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-01-27

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2026, Bloomberg reported that the Malaysian government announced a formal cost-benefit analysis of the controversial US-Malaysia Agreed Reciprocal Trade (ART) agreement — following months of mounting criticism from former AG Tommy Thomas (who called it “the worst agreement since independence”), former PM Mahathir, think tanks, and opposition politicians. But in the same breath, the government warned lawmakers not to quit the pact. East Asia Forum published a detailed analysis titled “The Art of the Deal Lost in US-Malaysia Pact,” arguing Malaysia surrendered critical mineral export controls, digital services taxation rights, and independent trade policy in exchange for a tariff reduction from 25% to 19% — still punitive by any standard. The deal’s details were first published on the White House website before being shared with Malaysian Parliament.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Announcing a cost-benefit analysis while pre-empting its conclusion is not governance — it is political theatre. “Study but don’t quit” tells you everything about this administration’s approach: acknowledge problems, change nothing. If the analysis shows the deal hurts Malaysia, will the government act? The answer is already in the framing. The study exists to absorb pressure, not to inform decisions. The fact that Parliament learned about the deal from the White House website is a governance failure of the first order — trade agreements that constrain national sovereignty should require parliamentary approval before signing, not after.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: “Study but don’t quit” is the Madani playbook in miniature: acknowledge the problem, promise a process, change nothing. It is the same approach applied to the AG-PP separation, UUCA repeal, toll abolition, and every other stalled reform. The cost-benefit analysis will either be buried, selectively quoted, or used to justify minor adjustments while the core agreement remains untouched. Anwar bypassed the legislature on the most consequential economic agreement in decades. When your own former AG calls your trade deal the worst since independence and your response is “we’ll study it but we’re keeping it,” the study is performative.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a history of trade agreements where the government commits first and reviews later. The TPPA under Najib faced similar criticism — Malaysia signed on to provisions critics said undermined sovereignty, pharmaceutical pricing, and labour rights. PH 1.0 withdrew from the TPPA in 2018, calling it unfair. PH 2.0 signed the ART — which critics say contains even more restrictive provisions — and now wants to “study” whether it was a good idea. The pattern of committing to deals under pressure, then conducting post-hoc reviews that change nothing, is a bipartisan Malaysian tradition.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: If you’re serious about the cost-benefit analysis, publish the full terms of the ART — not the White House summary, but the actual agreement. Let independent economists, not government-appointed task forces, evaluate the deal. If the analysis shows Malaysia got a bad deal, have the courage to renegotiate. “Study but don’t quit” is not a policy — it is a confession that you already know the answer and don’t want to act on it. Parliament deserves a vote on trade agreements that constrain national sovereignty. Give them one.

Zulkifli Hasan Says Work Stress Can Make People Gay — International Community Laughs, Malaysia Cringes

Said by: Zulkifli Hasan, Minister in PM’s Department (Religious Affairs)

Category: Social

Date: 2026-01-26

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On January 26, 2026, Religious Affairs Minister Zulkifli Hasan submitted a written parliamentary reply to PAS MP Siti Zailah Mohd Yusoff about LGBT trends in Malaysia. He cited a 2017 study and listed “societal influence, sexual experiences, work stress, and other personal factors” as possible causes of “LGBT behaviour.” The “work stress” claim went immediately viral — globally. CNA, SCMP, NDTV, Reuters, Hindustan Times, LGBTQ Nation, and them.us all covered it. Malaysians flooded social media with mockery, sarcastically asking whether parliament MPs had “turned gay” since they apparently never work hard enough to experience stress. Zulkifli also disclosed that from 2022 to 2025, 135 cases related to “LGBT activities” were recorded as arrests or prosecutions — in a country where same-sex relations remain criminalised.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: This isn’t just embarrassing — it’s damaging. A cabinet minister, speaking in an official parliamentary capacity, cited debunked pseudoscience to explain sexual orientation. This is the government’s official position on record in Hansard. The international coverage turned Malaysia into a global punchline while the country tries to attract foreign investment, host international summits, and position itself as a progressive Muslim-majority democracy. More importantly, the remark normalises the framing of LGBT identity as a pathology — something caused by external factors that can presumably be “fixed.” This framing underpins the continued criminalisation of same-sex relations and the use of state-funded “rehabilitation” programmes.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The 2017 study Zulkifli cited — Sulaiman et al. — is a small-scale Malaysian social study, not a peer-reviewed finding from any recognised international medical or psychological body. Every major medical and psychological organisation in the world — the WHO, APA, AMA, Royal College of Psychiatrists — has stated clearly that sexual orientation is not caused by stress, lifestyle, or environmental factors. The minister later issued a defensive statement saying people should “refer to the full written answer” — as if the problem was context. It wasn’t. The context makes it worse: it was an official, considered, pre-written reply. This wasn’t a slip of the tongue. It was deliberate.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has a long, inglorious history of ministers making scientifically illiterate statements about LGBT people. In 2018, then-Deputy PM Wan Azizah said the government would not recognise LGBT rights because they were “against the law of nature.” In 2019, Religious Affairs officials promoted “conversion therapy” camps. Malaysia’s Federal Territories Islamic Department (JAWI) regularly raids private premises suspected of hosting LGBT individuals. PH in opposition criticised BN’s persecution of LGBT Malaysians; in government, PH has continued the same policies and rhetoric. Zulkifli Hasan’s appointment itself was a political concession — he represents the Malay-Muslim establishment within PH’s coalition.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You campaigned on “Malaysia Madani” — a civilised, progressive Malaysia. Your Religious Affairs Minister just told parliament that work stress turns people gay. The world laughed. Malaysia’s reputation took yet another hit. Where was Anwar? Where was the PMO correction? Where was the cabinet disavowal? Silence. Because correcting Zulkifli would upset the conservative base PH desperately needs. So the government let a pseudoscientific claim stand as Malaysia’s official parliamentary position on sexual orientation. Madani means civilised. There is nothing civilised about citing junk science in parliament to justify the criminalisation of who people love.
