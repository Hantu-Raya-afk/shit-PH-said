Oil Past $100, Strait of Hormuz Shut, Rakyat Burning Fuel to Commute — Anwar Won't Mandate WFH, Freeze Tolls, Cap Food Prices, or Cut GLC Fat

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

The Iran-US war has pushed global oil past $100 per barrel, effectively shut down the Strait of Hormuz — through which nearly 80% of Asia’s oil transits — and sent Malaysia’s FBM KLCI plunging 2.55% in a single session on March 9. RON97 is up to RM3.25, diesel to RM3.12, and Anwar himself admitted on March 7 that the government can only hold RON95 at RM1.99 for ‘one or two more months.’ Freight costs are surging, food prices are next, and experts from NST to Hong Leong IB are warning of imported inflation hitting the rakyat’s kitchen table. Anwar’s response so far: a strongly worded tweet about solidarity, an emergency parliamentary motion condemning Israel, and Zafrul saying Malaysia is ‘prepared’ — without naming a single concrete domestic measure to protect workers and families from the economic fallout.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a theoretical crisis. Malaysians are already paying more at the pump for RON97 and diesel. Freight costs feed directly into food, medicine, and essential goods. The stock market is bleeding. And yet the Prime Minister — who is also the Finance Minister — has not announced a single emergency domestic economic measure. No crisis-mode intervention. No relief. Just speeches about geopolitics while the rakyat’s cost of living climbs in real time. Malaysia proved during COVID that the government can act decisively on domestic economic protection when it wants to. The difference is political will — and right now, Anwar’s will is directed at Parliament speeches about Zionist plots, not at shielding the people he governs.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Four things Anwar could do tomorrow with existing executive power — and won’t:

1. Mandate work-from-home for all WFH-compatible jobs. Malaysia already has a Flexible Work Arrangement framework under the Employment Act. During COVID, the government mandated WFH and it worked. With oil past $100 and commuters burning RM300-500/month on fuel and tolls, a temporary WFH directive for office-based workers would immediately cut household fuel costs, reduce demand on subsidised petrol, and ease the fiscal pressure Anwar says he can only hold for two months. Instead, millions of KL workers sit in traffic burning fuel the government can barely afford to subsidise.

2. Freeze or waive highway tolls during the crisis. The government already broke its toll abolition promise — paying RM591 million just to freeze hikes rather than remove tolls. A temporary toll waiver during a war-driven energy crisis is basic economic relief. Singapore waived ERP during COVID disruptions. Malaysia won’t even pause toll collection while fuel prices spike.

3. Impose emergency price controls on essential food items. Freight costs from Hormuz disruptions are already feeding into import prices. Cooking oil, flour, rice, and protein prices will follow. The Price Control and Anti-Profiteering Act 2011 gives the government power to gazette maximum prices for essential goods. Anwar used this power selectively before. Now, when a global war is about to hit Malaysian dinner tables, silence.

4. Freeze non-essential GLC and political appointment spending. The IDEAS report found 238 political appointments to FSBs and GLCs under Anwar. Every one of those comes with salaries, allowances, and perks funded by public money. During a war-driven fiscal crunch — with the subsidy bill ballooning and oil revenue uncertain — a responsible government would freeze all discretionary patronage spending and redirect it to rakyat relief. Instead, the appointments continue.

📜 Previous Cases

During COVID-19, the Muhyiddin government — for all its democratic illegitimacy — implemented a blanket WFH directive, moratoriums on loan repayments, cash handouts (BPN), and wage subsidies within weeks of the crisis hitting. The Ismail Sabri government continued with hybrid WFH arrangements and extended moratoriums. These were imperfect governments acting under crisis pressure. Anwar, who controls both the PM’s office and the Finance Ministry, has more concentrated fiscal power than either predecessor — and has used less of it in response to a war that is already hitting Malaysian wallets harder than the early weeks of COVID hit fuel prices. The precedent exists. The tools exist. The power exists. The will does not.

🎯 PH Callout

You told us you were different. You told us Madani meant the people come first. You told us having the PM also serve as Finance Minister would mean faster, more decisive economic management. So where is it? Oil is past $100. The Strait of Hormuz is disrupted. Your own deputy economy minister admits growth projections are shaky. Your own mouth admitted the RON95 subsidy has weeks left. And your crisis response is… a parliamentary motion about Iran and a tweet about solidarity? Mandate WFH. Freeze tolls. Cap food prices. Cut the GLC fat. You have the power. You have the precedent. You have the tools. Use them — or admit that ‘Ekonomi Madani’ was never about protecting the rakyat. It was about protecting the coalition.

PH Backbenchers Call for Snap Election Dressed Up as 'Reform' — Hold GE16 With All State Polls, Save Money, They Say

Said by: Azli Yusof (Amanah-Shah Alam) / Jimmy Puah (PKR-Tebrau)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, two Pakatan Harapan backbenchers — Shah Alam MP Azli Yusof (Amanah) and Tebrau MP Jimmy Puah (PKR) — called for all state assembly elections to be held concurrently with the 16th General Election. Speaking to FMT, both MPs argued that separate elections lead to lower voter turnout, waste public money, and produce state governments with weaker mandates. Azli said separate polls are “unfair to voters, especially those who live far away from where they vote.” Puah called it “simply too tiring for people to come out and vote twice.” Putrajaya spent RM725 million on GE15 in 2022 and a further RM420 million on six subsequent state elections in 2023.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a neutral policy suggestion. It is PH’s latest move in a coordinated signal — following DAP secretary-general Anthony Loke’s identical proposal last year and PH Johor chairman Aminolhuda Hassan’s recent push — to align the political calendar for a snap general election. By calling for simultaneous polls, PH backbenchers are doing the government’s messaging work: normalizing the idea of an early dissolution while packaging it as cost-saving reform. The real audience is not voters who are tired of going to polling stations — it is UMNO, GPS, and other coalition partners who need to be brought on board before the PM pulls the trigger.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH did not care about “voter fatigue” when it suited them. In 2022, six PH-governed states chose to delay their state elections rather than hold them with GE15, citing monsoon concerns. Now, suddenly, the same coalition wants everything held together — but only because the timing benefits them. The opposition is fractured after the Bersatu-PAS split, the economy is holding, and Anwar’s approval is still intact. This is not reform. This is election engineering with a reformist paint job. If PH were serious about alignment, they would legislate fixed election dates — not send backbenchers to float trial balloons in FMT.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has a long history of strategic election timing. Mahathir called snap elections when conditions favoured him. Najib dissolved Parliament early in 2013 and 2018. Even within the current cycle, the Sabah, Melaka, and Johor snap elections of 2020–2022 were all called for political advantage, not democratic principle. PH itself benefited from the chaos of misaligned polls — the August 2023 state elections became a de facto referendum on the unity government. The pattern is always the same: whoever controls the timing controls the narrative.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised institutional reform. You promised that governance would no longer be about political convenience. Yet here you are, using backbenchers to soften the ground for a snap election while pretending it is about saving the rakyat money. If you actually believe in synchronized elections, table a constitutional amendment to fix election dates — the way mature democracies do. But you will not, because the whole point is to keep the power to dissolve in the PM’s pocket. Stop dressing up electoral strategy as reform. Malaysians can tell the difference.

Police Question C4 Anti-Corruption CEO Under Section 124B — Anwar's Government Uses 'Plot to Topple' Probe to Interrogate Civil Society

Said by: Bukit Aman (Royal Malaysia Police) / C4 Center (Pushpan Murugiah, CEO)

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 10, 2026, the Center to Combat Corruption and Cronyism (C4) CEO Pushpan Murugiah was summoned to Bukit Aman and questioned for approximately 90 minutes under Section 124B of the Penal Code — activities detrimental to parliamentary democracy. The questioning was linked to the police investigation into an alleged plot to topple the government, reportedly because Pushpan’s name appeared in an email deemed relevant to the probe. But the interrogation went far beyond that email. Police questioned C4’s sources of funding, whether the organisation had received instructions to issue statements, and — remarkably — challenged C4’s right to comment on matters of national governance. C4 confirmed that several other political commentators and academics had also been summoned to Bukit Aman under the same investigation.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is not a routine police inquiry. This is the machinery of state — operating under a government that came to power on a reform mandate — being deployed to scrutinise, pressure, and delegitimise an independent anti-corruption watchdog. Section 124B is a serious charge. Using it to question a civil society leader about their organisation’s funding, autonomy, and standing to comment on public affairs is a textbook chilling effect. It signals to every NGO, academic, and political commentator in Malaysia: criticise the government’s handling of the MACC scandal, and you may find yourself in a police interview room explaining why you think you have the right to speak. This is the same playbook that Malaysians rejected when BN did it. The fact that PH is now running it should alarm every voter who believed the reformasi promise.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar Ibrahim built his entire political career on the claim that he was persecuted by a government that weaponised institutions against critics. He spent years in prison. He rallied millions with the promise that under PH, the police would not be used to silence dissent. Now his government is hauling in the CEO of an anti-corruption NGO and asking — with a straight face — whether C4 has the standing to comment on corruption. The hypocrisy is breathtaking. C4 has been non-partisan since 2014. It criticised BN, it criticised PN, and now it criticises PH — and this is the administration that sends the police. The “plot to topple the government” narrative has become a catch-all justification for intimidating anyone who asks uncomfortable questions about Azam Baki, the MACC, and the corporate mafia allegations. If your anti-corruption credentials can’t survive scrutiny from an anti-corruption NGO, the problem is not the NGO.

📜 Previous Cases

This pattern is not new in Malaysian politics — it is simply new for PH. Under Najib, civil society leaders and journalists were routinely summoned under the Sedition Act, SOSMA, and the Communications and Multimedia Act for criticising 1MDB and government corruption. Under Muhyiddin, the Emergency Ordinance was used to suspend Parliament and silence dissent. Anwar himself was the most prominent victim of institutional weaponisation — twice jailed on charges widely seen as politically motivated. Now, under his own government, Section 124B is being stretched to cover the activities of NGOs whose only offence is demanding accountability from the MACC. Bersih was told to stop criticising MACC “during Ramadan.” DAP’s own youth wing warned of a “climate of fear.” And now C4’s CEO is sitting in Bukit Aman explaining why an anti-corruption organisation dares to talk about corruption.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised institutional independence. You promised that the police would never again be used as a political weapon. Now your police are questioning the CEO of an anti-corruption NGO about their right to speak — under the same “plot to topple” investigation that you’ve used to smear Daim’s family, attack Bloomberg, and deflect from the Azam Baki scandal. Every hour that Anwar’s government spends intimidating civil society instead of reforming the MACC is another hour that proves the Madani brand is hollow. If you want Malaysians to believe in your reform agenda, start by not behaving exactly like the governments you replaced.

Sabah Rejected PH at the Ballot Box — Now Mustapha Sakmud Says He'll Be Their Voice in Putrajaya Anyway

Said by: Mustapha Sakmud, Sabah PH Chairman / Sabah and Sarawak Affairs Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-11

📌 What Happened

On March 11, 2026, Sabah PH chairman and Sabah and Sarawak Affairs Minister Mustapha Sakmud told FMT that Sabah PH will serve as a “conduit” to ensure state issues are voiced at the federal level. He said he intends to leverage his ministerial appointment — made on February 15 — to bring Sabahan concerns to Cabinet meetings and government agencies. “This is the role we aim to play, and we hope the people of Sabah will appreciate it when their fundamental needs are addressed,” he said.

🔥 Why It Matters

PH was annihilated in the November 29, 2025 Sabah state election. PKR scraped through with a single seat. DAP won nothing. GRS took 29 seats, Warisan took 25, and Sabahans made it abundantly clear they did not want PH representing them at the state level. Yet barely three months later, Anwar handed Mustapha a federal ministerial portfolio — Sabah and Sarawak Affairs — effectively using the federal Cabinet to insert PH into Sabah’s political space through the back door. This is not democratic representation. This is patronage as a substitute for a mandate.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

You cannot lose an election this badly and then claim you are the people’s voice. PH won one seat in the entire Sabah state assembly. That is not a conduit — that is a rejection letter. Mustapha’s argument boils down to: “Sabahans didn’t vote for us, but we got a Cabinet post, so we’ll represent them anyway.” This is the exact kind of unearned political entitlement PH once accused BN of practising. The appointment rewards loyalty to Anwar, not service to Sabah. If Sabahans wanted PH to speak for them, they would have voted for PH. They didn’t.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows a long Malaysian tradition of using federal appointments to compensate for electoral losses at the state level. BN did it for decades in Sabah and Sarawak — parachuting federal figures into state politics through GLCs, agencies, and development corridors. PH, in opposition, railed against this exact practice. Now in government, they’ve adopted the same playbook. Anwar’s December 2025 Cabinet reshuffle — which created the Sabah and Sarawak Affairs portfolio — was widely seen as a damage-control exercise after PH’s Sabah wipeout. The pattern is clear: when voters reject you, buy your way back in through Putrajaya.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reformasi. You promised that power would come from the people, not from Putrajaya. You told Sabahans for years that BN was using federal patronage to override their democratic choices. And now you’ve handed a Cabinet seat to a man whose coalition won one seat in an entire state election and told him to go represent the people who rejected him. If “the people of Sabah will appreciate it,” as Mustapha claims, then put it to a test — contest a by-election in Sabah on PH’s record. Otherwise, stop pretending a ministerial appointment is a mandate.

🎯 Teguran PH

Tetapi apabila Bloomberg mendedahkan cerita antirasuah terbesar dalam politik Malaysia sejak 1MDB, naluri pertama anda adalah melindungi PM, bukan orang awam. Tuntutan RCI yang datang berminggu lewat dan sengaja mengelak peranan PM bukan reformasi — ia teater. Jika anda mahu buktikan PAS salah, mulakan dengan menangani dakwaan Bloomberg tentang campur tangan Anwar dalam SPRM.

[BM] Dzulkefly Janji — Sekali Lagi — Nak Banteras Buli Tempat Kerja Di Hospital Kerajaan

Said by: Dzulkefly Ahmad, Health Minister

Category: Health

Date: 2026-03-10

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Menteri Kesihatan Dzulkefly Ahmad mengumumkan pada 10 Mac 2026 bahawa Kementerian Kesihatan akan “memperkukuh penguatkuasaan” terhadap buli tempat kerja di kemudahan kesihatan kerajaan. Beliau berkata hospital awam sepatutnya menjadi “persekitaran kerja yang profesional dan beretika” dan merujuk kepada pekeliling Jabatan Perkhidmatan Awam (JPA) yang melarang bahasa kasar dan penderaan lisan di tempat kerja. Beliau berjanji untuk “mempertingkatkan pelaksanaan” pekeliling tersebut.

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Ini bukan pengumuman baru. Dzulkefly telah membuat janji yang hampir sama sejak sekurang-kurangnya Ogos 2025, apabila Kementerian Kesihatan merekodkan 430 aduan buli dengan hanya empat di bawah siasatan. Beliau melancarkan ikrar “toleransi sifar,” mengumumkan garis panduan baru yang mengklasifikasikan ancaman lisan dan ejekan sebagai buli (sepatutnya pada Oktober 2025), dan juga mengancam akan memecat pegawai kanan yang menyembunyikan kes. Beberapa bulan kemudian, beliau kembali ke podium mengatakan perkara yang sama — yang memberitahu anda segala-galanya tentang sama ada janji sebelum ini membuahkan hasil.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

Apabila seorang menteri mengumumkan dasar yang sama tiga kali dalam setahun, pengumuman itu adalah dasarnya — bukan penguatkuasaan. Buku panduan Dzulkefly adalah tadbir urus PH klasik: kenal pasti masalah sebenar, adakan sidang akhbar, keluarkan pekeliling, kemudian tidak buat apa-apa sehingga pusingan akhbar buruk seterusnya memaksa sidang akhbar lain. Pekeliling yang dirujuknya sudah wujud. Garis panduan sudah dilancarkan. Pendirian toleransi sifar sudah diisytiharkan. Jika mana-mana langkah itu berkesan, beliau tidak perlu berdiri pada Mac 2026 berjanji “mempertingkatkan pelaksanaan.”

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Pada Ogos 2025, menteri yang sama menjadi tajuk berita kerana mengisytiharkan toleransi sifar terhadap buli selepas kes “Dr S,” seorang pegawai perubatan siswazah di Sabah yang didakwa dibuli sehingga terkurung di bilik selama bertahun-tahun sementara pelakunya berpindah ke luar negara menjalani latihan pakar. Pada Julai 2025, kementerian mengumumkan garis panduan baru mengklasifikasikan penghinaan, ancaman, dan ejekan sebagai buli tempat kerja.

🎯 Teguran PH

Kerajaan Madani Anwar berjanji reformasi institusi dan kerajaan yang berfungsi untuk rakyat. Dzulkefly adalah menteri kanan PH dalam portfolio kritikal, dan yang terbaik beliau boleh tawarkan selepas tiga tahun berkuasa ialah mengumumkan semula pekeliling yang sama dan memanggilnya kemajuan. Doktor junior — tulang belakang sistem kesihatan awam Malaysia — layak mendapat lebih daripada kenyataan akhbar kitar semula.

[BM] Penduduk Kampung Lee Kong Chian 99 Tahun Disuruh Keluar — RM50K Sekepala, Hannah Yeoh 'Tak Dapat Hadir'

Said by: Lo Su Fui, Deputy Minister (Federal Territories) / Hannah Yeoh, Minister (Federal Territories)

Category: Housing

Date: 2026-03-10

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Lebih 150 penduduk Kampung Lee Kong Chian di Setapak — satu-satunya kampung Cina swasta yang masih tinggal di Kuala Lumpur, ditubuhkan pada 1927 sebagai kuarters pekerja Kumpulan Getah Lee — mengadakan perhimpunan aman pada 8 Mac 2026 selepas 146 isi rumah menerima notis pengusiran pada November 2025 yang mengarahkan mereka mengosongkan premis menjelang September. Tanah telah dijual kepada syarikat swasta. Penduduk ditawarkan pampasan RM50,000 setiap isi rumah, yang mereka tolak, menuntut unit Projek Perumahan Rakyat (PPR) sebagai ganti. Menteri Wilayah Persekutuan Hannah Yeoh tidak hadir pada sambutan Chap Goh Meh terakhir kampung pada 10 Mac; Timbalan Menteri Lo Su Fui muncul, menyerahkan RM10,000 untuk jamuan makan malam, dan berkata beliau akan “membawa perkara ini kembali ke Jabatan Wilayah Persekutuan.”

🔥 Mengapa Ia Penting

Ini adalah kes klasik perpindahan bandar yang berpakaian transaksi pasaran. Kampung Lee Kong Chian bukan penempatan setinggan — ketua kampung Tan Lai Kee menegaskan rumah-rumah dibina dengan kelulusan rasmi pemilik tanah asal. Ini adalah 230 isi rumah, ramai daripada mereka keturunan pekerja yang membina kehidupan mereka di sekitar kilang Getah Lee hampir satu abad lalu. Apabila kerajaan membenarkan penjualan swasta menghapuskan komuniti lebih 1,000 orang dengan notis enam bulan dan cek RM50,000 — lebih kurang harga Myvi terpakai — ia memberitahu anda dengan tepat di mana rakyat biasa berada dalam hierarki keutamaan pembangunan.

💩 Mengapa Ia Karut

PH bertanding sebagai gabungan yang akan melindungi komuniti daripada pemaju rakus dan kronisme. Anwar sendiri berulang kali bercakap tentang kemampuan perumahan, tadbir urus mengutamakan rakyat, dan maruah rakyat. Jadi apabila kampung berusia 99 tahun — dengan perumahan berdokumen dan sah yang dibina di bawah kelulusan rasmi — diserahkan notis pengusiran enam bulan dan tawaran pampasan yang tidak mampu menampung deposit flat di Setapak, di mana kerajaan reformis? Hannah Yeoh menghantar timbalan dengan cek RM10,000 untuk jamuan makan malam. Itu bukan tadbir urus — itu corak menunggu.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini mengikuti corak yang sama seperti pengusiran Kampung Sungai Baru pada 2025. Kampung Baru KL — enklaf Melayu terakhir di pusat bandar — telah berdepan ancaman pembangunan semula selama berdekad. Kerajaan PH juga menolak Rang Undang-Undang Pembaharuan Bandar Nga Kor Ming, yang akan memberikan kuasa menteri yang luas ke atas pemerolehan harta — rang undang-undang yang begitu kontroversi sehingga ahli parlimen belakang PH sendiri memberontak.

🎯 Teguran PH

Anda berjanji Malaysia Madani — Malaysia yang bertamadun dan penyayang. Kampung berusia 99 tahun dengan 230 keluarga, keturunan pekerja yang membina bandar ini, disuruh keluar dengan RM50,000 dan jabat tangan. Menteri Wilayah Persekutuan anda tidak pun hadir. Jika penduduk ini tidak layak mendapat perumahan ganti PPR — program yang kerajaan anda sendiri mentadbir — berhentilah berpura-pura anda mentadbir untuk rakyat.

99-Year-Old Kampung Lee Kong Chian Residents Told to Get Out — RM50K Per Family, Hannah Yeoh 'Unable to Attend'

Said by: Lo Su Fui, Deputy Minister (Federal Territories) / Hannah Yeoh, Minister (Federal Territories)

Category: Housing

Date: 2026-03-10

📌 What Happened

More than 150 residents of Kampung Lee Kong Chian in Setapak — Kuala Lumpur’s sole surviving private Chinese village, established in 1927 as quarters for Lee Rubber Group factory workers — held a peaceful rally on 8 March 2026 after 146 households received eviction notices in November 2025 ordering them to vacate by September. The land has been sold to a private company. Residents were offered RM50,000 per household in compensation, which they rejected, demanding People’s Housing Project (PPR) units instead. Federal Territories Minister Hannah Yeoh was absent from the village’s final Chap Goh Meh celebration on 10 March; Deputy Minister Lo Su Fui showed up, handed over RM10,000 for the dinner, and said he would “bring this matter back to the Federal Territories Department to explore how we can best provide assistance.” The government’s official position, per NST, is that it will “review the situation before deciding whether to intervene.”

🔥 Why It Matters

This is a textbook case of urban displacement dressed up as market transaction. Kampung Lee Kong Chian is not a squatter settlement — village chief Tan Lai Kee stressed the houses were built with formal approval from the original landowner. These are 230 households, many of them descendants of workers who built their lives around the Lee Rubber factory nearly a century ago. When the government allows a private sale to erase a community of over 1,000 people with six months’ notice and a RM50,000 cheque — roughly the price of a used Myvi — it tells you exactly where ordinary Malaysians sit in the hierarchy of development priorities. The Federal Territories Ministry, under PH’s Hannah Yeoh, is the body with direct jurisdiction over KL housing and land disputes. Yet the minister was absent, and the government’s posture is to “review” and “explore” — the bureaucratic language of stalling while the clock runs out on residents.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH campaigned as the coalition that would protect communities from rapacious developers and crony capitalism. Anwar himself has spoken repeatedly about housing affordability, people-first governance, and the dignity of the rakyat. So when a 99-year-old village — with documented, legitimate housing built under formal approval — is handed a six-month eviction notice and a compensation offer that wouldn’t cover a deposit on a flat in Setapak, where is the reformist government? Hannah Yeoh sent a deputy with a RM10,000 dinner cheque. The deputy said he’d “bring this back” to the department. That’s not governance — that’s a holding pattern. If PH cannot protect a century-old KL community with clear historical legitimacy from being bulldozed by a private developer, then every “people first” slogan they’ve ever uttered is decoration.

📜 Previous Cases

This follows the same pattern as the Kampung Sungai Baru evictions in 2025, where residents were forced out amid redevelopment pressure with inadequate compensation and minimal government intervention. KL’s Kampung Baru — the last Malay enclave in the city centre — has faced decades of similar redevelopment threats, with successive governments oscillating between grand masterplans and inaction. The PH government has also pushed Nga Kor Ming’s Urban Renewal Bill, which would grant sweeping ministerial powers over property acquisition — a bill so controversial that even PH’s own backbenchers revolted. The pattern is consistent: communities with deep roots but no political leverage get treated as obstacles to development, while the government oscillates between performative sympathy and institutional passivity.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysia Madani — a civilised, compassionate Malaysia. A 99-year-old village with 230 families, descendants of workers who built this city, is being told to leave with RM50,000 and a handshake. Your Federal Territories Minister didn’t even show up to the last Chap Goh Meh these families will ever celebrate in their homes. You sent a deputy with dinner money. If this is what “reviewing the situation” looks like, then your review is a eulogy. These residents are not asking for handouts — they’re asking for replacement housing under PPR, a programme your own government administers. Either use the power you have to intervene, or stop pretending you govern for the people.
