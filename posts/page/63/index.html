KFC Closes 100+ Outlets Amid Pro-Palestine Boycott — Government Watches From the Sidelines

Said by: QSR Brands Holdings (KFC operator)

Category: Economy

Date: 2024-05-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In May 2024, KFC Malaysia temporarily closed over 100 outlets amid boycotts linked to the Gaza conflict. Thousands of Malaysian workers — mostly low-income B40 families — faced reduced hours or outright job losses. McDonald’s global sales fell 0.1% in 2024 and 1% in Q1 2025, driven largely by boycotts in Muslim-majority countries including Malaysia. The boycott movement was fueled by social media activists and opposition politicians — particularly PAS and PN — who framed consumption at Western fast-food chains as complicity in the Gaza conflict. The government’s response was conspicuously, strategically absent — no minister endorsed or opposed the boycott.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: The government’s silence was calculated cowardice. Supporting the boycott would anger investors, franchise operators, and the business community. Opposing it would anger the Malay-Muslim base and hand PN a political weapon. So the government said nothing while thousands of its own citizens lost income. QSR Brands — the KFC operator — is a Malaysian-owned company that employs Malaysian workers, pays Malaysian taxes, and sources from Malaysian suppliers. The boycott punished Malaysian businesses and workers for the perceived sins of a foreign brand.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: No government relief programme was targeted at affected F&B workers. Madani’s promise to prioritise B40 welfare evaporated the moment it conflicted with political convenience. These workers — cashiers, cooks, delivery riders — were collateral damage in a geopolitical conflict they had no role in. Opposition politicians who championed the boycott faced no scrutiny for the economic damage they encouraged. PAS MPs who called for boycotts offered no alternative employment plan for displaced workers. The government, by staying silent, let the opposition set the narrative and absorbed none of the accountability.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Consumer boycotts in Malaysia have historically been driven by ethno-religious mobilisation rather than economic analysis. The 1969 “Buy British Last” campaign, various anti-Chinese boycotts, and periodic anti-Israel product campaigns have all followed the same pattern: moral outrage drives consumer action, workers bear the cost, and politicians exploit the sentiment without managing the consequences. Moral clarity on Palestine does not require destroying the livelihoods of your own citizens. A responsible government would have separated legitimate solidarity from economic self-harm.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Thousands of B40 workers lost income because you were too afraid to take a position. Supporting Palestine is a moral imperative. Destroying Malaysian livelihoods is not. You could have funded Palestinian aid while protecting Malaysian workers. You could have spoken clearly: “boycott is your right, but these are Malaysian businesses employing Malaysian families.” Instead, you chose silence — the path of least political resistance. Cowardice is not governance.

Press Freedom Plunges 34 Spots to 107th — 'Difficult Situation' Under PH

Said by: Reporters Without Borders (RSF)

Category: Social

Date: 2024-05-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In May 2024, Reporters Without Borders (RSF) released the 2024 World Press Freedom Index, in which Malaysia plunged 34 places — from 73rd to 107th — under the PH government. RSF classified Malaysia as a “difficult situation” for press freedom. The collapse was driven by sedition charges against journalists and activists, MCMC content blocking, social media licensing requirements, and the arrest of FMT journalist Rex Tan. Even after a partial recovery to 88th in 2025, Malaysia still trails its 2023 ranking under PH.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: A 34-place drop in press freedom under a government that promised media reform is not a statistical fluctuation — it is an indictment. PH’s 2022 manifesto included commitments to repeal the Sedition Act, reform the Printing Presses and Publications Act, and protect press freedom. Instead, under PH, journalists have been arrested, news sites blocked, and a social media licensing regime introduced. RSF’s “difficult situation” classification places Malaysia alongside countries PH claims to be morally superior to. The ranking reflects not just specific incidents but a systemic deterioration in the media environment.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s response to the ranking drop was to blame “inherited problems” and promise improvement. But the specific incidents RSF cited — Chegubard’s sedition charge, Rex Tan’s arrest, MCMC blocking MalaysiaNow and Asia Sentinel — all occurred under PH’s watch. These are not inherited problems; they are PH-created problems. The government that promised to protect journalists is now prosecuting them. The government that promised to unblock websites is now blocking more. Every press freedom commitment has been reversed.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Under Najib, Malaysia’s press freedom ranking hovered around 140th — driven by 1MDB-related media suppression, the blocking of Sarawak Report, and sedition charges against editors. PH 1.0 achieved a dramatic improvement to 101st in 2019. PH 2.0 initially maintained the improvement before the 2024 collapse. The trajectory is clear: PH delivers press freedom gains in opposition and early government, then reverts to suppression once media scrutiny becomes inconvenient. The 34-place drop happened in a single year — suggesting a deliberate policy shift, not gradual decline.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You dropped 34 places in press freedom in a single year. RSF calls Malaysia a “difficult situation.” Your manifesto promised media freedom. Your government delivered media suppression. Unblock MalaysiaNow and Asia Sentinel. Drop charges against journalists. Repeal the Sedition Act as promised. Withdraw the social media licensing regime. Or keep falling in the rankings — and stop pretending Malaysia is a democracy when the world’s press freedom watchdog classifies it as difficult.

PH promised to break this pattern. Instead, Anwar’s response to Forest City reporting was indistinguishable from his predecessors: threaten jail, charge the critic, and hope the story dies.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: The correct response to false allegations is evidence, not threats. If there’s no casino planned at Forest City, publish the SFZ regulations and prove it. If there is, own it and defend the policy. But threatening jail for reporters asking questions about a publicly funded special zone is authoritarian — and you know it, because you spent 25 years on the receiving end of exactly this tactic. Stop threatening journalists. Start answering questions.

Sebabnya berlapis: kesulitan kewangan yang memaksa remaja ke alam pekerjaan, perjuangan kesihatan mental, kekecewaan dengan sistem pendidikan yang dianggap tidak relevan, dan perasaan yang semakin meningkat bahawa ijazah Malaysia mungkin tidak membawa kepada pekerjaan yang bermakna memandangkan 12.28% pengangguran belia.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: Kerajaan memperuntukkan perkongsian bajet yang semakin merosot kepada pendidikan — turun kepada 4.3% untuk pendidikan tinggi pada 2025 daripada 6.8% pada 2010. Apabila Menteri Pendidikan melaporkan 7,681 kes buli dan peningkatan pengunduran pelajar, respons sepatutnya adalah reformasi struktur, bukan pelan komunikasi. Tetapi reformasi struktur memerlukan wang dan kehendak politik — kedua-duanya diarahkan ke tempat lain. Bajet perkhidmatan awam berkembang setiap tahun. Bajet pendidikan menyusut. Apabila keutamaan bajet anda memberitahu pelajar mereka kurang penting daripada birokrat, pelajar merespons dengan pergi.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Keciciran dan pengunduran pelajar telah meningkat di Malaysia selama bertahun. Pandemik COVID mempercepatkan trend — beribu pelajar dari keluarga B40 tidak pernah kembali ke sekolah selepas penutupan. Respons kerajaan adalah program dan inisiatif yang menjana kenyataan akhbar tetapi tidak menangani punca: kemiskinan, kurikulum yang tidak relevan, kualiti guru, dan sistem kelayakan yang majikan tidak hargai. Setiap menteri pendidikan mengumumkan “transformasi.” Tiada yang mentransformasikan apa-apa.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Sepuluh ribu pelajar berputus asa dengan sistem pendidikan anda. Mereka tidak gagal — mereka memutuskan ia tidak layak untuk dihadiri. Apabila bajet anda untuk pendidikan terus menyusut sementara bajet untuk gaji perkhidmatan awam terus berkembang, anda memberitahu generasi seterusnya tepat di mana keutamaan anda. Dan mereka merespons dengan pergi. Balikkan kemerosotan bajet pendidikan. Reformasi kurikulum untuk relevansi. Tangani buli sebagai krisis sistemik, bukan statistik. Atau terus kehilangan pelajar — dan tertanya-tanya mengapa penghijrahan otak bermula sebelum mereka pun menamatkan pengajian.
