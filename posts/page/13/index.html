Anwar's 'Perfect Storm': Anti-Graft Crisis, Party Fractures, Coalition Jitters

Said by: CNA Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-07

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Multiple challenges are converging on Anwar Ibrahim that may force him to call early elections nearly two years ahead of the February 2028 deadline. An anti-graft crisis at MACC (Bloomberg exposés on Azam Baki), PKR internal fractures following Rafizi Ramli’s departure from cabinet, and coalition partners openly reconsidering their ties. CNA described it as a “perfect storm” of political instability. UMNO’s secret Bangkok meeting with opposition parties, DAP reviewing coalition ties, and the PKR factional war all threaten to bring down the Unity Government before its term ends.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: When CNA uses the phrase “perfect storm” to describe your political situation, you are in serious trouble. The convergence of MACC credibility collapse, PKR internal warfare, and coalition instability is unprecedented in Anwar’s tenure. Each crisis alone would be manageable; together, they threaten the government’s survival. If early elections are called, PH would face voters with most manifesto promises unmet, corruption perceptions unchanged, and the cost of living still rising. The political capital Anwar accumulated through years of struggle has been spent on coalition survival rather than reform delivery.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Anwar’s response to every crisis has been to consolidate power rather than address root causes. MACC under fire? Defend Azam Baki. Rafizi raising legitimate concerns? Sideline him. DAP unhappy? Reshuffle them into weaker portfolios. Bersih criticising? Call them unfair. Consolidation only works when you have political capital to spend — and that capital is depleting fast. A PM who spends all his energy managing crises he created through inaction has no energy left for governance.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: PH 1.0 collapsed in 2020 through the Sheraton Move — also triggered by coalition instability and internal betrayal. Muhyiddin’s government fell in 2021 due to loss of confidence. Ismail Sabri lasted barely a year. Malaysia has had five PMs in eight years. The pattern of coalition governments imploding under internal pressure is not new — what’s new is that PH was supposed to break this cycle. Instead, it’s repeating it.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You came to power promising stability and reform. Three years later, your coalition is fracturing, your anti-corruption agency is under fire, your own party is split, and analysts are talking about snap elections. This is not the “stability” you promised. Fix the MACC. Reconcile with Rafizi. Deliver on your manifesto. Or call elections and let the rakyat decide if three years of broken promises deserve a second term.

Rafizi Tells PH Voters to 'Wait Till June' — Hints at Breaking Away from PKR and 'Sailing His Own Ship'

Said by: Rafizi Ramli, Pandan MP / Former Economy Minister

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-07

📌 What Happened

Former Economy Minister Rafizi Ramli, who quit the cabinet in May 2025 after losing the PKR deputy presidency contest, told Pakatan Harapan supporters in a Sin Chew Daily interview to “wait till June,” hinting that a new political option would be unveiled. Rafizi said he and his allies are open to “sailing their own ship” and are exploring “different political configurations” — which could involve a new party, coalition, or political platform. He confirmed he will defend his Pandan parliamentary seat in the next general election but definitively not under the PKR banner. Separately, Reuters reported that several PKR lawmakers are considering contesting as independents, having lost confidence in Anwar’s leadership.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the most serious internal threat to Pakatan Harapan since the Sheraton Move. Rafizi isn’t a backbencher — he’s the most popular reformist figure in Malaysian politics among younger voters, the man who exposed the NFC and 1MDB scandals, and the architect of the “Ayuh Malaysia” campaign that helped deliver GE15 for Anwar. If Rafizi launches a third-force vehicle in Peninsular Malaysia — potentially aligned with Shafie Apdal’s Parti Warisan, which already swept Chinese votes in Sabah — it could split PH’s urban and reform-minded voter base right before a general election that sources say could come as early as late 2026.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Not Rafizi’s move — Anwar’s response to it. Instead of addressing the legitimate grievances that pushed Rafizi out (the rigged party election, the refusal to act on Azam Baki, the abandonment of reform), Anwar’s camp has reportedly weaponized MACC to harass Rafizi’s former aide James Chai and used government machinery to undermine the Pandan MP. The Prime Minister who built his career on reformasi is now using the exact same playbook Mahathir and Najib used against him. The man who once stood trial for challenging his boss is now the boss who punishes anyone who challenges him.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysian politics has seen this movie before. In 1998, Anwar himself was the reformist insider who broke away and formed a movement that eventually became PKR. In 2015, Muhyiddin Yassin was sacked for questioning Najib over 1MDB and later formed Bersatu. The pattern is consistent: Malaysian prime ministers treat internal dissent as treason rather than accountability. What makes this case uniquely damaging for PH is that Rafizi’s departure was entirely self-inflicted by Anwar — he reverted PKR’s party elections from a direct member vote to a delegate system specifically to remove Rafizi from the deputy president position, prioritizing dynastic loyalty over competence.

🎯 PH Callout

Anwar, you made Rafizi. He exposed the scandals you campaigned on. He built the ground game that got you to Putrajaya. And you repaid him by rigging your party’s internal elections, sidelining him from cabinet, and siccing the anti-corruption agency on his people. Now he’s building an alternative — and you’re terrified, because you know better than anyone what happens when a popular reformist breaks away from a corrupt establishment. You wrote that playbook in 1998. The difference is: Rafizi has data, youth support, and the receipts. You just have the machinery. And machinery didn’t save Najib either.

[BM] 238 Pelantikan Politik Ke FSB Dan GLC Di Bawah Anwar — Laporan IDEAS Dedah 'Kenalan Mengatasi Kecekapan'

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Daripada jumlah itu, 222 kepada FSB dan 16 kepada GLC. Laporan itu menyemak 135 agensi dan mendapati 78% jawatan pengerusi diisi berdasarkan kenalan politik dan bukannya kecekapan.

🔥 Kenapa Ia Penting

Laporan ini adalah dakwaan langsung, disokong data, terhadap kelayakan reformasi PH. Anwar datang berkuasa menjanjikan meritokrasi, ketelusan, dan menamatkan politik naungan yang mendefinisikan tadbir urus era BN. Tiga tahun kemudian, badan pemikir bebas dan dihormati telah mendokumenkan bahawa pelantikan politik bukan sahaja berterusan — ia mendominasi. Apabila 78% orang yang menjalankan badan berkanun anda mendapat jawatan melalui kenalan dan bukannya kelayakan, anda bukan mereformasi tadbir urus. Anda menukar nama pada senarai naungan.

💩 Kenapa Ia Karut

PH menjual pengundi idea bahawa mereka secara asasnya berbeza daripada UMNO dan BN. Bahawa era pengerusi yang berkaitan politik mengetuai agensi kerajaan sudah berakhir. Angka IDEAS mengatakan sebaliknya: 238 pelantikan dalam kurang daripada tiga tahun, dengan majoriti besar didorong oleh afiliasi politik dan bukannya merit profesional. Ini bukan reformasi — ia putaran. Gabungan berbeza, buku panduan sama. Satu-satunya yang berubah ialah logo parti pada surat pelantikan.

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Pelantikan politik kepada badan berkanun dan GLC telah menjadi ciri berulang tadbir urus Malaysia selama berdekad. Di bawah Najib Razak, amalan ini dikritik meluas — terutamanya di 1MDB, Tabung Haji, dan FELDA, di mana pengerusi berkaitan politik mengetuai salah urus dan rasuah terang-terangan. Manifesto PH 2018 secara eksplisit berjanji menamatkan ini.

🎯 Panggilan PH

Anda menjanjikan rakyat Malaysia Malaysia baru. Anda berkata era kroni politik sudah berakhir. IDEAS baru meletakkan angka pada janji itu: 238 pelantikan politik, 78% didorong politik, bukan merit. Itu bukan reformasi — itu perniagaan seperti biasa dengan penjenamaan lebih baik. Jika PH tidak mampu membersihkan proses pelantikan untuk badan berkanun — reformasi tadbir urus asas tanpa halangan undang-undang — mengapa sesiapa patut percaya janji lebih besar tentang kebebasan institusi?

[BM] Anwar Akui Hanya Mampu Tahan Subsidi RON95 'Satu Atau Dua Bulan' — Sementara Harga Minyak Meletup Merata

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 6 Mac 2026, Perdana Menteri dan Menteri Kewangan Anwar Ibrahim memberitahu pemberita bahawa Malaysia boleh mengekalkan harga petrol RON95 bersubsidi pada RM1.99 seliter di bawah skim Budi95 — tetapi hanya untuk ‘satu atau dua bulan.’ Kata-kata tepat beliau: ‘Kami akan cuba mengawal kesan konflik Iran, termasuk untuk RON95, iaitu RM1.99 seliter. Kami akan memantau keadaan ini kerana setakat ini, kami masih boleh menahan untuk satu atau dua bulan.’ Ini datang pada minggu yang sama RON97 naik 10 sen ke RM3.25 seliter, RON95 tidak bersubsidi naik 8 sen ke RM2.67, dan diesel di Semenanjung naik 8 sen ke RM3.12.

🔥 Kenapa Ia Penting

Ini kali pertama Anwar secara terbuka meletakkan had masa terhadap keupayaan kerajaan menahan subsidi minyak. Bahasa berhati-hati — ‘kami akan cuba,’ ‘menahan,’ ‘memantau’ — adalah perubahan ketara daripada jaminan sebelumnya. Jika gangguan Selat Hormuz berterusan, dan 20% bekalan minyak dunia kekal berisiko, harga RM1.99 menjadi tidak mapan secara fiskal. Tetapi Anwar tidak mampu secara politik untuk menaikkan harga minyak — terutamanya dengan Hari Raya beberapa minggu lagi dan khabar angin pilihan raya kilat untuk Julai.

💩 Kenapa Ia Karut

Anwar menghabiskan tiga tahun memberitahu rakyat Malaysia bahawa sistem subsidi bersasar Budi95 adalah kepakaran reformasi fiskal — lebih bijak, lebih adil, dan lebih mapan daripada subsidi menyeluruh. Kini, saat harga minyak global benar-benar menguji sistem, beliau sudah berhati-hati dan berkata hanya mampu menahannya beberapa bulan. Apa jadi dengan penjimatan RM800 juta yang kerajaan dakwa daripada Budi95? Kebenaran ialah subsidi bersasar sentiasa direka untuk laut tenang. Saat kejutan sebenar melanda, seluruh sistem mula goyah. Dan bukannya pelan, kita dapat ‘kami akan cuba.’

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini corak berulang dengan Anwar mengenai dasar minyak: janji kestabilan, sampaikan ketidakpastian. Harga minyak terus merangkak naik sejak Januari 2026, dengan diesel sahaja naik dari RM2.84 pada pertengahan Januari ke RM3.12 menjelang awal Mac. Setiap minggu membawa pelarasan harga naik, sementara kerajaan bertegas keadaan ’terkawal.’

🎯 Panggilan PH

Anda beritahu rakyat Malaysia Budi95 adalah reformasi. Anda beritahu mereka subsidi bersasar akan melindungi rakyat sambil menjimatkan wang kerajaan. Kini anda mengakui, secara terbuka, bahawa sistem hanya mampu bertahan ‘satu atau dua bulan’ tekanan global sebenar. Mana pelan luar jangka? Mana perbualan jujur dengan orang ramai tentang apa yang berlaku jika minyak mencecah USD100 setong dan kekal? Berhenti menguruskan tajuk dan mula menguruskan ekonomi.

[BM] Anwar Bangga Pelaburan Diluluskan 'Rekod' RM426.7B — Tapi 'Diluluskan' Bukan Bermakna 'Tiba'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 Apa Yang Berlaku

Pada 6 Mac 2026, Perdana Menteri Anwar Ibrahim mengumumkan Malaysia mencatatkan pelaburan diluluskan tertinggi sebanyak RM426.7 bilion pada 2025 — peningkatan 11% daripada RM384.4 bilion pada 2024. Menurut MIDA, 8,390 projek diluluskan, dengan potensi mewujudkan 244,902 pekerjaan. Anwar membingkaikan ini sebagai pembenaran agenda reformasinya dan bukti keyakinan global terhadap hala tuju ekonomi Malaysia.

🔥 Kenapa Ia Penting

Kerana pelaburan diluluskan tidak sama dengan pelaburan direalisasikan, dan orang awam Malaysia telah disuapkan tajuk tanpa perbezaan ini selama berdekad. MIDA menjejak kelulusan — pada dasarnya, surat niat dan sijil pelaburan. Wang sebenar yang mengalir ke dalam ekonomi — pelaburan langsung asing (FDI) direalisasikan — secara sejarahnya 50-60% daripada angka yang diluluskan di Malaysia. Ini bermakna sehingga separuh daripada angka tajuk ini mungkin tidak pernah diterjemahkan kepada kilang, pekerjaan, atau aktiviti ekonomi sebenar.

💩 Kenapa Ia Karut

Mari kita tepat tentang apa yang berlaku: Perdana Menteri berdiri di podium dan meraikan angka yang beliau tahu tidak mencerminkan pelaburan sebenar. Setiap laporan tahunan MIDA membezakan antara pelaburan diluluskan dan direalisasikan. Setiap ahli ekonomi serius di Malaysia tahu jurang realisasi. Anwar — seorang yang telah berada dalam dan sekitar penggubalan dasar ekonomi Malaysia selama tiga dekad — tahu ini juga. Namun: tiada kadar realisasi didedahkan. Tiada pecahan berapa banyak pelaburan 2023 atau 2024 yang diluluskan sebenarnya terwujud. Tiada metrik akauntabiliti. Hanya angka terbesar, tajuk terbaik, dan dakwaan “keyakinan global.”

📜 Kes Terdahulu

Ini bukan baru. Setiap kerajaan Malaysia bermain permainan diluluskan-lawan-direalisasikan. Di bawah Najib, pelaburan diluluskan kerap melebihi RM200B+ setahun, tetapi FDI direalisasikan ketinggalan jauh di belakang. Coraknya sentiasa sama: umumkan angka terbesar yang mungkin di sidang akhbar, biar media siarkan tajuk, dan tidak pernah membuat susulan dengan apa yang sebenarnya berlaku.

🎯 Panggilan PH

Anda menjanjikan ketelusan dan akauntabiliti sebagai jenama anda. Jadi mana laporan realisasi? Mana pecahan berapa banyak pelaburan 2023 dan 2024 yang diluluskan sebenarnya muncul sebagai wang sebenar, kilang sebenar, dan pekerjaan sebenar? Jika anda mahu penghargaan untuk RM426.7 bilion, tunjukkan resit — bukan jemputan. Sehingga itu, ini teater ekonomi yang sama yang rakyat Malaysia tonton sejak 1990-an, hanya dengan cat reformis.

Sama ada beritahu kebenaran dan panggil pilihan raya, atau berhenti bersiap sedia untuk satu. Rakyat bukan prop dalam garis masa pemilihan semula anda.

238 Political Appointments to FSBs and GLCs Under Anwar — IDEAS Report Exposes 'Connections Over Competence'

Said by: IDEAS Report / Anwar Ibrahim Administration

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 What Happened

The Institute for Democracy and Economic Affairs (IDEAS) released a report titled Who Sits at the Table: From Political to Public Appointees in Malaysia’s Federal Statutory Bodies on March 6, 2026, revealing that 238 political appointments were made to federal statutory bodies (FSBs) and government-linked companies (GLCs) under Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim’s administration between November 2022 and October 2025. Of these, 222 were to FSBs and 16 to GLCs. The report reviewed 135 agencies and found that 78% of chairperson positions were filled based on political connections rather than competence. Most lacked clearly defined qualification criteria. The appointments were concentrated in key ministries, and the governance provisions around them were described as inconsistent and fragmented.

🔥 Why It Matters

This report is a direct, data-backed indictment of PH’s reform credentials. Anwar came to power promising meritocracy, transparency, and an end to the patronage politics that defined BN-era governance. Three years in, an independent, respected think tank has documented that political appointments haven’t just continued — they dominate. When 78% of the people running your statutory bodies got the job through connections rather than qualifications, you are not reforming governance. You are changing the names on the patronage list. This is not a fringe accusation — IDEAS is one of Malaysia’s most credible policy institutes, and its Pantau Kuasa initiative has tracked appointments across multiple administrations.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH sold voters the idea that they were fundamentally different from UMNO and BN. That the era of politically connected chairs presiding over government agencies was over. IDEAS’ numbers say otherwise: 238 appointments in under three years, with the overwhelming majority driven by political affiliation rather than professional merit. This is not reform — it is rotation. Different coalition, same playbook. The only thing that changed is the party logo on the appointment letters. Anwar has gone from demanding accountability for political appointments in opposition to treating them as a natural entitlement of power in government. If competence mattered, these numbers would look different. They do not.

📜 Previous Cases

Political appointments to statutory bodies and GLCs have been a recurring feature of Malaysian governance for decades. Under Najib Razak, the practice was widely criticised — particularly at 1MDB, Tabung Haji, and FELDA, where politically connected chairs presided over mismanagement and outright corruption. PH’s 2018 manifesto explicitly promised to end this, and the short-lived PH 1.0 government made some initial moves toward meritocratic appointments before collapsing in the Sheraton Move. Under PH 2.0, the pattern has returned with a vengeance. IDEAS’ Pantau Kuasa data shows that Anwar’s government is not the outlier it promised to be — it is the continuation of a structural problem that PH once made its name fighting.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Malaysians a new Malaysia. You said the era of political cronies was over. You told voters that under Madani, competence would matter more than connections. IDEAS just put a number on that promise: 238 political appointments, 78% driven by politics, not merit. That is not reform — that is business as usual with better branding. If PH cannot even clean up the appointment process for statutory bodies — a basic, achievable governance reform with no legal barriers — why should anyone believe the bigger promises about institutional independence, anti-corruption, or meritocracy? The data speaks louder than the slogans.

Anwar Admits He Can Only Hold RON95 Subsidy for 'One or Two Months' — While Fuel Prices Spike Across the Board

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 What Happened

On March 6, 2026, Prime Minister and Finance Minister Anwar Ibrahim told reporters that Malaysia can maintain the subsidised RON95 petrol price at RM1.99 per litre under the Budi95 scheme — but only for ‘one or two months.’ His exact words: ‘We will try to control the effects of the Iranian conflict, including for RON95, which is RM1.99 per litre. We will monitor this situation because so far, we can still hold off for one or two months.’ This came the same week that RON97 petrol rose 10 sen to RM3.25 per litre, unsubsidised RON95 climbed 8 sen to RM2.67, and diesel in Peninsular Malaysia jumped 8 sen to RM3.12 — all driven by the escalating Middle East conflict and 200 vessels stuck in the Strait of Hormuz.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the first time Anwar has publicly put a time limit on the government’s ability to hold fuel subsidies. The hedging language — ‘we will try,’ ‘hold off,’ ‘monitor’ — is a significant shift from previous assurances. If the Strait of Hormuz disruption continues, and 20% of the world’s oil supply remains at risk, the RM1.99 price becomes fiscally unsustainable. But Anwar cannot politically afford a fuel price hike — especially not with Hari Raya weeks away, snap election rumours swirling for July, and RM15 billion in STR cash handouts already deployed. He’s caught between fiscal reality and electoral survival, and the ‘one or two months’ language tells you which one he’s prioritising.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent three years telling Malaysians that Budi95’s targeted subsidy system was a masterstroke of fiscal reform — smarter, fairer, and more sustainable than blanket subsidies. Now, the moment global oil prices actually test the system, he’s already hedging and saying he can only hold it for a couple of months. What happened to the RM800 million in savings the government claimed from Budi95? What happened to Petronas’s RM32 billion dividend to the government? The truth is that the targeted subsidy was always designed for calm seas. The moment a real shock hits — which anyone tracking Middle East geopolitics could have predicted — the whole system starts to wobble. And instead of a plan, we get ‘we will try.’

📜 Previous Cases

This is a recurring pattern with Anwar on fuel policy: promise stability, deliver uncertainty. In early March, he told Dewan Negara the government would ’try to keep’ RON95 at RM1.99 — vague language that gave no timeline. Fuel prices have been creeping up steadily since January 2026, with diesel alone rising from RM2.84 in mid-January to RM3.12 by early March. Each week brings a new price adjustment upward, while the government insists the situation is ‘under control.’ It mirrors the BN-era playbook: reassure the public while the numbers quietly deteriorate, then act surprised when the dam breaks.

🎯 PH Callout

You told Malaysians that Budi95 was reform. You told them targeted subsidies would protect the rakyat while saving the government money. Now you’re admitting, publicly, that the system can only survive ‘one or two months’ of real global pressure. If this is fiscal reform, it’s built on cardboard. Where is the contingency plan? Where is the honest conversation with the public about what happens if oil hits USD100 a barrel and stays there? Stop managing headlines and start managing the economy. The rakyat deserves more than ‘we will try’ from the man who is simultaneously Prime Minister and Finance Minister.

Anwar Boasts RM426.7B 'Record' Approved Investments — But 'Approved' Doesn't Mean 'Arrived'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-06

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On March 6, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim announced that Malaysia recorded its highest-ever approved investments of RM426.7 billion in 2025 — an 11% increase from RM384.4 billion in 2024. According to MIDA, 8,390 projects were approved, with the potential to create 244,902 jobs. Anwar framed this as a vindication of his reform agenda and evidence of global confidence in Malaysia’s economic direction.

The announcement was made at the launch of the Capital Market Masterplan 2026-2030, with Anwar also serving as Finance Minister — a dual role he has held since taking power in 2022.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Because approved investments are not the same as realized investments, and the Malaysian public has been fed this distinction-free headline for decades.

MIDA tracks approvals — essentially, letters of intent and investment certificates. These are commitments on paper. The actual money flowing into the economy — foreign direct investment (FDI) realized — has historically been 50-60% of approved figures in Malaysia. That means up to half of these headline numbers may never translate into actual factories, jobs, or economic activity.

Anwar is using approved investment figures the same way every Malaysian PM before him has: as a political headline, not an economic reality check. The 244,902 jobs are potential jobs — not confirmed, not created, not guaranteed.

This matters because Malaysians are living through cost-of-living pressures, stagnant real wages, and subsidy rationalization. A headline number that may be half-real does not pay anyone’s bills.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

Let’s be precise about what happened here: the Prime Minister stood at a podium and celebrated a number that he knows does not reflect actual investment.

Every MIDA annual report distinguishes between approved and realized investments. Every serious economist in Malaysia knows the realization gap. Anwar — a man who has been in and around Malaysian economic policymaking for three decades — knows this too.

And yet: no realization rate was disclosed. No breakdown of how much of 2023 or 2024’s approved investments actually materialized. No accountability metric. Just the biggest number, the best headline, and a claim of “global confidence.”

This is the economic equivalent of counting RSVPs as attendance. You don’t get to claim 244,902 jobs when those jobs exist only in approval letters. You don’t get to claim RM426.7 billion when a significant chunk of it may never arrive.

The UNCTAD Global Investment Trends Monitor (January 2026) itself noted that much of global FDI growth was concentrated in financial centres rather than productive assets. MIDA’s own press release acknowledged this — then quietly moved on to the headline number.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

This is not new. Every Malaysian government has played the approved-vs-realized game:

  • Under Najib, approved investments regularly topped RM200B+ annually, but realized FDI lagged significantly behind.
  • Under Mahathir 2.0, the same pattern continued — big approvals, quiet underperformance.
  • Anwar’s own government has not published a comprehensive, transparent realization audit since taking office. The pattern is always the same: announce the biggest possible number at a press conference, let the media run the headline, and never follow up with what actually happened. No PM has ever been held accountable for the gap between approved and realized investments.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You promised transparency and accountability as your brand. You said you were different from BN. You said you wouldn’t spin numbers.

So where is the realization report? Where is the breakdown of how much of 2023’s and 2024’s approved investments actually showed up as real money, real factories, and real jobs?

If you want credit for RM426.7 billion, show the receipts — not the invitations. Until then, this is the same economic theatre Malaysians have been watching since the 1990s, just with a reformist coat of paint.
