US Declares 'Victory Against China' After Securing Unlimited Minerals Pledge From Anwar

Said by: MalaysiaNow report

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-03

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 3 November 2025, MalaysiaNow reported that the United States had “declared victory against China” after securing a pledge from Anwar Ibrahim to not restrict exports of critical minerals and rare earth elements to the US. Under the US-Malaysia ART, Malaysia committed to “refrain from banning, or imposing quotas on, exports to the US of critical minerals or rare earth elements” — a binding trade commitment, not a goodwill gesture. Malaysia also agreed to partner with American firms “to create certainty for businesses to increase production capacity,” essentially guaranteeing US access to Malaysian mineral resources in perpetuity.

Personal Bankruptcies Rise 5.7% — Self-Declared Bankruptcy Trend 'Growing Concern'

Said by: Department of Insolvency Malaysia

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-01

The Insolvency Department Director-General flagged a “growing concern”: the rise in self-declared bankruptcies, where Malaysians are so financially distressed they voluntarily declare themselves bankrupt rather than waiting for creditors to force the process. Youth Unemployment Stuck at 12.28% — One in Eight Young Malaysians Can't Find Work

Said by: DOSM / ILO / World Bank Data

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-11-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: As of late 2025, Malaysia’s youth unemployment rate (ages 15-24) remains stuck at 12.28% — roughly 297,500 unemployed young Malaysians. This is three to four times higher than the overall national unemployment rate of 3.2%. DOSM, the ILO, and World Bank data all confirm the structural nature of the problem: Malaysia produces graduates the economy doesn’t need, while industries that are hiring can’t find workers with the right skills. The “Madani economy” that Anwar celebrates apparently doesn’t include young people.
