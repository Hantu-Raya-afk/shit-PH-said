Najib House Arrest Bid Rejected — Court Rules Royal Addendum Invalid

Said by: High Court Judge Alice Loke / Najib Razak

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-22

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 22 December 2025, the KL High Court ruled that the royal addendum allowing convicted former PM Najib Razak to serve his remaining 1MDB sentence under house arrest was invalid. Judge Alice Loke said the King’s discretion must be exercised “for the public good” and found the addendum lacked proper legal basis. Al Jazeera and Reuters both reported the ruling. The Selangor Sultan had earlier warned that pardons should not be “arbitrary for certain parties.” Anwar’s government had already halved Najib’s sentence via the Pardons Board in February 2024 — the house arrest bid was the next step in an escalating pattern of leniency for a convicted kleptocrat whose party Anwar now depends on.

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Economy

Civil Service is 90% Bumiputera — Malays Hold 80%+ of Top Posts

Said by: Eurasia Review Analysis

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-15

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: According to Eurasia Review analysis published in December 2025, Malaysia’s civil service of 1.3 to 1.6 million employees is approximately 90% Bumiputera. Malays hold 77.5% of all posts and over 80% of top management positions. The Straits Times reported the “Bumiputera-first policy debate” has taken on a “heady mix of health, education and politics.” When a UiTM professor suggested opening Bumiputera-only programmes to other races to address specialist shortages, the backlash was immediate and the idea was shut down.

Said by: Reuters / Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission

Category: Politics

Date: 2025-12-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 4 December 2025, Anwar Ibrahim’s senior political secretary Shamsul Iskandar Mohd Akin was charged with receiving bribes totalling RM176,829 from a local businessman.
