MCMC Blocks Opposition Media Sites Before State Elections — Fahmi Says 'I Gave No Instruction,' RSF Calls It Censorship

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister / MCMC

Category: Politics

Date: 2023-08-07

📌 What Happened

In August 2023, in the lead-up to six state elections, Malaysia’s MCMC blocked access to at least four pro-opposition news sites — MalaysiaNow, UtusanTV, Malaysia Today, and TV Pertiwi — as well as blogger Wee Choo Keong’s site. Users were redirected to an MCMC IP address. None of the outlets received any warning or explanation of which articles triggered the blocks. Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil denied giving any instruction to MCMC, saying ‘I have given no instruction to MCMC to block anyone.’ Reporters Without Borders (RSF) issued a statement slamming the crackdowns and urging Fahmi to end such censorship. While blocks on MalaysiaNow and UtusanTV were lifted within days after the elections, Malaysia Today and other sites remained blocked long after.

🔥 Why It Matters

PH came to power in GE14 in 2018 partly on a promise to end media repression. The Economist ranked Malaysia the most democratic country in Southeast Asia that year. Five years later, PH is blocking opposition media right before elections — the exact playbook of the BN regimes they replaced. The timing was not subtle: block critical voices before voting day, unblock some after results are in. This signalled that PH’s commitment to press freedom was conditional — free when it suits them, restricted when the polls are tight.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Fahmi’s ‘I gave no instruction’ defence is the plausible deniability playbook. MCMC falls under his ministry. The blocks happened during the most politically sensitive period of 2023. If the Communications Minister doesn’t know who ordered his own agency to block news sites days before an election, he is either incompetent or lying — and neither answer is acceptable. PH in opposition screamed when BN blocked media. PH in government says ’not my fault, MCMC acted independently.’ The hypocrisy is breathtaking. RSF saw right through it. The international community saw right through it. Only Fahmi pretends there’s nothing to see.

📜 Previous Cases

Media suppression is a bipartisan Malaysian tradition. BN blocked The Malaysian Insider and Sarawak Report. Muhyiddin’s government pressured Al Jazeera and detained journalists. PH promised to repeal the Printing Presses and Publications Act, the Sedition Act, and Section 233 of the CMA. None have been repealed. Instead, PH has been using the same laws — sometimes more aggressively — than their predecessors, as documented by the US State Department’s 2023 human rights report on Malaysia.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised media freedom. You promised to end MCMC censorship. You promised the rakyat that a PH government would never block news sites for political convenience. Then you blocked four outlets right before an election and told us it wasn’t you. If your ‘reform’ government cannot even guarantee that journalists can publish without being blocked by the state, you are not reformers — you are the same authoritarian impulse wearing a different jersey.

RM10 Billion Selangor Maritime Gateway Scandal — MACC Report Filed Over Land and Shares Deal

Said by: Amirudin Shari / Chegubard / MACC

Category: Economy

Date: 2023-08-07

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Activist Badrul Hisham Shaharin (Chegubard) lodged a MACC report in August 2023 over alleged RM10 billion irregularities in the Selangor Maritime Gateway project — a 56km development along the Selangor River. The project reportedly involved the sale of majority shares and hundreds of acres of government-owned land to a private company linked to businessman Vincent Tan. Amirudin sent a letter of demand to Kedah MB Sanusi Md Nor for commenting on the scandal publicly.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: RM10 billion in alleged irregularities involving state land transfers and share sales is not a minor governance issue — it’s the kind of scandal that ends political careers in functional democracies. The Selangor Maritime Gateway is the state’s largest infrastructure project, and allegations that government land was sold to a private entity below market value while shares were transferred under questionable circumstances strike at the heart of public trust.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Amirudin’s response to the RM10 billion allegation was not to open an investigation or demand a public audit — it was to send a legal letter to the opposition politician who talked about it. That tells you everything about PH’s approach to accountability: silence the critics, ignore the substance. If someone accuses you of mishandling RM10 billion in state assets, the correct response is an independent audit, not a lawyer’s letter.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: This is part of a broader pattern of Selangor asset management concerns under Amirudin — from sand mining corruption to MBI investigations to the SMUE elderly fund probe. Each case involves state resources, each involves MACC, and each is met with the same response: deny, deflect, threaten legal action.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: RM10 billion in state assets. Government land sold to private interests. Shares transferred. And your response was to sue the person who talked about it. If the deal is clean, publish the contracts, the valuations, and the approval documents. If it’s not clean, investigate. But threatening defamation suits against critics while MACC investigates your state is not reform — it’s cover-up with extra steps.

[BM] Fahmi Ugut Pengkritik: 'Jangan Marah Kalau Ada Kereta Rondaan Depan Rumah — Kami Memantau'

Category: Social

Date: 2023-07-28

Pada 2023, Menteri Komunikasi Fahmi Fadzil mengeluarkan apa yang hanya boleh digambarkan sebagai ugutan terbuka kepada pengkritik dalam talian, berkata: ‘Jangan marah dengan saya kalau ada panggilan atau kereta rondaan depan rumah anda. Seorang menteri dalam kerajaan yang mengaku ‘kerajaan reformasi’ memberitahu rakyat secara terbuka bahawa negara memerhati mereka dan mereka patut ‘jaga kelakuan’ adalah ugutan autoritarian dari buku teks. Ia yang menteri UMNO kata selama berdekad. Satu-satunya bezanya Fahmi kata sambil pakai lencana Reformasi. Penghapusan kandungan, sekatan media, ugutan pengawasan — ini bukan reformasi. Ini BN 2.0 dengan PR lebih baik.

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Sejarah menteri Malaysia mengugut pengkritik panjang dan memalukan. Di bawah Najib, Akta Hasutan dijadikan senjata terhadap akademik, kartunis, dan wartawan. Di bawah Muhyiddin, wartawan Al Jazeera disiasat. Di bawah Anwar, alat berubah — SKMM dan algoritma kandungan ganti instrumen kasar Akta Hasutan — tetapi naluri sama. Laporan hak asasi manusia 2023 Jabatan Negara AS mengenai Malaysia mendokumentasikan sekatan berterusan terhadap kebebasan bersuara di bawah PH.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

‘Kami memantau, jaga kelakuan.’ Tu reformasi korang? Tu Reformasi korang? Tu yang rakyat undi — Menteri Komunikasi yang bunyi macam kepala kampung ugut jiran? Korang janji tamatkan budaya ketakutan. Sebaliknya, menteri korang memberitahu rakyat Malaysia negara memerhati mereka dan mereka baik diam. Turunkan poster reformasi. Korang tak layak.

Fahmi Threatens Critics: 'Don't Get Upset If There's a Radio Car Outside Your House — We Are Monitoring'

Said by: Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Social

Date: 2023-07-28

📌 What Happened

In 2023, Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil issued what can only be described as a public threat to online critics, saying: ‘Don’t get upset with me if there is a call or a radio car outside of your house. We are monitoring, behave yourself.’ The statement was made in the context of the government’s escalating content takedown regime and increasing MCMC enforcement actions. Fahmi dismissed concerns about growing censorship, denying government involvement in blocking opposition-linked news sites and social media accounts in the lead-up to the August state elections. He insisted the government drew the line at content touching race, religion, and royalty — the same 3R framework used by every previous Malaysian government to justify silencing critics.

🔥 Why It Matters

A minister in a self-described ‘reform government’ publicly telling citizens that the state is watching them and they should ‘behave’ is textbook authoritarian intimidation. This is not a metaphor. This is the Communications Minister — who oversees MCMC, internet regulation, and content enforcement — telling Malaysians that criticism will be met with surveillance. Asia Sentinel’s reporting flagged Fahmi as a minister who came ‘particularly under attack for his repressive moves against bloggers and other media.’ Social media censorship surged during Anwar’s first year in power. The man tasked with protecting digital freedom became the face of digital repression.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

PH in opposition built its base on defending the right to criticise power. Fahmi himself rose through the ranks as a civil society-adjacent politician. Now in power, the mask drops: ‘We are monitoring, behave yourself’ is what authoritarian regimes say. It is exactly what Hishammuddin Hussein said as Home Minister under Najib. It is what UMNO ministers said for decades. The only difference is that Fahmi says it while wearing a Reformasi badge. The content takedowns, the media blocks, the surveillance threats — this is not reform. This is BN 2.0 with better PR.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia’s history of ministers threatening critics is long and inglorious. Under Najib, the Sedition Act was weaponised against academics, cartoonists, and journalists. Under Muhyiddin, Al Jazeera journalists were investigated for a documentary on migrant workers. Under Anwar, the tools have changed — MCMC and content algorithms replace the Sedition Act’s blunt instrument — but the instinct is identical. The 2023 US State Department human rights report on Malaysia documented ongoing government restrictions on free expression under PH.

🎯 PH Callout

‘We are monitoring, behave yourself.’ That’s your reform? That’s your Reformasi? That’s what the rakyat voted for — a Communications Minister who sounds like a kepala kampung threatening his neighbours? You promised to end the culture of fear. Instead, your minister is telling Malaysians that the state is watching them and they’d better shut up. Take down the reform posters. You don’t deserve them.

[BM] Hukuman Mati Masih Kekal Untuk 27 Kesalahan — 'Pemansuhan' Hanya Separuh Jalan

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration

Category: Social

Date: 2023-07-04

📌 APA YANG BERLAKU: Pada 4 Julai 2023, PH menggembar-gemburkan “pemansuhan” hukuman mati mandatori — menggantikannya dengan hukuman budi bicara untuk kesalahan berat. Walau bagaimanapun, hukuman mati itu sendiri masih kekal untuk 27 kesalahan, termasuk pengedaran dadah, pembunuhan, dan kesalahan senjata api. Hakim kini mempunyai budi bicara untuk menjatuhkan hukuman mati atau hukuman alternatif, tetapi tiang gantung masih tersedia. Amnesty International menyeru pemansuhan penuh. Pada akhir 2025, Malaysia mengumumkan “kajian” mengenai pemansuhan penuh bermula Januari 2026 — tiga tahun selepas reformasi awal.

🔥 MENGAPA INI PENTING: PH membingkaikan reformasi hukuman mati mandatori sebagai pencapaian hak asasi manusia yang bersejarah. Pada hakikatnya, ia adalah langkah separuh. Menghapuskan perkataan “mandatori” tidak memansuhkan hukuman mati — ia memindahkan keputusan daripada statut kepada budi bicara kehakiman. Hakim dalam sistem mahkamah Malaysia, yang PH sendiri telah mengkritik kerana campur tangan politik, kini menentukan siapa yang hidup dan siapa yang mati. Bagi kerajaan yang mendakwa memperjuangkan hak asasi manusia, mengekalkan hukuman mati untuk 27 kesalahan sambil mengumumkan “kajian” mengenai pemansuhan — tiga tahun selepas reformasi — bukan kemajuan. Ia penangguhan yang dijenamakan sebagai prinsip.

💩 MENGAPA INI KARUT: “Kajian” yang diumumkan untuk Januari 2026 adalah taktik penangguhan lalai kerajaan Malaysia: umumkan kajian, bentuk jawatankuasa, tunggu kitaran berita bertukar, dan tidak pernah melaksanakan penemuan. Jika PH percaya pada pemansuhan, ia akan memansuhkan hukuman mati sepenuhnya pada 2023 ketika ia mempunyai bilangan parlimen dan momentum awam. Sebaliknya, ia memilih pilihan yang selamat secara politik — buang “mandatori,” kekalkan hukuman, dan tuntut kredit untuk reformasi. Amnesty International nampak terus. Rakyat juga sepatutnya.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: Malaysia telah menghukum mati banduan di bawah setiap pentadbiran. Di bawah BN, hukuman mati digunakan terutamanya untuk pengedaran dadah — secara tidak seimbang menjejaskan defendan miskin, terpinggir, dan warga asing. PH 1.0 memulakan perbincangan mengenai pemansuhan tetapi runtuh sebelum bertindak. PH 2.0 mempunyai tiga tahun untuk menyelesaikan kerja. Sebaliknya, ia bersetuju dengan hukuman budi bicara dan “kajian” masa depan. Corak ini konsisten: kerajaan Malaysia mengakui hukuman mati bermasalah, membuat perubahan bertahap, dan tidak pernah menyelesaikan tugas.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda memanggilnya pemansuhan. Ia bukan. Hukuman mati masih kekal untuk 27 kesalahan. Anda mengumumkan “kajian” mengenai pemansuhan penuh — tiga tahun selepas reformasi awal anda. Jika anda perlukan tiga tahun untuk mengkaji sama ada negara patut membunuh warganya, jawapannya sudah tidak. Mansuhkan hukuman mati untuk semua kesalahan. Tukar hukuman mati sedia ada. Berhenti mengkaji dan mula bertindak. Atau akui bahawa “pemansuhan” itu hanya kenyataan akhbar, bukan dasar.

Death Penalty Still on Books for 27 Offences — 'Abolition' Was Only Half-Done

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim Administration

Category: Social

Date: 2023-07-04

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: On 4 July 2023, PH trumpeted the “abolition” of the mandatory death penalty — replacing it with discretionary sentencing for capital offences. However, the death penalty itself remains on the books for 27 offences, including drug trafficking, murder, and firearms offences. Judges now have discretion to impose death or alternative sentences, but the gallows remain available. Amnesty International called for full abolition. In late 2025, Malaysia announced a “study” on full abolition to begin in January 2026 — three years after the initial reform.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: PH framed the mandatory death penalty reform as a landmark human rights achievement. In reality, it was a half-measure. Removing the word “mandatory” does not abolish the death penalty — it transfers the decision from statute to judicial discretion. Judges in Malaysia’s court system, which PH itself has criticised for political interference, now decide who lives and who dies. For a government that claims to champion human rights, maintaining the death penalty for 27 offences while announcing a “study” on abolition — three years after reform — is not progress. It’s procrastination branded as principle.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: The “study” announced for January 2026 is the Malaysian government’s default delaying tactic: announce a study, form a committee, wait for the news cycle to move on, and never implement the findings. If PH believed in abolition, it would have abolished the death penalty entirely in 2023 when it had the parliamentary numbers and the public momentum. Instead, it chose the politically safe option — remove “mandatory,” keep the penalty, and claim credit for reform. Amnesty International saw through it immediately. The rakyat should too.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Malaysia has executed prisoners under every administration. Under BN, the death penalty was used primarily for drug trafficking — disproportionately affecting poor, marginalised, and foreign defendants. PH 1.0 initiated discussions on abolition but collapsed before acting. PH 2.0 had three years to complete the work. Instead, it settled for discretionary sentencing and a future “study.” The pattern is consistent: Malaysian governments acknowledge the death penalty is problematic, make incremental changes, and never finish the job.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You called it abolition. It wasn’t. The death penalty remains for 27 offences. You announced a “study” on full abolition — three years after your initial reform. If you need three years to study whether the state should kill its citizens, the answer is already no. Abolish the death penalty for all offences. Commute existing death sentences. Stop studying and start acting. Or admit that “abolition” was a press release, not a policy.

Analisis kos-manfaat ECRL tidak pernah diterbitkan sepenuhnya kepada umum. Beban faedah sahaja akan memakan hasil kerajaan selama berdekad. Penciptaan pekerjaan tempatan telah dikritik sebagai minimum — kebanyakan tenaga kerja pembinaan dan kepakaran kejuruteraan datang dari China. Anwar meneruskan projek yang dimulakan oleh Najib, diteruskan oleh setiap PM selepas itu, dan dipersoalkan oleh ahli ekonomi merentas spektrum politik — kerana pembatalan akan merosakkan hubungan China dan mencetuskan klausa penalti. Ini bukan keputusan dasar; ini adalah situasi tebusan.

📜 KES TERDAHULU: ECRL pada asalnya ditandatangani oleh Najib pada 2016 sebagai sebahagian daripada Inisiatif Jalur dan Laluan China, pada kos yang melambung yang dilihat secara meluas dikaitkan dengan rundingan penyelamatan 1MDB. Mahathir menggantungnya pada 2018, kemudian merundingkan semula pada kos yang lebih rendah. Muhyiddin menyambung semula pembinaan. Setiap kerajaan mewarisi dan meneruskan projek ini kerana kos tenggelam dan akibat diplomatik pembatalan bertambah dengan setiap tahun yang berlalu. ECRL adalah perangkap fiskal Malaysia — setiap PM menyentuhnya dan tidak boleh melepaskan.

🎯 SERUAN KEPADA PH: Anda mewarisi projek mega kontroversi yang disokong China daripada Najib, melalui Mahathir, melalui Muhyiddin, melalui Ismail Sabri. Bukannya menjalankan semakan kos-manfaat bebas, anda meneruskan dan memanggil kos “dikurangkan.” Terbitkan analisis kos-manfaat penuh. Dedahkan kadar faedah dan jadual pembayaran balik. Tunjukkan berapa banyak pekerjaan tempatan berbanding pekerja import yang dihasilkan projek ini. Jelaskan bagaimana laluan rel RM75 bilion melalui kawasan berkepadatan rendah akan menjana pulangan yang mewajarkan hutang. Atau akui bahawa anda meneruskan projek ini kerana menghentikannya lebih sukar secara politik daripada membayarnya.

ECRL Cost Balloons to RM75 Billion — Anwar Proceeds Anyway, Calls It 'Reduced'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2022-12-01

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Anwar Ibrahim revealed that the original East Coast Rail Link (ECRL) cost was RM86 billion and claimed his government “reduced” it to RM75 billion. Construction alone accounts for RM50.27 billion, with an additional RM24.69 billion in interest and land acquisition costs. The project — a China-backed megaproject connecting the east and west coasts of Peninsular Malaysia — was renegotiated under Mahathir’s PH 1.0 government and continued under Muhyiddin and Ismail Sabri. Anwar’s government proceeded with construction, framing the RM11 billion “reduction” as a fiscal achievement.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: RM75 billion is the single largest infrastructure commitment in Malaysian history. The interest component alone — RM24.69 billion — exceeds the entire annual budget of most government ministries. Critics, including economists and opposition MPs, have questioned whether the ECRL will generate sufficient economic returns to justify the cost, noting that the route primarily serves low-density areas with limited commercial activity. The project is funded through a loan from China’s Exim Bank, raising debt dependency concerns at a time when Malaysia’s debt-to-GDP ratio already exceeds 64%.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Calling a RM75 billion project “reduced” because it was originally RM86 billion is like celebrating a 13% discount on something you can’t afford. The ECRL’s cost-benefit analysis has never been publicly released in full. The interest burden alone will consume government revenue for decades. Local job creation has been criticised as minimal — much of the construction workforce and engineering expertise comes from China. Anwar is proceeding with a project initiated by Najib, continued by every subsequent PM, and questioned by economists across the political spectrum — because cancellation would damage the China relationship and trigger penalty clauses. This isn’t a policy decision; it’s a hostage situation.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The ECRL was originally signed by Najib in 2016 as part of China’s Belt and Road Initiative, at an inflated cost that was widely seen as linked to 1MDB bailout negotiations. Mahathir suspended it in 2018, then renegotiated at a lower cost. Muhyiddin resumed construction. Each government has inherited and continued the project because the sunk costs and diplomatic consequences of cancellation grow with each passing year. The ECRL is Malaysia’s fiscal tar baby — every PM touches it and can’t let go.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You inherited a controversial China-backed megaproject from Najib, via Mahathir, via Muhyiddin, via Ismail Sabri. Instead of conducting an independent cost-benefit review, you proceeded and called the cost “reduced.” Publish the full cost-benefit analysis. Disclose the interest rate and repayment schedule. Show how many local jobs versus imported workers the project creates. Explain how a RM75 billion rail line through low-density areas will generate returns that justify the debt. Or admit that you’re continuing the project because stopping it is politically harder than paying for it.
