Amir Hamzah Tells Malaysians 'Don't Speculate' on Fuel After Special Cabinet — While KLCI Crashes Below 1700 and Subsidy Bill Balloons to RM3.2 Billion

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Finance Minister II Datuk Seri Amir Hamzah Azizan held a press conference after the special Cabinet meeting on March 13 — the meeting that was supposed to deliver the government’s comprehensive crisis response to the West Asia conflict. His headline message: the Malaysian economy “remains resilient,” the government is sourcing additional oil, and Malaysians should stop spreading or acting on speculation about fuel availability because it “could create unnecessary negative consequences.” No new economic relief measures, no targeted aid packages, no toll freezes, no food price interventions — just reassurance.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was the special Cabinet meeting that was supposed to matter — the one Fahmi previewed on March 11, the one Fadillah built up on March 12, the one the whole country was watching. And the outcome? A Finance Minister telling citizens not to worry and not to talk about it. Meanwhile, the FBM KLCI crashed below 1,700 for the first time in years, the monthly fuel subsidy bill has ballooned to RM3.2 billion, and economists are warning of a potential global recession triggered by the oil shock. The gap between what the government says and what the market shows has never been wider.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

When your special Cabinet meeting produces nothing except “don’t speculate” and “economy is resilient,” you don’t have a crisis response — you have a PR operation. Amir Hamzah is literally telling Malaysians to stop talking about the problem instead of solving it. This is the same government that promised fiscal reform, subsidy rationalization, and institutional capacity. Three years in, their crisis playbook is: hold a special meeting, issue reassuring soundbites, tell people to stay calm, and defer every hard decision. Thailand mandated WFH immediately. Malaysia formed a committee, deferred to Tuesday, held a special meeting, and told citizens to shut up.

📜 Previous Cases

This is not new. When the ringgit fell past 4.80 to the dollar in early 2024, Anwar’s response was to blame speculators and tell Malaysians to have confidence. When inflation concerns mounted post-SST reintroduction, the messaging was “everything is under control.” When Bloomberg reported MACC misconduct, Fahmi’s first instinct was to question the source, not the substance. PH’s pattern is consistent: when the facts are bad, attack the people pointing out the facts. Amir Hamzah’s “don’t speculate” is just the latest variation.

🎯 PH Callout

You called a special Cabinet meeting — the whole country waited. And your answer is “don’t speculate”? The rakyat are paying RM3.85 for RON97, RM3.92 for diesel, watching their portfolios bleed, and wondering how long the RM1.99 RON95 lifeline will hold. They don’t need to be told to calm down. They need a government that shows up with a plan — not a press conference full of reassurance and empty of action. You promised competence, reform, and fiscal discipline. Deliver, or admit you can’t.

Anwar Admits Reforms 'Not As Quick As We'd Like' — After Three Years of Broken Promises, That's the Best He's Got

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and PKR President

Category: Politics

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

On March 13, 2026, Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim addressed Sarawak PKR members at a “mini convention” in Kuching and admitted that PKR is “not perfect” and that reforms are not being implemented “as quickly as we would like.” He told party members to “close ranks” and continue the reform struggle, declaring: “The true test of executing change or reforms is when one is in the seat of power, not after losing power or having been removed.” This was said on the same day his special Cabinet meeting deferred the WFH decision to yet another review, tripled the RON95 subsidy to RM2 billion instead of reforming it, and had the Agriculture Minister telling Malaysians to grow their own vegetables.

🔥 Why It Matters

Anwar’s admission is significant because it is the closest he has come to acknowledging the yawning gap between Pakatan Harapan’s reform promises and its delivery in government. After more than three years in power, the reform scorecard is dismal: the PM term limit bill failed in Parliament with 32 MPs absent and 44 abstaining, targeted fuel subsidy reform has been abandoned in favour of a ballooning blanket subsidy, the MACC chief remains protected despite credible misconduct allegations, and anti-corruption civil society organisations are being interrogated under Section 124B. The fact that Anwar chose to make this admission at a closed party event in Sarawak — far from the KL press corps — rather than at the post-Cabinet press conference tells you everything about who this message was really for: the faithful, not the public.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Admitting you’re “not perfect” is the political equivalent of a corporate apology that takes no responsibility. Anwar’s framing — that the “true test” of reform is when you’re in power — is supposed to sound humble and honest. But it’s actually an argument against his own government: if the true test is in the seat of power, and you’ve been in it for three years, then you are being graded right now, and you are failing. The PM term limit bill collapsed on his watch. Fuel subsidy reform, promised since 2023, is dead. The MACC scandal festers while his Communications Minister blocks transparency. “Not as quick as we’d like” is a masterclass in managed expectations — lowering the bar so the base accepts less while the patronage networks keep running.

📜 Previous Cases

Anwar has a long pattern of promising reform and delivering incrementalism or retreat. In January 2026, he pushed the PM term limit bill as a signature reform — it failed in March. In 2023-2024, targeted fuel subsidy reform was the centrepiece of fiscal policy — it has been shelved indefinitely. The CeIO “integrity culture” programme launched on March 12, 2026, the same week his government shielded Azam Baki and interrogated C4. PKR’s own internal reform credentials are thin: the Rafizi faction of 10 MPs pushing for parliamentary oversight of the public prosecutor has been sidelined. The party’s reform DNA has been diluted by three years of coalition management with UMNO.

🎯 PH Callout

You told the rakyat in 2022 that you were different. That you would reform the system, not just occupy it. Three years later, your own president stands before party faithful in Kuching and admits reforms aren’t happening fast enough — and your answer is to “close ranks.” Close ranks around what, exactly? A tripled fuel subsidy with no reform timeline? A protected MACC chief? An Agriculture Minister telling people to plant chilli in their backyards? The rakyat didn’t vote for managed expectations. They voted for Reformasi. If the true test of reform is when you’re in power, you are failing your own exam. Stop asking for patience and start delivering results — or admit that PH in government is just BN with better PR.

Anwar Says RON95 Subsidy Could Hit RM24 Billion — His Solution? 'Pray for Peace'

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister and Finance Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim revealed on March 13 that the government’s subsidy bill to maintain RON95 at RM1.99 per litre under the BUDI95 scheme could reach RM24 billion by year-end if the Middle East conflict persists. Speaking after the special Cabinet meeting, Anwar said the calculation was based on a monthly RON95 subsidy cost of RM2 billion. His proposed solution: “We must pray for a swift end to this conflict so that we won’t be burdened any further.” He added that the government would look for “other ways” to fund the subsidy through cost-cutting.

🔥 Why It Matters

The RM24 billion figure is a fiscal bombshell. The entire 2026 fuel subsidy allocation was originally a fraction of this. The number lays bare the total failure of subsidy reform — a policy Anwar promised since taking office in November 2022. Instead of a targeted subsidy mechanism, Malaysia now has a blanket subsidy that costs three times what it did a month ago, with no exit plan beyond hoping that a war ends. This is not fiscal management. This is fiscal surrender dressed up as compassion.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar had three years to implement targeted fuel subsidies. He launched BUDI95 in November 2025, but it was designed for stable oil prices, not a crisis. When the crisis hit, instead of activating the targeting mechanism he built, he froze RON95 at RM1.99 for everyone — including wealthy Malaysians driving luxury cars — and his solution is prayer. “Pray for peace” is not a fiscal strategy. It is the response of a government that spent its reform capital on election-cycle handouts and is now trapped by its own populism.

📜 Previous Cases

This echoes the BN playbook Anwar once campaigned against. Under Najib Razak, blanket fuel subsidies ballooned and were used as a political tool to buy loyalty before elections. Anwar explicitly promised to end this cycle. In 2023, he said targeted subsidies would be implemented “within months.” In 2024, he said “soon.” In 2025, he launched BUDI95 — and immediately undermined it by freezing the price floor when prices spiked. The RM24 billion figure is the cost of three years of broken promises.

🎯 PH Callout

You said you were different from BN. You said blanket subsidies were wasteful. You said targeting was the answer. Now you’re spending more on blanket subsidies than Najib ever did, and your backup plan is prayer. If RM24 billion doesn’t buy reform, what does it buy? It buys votes. And the rakyat — the ones you claim to protect — will pay for it in inflation, debt, and austerity down the road. This is not Madani. This is Najib with a better vocabulary.

Government Triples RON95 Subsidy to RM2 Billion — Three Years of 'Reform' Ends With a Bigger Blanket

Said by: Amir Hamzah Azizan, Finance Minister II

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

On Friday, March 13, Finance Minister II Amir Hamzah Azizan announced that the government would increase its spending on RON95 petrol subsidies from RM700 million to RM2 billion — nearly tripling the allocation — to maintain the fixed retail price of RM1.99 per litre amid surging global oil prices past $100/barrel. The announcement came after the Special Cabinet Meeting convened to address the West Asia conflict’s economic impact. The subsidy covers RON95, the most widely used transport fuel in Malaysia, consumed by the vast majority of motorists.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the final burial of Anwar’s subsidy reform agenda. Since taking office in late 2022, the Madani government has repeatedly promised to move from blanket to targeted fuel subsidies — a reform the World Bank, IMF, and Malaysia’s own fiscal advisors have urged for years. The diesel subsidy was partially rationalised in mid-2024. RON95 was supposed to follow. Instead, with a global energy crisis exposing every weakness in the current system, the government’s response is to pour three times more money into the same untargeted mechanism. The RM2 billion figure reveals the true fiscal cost of inaction: had targeted subsidies been implemented on schedule, the government would be spending a fraction of this amount while still protecting B40 households.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Three years. That’s how long this government has had to implement targeted fuel subsidies. Every budget speech since 2023 has included some version of “subsidy rationalisation will proceed in phases.” The T20 income verification system was supposedly ready. The Budi mechanism for diesel was supposed to be the template. And yet here we are in March 2026, and the only tool in the government’s kit is to triple the blanket subsidy — meaning T20 earners driving luxury SUVs get the same RM1.99 petrol as B40 workers on motorcycles. This is not crisis management. This is the consequence of three years of delayed reform catching up with a government that was too afraid of electoral backlash to act when oil was cheap.

📜 Previous Cases

Malaysia has been here before. Najib’s government removed fuel subsidies in 2014, then brought them back when oil prices dropped, missing the reform window entirely. Anwar criticised that flip-flop from the opposition bench. Now he’s doing the same thing with extra steps. The diesel subsidy reform of June 2024 was supposed to be Phase 1 of a broader rationalisation. Phase 2 (RON95) was initially targeted for late 2024, then pushed to 2025, then to “after economic conditions stabilise.” The conditions never stabilise because this government keeps waiting for the perfect moment that never comes.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised reform. You promised targeted subsidies. You had years of relatively stable oil prices to implement them. You didn’t. Now you’re tripling the blanket subsidy and calling it crisis management. Every ringgit of that RM2 billion that flows to T20 drivers is money that could have gone to B40 cash transfers, public transport, or food price interventions. You didn’t build the targeted system when you could. Now the rakyat pays for your procrastination — twice: once through inflation, and again through a fiscal bill that crowds out spending on everything else.

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

On March 13, Hannah Yeoh, Minister in the Prime Minister’s Department (Federal Territories), praised PM Anwar Ibrahim’s directive banning Aidilfitri open houses for government agencies and GLCs as a reflection of the “Madani Government’s commitment to being people-centred.” She made the remarks at a donation event in Segambut, where she distributed RM150 cash and bags of rice to 200 recipients including orphans, tahfiz students, and asnaf families — a modest handout worth RM30,000 total, funded by Ehsan Group, a private company.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is performative austerity wrapped in reform language. The Raya open house ban was announced the same week the government rolled out RM15 billion in STR cash handouts and RM500 civil servant bonuses — classic pre-election spending. Hannah Yeoh framing the open house ban as fiscal discipline is spin at its most transparent: the government isn’t saving money, it’s redirecting spending to vote-buying mechanisms while cancelling the one thing that costs ministries relatively little but generates public visibility. The “people-centred” claim rings hollow when the same minister was notably absent from the Kampung Lee Kong Chian eviction crisis days earlier, where 146 families in KL’s last private Chinese village were told to pack up for RM50,000 each.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Hannah Yeoh praising a Raya open house ban as evidence of good governance is like praising a shoplifter for returning the plastic bag. The open houses were never the fiscal problem — the RM15 billion in election-timed handouts are. And the optics of praising “cost-cutting” while personally distributing RM150 handouts at a private-company-sponsored event in your own constituency is not fiscal discipline; it’s constituency service dressed up as national policy. If the Madani government were serious about savings, it would freeze GLC executive bonuses, halt patronage appointments, or cancel the dozens of special committees that produce reports no one reads — not cancel Raya open houses that cost a fraction of any of those.

📜 Previous Cases

Hannah Yeoh has form in this space. As Women, Family and Community Development Minister in the first Anwar cabinet, she was known for symbolic announcements that rarely translated into structural reform. Her track record in Federal Territories has been similarly optics-heavy: she launched Yayasan Wilayah Persekutuan initiatives with great fanfare but faced criticism over the Kampung Lee Kong Chian evictions, rising rents in KL, and the lack of affordable housing delivery. PH’s broader pattern is the same — announce savings in visible, low-cost areas while maintaining or expanding spending in areas that benefit their political base.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised a government that would be different. You promised transparency, reform, and fiscal responsibility. Instead, you cancel Raya open houses — a gesture worth maybe a few million ringgit across all ministries — while spending RM15 billion on cash handouts timed for maximum electoral impact. Hannah Yeoh, you were in Segambut handing out RM150 bags while 146 families in Kampung Lee Kong Chian, in your own Federal Territories jurisdiction, are being evicted for RM50,000 each. That is not “people-centred.” That is camera-centred. If the Madani government wants Malaysians to believe in fiscal discipline, start with the RM15 billion — not the open houses.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised Ekonomi MADANI would protect the rakyat from exactly this kind of squeeze. Oil is at $100, your subsidy bill has tripled, food prices are climbing, and your Agriculture Minister’s best offer is ‘don’t panic, there’s enough rice until June.’ Where are the emergency food price interventions? The rakyat didn’t vote for gardening tips — they voted for governance.

Special Cabinet Meets for Oil Crisis — Anwar's WFH Decision? Another Review, Come Back Tuesday

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister / Fahmi Fadzil, Communications Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-13

📌 What Happened

On Friday, March 13, the Malaysian government held its much-anticipated Special Cabinet Meeting to address the economic fallout from the West Asia oil crisis, with Brent crude past $100/barrel and the Strait of Hormuz disrupted. Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil announced afterwards that Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim had instructed the Chief Secretary to the Government (KSN) and the Public Service Department director-general (KPPA) to “review the WFH proposal to ensure several aspects are properly coordinated.” The decision on whether to implement work-from-home for civil servants would be made on Tuesday, March 17 — four days later, and a full week after Thailand and Vietnam mandated WFH for their public sectors.

🔥 Why It Matters

This was supposed to be the crisis Cabinet — the meeting called specifically to take decisive action on the oil shock. Thailand’s Cabinet ordered mandatory WFH for government officials on March 10, the same day Vietnam issued similar directives. Both countries acted within 48 hours of the Strait of Hormuz disruption. Malaysia convened a special meeting three days later and produced… another review. The pattern is unmistakable: when confronted with urgent economic decisions that affect 1.7 million civil servants, this government defaults to study, review, and defer — even during what it calls a national crisis.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

A special Cabinet meeting is supposed to produce special decisions. Not another round of “let’s coordinate.” The KSN and KPPA have had since at least March 10 — when Human Resources Minister Ramanan first mentioned studying WFH — to prepare options. That’s three full working days. The Public Service DG himself told Bernama on March 12 that Malaysia already proved during COVID-19 that the public sector can adapt to WFH “through the use of technology and new working methods.” So the precedent exists, the infrastructure exists, the demand exists — what doesn’t exist is political will. Anwar’s government would rather spend RM2 billion on fuel subsidies than save a fraction of that by letting civil servants work from home.

📜 Previous Cases

This is vintage Madani governance: announce, study, defer, repeat. The PM term limit bill was tabled, failed, and will be “re-tabled in June.” Fuel subsidy reform has been “coming soon” since 2023. The Azam Baki report was discussed by Cabinet and punted to the Chief Secretary. In each case, the mechanism is identical — create the appearance of action while deferring the actual decision to a future date, by which time the news cycle has moved on.

🎯 PH Callout

You called a special Cabinet meeting for a crisis. You told the rakyat this was urgent. Thailand decided in one meeting. Vietnam decided in one meeting. Your special meeting produced a homework assignment for the KSN due next Tuesday. If your government cannot make a straightforward operational decision — let civil servants work from home during an energy crisis — during a meeting specifically convened for that crisis, then what exactly is the Cabinet for?

Beliau berkata kementerian telah “menyerahkan perkara itu kepada pihak berkuasa berkaitan untuk siasatan lanjut dan tindakan sewajarnya,” sambil menekankan kerjasama rapat dengan agensi penguatkuasaan untuk melindungi pelajar daripada pengaruh “terutamanya yang berkait rapat dengan Daesh dan keganasan.” Kenyataan ini dibuat berminggu-minggu selepas Cawangan Khas menangkap enam remaja lelaki berusia 16 hingga 21 tahun pada 14–15 Februari dalam operasi khas atas syak terlibat dalam aktiviti IS.

🔥 Kenapa Ini Penting

Tangkapan enam remaja kerana kaitan IS sepatutnya menjadi isyarat kecemasan bagi mana-mana Menteri Pendidikan. Sebaliknya, respons Fadhlina — diberi berminggu-minggu kemudian — adalah demonstrasi kepasifan birokrasi: “memantau,” “serahkan kepada pihak berkuasa,” “bekerjasama rapat.” Ini menteri yang sama yang menghabiskan 2025 berhadapan desakan letak jawatan gara-gara kegagalan keselamatan sekolah — kematian akibat buli, serangan seksual, dan budaya menutup kes yang diakui sendiri oleh Ketua Pengarahnya. Coraknya konsisten: ancaman serius timbul, Fadhlina cakap tentang pemantauan, dan kementerian tunggu orang lain bertindak. Kalau perekrut IS menyasarkan kanak-kanak Malaysia, peranan Kementerian Pendidikan bukan memerhati dari tepi — ia adalah merombak cara sekolah mengesan, melaporkan, dan bertindak balas terhadap radikalisasi. Sebaliknya, kita dapat sidang akhbar.

💩 Kenapa Ini Karut

Enam remaja ditangkap kerana kaitan IS, dan respons Menteri Pendidikan — sebulan penuh kemudian — ialah kementerian sedang “memantau.” Bukan lancarkan program deradikalisasi seluruh negara. Bukan umumkan latihan wajib guru untuk mengenal pasti tanda-tanda radikalisasi. Bukan semak semula kurikulum untuk kandungan antiekstremisme. Malah tidak dedahkan berapa ramai pelajar telah dibenderakan. Cuma “memantau” dan “serah kepada pihak berkuasa.” Fadhlina telah menjadikan minimalis reaktif sebagai seni. Bila pelajar dibuli sampai mati, beliau “mengambil serius.” Bila sekolah tutup kes serangan seksual, beliau ikrar “toleransi sifar.” Kini remaja direkrut IS, dan kementerian “memantau.” Pada tahap mana kerajaan mengakui bahawa mod operasi menteri ini adalah pemerhatian berterusan, bukan tindakan tegas?

📜 Kes-Kes Sebelum Ini

Rekod Fadhlina dalam memberi respons lambat dan pasif telah didokumentasikan dengan baik. Pada 2025, gelombang kes buli sekolah — termasuk kematian — mencetuskan kemarahan awam dan memorandum menuntut peletakan jawatannya, yang diterimanya di luar Parlimen pada Oktober 2025. Ketua Pengarah sendiri mengakui kes-kes telah “disapu bawah karpet.” Presiden MCA Wee Ka Siong secara terbuka menuntut pemecatannya pada Disember 2025, memanggil tempoh perkhidmatannya “tidak berkesan.” Penganalisis menamakan beliau ahli Kabinet paling terdedah dalam mana-mana rombakan. Sepanjang setiap krisis, respons Fadhlina mengikut skrip yang sama: akui masalah, ikrar kewaspadaan, rujuk kepada pihak berkuasa, dan tunggu kitaran berita bergerak.

🎯 Panggilan Kepada PH

PH janji kerajaan yang bertindak, bukan yang memantau. Kamu janji pembaharuan institusi, bukan pemerhatian institusi. Bila IS merekrut kanak-kanak di sekolah Malaysia, tugas Menteri Pendidikan bukan memberitahu pemberita bahawa beliau “serahkan kepada pihak berkuasa” — ia adalah memimpin. Fadhlina Sidek telah diberi peluang demi peluang demi peluang. Buli. Serangan seksual. Kini pengambilan keganasan. Setiap kali, respons yang sama: kami memantau. Anak-anak Malaysia layak mendapat kementerian yang bertindak sebelum tangkapan berlaku, bukan yang keluarkan kenyataan akhbar selepas polis sudah buat kerja. Kalau ini tawaran terbaik PH untuk pendidikan, maka janji Madani hanyalah slogan yang dilukis pada kementerian yang enggan bergerak.
