Record 236 Tons of Meth Seized in Region — Malaysia a Key Transit Hub

Hantu Raya

May 28, 2025

Said by: UNODC Report 2025

Category: Health

Date: 2025-05-28

UNODC reported a record 236 tons of methamphetamine seized in East and Southeast Asia in 2024, up 24% from 2023. Malaysia busted its largest-ever maritime drug trafficking ring — 18+ tonnes worth RM375M. Drug use is increasing among older demographics. Government approved a ‘communication plan’ — not enforcement reform. This matters because Malaysia abolished mandatory death for drug trafficking, then watched seizures hit record highs. The government’s response to a worsening drug crisis was a ‘communication plan’ — essentially a PR strategy instead of structural enforcement reform. Malaysia sits on a major trafficking corridor and its ports are clearly being exploited. Record seizures can mean better enforcement or a bigger problem — and when the UNODC says the regional supply is surging 24% year-on-year, it’s the latter. A government that responds to a drug tsunami with a communications strategy is not serious about the problem. Sources: UNODC | Vietnam Plus
