Goldman Sachs 1MDB Settlement Review — Anwar Threatens Lawsuits, Nothing Happens

Hantu Raya

August 21, 2023

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2023-08-21

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In August 2023, Anwar Ibrahim called the $3.9 billion Goldman Sachs 1MDB settlement “done hastily” under the previous government and threatened to take Goldman to court for a larger recovery. He vowed Malaysia would “recover every single sen.” Goldman reportedly made “fresh overtures” in response. Years later, the review has produced nothing concrete — no lawsuit, no renegotiation, no additional recovery. CNA uncovered details suggesting the confidential settlement agreement may have shortchanged Malaysia, with Goldman paying a fraction of the losses it facilitated.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Goldman Sachs helped arrange $6.5 billion in bonds for 1MDB, earning hundreds of millions in fees while billions were stolen. The $3.9 billion settlement — negotiated under Muhyiddin — was criticised as inadequate given the scale of Goldman’s role. Anwar’s promise to review the settlement and recover more raised expectations. But promises without follow-through are just performance. If the settlement was “done hastily” and shortchanged Malaysia, why hasn’t Anwar filed suit in three years? The answer is either that the legal case is weaker than claimed, or that Goldman’s political leverage is stronger than Anwar’s resolve.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Threatening Goldman Sachs with lawsuits makes for great headlines. Actually suing Goldman Sachs requires resources, political will, and a willingness to antagonise a powerful Wall Street institution with extensive business interests in Malaysia. Anwar chose the headline over the lawsuit. Three years of “review” with no outcome is not due diligence — it’s indefinite deferral. The rakyat is still paying the 1MDB debt — RM50 billion on the books — while the bank that facilitated the theft walks away with a settlement that Anwar himself called inadequate.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: The Goldman settlement was negotiated under Muhyiddin’s government in 2020 — a $3.9 billion package including $2.5 billion in cash and a $1.4 billion guarantee on 1MDB asset recovery. PH 1.0 under Mahathir had initiated legal proceedings against Goldman but was removed before completion. The Sheraton Move government settled. Anwar promised to reopen the case. Three administrations later, Goldman’s liability has been capped while Malaysia’s debt continues to compound.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You called the Goldman settlement “hasty” and promised to recover “every single sen.” That was three years ago. Where’s the lawsuit? Where’s the renegotiation? Where’s the additional recovery? Goldman facilitated the theft of billions from Malaysia. The rakyat is still paying the debt. Either file suit or explain why you can’t. But stop threatening action you have no intention of taking — because every day you delay, Goldman’s lawyers grow more confident that your threats were empty.
