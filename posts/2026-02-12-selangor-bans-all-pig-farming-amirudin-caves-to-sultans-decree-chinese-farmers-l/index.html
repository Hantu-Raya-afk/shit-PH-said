Selangor Bans All Pig Farming — Amirudin Caves to Sultan's Decree, Chinese Farmers Left With Nothing

Hantu Raya

February 12, 2026

Said by: Amirudin Shari, Selangor Menteri Besar

Category: Social

Date: 2026-02-12

📌 WHAT HAPPENED

On 12 February 2026, Selangor Menteri Besar Amirudin Shari announced that the state government has stopped issuing pig farming licences and will accelerate the closure of all existing pig farms. The decision followed a decree from Sultan Sharafuddin Idris Shah, who declared that no pig farms should operate in any district in Selangor.

An earlier plan to relocate pig farms to Bukit Tagar — a sparsely populated area in northern Selangor — was shelved after Malay residents objected due to proximity to Sungai Selangor, a major water treatment source. Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim intervened in January to halt the relocation.

Selangor will now fully import all pork to meet non-Muslim demand. Chinese pig farmers, many of whom have operated multigenerational farms in Tanjong Sepat, face the destruction of their livelihoods.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS

Selangor is Malaysia’s richest and most developed state — and it’s governed by PH. The pig farming ban affects a specific ethnic community’s livelihood and dietary needs. While environmental concerns about water pollution are legitimate, the total ban with no viable alternative is a capitulation, not a solution.

The message to Chinese voters is clear: when the palace decrees and Malay sentiment pushes back, PH will not fight for you. The Bukit Tagar relocation — which could have been a modern, regulated solution — was abandoned the moment it became politically uncomfortable.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT

PH governs Selangor. PH had years to develop a proper, regulated, environmentally sustainable pig farming solution. Instead, they proposed a relocation, got pushback, abandoned it, and then caved to a royal decree with a total ban.

The state that brands itself as progressive and inclusive just eliminated an entire industry that primarily serves its Chinese community. Pork importers — who stand to profit massively — are already backing the ban. One importer estimated imports already cover 80% of demand. So who benefits? Not the farmers. Not the consumers who prefer fresh local pork. The importers.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES

This follows a pattern of PH state governments failing to protect minority communities when the political cost gets too high. In Penang, DAP has governed for 16 years but still faces criticism for development that displaces communities. In Selangor, Amirudin has repeatedly been caught flat-footed — from flood management failures to the water leak crisis. Every time, the response is reactive, never proactive.

🎯 PH CALLOUT

You are the government of Selangor. You had the power to regulate, modernise, and relocate pig farming. Instead, you banned it entirely. You didn’t solve a pollution problem — you eliminated a community’s livelihood. Where is the DAP that promised to protect all Malaysians regardless of race? Where is the Madani government that talks about inclusivity? The Chinese farmers in Tanjong Sepat would like to know.
