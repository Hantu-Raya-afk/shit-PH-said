HRW 2024: 'Reform Agenda Stalled' — Government Failed to Uphold Pledges in First Year

Hantu Raya

January 11, 2024

Said by: Human Rights Watch

Category: Social

Date: 2024-01-11

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: In January 2024, Human Rights Watch released its 2024 World Report, delivering a damning verdict on Anwar Ibrahim’s first year in office: “Reform Agenda Stalled.” HRW stated that Anwar had “largely failed to uphold his pledges to address repression and corruption” and called on the government to “recommit to a rights-respecting agenda by turning its sidelined campaign promises into action.” The report documented failures across freedom of expression, assembly, media freedom, and institutional reform. A year later, HRW’s 2025 assessment was even harsher: “Rights Backslide.”

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Two consecutive years of HRW condemnation is not a bump in the road — it is a trajectory. Year 1: stalled. Year 2: backslide. Year 3: expanded internet controls, 90+ sedition arrests, and journalist arrests. When the world’s most prominent human rights organisation says your reform agenda has stalled — and then says it’s actively going backwards — the problem is not perception. It is policy. PH was elected on a reform mandate. The international human rights community is documenting, in real time, the abandonment of that mandate.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: PH’s response to HRW’s assessment was predictable: “They don’t understand our context.” “Coalition constraints.” “We need time.” But HRW gave PH a year before issuing the “stalled” verdict. Then another year before escalating to “backslide.” The organisation is not being impatient — it is documenting a government that promised reform and delivered regression. The Sedition Act was not just left on the books — it was expanded. Media freedom was not just neglected — journalists were arrested. Assembly rights were not just unprotected — Bersih rallies were suppressed. This is not stalling. This is reversal.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: HRW has criticised every Malaysian government. Under Najib, the assessments focused on 1MDB, repressive laws, and crackdowns on civil society. Under Muhyiddin and Ismail Sabri, the focus was pandemic-era restrictions and democratic backsliding. But PH was supposed to be different — it was the government that HRW’s recommendations aligned with. When the human rights organisation that should be your natural ally says you’ve stalled and backslid, the problem is not the critic. It’s the government.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: Human Rights Watch gave you a year. Then another year. The verdict got worse, not better. “Stalled” became “backslide.” If the trajectory continues, Year 4 will be “regression.” Implement the reforms HRW identified: repeal the Sedition Act, strengthen media freedom protections, guarantee assembly rights, and restore MACC independence. Or keep dismissing international criticism as “not understanding context” — while the context is that you’re doing exactly what BN did, with better English.
