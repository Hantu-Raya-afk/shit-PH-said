Debt-to-GDP Still at 64.6% — Anwar Says Wait Until 2028 for Relief

Hantu Raya

December 2, 2025

Said by: Anwar Ibrahim, Prime Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2025-12-02

📌 WHAT HAPPENED: Malaysia’s debt-to-GDP ratio stood at 64.6% in 2024 — above the government’s own 60% target ceiling set under the Public Finance and Fiscal Responsibility Act. In December 2025, Anwar told Bloomberg that debt pressure would “ease from end-2028” — six years into his tenure before fiscal health even begins to improve. New borrowings were reduced to RM75 billion from RM100 billion but remain massive. The 1MDB legacy alone still accounts for RM50 billion in liabilities. CNA reported on Malaysia’s “increased spending” alongside the “low debt ratio” aspiration that remains unfulfilled.

🔥 WHY IT MATTERS: Anwar campaigned on fiscal responsibility and ending the debt culture of previous administrations. Instead, his government is borrowing at near-record levels while 1MDB alone still accounts for RM50 billion in liabilities. Debt-to-GDP above 60% means Malaysia’s fiscal buffer against economic shocks is dangerously thin. If a global recession hits, or commodity prices crash, or a pandemic returns, the government has limited room to respond. Telling Malaysians to wait until 2028 for fiscal relief is asking them to trust a PM who may not even be in power by then.

💩 WHY IT’S BULLSHIT: Fiscal discipline means making hard choices now — not pushing timelines to the next election cycle. The 2028 target conveniently falls after GE16, meaning Anwar wants voters to re-elect him on the promise that fiscal improvement is “just around the corner” — the same promise he made in 2022. Meanwhile, spending continues to rise: civil service wages (1.6 million employees), subsidies (even after “rationalisation”), development projects (many driven by political rather than economic logic), and debt servicing (16% of revenue). The structural deficit means every year the government spends more than it earns, and borrows the difference. PH has not fundamentally changed this equation — it has just changed the rhetoric around it.

📜 PREVIOUS CASES: Every Malaysian government has promised fiscal responsibility while borrowing heavily. Najib’s 1MDB created the RM50 billion legacy that still haunts the books. Muhyiddin’s pandemic spending was necessary but poorly tracked. Anwar inherited a mess — that’s true. But three years is enough time to demonstrate a credible path to fiscal consolidation. Instead, the target keeps moving: first it was 2025, then 2027, now 2028. The can gets kicked, the debt compounds, and the next government inherits an even bigger mess.

🎯 PH CALLOUT: You’ve been in power for over three years. The debt-to-GDP ratio is STILL above your own 60% target. You’re telling Malaysians to wait until 2028 — conveniently after the next election — for relief. That’s not a fiscal plan, it’s an electoral strategy. Cut spending that doesn’t serve the public (238 political appointments, RM85.5M J-Kom propaganda, RM2.6B JAKIM). Enforce tax compliance on the wealthy and corporations. Stop borrowing to fund political patronage. Or admit that fiscal responsibility, like every other reform promise, is permanently “coming soon.”
