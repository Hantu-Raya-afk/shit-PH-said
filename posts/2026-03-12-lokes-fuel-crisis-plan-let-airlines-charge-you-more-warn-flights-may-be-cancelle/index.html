Loke's Fuel Crisis Plan: Let Airlines Charge You More, Warn Flights May Be Cancelled — Happy Hari Raya

Hantu Raya

March 12, 2026

Said by: Anthony Loke, Transport Minister

Category: Economy

Date: 2026-03-12

📌 What Happened

On March 12, 2026, Transport Minister Anthony Loke chaired a meeting with airline management, airport operators, jet fuel suppliers and aviation authorities to discuss the impact of the West Asia conflict on soaring fuel costs. Loke announced that the Transport Ministry had reached an “agreement in principle” to allow airlines to charge customers additional fees — fuel surcharges — to cover rising costs. He also warned that “if fuel prices continue to rise, airlines may need to suspend certain flight operations.” The ministry committed to weekly meetings with aviation stakeholders while the conflict persists. Short-term measures were discussed to keep air cargo services running for food supplies ahead of Aidilfitri.

🔥 Why It Matters

This is the government’s aviation crisis playbook in a single meeting: no intervention, no subsidy, no cost absorption — just permission for airlines to charge the rakyat more. Malaysia is heading into the peak Hari Raya travel season with oil past $100 a barrel and the Strait of Hormuz under military threat. Millions of Malaysians rely on domestic flights to balik kampung, especially those from Sabah and Sarawak. Loke’s response is to let airlines impose surcharges and float the possibility of flight cancellations. This is the same government that claims it is “cushioning the rakyat” from the crisis.

💩 Why It’s Bullshit

Anwar spent the week telling Malaysians the government would “protect” them from the economic fallout of the Middle East conflict. Cabinet maintained RON95 at RM1.99 and announced RM15 billion in STR handouts. But when it comes to aviation — a lifeline for East Malaysian families and a key cost for Hari Raya travel — the government’s answer is to let airlines pass the bill to passengers. No jet fuel subsidy. No temporary fare cap. No emergency allocation. Just “weekly meetings” and an agreement to let airlines charge more. Loke is essentially telling Malaysians: your Raya flights will cost more, and some may not fly at all. That is not crisis management. That is crisis delegation.

📜 Previous Cases

This pattern is familiar. During the 2022 post-pandemic aviation recovery, airlines hiked fares and the government did nothing to intervene despite promising consumer protections. Loke himself championed the Malaysian Aviation Consumer Protection Code amendments in 2024, requiring refunds for delays — but structural protections against price gouging during crises remain absent. Meanwhile, countries like Thailand and Indonesia have moved faster on fuel interventions and aviation support packages. Malaysia’s playbook remains the same: form a committee, hold a meeting, issue a press statement, and let the market do what it wants.

🎯 PH Callout

You promised a government that puts rakyat first. You spent the week announcing cash handouts and claiming fiscal discipline. But when millions of Malaysians need to fly home for Hari Raya and airlines are about to hit them with surcharges — or cancel flights entirely — your Transport Minister’s solution is to give airlines permission to charge more. Where is the emergency aviation fund? Where is the temporary jet fuel subsidy? Where is the fare cap for domestic routes during festive periods? You had time to ban Raya open houses for “fiscal discipline” but no time to protect the rakyat from being gouged at the airport. Reformasi, apparently, does not extend to airline pricing.
